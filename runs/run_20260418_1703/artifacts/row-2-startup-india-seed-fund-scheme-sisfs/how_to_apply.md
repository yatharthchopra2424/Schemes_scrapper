## Prerequisites
To be eligible for the Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS), the following criteria must be met:
* The startup must be recognized by DPIIT and incorporated not more than 2 years ago at the time of application.
* The startup must have a business idea to develop a product or a service with market fit, viable commercialization, and scope of scaling.
* The startup should be using technology in its core product or service, or business model, or distribution model, or methodology to solve the problem being targeted.
* Preference would be given to startups creating innovative solutions in sectors such as social impact, waste management, water management, financial inclusion, education, agriculture, food processing, biotechnology, healthcare, energy, mobility, defence, space, railways, oil and gas, textiles, etc.
* The startup should not have received more than Rs 10 lakh of monetary support under any other Central or State Government scheme.
* Shareholding by Indian promoters in the startup should be at least 51% at the time of application to incubator for the scheme, as per Companies Act, 2013 and SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018.

## Documents Required
The following documents are required for the application process:
1. **DPIIT Recognition Certificate**: A certificate of recognition from the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT).
2. **Incorporation Certificate**: A certificate of incorporation of the startup.
3. **Business Plan**: A detailed business plan outlining the startup's idea, market fit, commercialization, and scaling.
4. **Financial Projections**: Financial projections of the startup, including revenue, expenses, and funding requirements.
5. **Team Profile**: A profile of the startup's team, including their experience, skills, and expertise.
6. **Product/Service Description**: A description of the startup's product or service, including its features, benefits, and market potential.
7. **Market Research Report**: A market research report outlining the startup's target market, competition, and market size.
8. **Funding Request**: A request for funding, including the amount required and the proposed use of funds.
9. **Incubator Preference**: A preference for the incubator, including the reasons for choosing the incubator.
10. **Other Supporting Documents**: Any other supporting documents, such as patents, trademarks, or copyrights.

## Step-by-Step Application Process
The application process for the SISFS involves the following steps:
1. **Registration**: Register on the Startup India portal and obtain a DPIIT recognition number.
2. **Login**: Log in to the Startup India portal using the DPIIT recognition number and password.
3. **Application Form**: Fill out the application form, including the startup's details, business plan, financial projections, team profile, product/service description, market research report, funding request, and incubator preference.
4. **Upload Documents**: Upload the required documents, including the incorporation certificate, business plan, financial projections, team profile, product/service description, market research report, funding request, and incubator preference.
5. **Submit Application**: Submit the application form and uploaded documents.
6. **Evaluation**: The application will be evaluated by the incubator and the Experts Advisory Committee (EAC).
7. **Selection**: The startup will be selected based on the evaluation and the availability of funds.
8. **Funding**: The selected startup will receive funding from the incubator.

## Timeline & Deadlines
The application window for the SISFS is open throughout the year. The final deadline for completion of 100% startup selection is 15 June 2026.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
Some common mistakes to avoid during the application process include:
* **Ineligible Startups**: Applying for the scheme without meeting the eligibility criteria.
* **Incomplete Applications**: Submitting incomplete applications or uploading incomplete documents.
* **Lack of Clarity**: Failing to provide clear and concise information in the application form and uploaded documents.
* **Insufficient Funding**: Requesting insufficient funding or failing to provide a clear funding plan.

## After Submission
After submitting the application, the startup can track the status of their application on the Startup India portal. The incubator and the EAC will evaluate the application and select the startup based on the evaluation. The selected startup will receive funding from the incubator.

## Help & Support
For any queries or issues during the application process, startups can contact the Startup India team at dipp-startups@nic.in or visit the Startup India portal for more information.
