## Scheme at a Glance
The Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS) is an initiative by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT) to provide financial assistance to startups for proof of concept, prototype development, product trials, market entry, and commercialization. The scheme was launched on April 19, 2021, with a corpus of ₹945 crores to support an estimated 3,600 entrepreneurs through 300 incubators in the next four years.

## Programme Objectives
The primary objectives of the SISFS are:
* To provide financial assistance to startups for proof of concept, prototype development, product trials, market entry, and commercialization
* To enable startups to graduate to a level where they can raise investments from angel investors or venture capitalists or seek loans from commercial banks or financial institutions
* To support innovative business ideas and promote entrepreneurship in India
* To create a robust startup ecosystem in India
* To provide funding to startups with an innovative idea to conduct proof of concept trials

## Target Beneficiaries
The SISFS is designed for startups recognized by DPIIT, incorporated not more than two years ago, with a business idea to develop a product or service with market fit, viable commercialization, and scope of scaling. The scheme is sector-agnostic, but preference is given to startups creating innovative solutions in sectors such as social impact, waste management, water management, financial inclusion, education, agriculture, food processing, biotechnology, healthcare, energy, mobility, defense, space, railways, oil and gas, textiles, etc.

## Financial Architecture
The financial architecture of the SISFS is as follows:
| Parameter | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Fund Corpus | ₹945 crores |
| Max Per Entity | Up to ₹20 lakhs as grant for validation of Proof of Concept, or prototype development, or product trials, and up to ₹50 lakhs of investment for market entry, commercialization, or scaling up |

## Implementation Mechanism
The SISFS will be implemented through a network of incubators across India. The incubators will be selected based on their eligibility criteria, and they will be responsible for selecting startups, disbursing seed funds, and monitoring their progress. The incubators will also provide physical infrastructure, support for testing and validating ideas, mentoring for prototype or product development or commercialization, and developing capacities in finance, human resources, legal compliances, and other functions.

## Current Status & Impact
As of now, the SISFS has supported over 2917 startups across various sectors, with a total funding of ₹945 crores. The scheme has created a significant impact on the startup ecosystem in India, providing financial assistance to innovative startups and promoting entrepreneurship.

## Key Caveats
The key caveats of the SISFS are:
* The startup should not have received more than ₹10 lakhs of monetary support under any other Central or State Government scheme
* The startup should have a business idea to develop a product or service with market fit, viable commercialization, and scope of scaling
* The startup should be using technology in its core product or service, or business model, or distribution model, or methodology to solve the problem being targeted
* The incubator should be a legal entity, operational for at least two years, and have facilities to seat at least 25 individuals
