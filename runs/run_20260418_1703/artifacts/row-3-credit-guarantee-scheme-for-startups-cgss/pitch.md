## Why This Scheme Matters
The **Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS)** is a vital initiative aimed at providing credit guarantees to loans extended by Member Institutions (MIs) to finance eligible startups. This scheme addresses a significant challenge faced by startups in India, which is accessing credit without collateral. By providing a guarantee, the CGSS mitigates the risk for lenders, making it easier for startups to secure funding and grow their businesses.

## What You Get
The key benefits of the CGSS include:
* Credit guarantee up to a specified limit against loans extended by MIs to finance eligible startups
* Enhanced guarantee coverage, increasing the maximum limit from ₹10 crore to ₹20 crore per eligible borrower
* Automatic issue of guarantee cover under CGSS based on meeting eligibility parameters
* Support for various instruments, including venture debt, working capital, subordinated debt/mezzanine debt, debentures, and optionally convertible debt
* Transaction-based and umbrella-based guarantee cover options

## Who Qualifies
To be eligible for the CGSS, an entity must be a startup recognized by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT) and not in default to any lending/investing institution. The startup's eligibility must be certified by the Member Institution for the purpose of guarantee cover.

## Financial Support
The CGSS has a fixed corpus for providing credit guarantees. The maximum amount of debt eligible for guarantee cover under the scheme is ₹20 crore per borrower. The extent of the guarantee cover is up to 85% of the amount in default for loan amounts up to ₹10 crore and up to 75% for loan amounts exceeding ₹10 crore.

## How to Get Started
To apply for the CGSS, startups can follow these steps:
1. Visit the Jan Samarth Portal and complete the online application.
2. Approach the nearest branch of any Member Lending Institution (MLI) directly and enquire about the required process.
3. Register as a Member Institution (MI) by submitting a signed undertaking and Board Resolution.
4. Apply for guarantee cover on the NCGTC's portal after successful registration as an MI.

## Key Contact
For more information and to apply, visit the Jan Samarth Portal or contact the National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company (NCGTC) through their portal.
