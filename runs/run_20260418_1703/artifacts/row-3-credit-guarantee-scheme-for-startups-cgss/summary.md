## Scheme at a Glance
The Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS) is a government-backed initiative aimed at providing credit guarantees to loans extended by Member Institutions (MIs) to finance eligible startups. The scheme is designed to support DPIIT-recognized startups by providing a guarantee cover of up to ₹20 crore per borrower, thereby enabling them to access collateral-free debt funding.

## Programme Objectives
The primary objectives of the CGSS are:
* To provide credit guarantees to loans extended by MIs to finance eligible startups
* To support DPIIT-recognized startups in accessing collateral-free debt funding
* To enhance the credit availability for startups and promote entrepreneurship
* To encourage banks, NBFCs, and venture debt funds to lend to startups
* To provide a platform for startups to access funds for their business operations

## Target Beneficiaries
The CGSS is designed for DPIIT-recognized startups, which are defined as entities that meet the criteria specified by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT). The scheme is open to startups that are not in default to any lending institution and have not been classified as a Non-Performing Asset (NPA) as per RBI guidelines.

## Implementation Mechanism
The CGSS is implemented through a Trustee, the National Credit Guarantee Trustee Company (NCGTC), which provides guarantee cover to MIs that lend to eligible startups. The scheme operates through a transaction-based or umbrella-based guarantee model, where the guarantee cover is provided for a specified percentage of the loan amount.

## Current Status & Impact
The current status and impact of the CGSS are not specified in the provided text. However, the scheme is expected to enhance credit availability for startups and promote entrepreneurship in India.

## Key Caveats
The key caveats of the CGSS are:
* The scheme is only available for DPIIT-recognized startups
* The guarantee cover is limited to ₹20 crore per borrower
* The scheme does not provide guarantee cover directly to startups, but through MIs
* The guarantee cover is subject to the terms and conditions specified by the NCGTC
