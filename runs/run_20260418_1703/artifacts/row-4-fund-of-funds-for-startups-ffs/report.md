## 📋 Executive Overview
The Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS) is a government-backed investment fund that provides funding to startups in India. The scheme is managed by the Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) and has a corpus of INR 10,000 crores. The fund invests in SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) that in turn invest in startups.


---

## 🎯 Objectives
- To provide funding to startups in India
- To promote entrepreneurship and innovation in the country
- To create jobs and stimulate economic growth

## ✅ Eligibility
Startups that are registered in India and meet the eligibility criteria set by the government can apply for funding under the FFS scheme.


**Target Beneficiaries:** startups
**Geographic Scope:** Pan-India

---

## 🎁 Benefits
The FFS scheme provides funding to startups, which can be used to develop their business, hire employees, and expand their operations. The scheme also provides mentorship and networking opportunities to startups.


---

## 💳 Financial Support
The FFS scheme provides funding to startups through SEBI-registered AIFs. The fund invests in AIFs that in turn invest in startups.


---

## 📝 How to Apply

## Application Process
Startups can apply for funding under the FFS scheme through the Startup India website. The application process involves submitting a business plan, financial projections, and other relevant documents.


**Application Portal:** [Apply Here](https://www.startupindia.gov.in/)

## Required Documents
- Business plan
- Financial projections
- Registration certificate
- PAN card
- Bank account details


## ⏰ Deadlines
The application process for the FFS scheme is open throughout the year. However, the government may announce specific deadlines for applying to the scheme.

## ⚠️ Caveats & Conditions
- The FFS scheme is only available to startups that are registered in India.
- The scheme has a limited corpus, and funding is provided on a first-come, first-served basis.
