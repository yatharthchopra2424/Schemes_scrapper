## Scheme at a Glance
The Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS) is a scheme launched by the Government of India to support startups in the country. The scheme is managed by the Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) and has a corpus of ₹10,000 crores. The scheme aims to provide funding to startups through investments in venture capital funds and other alternative investment funds.

## Programme Objectives
The main objectives of the FFS scheme are:
* To provide funding to startups in India
* To promote entrepreneurship and innovation in the country
* To support the growth of startups and help them scale up their operations
* To create jobs and stimulate economic growth
* To provide a platform for startups to access funding and other resources

## Target Beneficiaries
The FFS scheme is designed to support startups in India, particularly those in the early stages of growth. The scheme is open to startups that are registered in India and have a unique product or service that has the potential to scale up.

## Financial Architecture
The FFS scheme has a corpus of ₹10,000 crores, which is managed by SIDBI. The scheme provides funding to startups through investments in venture capital funds and other alternative investment funds. The funding is provided in the form of equity investments, and the scheme has a focus on supporting startups in the early stages of growth.
| Parameter | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Fund Corpus | ₹10,000 crores |
| Investment Size | Up to ₹50 crores per startup |
| Investment Horizon | 7-10 years |
| Focus Areas | Technology, healthcare, education, and other sectors |

## Implementation Mechanism
The FFS scheme is implemented by SIDBI, which is responsible for managing the fund and making investments in venture capital funds and other alternative investment funds. The scheme has a robust governance structure, with a committee that oversees the investments and ensures that the scheme is aligned with its objectives.

## Current Status & Impact
The FFS scheme has been successful in supporting startups in India, with over ₹5,000 crores invested in over 500 startups. The scheme has helped to create jobs, stimulate economic growth, and promote entrepreneurship and innovation in the country.

## Key Caveats
The FFS scheme has some key caveats, including:
* The scheme is only open to startups that are registered in India
* The scheme has a focus on supporting startups in the early stages of growth
* The scheme provides funding in the form of equity investments, which means that the government will have a stake in the startup
* The scheme has a robust governance structure, with a committee that oversees the investments and ensures that the scheme is aligned with its objectives
