## Prerequisites
To apply for the **Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0**, the following prerequisites must be met:
* The startup must be recognized by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT).
* The startup must be incorporated as a private limited company or a limited liability partnership.
* The startup must have a turnover of less than ₹100 crore.
* The startup must be working towards innovation, improvement, or development of a product, process, or service.
* The startup must have a scalable business model with high potential for employment generation or wealth creation.

## Documents Required
The following documents are required to apply for the **Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0**:
1. Certificate of Incorporation
2. Memorandum of Association (MoA) and Articles of Association (AoA)
3. PAN card of the company
4. GST registration certificate
5. Bank account details
6. Audited financial statements for the last two years
7. Business plan and pitch deck
8. Proof of innovation and uniqueness of the product or service
9. Details of the founding team and their experience
10. Details of the funding required and proposed use of funds

## Step-by-Step Application Process
1. Register on the Startup India portal and obtain a DPIIT recognition number.
2. Prepare the required documents and information.
3. Fill out the online application form on the Startup India portal.
    a. Provide company details and founder information.
    b. Upload required documents.
    c. Submit the application.
4. Wait for the application to be processed and verified.
5. Receive a confirmation email with further instructions.

## Timeline & Deadlines
The application window for the **Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0** is open throughout the year. However, the processing time may take several weeks to several months. It is recommended to apply at least 6-12 months before the funding is required.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Incomplete application**: Ensure that all required documents and information are provided.
2. **Ineligible startup**: Verify that the startup meets the eligibility criteria before applying.
3. **Insufficient proof of innovation**: Provide adequate proof of innovation and uniqueness of the product or service.
4. **Poor business plan**: Ensure that the business plan is well-structured and convincing.
5. **Lack of follow-up**: Follow up with the Startup India team to ensure that the application is being processed.

## After Submission
After submitting the application, the startup can expect the following:
1. **Verification**: The application will be verified by the Startup India team.
2. **Shortlisting**: The startup will be shortlisted based on the eligibility criteria and the quality of the application.
3. **Due diligence**: The shortlisted startups will undergo a due diligence process.
4. **Funding**: The selected startups will receive funding from the **Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0**.
