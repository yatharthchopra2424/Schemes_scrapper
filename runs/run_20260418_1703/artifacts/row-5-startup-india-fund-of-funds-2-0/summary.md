## Scheme at a Glance
The Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0 is a scheme initiated by the Government of India to support and promote Indian startups. The scheme aims to provide financial assistance to startups and encourage entrepreneurship and innovation in the country.

## Programme Objectives
The primary objectives of the Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0 are:
* To provide financial support to startups and encourage entrepreneurship and innovation
* To promote the growth and development of startups in India
* To create a conducive ecosystem for startups to thrive and succeed
* To encourage investment in startups and provide funding opportunities
* To support the development of innovative and scalable business models

## Target Beneficiaries
The scheme is designed for startups, including those in the technology, manufacturing, agriculture, healthcare, and other sectors. The target beneficiaries are:
* Startups recognized by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT)
* Startups that are incorporated as private limited companies or registered as partnership firms or limited liability partnerships
* Startups that have a turnover of less than INR 100 crores and have been in existence for less than 10 years

## Financial Architecture
The financial architecture of the scheme is as follows:
| Parameter | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Fund Corpus | ₹ 10,000 crores |
| Max Per Entity | ₹ 50 crores |

## Implementation Mechanism
The scheme is implemented through a fund of funds, which invests in venture capital funds and other investment vehicles that support startups. The fund is managed by a professional investment manager, and the investments are made through a rigorous selection process.

## Current Status & Impact
The scheme has been successful in providing financial support to startups and promoting entrepreneurship and innovation in the country. As of now, the scheme has invested in over 100 startups, and the fund has committed over ₹ 5,000 crores to venture capital funds and other investment vehicles.

## Key Caveats
The key caveats of the scheme are:
* The scheme is only available to startups that are recognized by DPIIT and meet the eligibility criteria
* The scheme provides financial support through investments in venture capital funds and other investment vehicles, and not through direct grants or loans to startups
* The scheme has a rigorous selection process, and investments are made based on the potential for growth and scalability of the startup
