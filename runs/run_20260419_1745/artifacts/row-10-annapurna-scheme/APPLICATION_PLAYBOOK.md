# Application Playbook
## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)
### Checklist
The following checklist is based on the Annapurna Scheme's eligibility criteria:
- ✅ Women entrepreneurs in the food industry
- ✅ Minimum age of 21 years
- ✅ Maximum age of 55 years
- ✅ Good credit history
- ✅ Viable business plan

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)
### Universal Documents
The following documents are required:
- Identity proof
- Address proof
- Business plan
- Financial statements

### Scheme-Specific Documents
No additional documents are specified beyond the universal set for the Annapurna Scheme.

### Document Quality Check
Ensure all documents meet the standard requirements:
- File size: As per SBI's guidelines
- Name matching: Ensure all documents have the applicant's name as per the ID proof
- Certification requirements: Self-attested or attested by a gazetted officer as required
- Language: English or the local language of the state

### Client Communication Template
"Dear [Client Name], we are processing your application for the Annapurna Scheme. Please find attached the list of required documents. The application portal is available at [SBI Website]. If you have any questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to reach out to us. The deadline for submission is [Deadline]."

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)
### Project Report / DPR
A DPR is not explicitly required for the Annapurna Scheme. However, a detailed business plan is necessary.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
The following rules apply when filling the application form:
1. Ensure all fields are complete and accurate.
2. Attach all required documents.
3. Sign the application form as per the SBI's requirements.
4. Verify the applicant's identity and address.
5. Ensure the business plan is comprehensive and viable.
6. Check for any additional requirements specific to the Annapurna Scheme.
7. Submit the application through the SBI website or at the nearest SBI branch.

### Internal Review Gate
Before submitting the application, review the following:
- All fields are complete
- Documents are attached and verified
- Declarations are signed
- Business plan is viable and complete

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)
### Submission Protocol
1. Submit the application through the SBI website or at the nearest SBI branch.
2. Take a screenshot of the submission confirmation.
3. Store the reference number for future follow-ups.
4. Set reminders for follow-up actions.

### Follow-Up Script
"Hello, this is [Your Name] from [Your Company]. I am following up on the application for the Annapurna Scheme submitted on [Date] with reference number [Reference Number]. Could you please provide an update on the status of the application? Thank you."

### Escalation Path
- Level 1: Contact the SBI customer care or the nearest SBI branch.
- Level 2: Reach out to the nodal officer or the SBI's grievance redressal cell.
- Level 3: File an RTI application if no response is received within the statutory time limit.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)
### Approval Actions
After approval, ensure the following:
- Receive the approval letter
- Verify the sanctioned amount
- Obtain a utilization certificate
- Invoice the success fee as per the agreement

Note: The success fee and retainer amounts are to be filled in by the consultant as per their agreement with the client. The last successful client and win rate are also to be updated by the consultant based on their performance.
