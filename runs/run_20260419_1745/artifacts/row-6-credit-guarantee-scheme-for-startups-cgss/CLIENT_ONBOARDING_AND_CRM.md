## CRM Stage Pipeline
| Stage | Description | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 01 - Lead | Initial contact made | Schedule discovery call |
| 02 - Qualified | Eligibility confirmed for GENESIS Scheme | Send proposal |
| 03 - Engaged | Retainer signed | Start onboarding |
| 04 - Docs collection | Gathering documents | Chase missing items |
| 05 - In preparation | Drafting application on [portal URL](https://www.startupindia.gov.in/) | Internal review, note DPR/portal requirements |
| 06 - Submitted | Application filed on [portal](https://www.startupindia.gov.in/) | Begin follow-up |
| 07 - Under review | With implementing agency | Monitor + respond to queries |
| 08 - Approved | Sanction letter received | Invoice success fee |
| 09 - Disbursed | Money received by client | Close case, request testimonial |
| 10 - Rejected | Application declined | Analyze, re-apply or alternate scheme |

Note: The GENESIS Scheme is a central sector scheme that provides funding support to startups in Tier II and Tier III cities. The scheme aims to promote innovation and entrepreneurship in these cities by providing funding support to startups. The client must meet the eligibility criteria for the scheme, which includes being a startup as defined by the Government of India, having a turnover of less than INR 100 crore, and being working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products or processes. The client must also have a valid PAN, GST registration, and MSME registration. The application process involves submitting a proposal, followed by an internal review, and then filing the application on the Startup India portal. The implementing agency will review the application, and if approved, the client will receive the sanction letter and the money will be disbursed. If the application is rejected, the client can analyze the reasons for rejection and re-apply or explore alternative schemes.
