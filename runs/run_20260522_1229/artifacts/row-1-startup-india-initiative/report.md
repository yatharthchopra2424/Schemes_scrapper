# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The **Startup India Initiative** is a flagship program launched by the Government of India to foster innovation, support entrepreneurship, and build a robust startup ecosystem. Implemented by the **Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT)** under the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, the initiative provides recognition, financial support, tax benefits, regulatory ease, and access to funding and mentorship platforms. It operates on a **rolling basis** with no fixed deadline, accepting applications year-round via the portal: [https://www.startupindia.gov.in/](https://www.startupindia.gov.in/). The initiative has a total fund size of **INR 10,000 crore**, managed through the Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS), and has recognized **157,331 startups** as of the latest data.

### Objectives
- Reduce regulatory burden on startups to allow focus on core business and lower compliance costs  
- Create a single point of contact for the startup ecosystem to enable knowledge exchange and funding access  
- Promote awareness and adoption of Intellectual Property Rights (IPRs) and facilitate protection and commercialization  
- Provide an equal platform for startups in public procurement by relaxing prior experience/turnover criteria and Earnest Money Deposit (EMD) requirements  
- Enable faster exit provisions (winding up within 90 days under Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016)  
- Provide funding support for innovation-driven enterprises through Fund of Funds, Seed Fund Scheme, and Credit Guarantee Scheme  
- Catalyze entrepreneurship by providing credit to innovators across all sections of society  
- Promote investments in startups by mobilizing capital gains from sale of capital assets  

### Eligibility Matrix
An entity must meet the following criteria to be recognized as a startup under DPIIT:

| Criteria | Requirement | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Legal Structure** | Private Limited Company (Companies Act, 2013), Registered Partnership Firm (Partnership Act, 1932), Limited Liability Partnership (LLP Act, 2008), Multi-State Cooperative Society, or Cooperative Society under State/UT Act | Entity formed by splitting up or reconstruction of an existing business is **not eligible** |
| **Age Limit** | ≤10 years from incorporation/registration (≤20 years for Deep Tech startups) | Status expires upon completion of tenure or if turnover exceeds limits |
| **Turnover Limit** | ≤INR 100 crore in any financial year since incorporation (≤INR 300 crore for Deep Tech) | Turnover as defined under Companies Act, 2013 or applicable law |
| **Innovation & Scalability** | Working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/processes/services OR scalable business model with high potential for employment generation or wealth creation | Must not be investing in specified asset classes (immovable property, transport vehicles >INR 10 lakh, loans/advances, capital contribution to other entities) unless in ordinary course of business |
| **Original Entity** | Not formed by splitting up or reconstructing an existing business | Explicitly excluded |

> **Note**: DPIIT recognition does **not** automatically grant all benefits; it only makes startups **eligible to apply** for specific schemes (tax exemptions, seed fund, credit guarantee, etc.), each governed by its own rules and requiring separate application.

### Benefits & Financial Support
Startups recognized by DPIIT gain access to a wide range of benefits across tax, compliance, funding, and ecosystem support.

#### Tax Exemptions & Financial Incentives
| Benefit | Details | Eligibility/Conditions |
|--------|---------|------------------------|
| **Section 80-IAC** | 100% tax exemption on profits for **3 consecutive financial years** out of first 10 years | Must be DPIIT-recognized, Private Limited Company or LLP, incorporated on or after 1st April 2016; requires **Inter-Ministerial Board (IMB)** approval |
| **Section 56(2)(VIIB) (Angel Tax)** | Exemption on investments above fair market value; aggregate consideration exempt up to **INR 25 crore** | Must be DPIIT-recognized Private Limited Company; must not invest in specified assets (residential property, loans/advances, securities, etc.) for 7 years post-issue of shares at premium |
| **Capital Gains Tax Exemption** | Exemption on capital gains if invested in notified funds (under Section 54GB) | Startup must be incorporated after 1st April 2016; investment must be in eligible assets |
| **Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS)** | Credit guarantee up to **₹20 crore per borrower** for collateral-free debt funding | Startup must be DPIIT-recognized; not in default; eligibility certified by Member Institution (MI); applies via Jan Samarth Portal |
| **Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)** | Financial assistance for proof of concept, prototype development, product trials, market entry, commercialisation | Up to **INR 20 lakhs** per entity; managed through approved incubators |
| **Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS)** | Corpus of **INR 10,000 crore** managed by SIDBI; invests in SEBI-registered AIFs that fund startups | Indirect benefit; startups access via AIFs |
| **Government Loan Schemes** | Access to collateral-free debt via SIDBI, Mudra Yojana, etc. | Varies by scheme; often requires DPIIT recognition |

#### Non-Financial & Regulatory Benefits
| Benefit | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Self-Certification** | Under 6 labour laws and 3 environment laws; no inspections for 5 years unless credible complaint filed |
| **Labour Laws Covered** | Building and Other Construction Workers Act, Inter-State Migrant Workmen Act, Payment of Gratuity Act, Contract Labour Act, EPF & MP Act, ESI Act |
| **Environment Laws Covered** | Water (Prevention & Control) Act, Water Cess Amendment Act, Air (Prevention & Control) Act |
| **IPR Fast-Tracking** | 80% rebate on patent/trademark/design filing fees; government bears facilitator fees; startups pay only statutory fees |
| **Public Procurement Norms** | Exemption from prior experience/turnover criteria and Earnest Money Deposit (EMD) for manufacturing startups; must have own manufacturing facility in India |
| **Winding Up** | Can wind up within **90 days** under Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 if meeting specified criteria |
| **Ecosystem Access** | Access to BHASKAR (600K+ users), MAARG mentorship platform, National Startup Awards, States’ Startup Ranking, SCO Startup Forum, Investor Connect platform |
| **State/UT Incentives** | Additional benefits offered by individual state governments (e.g., grants, subsidies, infrastructure support) |

> **Warning**: Tax benefits under Section 80-IAC require separate IMB approval. Each incentive (tax exemption, seed fund, credit guarantee) has its own application process, eligibility, and governing guidelines. Recognition does not guarantee automatic access.

### Application Process
The process to obtain DPIIT recognition and access benefits involves the following steps:

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Register on Startup India Portal] --> B[Apply for DPIIT Recognition via NSWS]
    B --> C[Fill Startup Application Form]
    C --> D[Upload Required Documents]
    D --> E[Submit Application]
    E --> F[Await Approval (Typically 2 Working Days)]
    F --> G[Receive DPIIT Recognition Certificate]
    G --> H[Access Benefits via Startup India Portal]
    H --> I[Apply for Specific Schemes: Tax Exemption (80-IAC), SISFS, CGSS, etc.]
    I --> J[Submit Separate Applications for Each Benefit]
    J --> K[Await Approval from Respective Authorities]
```

#### Required Documents
1. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration  
2. PAN of the entity  
3. Memorandum of Association (Pvt. Ltd.) / LLP Deed  
4. Board Resolution (if any)  
5. Annual Accounts for last three financial years  
6. Income Tax Returns for last three financial years  
7. Pitch deck or business description  
8. Authorization letter from authorized signatory  
9. Details of funding received (if any)  

#### Key Portals
- **Startup India Portal**: [https://www.startupindia.gov.in/](https://www.startupindia.gov.in/) – Main hub for registration, benefits access, ecosystem resources  
- **National Single Window System (NSWS)**: [https://www.nsws.gov.in/](https://www.nsws.gov.in/) – For DPIIT recognition application (add "Registration as a Startup" to dashboard)  
- **Jan Samarth Portal**: [https://www.jansamarth.gov.in/](https://www.jansamarth.gov.in/) – For Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS) application  
- **BHASKAR Portal**: [https://www.startupindia.gov.in/bhaskar](https://www.startupindia.gov.in/bhaskar) – Knowledge access registry for networking and collaboration  
- **MAARG Portal**: Mentorship, Advisory, Assistance, Resilience and Growth platform for mentor-matchmaking  

> **Note**: Application status can be tracked on the Startup India portal dashboard. Recognition is typically issued within **2 working days** of submission.

### Key Caveats & Revocation Conditions
- Startup status expires when:  
  - Turnover exceeds **INR 100 crore** (non-Deep Tech) or **INR 300 crore** (Deep Tech)  
  - Completion of **10 years** (non-Deep Tech) or **20 years** (Deep Tech) from incorporation  
- Recognition can be **revoked** if:  
  - Obtained without uploading relevant documents  
  - Based on false information  
  - Startup invests in prohibited assets (residential property, land, loans/advances, capital contribution to other entities, shares/securities, motor vehicle >INR 10 lakh, jewellery held as stock-in-trade) within **7 years** of issuing shares at premium (for Angel Tax exemption)  
- DPIIT does not charge any fee for recognition or eligibility certificates  
- All benefits must be applied for separately; recognition is a gateway, not an entitlement
