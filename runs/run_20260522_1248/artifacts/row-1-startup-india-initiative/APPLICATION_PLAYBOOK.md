# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Entity is incorporated or registered in India as a private limited company, partnership firm, or limited liability partnership  
- [ ] Entity is within 10 years of incorporation/registration (up to 20 years for DeepTech startups)  
- [ ] Annual turnover does not exceed INR 100 crore (INR 300 crore for DeepTech startups)  
- [ ] Entity is working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/services or has a scalable business model with high potential for employment generation or wealth creation  
- [ ] Entity is not formed by splitting up or reconstructing an existing business  

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration  
- PAN of the entity  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last three financial years  
- GST registration certificate (if applicable)  
- Bank statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (for MSME classification)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Memorandum of Association for Pvt. Ltd. / LLP Deed  
- Board Resolution (if any)  
- Annual Accounts of the startup for the last three financial years  
- Pitch deck or business description  
- Authorization letter from authorized signatory  
- Details of funding received (if any)  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible scans or certified copies  
- File names must match the document type (e.g., "Certificate_of_Incorporation.pdf")  
- File size must not exceed 5 MB per document  
- Documents must be in English or accompanied by a certified translation  
- Signatures and stamps must be visible and valid  
- Financial statements must be audited or certified by a Chartered Accountant

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not required for DPIIT recognition under Startup India. However, a detailed pitch deck or business description highlighting innovation, scalability, and employment/wealth creation potential is mandatory. Verify with nodal officer if applying for linked schemes like SISFS or tax exemptions.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Register on the Startup India portal (startupindia.gov.in) to create a profile  
2. Access the National Single Window System (NSWS) at nsws.gov.in and add 'Registration as a Startup' to your dashboard  
3. Fill the application form with accurate entity details (name, CIN, incorporation date, address)  
4. Clearly describe the innovation, development, or improvement in products/services/processes  
5. Emphasize scalability and potential for employment generation or wealth creation  
6. Upload all required documents in the specified format and size limits  
7. Ensure the authorized signatory completes and signs the declaration section  
8. Double-check that turnover and age criteria are correctly reflected in the form  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All fields in the application form are complete and accurate  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents are attached and legible  
- [ ] Document file names are standardized and under 5 MB  
- [ ] Declarations are signed by the authorized signatory  
- [ ] Eligibility criteria (age, turnover, entity type, innovation, non-splitting) are verified  
- [ ] Application is ready for submission via NSWS  

---

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the National Single Window System (NSWS) at https://nsws.gov.in  
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing successful submission  
- Save the application reference number (ARN) for tracking  
- Set a follow-up reminder for 3 business days after submission  
- Monitor the application status on the Startup India portal dashboard

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Download and verify the DPIIT Recognition certificate from the Startup India portal  
- [ ] Confirm the recognition number and validity period (10 years from incorporation, or until turnover exceeds INR 100 crore)  
- [ ] Notify the client of approved benefits access: tax exemptions, self-certification, patent fast-tracking, procurement relaxations, etc.  
- [ ] Guide client to apply for specific benefits (e.g., Section 80-IAC tax exemption via IMB, Seed Fund Scheme via SISFS portal) using the recognition certificate  
- [ ] Track utilization of benefits and assist with compliance requirements (e.g., annual reporting for tax exemptions)  
- [ ] Follow up on success fee invoice upon client’s realization of scheme benefits
