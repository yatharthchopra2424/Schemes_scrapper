# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a woman entrepreneur (Indian citizen)
- [ ] Business is a proprietary concern owned and managed by a woman
- [ ] Business activity is setting up a food catering unit (food processing or catering sector)
- [ ] Applicant meets SBI's creditworthiness criteria
- [ ] Loan purpose is exclusively for setting up food catering unit (kitchen infrastructure, equipment, working capital)
- [ ] Maximum loan amount requested does not exceed ₹50,000
- [ ] Business is a micro-enterprise (implied by scheme targeting micro-enterprises)
- [ ] No collateral or third-party guarantee is required (scheme feature, not eligibility condition)

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Identity proof (Aadhaar card, PAN card, voter ID, or passport)
- Address proof (utility bill, rental agreement, or Aadhaar card)
- Passport-sized photographs
- Business plan detailing the food catering unit
- Proof of ownership or lease of business premises (if applicable)
- Bank account details

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Business plan detailing the food catering unit (explicitly required for this scheme)
- Proof of ownership or lease of business premises (if applicable) (explicitly required for this scheme)

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be clear, legible, and scanned in PDF or JPEG format
- File size should not exceed 5 MB per document
- Document names must match the applicant's name as per identity proof
- Self-attested copies are acceptable; notarization not required unless specified by branch
- Documents must be in English or Hindi; if in regional language, provide certified translation
- Photographs must be recent (within last 6 months) and passport-sized

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A detailed business plan is required (as per scheme evidence). While not explicitly called a DPR, it should include:  
- Executive summary  
- Business description (food catering unit model)  
- Market analysis  
- Operational plan (kitchen setup, equipment, staffing)  
- Financial projections (costs, revenue, break-even)  
- Loan utilization plan (how ₹50,000 will be used)  
- Repayment capacity assessment  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Use the official SBI loan application form for the Annapurna Scheme (available at branch or via YONO/Internet Banking)  
2. Fill all fields in block letters; avoid overwriting or correction fluid  
3. Ensure applicant name matches exactly across identity proof, address proof, and application form  
4. Specify loan purpose as: "Setting up food catering unit under Annapurna Scheme"  
5. Declare that funds will be used exclusively for food catering unit (kitchen infrastructure, equipment, working capital)  
6. Provide accurate bank account details for disbursement  
7. Disclose any existing loans or liabilities truthfully  
8. Sign and date the declaration section; ensure photographs are affixed where required  
9. Attach all required documents in the order specified in the checklist  
10. Verify portal-specific notes: If applying via YONO SBI app, navigate to 'Loans' > 'Government Schemes' > 'Annapurna Scheme' and follow digital prompts  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] Application form fully completed with no blank mandatory fields  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents attached  
- [ ] Identity and address proof are valid and not expired  
- [ ] Business plan clearly outlines food catering unit setup and loan utilization  
- [ ] Applicant signature matches identity proof  
- [ ] Declarations regarding loan purpose and women entrepreneurship are signed  
- [ ] No discrepancies in applicant details across documents  
- [ ] Loan amount requested ≤ ₹50,000  
- [ ] Business activity confirmed as food processing/catering

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Obtain acknowledgment receipt with application number and date/time stamp  
- If submitted online (YONO/Internet Banking), save screenshot of confirmation page with reference number  
- If submitted at branch, collect signed acknowledgment copy  
- Store reference number securely and set follow-up reminders for Day 25 and Day 30  
- Do not submit incomplete applications; ensure all documents are attached

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Collect and verify approval letter mentioning sanctioned amount, interest rate, and repayment terms  
- [ ] Confirm loan amount disbursed matches sanctioned amount (up to ₹50,000)  
- [ ] Ensure funds are used exclusively for food catering unit as per business plan  
- [ ] Monitor EMI repayment schedule (up to 36 months)  
- [ ] Obtain utilization certificate or proof of expenditure (e.g., invoices for kitchen equipment, infrastructure)  
- [ ] Track success fee invoice (if applicable per consultancy agreement)  
- [ ] Advise client on maintaining repayment discipline to avoid penalties  
- [ ] Guide client on availing top-up or refinance options after satisfactory repayment history (if offered by SBI)
