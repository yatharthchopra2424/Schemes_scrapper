# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping women entrepreneurs in the food catering sector access government-backed financial support. Many women like you are looking to start or expand their food catering units but face challenges in securing collateral-free funding. I’d like to understand your goals and see if the Annapurna Scheme by SBI could be a fit for your business.

### Discovery Questions
1. Are you an Indian woman citizen looking to start or expand a food catering unit?  
2. Is your business a proprietary concern owned and managed by you?  
3. Have you been in operation for less than 10 years, or are you in the planning stage?  
4. What is the primary use of funds you’re seeking—kitchen infrastructure, equipment, or working capital?  
5. Do you have a detailed business plan for your food catering unit?  
6. Do you have proof of ownership or lease for your business premises (if applicable)?  
7. Are you comfortable applying through an SBI branch, YONO SBI app, or internet banking?  
8. Are you aware that the loan must be used exclusively for setting up a food catering unit?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant is a woman entrepreneur  
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian citizen  
- [ ] Business is a proprietary concern owned and managed by a woman  
- [ ] Business is in the food processing or catering sector  
- [ ] Applicant meets SBI’s creditworthiness criteria  
- [ ] Loan amount required is up to ₹50,000  
- [ ] Funds will be used exclusively for setting up a food catering unit  
- [ ] Applicant has identity proof, address proof, photographs, business plan, premises proof (if applicable), and bank account details  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it looks like you may be eligible for the Annapurna Scheme by SBI, which offers collateral-free loans up to ₹50,000 specifically for women entrepreneurs setting up food catering units. Let me share how this scheme can support your goals.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Women entrepreneurs in the food catering sector often struggle to access institutional credit due to lack of collateral or credit history, which limits their ability to set up or expand kitchen infrastructure, purchase equipment, or manage working capital.

**Scheme intro:**
The Annapurna Scheme, offered by the State Bank of India (SBI), is a loan program designed for women entrepreneurs to set up food catering units. It provides collateral-free loans up to a maximum of ₹50,000, with interest rates linked to the bank's base rate or MCLR and a concessional spread for women borrowers. Repayment is structured over up to 36 months in equated monthly instalments (EMIs).

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are a woman entrepreneur seeking to start or expand a food catering unit  
- Your business is a proprietary concern owned and managed by you  
- You are an Indian citizen and meet SBI’s creditworthiness criteria  
- The loan amount you need falls within the ₹50,000 cap
