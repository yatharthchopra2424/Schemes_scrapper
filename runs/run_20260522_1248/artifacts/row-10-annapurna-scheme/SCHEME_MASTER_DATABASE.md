### Annapurna Scheme
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Loan
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** State Bank of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://www.sbi.co.in/web/agri-rural/agriculture-banking/annapurna-scheme
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Proprietary concern owned and managed by a woman
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Food processing or catering
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Must be Indian citizen; meet bank's creditworthiness criteria; business must be a food catering unit

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: ₹50,000 maximum loan amount
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit to applicant's account
- Duration of benefit: Up to 36 months repayment period

#### Documents Required
1. Identity proof (Aadhaar card, PAN card, voter ID, or passport)
2. Address proof (utility bill, rental agreement, or Aadhaar card)
3. Passport-sized photographs
4. Business plan detailing the food catering unit
5. Proof of ownership or lease of business premises (if applicable)
6. Bank account details

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Visit the nearest SBI branch or apply through the YONO SBI app or internet banking portal.
2. Fill out the loan application form for the Annapurna Scheme.
3. Submit required documents including identity proof, address proof, business plan, and photographs.
4. The bank will verify the documents and assess the creditworthiness of the applicant.
5. Upon approval, the loan amount will be disbursed to the applicant's account.
6. The applicant can then utilise the funds for setting up the food catering unit as per the business plan.
