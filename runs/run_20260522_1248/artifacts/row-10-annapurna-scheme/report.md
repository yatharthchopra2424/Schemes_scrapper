# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The **Annapurna Scheme** is a loan scheme specifically designed for **women entrepreneurs** under the **Women Entrepreneurship** category, implemented pan-India by the **State Bank of India (SBI)**. It operates on a **rolling basis** with no fixed deadline, offering financial support to set up food catering units. The scheme is identified by **Scheme ID: row-10** and falls under the loan type category.

### Objectives
The scheme aims to:
- Promote entrepreneurship among women by providing financial support for food catering units.
- Enable women to start or expand micro-enterprises in the food service sector.
- Support self-employment and income generation for women.
- Facilitate access to institutional credit without requiring collateral.
- Contribute to women's economic empowerment and financial inclusion.

### Eligibility Matrix
| Criteria | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Target Beneficiaries** | Women entrepreneurs |
| **Business Type** | Proprietary concern owned and managed by a woman |
| **Sector** | Food processing or catering (specifically food catering units) |
| **Citizenship** | Indian citizen |
| **Creditworthiness** | Must meet SBI's creditworthiness criteria |
| **Geographic Scope** | Pan-India |

> **Note**: The scheme is exclusively for women entrepreneurs setting up food catering units. No other business types or genders are eligible.

### Benefits & Financial Support
| Feature | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Maximum Loan Amount** | ₹50,000 per entity |
| **Collateral Requirement** | No collateral or third-party guarantee required |
| **Interest Rate** | Linked to SBI's base rate or MCLR, with a concessional spread for women borrowers |
| **Repayment Tenure** | Up to 36 months in equated monthly instalments (EMIs) |
| **Usage of Funds** | Setting up kitchen infrastructure, purchasing equipment, meeting working capital requirements |
| **Key Benefits** | Collateral-free access, flexible repayment, competitive interest rates, supports financial independence and socio-economic upliftment |

> **Caveats**:
> - Loan is restricted to women entrepreneurs only.
> - Funds must be used exclusively for setting up food catering units.
> - Maximum loan amount is capped at ₹50,000.
> - Repayment must be made as per the agreed EMI schedule.
> - The scheme may be subject to periodic review and modification by SBI.

### Required Documents
1. Identity proof (Aadhaar card, PAN card, voter ID, or passport)
2. Address proof (utility bill, rental agreement, or Aadhaar card)
3. Passport-sized photographs
4. Business plan detailing the food catering unit
5. Proof of ownership or lease of business premises (if applicable)
6. Bank account details

### Application Process
The application process involves the following steps:
1. Visit the nearest SBI branch or apply through the YONO SBI app or internet banking portal.
2. Fill out the loan application form for the Annapurna Scheme.
3. Submit required documents including identity proof, address proof, business plan, and photographs.
4. The bank verifies documents and assesses creditworthiness.
5. Upon approval, the loan amount is disbursed to the applicant's account.
6. The applicant utilises the funds for setting up the food catering unit as per the business plan.

**Application Portal**: [https://www.sbi.co.in/web/agri-rural/agriculture-banking/annapurna-scheme](https://www.sbi.co.in/web/agri-rural/agriculture-banking/annapurna-scheme)

> **Warning**: Although the official portal URL is provided, crawling attempts returned a 404 error. Consultants should verify the current portal status directly with SBI branches or official channels before guiding clients.
