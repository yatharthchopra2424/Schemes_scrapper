# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian national  
- [ ] Age between 15 and 45 years (inclusive)  
- [ ] Minimum educational qualification met as per the specific job role (ranging from 5th pass to 12th pass or equivalent)  
- [ ] Belongs to any category (SC/ST/OBC/Minorities/PwD/General) – no restriction  
- [ ] Meets eligibility criteria defined for the specific trade or course under PM-SETU  
- [ ] Target beneficiary group: youth, unemployed, school dropout, woman, SC/ST, minority, or person with disability (at least one applicable)  
- [ ] Aadhaar card available for identity verification  
- [ ] Date of birth proof available (e.g., birth certificate, school leaving certificate, Aadhaar)  
- [ ] Educational certificates (mark sheets and passing certificates) available as per course requirement  
- [ ] Caste certificate available (if applying under SC/ST/OBC category)  
- [ ] Disability certificate available (if applying as a person with disability)  
- [ ] Passport-sized photographs available (recent, white background)  
- [ ] Bank account details available (if stipend is expected and applicable)  
- [ ] Domicile certificate available (if required by the respective State Skill Development Mission)  

> **Note**: Eligibility is course-specific. Verify the exact educational and other requirements for the desired trade on the [PM-SETU portal](https://dgt.gov.in/en/scheme-guidelines-of-pmsetu) or with the training center.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Aadhaar Card  
- PAN Card  
- Income Tax Return (ITR) – if applicable (for self/family income verification, if stipend means-tested)  
- GST Certificate – if applicable (only if applicant is self-employed or running a business)  
- Bank Statements (last 3 months)  
- Udyam Registration Certificate – if applicable (for existing micro-enterprises)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Educational Certificates (Mark sheets and passing certificates) – as per the specific job role requirement (5th to 12th pass or equivalent)  
- Date of Birth Proof (Birth certificate, school leaving certificate, or Aadhaar)  
- Caste Certificate (if applicable – for SC/ST/OBC beneficiaries)  
- Disability Certificate (if applicable – for PwD beneficiaries)  
- Passport-sized photographs (recent, white background, 2–4 copies)  
- Bank account details (for stipend disbursement, if applicable)  
- Domicile certificate (if required by the State Skill Development Mission)  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are clear, legible scans or photographs (minimum 300 DPI)  
- [ ] File size per document: under 2 MB (preferably PDF format)  
- [ ] Name on all documents matches exactly as per Aadhaar (or legally updated name with gazette/notarized proof)  
- [ ] Photographs: recent (within last 6 months), white background, no spectacles or headwear (unless religious, with declaration)  
- [ ] Certificates are self-attested; gazetted officer attestation not required unless specified by training center  
- [ ] All documents are in English or Hindi; if in regional language, attach notarized translation  
- [ ] Bank account details include account holder name, account number, IFSC code, and bank name (matching Aadhaar/PAN)  
- [ ] Caste and disability certificates are valid and issued by competent authority

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not required. PM-SETU is a beneficiary-oriented skill training scheme; no Detailed Project Report (DPR) is needed for individual applications. However, if applying as a training partner or implementing agency, verify DPR requirements with the nodal officer (DGT/MSDE).  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application via the **State Skill Development Mission portal** or visit the nearest ITI/PMKK/accredited training center (as PM-SETU applications are processed locally).  
2. Fill in personal details exactly as per Aadhaar (name, date of birth, gender, address).  
3. Enter educational qualifications accurately – specify board, year of passing, and marks/grade as per certificates.  
4. Select the desired skill course from the NSQF-aligned list available at the training center.  
5. Upload all scheme-specific documents in the prescribed format (PDF/JPEG, under 2 MB).  
6. Declare eligibility for stipend (if applicable) based on category (woman, SC/ST, PwD) and project norms.  
7. Provide correct bank account details (name, number, IFSC) for stipend disbursement – any mismatch will delay payment.  
8. Review all entries before final submission; no edits are allowed after submission in most portals.  
9. Keep a screenshot of the submitted application and note the application/reference number.  
10. Attend counseling and pre-assessment if called by the training center.  

> **Portal Note**: The central guideline portal is [https://dgt.gov.in/en/scheme-guidelines-of-pmsetu](https://dgt.gov.in/en/scheme-guidelines-of-pmsetu), but actual application is through State Skill Development Mission portals or training centers.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal details match Aadhaar (name, DOB, address)  
- [ ] Educational qualifications meet the minimum requirement for the selected course  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are uploaded and legible  
- [ ] Bank details are correct and complete (for stipend, if applicable)  
- [ ] Caste/disability certificates uploaded if claiming benefit under those categories  
- [ ] Photographs meet specifications (recent, white background)  
- [ ] Application form is fully filled with no blank mandatory fields  
- [ ] Declarations and consent clauses are checked/accepted  
- [ ] Screenshot of submission saved and reference number recorded

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application either online via the State Skill Development Mission portal or in person at the ITI/PMKK/accredited training center.  
- Take a **screenshot of the confirmation page** showing application number, date, and time of submission.  
- Save the application/reference number in the case file and set a follow-up reminder for 7 days post-submission.  
- If submitted physically, obtain an acknowledgment receipt with seal and signature from the training center.  
- Store all documents (application copy, acknowledgment) in both digital and physical case folders.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Collect and verify the approval/enrollment letter from the training center (includes course name, batch start date, duration)  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned benefits: 100% training cost coverage and stipend eligibility (if applicable)  
- [ ] Monitor attendance – ensure beneficiary maintains minimum attendance as per norms (usually 80%) to continue in the program  
- [ ] Track progress: mid-term assessment, internal evaluations, and final exam schedule  
- [ ] Upon completion: collect certification (NCVT/SSC) and placement support documentation  
- [ ] If stipend is applicable: verify monthly disbursement into the beneficiary’s bank account  
- [ ] Assist in placement facilitation: resume building, interview preparation, and job fair participation (as offered by the center)  
- [ ] Post-placement: support in tracking employment retention for 3–6 months (as per scheme objective of 70% placement linkage)  
- [ ] Prepare utilization certificate (if required by implementing agency) and success fee invoice for consultancy services  
- [ ] Maintain records for audit: approval letter, attendance sheets, assessment marks, certificate, placement offer letter  

> **Scheme Objective Reference**: PM-SETU aims for placement linkage for at least 70% of certified trainees – monitor and support accordingly.  
> **Portal Reference**: [https://dgt.gov.in/en/scheme-guidelines-of-pmsetu](https://dgt.gov.in/en/scheme-guidelines-of-pmsetu)
