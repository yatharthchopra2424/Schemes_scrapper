# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Startup is recognized by DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade)
- [ ] Startup is incorporated not more than 2 years ago at the time of application
- [ ] Startup has a business idea to develop a product or service with market fit, viable commercialization, and scope of scaling
- [ ] Startup uses technology in its core product/service, business model, distribution model, or methodology to solve the targeted problem
- [ ] Startup has not received more than Rs. 10 lakh of monetary support under any other Central or State Government scheme (excluding prize money, subsidized working space, founder monthly allowance, access to labs, or prototyping facilities)
- [ ] Shareholding by Indian promoters in the startup is at least 51% at the time of application (as per Companies Act, 2013 and SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018)
- [ ] Startup applicant can avail seed support in the form of grant and debt/convertible debentures each once as per scheme guidelines
- [ ] Startup operates in a sector eligible for preference (social impact, waste management, water management, financial inclusion, education, agriculture, food processing, biotechnology, healthcare, energy, mobility, defence, space, railways, oil and gas, textiles, etc.) – though scheme is sector-agnostic, preference is given to these sectors

> **Sources**:  
> - Eligibility criteria from KEY FACTS and crawled pages (https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/about, https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/faq)  
> - Guidelines PDF (pages 2–3, 10–11)  
> - Gazette notification (points 1–7 under Eligibility Criteria for Startups)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration  
- PAN of the entity  
- ITR (Income Tax Returns) for last 2 financial years (if applicable)  
- GST registration certificate  
- Bank statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (MSME) certificate  
- DPIIT recognition certificate (mandatory for scheme eligibility)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Pitch deck or business description  
- Authorization letter from authorized signatory  
- Details of funding received (if any)  
- Board Resolution / Authorization Letter / POA (as per sample document)  
- Self-declaration (to be checked during application submission on portal)

> **Sources**:  
> - Required Documents from KEY FACTS  
> - Crawled FAQ: "Download the sample document for Board Resolution/ Authorization Letter/ POA" (slide 12 of seedfund-guide-for-startups-02-08-2021.pdf)  
> - Guidelines PDF (page 9, section 7.2: "The applicant may be asked to submit details on team profile, problem statement, product/service overview, business model, customer profile, market size, quantum of funds needed, projected utilization plan for funds, etc.")

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible and in PDF or JPEG/PNG format  
- [ ] File size does not exceed 5 MB per document  
- [ ] Name on documents matches the startup’s registered name exactly  
- [ ] PAN and GST numbers are valid and match MCA/Udyam records  
- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation is issued by ROC and not older than 2 years  
- [ ] DPIIT recognition certificate is valid and verifiable on startupindia.gov.in  
- [ ] Authorization letter is on company letterhead, signed, and stamped  
- [ ] Pitch deck includes problem statement, solution, market size, business model, team, financials, and fund utilization plan  
- [ ] All documents are in English or Hindi; if in another language, certified translation is attached  
- [ ] Bank statements show active account in the startup’s name

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A formal Detailed Project Report (DPR) is **not explicitly required** for the SISFS application. However, applicants must submit a **Project Report / Fund Utilization Plan** as part of the application form, covering:  
- Problem statement  
- Product/service overview  
- Business model  
- Customer profile and market size  
- Quantum of funds needed  
- Projected utilization plan for funds (milestone-based for grant; repayment terms for debt)  
- Team profile  
- Timeline for milestones (e.g., prototype development, product testing, market readiness)  

> **Sources**:  
> - Guidelines PDF (page 9, section 7.2: "The applicant may be asked to submit details on team profile, problem statement, product/service overview, business model, customer profile, market size, quantum of funds needed, projected utilization plan for funds, etc.")  
> - Crawled FAQ: "Fund Utilization Plan" as an evaluation criterion (page 10 of guidelines)  
> - Application Guide (slides 14–16): "Fill necessary details as per the fields"

If the incubator requests additional detail post-shortlisting, verify with the nodal officer or ISMC whether a full DPR is needed.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Login using DPIIT-recognized startup credentials** – Ensure you are logged into the Startup India portal before accessing the SISFS application.  
2. **Navigate to https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/** and click “Apply Now” – Do not use third-party agents; the scheme does not authorize consultants.  
3. **Entity Details are auto-populated** – Verify that your DPIIT-recognized startup details (name, CIN, incorporation date) are correctly pulled from Startup India portal; do not edit if auto-filled.  
4. **Fill all mandatory fields** – Including team profile, problem statement, product/service overview, business model, customer profile, market size, quantum of funds needed, and projected utilization plan.  
5. **Select up to 3 incubator preferences** – In order of preference; funding will be disbursed by the highest-preferred incubator that selects you.  
6. **Choose funding instrument** – Select Grant, Debt, or Convertible Debenture under “Funding Details” to view relevant incubator list.  
7. **Upload required documents** – Pitch deck, authorization letter, board resolution, funding details (if any). Ensure files are clearly named (e.g., StartupName_PitchDeck.pdf).  
8. **Check self-declaration box** – Confirm compliance with eligibility criteria before final submission.  
9. **Submit and save acknowledgment** – Click “Submit” and save the application reference number and confirmation email.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and save the **approval letter** from the selected incubator (via email or portal notification)  
- [ ] Verify the **sanctioned amount** and **funding instrument** (grant/debt) match the application and agreement  
- [ ] Sign the **legal agreement** with the incubator before first disbursement (mandatory as per guidelines)  
- [ ] Ensure funds are disbursed to the **company’s bank account** (not personal account)  
- [ ] For grants: Submit **interim progress update and utilization certificate** to trigger subsequent installments (first installment released within 60 days of application receipt)  
- [ ] For debt/convertible debentures: Adhere to agreed repayment schedule, interest rate (≤ prevailing repo rate), tenure (≤ 60 months), and moratorium (≤ 12 months)  
- [ ] Maintain records for **audited utilization certificate** at project end  
- [ ] Track and report **indicators of successful implementation** to incubator:  
  - Progress of proof of concept, prototype development, product development, field trials, market launch  
  - Quantum of loan, angel, or VC funding raised post-seed fund  
  - Jobs created by startup  
  - Turnover of startup  
- [ ] Submit **final report and audited utilization certificate** to incubator and DPIIT via portal at project completion  
- [ ] Ensure no use of seed fund for facility creation (strictly prohibited)  
- [ ] Do not charge any fees to the incubator for selection, disbursement, or monitoring (as per scheme)  

> **Sources**:  
> - Guidelines PDF (page 12: "The incubator shall execute a legal agreement with the selected startups before the release of the first installment")  
> - Guidelines PDF (page 12: "For grants, the first installment to any selected startup shall be released not more than 60 days from receipt of application")  
> - Guidelines PDF (page 12: "Startup shall submit final report and audited utilisation certificate at the end of the project duration")
