# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialize in helping early-stage, DPIIT-recognized startups access non-dilutive funding through government schemes like the Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS). Given your focus on innovation and scaling, I’d like to understand if this scheme aligns with your current stage and funding needs—without any obligation on your part.

### Discovery Questions
1. When was your startup incorporated, and do you have your DPIIT recognition certificate handy?
2. What stage is your product/service at—are you validating a proof of concept, developing a prototype, or ready for market entry and scaling?
3. Is technology a core part of your product, service, or business model?
4. Have you received more than ₹10 lakh in monetary support from any Central or State Government scheme (excluding prize money, subsidized space, or founder allowances)?
5. What is the shareholding percentage of Indian promoters in your startup?
6. Are you looking for grant funding (up to ₹20 lakhs) for validation/prototyping, or investment (up to ₹50 lakhs) via convertible debentures/debt for market scaling?
7. Which sectors does your innovation primarily address—such as social impact, healthcare, agriculture, biotechnology, or others listed in the scheme’s preference list?
8. Do you have a pitch deck, business description, and authorization letter ready for application?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Startup is DPIIT-recognized  
- [ ] Incorporated not more than 2 years ago at time of application  
- [ ] Has a business idea with market fit, viable commercialization, and scope of scaling  
- [ ] Uses technology in core product/service/business model/distribution model  
- [ ] Operating in preferred sectors (social impact, waste/water management, financial inclusion, education, agriculture, food processing, biotech, healthcare, energy, mobility, defence, space, railways, oil & gas, textiles, etc.)  
- [ ] Has not received >₹10 lakh from other Central/State Govt schemes (exclusions apply)  
- [ ] Indian promoter shareholding ≥51%  
- [ ] Has not previously received seed support under SISFS (grant or debt)  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it looks like you may be a strong fit for the **Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)**, which offers up to ₹20 lakhs as grant for proof of concept/prototype trials and up to ₹50 lakhs as investment for market entry and scaling—disbursed through DPIIT-approved incubators. Shall I walk you through how it works?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Early-stage startups often struggle to access capital during the proof of concept and prototype development phase, as angel investors and VCs typically wait for validation, and banks require asset-backed collateral. This funding gap causes many viable ideas to stall before they can scale.

**Scheme intro:**
The **Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)**, administered by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India, provides financial assistance of up to **₹20 lakhs as grant** for validation of proof of concept, prototype development, or product trials, and up to **₹50 lakhs as investment** for market entry, commercialization, or scaling through convertible debentures or debt/debt-linked instruments. The grant is disbursed in milestone-based installments, and there are no fees charged to startups by incubators for selection, disbursement, or monitoring.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are a DPIIT-recognized startup incorporated within the last 2 years  
- Your venture uses technology in its core offering and has a scalable business model  
- You have not received more than ₹10 lakh from other government monetary schemes  
- Indian promoters hold at least 51% shareholding in your company
