# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS) is a flagship initiative by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India, designed to address the critical capital inadequacy faced by early-stage startups in the seed and 'Proof of Concept' development stage. Launched on 19th April 2021 by Hon’ble Shri Piyush Goyal, the scheme operates with a total fund size of **Rs. 945 Crore** and aims to support an estimated **3,600 entrepreneurs** through **300 incubators** over a four-year period (2021-2025). The scheme is **Pan-India**, **sector-agnostic**, and provides financial assistance through **eligible incubators** across the country. Applications are accepted **year-round** with no application fee, and startups can apply independently via the Startup India Seed Fund Portal: **https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/**. The deadline for incubators to complete 100% selection is **30 June 2026**.

### Objectives
The scheme’s objectives are explicitly defined in the guidelines and supporting evidence:
- Provide financial assistance to startups for proof of concept, prototype development, product trials, market entry, and commercialization.
- Enable startups to graduate to a level where they can raise investments from angel investors, venture capitalists, or seek loans from commercial banks or financial institutions.
- Address capital inadequacy in the seed and 'Proof of Concept' development stage of the Indian startup ecosystem.
- Have a multiplier effect in validation of business ideas, leading to employment generation.
- Support sector-agnostic startups with preference for innovative solutions in sectors such as social impact, waste management, water management, financial inclusion, education, agriculture, food processing, biotechnology, healthcare, energy, mobility, defence, space, railways, oil and gas, textiles, etc.

Eligibility criteria are strictly defined for both startups and incubators. All criteria must be met on the date of application submission, with no exemptions.

> **> WARNING: Critical Eligibility Caveats**  
> - Seed fund shall **strictly not be used** by startups for creation of any facilities.  
> - A startup will **not receive seed support more than once each** for grant and debt/convertible debenture.  
> - Incubator must **not** disburse seed fund using third-party private funding.  
> - Shareholding by Indian promoters must be **≥51%** at time of application.  
> - Startup must **not** have received >Rs. 10 lakh monetary support from other Central/State Govt schemes (exclusions apply).  
> - Applications are **final** once submitted; incubator preference order **cannot be changed** post-submission.  
> - Rejected applicants may reapply **only after 6 weeks** from rejection date.

#### Non-Financial Support Provided by Incubators
Incubators provide critical non-financial support at **no cost** to the startup:
- Physical infrastructure for regular functioning
- Support for testing and validating ideas
- Mentoring for prototype/product development or commercialization
- Capacity building in finance, human resources, legal compliances, and other functions
- Networking with investors
- Opportunities for showcasing at national and international events
- No fees charged by incubator or staff for selection, disbursement, incubation, or monitoring

> **> KEY TAKEAWAY: Total Potential Support**  
> A single eligible startup can access **up to Rs. 70 Lakhs** in total seed support: **Rs. 20 Lakhs (grant)** + **Rs. 50 Lakhs (investment)**.  
> The scheme’s **Management Fee** for incubators is **5% of the Seed Fund grant** (e.g., Rs. 1 Crore grant → Rs. 1.05 Crore total assistance), to be used **only** for administrative expenditure, selection, due diligence, and monitoring — **not** for facility creation.

### Application Process
The application process is entirely online, free of cost, and requires no physical document submission. Startups apply directly via the Startup India Seed Fund Portal.

#### Process Details & Timelines
- **Application Fee**: **Zero** – no cost to apply.
- **Document Upload**: Required documents include:
  1. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
  2. PAN of the entity
  3. Pitch deck or business description
  4. Authorization letter from authorized signatory
  5. Details of funding received (if any)
  6. Board Resolution/ Authorization Letter/ POA (as per sample document)
- **Evaluation Timeline**: Incubators must evaluate and select startups for seed fund **within 45 days** of application receipt.
- **Preference Handling**: Startups can apply to **up to 3 incubators** in order of preference. Funding is disbursed by the **highest-preference incubator that selects the startup** (e.g., if Preference 1 and 2 both select, Preference 1 funds; if 1 rejects and 2 selects, 2 funds).
- **Preference Lock**: Incubator preference order **cannot be changed** after submission.
- **Reapplication**: Rejected applicants may reapply **after 6 weeks** to incorporate feedback.
- **Communication**: All communication, updates, and grievance redressal occur via the portal; email contacts: **dipp-startups@nic.in** (general) and **sisfs@investindia.org.in** (queries).
- **Grievance Mechanism**: A grievance cell at DPIIT addresses delayed evaluations, disbursements, etc. – accessible via the portal’s contact form.

#### Post-Selection Process
Upon selection by an incubator:
1. **Legal Agreement**: Startup **must** sign a legal agreement with the incubator before first installment release, detailing terms, conditions, and milestones.
2. **Fund Disbursement**:
   - **Grant**: First installment within 60 days of application; subsequent tranches on milestone achievement (interim progress update + utilization certificate).
   - **Debt/Debenture**: Similar timeline; interest ≤ prevailing repo rate; tenure ≤ 60 months; moratorium ≤ 12 months.
3. **Reporting**: Startup must submit progress updates **at least once every 15 days** (virtual/physical) and share on scheme dashboard **monthly**. Final report + audited utilization certificate required at project end.
4. **Monitoring**: Incubators report progress real-time to Startup India portal; EAC reviews quarterly.
5. **Exit**: Startup may withdraw post-selection by submitting a withdrawal letter; if funds received, must formally cancel via portal after acknowledgment.

## Consultant's Field Guide to Generated Files

### 1. SCHEME_MASTER_DATABASE.md
**Real-time Usage:** Keep this open in a background tab during all client calls. When a client asks "What is the turnover limit?" or "Who administers this?", CTRL+F in this document to give an immediate, authoritative answer without checking the portal.  
*Example Use Case:* During a discovery call, a founder asks, "Can I apply if my startup is 2 years and 1 month old?" Your startup is ineligible based on age."
