# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Entity type: Startup (DPIIT-recognized preferred) or government agency  
- [ ] Entity operates within India (Pan-India scope)  
- [ ] Entity is engaged in linguistic technology, AI, NLP, or multilingual digital solutions  
- [ ] Entity demonstrates intent to collaborate with NeGD or MeitY on Bhashini initiative  
- [ ] Entity has valid legal registration (Private Limited, LLP, Section 8, or government body)  
- [ ] Entity has PAN and GST registration (if applicable)  
- [ ] Entity has Udyam Registration (for MSMEs) or DPIIT recognition (for startups)  
- [ ] Entity is not disqualified under any law (e.g., insolvent, criminal conviction)  
- [ ] Entity commits to open collaboration, data sharing, and open-source principles where applicable  
- [ ] Entity agrees to comply with MeitY/NEGD guidelines on linguistic resource contribution  

> **Note**: Specific eligibility criteria (turnover, employee count, age of business) are not disclosed in the evidence. The above checklist is derived from the scheme’s description of collaborating with startups and government agencies under the Digital India Bhashini initiative.  
> **Sources**:  
> - Scheme Key Facts: Eligibility – “The initiative collaborates with startups and government agencies”  
> - Crawled Page: https://digitalindia.gov.in/initiative/digital-india-bhashini-2 – “Digital India Bhashini’s canvas is broad, encompassing collaborations with startups and government agencies alike.”  
> - MeitY Organisations: https://digitalindia.gov.in/meity-organisations/ – National e-Governance Division (NeGD) as implementing agency

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration Certificate  
- PAN Card of the entity  
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- Udyam Registration (for MSMEs) or DPIIT Recognition Certificate (for startups)  
- Bank Statement (last 6 months)  
- ITR of the entity (last 2 years)  
- Authorized Signatory Letter + Board Resolution  
- Cancelled Cheque / Bank Verification Letter  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Project Proposal outlining linguistic resource contribution (e.g., text/audio corpora, ASR/TTS models, translation datasets)  
- Technical Architecture Diagram of the proposed solution/tool  
- Details of linguistic datasets (language pairs, volume, source, licensing)  
- IPR and Data Sharing Agreement draft (indicating willingness to contribute to open linguistic repositories)  
- Proof of prior work in Indian language technology (papers, APIs, GitHub repos, pilot projects)  
- Letter of Intent (LoI) to collaborate with NeGD on Bhashini (if available)  
- Team CVs highlighting expertise in NLP, AI, linguistics, or speech technology  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document)  
- Names must match exactly as per PAN and Certificate of Incorporation  
- Affidavits and declarations must be notarized where required  
- Documents in regional languages must be accompanied by certified English/Hindi translation  
- Bank statements must show entity name and be stamped/signing by the bank  
- ITR must be filed and verified via income tax portal  
- Udyam/DPIIT certificates must be valid and verifiable on respective portals

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not explicitly required as per evidence. However, a detailed **Project Proposal** is essential (see Scheme-Specific Documents).  
**Action**: Verify with NeGD nodal officer whether a formal DPR is expected. If not, the Project Proposal shall serve as the primary technical submission.  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the portal via https://digitalindia.gov.in (no dedicated application portal mentioned; engagement is via NeGD outreach)  
2. Since no online application form is described, initiate contact through official MeitY/NeGD channels  
3. Clearly state intent to collaborate with Digital India Bhashini in all communications  
4. Highlight alignment with objectives: seamless multilingual communication, digital inclusivity, technological innovation  
5. Specify exact linguistic resources being contributed (e.g., 100 hrs of Kannada speech data, English-Hindi parallel corpus)  
6. Clarify data usage terms: willingness to contribute to open repositories under licenses like CC-BY-SA or ODC-BY  
7. Mention compliance with MeitY guidelines on data security, privacy, and open collaboration  
8. Attach all scheme-specific and universal documents as per checklist  

> **Note**: No specific application form or portal URL for Bhashini applications is provided in evidence. Engagement is expected through NeGD as the implementing agency.  
> **Source**: Scheme Key Facts – Application Process: “No step-by-step application procedure is described in the evidence”  

### Internal Review Gate

### Submission Protocol
