# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialize in helping startups and government agencies leverage Digital India initiatives like Bhashini to break language barriers and enhance digital service access. I’d love to understand your current challenges with multilingual communication and see if Bhashini’s technology-driven linguistic inclusivity could support your mission.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your organization’s incorporation or establishment date? (To assess if you qualify as a startup or government agency)
2. Are you currently developing or deploying any digital services that serve citizens across multiple Indian languages?
3. Do you face challenges in ensuring your digital platforms are accessible to linguistically diverse users, especially in rural or regional areas?
4. Are you collaborating with any technology partners to address language localization or translation needs?
5. Have you explored AI-driven language tools to improve citizen engagement or service delivery?
6. Are you looking to scale your digital offerings while maintaining linguistic inclusivity and compliance with Digital India goals?
7. Do you have any existing initiatives focused on empowering citizens through technology in Indian languages?
8. Are you interested in contributing to or benefiting from a national ecosystem that promotes linguistic pluralism through innovation?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Organization is a startup (DPIIT-recognized preferred) or a government agency
- [ ] Operates within India (Pan-India scope applicable)
- [ ] Engaged in digital service delivery or technology development
- [ ] Focus on enabling access to digital services across Indian languages
- [ ] Interested in collaborating on linguistic diversity and innovation initiatives
- [ ] Aligns with goals of breaking language barriers and enhancing digital inclusivity
- [ ] Seeks to empower citizens through multilingual technological solutions
- [ ] Willing to participate in a collaborative ecosystem with MeitY/NeGD

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like your work in [mention specific area, e.g., citizen-facing digital services or language access] aligns closely with the mission of **Digital India Bhashini** — which enables seamless multilingual communication and empowers citizens by breaking language barriers through technological innovation. Let me share how this initiative could support your goals.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many startups and government agencies struggle to make their digital services accessible across India’s linguistic diversity, limiting citizen engagement and inclusivity. Language barriers prevent effective delivery of essential services, especially in regional and rural areas, hindering the goal of a truly empowered digital society.

**Scheme intro:**
**Digital India Bhashini**, implemented by the National e-Governance Division (NeGD) under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Government of India, is a technology-driven initiative that facilitates seamless communication between various Indian languages and English. It promotes digital inclusivity, supports linguistic diversity through innovation, and empowers citizens by ensuring language is no longer a barrier in accessing digital services.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are a **startup or government agency** — core collaborators explicitly mentioned in the initiative
- You aim to **enhance access to digital services across Indian languages** — directly aligned with Bhashini’s benefits
- You seek to **leverage technology for linguistic inclusivity** — resonates with the mission to celebrate pluralism through innovation
- You operate **within India** — fitting the Pan-India geographic scope of the initiative
