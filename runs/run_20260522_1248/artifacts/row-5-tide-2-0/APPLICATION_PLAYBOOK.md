# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] The applicant is one of the 51 designated TIDE 2.0 Incubation Centres (from institutions of higher learning and R&D organizations having incubation facilities and established linkages with large companies, industry bodies, industry clusters)
- [ ] The applicant is an institution of higher learning or an R&D organization
- [ ] The applicant has established incubation facilities
- [ ] The applicant has established linkages with large companies, industry bodies, or industry clusters to provide mentorship and possible infusion of innovative businesses into larger business operations
- [ ] The applicant is proposing to support ICT startups using emerging technologies (IoT, AI, Blockchain, Robotics, etc.) in seven pre-identified areas of societal relevance: Healthcare, Education, Agriculture, Financial inclusion (including digital payments), Infrastructure and transportation, Environment and clean tech, Clean energy solutions
- [ ] The applicant is seeking support for Entrepreneurs-in-residence (EiR) for idea to Proof of Concept (up to Rs. 4 Lakh), Grants for prototype development (up to Rs. 7 Lakh), or Investment for product development and market outreach (up to Rs. 40 Lakh)
- [ ] The applicant agrees to the terms and conditions governing release of Grant-in-Aid (Annexure II), including utilization of funds within specified time, surrender of unutilized grant, maintenance of audited asset register, and submission of progress reports and audited statements of accounts

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation/Registration Certificate of the institution
- PAN Card of the institution
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)
- Audited Financial Statements (last 3 years)
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (if applicable as MSME)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of the institution (last 3 years)
- Authorization Letter from the Head of Institution designating the Nodal Officer for the scheme

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- MoU to be executed by Implementing Agencies/Partnering Institutes (as per Annexure II and release conditions)
- Terms & Conditions governing release of Grant-in-Aid (Annexure II) – signed and accepted
- Surety Bond by Implementing Agencies (as per release conditions)
- Utilization Certificate of Previous release (for subsequent installments)
- Technical & Financial Progress Report
- Audited statement of accounts
- Recommendations of the Project Review and Steering Group (PRSG)
- List of assets acquired out of MeitY grant (to be submitted annually and when seeking further installments)
- Project proposal detailing the plan for supporting EiRs, prototype development grants, and investment for product development and market outreach

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible scans or certified true copies
- File names must clearly indicate document type and date (e.g., "Utilization_Certificate_FY2022-23.pdf")
- PDF file size should not exceed 10 MB per document; compress if necessary
- All certificates and bonds must be on official letterhead and duly signed/sealed
- Documents in languages other than English or Hindi must be accompanied by a certified translation
- Audited financial statements must be certified by a Chartered Accountant
- Utilization Certificate must be in the prescribed format and countersigned by the Finance Officer and Head of Institution
- Surety Bond must be on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value and notarized

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A detailed Project Report is required for this scheme. It must include the following standard sections:
- Executive Summary
- Institutional Profile (details of the incubation centre, linkages with industry, etc.)
- Objectives and Outcomes (aligned with TIDE 2.0 goals: supporting EiRs, prototype development, product development & market outreach)
- Implementation Plan (year-wise activities, milestones, timelines)
- Financial Plan (budget utilization for EiR grants, prototype grants, investment support)
- Monitoring and Evaluation Framework (progress reporting, PRSG recommendations, utilization certificates)
- Sustainability Plan (post-grant continuity of incubation activities)
- Annexures (including MoU, Surety Bond, audited accounts, etc.)

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application portal at [https://msh.meity.gov.in](https://msh.meity.gov.in) and navigate to the TIDE 2.0 scheme section.
2. Ensure all fields are filled accurately as per the supporting documents; do not leave any mandatory field blank.
3. Upload documents in the prescribed format (PDF) and ensure file names match the checklist.
4. Double-check that the total requested grant amount does not exceed the permissible limits: Rs. 4 Lakh per EiR, Rs. 7 Lakh for prototype development, Rs. 40 Lakh for product development and market outreach.
5. Confirm that the proposal aligns with the seven pre-identified thematic areas: Healthcare, Education, Agriculture, Financial inclusion, Infrastructure and transportation, Environment and clean tech, Clean energy solutions.
6. Validate that the institution has the required linkages with large companies, industry bodies, or industry clusters as per eligibility.
7. Ensure the authorized signatory has signed the declaration and uploaded the authorization letter.
8. Save a draft before final submission and review all entries for consistency.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All eligibility criteria verified and documented
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents collected and quality-checked
- [ ] Project Report/Draft Application completed with all required sections
- [ ] Application form fields filled correctly and consistently with uploaded documents
- [ ] Declarations signed and authorization letter attached
- [ ] Total financial request within permissible limits (EiR: ≤ Rs. 4 Lakh, Prototype: ≤ Rs. 7 Lakh, Investment: ≤ Rs. 40 Lakh)
- [ ] Proposal aligned with one or more of the seven thematic areas
- [ ] MoU, Surety Bond, and Annexure II terms duly executed and uploaded
- [ ] Utilization Certificate, Progress Report, Audited Accounts, and PRSG recommendations included (if applicable for release stage)
- [ ] Final review by senior consultant before submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the MeitY Startup Hub portal at [https://msh.meity.gov.in](https://msh.meity.gov.in)
- Take a screenshot of the submission confirmation page showing the application reference number and timestamp
- Store the reference number securely in the case file
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up at 15 days and 30 days post-submission
- Notify the client of successful submission with the reference number and expected timelines

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and securely store the official approval letter from MeitY Startup Hub
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned amount against the requested grant (EiR, prototype, or investment support)
- [ ] Initiate utilization of funds as per the approved plan and timelines
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate, Technical & Financial Progress Report, Audited Statement of Accounts, and PRSG recommendations at 6-month intervals for subsequent installments
- [ ] Maintain an audited register of permanent and semi-permanent assets acquired out of MeitY grant
- [ ] Ensure assets are not disposed of, encumbered, or misused without prior sanction from MeitY
- [ ] At project conclusion, facilitate MeitY’s right to sell or dispose of assets acquired via grant, providing necessary support
- [ ] Submit annual list of assets to MeitY at the end of each financial year and when seeking further installments
- [ ] Render progress-cum-achievement reports every six months on all project aspects, including expenditure
- [ ] Submit final audited statement of accounts to MeitY upon project completion
- [ ] Coordinate with finance team to ensure successful utilization and compliance for invoicing success fee (as per consultancy agreement)
