# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I’m reaching out from MeitY Startup Hub, where we support deep-tech startups working on emerging technologies like IoT, AI, blockchain, and robotics to solve societal challenges in areas such as healthcare, education, agriculture, and clean energy. I’d love to learn about your incubator’s work and see if TIDE 2.0 could help you strengthen your support for ICT startups.

### Discovery Questions
1. What year was your incubation centre established, and is it affiliated with an institute of higher learning or premier R&D organisation?  
2. Do you currently have established linkages with large companies, industry bodies, or industry clusters to provide mentorship and potential business infusion for startups?  
3. Are you currently supporting ICT startups working in any of the seven TIDE 2.0 thematic areas: healthcare, education, agriculture, financial inclusion, infrastructure, environment/clean tech, or clean energy solutions?  
4. What stage of support do you primarily offer startups—idea to PoC (EiR), prototype development, or product development and market outreach?  
5. How many tech startups have you handholded in the last 12 months, and what is your target for annual startup support?  
6. Do you have the administrative and financial systems in place to manage grant-in-aid, including submission of utilization certificates, audited accounts, and progress reports every six months?  
7. Are you prepared to maintain an audited register of assets acquired under the grant and surrender any unspent funds to MeitY?  
8. Is your institution willing to comply with MeitY’s asset ownership rules, where assets purchased with grant funds remain MeitY’s property and require prior sanction for disposal or encumbrance?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Incubation centre is from an institute of higher learning or premier R&D organisation  
- [ ] Has established linkages with large companies, industry bodies, or industry clusters  
- [ ] Supports ICT startups in at least one of the seven pre-identified societal relevance areas  
- [ ] Capable of providing Entrepreneurs-in-residence (EiR), prototype development grants, or product development and market outreach investment  
- [ ] Able to handhold startups over the scheme period (target: ~2000 across 51 centres)  
- [ ] Has systems for six-monthly progress-cum-achievement reports and audited statements of accounts  
- [ ] Agrees to maintain asset register and surrender unspent grant funds  
- [ ] Accepts that assets acquired under grant are MeitY’s property and require prior sanction for disposal  
- [ ] Willing to send asset lists to MeitY at year-end and during further installment requests  
- [ ] Prepared to render progress reports and audited accounts to MeitY at six-month intervals  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like your centre aligns well with TIDE 2.0’s mission to strengthen incubation for deep-tech ICT startups. The scheme offers up to ₹40 lakh per startup for product development and market outreach through your centre—let me share how it works.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Incubation centres often struggle to provide sustained financial and technical support to ICT startups beyond the idea stage, especially for prototype development and market readiness, limiting their ability to scale innovations addressing national challenges in healthcare, agriculture, clean energy, and more.

**Scheme intro:**
Technology Incubation and Development of Entrepreneurs (TIDE 2.0), implemented by the Innovation and IPR Division, MeitY, provides grant-in-aid to 51 incubation centres for: Entrepreneurs-in-residence (EiR) up to ₹4 lakh, grants for prototype development up to ₹7 lakh, and investment for product development and market outreach up to ₹40 lakh per startup. The scheme has a total outlay of ₹264.62 crores over five years (FY 2018-19 to FY 2023-24) and aims to handhold approximately 2,000 tech startups.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your centre is from an institute of higher learning or premier R&D organisation with incubation facilities  
- You have established linkages with large companies, industry bodies, or industry clusters for mentorship and business infusion  
- You support ICT startups in emerging technologies like IoT, AI, blockchain, or robotics in societal relevance areas  
- You are capable of managing grant disbursement in stages with required documentation (MoU, surety bond, utilization certificates, audited accounts, PRSG recommendations)
