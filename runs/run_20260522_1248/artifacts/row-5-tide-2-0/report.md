# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Technology Incubation and Development of Entrepreneurs (TIDE 2.0) Scheme is a flagship initiative of the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Government of India, designed to promote tech entrepreneurship through financial and technical support to incubators engaged in supporting ICT startups using emerging technologies such as IoT, AI, Blockchain, Robotics, etc., in seven pre-identified areas of societal relevance. The scheme is implemented through 51 incubation centres across India and aims to handhold approximately 2,000 tech startups over a five-year period (01 February 2019 to 31 January 2024). The total budgetary outlay is Rs. 264.62 Crores, released as grant-in-aid over the scheme period.

### Objectives
- Promotion of tech entrepreneurship through financial and technical support to incubators engaged in supporting ICT startups using emerging technologies such as IoT, AI, Blockchain, Robotics, etc., in seven (07) pre-identified areas of societal relevance.
- To promote incubation activities through 51 incubators using a three-tiered approach at institutes of higher learning and premier R&D organizations, pan India.
- Handholding of approximately 2000 tech start-ups during the scheme period of five years.
- To create a networked ecosystem to support technology startups/incubates through identifying and creating linkages for leveraging complementary strengths.

### Eligibility Matrix
Proposals are invited from 51 TIDE 2.0 Incubation Centres, which must be institutions of higher learning and R&D organizations having incubation facilities and established linkages with large companies, industry bodies, and industry clusters to provide necessary mentorship and possible infusion of innovative businesses into their larger business operations.

| **Eligibility Criteria** | **Details** |
|--------------------------|-------------|
| **Applicant Type** | Institutions of higher learning and R&D organizations |
| **Requirement** | Must have incubation facilities |
| **Linkage Requirement** | Established linkages with large companies, industry bodies, industry clusters |
| **Purpose** | To provide necessary mentorship and possible infusion of innovative businesses into larger business operations |
| **Geographic Scope** | Pan-India |
| **Implementing Agency** | Innovation and IPR Division, MeitY |
| **Target Beneficiaries** | Startups; Incubators |
| **Scheme Period** | 01 February 2019 to 31 January 2024 (5 years) |

### Benefits & Financial Support
The scheme provides grant-in-aid to 51 incubation centres for:
- Entrepreneurs-in-residence (EiR): up to Rs. 4 Lakh (idea to Proof of Concept)
- Grants for prototype development: up to Rs. 7 Lakh
- Investment for product development and market outreach: up to Rs. 40 Lakh

### Application Process
The application process involves proposals from 51 TIDE 2.0 Incubation Centres obtained from eligible institutions. Each centre is categorized into three distinct groups (Group 1, Group 2, Group 3) for a layered approach. The scheme will be implemented through empowering 51 incubators in India and handholding close to 2000 tech start-ups over a period of 5 years.

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Start: Proposals from 51 TIDE 2.0 Incubation Centres] --> B{Eligible Institution?}
    B -->|Yes| C[Institution of higher learning/R&D org with incubation facilities]
    B -->|No| D[Reject: Ineligible]
    C --> E{Established linkages?}
    E -->|Yes| F[With large companies, industry bodies, industry clusters]
    E -->|No| D
    F --> G[Submit Proposal to MeitY]
    G --> H[Categorization into G1/G2/G3 Centres]
    H --> I[Group 1: 6 Centres]
    H --> J[Group 2: 25 Centres]
    H --> K[Group 3: 20 Centres]
    I --> L[Receive Grant-in-Aid per Stage Release]
    J --> L
    K --> L
    L --> M[Disbursement: EiR (up to Rs. 4L), Prototype (up to Rs. 7L), Scaleup (up to Rs. 40L)]
    M --> N[Handholding of Tech Startups]
    N --> O[Target: ~2000 Startups over 5 Years]
    O --> P[End: Scheme Completion 31 Jan 2024]
```

### Key Caveats
> **Important Warnings and Compliance Requirements:**
> - The grant amount shall be i) spent for the project within the specified time; and ii) Any portion of the grant, which is not ultimately required for expenditure for the approved purposes, shall be duly surrendered to MeitY.
> - The grantee institution shall maintain an audited record in the form of a register in the prescribed proforma for permanent, semi-permanent assets acquired solely or mainly out of MeitY grant.
> - The assets referred to in (2) above will be property of MeitY and should not, without prior sanction of MeitY, be disposed off or encumbered or utilized for the purposes other than those for which the grant has been sanctioned.
> - At the conclusion of the project, MeitY will be free to sell or otherwise dispose of the assets which are the property of MeitY and grantee institution shall render to MeitY the necessary support for facilitating the sale of these assets.
> - The grantee institution shall send to the MeitY at the end of each financial year as well as at the time of seeking further installments of the grant a list of assets referred to in (2) above.
> - Should at any time grantee institution cease to exist, such assets etc., shall revert to MeitY.
> - The grantee institution shall render progress-cum-achievement reports at interval of not exceeding six months on the progress made on all aspects of the project including expenditure incurred on various approved items during the period.
> - The grantee institution shall render an audited statement of accounts to MeitY.
