# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialize in helping DPIIT-recognised startups access collateral-free debt funding through government schemes like the Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS). This scheme is designed specifically for startups seeking working capital, venture debt, or mezzanine financing without diluting equity. I’d love to understand your current funding needs and see if CGSS could be a fit for your growth stage.

### Discovery Questions
1. When was your company incorporated, and are you currently DPIIT-recognised by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade?  
2. What type of financing are you seeking—working capital, venture debt, subordinated debt, or another facility—and what is the approximate amount you need?  
3. Are you currently in default with any lending or investing institution, or classified as a Non-Performing Asset (NPA) per RBI guidelines?  
4. Has your eligibility for guarantee cover been certified by a Member Institution (Scheduled Commercial Bank, NBFC, or SEBI-registered AIF)?  
5. If you're approaching an NBFC for lending, do they hold a minimum BBB rating from an RBI-accredited agency and a net worth of at least ₹100 crore?  
6. Are you planning to use the funds for crystallised debt obligations such as debentures, optionally convertible debt, or mezzanine instruments?  
7. Are you aware that CGSS provides guarantee cover through NCGTC to Member Institutions, not directly to startups?  
8. What is your primary use case for the funds—product development, market entry, scaling operations, or working capital?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Startup as recognized by DPIIT per Gazette Notifications  
- [ ] Not in default to any lending/investing institution  
- [ ] Not classified as Non-Performing Asset (NPA) per RBI guidelines  
- [ ] Eligibility certified by Member Institution for guarantee cover  
- [ ] Seeking fund-based or non-fund-based facilities (venture debt, working capital, mezzanine, debentures, etc.)  
- [ ] Loan amount does not exceed ₹20 crore per borrower  
- [ ] Member Institution is a Scheduled Commercial Bank, RBI-registered NBFC with BBB+ rating and ₹100 crore+ net worth, or SEBI-registered AIF  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it looks like you meet the core eligibility for the Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS), which offers guarantee cover of up to ₹20 crore per borrower through Member Institutions. Let me walk you through how this scheme works and how it can help you access collateral-free debt funding.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**  
Startups often struggle to access debt financing due to lack of collateral or credit history, forcing them to rely on expensive equity dilution or informal lending. This limits their ability to scale operations, manage working capital, or invest in growth initiatives without giving up ownership.

**Scheme intro:**  
The Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS), administered by the National Credit Guarantee Trust Company (NCGTC) under the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), provides credit guarantee cover to loans extended by Member Institutions (Scheduled Commercial Banks, NBFCs with BBB+ rating and ₹100 crore+ net worth, and SEBI-registered AIFs) to DPIIT-recognised startups. The maximum guarantee cover is ₹20 crore per borrower, supporting fund-based and non-fund-based facilities such as venture debt, working capital, subordinated debt, mezzanine debt, debentures, and optionally convertible debt.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**  
- You are a DPIIT-recognised startup (verified via incorporation date and recognition status)  
- You are not in default with any lender and not classified as an NPA per RBI guidelines  
- Your eligibility is certifiable by a Member Institution (bank/NBFC/AIF) for guarantee cover  
- You are seeking a debt facility within the ₹20 crore limit per borrower
