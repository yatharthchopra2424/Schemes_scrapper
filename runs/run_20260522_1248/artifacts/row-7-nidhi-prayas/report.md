# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
**Scheme Name:** NIDHI Prayas  
**Scheme ID:** row-7  
**Ministry / Category:** Science & Technology  
**Scheme Type:** grant  
**Geographic Scope:** Pan-India  
**Implementing Agency:** Department of Science and Technology (DST), Government of India  
**Status / Deadlines:** Rolling basis — applications are accepted throughout the year based on the availability of funds and capacity at NIDHI Prayas Centres.  
**Application Portal URL:** https://startupindia.gov.in/content/sih/en/government-schemes.html  

### Objectives
- To enable translation of innovative ideas to prototypes  
- To provide a platform for faster experimentation and modify approaches in the journey to market  
- To generate innovative solutions relevant to local and global problems  
- To attract youth to undertake problem solving and product development  
- To enhance the pipeline in terms of quality and quantity of innovative startups  
- To build a vibrant innovation ecosystem by connecting innovators, academia, mentors and incubators  

### Eligibility Matrix
| Criteria | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Applicant Type** | Individual innovators, startups, and student innovators from academic institutions |
| **Citizenship** | Must be an Indian citizen |
| **Innovation Stage** | Must be at the idea or prototype stage |
| **Commercialization Potential** | Innovation should have potential for commercialization and social impact |
| **Centre Association** | Applicants must be associated with a recognized NIDHI Prayas Centre (incubator) for implementation |
| **IP Compliance** | Innovation must not infringe on any existing intellectual property |

### Benefits & Financial Support
| Benefit Type | Details |
|--------------|---------|
| **Financial Support** | Grant-in-aid support of up to INR 10 lakhs per innovator or startup for prototype development. Funds are disbursed in milestones based on project progress, subject to review and approval by the NIDHI Prayas Centre. The grant is non-repayable and does not require equity dilution. |
| **Mentorship & Guidance** | Access to mentorship and technical guidance |
| **Infrastructure Access** | Use of fabrication labs and infrastructure at NIDHI Prayas Centres |
| **Networking Opportunities** | Networking with investors and industry experts |
| **IP & Product Development Support** | Support for IP filing and product development |

### Application Process (Mermaid Flowchart)
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Identify and apply to a recognized NIDHI Prayas Centre (incubator)] --> B[Submit application with innovation details, team, and development plan]
    B --> C[Present idea to selection committee at the Centre]
    C --> D{Selected?}
    D -->|Yes| E[Sign agreement with the Centre]
    D -->|No| F[Application rejected; may reapply]
    E --> G[Receive first tranche of funds upon approval]
    G --> H[Develop prototype as per agreed milestones]
    H --> I[Submit progress reports and utilization certificates]
    I --> J{Milestone completed?}
    J -->|Yes| K[Receive subsequent tranche based on milestone completion]
    J -->|No| L[Revise plan; resubmit for approval]
    K --> M{All milestones completed?}
    M -->|Yes| N[Final submission of prototype and project report]
    M -->|No| H
    N --> O[Receive final payment upon successful completion]
    O --> P[Project closed]
```

### Key Caveats
> - Funds are disbursed only through recognized NIDHI Prayas Centres (incubators)  
> - The grant must be used solely for prototype development as per the approved plan  
> - Beneficiaries must submit periodic progress reports and financial statements  
> - The innovation must not infringe on any existing intellectual property  
> - Funding is subject to availability and approval by the NIDHI Prayas Centre  

### Required Documents
1. Proof of identity (Aadhaar, PAN, etc.)  
2. Proof of address  
3. Detailed project proposal including innovation description, objectives, and methodology  
4. Team details and resumes  
5. Budget estimate for prototype development  
6. Any prior work or IP related to the innovation  
7. Letter of support from academic institution (if applicable)  
8. Declaration of originality and ownership of the idea
