# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] The applicant is a SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Fund (AIF)  
- [ ] The AIF has raised at least 50% of its stated fund size from other investors  
- [ ] The AIF is managed by experienced professionals with a proven track record  
- [ ] The AIF’s proposed fund size does not exceed twice the amount the FFS can contribute (i.e., FFS contribution capped at 50% of daughter fund size)  
- [ ] The AIF operates in sectors supported by FFS (manufacturing, agriculture, health, education, deep tech, micro VCs, tech-driven innovative manufacturing, or sector/stage agnostic)  
- [ ] Startups that the AIF intends to invest in are DPIIT-recognised startups  
- [ ] The AIF agrees to FFS having representation on its governance structure/board based on the contribution made  
- [ ] The AIF commits to mentoring and nurturing investee startups before exit or sale of investments  

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration  
- PAN Card  
- Memorandum of Association / LLP Deed / Partnership Deed  
- Board Resolution authorising application  
- Annual Accounts for the last three financial years  
- Income Tax Returns for the last three financial years  
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (if applicable)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- SEBI registration certificate for the AIF  
- Detailed fund proposal including stated fund size and investment strategy  
- Evidence of funds already raised (minimum 50% of stated fund size from other investors)  
- Track record of fund managers (CVs, past fund performance, investment history)  
- Governance structure document showing proposed FFS representation on board/committee  
- Sector-specific investment thesis aligned with FFS focus areas (deep tech, micro VCs, tech-driven innovative manufacturing, etc.)  
- Plan for mentoring and nurturing investee startups post-investment  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format  
- File names must match the document type (e.g., "SEBI_Registration_Certificate.pdf")  
- File size must not exceed 10 MB per document  
- All certificates must be valid and not expired  
- English language required; if documents are in another language, provide certified translation  
- Signatures and stamps must be visible and authentic

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not explicitly required for FFS applications per evidence. However, a detailed fund proposal covering investment strategy, target sectors, fund size, and team track record is essential. **Verify with nodal officer (SIDBI/DPIIT) if a formal DPR is expected.**

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Ensure all applicant details (name, registration number, PAN, address) are accurate and match supporting documents  
2. Clearly state the proposed fund size and confirm that the applicant has raised ≥50% from other investors  
3. Specify the exact percentage of FFS contribution sought (must not exceed 50% of stated fund size)  
4. Detail the investment focus areas aligned with FFS priorities (deep tech, micro VCs, tech-driven innovative manufacturing, etc.)  
5. Provide a clear track record of fund managers, including past investments and exits  
6. Explain how FFS will be represented in the AIF’s governance structure based on contribution  
7. Outline the post-investment support plan for startups (mentoring, nurturing, value addition)  
8. Double-check that all declarations are signed and dated  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All sections of the application form are fully completed  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents are attached and correctly labelled  
- [ ] Declarations are signed and dated by authorised signatory  
- [ ] Fund size and investor commitment ratios are arithmetically correct (≥50% raised externally)  
- [ ] FFS contribution request does not exceed 50% of stated daughter fund size  
- [ ] Investment strategy aligns with FFS sector priorities  
- [ ] Governance structure includes FFS representation as per contribution  
- [ ] Startup mentoring and nurturing plan is documented

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the completed application package to SIDBI (Implementation Agency) via the designated channel (email or portal as advised)  
- Take a screenshot of the submission confirmation or save the acknowledgment receipt  
- Store the application reference number securely in the case file  
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up at 15 days and 30 days post-submission

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and securely store the approval letter from SIDBI/DPIIT  
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned amount against the approved FFS contribution (≤50% of daughter fund size)  
- [ ] Track tranche-wise disbursement schedule as per VCIC recommendations and due diligence  
- [ ] Ensure utilisation certificates are submitted for each tranche before requesting the next  
- [ ] Monitor post-investment mentoring and nurturing activities of the AIF in its portfolio startups  
- [ ] Confirm that up to 5% of FFS returns are earmarked for ecosystem development activities (sensitization, workshops, capacity building, etc.) as per scheme design  
- [ ] Prepare for success fee invoicing upon achievement of milestones (if applicable per engagement terms)
