# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialize in helping startups access government funding through SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) that receive support from the Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS). Since FFS indirectly supports startups by investing in AIFs that then fund innovative ventures across sectors like manufacturing, agriculture, health, and education, I’d like to understand if your startup could benefit from this ecosystem. Could you share a bit about your venture and its current stage?

### Discovery Questions
1. When was your company incorporated or registered? (FFS benefits startups recognised by DPIIT, which requires incorporation within the last 10 years)
2. What is your current annual turnover? (Must not exceed Rs. 100 crore in any financial year since incorporation)
3. Are you incorporated as a Private Limited Company, Registered Partnership Firm, or Limited Liability Partnership? (Required entity types for DPIIT recognition)
4. Is your company working towards innovation, development, or improvement of a product, process, or service with a scalable business model? (Core eligibility for DPIIT recognition)
5. Has your entity been formed by splitting up or reconstructing an existing business? (Such entities are not eligible as startups)
6. Are you seeking equity or equity-linked funding from venture capital sources? (FFS supports AIFs that invest in startups via these instruments)
7. Do you operate in sectors like manufacturing, agriculture, health, education, or deep tech? (FFS supports a broad mix including these areas)
8. Are you looking for mentorship, networking, or ecosystem support alongside funding? (Up to 5% of FFS returns fund ecosystem development activities)

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Incorporated as Private Limited Company, Registered Partnership Firm, or LLP
- [ ] Incorporated/registered within the last 10 years
- [ ] Annual turnover not exceeding Rs. 100 crore in any financial year since incorporation
- [ ] Working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products/services/processes
- [ ] Scalable business model with high potential for wealth and employment creation
- [ ] Not formed by splitting up or reconstructing an existing business
- [ ] Seeking equity or equity-linked investment
- [ ] Operating in supported sectors (manufacturing, agriculture, health, education, deep tech, etc.)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your startup aligns with the criteria for DPIIT recognition, which is the gateway to accessing indirect support from the Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS). FFS enables SEBI-registered AIFs to invest in startups like yours through equity and equity-linked instruments, with a total corpus of INR 10,000 crore. Would you like me to explain how this works and whether we can help you connect with eligible AIFs?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Early-stage startups often struggle to access venture capital due to limited investor networks, high due diligence costs, and perceived risk, especially in Tier-II and Tier-III cities or emerging sectors like deep tech and innovative manufacturing. This funding gap hinders product development, market entry, and scaling, despite strong innovation potential.

**Scheme intro:**
The Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS), managed by the Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) as the Implementation Agency under the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), is a Government of India initiative with a total corpus of INR 10,000 crore. It does not invest directly into startups but contributes up to 50% of the capital of SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs), provided the AIF has already raised 50% or more from other investors. These AIFs then invest in DPIIT-recognised startups via equity and equity-linked instruments.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your startup must be recognised as a 'startup' by DPIIT under the Startup India initiative  
- Incorporated as a Private Limited Company, LLP, or Registered Partnership Firm  
- Not older than 10 years from date of incorporation  
- Annual turnover not exceeding Rs. 100 crore in any financial year  
- Engaged in innovation, development, or improvement of products/services with a scalable business model
