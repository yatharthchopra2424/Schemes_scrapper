### Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Equity
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI)
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://startupindia.gov.in/content/sih/en/government-schemes.html
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Commitments to AIFs spread over the 16th and 17th Finance Commission Cycles

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs)
- Age of business: Not specified for AIFs; startups must be recognised as 'startups' by the Central Government under DPIIT notification
- Sector: Broad mix including manufacturing, agriculture, health, education, deep tech, micro VCs, tech-driven innovative manufacturing, and sector/stage agnostic startups
- Revenue cap: Not applicable (scheme invests in AIFs, not directly in startups)
- Employee count: Not mentioned
- Other: AIFs must raise the balance 50% or more of the stated fund size from other investors to be eligible for FFS contribution; FFS shall have representation on the governance structure/board of the venture fund based on the contribution made

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Up to 50% of the stated daughter fund size; total corpus of INR 10,000 crore
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit to AIFs in tranches based on due diligence and VCIC recommendations
- Duration of benefit: Disbursed in tranches over the 16th and 17th Finance Commission Cycles

#### Documents Required
1. Proposal submission to the Implementation Agency (IA)
2. Due diligence documents as required by IA
3. Evidence of having raised 50% or more of the stated fund size from other investors
4. Details of track record and experience of fund managers
5. Governance structure details showing FFS representation rights
6. SEBI registration certificate for the AIF
7. Fund placement memorandum or investment strategy document
8. Commitment letters from other investors for the balance 50% or more

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. AIFs submit proposals to the Implementation Agency (IA), which includes SIDBI and other selected domestic IAs
2. IAs conduct due diligence on the proposals
3. A Venture Capital Investment Committee (VCIC), constituted by DPIIT and comprising industry experts, subject matter experts, and IAs, evaluates the proposals
4. VCIC considers AIFs managed by experienced professionals with proven track records for funding under the Scheme
5. Upon approval, AIFs draw funds from FFS in tranches to meet the target corpus
6. Post-investment, supported AIFs mentor and nurture their investee startups before exit or sale of investments
