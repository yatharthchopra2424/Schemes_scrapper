# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS) is a Government of India initiative under the Startup India Action Plan, designed to provide indirect financial support to startups by investing in SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs). It does not invest directly into startups but contributes to the corpus of venture funds that, in turn, invest in DPIIT-recognised startups. The scheme is implemented by the Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) and operates under the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry. The total corpus of the Fund of Funds is INR 10,000 crore, disbursed in tranches over the 16th and 17th Finance Commission Cycles. The scheme is currently active and was last updated in 2026, with the FFS 2.0 coming into force from April 13, 2026.

### Objectives
The Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS) aims to:
- Provide funding support for the development and growth of innovation-driven enterprises.
- Enable access to venture capital for startups across stages and sectors.
- Support the development of smaller Venture Capital funds with a focus on early growth stage startups.
- Nurture innovative ideas and promote innovation-driven manufacturing and long gestation technologies.
- Contribute to the corpus of SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) for investing in equity and equity-linked instruments of DPIIT-recognised startups.
- Promote ecosystem development by earmarking up to 5% of returns for capacity building activities such as sensitization, workshops, capacity building, plug-and-play shared facilities, mentorship, and regulatory support.
- Strengthen monitoring and evaluation through an Empowered Committee with inter-ministerial representation.

### Eligibility Matrix
The Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS) does not invest directly into startups. Instead, it contributes to the corpus of SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs). The eligibility criteria are as follows:

| **Criteria** | **Details** |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Entity Type** | SEBI-registered Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) |
| **Minimum Fundraising** | AIFs must have already raised 50% or more of the stated fund size from other investors before applying for FFS contribution. |
| **FFS Contribution Limit** | FFS contributes up to 50% of the stated daughter fund size. |
| **Governance** | FFS shall have representation on the governance structure/board of the venture fund based on the contribution made. |
| **Startup Recognition** | Startups seeking indirect benefits must be recognised as 'startups' by the Central Government under DPIIT notification (i.e., DPIIT-recognised startups). |
| **Geographic Scope** | Pan-India |
| **Target Sectors** | Broad mix of sectors including manufacturing, agriculture, health, and education. FFS 2.0 includes a segmented approach targeting deep tech, micro VCs, tech-driven innovative manufacturing, and sector/stage agnostic startups. |

> **Key Caveats**:
> - The Fund of Funds does not invest directly into startups; it invests only in SEBI-registered AIFs.
> - FFS contribution is limited to a maximum of 50% of the stated daughter fund size.
> - The daughter fund must have already raised 50% or more of its stated size from other investors.
> - Up to 5% of returns from FFS are earmarked for ecosystem development activities and deposited back to the Consolidated Fund of India.
> - The Scheme shall be known as 'Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0' and came into force from April 13, 2026.
> - Commitments to AIFs are spread over the 16th and 17th Finance Commission Cycles.

### Benefits & Financial Support
The Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS) operates as a Fund of Funds, meaning it does not invest directly into startups but participates in the capital of SEBI-registered Venture Funds (AIFs). It contributes up to 50% of the stated daughter fund size, provided the AIF has already raised the balance 50% or more from other investors. The total corpus is INR 10,000 crore, disbursed in tranches over the 16th and 17th Finance Commission Cycles. SIDBI acts as the Implementation Agency (IA) for FFS 2.0, continuing its role from FFS 1.0. Funds are disbursed to AIFs based on due diligence and recommendations from a Venture Capital Investment Committee (VCIC) constituted by DPIIT.

| **Aspect** | **Details** |
|------------|-------------|
| **Fund Size** | INR 10,000 crore |
| **Implementing Agency** | Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI) |
| **Funding Mechanism** | Contributes up to 50% of the stated daughter fund size to SEBI-registered AIFs, subject to the AIF having raised ≥50% from other investors. |
| **Disbursement** | Tranches over the 16th and 17th Finance Commission Cycles |
| **Governance** | Venture Capital Investment Committee (VCIC), constituted by DPIIT, evaluates proposals and recommends disbursements. |
| **Indirect Benefits to Startups** | AIFs receiving FFS contributions invest in DPIIT-recognised startups via equity and equity-linked instruments. |
| **Ecosystem Development** | Up to 5% of returns from FFS are earmarked for sensitization, workshops, capacity building, plug-and-play shared facilities, mentorship, and regulatory support. |

### Application Process
The application process for AIFs seeking contribution from the Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS) involves the following steps:

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[AIF submits proposal to Implementation Agency (IA)] --> B[IA conducts due diligence on the proposal]
    B --> C[Venture Capital Investment Committee (VCIC) evaluates the proposal]
    C --> D{VCIC approval?}
    D -->|Yes| E[AIF draws funds from FFS in tranches to meet target corpus]
    D -->|No| F[Application rejected; reasons provided]
    E --> G[Post-investment, supported AIFs mentor and nurture investee startups]
    G --> H[Exit or sale of investments]
```

**Detailed Steps**:
1. **Proposal Submission**: AIFs submit proposals to the Implementation Agency (IA), which includes SIDBI and other selected domestic IAs.
2. **Due Diligence**: IAs conduct due diligence on the proposals, assessing the AIF’s track record, team, investment strategy, and compliance.
3. **VCIC Evaluation**: A Venture Capital Investment Committee (VCIC), constituted by DPIIT and comprising industry experts, subject matter experts, and IAs, evaluates the proposals. The VCIC considers AIFs managed by experienced professionals with proven track records for funding under the Scheme.
4. **Approval**: Upon approval by the VCIC, AIFs are sanctioned to receive FFS contributions.
5. **Disbursement**: Approved AIFs draw funds from FFS in tranches to meet the target corpus, based on milestones and due diligence.
6. **Post-Investment Support**: Supported AIFs mentor and nurture their investee startups before exit or sale of investments.
7. **Monitoring**: Post-investment, the IA and VCIC monitor the performance of AIFs and their portfolio startups.

> **Important Notes**:
> - The VCIC evaluates AIFs based on the experience and track record of the fund managers.
> - Disbursements are tranche-based and linked to the AIF’s progress in fundraising and investment deployment.
> - The FFS does not take a controlling stake in the AIF but gains governance rights proportional to its contribution.
> - All disbursements are subject to ongoing compliance and reporting requirements by the AIF to the IA.

**Source**: [Startup India Government Schemes Portal](https://startupindia.gov.in/content/sih/en/government-schemes.html)
