# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] The applicant intends to establish a micro, small, or medium enterprise.
- [ ] The enterprise is classified based on investment in plant and machinery or equipment and turnover:
  - Micro enterprise: Investment ≤ ₹1 crore and turnover ≤ ₹5 crore.
  - Small enterprise: Investment ≤ ₹10 crore and turnover ≤ ₹50 crore.
  - Medium enterprise: Investment ≤ ₹50 crore and turnover ≤ ₹250 crore.
- [ ] All units with GSTIN listed against the same PAN are collectively treated as one enterprise.
- [ ] The applicant is a person (individual, proprietor, partner, karta, or authorised signatory for company/LLP/cooperative/society/trust).
- [ ] The applicant has an Aadhaar number (required for registration).
- [ ] The applicant has a PAN number (mandatory for all enterprise types except proprietorships not registered under any Act, where PAN may be used by the proprietor).
- [ ] The applicant is not filing more than one Udyam Registration (no duplicate registrations allowed).
- [ ] The applicant understands that registration is based on self-declaration; false information may lead to penalties under the MSMED Act, 2006.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Aadhaar number
- PAN number

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- No additional documents are required beyond Aadhaar and PAN. The Udyam Registration process is based on self-declaration with no requirement to upload documents, papers, certificates, or proof.

### Document Quality Check
- Ensure Aadhaar number is valid and matches the applicant’s identity.
- Ensure PAN number is valid and matches the applicant’s identity or enterprise details.
- For proprietorship firms not registered under any Act, the proprietor’s PAN may be used.
- For companies, LLPs, cooperative societies, societies, or trusts, the authorised signatory must provide GSTIN and PAN along with Aadhaar.
- All information must be in English or Hindi; no translation required.
- File size and naming conventions do not apply as no document upload is needed.

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not required. Udyam Registration is a simple self-declaration process and does not require a Detailed Project Report (DPR). However, if the client intends to apply for other MSME schemes post-registration, a DPR may be needed for those schemes — verify with the nodal officer of the specific scheme.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Visit the official Udyam Registration Portal: https://udyamregistration.gov.in
2. Click on **‘For New Enterprise who are not Registered yet as MSME’**.
3. Enter Aadhaar number and validate via OTP.
4. Enter PAN details and validate.
5. Fill in enterprise details:
   - Name of enterprise
   - Type of organisation (proprietorship, partnership, HUF, company, LLP, cooperative society, society, trust)
   - Address (registered office and plant location, if different)
   - Bank details (account number, IFSC code)
6. Provide investment and turnover details:
   - If prior ITR/GST data is available, the system may auto-fetch from Income Tax and GST networks.
   - For new enterprises without prior ITR, investment is based on self-declaration of the promoter (valid until first ITR filing).
   - Turnover calculation excludes exports of goods or services.
   - For enterprises without PAN, turnover is based on self-declaration until 31st March 2021; thereafter, PAN and GSTIN are mandatory.
7. Specify activities (manufacturing, service, or both) — any number of activities can be added in one registration.
8. Submit self-declaration.
9. Upon successful submission, the Udyam Registration Certificate with dynamic QR code will be issued immediately.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] Aadhaar number entered and validated.
- [ ] PAN number entered and validated (or proprietor’s PAN used for eligible proprietorships).
- [ ] Enterprise name, type, and address correctly filled.
- [ ] Bank details provided.
- [ ] Investment and turnover figures entered as per self-declaration or auto-fetched.
- [ ] Activities (manufacturing/service/both) specified.
- [ ] Self-declaration checkbox accepted.
- [ ] No duplicate Udyam Registration attempted (system prevents multiple filings).
- [ ] All fields marked as complete before final submission.
- [ ] Acknowledgement/reference number noted post-submission.

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the Udyam Registration Portal at https://udyamregistration.gov.in.
- Immediately after submission, screenshot the confirmation page showing the Udyam Registration Number.
- Store the reference number (Udyam Registration Number) in the client’s file.
- Set a follow-up reminder for 7 days to verify certificate download and QR code functionality.
- Download and save the Udyam Registration Certificate (e-certificate) from the portal.

- [ ] Confirm the certificate includes a dynamic QR code that redirects to the enterprise details on the Udyam Registration Portal.
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned status: Udyam Registration itself does not provide financial support but is mandatory to access benefits under MSME schemes and Priority Sector Lending from banks (per RBI notification no. RBI/2020-2021/26).
- [ ] Guide the client on leveraging the Udyam Registration Certificate to:
  - Apply for credit under Priority Sector Lending from banks.

  - Avail concessions and rebates under various central and state government schemes.
- [ ] Inform the client that no renewal is required — the registration is permanent.
