# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Entity is incorporated or registered in India as a private limited company (as defined in the Companies Act, 2013) OR registered as a partnership firm (registered under section 59 of the Partnership Act, 1932) OR a limited liability partnership (under the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008) OR a Multi-State Cooperative Society registered with the Central Registrar of Cooperative Societies (under the Multi-State Cooperative Societies Act, 2002) OR a Cooperative Society registered under any State or Union Territory Cooperative Societies Act with the respective Registrar of Cooperative Societies in India.
- [ ] Entity is within a period of ten years from the date of its incorporation or registration (up to twenty years for DeepTech startups).
- [ ] Turnover for any of the financial years since incorporation or registration does not exceed two hundred crore rupees (three hundred crore rupees for DeepTech startups).
- [ ] Entity is working towards innovation, development or improvement of products or processes or services, or is a scalable business model with a high potential of employment generation or wealth creation.
- [ ] Entity is NOT formed by splitting up or reconstruction of an existing business.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
- PAN of the entity
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the last three financial years
- GST registration certificate (if applicable)
- Bank statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (if applicable)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Pitch deck or business description
- Authorization letter from authorized signatory
- Details of funding received (if any)
- Memorandum of Association for Pvt. Ltd. / LLP Deed
- Board Resolution (if any)
- Annual Accounts of the startup for the last three financial years
- Incorporation number (CIN)
- LLPIN (if applicable)
- Registered Mobile Number
- Email ID of Authorized Representative

### Document Quality Check
- File size: Each document must be under 5 MB
- File name: Must match the document type (e.g., "Certificate_of_Incorporation.pdf")
- Certification: Scanned copies must be clear and legible; self-attested where required
- Language: Documents must be in English or accompanied by a certified translation
- Validity: Certificates and registrations must be current and not expired

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not required for DPIIT recognition under Startup India Initiative. However, a detailed pitch deck or business description highlighting innovation, scalability, and potential for employment/wealth creation is mandatory. Verify with nodal officer if additional project details are needed for linked schemes like SISFS or FFS.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Register on the Startup India portal (www.startupindia.gov.in) to obtain a Bhaskar ID.
2. Log in and navigate to the 'Get Recognised' section to begin the DPIIT recognition application.
3. Fill in all fields accurately: entity details, incorporation date, turnover, innovation description, and scalability aspects.
4. Upload documents in the specified order: Certificate of Incorporation, PAN, pitch deck/business description, authorization letter, funding details, MoA/LLP deed, Board Resolution, annual accounts, ITRs, CIN, LLPIN (if applicable), registered mobile, and authorized email.
5. Ensure the innovation description clearly states how the startup works towards development/improvement of products/services or has a scalable model with high employment/wealth potential.
6. Confirm that the entity is not formed by splitting up or reconstructing an existing business.
7. Double-check all entries before submission; incomplete or incorrect data may lead to rejection.
8. Submit the application and note the application reference number for tracking.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All application form fields are complete and accurate
- [ ] All required documents are uploaded and clearly legible
- [ ] Innovation and scalability criteria are explicitly addressed in the business description
- [ ] Declaration of non-splitting/reconstruction is included
- [ ] Authorized signatory details match uploaded authorization letter
- [ ] Contact details (mobile, email) are correct and verified
- [ ] CIN/LLPIN matches incorporation documents
- [ ] Turnover declaration aligns with uploaded financials
- [ ] Application is ready for submission via Startup India portal or NSWS

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the Startup India portal (https://www.startupindia.gov.in/) or NSWS (www.nsws.gov.in)
- Take a screenshot of the submission confirmation page showing the application reference number
- Store the reference number securely in the case file
- Set follow-up reminders for Day 25 and Day 30 to check application status

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Download the DPIIT Recognition Certificate from the Startup India portal, NSWS, or DigiLocker
- [ ] Verify the certificate includes the DPIIT recognition number and validity period
- [ ] Inform the client about accessible benefits: self-certification under 9 labour/environment laws, 80% patent filing rebate, income tax exemption eligibility (via separate IMB application), EMD exemption in government tenders, prior experience/turnover exemption in public procurement, faster winding up under IBC, access to FFS, CGSS, SISFS, MAARG, BHARKAR, and Investor Connect
- [ ] Advise client to apply separately for Section 80-IAC tax exemption via the Inter-Ministerial Board (IMB) if eligible (incorporated after 1st April 2016, private limited or LLP)
- [ ] Guide client on utilizing BHASKAR platform for mentorship and networking
- [ ] Prepare utilization certificate and success fee invoice as per engagement terms
- [ ] Monitor startup status for compliance: recognition remains valid for 10 years (20 years for DeepTech) or until turnover exceeds ₹200 Cr (₹300 Cr for DeepTech), whichever occurs first
