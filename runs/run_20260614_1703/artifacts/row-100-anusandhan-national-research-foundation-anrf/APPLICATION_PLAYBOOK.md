# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant(s) (PI and Co-PI(s)) are Indian citizens OR foreign nationals (OCI/NRI) fulfilling notified eligibility criteria  
- [ ] Applicant(s) hold a Ph.D. degree in Sciences, Mathematics and Engineering  
- [ ] OR Applicant(s) with Master’s Degree in medicine, surgery, or dental sciences (M.D. / M.S. / M.D.S. / M.V.Sc.) can apply two years after obtaining degree  
- [ ] OR Under Quantitative Social Sciences area, PI and Co-Investigator(s) hold Ph.D. degree in Science, Mathematics, Engineering, M.D. / M.S. / M.D.S. / M.V.Sc. degree or Ph.D degree in any Social Sciences area  
- [ ] Applicant(s) (PIs and Co-PIs) hold a regular position in a recognized academic institution or National Research Laboratory or in any other recognized research organization in India  
- [ ] Private Academic institutions with valid UGC / AICTE / PCI approval, Private R&D Institutions with valid DSIR-SIRO recognition and Voluntary and Non-Governmental Organizations registered under NITI-AAYOG Darpan portal are eligible to host a project  
- [ ] NGOs must have a valid DARPAN portal registration, and DSIR SIRO recognition to receive direct grant-in-aid under the program  
- [ ] PI should have at least 'the duration of the proposed project plus one year' of official service remaining at the time of submission of proposals  
- [ ] PI who will be superannuating during the duration of the project or already superannuated are eligible to apply (with a Co-PI having sufficient service remaining)  
- [ ] Under no condition will ANRF projects be allowed to continue with a PI or Co-PI aged 70  
- [ ] Investigators can hold a maximum of two ANRF (or erstwhile SERB) projects at any point of time  
- [ ] Investigators become eligible to apply for another project within six months from the date of completion of the ongoing project  
- [ ] Application for another project more than six months prior to the completion will not be considered  
- [ ] For proposals costing Rs. 1 Cr and above: Inclusion of Co-PI(s) is mandatory  
- [ ] If the Co-PI is from a different institution, an appropriate budget should be proposed and adequately justified  
- [ ] Inclusion of Co-PI (or PI) with justified role(s) from emerging academic research institutions is preferred (defined as any State University OR lower NIRF ranked research active public or private academic institution, i.e. those institutions having > 50 NIRF overall ranking)  
- [ ] Note: National Research Laboratories, R&D Organizations and Deemed Universities under any Ministries/Departments except Department of Higher Education are not considered as emerging academic research institutions  

> **Sources**: Key Facts (Eligibility section), ARG Eligibility Criteria (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/arg_anrf?HomePage=New)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation/Registration Certificate of the host institution  
- PAN Card of the applicant and host institution  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of the applicant for last 3 years  
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- Bank statements of the host institution for last 6 months  
- Udyam Registration Certificate (for MSMEs, if applicable)  
- Aadhaar Card of the applicant  
- Cancelled cheque of the host institution’s bank account  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Endorsement letter duly signed and stamped by competent authority of the institute  
- Biodata with complete list of Scientific / Technical publications and Patents, if any  
- Bio-data of the Applicant  
- Certificate from Principal Investigator  
- Endorsement Letter from the Head of Institution  
- Category Certificate (if applicable for SC/ST/OBC, etc.)  
- Plagiarism undertaking  
- Audited / interim Utilization Certificate (UC)  
- Statement of Expenditure (SE) for the financial year in the correct format  
- Annual / interim progress report, as per approved format  
- List of publications / patents, if any, from the project  
- List of Assets acquired till that Financial Year  
- Final Consolidated Statement of Expenditure (SE), giving expenditure financial year wise (FY) from date of start till date of completion  

> **Sources**: Key Facts (Required Documents section), IRG Application Guidelines (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/inrg_anrf?HomePage=New), ITS Documents Required (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/its)

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible scans (minimum 300 DPI)  
- [ ] File size does not exceed 10 MB per document (as per portal upload limit)  
- [ ] File names follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ApplicantName_Date.pdf`  
- [ ] All certificates are signed and stamped by competent authority  
- [ ] Endorsement letters are on official letterhead of the institution  
- [ ] Biodata includes complete list of publications and patents with DOI/ISSN where applicable  
- [ ] Utilization Certificate and Statement of Expenditure are in ANRF-prescribed format  
- [ ] All documents are in English or accompanied by certified English translation  
- [ ] No overwriting or tampering visible on scanned documents  
- [ ] Bank statements show consistent transactions and are stamped by the bank

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is **not explicitly required** as a standalone document. However, applicants must submit a **technical document (TD)** as a single PDF file not exceeding 10 MB, which should include:  
- Project title (max 500 characters)  
- Project summary (max 3000 characters)  
- Keywords (max 6)  
- Objectives of project (max 1500 characters)  
- Expected output and outcome of the proposal (max 1500 characters)  
- Budget breakdown (Research Personnel, Consumables, Travel, Equipment, Contingency, Overheads)  
- Detailed methodology and work plan  
- Timeline/Gantt chart  
- Expected deliverables and milestones  
- Publications and patents planned  
- Collaboration details (if any)  
- Justification for emerging academic institution involvement (if applicable)  
- Industry/international collaboration LoI (if sought)  

> **Sources**: IRG Application Guidelines (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/inrg_anrf?HomePage=New), ARG Guidelines (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/arg_anrf?HomePage=New)

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Register first**: Visit www.anrfonline.in → SIGN IN / REGISTER → NEW USER → fill registration form → submit → verify email via link sent  
2. **Login and navigate**: After login, go to Menu → Proposal Submission → Form Submission → Select scheme (ARG/IRG/ITS) → start filling form online  
3. **Fill mandatory fields**: Complete all sections including User Profile (Bio data, photo, Institute Address), Project Title, Summary, Keywords, Objectives, Expected Output/Outcome  
4. **Budget entry**: Enter budget under prescribed heads (Research Personnel, Consumables, Travel, Equipment, Contingency, Overheads) as per scheme limits  
5. **Upload technical document**: Attach the TD as a single PDF ≤10 MB containing all required project details  
6. **Upload supporting documents**: Upload all scheme-specific documents (endorsement, biodata, certificates, etc.) in respective sections  
7. **Declarations**: Ensure all declarations (plagiarism, fund utilization, etc.) are checked and digitally signed where required  
8. **Preview and submit**: Preview the entire form before final submission; submit only through www.anrfonline.in — no other mode accepted  
9. **Pre-proposal deadline**: For calls with pre-proposal stage (e.g., MAHA WATER), submit pre-proposal in prescribed format by deadline (e.g., 01-June-2026 to 10-July-2026 for MAHA WATER)  
10. **Shortlisting**: If shortlisted, prepare for full proposal submission and possible presentation  
11. **High-value proposals**: For proposals >₹1 Cr, ensure Co-PI inclusion and prepare for Level III evaluation by Empowered Technical Committee  
12. **Save reference**: After submission, save the application reference number and take screenshot of confirmation page  

> **Sources**: Application Process (Key Facts), ARG Application Guidelines (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/arg_anrf?HomePage=New), IRG How to Apply Online (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/inrg_anrf?HomePage=New), ITS How to Apply Online (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/its)

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All mandatory fields in the application form are filled  
- [ ] Technical document (TD) is uploaded as single PDF ≤10 MB  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are uploaded and legible  
- [ ] Endorsement letter is signed and stamped by competent authority  
- [ ] Biodata includes complete publication and patent list  
- [ ] Budget does not exceed scheme limits (ARG: ₹5 Cr; IRG: ₹60 lakh + overheads; ITS: actual to & fro fare, airport tax, visa, registration ≤₹50k)  
- [ ] For ARG >₹1 Cr: Co-PI included with justified budget and role  
- [ ] For ITS: Event start date within valid window (e.g., 13 Aug 2026 – 12 Sep 2026 as of 14 Jun 2026)  
- [ ] Plagiarism undertaking is signed  
- [ ] Utilization Certificate and Statement of Expenditure are in correct format  
- [ ] All declarations are checked and signed  
- [ ] Application is submitted only via www.anrfonline.in  
- [ ] Reference number and submission screenshot are saved

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application strictly through **www.anrfonline.in**  
- After final submission, take a **screenshot of the confirmation page** showing application reference number and timestamp  
- Save the **application reference number** in a secure location  
- Store all submitted documents in a dedicated folder named: `ANRF_[Scheme]_[CaseID]_[ApplicantName]`  
- Set follow-up reminders for:  
  - 7 days after submission: Check for acknowledgment  
  - 15 days after submission: Verify document receipt  
  - 30 days after submission: Initiate status inquiry if no update

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify the **approval letter** from ANRF specifying sanctioned amount, duration, and terms  
- [ ] Confirm the **sanctioned amount** matches the approved budget heads (Recurring/Non-recurring)  
- [ ] Ensure funds are released to an **interest-earning bank account** as per ANRF guidelines  
- [ ] Submit **Utilization Certificate (UC)** and **Statement of Expenditure (SE)** at the end of each financial year via the ANRF online portal  
- [ ] Submit **annual/interim progress report** in approved format through the portal  
- [ ] Upload **list of publications/patents** from the project as they occur  
- [ ] Maintain audited register of **assets acquired** under the project  
- [ ] Submit **Final Consolidated Statement of Expenditure (SE)** within 3 months of project completion, giving expenditure FY-wise from start to completion  
- [ ] Submit **Utilization Certificates (FY wise)** matching SE figures  
- [ ] Submit **Project Completion Report (PCR)** with list of publications and patents filed/granted  
- [ ] Refund any **unspent balance** via DD drawn in favor of "Science and Engineering Research Fund", payable at New Delhi  
- [ ] Prepare and submit **invoice for success fee** as per consultancy agreement  
- [ ] Ensure all **publication pre-prints** are shared in public domain as mandated  
- [ ] Ensure **data generated** is shared in appropriate depositories and managed  
- [ ] For equipment: Do not change approved/sanctioned equipment without prior ANRF approval  
- [ ] For ARG beyond 3 years: Ensure phased release (3+1 for 4 years, 3+1+1 for five years) is contingent on progress, impact, outcomes, publications, patents  
- [ ] For IRG: Ensure no reallocation between non-recurring and recurring budgets  
- [ ] For ITS: Submit claim bills and documents within 90 days of the last day of the event  

> **Sources**: Key Facts (Benefits, Financial Support, Utilization of Funds, Settlement of Accounts), IRG Terms & Conditions (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/inrg_anrf?HomePage=New), ITS Claim Reimbursement Stage (https://anrfonline.in/ANRF/its)
