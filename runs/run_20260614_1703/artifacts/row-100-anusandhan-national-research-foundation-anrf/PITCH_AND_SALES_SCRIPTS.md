# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping researchers and academic institutions access government R&D funding like the Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF) schemes. These grants support investigator-driven research across science, engineering, and interdisciplinary domains, with funding up to ₹5 Crores for Advanced Research Grants. I’d like to understand your current research goals and see if ANRF could be a strategic fit for your work.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your current research focus or project idea, and which domain does it fall under (e.g., fundamental frontier research, use-inspired basic research, applied/translational)?
2. Are you an Indian citizen or a foreign national (OCI/NRI) holding a regular position in a recognized academic institution or research organization in India?
3. Do you hold a Ph.D. in Sciences, Mathematics, Engineering, or (for medicine/dentistry) an M.D./M.S./M.D.S. obtained at least two years ago?
4. What is your current service status, and how many years of official service do you have remaining before superannuation?
5. Are you currently holding more than two ANRF (or erstwhile SERB) projects as PI or Co-PI?
6. Is your host institution a private academic institution with UGC/AICTE/PCI approval, a private R&D institution with DSIR-SIRO recognition, or an NGO with NITI-AAYOG Darpan and DSIR-SIRO registration?
7. If your proposed project budget exceeds ₹1 Crore, have you identified a Co-PI from your institution to meet the mandatory requirement?
8. Are you open to including industry or international partners in your proposal, and would you consider seeking cost-sharing or LoIs from them?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant holds Ph.D. in Sciences/Mathematics/Engineering or qualifying medical degree (2+ years post-degree)
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian citizen or eligible foreign national (OCI/NRI) meeting criteria
- [ ] Applicant holds a regular position in a recognized academic institution, National Research Lab, or recognized R&D organization in India
- [ ] Private academic institution has UGC/AICTE/PCI approval; private R&D has DSIR-SIRO; NGO has Darpan + DSIR-SIRO registration
- [ ] PI has at least (project duration + 1 year) of service remaining at time of proposal submission
- [ ] PI will not be superannuated during project duration (or has eligible Co-PI with sufficient service if superannuating)
- [ ] PI and Co-PI are under 70 years of age
- [ ] Investigator holds ≤2 ANRF/SERB projects at present (exempt if in IRG/EMEQ or mission-mode programs)
- [ ] If applicable, investigator has waited ≥6 months after prior project completion to reapply
- [ ] For projects >₹1 Cr: Co-PI from PI’s institution is included with justified role and budget
- [ ] Preference given to inclusion of PI/Co-PI from emerging academic research institution (State Uni or NIRF >50)
- [ ] No ongoing IRG/EMEQ project at time of application (for IRG eligibility, if applicable)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your research profile and institutional setup align well with the Anusandhan National Research Foundation’s Advanced Research Grant (ARG) program. This scheme offers up to ₹5 Crores in funding over five years for high-impact, investigator-driven research — exactly the kind of support that could accelerate your project. Let me walk you through how it works and whether it’s the right fit.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Researchers often struggle to secure long-term, flexible funding for ambitious, high-risk/high-reward projects — especially those requiring interdisciplinary collaboration, industry engagement, or significant equipment and personnel costs. Traditional grants may be too short-term, rigid in budget use, or lack support for open-access publishing, cloud computing, or international travel, limiting the scope and impact of innovative work.

**Scheme intro:**
The Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF) Advanced Research Grant (ARG) is a competitive, investigator-driven grant administered by ANRF under the Ministry of Education. It provides financial support of up to ₹5 Crores for a maximum period of five years, funding both recurring heads (research personnel, consumables, travel, field work, contingencies, cloud compute, software licenses, publication/patent charges, overheads) and non-recurring heads (equipment, software, long-term cloud/facility access), with strict separation between the two budget categories.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You hold a Ph.D. in a qualifying discipline and are in a regular position at a recognized institution  
- Your host institution meets eligibility criteria (e.g., UGC/AICTE/PCI approved private university or DSIR-SIRO recognized R&D lab)  
- You have sufficient service remaining (> project duration + 1 year) and are under 70 years of age  
- For projects over ₹1 Cr, you are prepared to include a mandated Co-PI from your institution with justified role and budget
