# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF) is a premier grant scheme established under the ANRF Act, 2023, by an Act of Parliament to provide high-level strategic directions for research, innovation, and entrepreneurship across India. It functions as an apex body aligned with the National Education Policy and Viksit Bharat 2047 goals, fostering collaborations among industry, academia, and government. The scheme operates through a centralized electronic proposal management system accessible via **www.anrfonline.in**, which serves as the sole portal for registration, application submission, and claim processing.

### Objectives
ANRF’s core objectives include:
- Providing high-level strategic direction for research, innovation, and entrepreneurship in natural sciences, mathematical sciences, engineering & technology, environmental & earth sciences, health & agriculture, and scientific & technological interfaces of humanities & social sciences.
- Promoting R&D and cultivating a research culture across universities, colleges, research institutions, and R&D laboratories.
- Acting as an apex body to steer scientific research per National Education Policy recommendations.
- Forging collaborations among industry, academia, government departments, and research institutions.
- Aligning strategies with Viksit Bharat 2047 and global best practices from leading R&D agencies.
- Supporting investigator-driven research across foundational, applied, and translational domains.
- Strengthening core scientific domains via fundamental frontier research with transformative long-term impact.
- Catalyzing interdisciplinary and cross-sectoral convergence to address complex scientific questions.

> **Key Caveats**  
> - No reallocation of funds between Non-recurring and Recurring budget heads under any circumstance.  
> - Investigators cannot exceed two concurrent ANRF/SERB projects (except for IRG/EMEQ or mission-mode PIs).  
> - Projects cannot continue with PI/Co-PI aged 70 or above.  
> - IRG does not allow Co-PIs.  
> - ITS grants are event-specific and cannot be transferred to another event.  
> - ANRF reserves the right to determine eligibility for cases not explicitly covered.

> **Critical Notes on Application Process**  
> - **Portal Exclusivity**: All applications **must** be submitted via **www.anrfonline.in**; no other mode accepted.  
> - **Documentation**: All supporting documents must be uploaded as a **single PDF file not exceeding 10 MB**.  
> - **ITS Specifics**: Applications open only for events with start dates within notified windows (e.g., as of 14 June 2026: events between 13 Aug 2026–12 Sep 2026).  
> - **ARG Specifics**: Pre-proposal mandatory; larger proposals (>₹1 Cr) trigger Level III review by Empowered Technical Committee.  
> - **Post-Award Compliance**: Strict monitoring via annual SE, UC, progress reports; final settlement requires Consolidated SE, UC, PCR, and DD for unspent funds drawn in favor of "Science and Engineering Research Fund", payable at New Delhi.
