# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The **GENESIS Program (MeitY)**, officially titled *Gen-Next Support for Innovative Startups (GENESIS)*, is a grant-based scheme launched by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), Government of India, to strengthen the startup ecosystem in Tier-II and Tier-III cities across India. With a total fund size of **₹490 Crore** over a **5-year duration**, the scheme aims to support approximately **1,600 tech startups** by addressing critical gaps in funding, mentorship, innovation infrastructure, and ecosystem support in non-metro regions. It is implemented through a network of **65+ Implementing Agencies (IAs)** activated across India, with MeitY Startup Hub (MSH) serving as the nodal agency for coordination and monitoring.

### Objectives
The scheme’s objectives are explicitly defined in the evidence and include:
- Consolidation of startup-related schemes and assets of MeitY for greater efficiency, asset utilization, and enhanced support for startups.
- Provision of Tier-II/III cities-focused funding to critically support Pilot/Investment, Early-stage, and deep-tech startups.
- Strengthening of incubators and building a stronger startup ecosystem via capacity-building initiatives in Tier-II/III cities.
- Support for approximately **1,600 tech startups** over the next 5 years.
- Discovery, growth, and nurturing of technology startups in Tier-II and Tier-III cities.

### Eligibility Matrix

### Application Process
The application process is not explicitly detailed in the crawled evidence, but the scheme directs applicants to official sources for procedural guidance. Based on standard MeitY scheme protocols and references in the evidence, the following Mermaid flowchart illustrates the typical application journey for a startup seeking GENESIS support:

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Startup identifies eligibility for GENESIS (Tier-II/III city, tech focus)] --> B[Visit official portal: https://msh.meity.gov.in/schemes/genesis]
    B --> C[Download and read: Administrative Approval PDF & Scheme Brochure PDF]
    C --> D[Contact Implementing Agency (IA) from the published list]
    D --> E[IA conducts preliminary eligibility screening]
    E --> F[Startup submits application via IA portal or MSH SAMARTH Portal 2.0]
    F --> G[Application undergoes technical and financial evaluation by IA]
    G --> H[Shortlisted applicants invited for pitch/presentation]
    H --> I[Final selection by IA and MSH joint committee]
    I --> J[Approval and sanction letter issued]
    J --> K[Funds disbursed in tranches based on milestones]
    K --> L[Ongoing monitoring, mentorship, and compliance reporting]
    L --> M[Submission of utilization certificate and impact report]
    M --> N[Scheme closure or transition to next funding stage]
```

### Key Caveats and Limitations
| **Caveat** | **Detail** | **Implication for Consultants** |
|------------|------------|----------------------------------|
| Geographic Restriction | Limited to Tier-II and Tier-III cities | Startups in Tier-I cities (e.g., Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, NCR) are likely ineligible unless they can demonstrate operational base in Tier-II/III |
| Funding Cap | Support limited to ~1,600 startups over 5 years | High competition; early application advised |
| Duration | Scheme active for 5 years from approval (launched Oct 2023) | Expected to run until ~2028; verify current phase |
| Component-Specific Rules | EiR, Pilot, Investment, Deep-Tech have distinct criteria (e.g., POs required for Pilot Funding) | Must align client stage with correct component |
| IA Dependency | Application routed through Implementing Agencies | Consultant must identify and engage the right IA early |
| Fund Utilization | Subject to audit and utilization certificates | Clients must maintain rigorous financial records |
