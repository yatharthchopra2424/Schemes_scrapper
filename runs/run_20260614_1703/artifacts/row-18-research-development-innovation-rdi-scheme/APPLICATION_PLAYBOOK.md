# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a legal entity registered in India under applicable Indian laws (Companies Act, 2013; Indian Partnership Act, 1932; Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008; or startup as defined in DPIIT Notification G.S.R. 127(E) dated 19 February 2019)
- [ ] Applicant is under the control of resident Indian citizens (as defined in DPIIT’s Consolidated FDI Policy 2020)
- [ ] Applicant has its registered global headquarters in India
- [ ] Applicant is engaged in developing Research, Development & Innovation (RDI)-intensive technology at Technology Readiness Level (TRL) 4 and above
- [ ] Applicant retains ownership of IPR registered in India under Indian laws/rules
- [ ] Applicant complies with GoI directions on security, export control, and other strategic concerns
- [ ] Applicant is an Alternative Investment Fund (AIF), Development Finance Institution (DFI), Non-Banking Finance Company (NBFC), or Focused Research Organisation (FRO)
- [ ] Applicant has no conflict of interest regarding RDIF decision-making (undertaking required)
- [ ] Applicant is not a government entity or CPSE unless partnering with private sector on strategic projects

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation/Registration
- PAN Card
- GST Registration Certificate
- Bank statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (if applicable)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of last 3 years
- Audited financial statements

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Certificate of Registration for the current Fund from SEBI under Regulation 6 of SEBI (Alternative Investment Funds) Regulations, 2012 (for AIFs)
- Names and details of Trustees (if Fund corpus is a Scheme of a Trust)
- Names and details of Directors, with DIN for Investment Manager/Asset Management Company or LLP
- GST Registration Certificate and PAN Card for Investment Manager/Asset Management Company or LLP
- Undertaking that the Applicant has no conflict of interest regarding RDIF decision-making
- Private Placement Memorandum of Fund submitted to SEBI
- Details on funds raised so far, supported by signed Contribution Agreements
- Trustee/Investment Manager Profile Briefs with examples of success in managing teams, leading deals/exits, driving scale-up, emerging technologies, product management, finance/investment, mentoring startups, building processes for performance/risk management, raising third-party capital, deal-sourcing/due diligence, portfolio management, exits, relevant priority sectors in India, TRLs from Level 4 onward, and venture development stages
- Relevant chapters in Private Placement Memorandum (e.g., Trustee or Investment Manager profiles)
- Curriculum Vitae/Biodata containing names and experience profiles of Partners, Investment Committee Members, Advisory Board Members, and number of funds with which individual has been associated (with exit details)
- Number of years Fund Management Team has worked together
- Manpower Details (Category, Number of personnel, Key expertise/role)
- Tabulated Portfolio Summary of investees of each Fund (including entry/exit valuations, Net IRR, MOIC, DPIC, Yield on Loans/Portfolio Yield for debt AIFs, Vintage Year Performance)
- Investment Thesis Note or Private Placement Memorandum showing technological focus, industry sector focus, market focus, technology maturity ranges (TRLs), and venture development stage
- Documentation of prior investments in RDI-intensive technology firms (with profiles on startup investments) and exits from RDI-intensive firms (with exit route details)

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible and in PDF format
- [ ] File size does not exceed 10 MB per document
- [ ] Document names match the checklist (e.g., "SEBI_Registration_Certificate.pdf")
- [ ] All certificates are valid and not expired
- [ ] Undertakings are signed and stamped by authorised signatory and Company Secretary
- [ ] Private Placement Memorandum is the version submitted to SEBI
- [ ] Contribution Agreements are signed and dated
- [ ] CVs/Biodata include complete experience profiles and exit details
- [ ] All documents are in English or accompanied by certified translation

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not required. The scheme does not require a Detailed Project Report (DPR) at the SLFM application stage. However, applicants must submit an Investment Thesis (Annexure A.2) and Private Placement Memorandum (for AIFs) or written statements/latest annual report (for loan applicants). Verify with nodal officer if any project-level DPR is needed post-selection.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Register on the RDIF Management Portal at www.rdifund.anrf.gov.in before initiating application.
2. Select the correct modality: Long-Term Loans or Contribution to AIF (Annexure A.1).
3. Complete Annexure A.2 (Investment Thesis) with technological focus, industry sector focus, market focus, TRL ranges, and venture development stage.
4. Submit all scheme-specific documents as separate uploads in the portal’s workflows.
5. Provide undertakings on letterhead with rubber stamp and signature of authorised signatory and Company Secretary.
6. Ensure Conflict of Interest undertaking (Annexure A.4) is included and signed.
7. For AIF applicants: complete Annexures B.1–B.9 as applicable; for loan applicants: complete Annexures C.1–C.6.
8. Do not leave any field blank; use “Nil” or “Not applicable” where appropriate.
9. Submit application before 1700 hours IST on 31 January 2026; portal will not accept late submissions.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify approval letter from RDIF
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned amount and funding modality (loan or AIF contribution)
- [ ] Execute Contribution/Loan Agreement with RDIF within reasonable time-period
- [ ] Issue Provisional Letter of Intent to private investors to raise matching capital
- [ ] Upon receipt of private Contribution Agreements, finalize and execute RDIF Contribution/Loan Agreement
- [ ] Begin disbursement process against Drawdown Notices
- [ ] Utilization certificate to be submitted as per reporting requirements (unaudited financials every 6 months, audited annually)
- [ ] Track investment in Eligible Technology Entities advancing RDI-intensive technologies at TRL 4+ in RDIF Priority Sectors
- [ ] Ensure net interest margin does not exceed 3% (for loan mode) or management fee does not exceed 2%/carried interest 20% (for AIF mode)
- [ ] Submit annual reports within 90 days of fiscal year end including audited financials, valuation of assets, and status report on investments
- [ ] Coordinate with RDIF for monitoring and joint review meetings
- [ ] Prepare for exit strategy tracking at 5/10/15 years post-investment
