# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a Startup, Incubator, Academia, or Accelerator  
- [ ] Project is in the field of Electronics & Information Technology (IT)  
- [ ] Project involves collaborative R&D between industry and academic/R&D institution(s)  
- [ ] Joint proposal is prepared and submitted by both industry and academic/R&D institution  
- [ ] For individual industry: Project duration preferably less than 2 years  
- [ ] For industry consortium: Project duration preferably less than 3 years  
- [ ] Government grant (if approved) will be disbursed to academic/R&D institution(s) only  
- [ ] Industry contribution will be matched by government grant up to 2x (i.e., grant up to twice industry contribution)  
- [ ] Project aims to develop commercially viable products/packages at institution level  
- [ ] Applicant has not exceeded maximum grant limit: Rs. 2.0 Crores (individual industry) or Rs. 4.0 Crores (consortium)  

> **Sources**:  
> - Scheme Portal: https://www.indiascienceandtechnology.gov.in/funding-opportunities/startups/multiplier-grants-scheme-mgs  
> - Key Facts: Eligibility, Financial Support, Application Process, Key Caveats

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation Certificate  
- PAN Card of the entity  
- Audited Financial Statements (last 2 years)  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of last 2 years  
- GST Registration Certificate  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (for MSMEs, if applicable)  
- Cancelled cheque or bank details for fund transfer  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Joint Project Proposal (prepared by industry and academic/R&D institution)  
- MoU or Collaboration Agreement between industry and academic/R&D institution  
- Detailed Project Report (DPR) including:  
  - Technical feasibility  
  - Commercialization potential  
  - Work plan and milestones  
  - Budget breakdown (industry vs. government contribution)  
- Proof of project domain in Electronics & Information Technology  
- Declaration that grant-in-aid will be utilized by academic/R&D institution(s) only  
- Undertaking that industry contribution will be provided to academic/R&D institution  
- CVs of key project personnel from both industry and institution  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format  
- File size per document: < 5 MB  
- File names must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_Version_Date.pdf` (e.g., `MGS_JointProposal_v1_20240515.pdf`)  
- All certificates must be valid and not expired  
- Financial documents must be certified by a Chartered Accountant  
- Declarations and undertakings must be signed and stamped by authorized signatories  
- Language: English or Hindi; if in regional language, certified translation required

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
**Required**: Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is mandatory as per the application process.  

**Standard Sections to Include**:  
1. Executive Summary  
2. Project Objectives and Outcomes  
3. Technical Approach and Methodology  
4. Innovation and Novelty Aspects  
5. Commercialization Potential and Market Analysis  
6. Work Plan with Timelines and Milestones (Gantt chart preferred)  
7. Budget Breakdown:  
   - Industry Contribution (cash/in-kind)  
   - Government Grant Request (max 2x industry contribution)  
   - Justification for each budget head  
8. Roles and Responsibilities of Industry and Academic/R&D Partner  
9. Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan  
10. Monitoring and Evaluation Framework  
11. Sustainability and Post-Project Utilization Plan  
12. Declarations and Undertakings (as per scheme guidelines)  

> **Note**: The DPR must clearly state that the government grant will be utilized **only by the academic/R&D institution(s)**.  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application portal: https://www.indiascienceandtechnology.gov.in/funding-opportunities/startups/multiplier-grants-scheme-mgs  
2. Ensure all fields are filled accurately; **do not leave any mandatory field blank**  
3. Upload documents in the specified order and format as per portal instructions  
4. Select correct applicant type: **Startup / Incubator / Academia / Accelerator**  
5. Clearly specify project domain: **Electronics & Information Technology**  
6. Enter correct financial figures:  
   - Industry Contribution (in Rs.)  
   - Requested Government Grant (must not exceed 2x industry contribution or Rs. 2 Cr / Rs. 4 Cr cap)  
7. Declare that the grant-in-aid will be utilized **only by academic/R&D institution(s)**  
8. Confirm joint submission by industry and academic/R&D institution (both must co-sign/upload)  
9. Preview the entire application before final submission  
10. Save a copy of the submitted application and acknowledgment  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All eligibility criteria verified and documented  
- [ ] Joint proposal prepared and agreed upon by both industry and academic/R&D partner  
- [ ] DPR completed with all required sections  
- [ ] Budget aligns with scheme limits (grant ≤ 2x industry contribution; max Rs. 2 Cr / Rs. 4 Cr)  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents collected and quality-checked  
- [ ] Declarations signed: grant utilization by institution only, industry contribution transfer  
- [ ] Application form filled without errors; all uploads successful  
- [ ] Final review by senior consultant before submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the portal: https://www.indiascienceandtechnology.gov.in/funding-opportunities/startups/multiplier-grants-scheme-mgs  
- Take a **screenshot of the confirmation page** showing:  
  - Application Reference Number  
  - Date and Time of Submission  
  - Applicant Name  
- Save the screenshot in the case folder with name: `MGS_SubmissionConfirmation_CaseID_Date.png`  
- Store the Application Reference Number securely and share with client  
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up at:  
  - 15 days post-submission  
  - 30 days post-submission  
  - Then every 15 days until outcome

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify approval letter from DeitY/MGS  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned amount matches approved request (≤ 2x industry contribution; within Rs. 2 Cr / Rs. 4 Cr cap)  
- [ ] Ensure grant-in-aid is disbursed **only to the academic/R&D institution(s)**  
- [ ] Verify industry contribution has been transferred to the academic/R&D institution as per undertaking  
- [ ] Monitor project milestones and timelines as per approved DPR  
- [ ] Maintain records of all expenditures (utilization certificates, invoices, bills)  
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate (UC) as per GFR 2017 rules  
- [ ] Track invoice for success fee as per consultancy agreement  
- [ ] Ensure project completion within preferred duration: <2 years (individual) or <3 years (consortium)  
- [ ] Support client in commercialization efforts and impact reporting  

> **Note**: All financial reporting must follow Government of India guidelines (GFR 2017). Utilization Certificates must be submitted periodically and at project end.
