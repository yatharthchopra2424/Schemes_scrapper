# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialize in helping startups, incubators, academia, and accelerators in the electronics and information technology sector access government funding for collaborative R&D projects. Many innovators in this space leave significant grants on the table simply because they don’t know how to structure industry-academia partnerships effectively. I’d love to understand your current R&D initiatives and see if the DST Multiplier & Collaborative R&D Grants could support your goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your organization’s primary focus—are you a startup, incubator, academia, or accelerator working in electronics & information technology?
2. Do you currently have an active R&D project in electronics or IT that aims to develop a product or package with commercialization potential?
3. Are you already collaborating with an industry partner (or academic/R&D institution) on this project, or are you seeking to establish such a partnership?
4. What stage is your project at—concept, prototype, or ready for scaling—and what is your estimated timeline for completion?
5. Have you explored government grants before, and if so, what challenges did you face in the application process?
6. What amount of funding are you seeking to support your R&D efforts, and are you open to matching it with industry contribution to unlock government support?
7. Is your project duration expected to be under 2 years (for individual industry) or under 3 years (for a consortium)?
8. Are you prepared to submit a joint proposal with your industry or academic partner, as required by the scheme?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Organization is a startup, incubator, academia, or accelerator  
- [ ] Project is in electronics & information technology  
- [ ] Project aims to develop a product or package with commercialization potential at institution level  
- [ ] Industry and academic/R&D institution are willing to collaborate and submit a joint proposal  
- [ ] For individual industry: project duration preferably < 2 years; funding ask ≤ Rs. 2.0 Crores  
- [ ] For industry consortium: project duration preferably < 3 years; funding ask ≤ Rs. 4.0 Crores  
- [ ] Industry contribution will be matched by government grant-in-aid (up to 2x industry input)  
- [ ] Funds will be disbursed to academic/R&D institution(s) only  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like you’re working on a collaborative R&D initiative in electronics & IT with strong commercialization potential—and the DST Multiplier & Collaborative R&D Grants is specifically designed to support exactly this kind of partnership. The scheme offers government funding of up to twice the industry contribution, which could mean significant non-dilutive capital for your project.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Startups and academic institutions in electronics & IT often struggle to fund collaborative R&D projects that bridge the gap between innovation and commercialization. Industry partners may hesitate to invest without de-risking, while academia lacks the funds to scale prototypes into market-ready products—leading to promising ideas stalling before reaching impact.

**Scheme intro:**
The DST Multiplier & Collaborative R&D Grants (MGS), implemented by the Department of Electronics and Information Technology (DeitY), Govt of India, is a grant scheme that encourages industry-academia collaboration in electronics and information technology. Under this scheme, for every Rs. 1 contributed by industry, the government provides up to Rs. 2 in grant-in-aid—meaning the government matches industry investment up to 2x. For individual industry projects, the maximum government grant is Rs. 2.0 Crores per project (with industry contributing at least Rs. 1.0 Crore); for industry consortia, it scales to Rs. 4.0 Crores in government support. The grant duration is preferably under 2 years for individual projects and under 3 years for consortia. Critically, both industry contribution and government grant are disbursed exclusively to the academic/R&D institution(s) involved.
