### Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Window:** Last date for submission of startup applications is 31 May 2026. Incubators to complete 100% startup selection by 30 June 2026.

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: DPIIT-recognized startup (Private Limited Company, Registered Partnership Firm, or Limited Liability Partnership)
- Age of business: Incorporated not more than 2 years ago at the time of application
- Sector: Sector-agnostic (preference for social impact, waste management, water management, financial inclusion, education, agriculture, food processing, biotechnology, healthcare, energy, mobility, defence, space, railways, oil and gas, textiles, etc.)
- Revenue cap: Not explicitly mentioned in evidence
- Employee count: Not mentioned
- Other: 
  - Startup must have a business idea to develop a product or service with market fit, viable commercialization, and scope of scaling
  - Startup must use technology in its core product/service, business model, distribution model, or methodology
  - Startup must not have received more than Rs 10 lakh monetary support under any other Central or State Government scheme (excluding prize money, subsidized working space, founder monthly allowance, access to labs, prototyping facility)
  - Shareholding by Indian promoters must be at least 51% at time of application
  - Startup can avail seed support in the form of grant and debt/convertible debentures each once only

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: 
  - Up to Rs. 20 Lakhs as grant for validation of Proof of Concept, prototype development, or product trials
  - Up to Rs. 50 Lakhs as investment for market entry, commercialization, or scaling up through convertible debentures or debt/debt-linked instruments
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit to company bank account (grant in milestone-based installments; debt/convertible debentures at interest rate not more than prevailing repo rate)
- Duration of benefit: One-time (grant and debt/convertible debentures each available once per startup)

#### Documents Required
1. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
2. PAN of the entity
3. Pitch deck or business description
4. Authorization letter from authorized signatory
5. Details of funding received (if any)
6. Board Resolution / Authorization Letter / POA (sample document available for download)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Ensure you are a DPIIT-recognized startup (if not, apply for recognition via https://www.startupindia.gov.in/content/sih/en/startupgov/startup_recognition_page.html)
2. Navigate to the Startup India Seed Fund Scheme portal (https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/) and click on Login
3. Enter your Startup India login credentials (username and password)
4. Click on 'Apply Now'
5. Fill or upload necessary details as per the fields in the application form (including entity details, team profile, problem statement, product/service overview, business model, customer profile, market size, quantum of funds needed, projected utilization plan for funds, etc.)
6. Recheck the data in the Entity Details page and click on Save and Next
7. Select incubator preferences (up to 3 incubators in order of preference) and click on Save and Next
8. Select the funding instrument (Grant/Debt/Debenture) under funding details to view the incubator preference list
9. Check the Self Declaration box and click on Submit
10. Your application is submitted successfully
11. Track the progress of your application on the Startup India portal dashboard using your login credentials
12. If rejected, you may apply afresh after 6 weeks
