### National Deep Tech Startup Policy (NDTSP)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** R&D schemes
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Office of the Principal Scientific Adviser (PSA) to the Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://psa.gov.in/deep-tech-policy
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Deep tech startups (entities working on producing a solution based on new knowledge/advancements within a scientific or engineering discipline or multiple disciplines, which is yet to be developed or is in the process of being developed)
- Age of business: Up to twenty years from the date of incorporation or registration (for Deep Tech Startup recognition under the notification)
- Sector: Deep tech (scientific or engineering disciplines, multiple disciplines)
- Revenue cap: Turnover not exceeding three hundred crore rupees for any of the financial years since incorporation or registration (for Deep Tech Startup recognition)
- Employee count: [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT]
- Other: 
  - High percentage of expenditure on research and development (R&D) activities as a percentage of revenue/funding
  - Owning or being in the process of creating significant novel intellectual property (IP) and taking steps to commercialize the same
  - Facing extended development timelines, long gestation periods, high capital and infrastructure requirements, and carrying large technical or scientific uncertainty
  - Entity must not be formed by splitting up or reconstruction of an existing business

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Access to venture capital, pilot debt-based funding, patient and global capital, flexibility in government funding caps, IP registration, legal support, dispute resolution, faster examinations, reduced fees, patent valuation support, institutional capacity building, centralized IP database, IP insurance, and global protection frameworks
- Disbursement mode: Multiple mechanisms including Deep Tech Fund of Funds (FoF), regulatory sandboxes, Deep Tech Translation Program, IP regime alignment, and Bharat Deep Tech Talent Pool
- Duration of benefit: Ongoing (policy framework with continuous support mechanisms)

#### Documents Required
1. Copy of Certificate of Incorporation or Registration
2. Write-up about the nature of business highlighting how it is working towards innovation, development or improvement of products or processes or services, or its scalability in terms of employment generation or wealth creation
3. Documents and information demonstrating that the entity meets the attributes specified under the 'Explanation' section (for Deep Tech Startup recognition)
4. Copy of Memorandum of Association, LLP/partnership Deed, Board Resolution/registration details
5. Annual audited accounts of the startup for the last three financial years (if applicable)
6. Supporting documents demonstrating Innovation, Wealth Creation, and Employment Generation (product/service descriptions, IPR filings, user or revenue growth metrics, awards and recognitions, funding details, employment data, or any other material demonstrating the startup’s innovation, scalability, and potential for economic impact)
7. Income Tax Permanent Account No.
8. Address and business location
9. Contact details of Startup (Phone No. and Email)
10. Declaration certifying that the above information furnished is true and no relevant information has been concealed

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Make the application specified on the portal set up by DPIIT
2. Accompany the application with a copy of Certificate of Incorporation or Registration
3. Accompany the application with a write-up about the nature of business highlighting how it is working towards innovation, development or improvement of products or processes or services, or its scalability in terms of employment generation or wealth creation
4. For entities applying to be recognised as 'Deep Tech Startup', provide documents and information as may be specified in the online application portal which demonstrate that the entity meets the attributes specified under point (n) under the 'Explanation' section of the notification
5. DPIIT may, after calling for such documents or information and making such enquiries, recognise the eligible entity as 'Startup', including as a 'Deep Tech Startup' as applicable, or reject the application by providing reasons
6. For certification under section 80-IAC of the Act, make an application in Form-1 along with documents specified therein to the Board
7. The Board may, after calling for such documents or information and making such enquiries, grant the certificate referred to in sub-clause (c) of clause (ii) of the Explanation to section 80-IAC of the Act, or reject the application by providing reasons
