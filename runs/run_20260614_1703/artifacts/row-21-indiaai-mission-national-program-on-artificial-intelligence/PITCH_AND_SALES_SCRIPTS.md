# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialize in helping startups, researchers, students, government agencies, and industry players access government-backed AI initiatives like the IndiaAI Mission – National Program on Artificial Intelligence. Since you're working in the AI or tech space, I’d love to understand your current goals and see if this national program could support your work in research, skilling, or responsible AI adoption.

### Discovery Questions
1. What stage is your organization or project currently at — idea, prototype, scaling, or policy implementation?  
2. Are you looking for access to AI research infrastructure, datasets, or collaboration with a national AI center?  
3. Do you or your team need upskilling in AI, or are you seeking to train others in AI technologies?  
4. Are you developing AI solutions with a focus on social impact, inclusion, or responsible governance?  
5. Are you affiliated with a startup, academic institution, government body, or industry enterprise?  
6. Have you previously accessed any MeitY or Digital India initiatives for tech development or skilling?  
7. What kind of support are you seeking — R&D collaboration, data access, training programs, or ethical AI frameworks?  
8. Are you aiming to collaborate nationally or contribute to India’s AI innovation ecosystem?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Target beneficiary: startup, researcher, student, government agency, or industry player  
- [ ] Engaged in or planning AI-related R&D, skilling, or responsible AI initiatives  
- [ ] Seeking access to National Center on AI, Data Management Office, or AI skilling programs  
- [ ] Aligned with goals of inclusion, innovation, and social impact in AI  
- [ ] Based in India (pan-India scope of the mission)  
- [ ] Interested in framework support for responsible AI governance  
- [ ] Not seeking direct financial grants (as none are detailed in evidence for individual beneficiaries)  
- [ ] Willing to engage through program pillars rather than individual application process  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like the IndiaAI Mission – National Program on Artificial Intelligence could be a strong fit — especially given its focus on providing access to AI research, data infrastructure, skilling, and responsible AI frameworks through its four pillars. Let me walk you through how it works and how it’s helped similar players like you.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many startups, researchers, and organizations struggle to access centralized AI research facilities, high-quality datasets, standardized skilling programs, or ethical AI governance frameworks — slowing innovation and adoption, especially for socially impactful AI projects.

**Scheme intro:**
The IndiaAI Mission – National Program on Artificial Intelligence, implemented by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), is an ongoing national umbrella initiative designed to promote inclusion, innovation, and adoption of AI for social impact. It provides access to four key pillars: the National Center on AI (for R&D), the Data Management Office (for AI data infrastructure), Skilling in AI programs (for talent development), and Responsible AI frameworks (for ethical governance). While it does not offer direct financial grants to individuals, it enables beneficiaries to leverage national AI infrastructure and programs.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are engaged in AI-related work as a [startup/researcher/student/government agency/industry player] — a core target beneficiary  
- You seek access to AI research infrastructure, data, or skilling — aligned with the mission’s pillars  
- Your work emphasizes inclusion, innovation, or social impact — core objectives of the IndiaAI Mission  
- You are based in India, making you eligible for pan-India program access

Many startups and researchers have already benefited through collaborations, data access, and training programs under its pillars. |
| "How much do you charge?" | [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT] |
| "I'll try it myself first" | Common mistakes include misunderstanding that this is not a direct grant scheme (so expecting financial disbursement), and overlooking the need to align with one of the four pillars (R&D, data, skilling, or responsible AI) to engage effectively. |
| "We're too small / too big" | The IndiaAI Mission has no revenue or employee thresholds — it is open to startups, researchers, students, government agencies, and industry players of all sizes, as long as they are engaged in AI-related work aligned with its pillars. |
| "I don't have the documents ready" | The 3 most commonly missing items are: proof of engagement in AI R&D or skilling, a clear outline of how you plan to use the National Center on AI or Data Management Office, and a statement on how your work supports responsible AI or social impact. |
| "We already work with a CA" | Great — we work alongside CAs. They handle your accounts; we handle the scheme strategy and applications. |
