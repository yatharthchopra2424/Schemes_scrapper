# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an Academic Institute, University, NGO, or Research Foundation registered/accredited by a government body (UGC affiliation certification, AICTE, CSIR/DSIR/SIRO certificate etc.)
- [ ] Principal Investigator (PI) is a permanent faculty of the applicant entity
- [ ] For AIR: PI has completed at least one extramural funded project in India with cumulative duration of 3 years in the same research area as the proposed project
- [ ] For AIR: Project/s executed as PI (not as Co-PI) only will be considered for eligibility
- [ ] For AIR: Related sanction order or funding note uploaded as proof of prior funded project
- [ ] For AIR: PI has authored at least one publication (not abstract) indexed in Scopus/Web of Science or peer-reviewed journal as first or lead author, OR has filed patents in the same research area
- [ ] For AIR: Evidence of proof of principle (POP) and preliminary data supporting the proposal is submitted (compulsory)
- [ ] For AIR: Proposal includes justification on how the project on completion would be CRS ready (strategy for taking forward outcomes towards product development with industrial partner)
- [ ] For AIR: If proposal involves agriculture, viable product/technology as outcome that can be considered for advanced trials by industry/authorized national agencies
- [ ] For AIR: If industry/LLP participation, collaborating company/LLP is more than 5 years after incorporation
- [ ] For CRS: Evidence of proof of Concept (PoC) i.e., TRL-3 and validation ready data supporting the proposal is submitted (compulsory)
- [ ] For CRS: If proposal received AIR funding, same industrial partner who collaborated for AIR project is retained (any deviation duly justified with clarity on IP governance)
- [ ] For CRS: Proposal accompanied by Commitment Letter by industrial partner to exercise first right for monetizing the product/technology
- [ ] For Industrial Partner (if applicable): Company incorporated under Companies Act, 2013 OR minimum 51% shares held by Indian Citizens holding Indian passport (excluding PIO/OCI)
- [ ] For Industrial Partner (if applicable): Adequate in-house facility to address project implementation OR incubated with recognized incubation facility
- [ ] Number of ongoing projects at given point does not exceed two as primary applicant and two as collaborator
- [ ] Undertaking submitted that there is no duplicate funding from any other funding agency for the same or similar proposal
- [ ] Applicant not debarred due to withdrawal after Apex committee approval or foreclosure due to inadequate funds/irregularity (unless withdrawal due to papers not being ready)
- [ ] Applicant not collaborating with companies defaulting on BIRAC loan repayment or irregular with instalments
- [ ] Applicant not a company/subsidiary/sister concern that has been BIRAC fund recipient and defaulted on loan/interest repayment, unspent balance payment, or royalty payment
- [ ] Applicant not having a Director of a defaulting company (as defined above)

**Application Portal**: http://www.birac.nic.in/

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation Certificate
- PAN Card
- ITR (last 3 years)
- GST Registration Certificate
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (if applicable)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Related Sanction order or funding note (proof of prior extramural funded project)
- Published paper (indexed in Scopus/Web of Science) or filing documents related to IP (patents filed)
- Evidence of proof of principle (POP) and preliminary data supporting the proposal
- Undertaking of no duplicate funding from any other funding agency
- Commitment Letter by the industrial partner (for CRS only)
- Proof of registration/accreditation from a government body (UGC affiliation certification, AICTE, CSIR/DSIR/SIRO certificate etc.)

### Document Quality Check
- All documents are legible scans or certified copies
- File names match document type and applicant name (e.g., "POP_Data_AIR_Proposal.pdf")
- File size does not exceed 10 MB per document
- All certificates are valid and not expired
- Documents are in English or accompanied by certified translation
- Undertakings are on letterhead, signed, and dated
- Commitment Letter from industrial partner is on company letterhead, signed, and authorized

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A detailed Project Report is required for both AIR and CRS proposals under PACE. Standard sections include:
- Executive Summary
- Background and Rationale
- Objectives
- Methodology
- Work Plan and Milestones
- Budget Breakdown (recurring/non-recurring)
- Expected Outcomes and Deliverables
- IPR Management Plan
- Commercialization/Translation Strategy (especially for AIR: justification on CRS readiness)
- Team Credentials (PI and collaborators)
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation
- Monitoring and Evaluation Plan
- Undertaking on no duplicate funding
- [For CRS] Commitment Letter from industrial partner
- [For AIR involving industry] Collaboration details and IP governance

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Register/Login**: Use Institution User Registration on http://www.birac.nic.in/ during active call window; new users receive computer-generated password via email.
2. **Navigate to PACE**: After login, go to Programmes → PACE → Active Call (highlighted).
3. **Select Correct Call**: Choose between AIR or CRS based on proposal type; ensure call is active (1st Apr–15th May or 1st Sep–15th Oct).
4. **Fill All Mandatory Fields**: No field can be left blank; use "NA" only if explicitly allowed.
5. **Upload Documents**: Attach all required documents in PDF format (<10 MB each) with clear, descriptive filenames.
6. **Undertaking**: Explicitly confirm no duplicate funding from any other agency for same/similar proposal.
7. **PI Declaration**: Ensure PI declares permanent faculty status and confirms eligibility criteria (publications, prior projects, POP/PoC evidence).
8. **Preview & Validate**: Use preview function to check for errors before final submission; system may block submission if mandatory docs missing.
9. **Submit Only During Active Call**: Online submission is disabled outside call windows; note system timestamp on submission.
10. **Save Reference**: Immediately save/download submission confirmation with application number; take screenshot of success page.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All application form fields completed accurately
- [ ] Scheme-specific documents uploaded and validated
- [ ] Universal documents (PAN, ITR, GST, etc.) attached
- [ ] Undertaking of no duplicate funding signed and dated
- [ ] For AIR: POP evidence, publication/patent, sanction order, CRS readiness justification included
- [ ] For CRS: PoC/TRL-3 evidence, same industrial partner justification (if applicable), Commitment Letter attached
- [ ] PI confirmed as permanent faculty of applicant entity
- [ ] Industrial partner (if any) meets eligibility: 51% Indian shareholding, adequate facility or incubated
- [ ] Ongoing projects ≤2 as primary applicant, ≤2 as collaborator
- [ ] Budget adheres to AIR ceiling (Rs. 50 lakhs, non-recurring ≤10%) or CRS guidelines (no ceiling)
- [ ] All declarations and checkboxes acknowledged
- [ ] Application previewed and validated before final submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit application only during active call window (1st Apr–15th May or 1st Sep–15th Oct)
- After final submission, take screenshot of confirmation page showing application number and timestamp
- Save PDF of submission receipt if generated
- Store application reference number in case file with date and time
- Set automated follow-up reminders for:
  - 7 days post-submission: Check for acknowledgment
  - 15 days post-submission: Initiate status inquiry if no update
  - 30 days post-submission: Escalate if no communication received

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and review approval letter from BIRAC specifying sanctioned amount and terms
- [ ] Verify sanctioned amount against proposed budget (AIR: ≤Rs. 50 lakhs; CRS: as per agreement)
- [ ] Confirm disbursement schedule tied to milestones (1st installment on GLA signing, subsequent on technical milestones, final on project completion report)
- [ ] Ensure Utilization Certificate (UC) and Statement of Expenses (SOE) format is downloaded from BIRAC guidelines
- [ ] Set up interest-bearing account with Scheduled Bank for grant funds (as per RBI Act, 1934)
- [ ] Plan for half-yearly UC and SOE submission to BIRAC (within 1 month of half-year closure)
- [ ] Arrange for audited annual reports, balance sheets, and P&L accounts submission to BIRAC within 6 months of financial year end
- [ ] Confirm IPR governance framework: Academia retains IP unless otherwise agreed; industry partner has first right of refusal for commercial exploitation
- [ ] For CRS: Validate that funds to academia are for in-house research validating PoC; funds to industry partner are for validation activities
- [ ] Track milestone completion and prepare Milestone Completion Reports (MCR) for PMC review
- [ ] Ensure no re-appropriation of BIRAC funds between budget heads without prior written approval
- [ ] Plan for project completion: Submit final consolidated UC, SoE, and project report; refund unutilized funds within 1 month of completion
- [ ] Acknowledge BIRAC support in all publications/presentations per guidelines (use of BIRAC logo requires written approval)
- [ ] Prepare for success fee invoicing as per consultancy agreement [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT]
