### Seed Fund for Bio Startups
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC), a Public Sector Enterprise set up by Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://birac.nic.in/seedFundNew.php
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Startup registered under Companies Act, 2013
- Age of business: Post-PoC (Proof of Concept) stage
- Sector: Biotech / Life Sciences
- Revenue cap: Not specified in evidence
- Employee count: Not specified in evidence
- Other: 
  - Startup must have at least 51% shareholders as Indian citizens (excluding OCI/PIO)
  - Startup must not have received BIRAC Seed Funding support previously
  - SEED and LEAP fund support to any startup must be non-concurrent

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Up to INR 30 lakhs per startup as equity or equity-linked instruments
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit (grant parked in incubator’s savings account under a separate head)
- Duration of benefit: One-time investment per startup; grant must be utilized within 36 months from date of signing SEED Fund Implementation Agreement (SIFA)

#### Documents Required
1. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
2. PAN of the entity
3. Shareholding pattern certified by CA/CS (minimum 51% Indian citizen holding)
4. Business description or pitch deck
5. Details of funding received (if any)
6. Authorization letter from authorized signatory
7. Project progress reports and utilization certificates (for half-yearly reporting)
8. Exit report with disinvestment summary (at exit)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. BIRAC identifies and selects eligible BioNEST incubators through the BIRAC Incubator SEED Fund Committee (BISF Committee)
2. Selected incubators sign a SEED Fund Implementation Agreement (SIFA) with BIRAC
3. BIRAC disburses grant-in-aid (INR 100–200 lakhs) to the incubator’s savings account under a separate head
4. Incubator designs a selection mechanism to screen startups based on vesting requirements
5. Incubator identifies Indian startups (registered under Companies Act, 2013, with ≥51% Indian citizen shareholders)
6. Incubator provides funding as equity/equity-linked instruments up to INR 30 lakhs per startup
7. A legally binding agreement is signed between incubator and startup with terms for funding and investment
8. Incubator monitors milestones and provides hand-holding support
9. Incubator submits half-yearly implementation reports to BIRAC including Utilization Certificates (UC), Statement of Accounts (SOA), and project progress
10. On exit, incubator retains 50% of gross return and restitutes 50% to BIRAC within 60 days
