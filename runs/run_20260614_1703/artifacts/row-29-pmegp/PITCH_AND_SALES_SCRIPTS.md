# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I’m calling from [Your Firm Name], and we specialise in helping entrepreneurs and micro-enterprises access government support schemes like PMEGP — the Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme — which provides up to 35% subsidy on project costs for setting up new ventures in manufacturing, business, or services. I’d like to understand your current plans and see if this could be a fit for you — no obligation, just a quick diagnostic.

### Discovery Questions
1. Are you planning to start a new micro-enterprise or expand an existing one in manufacturing, services, or business?
2. What is your estimated project cost for the venture (including machinery, raw materials, working capital)?
3. Have you previously received any government subsidy under PMRY, REGP, or any other central or state scheme?
4. Are you an individual above 18 years of age, or are you applying as part of a self-help group, institution, or cooperative?
5. If your project cost exceeds Rs. 10 lakh (manufacturing) or Rs. 5 lakh (services/business), do you have at least an 8th standard pass educational qualification?
6. Are you located in a rural, urban, hill, border, or North Eastern Region (NER) area — as this affects subsidy eligibility?
7. Do you belong to any special category such as SC/ST/OBC, woman, ex-serviceman, or physically handicapped — which may qualify you for higher subsidy?
8. Have you prepared a detailed project report for your proposed enterprise?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Individual above 18 years of age (or SHG/institution/MSME/entrepreneur)
- [ ] Project cost within limits: manufacturing ≤ Rs. 50 lakh, business/service ≤ Rs. 20 lakh (for subsidy calculation; no ceiling on loan)
- [ ] No prior subsidy availed under any central/state government scheme (PMRY, REGP, etc.)
- [ ] Existing unit not previously supported under PMRY, REGP, or any other government scheme
- [ ] Educational qualification: VIII standard pass if project cost > Rs. 10 lakh (manufacturing) or > Rs. 5 lakh (business/service)
- [ ] Project report prepared
- [ ] Identity, address, age, and educational proof documents available or obtainable
- [ ] Bank account details ready for loan and subsidy disbursement
- [ ] Special category certificates (if applicable: caste, rural area, ex-serviceman, etc.)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like you’re planning a new venture that could qualify for PMEGP — the Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme — which offers a government subsidy of 15% to 35% on project costs, with the remainder financed via bank loan. Let me walk you through how it works and whether it’s a fit for your plans.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many aspiring entrepreneurs struggle to secure sufficient startup capital due to lack of collateral or credit history, forcing them to rely on high-interest informal loans or abandon their ventures altogether. This limits job creation and self-employment opportunities, especially in rural and underserved areas.

**Scheme intro:**
The Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP), administered by the Khadi and Village Industries Commission (KVIC) under the Ministry of MSME, is a pan-India subsidy scheme that provides a non-repayable margin money subsidy ranging from 15% to 35% of the project cost. The maximum project cost eligible for subsidy is Rs. 50 lakh in manufacturing and Rs. 20 lakh in business/service sectors. The balance is financed as a bank loan at prevailing rates, with the subsidy credited to a special term deposit account after verification.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are an individual above 18 years of age (or SHG/institution/MSME), meeting the core eligibility criterion.
- Your proposed project cost falls within the allowable limits (≤ Rs. 50 lakh manufacturing / ≤ Rs. 20 lakh business/service) for subsidy calculation.
- You have not previously availed any government subsidy under PMRY, REGP, or other central/state schemes — a key requirement for PMEGP.
- [If applicable based on discovery] You belong to a special category (SC/ST/OBC/woman/ex-serviceman/physically handicapped/NER/hill/border area), which may entitle you to the higher end of the subsidy range (up to 35%).
