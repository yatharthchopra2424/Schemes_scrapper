## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping Micro and Small Enterprises (MSEs) access government support for technology upgradation. Many manufacturers and service providers in sectors like food processing, textiles, engineering, and chemicals are leaving money on the table by not claiming the upfront capital subsidy available for modernising plant and machinery. I’d like to understand your recent or planned technology investments to see if the Credit Linked Capital Subsidy Scheme for Technology Upgradation (CLCSS) could reduce your effective loan cost by up to Rs 15 lakh.

### Discovery Questions
1. When was your enterprise incorporated or registered under UDYAM?
2. Are you planning or have you recently availed a term loan from a bank or financial institution for purchasing new machinery or technology?
3. What is the sanctioned loan amount for this technology upgradation project?
4. Which sector or product line does your enterprise operate in (e.g., food processing, plastics, engineering components)?
5. Have you already selected or purchased the machinery/technology for upgradation?
6. Is the technology you are adopting listed among the 51 approved sub-sectors/products under CLCSS guidelines?
7. Have you already disbursed the loan amount and installed the machinery?
8. Are you seeking to reduce the effective interest burden on your term loan through government subsidy?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Enterprise is a Micro or Small Enterprise (MSE) with valid UDYAM Registration Certificate
- [ ] Has availed or is availing institutional finance (term loan) of up to Rs 1 crore for technology upgradation
- [ ] Technology adopted is among the 51 approved sub-sectors/products under CLCSS
- [ ] Loan is for term loan only (not working capital)
- [ ] Machinery/technology has been purchased and installed (or planned with imminent disbursement)
- [ ] Claim can be submitted within the stipulated period post-loan disbursement and installation
- [ ] Enterprise is not claiming subsidy for the same unit under any other similar scheme
- [ ] Authorization letter for subsidy claim can be provided to the lending institution

### Transition to Pitch

Let me explain how this works and how we can assist you in accessing this benefit.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many MSEs struggle with outdated machinery, leading to lower productivity, higher operational costs, and reduced competitiveness. Upgrading to modern technology requires significant capital, and while term loans are accessible, the interest burden impacts cash flow and delays ROI on technology investments.

**Scheme intro:**
The Credit Linked Capital Subsidy Scheme for Technology Upgradation (CLCSS), administered by the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME), Government of India, provides an upfront capital subsidy of 15% on institutional finance (term loans) availed by MSEs for technology upgradation. With a maximum eligible loan amount of Rs 1 crore, the scheme offers a maximum subsidy of Rs 15 lakh per unit. The subsidy is disbursed directly to the lending institution, which adjusts it against the borrower’s loan account, thereby reducing the effective debt burden.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are a registered MSME with a valid UDYAM certificate  
- You have availed or plan to avail a term loan of up to Rs 1 crore for technology upgradation  
- The technology you are adopting falls within the 51 approved sub-sectors/products (e.g., food processing, engineering, textiles)  
- The machinery has been or will be installed and commissioned, enabling subsidy claim
