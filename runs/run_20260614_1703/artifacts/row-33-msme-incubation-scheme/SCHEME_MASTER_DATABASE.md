### MSME Incubation Scheme
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Incubation
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://msme.gov.in
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Individuals, startups, and MSMEs
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Technology-driven innovative ideas
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Must be registered as MSME or in process of registration; idea must be novel, feasible, scalable, and capable of generating employment and economic value; preference for SC/ST, women, differently-abled, and aspirational districts

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Grant-in-aid for mentorship, infrastructure usage, prototype development, and other pre-incubation and incubation activities; quantum based on project proposal and stage of idea
- Disbursement mode: Staged disbursement based on milestones achieved
- Duration of benefit: Based on project milestones (not fixed duration)

#### Documents Required
1. Project proposal document
2. Proof of identity of applicant(s)
3. MSME registration or application proof
4. Details of the innovative idea or technology
5. Financial plan and budget estimate
6. Background/qualification of promoters
7. Any existing IP or prior work related to the idea

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Identify an approved incubator under the MSME Incubation Scheme.
2. Submit a detailed project proposal including business idea, innovation quotient, market potential, and financial plan.
3. The incubator evaluates the proposal based on novelty, feasibility, and scalability.
4. Shortlisted applicants are invited for a pitch or interview.
5. Upon selection, a memorandum of understanding is signed between the incubator and the incubatee.
6. Financial support is disbursed in stages based on milestones achieved.
7. Progress is monitored through regular reporting and review by the incubator and MSME authorities.
