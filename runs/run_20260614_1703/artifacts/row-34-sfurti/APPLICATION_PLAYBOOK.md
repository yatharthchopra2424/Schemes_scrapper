# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] IA is registered as an Implementing Agency on the SFURTI portal (https://sfurti.msme.gov.in)
- [ ] IA has submitted a Concept note along with necessary documents on the SFURTI portal
- [ ] IA is registered as an Implementing Agency on the portal (mandatory for proposal submission)
- [ ] IA has authenticated the list of artisans for the proposed cluster
- [ ] IA/SPV has formed a project-specific SPV as a 'for profit' entity
- [ ] Land availability for CFC construction is confirmed (owned or leased for 15+ years in IA/SPV's name)
- [ ] IA/SPV's share of 10% (5% for NER and Hilly areas) is confirmed
- [ ] IA selects a Nodal Agency (NA) from the approved list (KVIC for Khadi and Village Industry clusters, Coir Board for Coir-based clusters, or other national-level institutions with sectoral expertise in Traditional Industries, competence in cluster/collective development, project management, and facilitation of market access, operating in the field for at least five years)
- [ ] Target beneficiaries include traditional artisans, producers, SCs, STs, women, or disadvantaged communities
- [ ] Project focuses on traditional industries categorized as Khadi Industries, Village Industries, or Coir Industries

**Application Portal**: https://sfurti.msme.gov.in  
**Key Source**: SFURTI Scheme Key Facts (Scheme ID: row-34), Instructions For Filling Concept Scorecard

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation (with renewal if applicable)
- PAN Card
- GST Registration
- Audited Financial Statements (Income & Expenditure Account and Balance Sheet) for last 3 years
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration/MSME Certificate
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for last 3 years

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Project Initiation Reports
- Project Completion Reports
- Project Evaluation/Monitoring reports
- Self-attested year-wise project summary report
- Self-attested village-wise number of beneficiary summary report
- Self-attested summary report indicating types of interventions undertaken
- Utilisation Certificate (for Completed Projects)
- Grant Letter (for Ongoing Projects)
- Self-attested summary report mentioning interventions carried out in the area/for artisans
- Self-attested comparative table indicating present income of artisans and break-up/justification of income increase
- Registered lease deed (if land is leased by SPV)
- Registered sale or lease deed (long term of 15 years or more) in IA/SPV's name for land availability

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible scans or certified true copies
- [ ] File names match document type (e.g., "IA_Certificate_Incorporation.pdf")
- [ ] File size does not exceed 5 MB per document (as per portal upload limit per indicator)
- [ ] All self-attested documents are signed and stamped by authorized signatory
- [ ] Audited financial statements are certified by a Chartered Accountant
- [ ] Land documents are registered sale/lease deed with minimum 15-year term
- [ ] Documents are in English or Hindi; vernacular translations attached if required
- [ ] Supporting evidence for each scorecard indicator is uploaded as a single PDF (max 5 MB) per indicator

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required for forward progression after concept approval.  
**Standard DPR Sections** (as per Annexure-7 of Revised Guidelines):
- Part A: Executive Summary
- Part B: Diagnostic (Cluster Profile, Value Chain Mapping, Market Assessment, IA/TA/NA Profiles)
- Part C: Analysis and Planning (Need Gap Analysis, Proposed Value Addition, Project Concept and Strategy Framework)
- Part D: Detailed Intervention Plan (Hard Interventions, Soft Interventions, Project Cost and Means of Finance, Plan for Convergence, Project Timeline, Implementation Framework)
- Part E: Business Plan and Expected Impact (Detailed Business Plan, Business Sustainability Plan, Expected Impact)
- Part F: Cluster Governance Mechanism
- Part G: Information and Communication Matrix (to be displayed at CFC)
- Part H: Monitoring and Evaluation Framework
- Part I: Handover and Exit Plan (IA to SPV)
- Part J: Mandatory Documents
- Part K: Mandatory Declaration (refund liability for incorrect information)

*Note: DPR development occurs after concept scorecard approval by Nodal Agency. Verify exact DPR template with nodal officer.*

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Register as an Implementing Agency on the SFURTI portal (https://sfurti.msme.gov.in) to create user ID and password.
2. Log in and download the Instruction/Guidelines for filling Concept Application and Scorecard from the portal pop-up.
3. Select 'Scorecard' from the dashboard.
4. Fill the basic details form and save.
5. Select 'Edit' option to proceed after saving Cluster Details.
6. Upload the duly filled Concept Application Form (download format from dashboard if new applicant).
7. For each indicator, fill details, select options, and upload supporting evidence **simultaneously** before moving to the next indicator.
8. Upload supporting evidence for each indicator by choosing and uploading files (PDF, max 5 MB per indicator).
9. For indicators requiring multiple options, select multiple checkboxes as per instructions.
10. View the scorecard from the 'View Scorecard' tab.
11. Select the preferred Nodal Agency from the drop-down list and click the forward button to submit the scorecard with application and uploaded documents.
12. Upon forwarding, a submission intimation message is displayed; the scorecard can be printed if required.

**Critical Rules**:
- Only one Concept Application Form can be uploaded per project.
- All indicators are mandatory; incomplete forms may be rejected.
- Supporting documents must be correct and uploaded as evidence against respective indicators.
- For 'Nature of District' indicator, no supporting document is required (refer to Annexure A in Instructions For Filling Concept Scorecard).
- Total score is 100 marks; minimum 60% (60 marks) required to qualify for DPR development stage.
- IA must authenticate artisan list and confirm land availability (owned/leased 15+ years) and IA/SPV share (10%/5%).
- IA must select Nodal Agency based on sector: KVIC (Khadi/Village), Coir Board (Coir), or other empaneled NA with 5+ years in cluster development.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All scorecard indicators are filled with selected options
- [ ] Supporting evidence (PDF, max 5 MB) uploaded for each indicator (except 'Nature of District')
- [ ] Concept Application Form uploaded and saved

- [ ] Artisan list authenticated and beneficiary details prepared

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the completed scorecard via the SFURTI portal (https://sfurti.msme.gov.in) by selecting the Nodal Agency and clicking 'Forward'.
- Upon submission, a confirmation message "Submission Intimation" will be displayed.
- **Immediately after submission**:
  - Take a screenshot of the submission confirmation page showing timestamp and reference details.
  - Store the submission reference number (if generated) in the case file.
  - Save a copy of the submitted scorecard and all uploaded documents.
  - Set follow-up reminders for Day 25 and Day 30 to check application status.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and review the official approval letter from the Nodal Agency or Ministry of MSME
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned amount against the project cost and financial limits (Regular Cluster: ≤ Rs.2.50 crore; Major Cluster: ≤ Rs.5.00 crore)
- [ ] Confirm receipt of first installment of funds (50% of Hard Intervention, Soft Intervention, IA cost, and TA cost) in the dedicated joint bank account
- [ ] Ensure IA/SPV has deposited its 10% (5% for NER/Hilly areas) share for Hard Intervention
- [ ] Monitor utilization of funds against the approved DPR and Cluster/Annual Action Plan
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate (UC) and Progress Report to Nodal Agency upon 2/3 utilization of released funds for second installment release
- [ ] Track project milestones: land acquisition, CFC construction, machinery procurement, training initiation
- [ ] Upon project completion, ensure handover of cluster to SPV as per exit plan (IA exits after 1 year of functionalization)
- [ ] Verify that at least 90% of SPV surplus is shared among artisans (60% direct sharing, 30% reinvestment)
- [ ] Confirm submission of final Utilization Certificate and project completion report to Nodal Agency
- [ ] Coordinate with TA and NA for project monitoring and evaluation visits
- [ ] Ensure all assets created under SFURTI are maintained via user-fee based O&M model
- [ ] Submit invoice for success fee as per consultancy agreement

**Key Financial Notes**:
- Working Capital: Up to 20% of project cost for raw material procurement, direct wages, electricity, and water (revolving fund, recoupable from product sales)
- IA/SPV Share: 10% of Hard Intervention in cash (5% for NER, J&K, and hilly states)
- TA Fee: 40% on project approval, 30% on functionalization, 30% on handover to SPV (linked to milestones)
- NA Administrative Fee: 4% of total budget allocation (25% payable on functionalization, 25% on handover)
- Scheme Management Expenses: 2% of total budget (Ministry-level M&E, publicity, portal/MIS)
- Thematic Interventions: Additional 5% of budget for national marketing, branding, e-commerce, R&D, etc. (with SSe approval)

**Sources**: SFURTI Scheme Key Facts, Revised Guidelines (sfurti-new.pdf), Instructions For Filling Concept Scorecard, taempanelment.pdf, nodalagencylist.pdf
