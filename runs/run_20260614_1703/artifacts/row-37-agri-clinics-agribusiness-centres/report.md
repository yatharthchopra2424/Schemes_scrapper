# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Scheme Overview
The **Agri Clinics & Agribusiness Centres** scheme (Scheme ID: row-37) is a government initiative under the **Agriculture & Rural** category, designed to promote self-employment and entrepreneurship in the agricultural sector by establishing agri-clinics and agribusiness centres. The scheme aims to supplement the efforts of public extension systems by leveraging private sector participation in delivering agricultural extension services to farmers.

### Objectives
- To create gainful self-employment opportunities for unemployed agricultural graduates, diploma holders in agriculture, intermediate in agriculture, and science graduates with PG in agriculture-related subjects.
- To strengthen the extension system by providing input and service support to farmers through agri-clinics and agribusiness centres.
- To promote private sector participation in agricultural extension and technology dissemination.
- To enhance the availability of expert advice and services to farmers at the village level.

### Eligibility Matrix
The following table outlines the detailed eligibility criteria extracted from the scheme guidelines:

| **Criteria**               | **Details**                                                                 | **Source/Notes** |
|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------|
| **Educational Qualification** | - Agricultural graduates<br>- Diploma holders in agriculture<br>- Intermediate in agriculture<br>- Science graduates with PG in agriculture-related subjects (e.g., Horticulture, Animal Husbandry, Fisheries, Sericulture, Veterinary Sciences, Agricultural Engineering, etc.) | Scheme guidelines |
| **Age Limit**              | Minimum 18 years; No upper age limit specified                              | Scheme guidelines |
| **Training Requirement**   | Mandatory completion of a 2-month residential Agri-Clinics & Agribusiness Centres (AC&ABC) training program conducted by Nodal Training Institutes (NTIs) accredited by MANAGE | Scheme guidelines |
| **Project Location**       | Rural and semi-urban areas; preferably in blocks without existing agri-clinic services | Scheme guidelines |
| **Land/Infrastructure**    | Applicant must have access to land/space for setting up the agri-clinic/agribusiness centre (owned, leased, or rented) | Scheme guidelines |
| **Background Check**       | No prior conviction for moral turpitude or economic offenses                | Scheme guidelines |
| **Other**                  | Preference may be given to candidates from rural backgrounds, women, SC/ST, and OBC categories | Scheme guidelines |

> **Important Warning**: The training component is non-negotiable. Applicants must complete the full 2-month residential training at an accredited NTI before becoming eligible for subsidy disbursement. Failure to attend or complete training results in disqualification.

### Benefits & Financial Support
The scheme provides financial assistance in the form of a composite subsidy for setting up agri-clinics and agribusiness centres. The support structure is as follows:

| **Benefit Type**           | **Details**                                                                 | **Amount/Limits**                                                                 | **Source/Notes** |
|----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------|
| **Capital Subsidy**        | Composite subsidy for project cost                                          | 40% of project cost for general category<br>50% of project cost for special category (SC/ST, OBC, women, NE states, hill states, etc.) | Scheme guidelines |
| **Maximum Project Cost**   | Ceiling on eligible project cost for subsidy calculation                    | ₹20.00 lakhs                                                                     | Scheme guidelines |
| **Maximum Subsidy Amount** | Upper limit of subsidy disbursement                                         | ₹8.00 lakhs (40% of ₹20 lakhs for general)<br>₹10.00 lakhs (50% of ₹20 lakhs for special) | Scheme guidelines |
| **Subsidy Disbursement**   | Released in installments linked to project milestones                       | 50% after completion of training and submission of project report<br>50% after verification of unit establishment and commencement of activities | Scheme guidelines |
| **Refinance Facility**     | Available through NABARD for the remaining project cost                     | Up to 50% of project cost as term loan                                           | NABARD guidelines (linked scheme) |
| **Training Stipend**       | Provided during the 2-month residential training                            | ₹1,500 per month per candidate                                                   | Scheme guidelines |
| **Insurance Coverage**     | Group Personal Accident Insurance Cover                                     | Provided during training period                                                  | Scheme guidelines |

> **Key Takeaway**: The maximum financial support available under the scheme is **₹10.00 lakhs** for special category beneficiaries, covering 50% of a maximum project cost of ₹20.00 lakhs. The remaining 50% must be arranged by the beneficiary through own funds or bank loans.

### Application Process
The application process for the Agri Clinics & Agribusiness Centres scheme involves multiple stages, from training to subsidy disbursement. The flowchart below illustrates the end-to-end process:

```mermaid

    A[Start: Candidate Identification] --> B{Eligibility Check}
    B -->|Meets Criteria| C[Apply to Nodal Training Institute (NTI)]
    B -->|Does Not Meet| Z[End: Not Eligible]
    C --> D[Selection & Admission to 2-Month Training]
    D --> E[Complete Residential Training (2 Months)]
    E --> F[Receive Training Stipend & Certificate]
    F --> G[Prepare Project Report]
    G --> H[Submit Project Report to Nodal Bank]
    H --> I[Bank Appraisal & Sanction]
    I --> J[Disbursement of 1st Installment (50% Subsidy)]
    J --> K[Establish Agri-Clinic/Agribusiness Centre]
    K --> L[Commence Operations & Services to Farmers]
    L --> M[Submit Verification Report to Bank]
    M --> N[Bank Verification & Disbursement of 2nd Installment (50% Subsidy)]
    N --> O[End: Subsidy Disbursement Complete]
    style A fill:#e3f2fd,stroke:#1565c0
    style O fill:#c8e6c9,stroke:#2e7d32
    style Z fill:#ffcdd2,stroke:#c62828
```

**Application Portal**: Applications are processed through designated Nodal Training Institutes (NTIs) accredited by the National Institute of Agricultural Extension Management (MANAGE). Prospective candidates must apply directly to these NTIs for the training program. The official MANAGE website ([https://www.manage.gov.in](https://www.manage.gov.in)) provides the list of accredited NTIs and application details.

> **Critical Note**: There is no centralized online portal for direct application to the scheme. All applications flow through the NTI → Nodal Bank → MANAGE/NABARD chain. Candidates must first secure admission to the training program at an NTI to proceed.

### Implementation & Monitoring
- **Nodal Agency**: National Institute of Agricultural Extension Management (MANAGE), Hyderabad
- **Implementing Partners**: 
  - Nodal Training Institutes (NTIs) for training delivery
  - Nodal Banks (primarily public sector banks) for project appraisal and subsidy disbursement
  - National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) for refinance facilitation
- **Monitoring Mechanism**: 
  - Monthly progress reports from NTIs to MANAGE
  - Utilization certificates and audit reports from beneficiaries
  - Periodic field inspections by bank officials and MANAGE representatives
  - Third-party evaluation studies commissioned periodically

### Recent Updates & Caveats
- The scheme has been operational since 2002 and has undergone periodic revisions in subsidy patterns and eligibility.
- As of the latest guidelines, preference is given to projects integrating modern technologies (e.g., soil testing, seed treatment, agro-advisory via ICT).
- Beneficiaries are required to provide services to a minimum of 200 farmers annually post-establishment.
- Non-compliance with service norms or misuse of subsidy may lead to recovery of funds with interest.
- The scheme is implemented across all states and union territories of India.
