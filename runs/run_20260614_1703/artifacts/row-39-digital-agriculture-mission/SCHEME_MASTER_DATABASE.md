### Digital Agriculture Mission
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (DA&FW), Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://agriwelfare.gov.in/en/DigiAgriDiv
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Proposals should be submitted preferably in the first quarter but not later than the second quarter of the Financial Year

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: States/UTs
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Agriculture & Rural
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: States/UTs are eligible to submit proposals under the Digital Agriculture Mission, particularly for projects involving Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) for Agriculture, Digital General Crop Estimation Survey (DGCES), support to Mahalanobis National Crop Forecasting Centre (MNCFC), and IT initiatives for States, Academic & Research Institutions under the Mission. Proposals must include DPR, financial implications, clear timelines, and technology involved, and should preferably be submitted in the first quarter but not later than the second quarter of the Financial Year.

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Fund Size: ₹2817 Crore (including central share of ₹1940 Crore); Financial assistance for Digital Crop Survey (DCS) pilots, manpower deployment for DCS/Help Desk/PMU, procurement of DCS-related infrastructure, utilization of existing state funds for DCS activities; Academic and research institutes may receive 100% funds for building scalable digital solutions using advanced technologies aligned with Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) in Agriculture, subject to recommendation by a High-level Evaluation Committee
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit (Funds under the Digital Agriculture Mission are released to States/UTs in 4 equal tranches as per the latest guidelines of the Department of Expenditure (DoE), subject to submission of satisfactory status reports via PFMS)
- Duration of benefit: One-time (Funds released in tranches based on project milestones; project must be completed within stipulated time, extendable by up to one year at a time with prior approval, up to a maximum of two years)

#### Documents Required
1. DPR (Detailed Project Report)
2. Financial implication details
3. Clear timelines
4. Technology involved
5. Cost-Benefit analysis (for feedback and monitoring)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. States/UTs should submit proposals under NeGP-A preferably in the first quarter but not later than the second quarter of the Financial Year.
2. The proposal must contain DPR, financial implication, clear timelines, and technology involved.
3. Proposals are examined based on use of latest technology, innovation, scalability, success, and viability of the proposed solution before proposing for pilot.
4. The States/UTs are advised to come up with proposals whose life-cycle from conception to implementation is preferably for one year with clear timelines of implementation.
5. Funds to the States/UTs are released in 4 equal tranches as per DoE guidelines, subject to submission of satisfactory status reports via PFMS.
6. Next installment is released after receipt of satisfactory status report from the States/UTs.
7. Pilot projects requiring basic infrastructure (hardware & manpower) have such costs borne by the concerned state.
8. The Centre has co-ownership of intellectual property created during the pilot, requiring States/UTs to share details and outcomes with the Centre.
9. States/UTs are required to share Cost-Benefit analysis with the Central Government as part of feedback project monitoring.
10. The sanctioned project must be completed within the stipulated time, extendable by up to one year at a time with prior approval, up to a maximum of two years; unspent amount must be surrendered if not completed in the extended year.
