# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Scheme Overview
The **Digital Agriculture Mission** (Scheme ID: row-39) is a **subsidy**-type scheme implemented by the **Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (DA&FW)**, Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, with **Pan-India** geographic scope. It aims to establish a comprehensive farmer-centric digital and space-tech ecosystem to enhance transparency and efficiency in government program execution while supporting evidence-based policy making for farmers' welfare. The scheme has a fund size of **₹2,817 Crore** and was last updated in **2024**. The implementing agency is DA&FW, with contact details: **Email: us-it@nic.in | Phone: 011-23382926** (Under Secretary, IT Division).

### Objectives
- To create a robust and holistic farmer-centric digital ecosystem in the country  
- To improve transparency and efficiency in the implementation of government initiatives  
- To support the government in making informed policy decisions  
- To promote public and private innovation and partnerships, encouraging Agri techs in India’s digital economy

### Key Caveats & Critical Notes
> **WARNING**: Pilot projects requiring basic infrastructure (hardware & manpower) have such costs borne by the concerned state.  
> **WARNING**: The Centre has co-ownership of intellectual property created during the pilot; States/UTs must share details and outcomes with the Centre.  
> **WARNING**: States/UTs must share Cost-Benefit analysis with the Central Government for feedback and project monitoring.  
> **WARNING**: The project must be completed within the stipulated time; extension up to one year at a time is allowed with prior approval, up to a maximum of two years.  
> **WARNING**: If the project is not completed in the extended year, the State/UT must surrender the unspent amount.  
> **WARNING**: New proposals are considered only after confirmation from PAO on receipt of refund amount from previous unspent funds.  

> **KEY TAKEAWAY**: Funds are released in 4 equal tranches contingent on satisfactory PFMS status reports. Non-compliance results in fund withholding or surrender of unspent amounts.  
> **KEY TAKEAWAY**: Academic/research institutes can access 100% funding for DPI-aligned solutions via High-level Evaluation Committee recommendation.  
> **KEY TAKEAWAY**: Proposals must be submitted preferably in Q1 but no later than Q2 of the Financial Year to be considered.
