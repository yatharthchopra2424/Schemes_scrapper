# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Business is registered as a Farmer Producer Organization (FPO), Farmer Producer Company (FPC), or Cooperative Society under relevant Indian statutes  
- [ ] Entity is engaged in the production, procurement, or distribution of agricultural inputs (fertilizers, pesticides, seeds) or related agri-input services  
- [ ] Annual turnover does not exceed ₹250 crore (as per MSME classification for medium enterprises, applicable under PM-PRANAM guidelines for input suppliers)  
- [ ] Entity has been operational for at least 1 year as of the date of application  
- [ ] Business is involved in promoting or facilitating the use of alternative fertilizers (organic, bio-fertilizers, or reduced chemical fertilizer usage) as part of the PM-PRANAM objective  
- [ ] Entity is not blacklisted by any central or state government agency  
- [ ] Entity has valid GST registration and files returns regularly  
- [ ] Entity maintains audited financial statements for the last 2 financial years  

> **Note**: Eligibility criteria for PM-PRANAM are inferred from scheme objectives promoting reduction in chemical fertilizer use through incentivizing states and supporting alternative input systems. Direct beneficiary eligibility for private agri-input suppliers is not explicitly detailed in public sources; verification with nodal officer is recommended.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration (FPO/FPC/Cooperative)  
- PAN Card of the entity  
- GST Registration Certificate  
- Audited Financial Statements (last 2 years)  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of last 2 years  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration Certificate (if applicable under MSME)  
- Cancelled cheque or bank verification letter  
- Authorization letter/signatory details (Board resolution for company, resolution for cooperative)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Project proposal detailing promotion of alternative fertilizers (organic, bio-fertilizers, or reduced chemical usage)  
- Documentation of current agri-input business model (fertilizer/pesticide/seed distribution)  
- Plan for transitioning or promoting non-chemical/organic input alternatives  
- Letters of intent or MoUs with farmers/FPOs for adoption of alternative inputs  
- Technical details of alternative fertilizer products being promoted (if applicable)  
- Certification for organic/bio-fertilizer products (if claiming such status)  
- Details of training or awareness programs conducted for farmers on balanced fertilizer use  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document)  
- File names must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ClientName_Date.pdf` (e.g., `PM-PRANAM_GSTCertificate_ABCFPO_20240515.pdf`)  
- Certificates (GST, Udyam, Incorporation) must be valid and not expired  
- Financial statements and ITR must be audited and certified by a CA  
- All documents must be in English or Hindi; vernacular documents require certified translation  
- Signatures on authorization letters and proposals must match PAN/GST records  
- Project proposal must include clear objectives, timeline, budget, and expected outcomes

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a detailed project proposal is required (as inferred from scheme-specific documents). It should include:  
- Executive Summary  
- Background and Objectives (aligned with PM-PRANAM goals: reduce chemical fertilizer use, promote alternatives)  
- Current Business Profile (agri-input operations)  
- Detailed Intervention Plan (promotion of organic/bio-fertilizers, farmer training, awareness campaigns)  
- Implementation Timeline (month-wise activities)  
- Budget Breakdown (funds required, utilization plan)  
- Expected Outcomes (quantifiable reduction in chemical fertilizer use, increase in alternative input adoption)  
- Monitoring and Evaluation Framework  
- Sustainability Plan post-funding  
- Annexures: MoUs, product certifications, team details  

*Note: Confirm DPR format and length requirements with the nodal officer, as PM-PRANAM primarily targets state-level incentives; direct private entity support mechanisms may vary.*

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application portal via the official Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare website ([https://agricoop.nic.in](https://agricoop.nic.in)) or designated PM-PRANAM portal (verify live link with nodal officer)  
2. Register entity using official email and mobile number linked to GST/PAN  
3. Fill all fields in English; avoid abbreviations unless standard (e.g., FPO, GST)  
4. Ensure turnover, employee count, and registration details match Udyam/GST records exactly  
5. Upload documents in prescribed format (PDF, <5 MB) with correct naming convention  
6. In the project proposal section, explicitly link activities to PM-PRANAM’s goal of reducing chemical fertilizer consumption  
7. Declare that the entity is not availing similar benefits for the same activity under another central scheme  
8. Review all entries before final submission; no edits allowed post-submission  
9. Keep digital and physical copies of the filled application and documents  
10. Note: If the portal redirects to a state-specific interface, confirm eligibility for central support with nodal officer  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal and business details (name, PAN, GST, address) are accurate and consistent  
- [ ] Turnover, employee count, and registration date match supporting documents  
- [ ] Scheme-specific documents (proposal, MoUs, certifications) are complete and relevant  
- [ ] File sizes are within limits and names follow protocol  
- [ ] Declarations regarding non-duplication of benefits are signed  
- [ ] Application form is free of spelling errors and incomplete fields  
- [ ] Digital signatures (if required) are applied  
- [ ] Final PDF of application is generated and reviewed by senior consultant

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application only after internal review sign-off  
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing **Application Reference Number** and timestamp  
- Save the reference number in the case file with format: `PM-PRANAM_REF_[ClientID]_[YYYYMMDD]`  
- Store all submitted documents and screenshots in a dedicated folder (digital and physical)  
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days post-submission  
- Monitor registered email and portal inbox for queries or deficiency notices

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify approval letter specifying sanctioned amount and validity period  
- [ ] Confirm disbursement schedule and mode (direct benefit transfer, reimbursement, etc.)  
- [ ] Initiate utilization of funds as per approved project proposal  
- [ ] Maintain detailed records of all expenditures (bills, invoices, vouchers)  
- [ ] Prepare Utilization Certificate (UC) as per GFR 2017 format, certified by CA  
- [ ] Submit UC and progress report within stipulated timeline (typically within 30 days of fund utilization)  
- [ ] Monitor and document outcomes: reduction in chemical fertilizer use, increase in alternative input adoption, farmer reach  
- [ ] Coordinate with nodal officer for any mid-term review or site visit  
- [ ] Upon successful UC submission, initiate success fee invoicing as per engagement letter  
- [ ] Retain all records (approval, UC, correspondence) for minimum 5 years for audit purposes  

> **Note**: PM-PRANAM primarily incentivizes states to reduce chemical fertilizer use; direct fund flow to private agri-input entities may be indirect (e.g., via state-led programs). Confirm disbursement mechanism with nodal officer post-approval.
