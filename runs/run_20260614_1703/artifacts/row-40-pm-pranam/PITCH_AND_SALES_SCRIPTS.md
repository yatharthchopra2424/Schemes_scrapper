# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping agricultural input retailers and farming cooperatives access government incentives that reduce input costs and promote sustainable practices. Given your work in the agriculture sector, I wanted to explore whether you might benefit from the PM-PRANAM initiative, which supports efforts to reduce chemical fertiliser use through alternative nutrient management.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is the primary focus of your agricultural input business — do you manufacture, distribute, or retail fertilisers or pesticides?
2. Are you currently promoting or planning to promote alternative fertilisers such as organic, bio-fertilisers, or micronutrient-based products?
3. What is your annual turnover from agricultural input sales, and has it been under ₹250 crore in any of the last three financial years?
4. Is your business registered as a proprietorship, partnership, LLP, or company under Indian law?
5. Have you undertaken any initiatives in the past two years to reduce chemical fertiliser sales or promote integrated nutrient management?
6. Do you maintain records of input sales that could demonstrate a shift from chemical to non-chemical fertilisers?
7. Are you willing to collaborate with state agricultural departments or nodal agencies on reporting and verification?
8. Have you previously applied for any central or state government subsidy or incentive scheme related to agriculture inputs?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Business engaged in manufacture, distribution, or retail of agricultural inputs (fertilisers/pesticides)
- [ ] Annual turnover from agricultural inputs ≤ ₹250 crore in at least one of the last three financial years
- [ ] Registered as a legal entity in India (proprietorship, partnership, LLP, or company)
- [ ] Involved in promotion or sale of non-chemical fertilisers (organic, bio-fertilisers, micronutrients)
- [ ] Undertaken initiatives to reduce chemical fertiliser use or promote integrated nutrient management
- [ ] Willing to report data and collaborate with state nodal agencies for verification
- [ ] Maintains verifiable sales records of input products
- [ ] Not blacklisted or debarred from any government scheme

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your business aligns with the goals of the PM-PRANAM scheme — particularly your focus on promoting alternative fertilisers and reducing chemical input reliance. The scheme offers financial incentives to eligible input retailers like yours for verified efforts in sustainable nutrient management. Let me explain how it works.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Agricultural input businesses face mounting pressure to reduce chemical fertiliser use due to environmental concerns and soil health degradation, yet transitioning to alternative fertilisers often involves higher costs, lower margins, and limited customer demand — making it difficult to invest in sustainable practices without financial support.

**Scheme intro:**
The PM-PRANAM (Prime Minister Programme for Restoration, Awareness, Nourishment and Amelioration of Mother Earth) is a central sector scheme administered by the Department of Fertilizers, Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers. It provides financial incentives to eligible agricultural input retailers based on verified reduction in chemical fertiliser sales and promotion of alternative fertilisers, with incentives calculated as a percentage of the savings generated.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your business sells agricultural inputs and has a turnover under ₹250 crore in at least one of the last three financial years  
- You are actively promoting or planning to promote non-chemical fertilisers such as organic or bio-fertilisers  
- You have undertaken initiatives to reduce chemical fertiliser use or improve nutrient management practices  
- You are registered as a legal entity in India and willing to collaborate with state agricultural departments for verification
