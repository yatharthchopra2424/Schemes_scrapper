### Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://pmmsy.dof.gov.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open (proposals can be submitted throughout the year as per State/UT-specific windows aligned with financial year cycles)

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Fishers, fish farmers, fish vendors, fish workers, SC/ST, women, entrepreneurs, startups, self-help groups, cooperatives, NGOs, private companies, state governments, union territories, and other entities engaged in fisheries and aquaculture activities
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Fisheries and aquaculture
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Subject to state-specific guidelines and implementation through District Fisheries Officers

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Central subsidy ranging from 40% to 60% of the project cost for general category beneficiaries, and 50% to 70% for SC/ST, women, and beneficiaries from North Eastern and Himalayan states
- Disbursement mode: Released to State Implementing Agencies based on approved Detailed Project Reports (DPRs); funds released in installments based on physical and financial progress
- Duration of benefit: One-time (per approved project)

#### Documents Required
1. Detailed Project Report (DPR)
2. Land ownership/lease documents
3. Bank account details
4. PAN/Aadhaar of beneficiary
5. Quotations for equipment and civil works
6. No Objection Certificate (NOC) from concerned departments (if applicable)
7. Environmental clearance (if required)
8. Beneficiary contribution proof
9. Photographs of site before and after intervention

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Identify the activity and prepare a Detailed Project Report (DPR) as per guidelines.
2. Submit the DPR to the District Fisheries Officer (DFO) of the respective district.
3. DFO forwards the proposal to the State Fisheries Department for technical and financial appraisal.
4. State Level Sanctioning Committee (SLSC) approves the proposal.
5. Approved proposal is sent to the Department of Fisheries, Government of India for final approval and fund release.
6. Funds are released to the State Implementing Agency in installments based on physical and financial progress.
7. Beneficiary implements the project and submits utilization certificates and completion reports.
