# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Pradhan Mantri Matsya Sampada Yojana (PMMSY) is a flagship central sector scheme launched by the Government of India under the Aatmanirbhar Bharat Package. Implemented by the Department of Fisheries, Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry and Dairying, it aims for holistic development of the fisheries and aquaculture sector across India. The scheme operates on a rolling basis with proposals accepted throughout the year, aligned with State/UT-specific financial year windows. The total fund size is ₹20,050 crore, with financial support provided as central subsidy ranging from 40% to 60% for general category beneficiaries and 50% to 70% for SC/ST, women, and beneficiaries from North Eastern and Himalayan states. The scheme is implemented through the Centrally Sponsored Scheme mode, with funds released to State Implementing Agencies based on approved Detailed Project Reports (DPRs).

### Objectives
PMMSY seeks to:
- Harness fisheries potential in a sustainable, responsible, inclusive, and equitable manner.
- Enhance fish production and productivity through expansion, intensification, diversification, and productive utilization of land and water.
- Modernize and strengthen the value chain through post-harvest management and quality improvement.
- Double the incomes of fishers and fish farmers through employment generation and livelihood support.
- Enhance contribution to agriculture GVA and exports.
- Ensure social, physical, and economic security for fishers and fish farmers.
- Establish a robust fisheries management and regulatory framework.
- Facilitate private investment and entrepreneurship development in the sector.

### Eligibility Matrix
| Beneficiary Category | Eligible Entities | Notes |
|----------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Individuals | Fishers, fish farmers, fish vendors, fish workers, SC/ST, women, entrepreneurs | Subject to state-specific guidelines |
| Groups | Self-help groups, cooperatives, NGOs | Must be engaged in fisheries/aquaculture activities |
| Enterprises | Startups, private companies | Includes MSMEs |
| Government Bodies | State governments, Union Territories | Through District Fisheries Officers |
| Other Entities | Any entity engaged in fisheries and aquaculture activities | Must comply with state fisheries acts, environmental norms, and CRZ guidelines |

**Target Beneficiaries**: Fishers, fish farmers, entrepreneurs, coastal communities, women, SC/ST, MSMEs, startups.

### Benefits & Financial Support
| Activity Type | Financial Support (Subsidy) | Details |
|---------------|-----------------------------|---------|
| General Category Beneficiaries | 40% to 60% of project cost | Central subsidy under CSS mode |
| SC/ST, Women, NE & Himalayan States | 50% to 70% of project cost | Higher subsidy for disadvantaged groups |
| Supported Activities | Construction of ponds, cages, hatcheries, biofloc units, ice plants, cold storage, fish kiosks, value addition units, aquaculture in saline/alkaline lands, seaweed cultivation, ornamental fisheries, integrated aquaparks, nucleus breeding centres, fishing vessel upgradation, bio-toilets, safety kits, livelihood and nutritional support during fishing bans, training and skill development, technology adoption (RAS, biofloc, aquaponics, IMTA), market linkage, branding, export promotion | As per DPR guidelines |

**Key Financial Notes**:
- Subsidy released only after physical verification and submission of utilization certificates.
- Beneficiary must bear the non-subsidiary portion of the project cost.
- Central assistance limited to approved cost; cost overruns borne by beneficiary.
- No similar subsidy for same activity from other central/state schemes.

### Required Documents
1. Detailed Project Report (DPR)
2. Land ownership/lease documents
3. Bank account details
4. PAN/Aadhaar of beneficiary
5. Quotations for equipment and civil works
6. No Objection Certificate (NOC) from concerned departments (if applicable)
7. Environmental clearance (if required)
8. Beneficiary contribution proof
9. Photographs of site before and after intervention

### Application Process (Mermaid Flowchart)
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Identify Activity & Prepare DPR] --> B[Submit DPR to District Fisheries Officer (DFO)]
    B --> C[DFO Forwards to State Fisheries Department]
    C --> D[State Level Sanctioning Committee (SLSC) Appraisal & Approval]
    D --> E[Send to Department of Fisheries, GoI for Final Approval]
    E --> F[Funds Released to State Implementing Agency in Installments]
    F --> G[Beneficiary Implements Project]
    G --> H[Submit Utilization Certificates & Completion Reports]
    H --> I[Subsidy Released Post Physical Verification]
```

**Key Caveats**:
> - Subsidy is released only after physical verification and submission of utilization certificates  
> - Beneficiary must bear the non-subsidiary portion of the project cost  
> - Projects must comply with state fisheries acts, environmental norms, and coastal regulation zone (CRZ) guidelines  
> - Central assistance is limited to the approved cost; any cost overrun is borne by the beneficiary  
> - Beneficiary cannot avail similar subsidy for the same activity from other central or state schemes
