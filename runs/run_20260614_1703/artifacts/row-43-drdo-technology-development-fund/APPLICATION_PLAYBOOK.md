# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] The applicant is an Indian industry (private, government, DPSU, or PSU) engaged in defence manufacturing.
- [ ] The applicant is an MSME or startup (as per the scheme's focus on Indian industries, especially MSMEs and startups).
- [ ] The proposed project aligns with DRDO's technology development priorities (indigenisation of defence products, subsystems, components, or development of new technologies as required by DRDO, Services, and DPSUs).
- [ ] The project cost does not exceed Rs 10 Cr per project.
- [ ] The applicant is prepared to undertake developmental work and accept attendant financial and commercial risks.
- [ ] The applicant adheres to prevailing Government of India policies and regulations on Foreign Direct Investment (FDI).
- [ ] The applicant has the capacity and capability to undertake the proposed technology development work.
- [ ] The applicant is willing to comply with government policies, including undertaking for compliance with government policies.
- [ ] The applicant agrees to the royalty structure: 2% for sales in Indian commercial market and exports; no ToT fee for Development cum Production Partner (DcPP)/Development Partner (DP)/Production Agency (PA); one-time ToT fee @5% of total project sanction cost for other industries.
- [ ] The applicant understands that funding is subject to technical and financial evaluation by DRDO experts.
- [ ] The applicant acknowledges that intellectual property rights may be governed by DRDO's IPR policy.
- [ ] The applicant accepts that testing and certification support may be subject to availability and prior approval.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Company registration documents (Memorandum of Association, Certificate of Incorporation)
- PAN card of the company and authorized signatory
- Audited financial statements (Balance Sheet, Profit & Loss Account) for the preceding three years
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for the preceding three years
- GST registration certificate
- Bank account details (cancelled cheque or bank statement)
- Udyam Registration Certificate (for MSMEs)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Project proposal document
- Technical scope and specifications
- Detailed budget breakdown
- Timeline and milestones
- Expected outcomes and deliverables
- Tax clearance certificates
- Undertaking for compliance with government policies
- Reference letter from college/institution (if applicable for academic collaboration)
- Certificates of registration as a manufacturing unit
- Details of shareholding/ownership pattern

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format, and clearly named (e.g., "Company_Name_Audited_FY2023-24.pdf").
- File size for each document should not exceed 5 MB unless otherwise specified.
- Names on documents must match exactly as per the company registration and PAN.
- Undertakings and declarations must be signed and dated by the authorized signatory.
- Financial statements and ITRs must be certified by a Chartered Accountant.
- All documents must be in English or accompanied by a certified translation.
- Undertaking for compliance with government policies must be on company letterhead and notarized if required.

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required for this scheme. Based on the evidence, the project proposal must include:  
- Technical scope and specifications  
- Detailed budget breakdown  
- Timeline and milestones  
- Expected outcomes and deliverables  
- Alignment with TDF objectives (indigenisation, IDDM products, defence R&D ecosystem strengthening)  
- Details of shareholding/ownership pattern  
- Undertaking for compliance with government policies  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application portal at https://tdf.drdo.gov.in and register as a new user if applying for the first time.  
2. Ensure all mandatory fields in the online form are completed; incomplete forms will not be accepted.  
3. Upload all required documents in PDF format, ensuring file names are clear and match the checklist (e.g., "Project_Proposal_TDF.pdf").  
4. Double-check that the project cost does not exceed Rs 10 Cr and that the requested TDF funding is up to 90% of the total cost.  
5. In the "Expected Outcomes" section, clearly link the project to DRDO's technology development priorities and the goal of achieving self-reliance in defence technologies.  
6. Attach the undertaking for compliance with government policies as a separate document; do not embed it in the proposal unless specified.  
7. Verify that the authorized signatory has signed and dated all declarations and undertakings before upload.  
8. Before final submission, preview the entire application to confirm all sections are complete and documents are attached.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All sections of the application form are fully completed.  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents are attached and clearly labeled.  
- [ ] The project proposal includes technical scope, budget, timeline, milestones, and expected outcomes.  
- [ ] The undertaking for compliance with government policies is signed and dated.  
- [ ] Financial documents (audited statements, ITRs) are CA-certified and for the last three years.  
- [ ] The requested TDF funding does not exceed 90% of the project cost, and the project cost is ≤ Rs 10 Cr.  
- [ ] The applicant’s registration type (MSME/startup/industry) is correctly reflected in the form and supported by Udyam or incorporation documents.  
- [ ] Declarations regarding FDI compliance and IPR awareness are included.  
- [ ] The application is free of spelling/formatting errors and uses professional language.

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the portal https://tdf.drdo.gov.in.  
- Immediately after submission, take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing the application reference number and timestamp.  
- Store the reference number securely in the case file and set a calendar reminder for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days post-submission.  
- Notify the client of successful submission and share the reference number.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and securely store the official approval letter from DRDO specifying the sanctioned amount and project duration.  
- [ ] Verify that the sanctioned amount aligns with the approved project cost and TDF’s 90% funding limit (max Rs 10 Cr).  
- [ ] Sign the formal agreement with DRDO outlining milestones, tranche release schedule, IPR terms, and compliance obligations.  
- [ ] Utilize the first financial tranche only after agreement execution and as per the approved milestone plan.  
- [ ] Submit periodic progress reports (technical and financial) to DRDO as stipulated in the agreement.  
- [ ] Ensure proper utilization of funds for approved project activities only; maintain auditable records.  
- [ ] Prepare and submit the Utilization Certificate (UC) upon project completion, duly audited by a Chartered Accountant.  
- [ ] Submit the final project report, including outcomes, deliverables, and technology transfer details.  
- [ ] Coordinate with DRDO for testing, certification, or handholding support as needed during project execution.  
- [ ] Adhere to royalty and ToT obligations: 2% on sales in Indian commercial market and exports; applicable ToT fee based on partner type (DcPP/DP/PA vs. other industries).  
- [ ] Upon successful closure, discuss opportunities for follow-on projects or technology dissemination with DRDO.  
- [ ] Invoice the success fee as per the consultancy agreement with the client.
