# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping Indian MSMEs and startups in the defence sector access government funding for technology development. The DRDO Technology Development Fund (TDF) is designed specifically to support indigenisation of defence products, subsystems, and components — exactly the kind of innovation many defence-focused businesses are pursuing. I’d like to understand your current projects and see if this scheme aligns with your goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your company’s incorporation date, and are you classified as an MSME or startup under government definitions?  
2. Is your current or planned project focused on defence products, subsystems, components, or dual-use technologies?  
3. Does your project align with DRDO’s technology development priorities, such as those outlined in their Make in India or IDDM initiatives?  
4. What is the estimated total project cost, and are you seeking funding for up to 90% of that cost?  
5. Do you have a detailed project proposal including technical scope, budget, timeline, and expected outcomes?  
6. Are you prepared to comply with government policies on Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) and DRDO’s IPR policy?  
7. Have you previously worked with DRDO laboratories or accessed their testing facilities?  
8. What are your expected deliverables and milestones for the project?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Indian industry, especially MSME or startup  
- [ ] Project focused on indigenisation of defence products, subsystems, components, or new technologies as required by DRDO/Services/DPSUs  
- [ ] Project cost up to Rs 10 Cr  
- [ ] Seeking funding up to 90% of project cost  
- [ ] Alignment with DRDO’s technology development priorities  
- [ ] Willingness to comply with FDI policies and DRDO’s IPR policy  
- [ ] Availability of required documents (project proposal, technical scope, budget, timeline, company registration, audited financials, tax clearance, etc.)  
- [ ] Undertaking for compliance with government policies  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your project aligns well with the DRDO Technology Development Fund’s objectives. This scheme offers financial support of up to 90% of project cost (capped at Rs 10 Cr), along with access to DRDO’s expertise and testing facilities — which could significantly de-risk your development journey. Let me walk you through how it works.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many Indian MSMEs and startups in the defence sector struggle with high R&D costs, limited access to advanced testing infrastructure, and difficulty in collaborating with DRDO labs — critical barriers to developing indigenous defence technologies and achieving self-reliance.

**Scheme intro:**
The DRDO Technology Development Fund (TDF), administered by the Defence Research & Development Organisation (DRDO) under the Ministry of Defence, Government of India, is a grant scheme offering financial support of up to 90% of project cost, with a maximum of Rs 10 Cr per project. It supports indigenisation of defence products, subsystems, components, and development of new technologies as required by DRDO, Services, and DPSUs.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are an Indian MSME or startup, which is the primary target beneficiary of TDF  
- Your project focuses on defence/double-use systems or components, aligning with TDF’s indigenisation objective  
- You are seeking funding for a technology development project, which TDF explicitly supports  
- You have (or can prepare) the required documentation, including project proposal, technical scope, budget, timeline, and company registration documents
