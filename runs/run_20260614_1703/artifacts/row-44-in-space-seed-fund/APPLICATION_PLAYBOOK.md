# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Entity is incorporated in India (Certificate of Incorporation required)
- [ ] Entity is a startup (as per DPIIT definition or self-declared early-stage status)
- [ ] Entity is working on innovative space technologies, applications, or services
- [ ] Project has potential for commercialization and scalability
- [ ] Entity demonstrates technical feasibility of the proposed technology/solution
- [ ] Entity demonstrates innovation in approach or technology
- [ ] Project aligns with national space goals (as defined by IN-SPACe/ISRO)
- [ ] Entity has not previously received funding under this specific Seed Fund (verify if re-application allowed)
- [ ] Entity possesses valid PAN
- [ ] Entity has a defined project scope suitable for prototype development, technology validation, or market discovery

> **Note**: No explicit turnover, employee count, or age-of-business thresholds are specified in the evidence. Eligibility focuses on innovation, technical feasibility, and alignment with national space goals. Verify current guidelines on the portal for any unstated criteria.

**Sources**:  
- Application Portal: https://www.inspaceindia.org/  
- Key Facts: Eligibility, Target Beneficiaries, Required Documents

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation  
- [ ] PAN Card of the entity  
- [ ] GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- [ ] Udyam Registration Certificate (MSME)  
- [ ] Bank Statement (last 6 months)  
- [ ] Income Tax Returns (ITR) of the entity for last 2 financial years (if applicable)  
- [ ] Authorized Signatory Letter / Board Resolution  
- [ ] Cancelled Cheque / Bank Details for NEFT/RTGS  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Detailed Project Proposal (including technical approach, innovation, commercialization plan, and budget)  
- [ ] Technical Feasibility Report  
- [ ] Budget Breakdown and Utilization Plan (milestone-wise)  
- [ ] Team Credentials and Experience (CVs of key personnel, roles, relevant expertise)  
- [ ] Documentation of Existing IP or Prior Work (patents, prototypes, research papers, etc.)  
- [ ] Any additional documents specified in the latest guidelines on the portal  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible, scanned in PDF format (unless otherwise specified)  
- [ ] File names follow convention: `SchemeName_DocumentType_EntityName_Date.pdf` (e.g., `IN-SPACe_SeedFund_ProjectProposal_ABCSpace_20240515.pdf`)  
- [ ] Entity name matches exactly across all documents (Certificate of Incorporation, PAN, Bank Account, etc.)  
- [ ] Documents are certified true copy (if applicable) or self-attested with authorized signatory  
- [ ] No password protection or encryption on files  
- [ ] File size per document < 10 MB; total upload size within portal limits  
- [ ] Documents are in English or accompanied by certified English translation  
- [ ] Project proposal and technical report include clear milestones, timelines, and measurable outcomes

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A formal Detailed Project Report (DPR) is **not explicitly required**, but a **Detailed Project Proposal** is mandatory as per the scheme guidelines. This proposal must include:  
- Technical approach and methodology  
- Innovation statement and novelty assessment  
- Commercialization plan and scalability strategy  
- Milestone-based budget breakdown and utilization plan  
- Timeline for prototype development, technology validation, and market discovery  
- Team qualifications and relevant experience  
- Risk assessment and mitigation plan  
- Expected outcomes and deliverables  

> **Note**: While not called a DPR, the proposal must be technically rigorous. Verify with the nodal officer if any specific format or annexures are prescribed.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Access the portal**: Navigate to https://www.inspaceindia.org/ and locate the Seed Fund section under “Schemes” or “Opportunities”.  
2. **Register/Login**: Create an account using the entity’s official email and verify via OTP/link sent to registered email.  
3. **Fill all mandatory fields**: Ensure no section is left blank; use “Not Applicable” (NA) only if explicitly allowed and justified.  
4. **Upload documents**: Attach all required files in PDF format, ensuring each file is under 10 MB and named correctly.  
5. **Double-check entity details**: Verify that the name, PAN, and incorporation number match exactly across the form and uploaded documents.  
6. **Save draft frequently**: Use the “Save as Draft” option to avoid data loss due to session timeout.  
7. **Review before submission**: Preview the entire application to confirm all sections are complete and documents are attached.  
8. **Submit only once**: Avoid multiple submissions; note the application reference number immediately after successful submission.  

> **Portal Note**: The evidence specifies submission via “designated online portal or email as specified.” Monitor the portal for updates; if email submission is allowed, use the official IN-SPACe Seed Fund email address (to be confirmed on portal).

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All sections of the application form are completed  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents are attached and correctly named  
- [ ] Entity details (name, PAN, incorporation number, bank info) are consistent across form and documents  
- [ ] Project proposal clearly outlines innovation, technical feasibility, commercialization potential, and alignment with national space goals  
- [ ] Budget breakdown is realistic, milestone-linked, and justifies grant utilization  
- [ ] Team credentials demonstrate relevant expertise in space technology or related domains  
- [ ] Declarations and undertakings (if any) are signed by authorized signatory  
- [ ] Application has been reviewed for spelling, formatting, and clarity  
- [ ] Final preview confirms no missing fields or unattached documents  
- [ ] Application reference number will be noted upon submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the official IN-SPACe portal: https://www.inspaceindia.org/  
- Upon successful submission, take a **screenshot of the confirmation page** showing:  
  - Application Reference Number  
  - Date and Time of Submission  
  - Entity Name  
  - Scheme Name: IN-SPACe Seed Fund  
- Save the screenshot in the case folder with naming: `IN-SPACe_SeedFund_SubmissionConfirmation_[CaseID]_[Date].png`  
- Store the application reference number in the case tracker and set automated reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days post-submission  
- Notify the client of successful submission with reference number and next steps

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the official approval letter from IN-SPACe specifying:  
  - Sanctioned grant amount  
  - Project title and objectives  
  - Milestone-wise disbursement schedule  
  - Reporting and audit requirements  
  - Validity period of the sanction  
- [ ] Confirm that the sanctioned amount aligns with the proposed budget and utilization plan  
- [ ] Ensure the grant is disbursed in tranches linked to milestone completion (as per scheme design)  
- [ ] Utilize funds strictly for approved project objectives (prototype development, technology validation, market discovery)  
- [ ] Maintain auditable records of all expenditures (bills, invoices, bank statements)  
- [ ] Submit periodic progress reports and utilization certificates as required by IN-SPACe  
- [ ] Coordinate with IN-SPACe for access to mentorship, testing facilities, or regulatory facilitation (if offered)  
- [ ] Upon project completion, submit final report, audited utilization certificate, and proof of outcome achievement  
- [ ] Follow up on invoice for success fee as per consultancy agreement (to be confirmed internally)  
- [ ] Explore opportunities for follow-on funding or scaling support from IN-SPACe or other government schemes  

> **Note**: The scheme emphasizes milestone-based disbursement and compliance with reporting/audit requirements. Non-compliance may result in recovery of funds or blacklisting.
