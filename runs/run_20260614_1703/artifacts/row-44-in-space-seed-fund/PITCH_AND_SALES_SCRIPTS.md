# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon — thanks for taking the time to speak. I work with early-stage space technology startups across India to help them access non-dilutive funding for prototype development and technology validation. Given your work in the space sector, I wanted to explore whether the IN-SPACe Seed Fund — which provides grant-based support for early-stage activities like prototype development and market discovery — might be a fit for your current stage and goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. When was your company incorporated, and are you currently registered as an Indian entity?  
2. What specific space technology, application, or service are you developing, and what stage of development are you at (e.g., idea, prototype, lab testing)?  
3. Have you begun work on a detailed project proposal outlining your technical approach, innovation, and commercialization plan?  
4. Do you have a technical feasibility report or proof-of-concept data to support your technology’s viability?  
5. Are you seeking funding specifically for early-stage activities such as prototype development, technology validation, or market discovery?  
6. What is the approximate budget you have in mind for your next milestones, and have you outlined a utilization plan?  
7. Can you share details about your team’s qualifications and relevant experience in space technologies or related domains?  
8. Do you have any existing intellectual property, prior work, or documentation that demonstrates your technical progress to date?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Indian-incorporated startup (Certificate of Incorporation available)  
- [ ] Working on innovative space technologies, applications, or services  
- [ ] Demonstrates potential for commercialization and scalability  
- [ ] Shows technical feasibility and innovation  
- [ ] Aligns with national space goals (as evidenced in proposal)  
- [ ] Has or can prepare: detailed project proposal, technical feasibility report, budget breakdown, team credentials, PAN, and IP documentation  
- [ ] Seeking support for prototype development, proof-of-concept validation, or early-stage product development  
- [ ] Not currently receiving conflicting duplicate funding for the same objectives (implied from caveats)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like you’re working on an early-stage space innovation with clear development needs — exactly the kind of project the IN-SPACe Seed Fund is designed to support. This scheme offers grant-based funding for activities like prototype development and technology validation, along with access to ISRO expertise and testing facilities. Let me walk you through how it works and whether it aligns with your goals.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Early-stage space startups often struggle to bridge the financing gap between idea and prototype — traditional investors hesitate due to high technical risk and long development cycles, while government grants are either inaccessible or lack sector-specific support. This leaves many promising innovations stuck in the lab, unable to validate technology or reach market readiness.

**Scheme intro:**
The IN-SPACe Seed Fund is a grant-based scheme administered by the Indian National Space Promotion and Authorization Centre (IN-SPACe) under the Department of Space. It provides financial support for early-stage activities such as prototype development, technology validation, and market discovery. The exact grant amount is determined by project scope and milestones, subject to fund availability and evaluation by the screening committee. In addition to funding, recipients receive mentorship, technical guidance from ISRO and IN-SPACe experts, access to testing facilities, and regulatory facilitation.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are an Indian-incorporated startup working on an innovative space technology or application  
- Your project demonstrates technical feasibility and innovation, with potential for commercialization and scalability  
- You are seeking support for early-stage activities like prototype development or technology validation — core focus areas of the fund  
- Your work aligns with national space goals, as reflected in your project proposal and technical approach
