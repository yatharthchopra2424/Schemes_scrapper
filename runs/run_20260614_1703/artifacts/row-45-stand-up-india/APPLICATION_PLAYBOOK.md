# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Borrower is an SC/ST and/or woman entrepreneur  
- [ ] Borrower is above 18 years of age  
- [ ] Enterprise is a greenfield venture (newly established, not an existing business)  
- [ ] For non-individual enterprises (e.g., partnership, company): at least 51% shareholding and controlling stake held by SC/ST and/or woman entrepreneur  
- [ ] Enterprise operates in manufacturing, services, agri-allied, or trading sector  
- [ ] Loan amount requested is between ₹10 lakh and ₹1 Crore  
- [ ] Promoter is prepared to contribute 15% margin money from own sources  
- [ ] Security will be covered under Credit Guarantee Fund Scheme for Stand-Up India Loans (CGFSIL)  

> **Note**: Loans are only for greenfield enterprises. Promoter contribution of 15% margin money is mandatory. At least one SC/ST and one woman borrower per bank branch is an operational target of the scheme.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, Passport, or Driving License)  
- PAN card of the applicant and enterprise (if applicable)  
- Latest ITR (Income Tax Return) – if applicable, or declaration of non-filing  
- GST registration certificate (if applicable)  
- Bank statements (last 6 months) of personal and/or business account  
- Udyam Registration Certificate (MSME registration)  
- Cancelled cheque or bank passbook copy for account verification  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Business plan or project report (detailing greenfield enterprise setup)  
- Details of the greenfield enterprise (nature of activity, location, investment plan)  
- Proof of SC/ST or woman entrepreneur status (caste certificate for SC/ST, or self-declaration supported by ID proof for women)  
- Bank account details (for loan disbursement)  
- Any additional documents as required by the lending bank (varies by bank)  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be clear, legible scans or photocopies  
- File size: Each document < 5 MB; total upload < 25 MB  
- File names must match document type (e.g., "PAN_Card.pdf", "Aadhaar_Identity.pdf")  
- Identity proof and address proof must show matching name and address  
- SC/ST certificate must be issued by competent authority (if applicable)  
- Woman entrepreneur status: self-attested declaration acceptable if supported by government ID showing gender  
- Business plan must be in English or Hindi; include project cost, means of finance, profitability projections  
- All declarations must be signed and dated by the applicant

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a **business plan or project report is required** as per scheme guidelines.  

**Standard Sections to Include**:  
- Executive Summary  
- Promoter Details (background, experience, SC/ST/woman status)  
- Enterprise Description (name, activity, location, greenfield status)  
- Project Cost Breakdown (land, building, plant & machinery, working capital, pre-operative expenses)  
- Means of Finance (promoter contribution 15%, loan amount 85%)  
- Loan Utilization Plan (term loan vs. working capital split)  
- Marketing and Sales Strategy  
- Projected Profitability (3–5 years)  
- Break-even Analysis  
- Employment Generation Plan  
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation  
- Implementation Timeline  

> **Note**: If the lending bank requires a Detailed Project Report (DPR) in a specific format, verify with the nodal officer or bank relationship manager.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Begin application only after verifying eligibility (SC/ST/woman, >18 years, greenfield enterprise)  
2. Use the official portal: **https://standupmitra.in/** – click “Apply Here” to start  
3. Fill all fields accurately; do not leave mandatory sections blank  
4. Upload documents in prescribed format (PDF/JPEG, <5 MB each)  
5. Ensure business plan clearly states it is a **greenfield enterprise**  
6. Specify promoter contribution as **15% of total project cost**  
7. Select correct enterprise type (manufacturing/services/agri-allied/trading)  
8. Declare SC/ST/woman entrepreneur status truthfully; attach supporting proof  
9. Save draft frequently; take screenshots before final submission  
10. After online submission, **approach a participating bank branch** with your application number and documents for further processing  
11. Follow up with the bank for loan sanction under the Stand-Up India Scheme  
12. Retain application reference number for tracking

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit application via **https://standupmitra.in/**  
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing application number and timestamp  
- Store the screenshot in the client’s case folder with label: “StandUpIndia_Submission_Confirmation_[CaseID]_[Date].png”  
- Save the application reference number in the case tracker  
- Set follow-up reminders:  
  - Day 3: Confirm receipt with bank branch  
  - Day 7: Check application status with bank

  - Day 30: Final follow-up before considering escalation

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify approval/sanction letter from the bank mentioning “Stand-Up India Scheme”  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned loan amount is between ₹10 lakh and ₹1 Crore  
- [ ] Verify that loan is structured as a composite loan (term loan + working capital)  
- [ ] Confirm promoter has contributed 15% margin money before disbursement  
- [ ] Ensure security cover is availed under Credit Guarantee Fund Scheme for Stand-Up India Loans (CGFSIL)  
- [ ] Collect disbursement confirmation and loan account details  
- [ ] Guide client on utilization of funds as per approved business plan  
- [ ] Monitor utilization and assist in preparing Utilization Certificate (if required by bank or scheme)  
- [ ] Verify invoice for success fee as per consultancy agreement  
- [ ] Advise client on handholding support availability through designated Handholding Agencies (HHAs) for operational guidance  
- [ ] Maintain records for post-loan monitoring and potential renewal/top-up under scheme guidelines  

> **Note**: Handholding Agencies (HHAs) provide support in business planning, marketing, compliance, and growth strategies. Clients can request HHA assistance via the Stand-Up India portal if needed.
