What is Stand-Up India?

Stand-Up India Scheme Facilitates bank loans between ₹10 lakh and ₹1 Crore to at least one Scheduled Caste (SC) or Scheduled Tribe (ST) borrower and at least one woman borrower per bank branch for setting up a greenfield enterprise.

This enterprise may be in manufacturing, services, agri-allied activities or the trading sector. In case of non-individual enterprises at least 51% of the shareholding and controlling stake should be held by either an SC/ST or woman entrepreneur.

Salient Features

Borrower Type
Loans to SC/ST and women entrepreneurs above 18 years.
Enterprise
Loans for setting up greenfield enterprise.
Loan size
Loans between ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore.
Nature of Loan
Composite loan (inclusive of Term Loan and Working Capital).
Promoter Contribution
15% Margin money.
Security
Security cover under Credit Guarantee Fund Scheme for Stand-Up India Loans (CGFSIL).

Even now when Kumar goes back home, he looks at the electric pole near his house with a banner he had painted as a teenager, directing everyone to the salon next door. FUNRALLY GAMES PVT LTD
Location DELHI / EAST
Activity Publishing of computer games for all platforms

Differentiator In 1991, at the age of 15, Siddhant Paswan became Siddhant Kumar, to avoid the shadow of caste discrimination. Kumar’s journey began with painting signboards, election banners, school walls. He enrolled in Jamia Millia Islamia University’s (Faculty Of Fine Arts) and later completed his masters in design from IIT Bombay. Kumar studied by day and painted walls and ceilings at night—earning Rs. 200 per day. With a friend from NIT, Bhopal, and another from NIFT, Chennai, Kumar founded FunRally Games. The company designs board games, card games, dice-based games and miniature war games —mostly exploring how to use different concepts to make instructional games and reinvent the traditional games from villages. Further they are into manufacturing and selling of daily used and household articles with denim decoring. “For initial years, we had absolutely no money,” he says. There were a few months when they had to stop working because they fell short of funds, by as little as Rs.10,000/-. “We had used every penny we had. Our parents’, our own money, everything was in it,” says Kumar. “While entrepreneurs in India face obstacles because of lack of credit from the formal banking system, potential Dalit entrepreneurs are doubly handicapped because they almost invariably lack the collateral and also because of their more limited access to informal credit through community networks. However with the launch of ‘Stand Up India’ he approached Vijaya Bank,Patparganj Branch for a bank finance of term loan of Rs. 25.00 Lakh for setting up Machineries like Sewing Machines, Computer Systems, Commercial Vehicle, etc. and also a working capital of Rs. 25.00 Lakh for day to day operations. On getting timely financial support, they have now set up their business and are accepting more and more orders. Kumar’s FunRally Games employs seven people and have projected an ambitious sales of Rs. 2 crore in this financial year. Even now when Kumar goes back home, he looks at the electric pole near his house with a banner he had painted as a teenager, directing everyone to the salon next door. And every time he looks at it, it strikes him that had he not got all financial supports, he would probably have still been the man with a paintbrush, painting people’s houses.
