# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian woman entrepreneur (individual)
- [ ] Applicant is a Self-Help Group (SHG) composed primarily of women
- [ ] Applicant is an NGO working for women's empowerment or related causes
- [ ] Applicant is engaged in manufacturing, production, or sale of products and/or services
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian citizen or entity registered in India
- [ ] No registration fee or cost is required to list products on the portal
- [ ] Business can be of any age (no minimum operational period specified)
- [ ] No turnover threshold or employee count restrictions apply
- [ ] Open to all sectors (handmade, homemade, homegrown products and services encouraged)
- [ ] Applicant has valid identity and address proof documents
- [ ] Applicant has an active bank account for receiving payments
- [ ] Applicant can provide product images and descriptions for listing

> **Note**: The scheme is open on a rolling basis with no deadlines. Registration and product listing are available throughout the year.  
> **Source**: [Mahila E-Haat Portal](https://www.mahilahaat.gov.in/)

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] Identity Proof (Aadhaar card, PAN card, or Voter ID)
- [ ] Address Proof (Aadhaar, utility bill, passport, or ration card)
- [ ] PAN Card (for tax and banking purposes)
- [ ] Bank Account Details (cancelled cheque or bank passbook copy)
- [ ] Udyam Registration (if applicable for micro/small enterprises)
- [ ] GSTIN (if registered under GST)
- [ ] Latest ITR (if applicable for income verification)
- [ ] Business Registration Proof (if applicable: Shop Act, LLP deed, MoA, etc.)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] High-quality product images (minimum 3–5 per product, clear background, well-lit)
- [ ] Detailed product descriptions (including materials used, dimensions, weight, price, usage instructions)
- [ ] For SHGs: Registration certificate and member list
- [ ] For NGOs: Registration certificate under Societies Act or Trust Act, and 12A/80G certification (if available)
- [ ] Sample product video (optional but recommended for better visibility)

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are clear, legible, and scanned in PDF or JPEG format
- [ ] File size does not exceed 5 MB per document
- [ ] File names follow format: `CaseID_DocumentType_Name.pdf` (e.g., `CASE001_IdentityProof_Aadhaar.pdf`)
- [ ] Identity and address proof show matching names and addresses
- [ ] Bank account details include account holder name, account number, and IFSC code
- [ ] Product images are in JPEG/PNG format, minimum 1080px width, and show actual products
- [ ] Product descriptions are free of spelling/grammar errors and include all key details
- [ ] Documents are in English or Hindi; if in regional language, provide certified translation
- [ ] No watermarks, logos, or third-party branding on product images (unless owned by applicant)

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Not required**. Mahila E-Haat is a registration-based marketplace and does not require a Detailed Project Report (DPR) or project proposal for seller registration.
- **Note**: If the client wishes to apply for complementary schemes (e.g., credit, training), a DPR may be needed — verify with the nodal officer or scheme guidelines in such cases.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
Follow these 8 critical rules when filling the Mahila E-Haat seller registration form:

1. **Access the correct portal**: Only use https://www.mahilahaat.gov.in/ — avoid third-party sites or agents charging fees.
2. **Use accurate legal name**: Enter the applicant’s name exactly as it appears on identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Voter ID).
3. **Match address details**: Ensure the address entered matches the address proof document (no abbreviations or variations).
4. **Valid contact information**: Provide an active mobile number and email ID for OTP verification and communication.
5. **Bank details accuracy**: Enter bank account number and IFSC code correctly — any error will delay payment settlements.
6. **Product listing compliance**: Upload only products you manufacture, produce, or sell; reselling branded goods without authorization may lead to removal.
7. **No prohibited items**: Do not list alcohol, tobacco, drugs, weapons, or any items banned under Indian law.
8. **Review before submission**: Double-check all fields, especially product price, description, and images, before final submit — edits post-approval may require re-verification.

> **Portal-Specific Note**: The registration process includes OTP verification via mobile/email. Ensure the client has access to the registered mobile number and email during application (Source: [Mahila E-Haat Portal](https://www.mahilahaat.gov.in/)).

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal details (name, DOB, contact) match identity proof
- [ ] Address entered matches address proof document
- [ ] Bank account number and IFSC code are correct and verified
- [ ] Applicant type (individual/SHG/NGO) is correctly selected
- [ ] Product images are clear, well-lit, and show actual items for sale
- [ ] Product descriptions include material, size, weight, price, and usage
- [ ] No prohibited or restricted items are listed
- [ ] Declarations regarding product ownership and compliance are acknowledged
- [ ] OTP verification completed successfully on portal
- [ ] Application submitted and reference number recorded

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- [ ] Submit the application only after completing all fields and uploading documents
- [ ] Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing "Application Submitted Successfully" or similar message
- [ ] Save the application reference number (if provided) in the case file
- [ ] Note down the expected verification timeline (typically 3–7 working days)
- [ ] Set a follow-up reminder for Day 25 to check application status
- [ ] Store all screenshots, reference numbers, and communication in the case folder (digital and/or physical)

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Collect and save the approval notification (email or portal notification)
- [ ] Verify that the seller profile is live and searchable on the Mahila E-Haat portal
- [ ] Confirm that products are visible with correct images, descriptions, and prices
- [ ] Guide the client on how to manage orders, update inventory, and mark items as shipped
- [ ] Advise on generating invoices for sales (platform does not generate them automatically)
- [ ] Remind client that Mahila E-Haat does not charge any commission or fee on sales
- [ ] Ensure client understands responsibility for product quality, packaging, delivery, and after-sales service
- [ ] Suggest enabling online payment methods (UPI, bank transfer) for seamless transactions
- [ ] Recommend periodic updating of product listings and adding new items to maintain visibility
- [ ] Track first sale and client feedback for case closure and success reporting
- [ ] Inform client about the option to renew or update profile annually (if required — verify on portal)
- [ ] Document utilization of platform (number of listings, views, inquiries) for impact assessment (if applicable for reporting)

> **Post-Approval Note**: Since Mahila E-Haat does not provide financial grants or subsidies, there is no utilization certificate or subsidy claim process. Success is measured by sales generated, income earned, and market access achieved.  
> **Source**: [Mahila E-Haat Key Facts](https://www.mahilahaat.gov.in/) – "Does not provide direct financial support, grants, loans, or subsidies. It is a free online marketplace..."
