# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping women entrepreneurs, self-help groups, and NGOs across India leverage government-backed digital platforms to expand their market reach without any upfront costs. The Mahila E-Haat initiative by the Ministry of Women and Child Development is designed specifically to support women-led businesses like yours in selling handmade, homemade, or homegrown products online — completely free of charge. I’d like to understand your current sales channels and see if this platform could be a useful addition to your growth strategy.

### Discovery Questions
- Are you an Indian woman entrepreneur, or do you represent a self-help group or NGO engaged in manufacturing, production, or sale of goods or services?
- What types of products do you currently make or sell (e.g., handicrafts, textiles, food items, organic produce)?
- Are you currently selling online, or are your sales limited to local markets, exhibitions, or word-of-mouth?
- Do you have a bank account in your name or the organisation’s name for receiving online payments?
- Do you have clear images and descriptions of the products you wish to sell?
- Have you faced challenges in reaching customers beyond your local area due to lack of marketing resources or platform access?
- Are you looking for a zero-cost way to list products and connect directly with buyers across India?
- Are you comfortable managing order fulfilment, delivery, and customer service independently?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian woman entrepreneur, self-help group, or NGO
- [ ] Engaged in manufacturing, production, or sale of products/services
- [ ] Has valid identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Voter ID)
- [ ] Has valid address proof
- [ ] Has active bank account details for payouts
- [ ] Can provide product images and descriptions
- [ ] Understands platform does not guarantee sales or fix prices
- [ ] Accepts responsibility for product quality, delivery, and after-sales service

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like Mahila E-Haat could be a strong fit for expanding your reach without any listing or transaction fees. Let me explain how this free online marketplace, run by the Ministry of Women and Child Development, works and how it has helped similar women entrepreneurs gain national visibility and direct access to buyers.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many women entrepreneurs, self-help groups, and NGOs struggle to reach customers beyond their local area due to high marketing costs, lack of digital presence, or dependence on intermediaries that reduce profits. Selling online often requires technical know-how, platform fees, or upfront investment — barriers that prevent talented women from scaling their businesses.

**Scheme intro:**
Mahila E-Haat is a free online marketplace initiated by the Ministry of Women and Child Development, Government of India. It enables Indian women entrepreneurs, self-help groups, and NGOs to list their products at zero cost, interact directly with buyers across India, receive online payments, and benefit from promotional support — without paying any commission or fee to the platform.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are an Indian woman entrepreneur, self-help group, or NGO involved in manufacturing or selling goods/services  
- You have identity proof (Aadhaar/PAN/Voter ID), address proof, and bank account details ready  
- You can provide product images and descriptions for listing  
- You understand that the platform facilitates connections but does not handle transactions, delivery, or after-sales service

As long as you’re an Indian woman entrepreneur, SHG, or NGO with valid ID, address, bank details, and product information, your registration is typically approved quickly. The platform is designed for ease of access, not restriction. |

| "I'll try it myself first" | That’s completely fine — many women do register independently. We help avoid these pitfalls to get you live faster and with a professional storefront. |
| "We're too small / too big" | There are no size restrictions — whether you’re an individual artisan selling a few handmade items or an NGO with a wide product range, you’re eligible as long as you’re an Indian woman-led entity. | "I don't have the documents ready" | The three most commonly missing documents are: clear, well-lit product images (multiple angles), detailed product descriptions (including materials, usage, and price), and accurate bank account details (account number, IFSC, and account holder name matching your identity proof). |
