# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Beneficiary is part of a Self-Help Group (SHG) under DAY-NRLM  
- [ ] Beneficiary is part of a Village Organization (VO) under DAY-NRLM  
- [ ] Beneficiary is identified as rural poor  
- [ ] Beneficiary is interested in setting up or stabilizing an early-stage enterprise in a rural area  
- [ ] Enterprise is in the early stage or is a new enterprise (not an established business seeking scaling)  
- [ ] Enterprise focuses on non-farm livelihoods, preferably skill-based work  
- [ ] Beneficiary lacks access to formal financial systems and dignified work opportunities  
- [ ] Beneficiary is part of the target group: rural poor, youth, or women  

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Identity Proof (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.)  
- PAN Card  
- Bank Account Details (passbook or statement)  
- Udyam Registration (if applicable)  
- GST Registration (if applicable)  
- Latest ITR (if applicable)  
- Address Proof  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- General information of entrepreneur  
- Education and work profile  
- Family profile  
- Income profile  
- Credit profile  
- Expenditure profile  
- SVEP training details  
- Business plan  
- Activity report  
- Consultancy slip  
- Loan repayment data  
- PTS (Performance Tracking System) data  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible and in PDF or clear image format  
- File names must match the document type (e.g., "Business_Plan.pdf")  
- Documents must be self-attested or certified by CRP-EP/BRC as applicable  
- Language: Hindi or English; regional language acceptable if accompanied by certified translation  
- File size: Individual documents < 5 MB; total upload < 50 MB  
- Photographs must be recent (within last 3 months)

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
DPR is **not required** at the individual beneficiary level. Business plans are prepared by CRP-EP/BDSP at the grassroots level and approved at the Block Resource Centre (BRC) level. Verify with the nodal officer (BRC/CRP-EP) if a formal DPR is needed for convergence with other schemes.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the portal via https://svep.nrlm.gov.in/login using credentials provided by CRP-EP/BRC  
2. Ensure all fields in the entrepreneur profile (6 stages) are completed: general information, education/work, family, income, credit, expenditure, and SVEP training details  
3. Upload the business plan in the designated section under “Business Plan” → “Pending” tab  
4. Attach the consultancy slip as proof of activity completion for each task undertaken  
5. Ensure loan repayment data and PTS data are updated regularly in the mobile app and synced to the web portal  
6. Verify that all uploaded documents are under 5 MB and in PDF/JPG format  
7. Confirm that the enterprise is tagged as “New” or “Existing” correctly (80% of supported enterprises are new)  
8. Do not submit the business plan if total project cost is zero—ensure all financial details are filled  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All 6 stages of entrepreneur profile completed  
- [ ] Business plan uploaded and in “Submitted” status  
- [ ] Consultancy slips attached for all claimed activities  
- [ ] Loan repayment data and PTS data updated and synced  
- [ ] Documents verified for authenticity by CRP-EP/BRC  
- [ ] Declarations signed (where applicable in the app)  
- [ ] Application reviewed for consistency (e.g., income vs. expenditure)

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page after submitting the business plan  
- Store the application/reference number generated by the system  
- Set a follow-up reminder for 15 days post-submission to check status  
- Monitor the portal for updates: “In Progress”, “Pipeline (awaiting approval)”, “Approved”, or “Rejected”

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and store the approval letter from BRC/OSF-MC  
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned CEF loan amount against the business plan proposal  
- [ ] Ensure loan disbursal is confirmed via PTS data and Nodal-CLF verification  
- [ ] Monitor enterprise performance through PTS for at least 6 months post-setup  
- [ ] Prepare utilization certificate for fund usage (as guided by CRP-EP/BRC)  
- [ ] Track hand-holding support from CRP-EP and mentors  
- [ ] Follow up on convergence opportunities with other government schemes (e.g., MUDRA, PMKVY)  
- [ ] Invoice success fee only after fund utilization and enterprise stabilization confirmation
