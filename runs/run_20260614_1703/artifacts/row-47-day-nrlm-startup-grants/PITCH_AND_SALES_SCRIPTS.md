# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I’m calling from [Your Firm Name], and we specialize in helping rural entrepreneurs access government support like the DAY-NRLM Startup Grants (SVEP-NRETP). This scheme is designed specifically for rural poor individuals—especially youth and women—who are part of self-help groups (SHGs) or village organizations (VOs) and want to set up or stabilize early-stage enterprises. I’d like to understand your current situation to see if this could be a fit for you or your community group.

### Discovery Questions
1. Are you currently a member of a self-help group (SHG) or village organization (VO) under DAY-NRLM?  
2. What is your primary motivation for starting or stabilizing an enterprise—e.g., income generation, skill utilization, or creating local employment?  
3. Have you already identified a business idea or are you in the early stages of planning one?  
4. What is your educational background and work experience—do you have any prior training in business management or soft skills?  
5. What is your current income profile and expenditure pattern—are you looking to supplement or replace your current livelihood?  
6. Do you have access to formal banking services, or have you faced challenges in accessing credit from banks or moneylenders?  
7. Are you interested in receiving mentoring and hand-holding support from a Community Resource Person–Enterprise Promotion (CRP-EP) for at least six months post-setup?  
8. Are you prepared to participate in the Performance Tracking System (PTS) and provide regular updates on your enterprise’s performance?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Beneficiary is part of an SHG or VO under DAY-NRLM  
- [ ] Beneficiary is rural poor, particularly youth or woman  
- [ ] Beneficiary lacks access to formal financial systems  
- [ ] Beneficiary is interested in setting up or stabilizing an early-stage enterprise  
- [ ] Enterprise is non-farm and skill-based (e.g., trading, services, manufacturing)  
- [ ] Beneficiary is willing to undergo SVEP training and create a business plan  
- [ ] Beneficiary agrees to performance tracking via PTS for at least six months  
- [ ] Beneficiary resides in a rural area covered by DAY-NRLM (Pan-India eligibility)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like you’re aligned with the DAY-NRLM Startup Grants’ focus on supporting rural poor entrepreneurs through financial assistance, training, and mentoring. Let me share how this scheme can help you access startup funding and support via the Community Enterprise Fund (CEF) to launch and stabilize your enterprise.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Rural entrepreneurs, especially women and youth from SHGs/VOs, often struggle to access startup capital, business training, and mentoring due to limited access to formal financial systems and lack of tailored support for early-stage enterprises. This gap prevents viable ideas from launching and stabilizing, keeping households dependent on irregular or low-income livelihoods.

**Scheme intro:**
The DAY-NRLM Startup Grants (SVEP-NRETP), implemented by the Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM) under the Ministry of Rural Development, provides financial assistance through the Community Enterprise Fund (CEF) for enterprise setup and stabilization. It includes training in business management and soft skills, mentoring via CRP-EPs, access to banking systems, and hand-holding support until stabilization. The scheme operates on a rolling basis via the portal: https://svep.nrlm.gov.in/landing.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are part of an SHG or VO under DAY-NRLM (confirmed in discovery)  
- You are rural poor, particularly youth or woman, lacking access to formal finance  
- You are interested in setting up or stabilizing an early-stage, non-farm, skill-based enterprise  
- You are willing to undergo SVEP training and participate in performance tracking via PTS
