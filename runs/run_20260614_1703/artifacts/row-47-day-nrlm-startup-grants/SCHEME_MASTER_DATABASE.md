### DAY-NRLM Startup Grants
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM), Ministry of Rural Development
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://svep.nrlm.gov.in/landing
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Early-stage or new enterprises in rural areas
- Age of business: New enterprises (startup) or existing enterprises seeking stabilization
- Sector: Non-farm livelihoods, especially skill-based work and small enterprises (manufacturing, trading, services)
- Revenue cap: Not explicitly mentioned in evidence
- Employee count: Not mentioned in evidence
- Other: Must be part of self-help groups (SHGs) and village organizations (VOs) under DAY-NRLM; rural poor, particularly youth and women; identified through community-based organisations; interested in setting up or stabilizing early-stage enterprises in rural areas; focus on those lacking access to formal financial systems and dignified work opportunities

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Financial assistance through Community Enterprise Fund (CEF) for enterprise setup and stabilization; specific quantum not detailed in evidence
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit via Community Enterprise Fund (CEF) managed at community level by community-based organisations
- Duration of benefit: One-time financial assistance for setup and stabilization, with hand-holding support for at least six months post-setup

#### Documents Required
1. General information of entrepreneur
2. Education and work profile
3. Family profile
4. Income profile
5. Credit profile
6. Expenditure profile
7. SVEP training details
8. Business plan
9. Activity report
10. Consultancy slip
11. Loan repayment data
12. PTS data

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Identify rural poor individuals through self-help groups (SHGs) and village organizations (VOs) under DAY-NRLM
2. Community Resource Persons–Enterprise Promotion (CRP-EP) conduct entrepreneur registration and profile creation in six stages (general information, education/work, family, income, credit, expenditure profiles, and SVEP training details)
3. CRP-EP supports beneficiaries in preparing business plans and activity reports
4. Business plans are reviewed and approved at the Block Resource Centre (BRC) level with input from mentors and functional experts
5. Approved enterprises receive financial assistance via the Community Enterprise Fund (CEF) for setup and stabilization
6. CRP-EP and BRC monitor enterprise performance through the Performance Tracking System (PTS) and provide hand-holding support for at least six months post-setup
