# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The DAY-NRLM Startup Grants scheme, also known as the Start-up Village Entrepreneurship Program (SVEP), is a centrally sponsored sub-scheme under the Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana-National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM), implemented by the Ministry of Rural Development. It aims to bridge the gap in capital and support for rural enterprises by promoting sustainable livelihoods and self-employment opportunities through financial assistance, training, mentoring, and access to banking systems. The scheme operates on a rolling basis with no fixed deadline and was last updated in January 2026. The application portal is https://svep.nrlm.gov.in/landing.

### Objectives
- Provide sustainable livelihoods and self-employment opportunities with financial assistance and training in business management and soft skills  
- Create a local community cadre for promotion of enterprises  
- Build and support early-stage rural enterprises by providing business development support, mentoring, finance, and access to the banking system  
- Guide convergence and integration with other government schemes  
- Democratise entrepreneurship by putting funding decisions into the hands of community-based organisations  
- Promote non-farm livelihoods, especially skill-based work and small enterprises, to create jobs and stimulate long-term economic growth  
- Produce social benefits through inclusive entrepreneurship

### Benefits & Financial Support
| Benefit Type | Details | Source |
|--------------|---------|--------|
| Financial Assistance | Provided through the Community Enterprise Fund (CEF) for enterprise setup and stabilization; managed at community level by community-based organisations; disbursement decisions made locally; revolving fund mechanism indicated by mid-term report tracking of CEF loan disbursement and repayment | KEY FACTS |
| Non-Financial Support | Training in business management and soft skills; mentoring through Community Resource Persons–Enterprise Promotion (CRP-EP); access to banking systems; guidance for convergence with other government schemes; hand-holding support until enterprise stabilization (minimum six months post-setup) | KEY FACTS |
| Enterprise Development | Support for creating a local community cadre for enterprise promotion; business development support; mentoring; access to CEF for credit linkage | KEY FACTS |

### Key Caveats
- Funding decisions are made by community-based organisations, not central authorities  
- Enterprises must be in the early stage or new to qualify for support  
- Support is contingent on enterprise stabilization and performance tracking via PTS  
- Beneficiaries must be part of SHGs/VOs under DAY-NRLM  
- The programme focuses on non-farm livelihoods and skill-based work
