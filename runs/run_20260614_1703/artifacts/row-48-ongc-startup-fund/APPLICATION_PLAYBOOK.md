# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Implementing agency must be eligible for implementing CSR projects as defined in Rule 4 of the Companies (CSR) Rules, 2014 and its subsequent amendments  
- [ ] Agency must be one of the following:  
  - ONGC Foundation (trust set up by ONGC for CSR activities)  
  - A company established under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013  
  - A registered trust or registered society established by Central or State Government  
  - Any entity established under an Act of Parliament or State legislature  
  - A company under Section 8, or a registered public trust or registered society, exempted under sub-clauses (iv), (v), (vi) or (via) of clause (23C) of Section 10, or registered under Section 12A and approved under 80G of the Income Tax Act, 1961, with an established track record of at least three years in similar activities  
- [ ] CSR-1 registration number is mandatory (copy to be enclosed)  
- [ ] NGO Darpan registration is mandatory for agencies registered under clauses (a) and (d) above  
- [ ] Agency must not be blacklisted on the NGO Darpan portal of NITI Aayog  
- [ ] Project must be a CSR project as per liberal interpretation of activities listed in Schedule-VII of the Companies Act, 2013  
- [ ] Project must be undertaken within the geographical limits of India alone  
- [ ] Preference given to projects benefiting marginalized, disadvantaged, poor and deprived sections of the community and environment, and contributing to Sustainable Development Goals  
- [ ] No direct contribution to Government initiatives unless specified by the Act/Rules  
- [ ] Administrative overheads must not exceed 5% of total CSR expenditure  
- [ ] Surplus from CSR projects must not form part of business profit and must be ploughed back or transferred per Schedule VII

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Organization Details (brief description, goals & objectives)  
- PAN Card copy  
- Audited financial statements of previous 3 financial years  
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable) or CA certificate stating GST not applicable  
- Bank statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (if applicable)  
- Cancelled cheque  
- ECS Form (Annexure 2)  
- Affidavit (Annexure 1) on Rs. 100 non-judicial stamp paper, duly notarized  
- CSR-1 Registration Certificate  
- NGO Darpan Registration (if applicable)  
- 80G and 12A Registration Certificates (if applicable)  
- Registration on NITI Aayog Darpan Portal (if any)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Details of similar projects ongoing/completed in previous three years  
- Impact assessment of previous projects in similar focus areas (if any)  
- Details of previous support received from ONGC  
- Details of largest project undertaken by agency in recent past with financial implication  
- Brief about goals and objectives of the proposed CSR project  
- Report of Need Assessment/Baseline Survey (if any)  
- Brief about targeted beneficiaries  
- Methodology of beneficiary selection and number  
- Details about physical and social milestones/deliverables  
- Detail timeline/duration of the project  
- Budget estimate with detailed break-up and documents proving rate reasonability  
- Monitoring mechanism for project activities and benefits  
- Documents depicting funding sources other than ONGC and amounts funded by each  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be in English or Hindi; if in local language, certified translation must be submitted  
- File size: Each document should not exceed 5 MB; combined PDF under 25 MB  
- File naming convention: Use clear, descriptive names (e.g., "Agency_Name_Audited_FY2022-23.pdf")  
- All copies must be self-attested by authorized signatory  
- Affidavit must be on Rs. 100 non-judicial stamp paper and duly notarized  
- CSR-1, 80G, 12A certificates must be valid and current  
- Audited financial statements must be signed and stamped by Chartered Accountant  
- Bank details in ECS form must match cancelled cheque and bank statement

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required. As per the application process:  
"Prepare Detailed Project Report (DPR) with need-based assessment, objectives, beneficiary details, timelines, budget break-up"  

Standard sections to include:  
1. Executive Summary  
2. Organization Background and Credentials  
3. Need Assessment / Baseline Survey  
4. Project Objectives and Goals  
5. Target Beneficiaries (demographics, number, selection methodology)  
6. Project Description (activities, methodology)  
7. Physical and Social Milestones/Deliverables  
8. Detailed Timeline (Gantt chart or month-wise schedule)  
9. Budget Estimate with detailed break-up and rate reasonability documents  
10. Monitoring and Evaluation Mechanism  
11. Sustainability Plan  
12. Funding Sources (ONGC and others)  
13. Annexures: Audited financials, CSR-1, affidavit, ECS form, etc.  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the ONGC CSR portal at **www.ongccsr.co.in** and register the implementing agency if not already done.  
2. Ensure all mandatory fields in the online form are filled; incomplete forms will be rejected.  
3. Upload all documents in PDF format, clearly labeled and within size limits (max 5 MB per file).  
4. Select the correct CSR project category aligned with Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013.  
5. Provide accurate bank details in the ECS form matching the cancelled cheque and bank statement.  
6. Ensure the affidavit is notarized and uploaded as a scanned copy.  
7. Double-check that the project duration, budget, and beneficiary details are consistent across all documents.  
8. Submit only after internal review gate confirmation; no edits allowed post-submission without portal permission.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All fields in the application form are complete and accurate  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents are attached and meet quality standards  
- [ ] Affidavit is notarized and on Rs. 100 stamp paper  
- [ ] CSR-1 registration certificate is valid and uploaded  
- [ ] Bank details in ECS form match cancelled cheque and bank statement  
- [ ] Budget break-up is detailed with supporting quotations or rate proofs  
- [ ] Project timeline is realistic and matches proposed activities  
- [ ] Beneficiary details include number, selection methodology, and demographic breakdown  
- [ ] Monitoring mechanism is clearly defined (internal/external, frequency, reporting)  
- [ ] No duplication with existing government or other funded schemes (as per affidavit declaration)  
- [ ] Authorized signatory has declared and signed all documents

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the approval letter from ONGC specifying the sanctioned amount and project duration  
- [ ] Confirm the fund disbursement schedule as per the agreement  
- [ ] Utilize funds strictly as per the approved budget and project activities  
- [ ] Maintain all financial records (cash book, ledger, bills, vouchers) for a minimum of 3 years as per affidavit undertaking  
- [ ] Submit periodic progress reports as agreed in the project timeline  
- [ ] Prepare and submit Fund Utilization Certificate (FUC) to ONGC, issued by a Chartered Accountant, using the format in Annexure-IV  
- [ ] Allow ONGC representatives to conduct audit/inspection of project documents upon request  
- [ ] Ensure impact assessment is conducted for projects with outlay of ₹1 Crore or more, as per scheme guidelines  
- [ ] Submit utilization report and outcomes for inclusion in ONGC’s annual CSR report  
- [ ] Retain all project documents for 3 years post-completion for audit purposes  
- [ ] Calculate and remit success fee as per consultancy agreement with the client
