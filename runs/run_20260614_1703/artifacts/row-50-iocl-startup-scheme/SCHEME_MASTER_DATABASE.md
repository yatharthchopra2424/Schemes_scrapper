### IOCL Startup Scheme
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Incubation
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Indian Oil Corporation Limited (IOCL)
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://iocl.com
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Registered entities under the Companies Act, 2013, LLP Act, 2008, or partnership firm
- Age of business: Not more than 10 years old from the date of incorporation
- Sector: Renewable energy, green hydrogen, biofuels, carbon capture, AI/ML for energy optimization, sustainable mobility
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Preference given to startups with a working prototype or minimum viable product (MVP); must be working on innovative products, services, or processes

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Financial support provided on a case-to-case basis, typically as grant funding, seed investment, or milestone-based disbursements for joint development projects; quantum depends on scope, technology readiness level, and commercial potential
- Disbursement mode: Grant funding, seed investment, or milestone-based disbursements
- Duration of benefit: Based on project milestones and mutual agreement; not fixed

#### Documents Required
1. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
2. PAN of the entity
3. Audited financial statements (if available)
4. Detailed project proposal
5. Proof of concept or prototype details (if any)
6. Team resumes and background
7. Intellectual property details (if applicable)
8. Declaration of compliance with eligibility criteria

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Visit the official IOCL website and navigate to the Startup Scheme section.
2. Review the eligibility criteria and focus areas.
3. Prepare a detailed proposal including problem statement, solution, technology readiness, team details, and potential impact.
4. Submit the application via the designated online portal or email address as specified.
5. Applications are screened by IOCL’s innovation and R&D team.
6. Shortlisted startups are invited for presentations or discussions.
7. Final selection is made based on innovation, feasibility, and alignment with IOCL’s strategic goals.
8. Selected startups enter into a memorandum of understanding (MoU) or project agreement with IOCL.
