# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Business is registered as a Micro, Small, or Medium Enterprise (MSME) under the MSMED Act, 2006  
- [ ] Valid Udyam Registration Certificate is held by the enterprise  
- [ ] Enterprise operates in the manufacturing or service sector (as per MSME classification)  
- [ ] Business has been operational for a minimum of 1 year (as per audited financials or Udyam registration date)  
- [ ] Annual turnover does not exceed ₹250 crore (as per latest audited financial statements or ITR)  
- [ ] Enterprise is not a subsidiary of a large corporate or PSU (independent ownership structure)  
- [ ] Promoters/directors have no history of loan default or willful default with any bank or financial institution  
- [ ] Business is engaged in activities eligible for support under PSU-linked innovation or entrepreneurship programs (e.g., technology adoption, market linkage, skill development)  
- [ ] Enterprise is not currently under investigation by any regulatory authority (e.g., SEBI, RBI, MCA)  
- [ ] No pending legal disputes that could affect fund utilization or repayment capacity  

> **Note**: Eligibility criteria for BPCL Ankur Fund are inferred from typical PSU-backed MSME support schemes due to low-confidence scheme details in evidence. Verify exact thresholds with BPCL’s nodal officer or official guidelines before proceeding.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation Certificate (Partnership Deed / LLP Agreement / MoA & AoA)  
- PAN Card of the Enterprise  
- PAN Cards of all Promoters/Directors  
- Aadhaar Cards of all Promoters/Directors  
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- GST Returns (GSTR-1 & GSTR-3B) for last 6 months  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of the enterprise for last 2 financial years  
- Bank Statements (primary business account) for last 6 months  
- Udyam Registration Certificate  
- Cancelled Cheque / Bank Passbook copy (for account verification)  
- Board Resolution authorizing application (for companies/LLPs)  
- Audited Financial Statements (Balance Sheet, P&L, Cash Flow) for last 2 years  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Project Proposal outlining innovation, scalability, and alignment with BPCL’s strategic interests (e.g., energy efficiency, sustainable fuels, digital transformation in petroleum sector)  
- Detailed Budget Estimate for fund utilization (item-wise cost breakdown)  
- Letters of Intent or MoUs from potential customers or partners (if applicable)  
- Technical feasibility report or prototype details (for product/tech-based ventures)  
- Profile of key management team highlighting relevant experience  
- Declaration that the enterprise has not received similar funding from other PSU schemes for the same purpose  
- Undertaking to utilize funds strictly for the approved purpose and submit utilization certificates  

> **Sources**: Scheme-specific document requirements are derived from standard PSU innovation fund guidelines (e.g., similar to SIDBI Fund of Funds, IIFCL, or CPSU venture schemes). BPCL Ankur Fund specifics are not fully detailed in evidence; confirm with nodal officer.

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (minimum 300 DPI)  
- File size for each document must not exceed 5 MB  
- File names must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ClientName_Date.pdf` (e.g., `BPCLAnkurFund_UdyamCert_ABCPvtLtd_20240515.pdf`)  
- All certificates (Udyam, GST, PAN) must be valid and not expired on date of submission  
- Financial statements must be audited and signed by a practicing Chartered Accountant  
- Bank statements must be stamped and signed by the bank authority  
- Non-English documents must be accompanied by certified Hindi/English translations  
- Promoter IDs (PAN/Aadhaar) must match names in incorporation and bank documents  
- Declaration and undertaking must be on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value (as per state stamp act) and notarized

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required for the BPCL Ankur Fund application.  
Standard sections to include:  
- Executive Summary  
- Promoter Profile and Business Background  
- Problem Statement and Innovation Solution  
- Market Analysis and Competitive Landscape  
- Technology/Process Description  
- Implementation Plan (timeline, milestones)  
- Marketing and Sales Strategy  
- Financial Projections (5-year): Revenue, Profitability, Break-even, Funding Requirement  
- Fund Utilization Plan (item-wise allocation of Ankur Fund grant/loan)  
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan  
- Alignment with BPCL’s Strategic Interests (e.g., sustainability, energy efficiency, digital enablement in petroleum sector)  
- Expected Outcomes: Employment Generation, Export Potential, Import Substitution, Social Impact  
- Annexures: Quotations, Technical Drawings, Certifications, MoUs  

> **Note**: Confirm DPR format and length requirements with BPCL’s nodal officer, as evidence does not specify exact guidelines.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official BPCL Ankur Fund application portal (verify URL from nodal officer or BPCL CSR/innovation wing)  
2. Register using the enterprise’s Udyam Registration Number and authenticated mobile/email  
3. Fill all fields in **Block Letters** as per supporting documents (no abbreviations unless standard)  
4. Ensure promoter names, PAN, and Aadhaar numbers match exactly across form, Udyam, and bank records  
5. Enter financial figures (turnover, profit, assets) as per latest audited financial statements (do not use provisional data)  
6. In the “Project Description” field, summarize innovation, scalability, and BPCL relevance in ≤500 words  
7. Upload all documents in PDF format only; ensure each file is <5 MB and correctly named  
8. Review the declaration section carefully; digital signature or OTP-based authentication may be required  
9. Do not leave any mandatory field blank; if not applicable, write “NA” or “Not Applicable”  
10. Take a screenshot of the final submission confirmation page before exiting  
11. Save the application reference number immediately upon generation  
12. If the portal times out, log back in and verify auto-saved data; re-upload documents if needed  

> **Portal Note**: Exact application URL is not specified in evidence. Consultants must confirm with BPCL’s nodal officer or CSR department.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the official approval letter from BPCL Ankur Fund (check for letterhead, reference number, date, and authorized signatory)  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount matches the approved utilization plan in the DPR  
- [ ] Ensure the grant/loan is disbursed to the enterprise’s designated bank account (verify UTR and bank statement)  
- [ ] Guide the client in utilizing funds strictly as per the approved project plan and timeline  
- [ ] Assist in maintaining proper utilization records (bills, invoices, work orders, payment proofs)  
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate (UC) within the stipulated timeframe (usually within 30 days of expenditure completion)  
- [ ] UC must be signed by the promoter, verified by a Chartered Accountant, and countersigned by the bank (if required)  
- [ ] Track milestone achievement and impact metrics (e.g., prototype developed, revenue generated, jobs created)  
- [ ] Coordinate with BPCL’s nodal officer for any mid-term review or site visit (if applicable)  
- [ ] Upon project completion, support client in submitting final report and impact assessment  
- [ ] Invoice success fee as per engagement agreement only after UC acceptance and fund closure confirmation  
- [ ] Archive all documents (approval, UC, correspondence, disbursement proofs) in case file for audit readiness  

> **Note**: Post-approval compliance requirements (UC format, audit, timelines) are based on standard PSU scheme guidelines. Confirm exact procedures with BPCL Ankur Fund nodal officer.
