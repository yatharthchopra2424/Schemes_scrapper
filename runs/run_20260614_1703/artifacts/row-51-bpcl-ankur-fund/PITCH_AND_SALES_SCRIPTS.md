# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping early-stage startups in the energy and allied sectors access non-dilutive funding through government-backed initiatives like the BPCL Ankur Fund. This scheme is designed specifically to support innovative ventures working in areas aligned with Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited’s strategic interests, such as clean energy, mobility, and sustainable technologies. I’d like to understand if your venture might be a fit — no obligation, just a conversation to explore whether this opportunity aligns with your current stage and goals.

### Discovery Questions
- When was your company incorporated, and what is your current stage of development?
- What sector or technology area is your startup focused on (e.g., renewable energy, EV infrastructure, hydrogen, biofuels, energy efficiency)?
- Are you currently seeking funding for product development, pilot projects, or scaling a proof-of-concept?
- Is your startup registered in India, and do you have a working prototype or MVP?
- Have you previously applied for or received any government grants or startup funding?
- Are you looking for non-dilutive capital that doesn’t require equity dilution?
- Do you have a clear plan for how the funds would be used to advance a project relevant to BPCL’s energy transition goals?
- Are you open to engaging with a corporate-backed innovation program that may include mentorship or pilot opportunities?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Startup incorporated in India
- [ ] Company age typically under 5–7 years (early-stage focus based on PSU startup fund norms)
- [ ] Operating in sectors aligned with BPCL’s interests: clean energy, mobility, sustainability, EV, hydrogen, biofuels, or energy efficiency
- [ ] Has a working prototype, MVP, or proof-of-concept
- [ ] Seeking funding for innovation, pilot, or scale-up (not pure idea stage)
- [ ] Indian-owned and controlled entity
- [ ] Not currently receiving duplicative funding for the same project from another central government scheme
- [ ] Willing to engage with BPCL’s innovation ecosystem (e.g., mentorship, pilot collaboration)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like your venture is working on [mention specific area from response, e.g., EV charging infrastructure] and is at a stage where non-dilutive support could really accelerate your progress. The BPCL Ankur Fund is designed exactly for startups like yours — offering grant-based support to innovative energy-focused ventures. Let me walk you through how it works and whether you might qualify.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Early-stage startups in the energy and sustainability space often struggle to bridge the gap between prototype and market due to high R&D costs, long development cycles, and limited access to patient capital. Traditional VCs may hesitate to invest at this stage, leaving founders to rely on personal savings or dilute equity too early. The BPCL Ankur Fund addresses this critical gap by providing non-dilutive grant support to de-risk early innovation.

**Scheme intro:**
The BPCL Ankur Fund is a corporate-backed innovation grant scheme administered by Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited (BPCL), a Maharatna PSU under the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas. It offers financial support of up to ₹50 lakhs per selected startup to develop and scale innovative solutions in areas such as clean energy, mobility, sustainability, and allied sectors. The fund is part of BPCL’s commitment to fostering indigenous innovation and supporting India’s energy transition.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your startup is incorporated in India and operating in a BPCL-aligned sector (e.g., renewable energy, EV, hydrogen)
- You have a working prototype or MVP, indicating readiness for pilot or scale-up support
- You are seeking non-dilutive funding to avoid equity dilution at this early stage
- Your project has clear relevance to BPCL’s strategic goals in sustainability and energy innovation
