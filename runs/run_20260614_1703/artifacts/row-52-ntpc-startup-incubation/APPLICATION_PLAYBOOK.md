# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] **Registration Type**: Must be a Bank or Financial Institution (FI) eligible to participate in NTPC's RFP process for term loans.
- [ ] **Turnover Threshold**: Minimum loan offer of ₹100 crore per bidder, in multiples of ₹100 crore thereafter (as per NTPC Mining Limited RFP).
- [ ] **Employee Count**: Not applicable; eligibility is based on institutional capacity, not employee count.
- [ ] **Sector**: Banking/Financial Institution sector; must be authorized to lend in India.
- [ ] **Age of Business**: Not specified; eligibility based on credit rating and compliance with RFP terms.
- [ ] **Other Conditions**: 
  - Must quote interest rate linked to MCLR or other market-determined external benchmark.
  - Reset period for interest rate must not be less than one month.
  - Bid must be unconditional and irrevocable; conditional bids liable for rejection.
  - Minimum offered loan amount: ₹100 crore.
  - Tenor: 12 years or more (door to door).
  - Moratorium: NIL.
  - Security: Unsecured, against negative lien on fixed assets.
  - Must submit bid in prescribed format (Annexure-I) via hand/post in sealed envelope; online/e-mail bids not accepted.
  - Must accept all terms and conditions of the RFP without deviation.
  - Must provide validity of offer for minimum one month from bid opening date.

> **Sources**:  
> - NTPC Mining Limited RFP for ₹500 Cr term loan (Ref: 01/NML Fin/Debt/2025-26/1, dated 27-01-2026)  
> - NTPC RFP for ₹1000 Cr term loan (last date extended to 12:30 PM of 09.03.2026)  
> - Scheme Key Facts: Financial Support section detailing loan covenants (3:1 total liabilities to net worth, EBITDA to interest expense ≥ 1.75:1)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
- PAN Card of the bidding entity
- Audited Financial Statements (last 3 years)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of the entity (last 3 years)
- GST Registration Certificate
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (if applicable, though primarily for MSMEs; FIs may not require)
- Board Resolution authorizing participation in the RFP and loan bidding
- KYC documents of authorized signatory

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Completed Bid Format (Annexure-I) on bank/FI letterhead, including:
  - Name of entity
  - Amount offered (in figures and words)
  - Tenor (12 years or more)
  - Interest rate quoted (annual, up to two decimal places, in figures and words)
  - Basis of rate of interest (MCLR or external benchmark)
  - Reset period (must be ≥1 month)
  - Validity of offer (minimum one month from bid opening)
  - Undertakings as per Annexure-I
- Draft Loan Agreement (for review; not submitted with bid but required post-award)
- Credit rating certificate (if available; NTPC Mining Limited has CRISIL AAA/Stable, CARE AAA; Stable)
- Audited annual financial statements to verify compliance with financial covenants (total liabilities/net worth ≤ 3:1, EBITDA/interest expense ≥ 1.75:1)
- Certificate of no default on existing loans
- Solvency certificate or net worth certificate from statutory auditor

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible, certified true copies where applicable
- [ ] File names match the document type and include Case ID (e.g., `CaseID_BankName_AnnexureI.pdf`)
- [ ] PDF file size < 10 MB per document; compress if necessary
- [ ] All financials audited and signed by CA
- [ ] Documents in English or accompanied by certified translation
- [ ] Signatures and seals present where required
- [ ] Dates are current and valid (e.g., board resolution not older than 6 months)
- [ ] Undertakings in Annexure-I are signed and sealed

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not applicable. This scheme involves bidding for a term loan facility, not a project requiring a Detailed Project Report (DPR). The loan proceeds are for general corporate purposes, capacity addition, refinancing, etc., as specified in the RFP.  
**Note**: Verify with nodal officer if any specific end-use certification is required post-disbursement (as per undertaking ix in loan agreements).

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Use only the prescribed format (Annexure-I) on the bank/FI’s official letterhead.
2. Fill all fields; leave no blanks. Use “NA” only if explicitly allowed.
3. Quote interest rate in **both figures and words** (e.g., "8.50% p.a." and "Eight point five zero percent per annum").
4. Clearly specify the **basis of rate of interest** (e.g., "Linked to 1-year MCLR of SBI").
5. State **reset period** explicitly (must be ≥1 month; e.g., "Quarterly" or "Every 3 months").
6. Confirm **validity of offer** is minimum one month from bid opening date (16.02.2026).
7. Ensure amount offered is in **multiples of ₹100 crore** and not less than ₹100 crore.
8. Sign and affix official seal; include name, designation, place, and date of authorized signatory.
9. Attach a copy of the board resolution authorizing the bid and setting the borrowing limit.
10. Do not alter the format or add conditions; conditional bids are liable for rejection.
11. Retain a copy of the submitted bid and obtain proof of submission (acknowledgment/receipt).
12. Verify that the total liabilities to net worth ratio and EBITDA to interest expense ratio of your institution meet the covenants (if applicable for internal assessment).

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and sanction letter from NTPC Mining Limited specifying loan amount, interest rate, tenor, and terms
- [ ] Execute the formal Loan Agreement on non-judicial stamp paper (as per draft provided in RFP enclosure)
- [ ] Fulfill all pre-disbursement conditions (e.g., submission of insurance certificate, board resolution for borrowing)
- [ ] Submit utilization certificate or end-use certificate as per undertaking (ix) in loan agreement: *"Company undertakes to submit end use certificate from the competent authority of the company, at the end of each financial year."*
- [ ] Ensure compliance with financial covenants (total liabilities/net worth ≤ 3:1; EBITDA/interest expense ≥ 1.75:1) and provide annual compliance certificate
- [ ] Track disbursement schedule as per mutually agreed drawdown
- [ ] Monitor repayment schedule (12 equal annual instalments, first due 12 months after first disbursement)
- [ ] Verify no prepayment or foreclosure charges apply (as per RFP and loan agreement)
- [ ] Confirm stamp duty and other statutory payments are made
- [ ] Maintain all records for minimum 5 years as per internal policy and regulatory requirements
- [ ] Submit invoice for success fee as per engagement letter with client
