# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Enterprise is located in a rural block (as per Census or state rural classification)  
- [ ] Enterprise engages in non-farm economic activity (manufacturing, trading, or services)  
- [ ] Applicant is from a rural poor household registered under DAY-NRLM  
- [ ] Applicant is a Self-Help Group (SHG) member or an immediate family member of an SHG member  
- [ ] Applicant is a first-time entrepreneur (no prior enterprise under similar central/state scheme)  
- [ ] Applicant belongs to a priority group: SC/ST, woman, or youth (if applicable for prioritization)  
- [ ] No prior financial assistance received under SVEP or similar entrepreneurship grant schemes  
- [ ] Enterprise is new and not already operational or previously supported  
- [ ] Applicant has identity proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID) and address proof  
- [ ] Applicant has an active bank account in their name  
- [ ] Applicant can provide a Detailed Project Report (DPR)  
- [ ] Applicant can provide quotations for equipment/machinery (if applicable to the business idea)  
- [ ] Applicant can provide proof of enterprise location in a rural setting  
- [ ] Applicant can provide a consent letter from family (if required by local implementing agency)  
- [ ] Applicant provides self-declaration of no prior enterprise under similar scheme  

> **Sources**:  
> - Eligibility: Targets rural poor households under DAY-NRLM, SHG members and families; first-time entrepreneurs from SC/ST, women, youth; rural blocks; non-farm activities.  
> - Required Documents: Identity proof, address proof, bank details, DPR, equipment quotations, rural location proof, family consent letter (if required), self-declaration.  
> - Application Process: Identification by CRP-EPs, feasibility assessment, DPR submission, Block Level Committee approval.  
> - Key Caveats: Only non-farm sector in rural areas; priority to DAY-NRLM SHG members; new enterprises only; subject to verification; loan repayment required.  
> - Portal: Applications processed through DAY-NRLM implementation structure; no central online portal — submissions via Block Resource Centres (BRCs) and Community Resource Persons - Enterprise Promotion (CRP-EPs).

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] PAN card of applicant  
- [ ] Aadhaar card (identity and address proof)  
- [ ] Bank account details (passbook or cancelled cheque)  
- [ ] Udyam Registration (if enterprise is already registered; otherwise, note as "to be applied post-approval")  
- [ ] Latest ITR (if applicable; otherwise, notarized income declaration)  
- [ ] GSTIN certificate (if applicable; otherwise, declaration of non-applicability)  
- [ ] Bank statements (last 6 months)  
- [ ] Passport-sized photographs (as per specification)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Detailed Project Report (DPR) – mandatory for appraisal  
- [ ] Quotations for equipment/machinery (if applicable to the proposed enterprise)  
- [ ] Proof of enterprise location in a rural area (e.g., village certificate, Gram Panchayat letter, or utility bill with rural address)  
- [ ] Consent letter from family (if required by Block Level Committee or CRP-EP)  
- [ ] Self-declaration of no prior enterprise under similar central or state scheme (on stamp paper if required)  
- [ ] DAY-NRLM SHG membership proof (SHG passbook, letter from Village Organisation, or BRC certification)  
- [ ] Caste certificate (for SC/ST applicants seeking priority)  
- [ ] Age proof (for youth applicants, if claiming priority)  
- [ ] Gender proof (for women applicants, if claiming priority)  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 2 MB per document)  
- [ ] Name on all documents matches exactly as per Aadhaar/PAN  
- [ ] Bank account is in the name of the applicant (or joint with spouse if applicable)  
- [ ] Address proof confirms rural residence (village, block, district)  
- [ ] DPR is prepared in the prescribed format (includes project cost, means of finance, profitability, marketing plan)  
- [ ] Equipment quotations are on vendor letterhead, valid, and include GST details  
- [ ] Consent letter is notarized if required by local implementing agency  
- [ ] Self-declaration is on stamp paper of appropriate value (as per state norms) and signed by applicant  
- [ ] DAY-NRLM SHG proof is recent and issued by authorised body (VO, BRC, or CRP-EP)  
- [ ] All documents are in English or Hindi; if in regional language, certified translation attached

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Required**: Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is mandatory for appraisal under SVEP.  
- **Standard Sections to Include**:  
  1. Executive Summary  
  2. Promoter Details (name, address, SHG affiliation, background)  
  3. Enterprise Description (activity, location, rural setting justification)  
  4. Market Analysis (demand, competition, target customers)  
  5. Technical Details (process, machinery, raw materials, technology)  
  6. Financials (project cost, means of finance, grant/loan utilization, profitability, break-even, ROI)  
  7. Implementation Timeline (milestones for disbursement)  
  8. Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan  
  9. Employment Generation Potential  
  10. Sustainability and Growth Plan  
  11. Annexures (quotations, identity proof, SHG proof, rural location proof)  

> **Note**: DPR preparation is supported by CRP-EPs and BRCs. Ensure alignment with SVEP guidelines and local verification requirements.  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Use only the official SVEP application format provided by the Block Resource Centre (BRC) or CRP-EP — do not use generic forms.  
2. Fill all fields in block letters; avoid overwriting or use of correction fluid.  
3. Ensure applicant name matches exactly as per Aadhaar and PAN across all fields.  
4. Clearly state the enterprise activity and confirm it is non-farm (manufacturing, trading, or services).  
5. Specify the exact rural location (village, block, district) and attach proof.  
6. Declare DAY-NRLM SHG membership status and provide reference number if available.  
7. Explicitly state that this is a first-time enterprise and no prior support has been received under similar central/state schemes.  
8. Attach self-declaration on stamp paper (if required) confirming no prior enterprise under similar scheme.  
9. Verify that the total project cost aligns with the grant (up to INR 1.5 lakh) and loan (up to INR 1.0 lakh) components.  
10. Sign the form in the presence of the CRP-EP or BRC official as witness, if required.  
11. Retain a photocopy of the submitted form for internal review and records.  
12. Confirm that all supporting documents are annexed and referenced correctly in the form.  

> **Portal/Process Note**: Since there is no central online portal, the application is a physical form submitted to the CRP-EP, forwarded to BRC for appraisal, and then to the Block Level Committee for approval. Follow local DAY-NRLM/SVEP office instructions for format and submission.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal details (name, address, DOB) match Aadhaar/PAN  
- [ ] Enterprise activity is clearly non-farm and located in a rural block  
- [ ] DAY-NRLM SHG membership is verified and documented  
- [ ] Applicant is confirmed as a first-time entrepreneur (no prior similar scheme benefits)  
- [ ] DPR is complete, financially sound, and includes all standard sections  
- [ ] Grant amount requested does not exceed INR 1.5 lakh; loan component does not exceed INR 1.0 lakh  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are attached and meet quality checks  
- [ ] Self-declaration of no prior enterprise is signed and notarized (if required)  
- [ ] Consent letter from family is included (if flagged as required by local agency)  
- [ ] Application form is filled completely, signed, and witnessed as per local protocol  
- [ ] Photocopies of all submitted documents and form are retained in case file  
- [ ] Reference number or submission receipt is obtained from CRP-EP/BRC upon submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Obtain a signed acknowledgment receipt from the Community Resource Person - Enterprise Promotion (CRP-EP) upon submission of the application and DPR.  
- Ensure the receipt includes: date of submission, applicant name, enterprise name, and CRP-EP signature/seal.  
- Photocopy the acknowledgment and store it in the case file with digital backup (scan/photo).  
- Note the expected timeline for appraisal (typically 15–20 days post-submission, as per BRC processing).  
- Set a calendar reminder to follow up with the BRC after 10 days if no update is received.  
- Maintain a log of all interactions (dates, officials spoken to, summary of discussion).

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the official approval letter from the Block Level Committee (signed and sealed)  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned grant amount (should not exceed INR 1.5 lakh) and loan component (up to INR 1.0 lakh)  
- [ ] Ensure the approval letter specifies the enterprise name, location, activity, and disbursement schedule  
- [ ] Facilitate bank linkage for the loan component — coordinate with the designated bank and ensure loan sanction letter is received  
- [ ] Verify that grant disbursement is released in stages as per project milestones (typically: 50% on approval, 30% on intermediate milestone, 20% on completion)  
- [ ] Monitor utilization of funds against the approved DPR and project cost breakdown  
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate (UC) in the prescribed format (GFR 12-A or state equivalent) after full utilization  
- [ ] Ensure all invoices and vouchers are retained for audit and verification purposes  
- [ ] Confirm that handholding support (training, mentorship, market linkages) is being provided by CRP-EPs/BRCs as per SVEP norms  
- [ ] Track enterprise progress against milestones and prepare monthly progress reports for internal and scheme compliance  
- [ ] Follow up on loan repayment schedule — ensure timely repayments to avoid default  
- [ ] Conduct final verification and project completion report submission to BRC/DRDA  
- [ ] Invoice success fee only after confirmation of grant receipt and loan sanction (as per firm policy)  
- [ ] Advise client on convergence with other schemes (e.g., PMFBY, PMEGP, or state rural livelihoods schemes) for scaling up  

> **Sources**:  
> - Financial Support: Grant up to INR 1.5 lakh (capital expenditure), loan up to INR 1.0 lakh (repayable), total up to INR 2.5 lakh; disbursed in stages based on milestones.  
> - Benefits: Handholding support including business planning, training, market linkages, mentorship, banking linkage, post-sanction support.  
> - Application Process: Disbursement of grant and facilitation of loan through bank linkage; post-sanction handholding support.  
> - Key Caveats: Loan component must be repaid as per schedule with lending institution.  
> - Utilization Certificate: Standard government requirement for grant funds; refer to GFR rules or state-specific formats.
