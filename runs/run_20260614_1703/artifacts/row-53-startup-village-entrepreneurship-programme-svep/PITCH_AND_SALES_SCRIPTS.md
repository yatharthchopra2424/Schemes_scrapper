# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping rural entrepreneurs access government support for starting or growing non-farm enterprises. The Startup Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP) is designed specifically for individuals from rural poor households, especially those linked to Self-Help Groups (SHGs) under DAY-NRLM, who want to launch ventures in manufacturing, trading, or services. I’d like to understand your background and goals to see if this scheme could support your enterprise journey.

### Discovery Questions
- Are you currently based in a rural block or village area of India?
- Are you or a family member a member of a Self-Help Group (SHG) registered under DAY-NRLM?
- Are you planning to start a new enterprise in the non-farm sector (e.g., manufacturing, trading, or services)?
- Have you previously received financial support under any central or state government entrepreneurship scheme?
- What is your primary motivation for starting this enterprise — income generation, employment creation, or skill utilisation?
- Do you have access to a bank account in your name for potential loan linkage?
- Are you a first-time entrepreneur, or have you operated a similar enterprise before?
- Do you belong to a marginalized community such as SC/ST, or are you a woman or youth entrepreneur seeking support?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant resides in a rural block (non-urban area)
- [ ] Applicant is from a rural poor household, preferably DAY-NRLM SHG member or family
- [ ] Enterprise is new and not previously supported under similar central/state schemes
- [ ] Proposed activity is in non-farm sector (manufacturing, trading, or services)
- [ ] Applicant is a first-time entrepreneur (priority given)
- [ ] Belongs to marginalized group (SC/ST, woman, youth) — priority criterion
- [ ] Has identity proof (Aadhaar/Voter ID), address proof, and bank account details
- [ ] Willing to prepare a Detailed Project Report (DPR) with support
- [ ] Enterprise location can be verified as rural

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like you may be eligible for the Startup Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP), which offers up to INR 1.5 lakh in grant and up to INR 1.0 lakh in loan support — totalling INR 2.5 lakh — along with training and mentorship. Let me explain how this could work for your situation.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many rural entrepreneurs struggle to start non-farm enterprises due to lack of initial capital, limited access to business planning support, and difficulty connecting with banks or markets. Without structured guidance, even viable ideas often fail to take off or sustain beyond the early stages.

**Scheme intro:**
The Startup Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP), implemented by the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India, provides a grant of up to INR 1.5 lakh for capital expenditure and facilitates a loan of up to INR 1.0 lakh through bank linkages. The total financial assistance can reach INR 2.5 lakh, disbursed in stages based on project milestones. Beyond funding, SVEP offers comprehensive handholding support including business planning, skill training, market linkages, mentorship, and post-sanction guidance to ensure enterprise sustainability.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are based in a rural block and planning a non-farm enterprise (manufacturing, trading, or services)
- You are (or a family member is) a member of a DAY-NRLM SHG, making you a priority beneficiary
- This would be your first enterprise, and you have not previously received support under similar central or state schemes
- You possess the required documents: identity proof, address proof, and bank account details

## 3. Objection Handlers

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I've heard these schemes never get approved" | SVEP has a structured village-level implementation process through Community Resource Persons - Enterprise Promotion (CRP-EPs) and Block Resource Centres (BRCs). Approvals are based on verified business plans and Block Level Committee appraisal. We’ve supported many first-time rural entrepreneurs through this exact pathway — especially those from SHG backgrounds — to successfully access both grant and loan components. |
| "How much do you charge?" | [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT] |
| "I'll try it myself first" | Common mistakes include applying for farm-based activities (which are ineligible) or submitting a business plan without proper quotations or location proof. We help avoid these by ensuring your DPR meets appraisal standards and your enterprise is correctly classified as non-farm and rural-based. |
| "We're too small / too big" | SVEP targets micro-enterprises at the village level. There’s no strict revenue or employee threshold, but the grant (up to INR 1.5 lakh) and loan (up to INR 1.0 lakh) are designed for early-stage, low-capital ventures. If your enterprise requires significantly more funding, we can explore convergence with other schemes for infrastructure or scaling. |
| "I don't have the documents ready" | The three most commonly missing documents are: (1) a detailed Project Report (DPR) with realistic cost estimates, (2) quotations for equipment or machinery (if applicable), and (3) clear proof of enterprise location in a rural setting (e.g., utility bill, gram panchayat confirmation). We assist in preparing all of these with support from local resource persons. |
| "We already work with a CA" | Great — we work alongside CAs. They handle your accounts and compliance; we handle the scheme strategy, application preparation, and liaison with implementing agencies like CRP-EPs and BRCs. |
