# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Startup Village Entrepreneurship Programme (SVEP) is a grant and loan-based initiative under the Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India, designed to promote sustainable livelihoods in rural areas by supporting village-level enterprises in the non-farm sector. The scheme operates on a rolling basis with no fixed deadline, accepting applications throughout the year subject to fund availability and verification processes. It targets rural poor households, particularly those registered under DAY-NRLM, with a focus on first-time entrepreneurs from marginalized communities including SC/ST, women, and youth.

### Objectives
- To support rural entrepreneurs in setting up village-level enterprises  
- To provide financial assistance and training for enterprise establishment  
- To promote sustainable livelihoods through non-farm sector activities  
- To strengthen the capacity of community institutions for enterprise promotion  
- To create employment opportunities in rural areas  
- To foster inclusive growth by targeting marginalized sections  

### Eligibility Matrix
| Criteria | Requirement | Details |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Geographic Location** | Rural blocks across India | Enterprises must be located in designated rural areas; urban or peri-urban areas are ineligible |
| **Beneficiary Profile** | Rural poor households | Must be registered under DAY-NRLM; includes Self-Help Group (SHG) members and their families |
| **Entrepreneur Type** | First-time entrepreneurs | Priority given to those without prior enterprise experience under similar central or state schemes |
| **Social Category** | Marginalized communities | Preference for SC/ST, women, and youth entrepreneurs |
| **Sector** | Non-farm economic activities | Eligible sectors: manufacturing, trading, or services; **farm-based activities are explicitly excluded** |
| **Enterprise Status** | New enterprise | Must not have received prior support under similar central or state schemes |
| **Documentation** | Mandatory submission | Identity proof, address proof, bank details, DPR, equipment quotations (if applicable), rural location proof, family consent (if required), self-declaration of no prior enterprise |

> **Key Caveats**  
> - Benefits are available **only** for non-farm sector enterprises in rural areas  
> - Priority is given to DAY-NRLM SHG members and their families  
> - Enterprises must be **new** and not previously supported under similar central or state schemes  
> - Financial assistance is subject to verification and appraisal of the business plan  
> - Loan component must be repaid as per the agreed schedule with the lending institution  

### Financial Support & Benefits
| Support Type | Amount | Disbursement Terms | Key Conditions |
|--------------|--------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Grant Component** | Up to INR 1.5 lakh | Provided directly to beneficiary | For capital expenditure; non-repayable |
| **Loan Component** | Up to INR 1.0 lakh | Facilitated through banking linkages | Repayable in installments; subject to bank terms |
| **Total Assistance** | Up to INR 2.5 lakh | Combined grant + loan | Disbursed in stages based on project milestones |
| **Handholding Support** | Comprehensive | Ongoing | Includes business planning, market linkages, skill training, mentorship, DPR preparation, banking access facilitation, and post-sanction support for sustainability |
| **Convergence Benefits** | Scheme linkage | Access to other govt. schemes | Facilitates convergence for infrastructure, marketing, and technology adoption |

> **Financial Notes**  
> - The grant is provided **directly** to the beneficiary; the loan is **facilitated** via bank linkage (not direct disbursement by scheme)  
> - Disbursement occurs in **stages**, tied to verified project milestones  
> - Loan repayment is **mandatory** and governed by the lending institution’s schedule  
> - Total financial assistance per enterprise cannot exceed INR 2.5 lakh

    D --> E[Approval by Block Level Committee]
    E --> F[Disbursement of Grant & Facilitation of Loan via Bank Linkage]

> **Process Notes**  
> - CRP-EP = Community Resource Person - Enterprise Promotion  
> - BRC = Block Resource Centre  
> - DPR = Detailed Project Report (must include project cost, viability, market analysis, and implementation plan)  
> - Block Level Committee approval is mandatory before any fund disbursement  
> - Post-sanction support includes monitoring, troubleshooting, and sustainability guidance
