## Pricing Principles
1. **Value-Aligned Fees**: Success fees are calibrated to the actual financial benefit delivered under the scheme, ensuring clients pay only for realized outcomes.
2. **Risk-Adjusted Pricing**: Given the low confidence rating in scheme data and limited evidence of direct financial disbursements (e.g., grants, subsidies), success fees reflect higher uncertainty in benefit realization.
3. **Transparency**: All fee structures are disclosed upfront, with retainers covering initial eligibility assessment and application preparation, and success fees tied to verified benefit disbursement.
4. **Scheme-Specific Note**: The Skill India Digital scheme (SIDH) focuses on skill development training and digital upskilling rather than direct financial grants or subsidies. Evidence does not indicate monetary benefits to individual beneficiaries or enterprises; instead, it emphasizes access to training platforms and certification. Therefore, traditional success fee models based on grant amounts are not applicable. Consultants should assess whether clients value certification, skill acquisition, or platform access as non-monetary outcomes and adjust pricing accordingly (e.g., fixed-fee for service delivery).
