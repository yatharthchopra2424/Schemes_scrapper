# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- ✅ The applicant is an Industrial Training Institute (ITI) or National Skill Training Institute (NSTI) under the administrative and financial control of a State Government or Union Territory Administration.
- ✅ The applicant is either a Government ITI, Private ITI, or NSTI under DGT’s direct control.
- ✅ The applicant is willing to participate in a hub-and-spoke consortia (HSIC) for training upgradation and industry linkage activities.
- ✅ The applicant is affiliated with the Directorate General of Training (DGT) or eligible for affiliation under DGT norms.
- ✅ The applicant is located in India (Pan-India scope).
- ✅ The applicant is prepared to develop and implement upgrading plans, including industry partnerships and curriculum updates.
- ✅ The applicant agrees to monitor progress via disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs) related to ITI governance, trainee outcomes, and industry linkage.
- ✅ The applicant commits to achieving verified results for fund reimbursement under the Program-for-Results (PforR) mechanism.

> **Sources**:  
> - Scheme Key Facts: Eligibility targets ITIs and NSTIs under State/UT Governments or DGT’s direct control, willing to participate in hub-and-spoke consortia.  
> - Application Process: States/UTs submit proposals through DGT; selected states identify and nominate ITIs/NSTIs for HSICs.  
> - Portal: https://dgt.gov.in/en (DGT Homepage – use for affiliation and scheme updates)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
- PAN Card of the Institute
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for last 3 years
- GST Registration Certificate
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (for MSME classification, if applicable)
- Affiliation Certificate from DGT / State Directorate of Training
- Board Resolution authorizing application
- Authorized Signatory Details

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Proposal from State/UT Government for participation in PM-SETU
- Commitment letter for hub-and-spoke consortia formation
- Details of selected ITIs and NSTIs for upgradation (including ITI/NSTI codes, locations, trades offered)
- Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Industry Training Partners (ITPs)
- Strategic Investment Plan (SIP) for NSTIs (applicable only for RA3 – NSTI transformation)
- Baseline data on ITI performance and trainee outcomes (enrollment, pass-out, placement rates)
- Plan for monitoring and evaluation of disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs)
- Environmental and Social Management Plan (if construction/upgradation is involved)
- Safety-First Cell establishment plan (for EHS compliance in workshops)

### Document Quality Check
- ✅ All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document unless otherwise specified)
- ✅ Document names must match the checklist (e.g., “MoU_with_ITP.pdf”, “SIP_NSTI_Location.pdf”)
- ✅ All certificates must be valid and not expired (check dates on registration, affiliation, etc.)
- ✅ Hindi/English documents acceptable; non-English documents must include certified translation
- ✅ Signatures and stamps must be visible on official letters and resolutions
- ✅ Bank statements must show institute name and be stamped by the bank
- ✅ Udyam and GST certificates must be downloadable from official portals with valid QR codes

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **DPR Required?** Yes, in the form of a **Strategic Investment Plan (SIP)** for NSTIs under RA3. For ITIs in hub-and-spoke consortia, a holistic five-year SIP with KPIs is required under RA1.
- **Standard Sections** (if DPR/SIP is required):
  - Executive Summary
  - Institutional Profile and Baseline Data
  - Objectives and Expected Outcomes (aligned with PM-SETU DLIs)
  - Hub-Spoke Consortium Structure and Partner Roles
  - Industry Partnership Plan (MoUs with ITPs)
  - Curriculum Upgradation Plan (NSQF-aligned, industry-relevant, green skills)
  - Infrastructure Upgradation Plan (workshops, classrooms, digital tools)
  - Faculty Development Plan (CITS upskilling, trainer certification)
  - Student Support Services (placement, counseling, remedial classes)
  - Monitoring and Evaluation Framework (DLI tracking, MIS, third-party verification)
  - Budget and Financial Plan (fund utilization, state/central share)
  - Risk Management and Mitigation Plan (environmental, social, safety)
  - Sustainability Plan (post-program continuation)
  - Annexures: MoUs, SIPs, baseline data, DLI protocol

> **Note**: If applying as an ITI under RA1/RA2, verify with the nodal officer (DGT) whether a full DPR or consortium-level SIP is required.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application portal via the State/UT nodal officer or DGT’s official communication (no public online form; proposals are submitted through State/UT to DGT).
2. Ensure all fields in the State/UT proposal template are complete — leave no blank sections.
3. Attach all scheme-specific and universal documents as per the checklist.
4. Clearly mark the hub-and-spoke consortia structure (lead ITI, spoke ITIs, NSTIs, ITPs).
5. Align all proposed activities with disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs): ITI governance, trainee outcomes, industry linkage.
6. Declare financial contribution readiness (state share: typically 25%, central: 75% as per similar schemes).
7. Sign and stamp the declaration form by the authorized signatory (Principal/Director of ITI or State Nodal Officer).
8. Submit via official channel (email/post) to DGT and retain acknowledgment.

> **Portal Note**: No public application portal exists; proposals are routed through State/UT Governments to DGT. Use https://dgt.gov.in/en for guidelines and contact.

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the complete proposal package to the **State/UT Skill Development Department** for forwarding to DGT.
- Obtain a **submission receipt or diary number** from the State/UT nodal officer.
- If submitting directly to DGT (via email/post), save:
  - Screenshot of email sent (with timestamp and attachments)
  - Postal tracking ID (if sent by courier/post)
  - DGT inward number (if received via post)
- Store all proof in the case folder: both digital and physical.
- Set follow-up reminders for:
  - Day 25: Check-in with State/UT nodal officer
  - Day 30: Escalation if no acknowledgment received

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- ✅ Receive and verify approval letter from DGT (check scheme name, sanction amount, validity period)
- ✅ Confirm sanctioned amount and disbursement tranche (linked to DLI achievement)
- ✅ Sign Program-for-Results (PforR) agreement with Government of India and World Bank (if State/UT is lead)
- ✅ Onboard ITIs/NSTIs into hub-and-spoke consortia (HSIC) and formalize MoUs with ITPs
- ✅ Begin implementation of upgrading plan: infrastructure, curriculum, industry partnerships
- ✅ Set up monitoring system for DLIs (trainee outcomes, placement, industry linkage, EHS compliance)
- ✅ Prepare for Independent Verification Agency (IVA) assessment for fund release
- ✅ Utilize first tranche of funds upon verified DLI achievement
- ✅ Submit utilization certificates and expenditure statements as per schedule
- ✅ Track and claim success fee (if applicable per consultancy agreement) upon fund disbursement

> **Sources**:  
> - Scheme Key Facts: Financial support via PforR; reimbursement against verified results; central-state sharing (75:25 similar to STRIVE/LWE)  
> - Application Process: States sign PforR agreement with GoI and World Bank; funds released upon DLI verification  
> - Contact: dirgen-msde[at]gov[dot]in | 011-24671101 (DGT Director General – Shri Dilip Kumar, per Who’s Who)  
> - Portal: https://dgt.gov.in/en (DGT Homepage – for updates, guidelines, and contact)
