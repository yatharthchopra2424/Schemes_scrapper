# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I’m calling from [Your Firm Name], and we specialize in helping Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) and National Skill Training Institutes (NSTIs) access government funding for upgradation and industry linkage. The PM-SETU scheme is designed specifically for institutions like yours that want to improve training quality, strengthen industry partnerships, and enhance trainee employability through hub-and-spoke consortia. I’d like to understand your current goals and challenges to see if PM-SETU aligns with your needs.

### Discovery Questions
1. What type of institute are you—Government ITI, Private ITI, or NSTI—and under which State/UT administration do you operate?  
2. Are you currently part of, or interested in joining, a hub-and-spoke consortium (HSIC) for training upgradation and industry linkage activities?  
3. What is your institute’s current NSQF compliance status, and are you looking to update curricula to align with industry demands or emerging technologies?  
4. Do you have existing Memorandums of Understanding (MoUs) with Industry Training Partners (ITPs), or are you seeking to establish new industry partnerships?  
5. What infrastructure upgrades (e.g., workshops, classrooms, digital tools) are you planning, and do you have a Strategic Investment Plan (SIP) in place for transformation?  
6. Are you able to provide baseline data on trainee outcomes, placement rates, or current ITI performance for monitoring and evaluation purposes?  
7. Has your State/UT Government expressed interest in participating in PM-SETU, or are you seeking support to engage them in the proposal process?  
8. Are you prepared to commit to achieving Disbursement-Linked Indicators (DLIs) related to governance, trainee outcomes, and industry linkage as a condition for funding release?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Entity is an Industrial Training Institute (ITI) or National Skill Training Institute (NSTI)  
- [ ] Under administrative and financial control of a State Government or Union Territory Administration  
- [ ] Willing to participate in hub-and-spoke consortia (HSICs) for training upgradation and industry linkage  
- [ ] Government or Private ITI (affiliated) or NSTI under DGT’s direct control  
- [ ] Prepared to submit proposal through State/UT Government to DGT  
- [ ] Able to provide commitment letter for hub-and-spoke consortia formation  
- [ ] Capable of delivering baseline data on ITI performance and trainee outcomes  
- [ ] Ready to develop and implement upgrading plans with industry partnerships and curriculum updates  
- [ ] NSTI applicants must be prepared to develop a five-year Strategic Investment Plan (SIP)  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like PM-SETU could be a strong fit—especially if you're looking to upgrade infrastructure, strengthen industry ties, and improve trainee employability through a hub-and-spoke model. The scheme offers financial support for exactly these activities, with funds released upon achievement of verified results. Shall I walk you through how it works?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many ITIs and NSTIs struggle with outdated infrastructure, weak industry linkages, and curricula that don’t match market needs—leading to low placement rates and limited trainee employability. States often lack the capacity to coordinate large-scale upgradation or monitor performance effectively, and private sector engagement remains fragmented.

**Scheme intro:**
PM-SETU (Pradhan Mantri Skills Enhancement and Training Upgradation) is a central sector subsidy scheme implemented by the Directorate General of Training (DGT), Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship. It provides financial support through a Program-for-Results (PforR) financing instrument backed by a US$900 million World Bank loan. Funds are released to States/UTs upon verification of achieved Disbursement-Linked Indicators (DLIs), supporting ITI upgradation, NSTI transformation, industry partnership facilitation, and system-level reforms under a hub-and-spoke model.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are an ITI or NSTI under State/UT Government control — a core eligibility requirement  
- You are willing to join or form a hub-and-spoke consortium (HSIC) for industry linkage and training upgradation  
- You can provide baseline data on trainee outcomes and ITI performance for DLI monitoring  
- You are prepared to develop upgrading plans and, if an NSTI, a five-year Strategic Investment Plan (SIP)
