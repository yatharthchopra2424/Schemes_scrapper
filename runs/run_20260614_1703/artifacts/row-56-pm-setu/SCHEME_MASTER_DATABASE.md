### PM-SETU
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Directorate General of Training (DGT), Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://dgt.gov.in/en/scheme-guidelines-of-pm-setu
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) and National Skill Training Institutes (NSTIs)
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Skill Development / Vocational Training
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Eligible entities include affiliated ITIs (Government and Private) and NSTIs under DGT’s direct control that are willing to participate in hub-and-spoke consortia for training upgradation and industry linkage activities.

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Financial support through a Program-for-Results (PforR) financing instrument from the World Bank, with a proposed loan amount of US$900 million. Central government allocates funds to states/UTs based on achievement of disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs).
- Disbursement mode: Reimbursement against verified results
- Duration of benefit: Based on achievement of disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs), tied to program cycles

#### Documents Required
1. Proposal from State/UT Government
2. Commitment letter for hub-and-spoke consortia formation
3. Details of selected ITIs and NSTIs for upgradation
4. Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Industry Training Partners (ITPs)
5. Strategic Investment Plan (SIP) for NSTIs (for RA3)
6. Baseline data on ITI performance and trainee outcomes
7. Plan for monitoring and evaluation of disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. States/UTs submit proposals for participation in the PM-SETU program through the Directorate General of Training (DGT).
2. DGT evaluates proposals based on readiness, infrastructure, and commitment to reforms.
3. Selected states/UTs sign a Program-for-Results (PforR) agreement with the Government of India and World Bank.
4. States/UTs identify and nominate ITIs and NSTIs for inclusion in hub-and-spoke consortia (HSICs).
5. HSICs develop and implement upgrading plans, including industry partnerships and curriculum updates.
6. NSTIs develop five-year Strategic Investment Plans (SIPs) for transformation into world-class institutes.
7. Progress is monitored via disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs) related to ITI governance, trainee outcomes, and industry linkage.
8. Funds are released to states/UTs upon verification of achieved results.
