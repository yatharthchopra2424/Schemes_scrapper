# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
**Scheme Name:** PM-SETU  
**Scheme ID:** row-56  
**Ministry / Category:** Skill Development  
**Scheme Type:** subsidy  
**Geographic Scope:** Pan-India  
**Implementing Agency:** Directorate General of Training (DGT), Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship  
**Status / Deadlines:** Rolling basis — proposals are accepted and evaluated periodically as per program cycles.  
**Last Updated:** 2025  
**Application Portal URL:** https://dgt.gov.in/en/scheme-guidelines-of-pm-setu  
**Contact Details:** Email: dirgen-msde[at]gov[dot]in | Phone: 011-24671101  

### Objectives
- Increase the number of ITI graduates and enhance their employability and labor market outcomes  
- Strengthen governance and management of selected ITIs through hub-and-spoke consortia (HSICs)  
- Build state/UT-level capacity to facilitate selection and creation of HSICs and monitor their performance  
- Strengthen central-level capacity in policy coordination, program management, and monitoring and evaluation  
- Develop five-year Strategic Investment Plans (SIPs) for NSTIs to transform into world-class institutes for vocational teacher training  
- Foster public-private sector dialogue across economic sectors to improve labor market outcomes  
- Enhance data systems and liaison with central and state government agencies for better coordination  
- Support labor migration initiatives to synergize with program objectives  

### Eligibility Matrix
| Eligible Entity | Description | Administrative Control | Participation Requirement |
|-----------------|-------------|------------------------|----------------------------|
| Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) | Government and Private ITIs | State Governments or Union Territory Administrations | Willing to participate in hub-and-spoke consortia for training upgradation and industry linkage activities |
| National Skill Training Institutes (NSTIs) | Under DGT’s direct control | Directorate General of Training (DGT) | Willing to participate in hub-and-spoke consortia for training upgradation and industry linkage activities |
| Private ITI Participation Limit | Limited to 100 out of 500 selected ITIs in similar STRIVE-like components | N/A | Cap applies to private ITI selection within hub-and-spoke consortia |

### Benefits & Financial Support
**Financial Support Mechanism:**  
- Program-for-Results (PforR) financing instrument from the World Bank  
- Proposed loan amount: **US$900 million**  
- Central government allocates funds to states/UTs based on achievement of Disbursement-Linked Indicators (DLIs)  
- Financial mechanism involves reimbursement against verified results  
- Central and state sharing as per scheme design (e.g., 75% central, 25% state in similar schemes like LWE)  

**Benefits:**  
| Benefit Category | Specific Benefits |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Training Quality | Improved training quality through industry-aligned curricula; access to blended learning resources; certification under NSQF-compliant trades |
| Employability | Enhanced employability of trainees via exposure to latest technologies; increased placement opportunities; trainees gain hands-on skills through industry training components |
| Infrastructure | Upgraded infrastructure in ITIs and NSTIs; strengthened industry linkages; access to blended learning resources |
| Instructor Development | Instructors receive upskilling under Craft Instructor Training Scheme (CITS) |
| System Reforms | Data-driven grading of ITIs; strengthened apprenticeship training through industry clusters; strengthened central-level capacity in policy coordination and M&E |
| Industry Linkages | Industry partnership facilitation; public-private sector dialogue across economic sectors |

### Application Process (Mermaid Flowchart)
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[States/UTs submit proposals for participation in PM-SETU through DGT] --> B[DGT evaluates proposals based on readiness, infrastructure, and commitment to reforms]
    B --> C[Selected states/UTs sign a Program-for-Results (PforR) agreement with Government of India and World Bank]
    C --> D[States/UTs identify and nominate ITIs and NSTIs for inclusion in hub-and-spoke consortia (HSICs)]
    D --> E[HSICs develop and implement upgrading plans, including industry partnerships and curriculum updates]
    E --> F[NSTIs develop five-year Strategic Investment Plans (SIPs) for transformation into world-class institutes]
    F --> G[Progress monitored via disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs) related to ITI governance, trainee outcomes, and industry linkage]
    G --> H[Funds released to states/UTs upon verification of achieved results]
```

### Key Caveats
> **Warning:** Funding is contingent on achievement of disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs)  
> **Warning:** Implementation is carried out by State/UT Governments; DGT provides only central coordination  
> **Warning:** The scheme relies on country-level systems for environmental and social management as per World Bank PforR Policy  
> **Warning:** Private ITI participation is limited to 100 out of 500 selected ITIs in similar STRIVE-like components  
> **Warning:** Industry partners must meet minimum employee and turnover criteria to participate in hub-spoke models  

### Required Documents
1. Proposal from State/UT Government  
2. Commitment letter for hub-and-spoke consortia formation  
3. Details of selected ITIs and NSTIs for upgradation  
4. Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with Industry Training Partners (ITPs)  
5. Strategic Investment Plan (SIP) for NSTIs (for RA3)  
6. Baseline data on ITI performance and trainee outcomes  
7. Plan for monitoring and evaluation of disbursement-linked indicators (DLIs)  

### Target Beneficiaries
- Youth  
- ITI trainees  
- Vocational trainees  
- MSME  
- Industry workers
