# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant belongs to one of the target beneficiary groups: Scheduled Castes (SC), Other Backward Classes (OBC), Safai Karamcharis (including waste pickers), Denotified Tribes (DNT), Sem Nomadic Tribes, or Manual Scavengers  
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian citizen  
- [ ] Applicant’s age is between 18 and 45 years (relaxation possible for certain categories as per nodal officer discretion)  
- [ ] Applicant belongs to an economically weaker section within the eligible community (priority given)  
- [ ] Applicant is not currently enrolled in another government-funded skill development scheme (verify if applicable)  
- [ ] Applicant has valid contact details (mobile number and email ID) for communication  

> **Note**: Applications are accepted on a rolling basis throughout the year subject to availability of training slots. No turnover, employee count, or business registration criteria apply as this is an individual beneficiary scheme.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] PAN Card  
- [ ] Aadhaar Card (serves as identity, address, and age proof in many cases)  
- [ ] Bank account details (for stipend transfer, if applicable)  
- [ ] Passport-sized photographs (recent, with white background)  
- [ ] Mobile number and email ID (for OTP and communication)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Caste Certificate (SC/OBC/DNT as applicable, issued by competent authority)  
- [ ] Age Proof (Birth Certificate, School Leaving Certificate, or 10th Marksheet showing date of birth)  
- [ ] Address Proof (Aadhaar Card, utility bill, ration card, or voter ID)  
- [ ] Educational Qualification Certificate (if applicable for course selection)  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are clear, legible scans or photographs (minimum 300 DPI)  
- [ ] File size does not exceed 2 MB per document  
- [ ] File names follow format: `DocumentType_Name_CaseID.pdf` (e.g., `CasteCertificate_RajuSharma_CASE001.pdf`)  
- [ ] Details on documents match exactly (name, date of birth, address) as entered in the application form  
- [ ] Certificates are self-attested if not gazetted; notarization not required unless specified by training partner  
- [ ] Documents are in English or Hindi; if in regional language, attach certified translation

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Not required**. PM-DAKSH is an individual beneficiary skill development scheme; no Detailed Project Report (DPR) or business plan is needed for application.  
- If the client expresses interest in self-employment post-training, verify with the nodal officer or training partner whether a business proposal is needed for additional support (not part of core scheme).  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official portal: https://pmdaksh.dosje.gov.in and click on **‘Beneficiary Registration’** or **‘Apply Now’**.  
2. Use a valid mobile number and email ID to register and verify via OTP.  
3. Fill in **personal details** exactly as per identity documents (name, date of birth, gender, father’s/mother’s name).  
4. Select the correct **beneficiary category** (SC/OBC/DNT/Sem Nomadic Tribes/Manual Scavenger/Safai Karamcharis) — mismatch leads to rejection.  
5. Enter **permanent and current address** matching address proof documents.  
6. Provide **educational qualification** details (if any) — helps in course recommendation but not mandatory for eligibility.  
7. Upload **scheme-specific documents** in the prescribed format (PDF/JPEG, <2 MB).  
8. Review all entries before submission — **no edits allowed after final submit**.  
9. Submit the application and **save/download the acknowledgment receipt** with Application ID.  
10. Monitor registered email and SMS for updates on verification, training center allotment, and course commencement.  

> **Portal Note**: The PM-DAKSH portal is managed by the Department of Social Justice and Empowerment. Technical issues should be reported via the helpdesk on the portal.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal details match supporting documents (no spelling variations)  
- [ ] Correct beneficiary category selected and supported by caste certificate  
- [ ] Age falls within 18–45 years (with relaxation justification if applicable, noted in remarks)  
- [ ] All mandatory documents uploaded and legible  
- [ ] Mobile number and email ID are active and accessible  
- [ ] Application submitted and acknowledgment receipt saved  
- [ ] Beneficiary informed about next steps (verification, training center selection)  

---

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application only after internal review gate clearance.  
- Take a **screenshot of the confirmation page** showing Application ID, date, and time of submission.  
- Save the **acknowledgment receipt** (PDF) sent to registered email.  
- Store Application ID securely in case file and set reminders for:  
  - Follow-up after 7 days if no verification update  
  - Training center allotment (typically within 15–20 days of approval)  
  - Course commencement date

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify approval letter/intimation from PM-DAKSH portal or training center  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned benefits: 100% training cost coverage, free course material, stipend (if applicable), placement support  
- [ ] Assist beneficiary in reporting to the allotted empaneled training center on the scheduled date  
- [ ] Monitor attendance and progress (if permitted by training partner guidelines)  
- [ ] Ensure completion of training and final assessment  
- [ ] Collect nationally recognized certificate (NSQF-aligned) upon successful completion  
- [ ] Facilitate access to placement support or self-employment assistance as offered by the training partner  
- [ ] Utilization Certificate: Not required from beneficiary (government pays training provider directly based on outcomes)  
- [ ] Invoice success fee to client only after training completion and certification (as per firm’s engagement terms)  
- [ ] Document case closure: application ID, training details, certificate copy, placement outcome (if any)  

> **Note**: The government reimburses accredited training providers directly upon successful completion and placement. Beneficiaries do not receive funds directly; hence, no utilization certificate or fund tracking is required from their end.
