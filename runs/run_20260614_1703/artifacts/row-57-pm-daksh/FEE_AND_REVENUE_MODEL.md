## Pricing Principles
1. **Value-Based Alignment**: Fees are structured to reflect the actual benefit delivered — in this case, 100% financial support for skill development training, including course fees, materials, and stipends (where applicable), paid directly to empaneled training providers.
2. **Risk-Adjusted Pricing**: Given the scheme’s rolling application basis and dependency on training slot availability, success is contingent on beneficiary approval and enrollment — not direct fund disbursement to the client. This introduces moderate execution risk, warranting success fees in the 4–8% range depending on client tier.
3. **Client Capacity Matching**: Since PM-DAKSH targets individual beneficiaries from marginalized communities (SC, OBC, Safai Karamcharis, DNT, etc.) and provides no direct cash transfer or financial benefit to intermediaries or organizations, the scheme does not generate a quantifiable financial value for corporate or institutional clients. Thus, traditional retainer/success fee models based on scheme value are not applicable.
4. **Transparency & Compliance**: All engagements must clearly disclose that the scheme offers training support only, with no direct monetary benefit to applicants or sponsoring entities, ensuring alignment with government guidelines and managing client expectations.

## Invoice Templates

### Retainer Invoice
**Description**: Retainer fee for consulting services related to PM-DAKSH scheme advisory, including eligibility assessment, documentation support, application guidance, and liaison with empaneled training centers under the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment.

### Success Fee Invoice
**Description**: Success fee for successful beneficiary enrollment in an approved PM-DAKSH training course, contingent on verification of application approval and admission confirmation via the official portal (https://pmdaksh.dosje.gov.in), as implemented by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India.
