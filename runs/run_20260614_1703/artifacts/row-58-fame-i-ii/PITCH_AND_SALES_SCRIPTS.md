## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping businesses and buyers in the electric mobility sector access government incentives that reduce upfront costs. The FAME I & II scheme, administered by the Department of Heavy Industries, provides direct financial incentives at the point of sale for electric vehicles — from two-wheelers to buses — making EV adoption more affordable. I’d like to understand your current plans around electric vehicles to see if this scheme could support your goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. Are you planning to purchase or already using electric vehicles for personal, commercial, or fleet use?
2. What type of electric vehicle(s) are you considering (two-wheeler, three-wheeler, four-wheeler car/LCV, or bus)?
3. Have you checked if the specific vehicle model you’re interested in is approved and listed on the FAME portal?
4. Are you buying from a dealer registered under the FAME scheme, or planning to?
5. What is your expected timeline for purchasing the electric vehicle(s)?
6. Are you an individual buyer, institutional buyer, fleet operator, or representing a manufacturer/service provider?
7. Do you have access to the required documents such as the vehicle invoice, registration certificate, and FAME compliance certificate?
8. Are you aware that the incentive is passed on to you only if purchased through a FAME-registered dealer?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Buyer is purchasing an electric vehicle (two-wheeler, three-wheeler, four-wheeler, or bus)
- [ ] Vehicle model is approved and registered on the FAME portal (verified via ARAI/ICAT testing)
- [ ] Purchase is made through a dealer registered under the FAME scheme
- [ ] Buyer is an individual, institutional buyer, fleet operator, or manufacturer/service provider
- [ ] Vehicle meets technical standards specified under FAME-II
- [ ] Applicant can provide: vehicle invoice, RC, proof of purchase, FAME compliance certificate, bank details, and Aadhaar (for individuals)
- [ ] Application is for a vehicle intended for personal use, public transport, shared mobility, or commercial purposes

This scheme, run by the Department of Heavy Industries, offers up to ₹1.5 lakh for electric cars and proportionally higher for buses — directly reducing your purchase price. ## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Electric vehicles have higher upfront costs compared to conventional vehicles, which remains a key barrier to adoption for individuals and businesses alike, despite lower running costs over time.

**Scheme intro:**
The FAME I & II (Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Hybrid and Electric Vehicles) scheme is a subsidy programme administered by the Department of Heavy Industries, Ministry of Heavy Industries and Public Enterprises. It provides financial incentives at the point of sale to reduce the upfront cost of electric vehicles. Under FAME-II, incentives go up to ₹15,000 per kWh for two-wheelers (capped at 40% of vehicle cost), ₹10,000 per kWh for three- and four-wheelers (up to ₹1.5 lakh for electric cars and ₹1 lakh for LCVs), and up to ₹20,000 per kWh for buses, based on battery size. The scheme has a total outlay of ₹10,000 crores, with ₹8,596 crores allocated for demand incentives.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are planning to purchase an electric vehicle that is on the FAME portal’s list of approved models  
- You intend to buy from a dealer registered under the FAME scheme  
- The vehicle you’re considering is intended for personal use, commercial use, or fleet operation — all eligible beneficiary types  
- You can provide the required documents: invoice, RC, proof of purchase, FAME compliance certificate, bank details, and Aadhaar (if individual)
