### FAME I & II
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department of Heavy Industries, Ministry of Heavy Industries and Public Enterprises
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://fame.gov.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Operational until further notice (as per government notifications)

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Individual buyers, institutional buyers, fleet operators, manufacturers, service providers
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Electric and hybrid vehicles (two-wheelers, three-wheelers, four-wheelers including passenger cars and LCVs, buses)
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Vehicles must be approved and registered on the FAME portal; purchase must be made from a registered dealer; models must be tested and certified by designated agencies (e.g., ARAI, ICAT)

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Incentive varies by vehicle type: up to ₹15,000/kWh for two-wheelers (max 40% of cost), ₹10,000/kWh for three- and four-wheelers (capped at ₹1.5 lakh for electric cars and ₹1 lakh for LCVs), ₹20,000/kWh for buses
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit to manufacturers/sellers, who pass on the benefit to end consumers at point of sale
- Duration of benefit: One-time per vehicle purchase

#### Documents Required
1. Vehicle invoice showing chassis number and model details
2. Registration certificate (RC) of the vehicle
3. Proof of purchase and payment
4. FAME compliance certificate from the manufacturer
5. Bank account details of the beneficiary (for direct benefit transfer, if applicable)
6. Aadhaar card of the buyer (for individual beneficiaries)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Manufacturers must get their electric vehicle models tested and approved by designated testing agencies (e.g., ARAI, ICAT)
2. Approved models are registered on the FAME portal by the manufacturer
3. Buyers purchase the eligible vehicle from a registered dealer
4. The dealer or manufacturer uploads the sale details on the FAME portal
5. The Department of Heavy Industries verifies the claim and disburses the incentive amount to the manufacturer/dealer
6. The end consumer benefits from the reduced purchase price at the point of sale
