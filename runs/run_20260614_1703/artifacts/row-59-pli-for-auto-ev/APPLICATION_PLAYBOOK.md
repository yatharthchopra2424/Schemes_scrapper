# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Business is registered as a company under the Companies Act, 2013 or as a partnership firm/LLP under relevant Acts  
- [ ] Annual turnover of the applicant entity is at least ₹250 crore in the preceding financial year  
- [ ] Engaged in manufacturing of automobiles or automotive components (including electric vehicles and EV components)  
- [ ] Unit is located in India and has commenced commercial production  
- [ ] Applicant has made a minimum investment of ₹250 crore in plant and machinery (excluding land and building)  
- [ ] Applicant is not a defaulter to any bank or financial institution  
- [ ] Applicant has not availed benefits under any other central government PLI scheme for the same product  
- [ ] Applicant possesses valid GST registration and Udyam Registration (if applicable)  
- [ ] Applicant has filed income tax returns for the last three financial years  

> **Note**: Eligibility criteria are inferred from typical PLI for Auto & EV scheme structure due to low confidence in extracted evidence. Verify exact thresholds and conditions with the official scheme guidelines published by the Department of Heavy Industry.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed / LLP Agreement  
- PAN Card of the business entity  
- GST Registration Certificate  
- Udyam Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- Audited Financial Statements (Balance Sheet, P&L Account) for last 3 years  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for last 3 financial years  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Cancelled Cheque / Bank Certificate for account verification  
- Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) of authorized signatory  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed Project Report (DPR) outlining proposed investment, production capacity, and employment generation  
- Certificate from Chartered Engineer certifying plant and machinery investment (excluding land and building)  
- List of proposed automobile/EV products to be manufactured under the scheme  
- Land possession/lease documents for the manufacturing unit  
- Power sanction letter from electricity board  
- No Objection Certificate (NOC) from Pollution Control Board (if applicable)  
- Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) or agreement with technology collaborator (if applicable)  
- Export performance data (if claiming export-linked incentives)  
- Board resolution authorizing application and designating signatory  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (text-selectable where possible)  
- File size for each document must not exceed 5 MB  
- Document filenames must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ClientName_Date.pdf` (e.g., `PLI_AutoEV_CertificateOfIncorporation_ABC Ltd_20240615.pdf`)  
- All certificates must be valid and not expired on the date of submission  
- Financial documents must be audited and signed by a Chartered Accountant  
- Declarations and undertakings must be on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value and notarized where required  
- Hindi or English language only; if in another language, provide certified translation

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
✅ **Required**  
The Detailed Project Report (DPR) is mandatory for the PLI for Auto & EV scheme. It must include:  
- Executive Summary  
- Promoter and Company Background  
- Project Location and Site Details  
- Manufacturing Process and Technology Chosen  
- List of Plant and Machinery with Cost Breakdown (must show ≥ ₹250 crore investment)  
- Proposed Product Mix (automobiles, EVs, components) with annual capacity  
- Employment Generation Plan (direct and indirect)  
- Implementation Schedule (Gantt chart)  
- Capital Structure and Means of Financing  
- Working Capital Requirements  
- Revenue Projections for 5 years  
- Export Projections (if applicable)  
- Environmental, Health, and Safety (EHS) Compliance Plan  
- Risk Analysis and Mitigation Measures  
- Compliance with PLI Scheme Guidelines (eligibility, incentive structure, sales threshold)  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official application portal via the Department of Heavy Industry website (https://heavyindustries.gov.in) – verify exact URL for PLI Auto & EV application  
2. Use only the latest version of the application form released for the current incentive period  
3. Fill all fields in BLOCK LETTERS as per supporting documents (name, PAN, GST, address must match exactly)  
4. Ensure investment in plant and machinery is reported excluding land and building, and meets the ₹250 crore threshold  
5. Clearly specify the segment of auto/EV components for which incentive is sought (e.g., lithium-ion batteries, electric powertrains, chassis)  
6. Declare any availed benefits under other central/state schemes to avoid duplication  
7. Attach all documents in the prescribed order and format as specified in the portal’s document upload section  
8. Review the declaration section carefully – unauthorized signing may lead to rejection or penalties  
9. Save draft periodically and take screenshots of each completed section before final submission  
10. Do not submit until internal review gate is cleared  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal and business details (name, PAN, GST, address) match across documents and form  
- [ ] Investment in plant and machinery ≥ ₹250 crore (verified via CA and Chartered Engineer certificates)  
- [ ] Turnover ≥ ₹250 crore in preceding FY (verified via ITR and financial statements)  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents are attached and legible  
- [ ] DPR is complete, signed by promoter and CA, and aligned with form data  
- [ ] Declarations regarding no default, no duplicate benefits, and accuracy of information are signed  
- [ ] Authorized signatory’s DSC is valid and used for form submission  
- [ ] Application reference number is noted post-submission (to be filled after submission)

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Log in to the portal using authorized credentials and submit the application  
- Upon successful submission, download and save the acknowledgment receipt with application number and timestamp  
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing “Application Submitted Successfully”  
- Store the application number, submission date, and portal URL in the case file  
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days post-submission  
- Monitor registered email and portal inbox for queries or deficiency notices

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the approval letter from the Department of Heavy Industry specifying sanctioned incentive amount and tenure  
- [ ] Confirm the approved investment threshold and eligible product list as per approval  
- [ ] Initiate communication with client regarding utilization timeline and compliance requirements  
- [ ] Guide client on maintaining separate books of account for incentivized unit (if required)  
- [ ] Prepare utilization certificate format as per scheme guidelines for periodic submission  
- [ ] Advise on timely submission of quarterly/annual sales and investment claims to avail disbursements  
- [ ] Verify that first disbursement is received post-verification of initial sales/investment milestone  
- [ ] Track milestone achievement (e.g., commercial production, sales threshold) for subsequent incentive tranches  
- [ ] Ensure all declarations regarding continued compliance (no change in product mix, investment maintenance) are filed on time  
- [ ] Prepare and submit final utilization certificate and project completion report at end of incentive period  
- [ ] Invoice success fee as per engagement letter post-approval confirmation and first disbursement  

> **Note**: Specific disbursement milestones, sales thresholds, and incentive rates (as % of eligible sales) must be verified from the official PLI for Auto & EV scheme guidelines. Refer to latest notification on https://heavyindustries.gov.in for accurate figures.
