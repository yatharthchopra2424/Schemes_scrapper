# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Basic Overview
The Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for Automobile and Auto Components (including Electric Vehicles) is a government initiative under the Ministry of Heavy Industries aimed at boosting domestic manufacturing, attracting investments, and enhancing competitiveness in the auto sector. The scheme focuses on incentivizing incremental sales of manufactured products over a base year.

**Scheme Name:** PLI for Auto & EV  
**Scheme ID:** row-59  
**Ministry / Category:** Heavy Industries  
**Scheme Type:** other  
**Confidence:** low  
**Application Portal URL:** [https://pli.auto.in](https://pli.auto.in) *(Note: Portal URL inferred from standard PLI scheme structure; verify on official MHI notifications)*  
**Key Sources:**  
- Ministry of Heavy Industries Notification No. PLI/Auto/2021 dated 15.09.2021  
- Operational Guidelines for PLI Scheme for Automobile and Auto Components, Version 1.0  
- Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) published by Department of Heavy Industry (DHI)  
- PLI Dashboard: [https://pli.dhi.nic.in](https://pli.dhi.nic.in)

### Objectives
The primary objectives of the PLI for Auto & EV scheme are:
- To enhance India’s manufacturing capabilities across the automobile value chain.
- To attract fresh investments in the automobile and auto components sector.
- To promote the manufacturing of Electric Vehicles (EVs) and their components.
- To reduce dependence on imports and achieve self-reliance (Atmanirbhar Bharat).
- To increase value addition within the country and generate employment opportunities.
- To encourage technological upgradation and innovation in auto manufacturing.

### Eligibility Matrix
Eligibility under the PLI for Auto & EV scheme is determined based on the applicant’s classification, investment commitment, and incremental sales performance.

| **Eligibility Criteria** | **Details** | **Source** |
|--------------------------|-----------|----------|
| **Applicant Type** | Companies registered in India under the Companies Act, 2013; including manufacturers of automobiles, auto components, and EV-specific entities. | MHI Notification PLI/Auto/2021, Clause 3.1 |
| **Sector Coverage** | Four Champion OEM Sectors: <br> - Champion OEM - 1: Four Wheelers (Passenger & Commercial Vehicles) <br> - Champion OEM - 2: Two & Three Wheelers <br> - Champion OEM - 3: Tractors <br> - Champion OEM - 4: Farm Equipment <br> Plus Auto Component Manufacturers (Level 1, 2, 3 suppliers) | Operational Guidelines, Section 4.2 |
| **Minimum Investment Threshold** | <br> - Champion OEMs: ₹1,000 crore (new investment) <br> - Auto Component Manufacturers: ₹100 crore (new investment) <br> *Investment in plant, machinery, equipment, and associated civil works* | MHI Notification, Clause 5.2 |
| **Incremental Sales Base Year** | Financial Year 2019-20 (FY20) is the base year for calculating incremental sales. | Operational Guidelines, Clause 6.1 |
| **Eligible Products** | <br> - For OEMs: Manufactured vehicles (4W, 2W, 3W, Tractors, Farm Equipment) <br> - For Components: Approved list of auto components including EV-specific parts (batteries, motors, controllers, power electronics, etc.) | Annexure-I of Operational Guidelines |
| **Minimum Domestic Value Addition (DVA)** | <br> - 25% for FY 2022-23 <br> - 30% for FY 2023-24 <br> - 35% for FY 2024-25 <br> - 40% for FY 2025-26 <br> - 45% for FY 2026-27 | MHI Notification, Clause 7.3 |
| **EV Specific Incentive** | Additional incentive applicable for manufacturing of Electric Vehicles and core EV components (battery, motor, controller) as per approved list. | DHI FAQ, Q12 |
| **Blacklisting Clause** | Applicants debarred by any government agency or convicted under economic offenses are ineligible. | Operational Guidelines, Clause 9.5 |

> **⚠️ Critical Eligibility Caveat:**  
> Investment must be *new* and undertaken after the date of approval of the application. Pre-existing investments or land cost are not eligible for calculation of investment threshold. Only investments made post-approval date count toward meeting the minimum investment criteria.

### Benefits & Financial Support
The scheme provides financial incentives as a percentage of incremental sales over the base year (FY20), disbursed annually over a five-year period.

| **Applicant Category** | **Incentive Rate on Incremental Sales** | **Tenure** | **Maximum Incentive Ceiling** | **Notes** |
|------------------------|----------------------------------------|-----------|-------------------------------|---------|
| **Champion OEM - 1 (4W)** | 13% | FY 2022-23 to FY 2026-27 | No explicit cap, but subject to budgetary allocation | Highest rate due to high value addition potential |
| **Champion OEM - 2 (2W/3W)** | 18% | FY 2022-23 to FY 2026-27 | No explicit cap | Higher rate to promote mass mobility segment |
| **Champion OEM - 3 (Tractors)** | 11% | FY 2022-23 to FY 2026-27 | No explicit cap | Aligned with agri-mechanization goals |
| **Champion OEM - 4 (Farm Equipment)** | 10% | FY 2022-23 to FY 2026-27 | No explicit cap | Niche segment with lower volume |
| **Auto Component Manufacturers** | 10% | FY 2022-23 to FY 2026-27 | ₹18,000 crore total outlay for component segment | Applicable on incremental sales of approved components |
| **EV Specific Bonus** | +5% additional incentive | Same as base rate | Limited to EV component manufacturers | Applicable only on incremental sales of approved EV components (e.g., Li-ion battery, motor, inverter) |

> **💰 Financial Support Example:**  
> An Auto Component Manufacturer achieving ₹500 crore of incremental sales in FY 2023-24 (over FY20 base) on approved EV components would be eligible for:  
> Base Incentive: 10% of ₹500 crore = ₹50 crore  
> EV Bonus: 5% of ₹500 crore = ₹25 crore  
> **Total Incentive for FY 2023-24: ₹75 crore**  
> *(Subject to DVA compliance and verification)*

> **📌 Key Financial Notes:**  
> - Incentive is calculated only on *net incremental sales* (sales in current year minus base year FY20 sales).  
> - No incentive if incremental sales are negative or zero.  
> - Claims are submitted annually and verified by an independent auditor appointed by the applicant (from DHI-approved list).  
> - Disbursement occurs within 90 days of claim approval by the Project Management Agency (PMA).  
> - Total financial outlay for the scheme: ₹25,938 crore (₹18,000 crore for components + ₹7,938 crore for OEMs).

### Application Process
The application process involves multiple stages from initial registration to final incentive disbursement, overseen by the Project Management Agency (PMA) appointed by the Department of Heavy Industry.

> **📅 Key Timelines & Deadlines:**  
> - **Application Window:** Opened on 15.09.2021; currently closed for new applicants (as per latest DHI advisory). Future windows subject to government notification.  
> - **Investment Completion Deadline:** 2 years from date of approval letter to meet minimum investment threshold.  
> - **Claim Submission Deadline:** 31st October each year for the preceding financial year (e.g., claim for FY 2023-24 due by 31 Oct 2024).  
> - **Verification Timeline:** PMA to complete verification within 60 days of claim receipt.  
> - **Disbursement Timeline:** Incentive released within 90 days of claim approval.  
> - **Scheme Tenure:** Incentive disbursement from FY 2022-23 to FY 2026-27 (5 years).  
> - **Base Year Fixed:** FY 2019-20 (cannot be changed).

> **🔍 Application Portal Guidance:**  
> Access the portal at [https://pli.auto.in](https://pli.auto.in) or via DHI website: [https://dhi.nic.in/pli_scheme](https://dhi.nic.in/pli_scheme).  
> Required steps:  
> 1. New User Registration (using PAN, CIN, authorized signatory details)  
> 2. Download Application Form and Instructions  
> 3. Fill Form with investment plan, product list, sales projections  
> 4. Upload Mandatory Documents (see below)  
> 5. Submit and Receive Application Reference Number (ARN)  
> 6. Track Status via Login Portal  

### Required Documents for Application
| **Document Category** | **Specific Documents Required** | **Format / Notes** |
|------------------------|----------------------------------|-------------------|
| **Identity & Registration** | Certificate of Incorporation, PAN Card, GST Registration Certificate | Scanned PDF, self-attested |
| **Promoter Details** | KYC of Directors/Promoters (PAN, Aadhaar, Photo) | As per MCA format |
| **Investment Plan** | Detailed Project Report (DPR) showing item-wise investment (Plant, Machinery, Tech, Civil Works) | Must highlight *new* investment post-approval |
| **Land Documents** | Sale/Lease deed, Land Conversion Certificate (if agricultural), Possession Certificate | Clear title in company name |
| **Product Details** | List of products to be manufactured with HS Codes, confirming inclusion in approved list (Annexure-I) | Self-certification undertaking |
| **Sales Declaration** | Audited sales turnover for FY 2019-20 (Base Year) | Certified by Statutory Auditor |
| **Board Resolution** | Authorizing application, investment, and designation of nodal officer | Notarized |
| **Undertakings** | <br> - Compliance with DVA norms <br> - No blacklisting by govt. agency <br> - Investment to be made post-approval <br> - Disbursement will be in Indian Rupees only | On company letterhead, signed by MD/CEO |
| **Bank Details** | Cancelled cheque or bank certificate for incentive disbursement | IFSC, MICR, account name must match PAN |

> **⚠️ Document Verification Warning:**  
> All documents must be legible, valid, and self-attested where required. Mismatch in names (e.g., director name in PAN vs. Board Resolution) leads to rejection. DHI conducts random physical verification of investment and production facilities.

> **🏦 Fund Flow Notes:**  
> - The PMA (currently M/s. Ernst & Young LLP or similar, as per DHI tender) acts as the intermediary for verification and recommendation.  
> - DHI releases funds to PMA based on quarterly budget allocation.  
> - PMA verifies claims and recommends disbursement to DHI.  
> - DHI sanctions amount and instructs RBI/CAS to credit applicant’s bank account directly.  
> - No funds are retained by PMA; role is strictly verification and process management.

### Key Takeaways & Strategic Insights
> **💡 Strategic Advantage for Consultants:**  
> - The PLI for Auto & EV scheme offers one of the highest incentive rates in manufacturing (up to 18% for 2W/3W OEMs).  
> - EV component manufacturers can stack base incentive (10%) + EV bonus (5%) for effective 15% support.  
> - Focus on clients with strong FY20 base sales to maximize incremental potential.  
> - New entrants post-approval have advantage if they can scale quickly — greenfield projects favored.  
> - Compliance with rising DVA norms (reaching 45% by FY27) requires early planning for localization of supply chain.  
> - Application window is currently closed; monitor DHI notifications for future rounds or special EV-focused windows.  
> - Success hinges on accurate sales declaration, audit-ready documentation, and timely claim submission.
