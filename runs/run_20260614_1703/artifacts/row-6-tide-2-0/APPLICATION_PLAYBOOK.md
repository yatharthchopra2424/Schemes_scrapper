# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is one of the 51 designated TIDE 2.0 Incubation Centres from institutions of higher learning and R&D organisations  
- [ ] Incubation Centre has established incubation facilities  
- [ ] Incubation Centre has established linkages with large companies, industry bodies, or industry clusters  
- [ ] Incubation Centre is capable of providing necessary mentorship and possible infusion of innovative businesses into larger business operations  
- [ ] Proposal is submitted by the Implementing Agency (Incubation Centre) for funding under TIDE 2.0  
- [ ] Incubation Centre is located in India (Pan-India scope)  
- [ ] Incubation Centre is not disqualified due to cessation of existence (assets would revert to MeitY if applicable)  

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration of the Incubation Centre  
- PAN Card of the Incubation Centre  
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (if applicable as MSME)  
- Audited Financial Statements (last 2 years)  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of the Incubation Centre (last 2 years)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- MoU executed by Implementing Agencies/Partnering Institutes  
- Surety Bond by Implementing Agencies  
- Utilization Certificate of Previous release (if seeking subsequent installment)  
- Technical & Financial Progress Report  
- Audited statement of accounts  
- Recommendations of the PRSG (Project Review and Steering Group)  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible scans or certified true copies  
- File names must match the document type (e.g., "Utilization_Certificate_FY2023-24.pdf")  
- PDF file size should not exceed 10 MB per document  
- Documents must be in English or accompanied by a certified translation  
- Signatures and seals must be visible and authentic  
- Utilization Certificate and Audited Statements must be certified by a Chartered Accountant  
- MoU and Surety Bond must be on stamp paper of appropriate value and notarized if required

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A formal Detailed Project Report (DPR) is not explicitly mandated in the evidence. However, the **Technical & Financial Progress Report** and **Audited statement of accounts** serve as critical performance and financial documentation. It is advised to **verify with the nodal officer (MeitY Startup Hub)** whether a DPR is required for new proposals or if the progress report suffices for ongoing funding requests.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Ensure all applicant details (name, address, contact, PAN, GST) match exactly with official records.  
2. Select the correct scheme: **TIDE 2.0 (Technology Incubation and Development of Entrepreneurs)**.  
3. Specify the type of support sought: EiR (₹4 Lakh), Grant (₹7 Lakh), or Investment (₹40 Lakh) based on the startup stage.  
4. Clearly state the incubation centre’s name, location, and affiliation (institute of higher learning or R&D organisation).  
5. Detail linkages with large companies, industry bodies, or clusters to demonstrate mentorship and infusion capacity.  
6. Attach all scheme-specific documents in the prescribed order: MoU, Surety Bond, Utilization Certificate, Technical & Financial Progress Report, Audited Accounts, PRSG Recommendations.  
7. Declarations must be signed by the authorised signatory of the Incubation Centre with date and seal.  
8. Do not leave any mandatory field blank; if not applicable, write “NA” with justification.  
9. Retain a copy of the submitted application and all supporting documents for audit and follow-up.  
10. Submit via the designated portal or nodal office as advised; verify receipt acknowledgment.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All sections of the application form are fully completed  
- [ ] Universal documents (PAN, ITR, GST, bank statements, Udyam) are attached and valid  
- [ ] Scheme-specific documents (MoU, Surety Bond, UC, Progress Report, Audited Accounts, PRSG Recs) are attached  
- [ ] Financial figures in Utilization Certificate and Audited Accounts match and are CA-certified  
- [ ] Declarations are signed and dated by authorised signatory  
- [ ] No overwriting or use of correction fluid on physical documents (if applicable)  
- [ ] Application reference number (if generated) is noted  
- [ ] Copy of complete application package is archived internally

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the designated channel (email to meity-sthub@gov.in or through the MeitY Startup Hub portal)  
- Take a screenshot of the submission confirmation or save the acknowledgment receipt  
- Store the application reference number (if provided) in the case file  
- Set a follow-up reminder for 15 days post-submission  
- Notify the client of successful submission with reference number and expected timeline

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the official approval letter from MeitY Startup Hub  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount matches the requested support (EiR: ₹4 Lakh, Grant: ₹7 Lakh, Investment: ₹40 Lakh)  
- [ ] Review and note the stipulated timelines for fund utilization and reporting  
- [ ] Ensure the Utilization Certificate format is understood for future installments  
- [ ] Initiate internal preparation for the first Utilization Certificate submission (due at 6 months post-release)  
- [ ] Track invoice for success fee as per consultancy agreement  
- [ ] Advise client on compliance: grant must be spent for approved purposes; unspent amount to be surrendered to MeitY  
- [ ] Remind client to maintain audited record of assets acquired via MeitY grant  
- [ ] Inform client that MeitY retains rights to dispose of assets post-project; institution must support sale process  
- [ ] Ensure client agrees to submit annual asset list to MeitY and allow access to books of accounts for audit  
- [ ] Confirm IPR ownership agreement is in place between participating organisations as per scheme terms  
- [ ] Advise client that IPR reports must be submitted to MeitY for minimum 5 years post-project  
- [ ] Verify that IPR resides in India to safeguard national interest
