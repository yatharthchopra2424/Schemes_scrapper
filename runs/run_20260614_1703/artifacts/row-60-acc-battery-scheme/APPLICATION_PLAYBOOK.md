# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Business is registered as a Private Limited Company, Public Limited Company, LLP, or Partnership Firm under Indian law  
- [ ] Annual turnover does not exceed ₹250 crore (as per MSME classification for medium enterprises)  
- [ ] Employee count is within the limit for medium enterprises (≤ 250 employees)  
- [ ] Business operates in the manufacturing sector, specifically engaged in the production of advanced chemistry cells (ACC) for batteries  
- [ ] Unit is engaged in manufacturing of lithium-ion batteries or other advanced chemistry cells used in electric vehicles or energy storage systems  
- [ ] Business has been operational for at least 1 year as of the date of application  
- [ ] Unit has obtained necessary industrial clearances and complies with environmental and safety norms  
- [ ] Applicant has not availed benefits under any other central government scheme for the same purpose (double benefit check)  
- [ ] Unit possesses valid Udyam Registration certificate  
- [ ] Applicant has submitted a Detailed Project Report (DPR) outlining technology, capacity, investment, and timelines  

> **Note**: Confidence in eligibility criteria is low due to limited scheme details in evidence. Verify exact thresholds with nodal officer or official guidelines.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed / LLP Agreement  
- PAN Card of the business entity  
- GST Registration Certificate  
- Udyam Registration Certificate  
- Audited Financial Statements (Balance Sheet, P&L) for last 2 years  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for last 2 years  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Cancelled Cheque / Bank Verification Letter  
- Authorization Letter / Board Resolution for application signing  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed Project Report (DPR) covering technology selection, production capacity (in GWh/year), investment plan, and implementation timeline  
- Land ownership or lease documents for the manufacturing unit  
- Clearances from Pollution Control Board (if applicable)  
- Details of proposed battery chemistry (e.g., LFP, NMC, LMO) and manufacturing process  
- List of plant and machinery with quotations from suppliers  
- Power and water sanction letters from concerned authorities  
- Undertaking on non-availment of similar central subsidies for the same project  
- Declaration of domestic value addition (if applicable under scheme guidelines)  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document)  
- File names must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ClientID_Date.pdf` (e.g., `ACC_Battery_Scheme_Udyam_Case001_20240515.pdf`)  
- Certificates (Udyam, GST, PAN) must be valid and not expired  
- Financial documents must be certified by a Chartered Accountant  
- DPR must be signed by authorized director/partner and stamped  
- All documents must be in English or accompanied by a certified Hindi/English translation  
- Affidavits and undertakings must be on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value and notarized

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
✅ **Required** — Based on scheme objectives for ACC manufacturing, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is mandatory.  

**Standard DPR Sections to Include**:  
- Executive Summary  
- Promoter Profile and Experience  
- Project Location and Site Details  
- Technology Selection (battery chemistry: LFP/NMC/etc., manufacturing process)  
- Production Capacity (annual output in GWh/year)  
- Plant Layout and Machinery Details  
- Implementation Schedule (timelines for each phase)  
- Investment Plan and Means of Financing  
- Working Capital Requirements  
- Manpower Plan  
- Marketing and Sales Strategy  
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation Measures  
- Environmental, Health, and Safety (EHS) Compliance  
- Financial Projections (IRR, NPV, payback period) for 5–7 years  
- Domestic Value Addition Plan (if applicable)  
- Undertaking on Compliance with Scheme Guidelines  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official application portal for the ACC Battery Scheme (verify URL with Ministry of Heavy Industries nodal officer)  
2. Use only the latest version of the application form; outdated forms will be rejected  
3. Fill all fields in **Block Letters** using English language only  
4. Ensure numeric fields (turnover, investment, capacity) are entered without commas or currency symbols (e.g., 50000000 for ₹5 crore)  
5. Match all names, addresses, and registration numbers exactly as per PAN, Udyam, and GST certificates  
6. Select correct MSME category based on latest audited turnover and employee count  
7. Attach all documents in the specified order and format as per portal instructions  
8. Declarations and undertakings must be digitally signed or physically signed and scanned; unsigned forms are invalid  
9. Preview the entire form before final submission; no edits allowed after submission  
10. Save the application reference number immediately upon successful submission  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal and business details match supporting documents (PAN, Udyam, GST)  
- [ ] Turnover, employee count, and investment figures are accurate and verifiable  
- [ ] Scheme-specific documents (DPR, land, machinery, clearances) are complete and signed  
- [ ] Declarations on non-duplication of benefits and domestic value addition are included  
- [ ] All files are uploaded in correct format and size (<5 MB each)  
- [ ] Application form is previewed and validated for mandatory fields  
- [ ] Authorized signatory has signed the declaration page  
- [ ] Application reference number is noted and communicated to client

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing **Application Reference Number** and timestamp  
- Save the reference number in the case file and inform the client via email/SMS  
- Download and store a PDF copy of the submitted application for audit purposes  
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days post-submission  
- Monitor the portal regularly for status updates or queries from the nodal agency

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the official approval letter from the Ministry of Heavy Industries  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned amount, disbursement schedule, and any conditions precedent to fund release  
- [ ] Ensure utilization certificate format is understood and prepared in advance  
- [ ] Track milestone-based disbursements linked to project progress (land acquisition, civil works, machinery installation)  
- [ ] Maintain records of all invoices, bank statements, and utilization reports for audit  
- [ ] Coordinate with client for timely submission of utilization certificates and progress reports  
- [ ] Calculate and invoice success fee as per engagement terms (to be filled by consultant)  
- [ ] Advise client on compliance with post-approval requirements (e.g., minimum operational period, employment commitments)  
- [ ] Notify client of any reporting obligations (quarterly/annual) to the nodal agency  

> **Note**: Specific disbursement mechanics, utilization timelines, and reporting frequency for the ACC Battery Scheme must be confirmed with the nodal officer, as detailed operational guidelines were not fully available in the evidence.
