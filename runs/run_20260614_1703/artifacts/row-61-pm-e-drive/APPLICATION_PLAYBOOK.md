# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] OEM is registered with Ministry of Heavy Industries (MHI)
- [ ] EV model is manufactured in India
- [ ] EV model meets Phased Manufacturing Programme (PMP) requirements as per Annexure-5 (for e-2W/e-3W) or Annexure-6 (for EVPCS)
- [ ] EV model obtains PM E-DRIVE eligibility assessment certificate from recognised testing agency
- [ ] EV model is equipped with advanced battery (specific energy density ≥70 Wh/kg, cycle life ≥1000 cycles)
- [ ] For e-2W/e-3W: registered for commercial use OR privately/corporately owned registered e-2W
- [ ] For e-3W (e-rickshaw & e-cart): registered solely for commercial use
- [ ] For e-3W (L5): registered within validity period of PM E-DRIVE certificate (closed for new registrations after 26-12-2025)
- [ ] For e-truck: buyer must furnish Certificate of Deposit (CD) from MoRTH-authorised RVSF for ICE truck of equal or higher GVW
- [ ] For EVPCS: eligible entity appoints nodal agency to aggregate demand and submit proposal via online portal
- [ ] EV model is not imported in CKD form from single supplier
- [ ] Battery modules are not imported
- [ ] EV purchased by Central/State Government departments or agencies is not eligible for demand incentives
- [ ] No more than one EV of a particular category per individual is incentivised
- [ ] Resold EVs are not eligible; only original purchase qualifies

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Company Registration Certificate
- PAN Card
- GST Registration Certificate
- Bank Statements
- Udyam Registration
- ITR (last 2 years)
- Audited Financial Statements

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Application for manufacturer registration
- Trade License
- Testing/Homologation Certificate complying with PM E-DRIVE eligibility criteria
- Pre-registration of EV model as per Annexure-II
- Complete set of Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Articles of Association (AOA)
- Proof of OEM’s sales and service network
- Photographs of the type-approved vehicle
- Undertaking for reimbursement claim
- Board resolution authorising signatory
- Photo identification document (PAN/Voter ID/Passport/Driving Licence)
- Certificate of Deposit (CD) for e-trucks (from MoRTH-authorised RVSF)
- Undertaking for first tranche subsidy disbursement (for EVPCS)

### Document Quality Check
- File size: <5 MB per document
- File name: Must match document type (e.g., "PAN_Card.pdf")
- All documents must be self-attested or certified
- Language: English or Hindi; if other language, provide certified translation
- Scanned copies must be legible and in colour where applicable
- Undertakings must be on company letterhead and signed by authorised signatory
- Board resolution must be certified true copy

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
DPR is **not required** for OEM registration or demand incentive claims under PM E-DRIVE. However, for EVPCS proposals, a detailed project report must be submitted as part of the proposal via the online portal, specifying locations, number of chargers, vehicle segments, charger capacities, subsidy requested, and implementation mechanism. Verify with nodal officer if DPR is needed for your specific sub-component.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Ensure all fields in the OEM registration form are filled accurately; leave no blank fields.
2. Upload all required documents in the specified format (PDF, <5 MB).
3. Use the exact naming convention for uploaded files as per portal instructions.
4. Select correct vehicle segment (e-2W, e-3W, e-truck, e-bus, EVPCS) during registration.
5. For e-trucks, upload Certificate of Deposit (CD) and verify GVW matching.
6. For EVPCS, specify location category (A/B/C/D) and subsidy % as per operational guidelines.
7. Confirm that the EV model is pre-registered as per Annexure-II before submitting production data.
8. Do not claim incentive for EVs sold to Central/State Government departments.
9. Ensure invoice amount charged to dealer excludes the incentive amount (deducted after taxes).
10. Submit reimbursement claim weekly but not later than 120 days from sale of vehicle at dealer end.
11. For e-3W (L5), note that registrations after 26-12-2025 are ineligible; last claim submission date is 25.04.2026 for vehicles sold on 26.12.2025.
12. Use the e-Voucher system for end customers to avail incentive at time of purchase (if applicable).
13. Retain all documents for 5 years from date of claim for audit purposes.
14. Refer to portal: https://pmedrive.heavyindustries.gov.in/ for form access and updates.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and save approval letter from MHI
- [ ] Verify sanctioned amount against approved incentive (based on battery capacity, ex-factory price, or GVW cap)
- [ ] For OEMs: Ensure incentive is deducted from customer invoice after all taxes
- [ ] For EVPCS: Submit undertaking for first tranche (70%) after demand notes, PMP compliance, and charging standards
- [ ] After commissioning and energization, submit undertaking for second tranche (30%) with:
  - Successful commissioning/energization proof
  - PMP compliance certificate
  - Onboarding on National Unified Hub
- [ ] Maintain utilization records for 5 years
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate (GFR 12 – A or C as applicable) post-project completion
- [ ] Confirm invoice success fee structure with client (if applicable)
- [ ] Ensure all refundable deposits are excluded from subsidy calculation
- [ ] Monitor for any communication regarding fund exhaustion or scheme closure (terminal dates: e-2W – 31st July 2026; e-3W (e-rickshaw/e-cart) – 31st March 2028; e-3W (L5) – closed 26-12-2025)
