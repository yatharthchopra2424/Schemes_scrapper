# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping electric vehicle manufacturers and charging infrastructure providers access government incentives under the PM E-DRIVE scheme. Given your work in the EV space—whether manufacturing two/three-wheelers, buses, trucks, or setting up charging stations—I’d like to understand your current operations to see if this scheme can support your growth plans.

### Discovery Questions
1. What type of electric vehicles or charging infrastructure does your business focus on (e-2W, e-3W, e-bus, e-truck, or EV public charging stations)?
2. When was your company incorporated, and are your EV models manufactured and registered within the PM E-DRIVE validity period?
3. Do your EV models meet the advanced battery requirements (specific energy density ≥70 Wh/kg, cycle life ≥1000 cycles) as per CMVR standards?
4. Have you obtained or are you planning to obtain PM E-DRIVE eligibility certification from recognized testing agencies (ARAI, iCAT, GARC, NATRAX)?
5. Are your EV models compliant with the Phased Manufacturing Programme (PMP) requirements for domestically manufactured components?
6. For e-truck applicants: Are you prepared to furnish a Certificate of Deposit (CD) from an MoRTH-authorised RVSF for scrapped ICE trucks of equal or higher GVW?
7. For EVPCS applicants: Are you a Government of India ministry, CPSE, State/UT, or PSU eligible to submit proposals through a nodal agency?
8. What is your target timeline for vehicle sales or charging station deployment, and are you aware of the 120-day claim submission window post-sale?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Business operates in e-2W, e-3W, e-bus, e-truck, or EVPCS segment
- [ ] EVs manufactured and registered within PM E-DRIVE validity period (till 31 July 2026 for e-2W, 31 March 2028 for e-rickshaw/e-cart)
- [ ] EVs equipped with advanced batteries (≥70 Wh/kg specific energy, ≥1000 cycle life)
- [ ] OEM registered with MHI or willing to apply
- [ ] EV model has PM E-DRIVE eligibility certificate from recognized testing agency
- [ ] Compliance with PMP guidelines for domestically manufactured components
- [ ] For e-trucks: Ability to furnish CD from MoRTH-authorised RVSF
- [ ] For EVPCS: Eligible entity type (GoI ministry, CPSE, State/UT, PSU) with nodal agency arrangement
- [ ] Awareness of 120-day claim submission deadline from sale date

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your business aligns well with the PM E-DRIVE scheme’s support for [specific segment from discovery]. The scheme offers upfront financial incentives—such as ₹10,000 per kWh for e-buses or ₹5,000 per kWh for e-2Ws in FY 2024-25—directly reducing acquisition costs. Shall I walk you through how this works for your specific use case?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
EV adoption is hindered by high upfront costs, particularly for batteries and charging infrastructure. Manufacturers struggle to price competitively, while CPOs and government entities face capital constraints in deploying public charging stations. Without subsidies, widespread EV adoption and infrastructure rollout remain financially challenging for businesses and public agencies alike.

**Scheme intro:**
The PM Electric Drive Revolution in Innovative Vehicle Enhancement (PM E-DRIVE) Scheme, notified by the Ministry of Heavy Industries (MHI) vide S.O. 4259(E) on 29 September 2024, provides demand incentives and capital subsidies to accelerate EV adoption. It offers financial support of up to ₹10,900 crore, including demand incentives for EVs (up to ₹35 lakh per e-bus, ₹50,000 per e-3W L5, ₹10,000 per e-2W) and grants for EV public charging stations (up to 100% of upstream and EVSE costs in Category A locations). Applications are submitted via the portal: https://pmedrive.heavyindustries.gov.in/

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your EVs are manufactured in India and registered under CMVR, 1989  
- They are equipped with advanced batteries meeting ≥70 Wh/kg specific energy and ≥1000 cycle life  
- You comply with or are willing to comply with Phased Manufacturing Programme (PMP) for domestic value addition  
- [If e-truck: You can furnish Certificate of Deposit from MoRTH-authorised RVSF for scrapped ICE trucks]  
- [If EVPCS: You are a GoI ministry, CPSE, State/UT, or PSU eligible to submit via nodal agency]
