### PM E-DRIVE
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Ministry of Heavy Industries, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://pmedrive.heavyindustries.gov.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** 1st October 2024 to 31st March 2028 (with sub-component extensions: e-2Ws till 31st July 2026; e-3Ws (e-rickshaws & e-carts) till 31st March 2028; e-3W (L5) closed on 26-12-2025)

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: OEMs (Original Equipment Manufacturers) registered with MHI; eligible entities for EVPCS include GoI ministries, CPSEs, States/UTs and PSUs under them
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Electric vehicles (e-2W, e-3W, e-ambulances, e-trucks, e-buses) and EV public charging stations (EVPCS), battery swapping stations (BSS), battery charging stations (BCS)
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Vehicles must be registered under CMVR, 1989 and comply with PM E-DRIVE eligibility criteria; all e-2W/e-3W must be manufactured and registered within the validity period of PM E-DRIVE certificate; privately or corporately owned registered e-2Ws eligible in addition to commercial use; e-3Ws (e-rickshaws & e-carts) eligible solely for commercial use; e-ambulance eligibility criteria being developed in consultation with MoHFW; for e-trucks, incentive provided only against furnishing scrapping certificate issued by MoRTH-authorised RVSF for ICE trucks of equal or higher GVW; no more than one EV of a particular category per individual incentivised; EVs purchased by Central/State Government departments or agencies not eligible for demand incentives; resold EVs not eligible; import of PMP components and all other components of electric vehicles in CKD form from single supplier not permitted; import of battery modules not permitted; import of EVPCS PMP components in CKD form from single supplier not permitted

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: 
  - e-2W: up to ₹10,000 per vehicle (FY 2024-25) or ₹5,000 (FY 2025-26)
  - e-rickshaw/e-cart: up to ₹25,000 per vehicle (FY 2024-25) or ₹12,500 (FY 2025-26)
  - e-3W L5: up to ₹50,000 per vehicle (FY 2024-25) or ₹25,000 (FY 2025-26)
  - e-bus: up to ₹35,00,000 per vehicle (standard bus)
  - e-truck: up to ₹9.3 lakh per vehicle (GVW >35 & <=55 tonnes)
  - EVPCS: subsidy calculated as percentage of benchmark costs or actual cost, whichever is lower, with 100% on upstream infrastructure and EVSE for Category A locations, 80% upstream and 70% EVSE for Category B, and 80% upstream for Category C and D (BSS/BCS)
- Disbursement mode: Reimbursement to OEMs by MHI after upfront reduced purchase price; for EVPCS, first tranche (70%) after submission of undertaking, second tranche (30%) after commissioning and energization
- Duration of benefit: One-time per vehicle

#### Documents Required
1. Application for manufacturer registration
2. Company Registration Certificate
3. Trade License
4. GST Registration Certificate
5. PAN Card
6. Testing/Homologation Certificate complying with PM E-DRIVE eligibility criteria
7. Pre-registration of EV model as per Annexure-II
8. Complete set of memorandum of association (MOA) and articles of association (AOA)
9. Proof of OEM’s sales and service network
10. Photographs of the type approved Vehicle
11. Undertaking for reimbursement claim
12. Board resolution authorising signatory
13. Photo identification document (PAN/Voter ID/Passport/Driving Licence)
14. Certificate of Deposit (CD) for e-trucks (from MoRTH-authorised RVSF)
15. Undertaking for first tranche subsidy disbursement (EVPCS)

#### Application Process
1. OEMs apply for registration with MHI by submitting application form, company registration certificate, trade license, GST registration, PAN card, testing/homologation certificate, pre-registration of EV model, MOA/AOA, proof of sales/service network, and photographs of type-approved vehicle.
2. After MHI approval, OEMs upload production data of EVs for which they intend to claim incentives.
3. At time of sale, OEMs must deduct incentive after all taxes and ensure invoice amount is charged only from dealer.
4. OEMs submit reimbursement claims weekly but not later than 120 days from sale of vehicle at dealer end, accompanied by an undertaking on company letterhead.
5. Claims are submitted online via the PM E-DRIVE portal.
6. For e-trucks, buyers must furnish Certificate of Deposit (CD) from MoRTH-authorised RVSF for ICE trucks of equal or higher GVW; dealer verifies CD authenticity via portal.
7. For EVPCS, eligible entities appoint nodal agency to aggregate demand, submit proposal via online portal specifying locations, number of chargers, vehicle segments, charger capacities, subsidy requested, and implementation mechanism.
8. MHI reviews and approves proposal with modifications if needed.
9. Upon approval, nodal agency/CPO initiates procurement, applies for electricity connections, and requests first tranche (70% of eligible subsidy) after submitting undertaking on demand notes, PMP compliance, and charging standards.
10. After commissioning and energization, second tranche (30%) is released after submitting undertaking on successful commissioning, PMP compliance, and onboarding on National Unified Hub.
