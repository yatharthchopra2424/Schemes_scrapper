# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
PM E-DRIVE (PM Electric Drive Revolution in Innovative Vehicle Enhancement) is a subsidy scheme launched by the Ministry of Heavy Industries, Government of India, with a total outlay of ₹10,900 crore. It was notified via Gazette S.O. 4259(E) on 29th September 2024 and is implemented from 1st October 2024 to 31st March 2028 (with extensions for specific vehicle segments). The scheme aims to accelerate electric vehicle (EV) adoption, develop charging infrastructure, and strengthen the EV manufacturing ecosystem in India.

### Objectives
- Faster adoption of electric vehicles (EVs)
- Setting up of charging infrastructure
- Development of EV manufacturing eco-system
- Promotion of electric mobility in the country
- Incentivize ~24.79 lakh electric two-wheelers (e-2Ws)
- Incentivize around 3.2 lakh electric three-wheelers (e-3Ws)
- Incentivize over 14,000 e-buses across nine major cities
- Upgrade and modernize testing agencies under MHI

### Eligibility Matrix
| Vehicle Segment | Eligibility Criteria | Manufacturing & Registration Period | Special Conditions |
|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------|
| **e-2W** | Registered under CMVR, 1989; equipped with advanced batteries | Must be manufactured and registered within PM E-DRIVE certificate validity period | Both commercial and privately/corporately owned registered e-2Ws eligible |
| **e-3W (e-rickshaw & e-cart)** | Registered under CMVR, 1989; equipped with advanced batteries; solely for commercial use | Must be manufactured and registered within PM E-DRIVE certificate validity period | Eligible solely for commercial use |
| **e-3W (L5)** | Registered under CMVR, 1989; equipped with advanced batteries | Closed on 26-12-2025; no new registrations eligible after this date | Terminal date for registered e-3W (L5) is 26-12-2025 |
| **e-Ambulance** | Eligibility criteria under development in consultation with MoHFW | To be notified separately | — |
| **e-Truck (N2/N3)** | Registered under CMVR, 1989; N2: 3.5–12 tonnes GVW; N3: 12–55 tonnes GVW | Must be manufactured and registered within PM E-DRIVE certificate validity period | Incentive only against furnishing Certificate of Deposit (CD) from MoRTH-authorised RVSF for ICE trucks of equal/higher GVW |
| **e-Bus** | Registered under CMVR, 1989; must meet size-based incentive caps | Must be manufactured and registered within PM E-DRIVE certificate validity period | Only e-buses with ex-factory price < ₹2 crore incentivised |
| **EV Public Charging Stations (EVPCS)** | GoI ministries, CPSEs, States/UTs, PSUs under them | Nodal agency appointed to aggregate demand | Subsidy based on location category (A/B/C/D) and benchmark costs |
| **Battery Swapping/Charging Stations (BSS/BCS)** | Same as EVPCS | Nodal agency appointed to aggregate demand | 80% subsidy on upstream infrastructure only |

> **Important Notes on Eligibility:**
> - No more than one EV of a particular category per individual will be incentivised.
> - EVs purchased by Central/State Government departments or agencies are **not eligible** for demand incentives.
> - Resold EVs are **not eligible**; only original purchase qualifies.
> - Import of PMP components and all other components of EVs in CKD form from a single supplier is **not permitted**.
> - Import of battery modules shall **not be permitted**.
> - Import of EVPCS PMP components in CKD form (from single supplier) shall **not be permitted**.

### Benefits & Financial Support

#### Demand Incentive Structure
The demand incentive is the **lowest** of:
1. ₹5,000 × battery capacity in kWh (FY 2024-25) or ₹2,500 × battery capacity in kWh (FY 2025-26) for e-2W/e-3W
2. 10% of ex-factory price (excluding trailer for e-trucks)
3. Maximum incentive based on GVW (as per tables below)

#### EV Public Charging Stations (EVPCS) Subsidy
Subsidy is calculated as the **lower** of:
- % of benchmark costs (published by BEE, MoP)
- % of actual cost
All refundable deposits excluded from eligible costs.

| Location Category | Description | % Subsidy on Upstream Infrastructure | % Subsidy on EVSE |
|-------------------|-------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------|
| **A** | State/Central Govt. Premises (govt offices, hospitals, educational institutes, CPSEs, etc.) with free public access | 100% | 100% |
| **B** | Locations in cities/highways owned/controlled by State/Central Govt or PSUs (railway stations, airports, STU bus stations, metro stations, municipal parking, public sector ports, NHAI/State govt toll plazas) | 80% | 70% |
| **C** | All other locations (city streets, malls, market complexes, highways/expressways not in A/B) | 80% | 0% |
| **D** | Battery Swapping Stations (BSS) / Battery Charging Stations (BCS) at any location | 80% | 0% |

### Application Process
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[OEM Applies for Registration with MHI] --> B[Submit: Application Form, Company Reg Cert, Trade License, GST Reg, PAN, Testing/Homologation Cert, Pre-registration of EV Model, MOA/AOA, Proof of Sales/Service Network, Photos of Type-Approved Vehicle]
    B --> C{MHI Approval?}
    C -->|Yes| D[OEM Uploads Production Data of EVs for Incentive Claim]
    C -->|No| E[Revise & Resubmit Application]
    D --> F[At Time of Sale: Deduct Incentive After All Taxes; Invoice Amount Charged Only from Dealer]
    F --> G[OEM Submits Reimbursement Claim Weekly but Not Later Than 120 Days from Sale at Dealer End]
    G --> H[Submit Undertaking on Company Letterhead]
    H --> I[Claims Submitted Online via PM E-DRIVE Portal: https://pmedrive.heavyindustries.gov.in/]
    I --> J{MHI Reviews Claim}
    J -->|Approved| K[Subsidy Disbursed to OEM]
    J -->|Rejected| L[OEM Addresses Deficiencies & Resubmits]
    F --> M[For e-Trucks: Buyer Furnishes CD from MoRTH-Authorised RVSF for ICE Trucks of Equal/Higher GVW]
    M --> N[Dealer Verifies CD Authenticity via Portal]
    N --> O[Proceed with Sale]
    F --> P[For EVPCS: Eligible Entity Appoints Nodal Agency to Aggregate Demand]
    P --> Q[Nodal Agency Submits Proposal via Online Portal Specifying: Locations, No. of Chargers, Vehicle Segments, Charger Capacities, Subsidy Requested, Implementation Mechanism]
    Q --> R[MHI Reviews & Approves Proposal with Modifications if Needed]
    R --> S[Nodal Agency/CPO Initiates Procurement, Applies for Electricity Connections]
    S --> T[Requests First Tranche (70% of Eligible Subsidy) After Submitting Undertaking on Demand Notes, PMP Compliance, Charging Standards]
    T --> U[After Commissioning & Energization: Requests Second Tranche (30%) After Submitting Undertaking on Successful Commissioning, PMP Compliance, Onboarding on National Unified Hub]
    U --> V[MHI Reviews Undertaking & Utilization Certificate]
    V -->|Approved| W[Second Tranche Disbursed]
    V -->|Rejected| X[Address Deficiencies & Resubmit]
```

### Key Caveats & Warnings
> **Fund Limitation:** Total payout under the Scheme is limited to the scheme outlay of ₹10,900 crore. If funds for the Scheme or its relevant sub-components are exhausted prior to the terminal date, the Scheme or sub-components will be closed accordingly (no further claims entertained).
>
> **Individual Limit:** No more than one EV of a particular category per individual will be incentivised.
>
> **Government Purchase Ban:** EVs purchased by Central/State Government departments or agencies are **not eligible** for demand incentives.
>
> **Resale Ban:** Resold EVs are **not eligible**; only original purchase qualifies.
>
> **CKD Import Ban:** Import of PMP components and all other components of electric vehicles (e-2W & e-3W) in CKD form (from single supplier) is **not permitted**.
>
> **Battery Module Import Ban:** Import of battery modules shall **not be permitted**.
>
> **EVPCS CKD Import Ban:** Import of EVPCS PMP components in CKD form (from single supplier) shall **not be permitted**.
>
> **e-Truck Scrapping Requirement:** For e-trucks, incentive will be provided **only** against furnishing Certificate of Deposit (CD) issued by MoRTH-authorised registered vehicle scrapping facility(ies) (RVSF) for ICE trucks of equal or higher GVW.
>
> **e-Voucher System:** The scheme portal generates an e-KYC Aadhaar FACE authenticated e-Voucher for the customer at time of purchase; link sent to registered mobile number.
>
> **Technology Requirements:** Vehicles must be equipped with advanced batteries (specific energy density ≥70 Wh/kg, cycle life ≥1000 cycles) and Electric Regenerative Braking System.
>
> **Local Manufacturing:** PMP compliance requires domestic manufacturing of key components (traction battery pack, BMS, DC-DC converter, VCU, on-board charger, traction motor, etc.) with specific effective dates for indigenisation.
