# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] IEC (Importer Exporter Code) is obtained and valid  
- [ ] Business is a manufacturer exporter, merchant exporter, or service provider  
- [ ] Pre-requisites as mentioned in Chapter 5 of Foreign Trade Policy and Handbook of Procedures are satisfied  
- [ ] Capital goods to be imported fall under the defined category (Capital Goods as defined in Chapter 9, computer systems and software part of capital goods, spares, moulds, dies, jigs, fixtures, tools & refractories, catalysts for initial charge plus one subsequent charge)  
- [ ] Intended use of capital goods is for pre-production, production, and/or post-production  
- [ ] Export obligation (EO) understanding is clear (physical exports required for EPCG benefits)  
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian national or entity registered in India  

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- IEC (Importer Exporter Code)  
- PAN Card  
- ITR (Income Tax Returns) of last 2–3 years  
- GST Registration Certificate  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (if applicable)  
- Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed / Proprietorship Proof  
- Address Proof (Utility Bill, Rent Agreement, etc.)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- ANF-5A application form (for EPCG authorization)  
- Documents as per Chapter 5 of Foreign Trade Policy and Handbook of Procedures (refer to FTP 2023 and HBP)  
- Installation Certificate (post-import, if applicable)  
- Export Obligation (EO) undertaking / Bond-cum-Legal Undertaking (BLUT)  
- Bank Guarantee (if required for EO fulfillment)  
- Details of capital goods to be imported (specification, value, HS code, supplier details)  
- Authorization for EPCG Committee request (if seeking policy relaxation)  
- Annual Reporting of EO Fulfilment (for post-approval compliance)  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format  
- File size per document should not exceed 5 MB  
- Names on documents must match exactly with IEC and PAN details  
- Self-attested copies where required; notarized if specified  
- Documents in English or accompanied by certified translation  
- Digital signatures (if using DSC) must be valid and verified

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is **not explicitly required** for EPCG scheme application per the evidence. However, applicants must refer to Chapter 5 of Foreign Trade Policy and Handbook of Procedures for pre-requisites. **Verify with nodal officer** if a project report is needed for specific capital goods or sector-specific cases.  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Ensure IEC is active and linked to your DGFT profile  
2. Download and fill ANF-5A form accurately from the DGFT portal  
3. Select correct application type: New EPCG Authorization  
4. Enter correct IEC, PAN, and business details matching official records  
5. Specify capital goods description, value, HS code, and country of origin  
6. Clearly state intended use: pre-production, production, and/or post-production  
7. Undertake Export Obligation (EO) as per FTP norms (typically 6 times duty saved over 6 years)  
8. Attach all required documents in prescribed format and size  
9. Declaration must be signed digitally or physically as per portal requirement  
10. Preview form before final submission to avoid errors  
11. Use helpline 1800-572-1550 / 1800-111-550 or email dgftedi[at]nic[dot]in for technical support  
12. Refer to EPCG Help Document and FAQs on DGFT portal for guidance  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All fields in ANF-5A form are complete and accurate  
- [ ] IEC, PAN, GST details match across documents  
- [ ] Capital goods list aligns with EPCG-eligible items (Chapter 9, software, spares, etc.)  
- [ ] Export Obligation (EO) is correctly calculated and undertaken  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are attached and legible  
- [ ] Declarations are signed and dated  
- [ ] File sizes are within limits and naming is consistent  
- [ ] Application is previewed for errors before submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via https://dgft.gov.in/CP/?opt=epcg  
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing Application Reference Number  
- Save the reference number securely in case file  
- Set follow-up reminders for Day 25 and Day 35  
- Monitor email and portal for updates or queries from DGFT

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify EPCG authorization letter from DGFT  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned capital goods value and zero customs duty benefit  
- [ ] Ensure Installation Certificate is attached post-import (if applicable)  
- [ ] Monitor Export Obligation (EO) fulfillment timeline (typically 6 years)  
- [ ] Prepare and submit Annual Reporting of EO Fulfilment via DGFT portal  
- [ ] Apply for EO/Block Extension if needed (using ANF-5C or relevant form)  
- [ ] Pursue closure and issuance of Post Export Scrip upon EO completion  
- [ ] Maintain records for customs audit and DGFT verification  
- [ ] Coordinate with chartered accountant for utilization certificate and success fee invoicing
