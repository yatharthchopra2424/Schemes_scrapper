## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)

Many businesses in your sector are unaware they can import machinery, software, and tools at zero customs duty. I’d like to understand your current export operations and see if EPCG could support your growth.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your Importer Exporter Code (IEC) status — do you have an active IEC issued by DGFT?
2. Are you currently importing capital goods such as machinery, computer systems, or software for your production or export activities?
3. What type of exporter are you — manufacturer exporter, merchant exporter tied to a supporting manufacturer, or service provider?
4. Are you planning to import capital goods for pre-production, production, or post-production stages related to physical exports?
5. Have you previously applied for any DGFT schemes like Advance Authorisation or MEIS?
6. What is your approximate annual export value or volume — are you looking to scale up capacity?
7. Are you aware of the export obligation (EO) linked to EPCG authorisations, and do you have confidence in fulfilling it within the stipulated period?
8. Do you source capital goods domestically, or are you considering imports to access better technology or cost advantages?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many exporters face high upfront costs when importing capital goods like machinery, software, or tooling due to customs duty, IGST, and Compensation Cess. This strains working capital, limits technology upgrades, and reduces competitiveness in global markets — especially for manufacturers aiming to scale production or improve quality.

**Scheme intro:**
The Export Promotion Capital Goods (EPCG) scheme, administered by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) under the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, allows zero customs duty on import of capital goods for pre-production, production, and post-production. Additionally, capital goods imported under EPCG for physical exports were exempt from IGST and Compensation Cess up to 31.03.2020. Exporters may also procure capital goods domestically under paragraph 5.07 of the Foreign Trade Policy (FTP). Applications are submitted via ANF-5A form on the DGFT portal: https://dgft.gov.in/CP/?opt=epcg.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You hold a valid IEC, which is a mandatory pre-requisite for EPCG application  
- You are engaged in manufacturing, merchant exporting, or services — all eligible beneficiary categories  
- You intend to import capital goods such as machinery, computer systems, or tools for use in export-related production  
- You are willing to fulfil the export obligation (EO) tied to the authorisation, as required under Chapter 5 of FTP
