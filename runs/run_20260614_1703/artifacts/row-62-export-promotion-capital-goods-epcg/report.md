# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Export Promotion Capital Goods (EPCG) Scheme is a subsidy-type initiative implemented by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India. It operates on a pan-India geographic scope with a rolling basis application status—no fixed deadline, accepting applications year-round. The scheme was last updated in 2026.

### Objectives
The primary objectives of the EPCG Scheme are:
- Facilitate import of capital goods for producing quality goods and services
- Enhance India's manufacturing competitiveness
- Allow import of capital goods at zero customs duty
- Provide exemption from IGST and Compensation Cess up to 31.03.2020
- Support physical exports through capital goods import

### Benefits & Financial Support
| Benefit Type | Details | Validity/Notes | Source |
|--------------|---------|----------------|--------|
| Customs Duty Exemption | Zero customs duty on import of capital goods for pre-production, production, and post-production. | Applicable for physical exports under EPCG. | Key Facts, Crawled Page (https://dgft.gov.in/CP/?opt=epcg) |
| IGST Exemption | Exemption from IGST on capital goods imported under EPCG for physical exports. | Available only up to 31.03.2020. | Key Facts, Crawled Page (https://dgft.gov.in/CP/?opt=epcg) |
| Compensation Cess Exemption | Exemption from Compensation Cess on capital goods imported under EPCG for physical exports. | Available only up to 31.03.2020. | Key Facts, Crawled Page (https://dgft.gov.in/CP/?opt=epcg) |
| Domestic Market Procurement | Option to procure capital goods from domestic market in accordance with paragraph 5.07 of FTP. | Alternative to import route. | Key Facts, Crawled Page (https://dgft.gov.in/CP/?opt=epcg) |

> **Important Caveat**: The IGST and Compensation Cess exemption under EPCG is available **only up to 31.03.2020**. Post this date, these exemptions are no longer applicable, though zero customs duty benefit continues.

### Application Process
The application process for EPCG involves the following steps, accessible via the DGFT portal:

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Start: Ensure IEC is obtained] --> B[Refer to Chapter 5 of FTP and Handbook of Procedures for pre-requisites]
    B --> C[Apply for EPCG via ANF-5A form on DGFT portal]
    C --> D[Access EPCG Services Portal]
    D --> E[Amendment of Fields of EPCG Authorisation]
    D --> F[Invalidation/Certificate of Supplies of EPCG Authorisation]
    D --> G[Revalidation of EPCG Authorisation]
    D --> H[Clubbing of EPCG Authorisation (ANF-5C)]
    D --> I[Closure of EPCG/Issuance of Post Export Scrip]
    D --> J[Attach Installation Certificate to EPCG Authorisation]
    D --> K[Check License Status at Customs]
    D --> L[Apply for EO/Block Extension]
    D --> M[Check Authorisation Transmission Status from DGFT to CBIC]
    D --> N[Manual EODC Status Update]
    D --> O[Apply for EPCG Committee Request]
    D --> P[Apply for Review Application of EPCG (ANF-2D)]
    D --> Q[Transfer of Authorisation]
    D --> R[Annual Reporting of EO Fulfilment]
    D --> S[Help and FAQs]
```

### Required Documents
1. IEC (Importer Exporter Code)  
2. ANF-5A application form  
3. Documents as per Chapter 5 of Foreign Trade Policy and Handbook of Procedures  

> **Note**: The scheme supports a wide range of services including amendment, invalidation, revalidation, clubbing, closure, installation certificate, license status, EO/block extension, authorisation transmission, manual EODC status update, EPCG committee request, review application, transfer of authorisation, annual reporting of EO fulfilment, and help/FAQs through the DGFT portal.

## Consultant's Field Guide to Generated Files

### 1. SCHEME_MASTER_DATABASE.md
**Real-time Usage**: Keep this open in a background tab during all client calls. When a client asks "What is the turnover limit?" or "Who administers this?", CTRL+F in this document to give an immediate, authoritative answer without checking the portal.  
**Specific Scenarios**:  
- During a discovery call, if a client queries about eligibility (e.g., "Do service providers qualify?"), instantly verify using the eligibility matrix.  
- When discussing benefits, quickly confirm the IGST exemption cutoff date (31.03.2020) to set accurate expectations.  
- If a client asks about the implementing agency, retrieve "Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT)" in seconds.  

### 2. PITCH_AND_SALES_SCRIPTS.md
**Real-time Usage**: Open this file 5 minutes before your first Discovery Call with a lead. Read the "Problem Framing" out loud to hook them, then use the Qualification Checklist to interrogate their eligibility live on the phone. Keep the Objection Handlers table visible so you can immediately counter when they say "We're too small for this."  
**Specific Scenarios**:  
- **Problem Framing**: Use the script: "Many manufacturers struggle with high capital goods import costs eating into export margins—have you faced this?" to engage merchant/exporter leads.  
- **Qualification Checklist**: Ask: "Do you have a valid IEC?" and "Are you importing capital goods for pre-production, production, or post-production?" to filter leads.  
- **Objection Handling**: If client says "We’re too small," respond with: "EPCG has no minimum turnover—service providers and merchant exporters with IEC qualify, regardless of scale."  

### 3. APPLICATION_PLAYBOOK.md
**Real-time Usage**: Print this out or pin it to your desktop once the client signs the retainer. Check off each box in "Stage 1" before moving to "Stage 2". Use the "Client Communication Template" to copy-paste directly into your email when chasing them for pending documents.  
**Specific Scenarios**:  
- **Stage 1 (Pre-application)**: Verify IEC validity and Chapter 5 pre-requisites; check off after client provides IEC copy and FTP reference notes.  
- **Stage 2 (Form Preparation)**: Use the ANF-5A checklist to ensure all fields (e.g., capital goods description, IEC details) are complete before submission.  
- **Chasing Documents**: When client delays sending capital goods invoice, use the template: "Per DGFT EPCG guidelines, we require the proforma invoice for capital goods to file ANF-5A. Kindly share by [date] to avoid timeline slippage."  

### 4. CLIENT_ONBOARDING_AND_CRM.md
**Real-time Usage**: Fill this out during or immediately after the onboarding call. Use the Needs Assessment to record their exact pain points. Update the "Compliance Status" table as they email you documents to maintain a single source of truth for what's missing.  
**Specific Scenarios**:  
- **Needs Assessment**: Document if client’s pain point is "high customs duty on CNC machines" or "IGST burden on software imports."  
- **Compliance Status Tracking**: When client emails IEC, mark "IEC: ✅ Received"; when they send capital goods specification, update "Capital Goods Details: ⏳ Pending".  
- **Gap Identification**: If "ANF-5A form" remains ⏳ for >48hrs, trigger a follow-up call using the script from PITCH_AND_SALES_SCRIPTS.md.  

### 5. LIVE_CASE_TRACKER.md
**Real-time Usage**: Review this document every morning during your standup. Update the "Stage" column daily. If a case hits "Stage 07 - Under review", use the Escalation Path notes here to know exactly who to call at the government department today.  
**Specific Scenarios**:  
- **Daily Standup**: Sort cases by "Stage" descending; focus on those in "Stage 06 - Applied" to push for submission completeness.  
- **Escalation at Stage 07**: If case is "Under review" at DGFT, call the RA Helpdesk (1800-572-1550) and reference the application number to inquire about review timelines.  
- **Stage Progression**: Upon receiving "Installation Certificate" request from DGFT, update stage to "Stage 08 - Post-approval Compliance" and notify client via CRM template.  

### 6. FEE_AND_REVENUE_MODEL.md
**Real-time Usage**: Use this file when drafting the proposal. Look at the client's turnover, map them to the pricing tier in the table, and quote that exact Retainer and Success Fee. Use the monthly projection table to update your personal sales pipeline forecast for the quarter.  
**Specific Scenarios**:  
- **Pricing Tier Mapping**: For a client with ₹50 Cr turnover, apply Tier 2 (₹25-100 Cr) → Retainer: ₹1.5 Lakh, Success Fee: 8% of duty saved.  
- **Forecast Update**: After closing a EPCG client, add projected success fee (e.g., ₹4 Lakh) to Q3 revenue pipeline in the projection table.  
- **Proposal Justification**: Show client the tier table to explain why retainer is ₹1.5 Lakh (not flat fee), linking it to their turnover and service complexity.  

### 7. CLIENT_PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE.md
**Real-time Usage**: Copy this entire file, paste it into an email or PDF generator, replace the [PLACEHOLDER] tags with the client's actual details gathered from the CRM, and send it immediately after a successful discovery call.  
**Specific Scenarios**:

### 8. COMPLIANCE_AND_LEGAL_PACK.md
**Real-time Usage**: Attach sections 8A and 8B as PDFs to the proposal email. Refuse to start Step 1 of the Application Playbook until the client signs these. Use the Disclaimers to protect yourself legally if the client is rejected by the government agency.  
**Specific Scenarios**:  
- **Pre-engagement Gate**: Before collecting retainer, send 8A (EPCG Scheme Terms) and 8B (Consultant Engagement Letter); halt work until signed copies are received.  
- **Disclaimer Deployment**: If client’s EPCG application is rejected due to ineligible capital goods (e.g., non-capital item like raw material), cite Section 8B: "Consultant eligibility assessment is advisory; final determination rests with DGFT."  
- **Legal Safeguard**: Attach 8A to every proposal to prove client acknowledged IGST exemption cutoff (31.03.2020) upfront, preventing fee disputes post-rejection.
