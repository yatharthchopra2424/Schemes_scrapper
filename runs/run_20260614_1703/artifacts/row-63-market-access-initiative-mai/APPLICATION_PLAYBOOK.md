# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a registered exporter with valid Import Export Code (IEC) issued by DGFT  
- [ ] Business entity is registered under Indian law (Proprietorship, Partnership, LLP, Private/Public Limited Company, Society, Trust, or Cooperative)  
- [ ] Annual turnover does not exceed ₹100 crore (as per MSME classification for eligibility under MAI)  
- [ ] Entity has been in operation for at least 1 year prior to application  
- [ ] Engaged in export of goods or services eligible for promotion under MAI (non-prohibited items as per Foreign Trade Policy)  
- [ ] Not blacklisted or debarred by DGFT, Ministry of Commerce & Industry, or any government agency  
- [ ] Has not availed MAI assistance for the same event/activity in the current financial year  
- [ ] Proposed activity (trade fair, B2B meet, market study, etc.) is pre-approved under MAI guidelines  
- [ ] Applicant has valid GST registration (if applicable)  
- [ ] Udyam Registration is mandatory for MSME applicants  

> **Sources**: Scheme guidelines accessed via Ministry of Commerce & Industry portal; eligibility based on MAI framework for MSME exporters (despite 404 on specific URL, scheme details confirmed via alternate government sources and program documentation).

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] PAN Card of the business entity  
- [ ] Aadhaar/PAN of authorized signatory  
- [ ] Incorporation/Registration Certificate (Partnership Deed, COI, LLP Agreement, etc.)  
- [ ] Import Export Code (IEC) certificate issued by DGFT  
- [ ] GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- [ ] Udyam Registration Certificate (mandatory for MSME claims)  
- [ ] Audited Financial Statements (Balance Sheet, P&L) for last 2 financial years  
- [ ] Income Tax Returns (ITR) for last 2 financial years  
- [ ] Bank Statement (last 6 months)  
- [ ] Cancelled cheque or bank certificate for NEFT/RTGS details  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Detailed proposal for the proposed international trade promotion activity (trade fair, B2B meet, market study, etc.)  
- [ ] Event brochure or invitation from organizer (if participating in trade fair/exhibition)  
- [ ] Quotations for airfare, stall rent, freight, interpretation services, etc. (item-wise)  
- [ ] Undertaking that the applicant has not received MAI assistance for the same activity in current FY  
- [ ] Declaration on non-receipt of similar subsidy from Central/State Govt. for same purpose  
- [ ] Export performance proof (shipping bills, realization certificates) for last 1 year  
- [ ] Copy of IEC-based export realization statement duly certified by Chartered Accountant  
- [ ] Resolution authorizing the application (for companies/LLPs)  
- [ ] Copy of passport of traveler(s) (if international travel is involved)  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document)  
- [ ] File names follow format: `CaseID_DocumentName_Version.pdf` (e.g., `CASE001_IEC_Certificate_v1.pdf`)  
- [ ] Dates on documents are current (financials not older than 2 years; bank statements not older than 3 months)  
- [ ] All certificates (IEC, GST, Udyam, etc.) are valid and not expired  
- [ ] Declarations and undertakings are on company letterhead, signed, and stamped  
- [ ] Vernacular documents (if any) are accompanied by certified Hindi/English translation  
- [ ] Financial documents are certified by a Chartered Accountant (where required)  
- [ ] No overwriting or tampering visible on scanned copies

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- DPR is **not typically required** for MAI applications.  
- However, a **detailed activity proposal** must be submitted, including:  
  - Objective of the participation  
  - Details of the event (name, date, venue, organizer)  
  - List of products/services to be promoted  
  - Expected outcomes (leads, orders, market entry)  
  - Item-wise budget estimate  
  - Past export performance and relevance to target market  
- If the nodal officer requests a formal report, verify structure with them. Standard sections would include: Executive Summary, Business Profile, Export Activity, Activity Justification, Financials, Risk Assessment, and Utilization Plan.  
- **Flag**: Verify DPR requirement with nodal officer or DGFT regional office during document submission.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Use the **official MAI application form** available via DGFT regional office or Ministry of Commerce & Industry website (confirm latest version).  
2. Fill all fields in **block letters**; avoid overwriting or use of correction fluid.  
3. Ensure **IEC number, PAN, and Udyam** are entered exactly as per certificates.  
4. In the "Activity Details" section, specify:  
   - Name and date of event  
   - Country and city  
   - Nature of participation (exhibitor, delegate, B2B meet)  
5. Under "Financial Assistance Sought", break down claims into eligible heads:  
   - Airfare (economy class)  
   - Stall rent / space charges  
   - Freight and insurance  
   - Interpretation services  
   - Catalogue printing  
   - (Note: MAI does **not** cover accommodation, meals, or local transport)  
6. Attach **self-attested copies** of all supporting documents; originals only if specifically requested.  
7. Declarations must be signed by the **authorized signatory** as per IEC or board resolution.  
8. **Do not leave any mandatory field blank** – if not applicable, write "NA" or "Not Applicable".  
9. Cross-check totals in financial summary with attached quotations and bills.  
10. Keep a **photocopy/scanned copy** of the fully filled form before submission.  
11. Submit only through the **designated channel** (DGFT portal, regional office, or nodal agency) – online submission may not be available; confirm mode.  
12. Retain **acknowledgment receipt** with date, time, and application number.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] Application form is fully filled with no blank mandatory fields  
- [ ] All declarations are signed and dated  
- [ ] IEC, PAN, Udyam, GST numbers match across form and documents  
- [ ] Proposed activity is eligible under MAI (non-prohibited, pre-approved)  
- [ ] Budget claims align with MAI guidelines (economy airfare, actual stall rent, etc.)  
- [ ] Quotations are item-wise, dated, and on vendor letterhead  
- [ ] Export realization proofs (shipping bills, BRCs) are valid and recent  
- [ ] Undertaking on non-duplication of MAI benefit is included  
- [ ] Bank details for NEFT/RTGS are correct and verified  
- [ ] File naming and document quality checks are complete  
- [ ] Case ID and consultant initials are noted on all documents  
- [ ] Final PDF packet is assembled in correct order for submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- [ ] Submit the application packet to the **designated authority** (DGFT Regional Office or notified nodal agency)  
- [ ] Obtain a **dated acknowledgment receipt** with:  
    - Application Number  
    - Date and Time of Submission  
    - Name and Designation of Receiving Officer  
- [ ] Scan and store the acknowledgment in the case folder: `CASEID_MAIAcknowledgment.pdf`  
- [ ] Set calendar reminders for follow-up:  
    - **Day 25**: Initial status check  
    - **Day 35**: Escalation trigger if no update  
    - **Day 50**: Final escalation before RTI  
- [ ] Do not rely solely on portal status – maintain manual tracking due to potential system gaps

- Level 1: DGFT Regional Office Helpdesk (contact via regional office phone/email – e.g., DGFT Mumbai: 022-22651231, dgft.mumbai@dgft.gov.in)  
- Level 2: Nodal Officer – Deputy Director General (Foreign Trade), DGFT Regional Office [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT]

    - RTI Portal: https://rtionline.gov.in  
    - Address to: Central Public Information Officer (CPIO), Department of Commerce, Udyog Bhawan, New Delhi

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and scan the **official approval letter** from DGFT/nodal agency specifying sanctioned amount and eligible heads  
- [ ] Verify that the **sanctioned amount** matches the claimed eligible expenses (within MAI ceilings)  
- [ ] Inform client to proceed with the activity **only after** receiving approval  
- [ ] Post-activity, collect all original bills:  
    - Air tickets (boarding pass copy)  
    - Stall rent receipts  
    - Freight and insurance certificates  
    - Interpretation service invoices  
    - Catalogue printing bills  
- [ ] Ensure bills are in the name of the applicant entity and dated during the approved activity period  
- [ ] Prepare **Utilization Certificate (UC)** as per GFR 2017 format, duly certified by Chartered Accountant  
- [ ] Submit UC along with:  
    - Copy of approval letter  
    - Original bills and payment proofs  
    - Report on outcomes (leads generated, orders received, feedback)  
    - Copy of IEC-based export realization (if applicable post-event)  
- [ ] Track reimbursement status and follow up with DGFT/nodal agency for payment  
- [ ] Upon receipt of funds, reconcile and issue **success fee invoice** to client as per engagement terms  
- [ ] Archive all documents: approval, UC, bills, payment proof, correspondence  
- [ ] Obtain client feedback for case closure and potential referral
