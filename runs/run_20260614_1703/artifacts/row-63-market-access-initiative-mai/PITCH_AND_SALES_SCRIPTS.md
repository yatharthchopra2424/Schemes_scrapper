# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping Indian exporters access government support to expand into international markets — particularly through initiatives like the Market Access Initiative (MAI) scheme, which provides financial assistance for trade promotion activities such as participation in overseas exhibitions, buyer-seller meets, and market studies. Many businesses we work with weren’t aware they qualified for this support until we reviewed their export activities. I’d like to understand your current international trade efforts to see if MAI could support your growth — no obligation, just a diagnostic conversation.

### Discovery Questions
1. What products or services does your business currently export, or plan to export in the next 12 months?
2. Which international markets are you targeting or already active in (e.g., specific countries or regions)?
3. Have you participated in any international trade fairs, exhibitions, or buyer-seller meets in the last 2 years?
4. Are you currently conducting or planning any market research or feasibility studies for overseas markets?
5. What is your annual export turnover (in INR), or what percentage of your total revenue comes from exports?
6. How long has your business been engaged in export activities (or when did you commence exports)?
7. Are you a manufacturer, service provider, merchant exporter, or deemed exporter?
8. Are you registered with any Export Promotion Council (EPC) or commodity board recognised by the Department of Commerce?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant is an individual, partnership, company, corporation, cooperative society, or registered society engaged in export activities  
- [ ] Applicant is a recognised Export Promotion Council (EPC), Industry Association, or Federation of Indian Chambers of Commerce and Industry (FICCI) / Associated Chambers of Commerce and Industry of India (ASSOCHAM) / Confederation of Indian Industry (CII)  
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian diplomatic mission abroad (for specific activities)  
- [ ] Applicant is a Central or State Government agency, Public Sector Undertaking (PSU), or Agricultural/Processed Food Products Export Development Authority (APEDA) / Marine Products Export Development Authority (MPEDA) etc., engaged in export promotion  
- [ ] Applicant is involved in eligible activities: participation in international trade fairs/exhibitions, organising buyer-seller meets, conducting market surveys/studies, or similar trade promotion initiatives  
- [ ] Activities are focused on promoting India’s exports in overseas markets  
- [ ] Applicant has the necessary infrastructure and capability to implement the proposed activity  
- [ ] Proposal includes clear objectives, budget, timeline, and expected outcomes  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like your export activities in [specific market/activity mentioned] could align well with the Market Access Initiative (MAI) scheme, which offers financial support for exactly the kind of trade promotion work you’re doing — or planning. Let me walk you through how it works and whether you might qualify.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Indian exporters often face high costs when trying to enter or expand in overseas markets — whether it’s fees for international trade fairs, logistics for buyer-seller meets, or expenses for conducting market research. These costs can be prohibitive, especially for SMEs, limiting their ability to compete globally and diversify their export baskets.

**Scheme intro:**
The Market Access Initiative (MAI) scheme, administered by the Department of Commerce under the Ministry of Commerce & Industry, provides financial assistance to eligible exporters, trade bodies, and government agencies for undertaking export promotion activities. Support includes reimbursement of expenses for participation in international trade fairs and exhibitions, organising buyer-seller meets in foreign countries, conducting market surveys and feasibility studies, and similar initiatives aimed at boosting India’s exports. The extent of assistance varies by activity and applicant type but can cover up to a significant portion of eligible costs — subject to scheme guidelines and budget availability.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are actively engaged in exporting [product/service] to [market], meeting the core beneficiary criterion  
- Your planned participation in [specific trade fair/exhibition] or buyer-seller meet qualifies as an eligible activity under MAI  
- You have the operational capacity to implement the proposed activity, as evidenced by your existing export infrastructure  
- Your focus on [specific overseas market] aligns with MAI’s objective of promoting India’s exports in targeted international markets
