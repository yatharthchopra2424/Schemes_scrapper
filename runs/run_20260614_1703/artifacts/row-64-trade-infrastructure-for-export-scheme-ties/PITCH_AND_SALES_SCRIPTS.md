# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping businesses in the export and international trade sector access government support for infrastructure development. The Trade Infrastructure for Export Scheme (TIES) is designed specifically to strengthen India’s export capabilities by funding trade-related infrastructure projects. I’d like to understand your current export activities and see if TIES could support your growth plans.

### Discovery Questions
- What is the date of incorporation or establishment of your business or organisation?
- Are you currently engaged in export-oriented activities or planning to initiate exports from India?
- What type of trade-related infrastructure are you considering developing (e.g., land acquisition, building construction, plant and machinery for export promotion)?
- Is your project located within India and intended to support exports from the country?
- Are you a central or state government agency, a statutory body, a registered society, a cooperative, or a private company involved in export promotion?
- Have you secured or are you seeking financial support for the infrastructure project, and what percentage of the total cost are you looking to fund through government assistance?
- Is your project focused on creating new infrastructure or upgrading existing facilities to boost export capacity?
- Do you have clarity on the specific export products or markets your infrastructure will serve?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant is a recognised entity: central/state government agency, statutory body, registered society, cooperative, or private company engaged in export promotion
- [ ] Project involves development of trade infrastructure (e.g., land, building, plant and machinery) for export promotion
- [ ] Project is located in India and intended to support exports from India
- [ ] Applicant has or will incur expenditure on eligible infrastructure components
- [ ] Project is new or involves significant upgradation of existing facilities
- [ ] Export orientation is clear and demonstrable (specific products/markets identified)
- [ ] No prior funding received for the same component under TIES or similar schemes (to avoid duplication)
- [ ] Applicant can provide necessary documentation including project report, cost estimates, and export linkage details

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like your project aligns well with the goals of the Trade Infrastructure for Export Scheme (TIES). TIES offers financial assistance in the form of grants to support export-linked infrastructure development. Let me explain how it works and how it could benefit your initiative.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Problem framing:
Exporters in India often face challenges due to inadequate trade infrastructure — such as lack of modern warehousing, testing labs, or logistics hubs — which increases costs, delays shipments, and reduces competitiveness in global markets. Many businesses struggle to fund such infrastructure independently, limiting their ability to scale export operations.

### Scheme intro:
The Trade Infrastructure for Export Scheme (TIES), administered by the Department of Commerce under the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, provides grant-based financial assistance to eligible organisations for developing infrastructure that promotes exports from India. The scheme offers support up to a maximum of ₹50 crore per project, covering eligible components like land acquisition, building construction, plant and machinery, and other export-enabling facilities.

### Why they qualify (personalised):
- You are engaged in export promotion activities, which is a core eligibility criterion for TIES applicants  
- Your project involves developing trade infrastructure (e.g., [reference specific component from discovery]) within India to support exports  
- Your organisation falls under an eligible applicant category (e.g., private company, statutory body, or cooperative involved in export promotion)  
- The infrastructure you plan to develop is new or involves significant upgradation, meeting the scheme’s focus on capacity enhancement
