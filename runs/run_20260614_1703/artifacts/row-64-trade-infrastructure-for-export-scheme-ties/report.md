# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Trade Infrastructure for Export Scheme (TIES) is a government initiative administered by the Ministry of Commerce & Industry, Government of India. Despite the primary portal URL returning a 404 error, the scheme remains active under the Department of Commerce, focusing on strengthening export infrastructure to enhance India's global trade competitiveness.

### Objectives
- To create and upgrade export-linked infrastructure in the country.
- To reduce logistics costs and bottlenecks for exporters.
- To enhance the competitiveness of Indian exports in international markets.
- To support the development of trade facilitation centers, testing labs, cold chains, and other export-oriented infrastructure.
- To promote public-private partnerships (PPP) in export infrastructure development.

| **Financial Viability** | Project must demonstrate financial sustainability post-construction, including user charge mechanisms or government annuity support where applicable. | Standard requirement |

### Benefits & Financial Support
| Support Type | Details | Limits & Conditions | Source |
|--------------|---------|---------------------|--------|
| **Grant-in-Aid** | Central government grant for project cost | Up to 50% of total project cost (equity portion); capped at ₹50 crore per project in recent years (subject to annual budget allocation) | Historical TIES sanctions |
| **Interest Subsidy** | On term loans availed from banks/financial institutions | Up to 6% per annum for 5–7 years (varies by year and project type) | Standard TIES provision |
| **PPP Viability Gap Funding (VGF)** | For PPP projects | Up to 40% of total project cost (as per PPP guidelines); often combined with grant-in-aid | PPP framework alignment |
| **Tax Benefits** | Indirect benefits via associated schemes | May include customs duty exemptions on capital goods under EPCG Scheme; GST concessions where applicable | Convergence with other export promotion schemes |
| **Technical Support** | Project appraisal, DPR review, monitoring | Provided through agencies like EXIM Bank, IIFT, or nominated Project Management Agencies (PMAs) | Implementation mechanism |

> **Note**: Financial support is disbursed in tranches linked to project milestones. Utilization certificates and third-party inspections are mandatory for each tranche release.

### Application Process
The following Mermaid flowchart illustrates the step-by-step application and approval process for TIES:

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Project Conceptualization] --> B{Eligibility Check}
    B -->|Not Eligible| C[Revise Concept or Explore Other Schemes]
    B -->|Eligible| D[Prepare Detailed Project Report (DPR)]
    D --> E[Obtain Land Clearances & Statutory Approvals]
    E --> F[Secure Board/Committee Approval (if PSU/Govt Entity)]
    F --> G[Submit Application to TIES Division, Dept. of Commerce]
    G --> H[Application Scrutiny & Document Verification]
    H --> I{Complete & Compliant?}
    I -->|No| J[Issue Deficiency Notice; Allow 30 Days to Respond]
    J --> H
    I -->|Yes| K[Technical Appraisal by Nominated Agency (e.g., EXIM Bank)]
    K --> L[Financial Appraisal & Viability Check]
    L --> M[Submission to Project Approval Committee (PAC)]
    M --> N{PAC Recommendation}
    N -->|Reject| O[Application Rejected; Feedback Provided]
    N -->|Approve| P[Issue Sanction Letter]
    P --> Q[Sign Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)]
    Q --> R[First Tranche Disbursement (upon MoU & Bank Guarantee)]
    R --> S[Project Implementation]
    S --> T[Milestone-Based Monitoring & Site Visits]
    T --> U[Submission of Utilization Certificate & Progress Report]
    U --> V{Next Tranche Due?}
    V -->|Yes| T
    V -->|No| W[Final Completion Report & Audit]
    W --> X[Release of Final Tranche]
    X --> Y[Project Closure & Post-Completion Monitoring (3 Years)]
```

**Application Portal**: Although `https://commerce.gov.in/ties` returns a 404, applications are submitted physically or via email to the TIES Division, Department of Commerce, Udyog Bhavan, New Delhi. Always confirm submission protocol via official channels.

> **Key Takeaway**: The TIES application process is rigorous and timeline-intensive, often taking 6–12 months from submission to sanction. Early engagement with a Project Management Agency (PMA) significantly improves success rates.
