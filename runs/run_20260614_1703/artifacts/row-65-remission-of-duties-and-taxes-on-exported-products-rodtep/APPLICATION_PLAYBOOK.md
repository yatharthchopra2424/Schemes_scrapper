# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Business is registered as a manufacturer or exporter in India (Proprietorship, Partnership, LLP, Private Limited, Public Limited, or other recognized entity)
- [ ] Business is engaged in the export of goods (products) notified under RoDTEP
- [ ] Exports are made under Foreign Trade Policy (FTP) and are eligible for duty drawback or similar benefits
- [ ] Business holds a valid Importer Exporter Code (IEC) issued by DGFT
- [ ] Exported products are covered under the RoDTEP rate notification issued by the Department of Revenue
- [ ] Business has filed GST returns regularly (if applicable under GST regime)
- [ ] Business is not availing of any other duty remission scheme for the same exported products (e.g., not claiming drawback under Section 75 of Customs Act for same items)
- [ ] Exports are made in freely convertible foreign exchange (unless specific exemptions apply)
- [ ] Business has not been blacklisted or debarred by DGFT or customs authorities
- [ ] Export realization is received through normal banking channels (as per FEMA guidelines)

> **Note**: RoDTEP eligibility is product-specific and rate-dependent. Verify the latest RoDTEP rate list for the exported HSN code via [DGFT RoDTEP Portal](https://rodtep.dgft.gov.in/). Turnover, employee count, and age of business are not eligibility criteria under RoDTEP.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] PAN Card of the business entity
- [ ] IEC (Importer Exporter Code) certificate issued by DGFT
- [ ] GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)
- [ ] Udyam Registration Certificate (MSME, if applicable)
- [ ] Bank statement (last 6 months) showing export realization
- [ ] Cancelled cheque or bank account verification document
- [ ] Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) of authorized signatory
- [ ] Address proof of business (electricity bill, lease agreement, or property tax receipt)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Shipping Bills/Bills of Export for the claimed export period (customs-certified copies)
- [ ] Realization certificates from bank (FIRA/FIRC) for each export shipment
- [ ] GST invoices for the exported goods (if GST was paid on inputs)
- [ ] Packing List and Invoice for each export shipment
- [ ] Copy of IEC and GST registration linked to the shipping bills
- [ ] Self-declaration on non-availment of other duty remission schemes for the same products (format as per DGFT guidelines)
- [ ] RoDTEP claim statement in the prescribed format (as per DGFT Public Notice)
- [ ] Authorization letter for DSC holder (if different from proprietor/partner/director)

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per file unless otherwise specified)
- [ ] Names on all documents (PAN, IEC, GST, bank) match exactly as per business registration
- [ ] Export documents (shipping bills, invoices) show matching IEC, GSTIN, and product description
- [ ] Bank realization certificates (FIRC/FIRA) clearly indicate receipt in convertible foreign exchange (or exempted category)
- [ ] All declarations are signed and stamped by authorized signatory
- [ ] Documents are in English or accompanied by a certified translation
- [ ] DSC used for signing is valid and not expired (check via MCA portal)

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Not required**. RoDTEP is a duty remission scheme based on actual export realization and notified rates. No Detailed Project Report (DPR) or project proposal is needed.  
- **Verify with nodal officer**: Confirm if any sector-specific documentation is advised by the jurisdictional DGFT office, though standard practice does not require a DPR.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official RoDTEP portal: [https://rodtep.dgft.gov.in](https://rodtep.dgft.gov.in) (verify URL via DGFT website if redirected)  
2. Use only the authorized DSC for login and signing; ensure it is registered with DGFT  
3. Select the correct financial year and export period for which the claim is being made  
4. Enter IEC and GSTIN accurately; system will auto-validate against DGFT and GSTN databases  
5. Upload shipping bills one by one or in bulk (as per portal functionality); ensure each is customs-certified and matches the realized export  
6. Enter FOB value and realized amount in foreign currency and INR as per bank certificate (FIRC/FIRA)  
7. Select the correct HSN code for each product; the system will apply the applicable RoDTEP rate from the latest notification  
8. Do not claim RoDTEP on goods already benefited under drawback, GST refund, or any other remission scheme  
9. Preview the claim summary before final submission; verify total claim amount and document attachments  
10. Apply DSC to sign the declaration and submit; generate and save the acknowledgment receipt  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] IEC and GSTIN validated in portal match master records  
- [ ] All shipping bills uploaded are customs-certified and within the claim period  
- [ ] Bank realization certificates (FIRC/FIRA) correspond 1:1 with shipping bills and show actual receipt  
- [ ] FOB value in shipping bill matches invoice and realization document  
- [ ] HSN codes declared are correct and RoDTEP-rate notified for the product  
- [ ] No duplicate claims for same shipping bill or export realization  
- [ ] Self-declaration on non-availment of other schemes is signed and attached  
- [ ] All documents are in correct format, size, and language (English)  
- [ ] DSC used for signing is valid and belongs to authorized signatory  
- [ ] Acknowledgment receipt is generated and stored post-submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via [https://rodtep.dgft.gov.in](https://rodtep.dgft.gov.in) using valid DSC  
- Immediately after submission, download and save the **acknowledgment receipt** with Application Reference Number (ARN)  
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing ARN, date, and status  
- Store all submission evidence (acknowledgment, screenshots, DSC log) in the client’s case folder  
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days post-submission  
- Monitor application status regularly on the portal using ARN

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Download and verify the RoDTEP approval letter from the portal (check ARN status)  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned amount matches the claimed amount (or note any reductions with reasons)  
- [ ] Verify that the RoDTEP credit has been issued to the designated bank account linked to IEC  
- [ ] Utilization Certificate (if required): Not typically applicable as RoDTEP is a direct duty credit scrip (e-scrip) transfer; however, maintain records for audit  
- [ ] Maintain all records (shipping bills, bank realization, approval, DSC logs) for minimum 5 years as per Foreign Trade Policy  
- [ ] Inform client of successful claim and provide copy of approval and credit advice  
- [ ] Follow up for any discrepancies in credited amount within 15 days of credit notification  
- [ ] Prepare and submit invoice for success fee as per engagement terms  

> **Note**: RoDTEP benefits are issued as transferable duty credit scrips (e-scrips) via electronic ledger in the customs portal (ICEGATE). Monitor ICEGATE for scrip generation and utilization.
