## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**  
Indian exporters often bear the cost of duties and taxes (such as VAT on fuel used in transportation, electricity duty, mandi tax, etc.) that are not refunded under existing mechanisms like GST or duty drawback. These embedded taxes increase the cost of exported goods, reducing competitiveness in global markets. RoDTEP was introduced to address this gap by remitting such unrecovered taxes.

**Scheme intro:**  
The Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products (RoDTEP) is a WTO-compliant scheme administered by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT), Ministry of Commerce & Industry. It provides refunds as transferable electronic scrips (credited to the exporter’s ICEGATE account) based on a percentage of the FOB value of exports. Rates vary by product (HS code) and are notified periodically — typically ranging from 0.5% to 4.3% of export value.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**  
- You export goods (not services), which is the core beneficiary criteria for RoDTEP  
- Your exports are realised in foreign exchange (confirmed via your banking realization)  
- You maintain GST compliance and file shipping bills — essential for claim validation  
- You are not claiming RoSCTL on the same shipments (ensuring scheme compatibility)
