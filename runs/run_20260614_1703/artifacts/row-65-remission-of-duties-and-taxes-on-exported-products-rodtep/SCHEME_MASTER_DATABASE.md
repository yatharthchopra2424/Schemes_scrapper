### Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products (RoDTEP)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** India
- **Administering Body:** Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT), Ministry of Commerce & Industry
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://dgft.gov.in/rodtep
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Window:** Rolling

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Exporters
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: All export-oriented sectors
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Must be registered with DGFT and have valid IEC (Import Export Code); exports must be made under GST or customs procedures; only applicable to notified products and sectors as per RoDTEP rates notified by DGFT

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Refund of embedded central, state, and local duties/taxes not exempted or remitted under other schemes; rates vary by product (as per RoDTEP rate notifications)
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit to exporter's bank account via electronic scroll in GSTN
- Duration of benefit: Per shipment basis (claimable per export transaction)

#### Documents Required
1. Shipping Bill
2. EGM (Export General Manifest)
3. GSTIN details
4. IEC (Import Export Code) certificate
5. Bank realisation certificate (BRC) or e-BRC
6. Invoice and packing list
7. Proof of payment of duties/taxes (if applicable)
8. Self-declaration form as per DGFT format
9. Any other document as specified in the RoDTEP guidelines for specific products

#### Application Process
1. Register on the DGFT RoDTEP portal (https://dgft.gov.in/rodtep) using IEC and GSTIN
2. Upload shipping bill and EGM details for each export shipment
3. Submit claim with supporting documents (invoice, packing list, BRC, etc.)
4. System validates data against GSTN and customs records
5. Claim is processed and scroll is generated for approved amounts
6. Refund is credited directly to the exporter's bank account linked with GSTIN
7. Track claim status via the DGFT portal dashboard
