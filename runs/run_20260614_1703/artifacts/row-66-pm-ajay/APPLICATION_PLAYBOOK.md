# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] **Registration Type**: State Government or Union Territory Administration (as implementing agency)  
- [ ] **Geographic Scope**: State/UT must be among the 28 covered States/UTs (not Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Meghalaya, Mizoram, A&N Islands, Dadra Nagar Haveli, Daman & Diu, Ladakh, Lakshadweep)  
- [ ] **Component Selection**: Proposal must fall under one of the three components: Adarsh Gram, Grants-in-Aid, or Construction/Repair of Hostels  
- [ ] **Adarsh Gram Eligibility (if applicable)**: Village must have SC population ≥ 40% and total population ≥ 500 (per latest Census)  
- [ ] **Grants-in-Aid Eligibility (if applicable)**: Projects must be for socio-economic betterment of SCs; income generation projects prioritize families with annual income ≤ ₹2.50 lakh; infrastructure projects must be in SC-dominated villages (SC population ≥ 50%)  
- [ ] **Hostel Eligibility (if applicable)**: Higher educational institutions must be top-ranked per NIRF or other Central/State Institutes fully/partially funded by Centre/State; schools must be fully/partially funded by State/UT and recommended by State/UT Governments  
- [ ] **Financial Year Timing**: Action Plan (AP) must be prepared before the beginning of the financial year  
- [ ] **Perspective Plan Requirement (Grants-in-Aid)**: For Grants-in-Aid component, Perspective Plan must be submitted for the entire approved scheme period (up to 2025-26)  
- [ ] **Utilization Certificate (UC) Status**: Submission of UCs for previously released funds is required as part of the Action Plan  
- [ ] **Convergence Requirement**: Projects must avoid duplication with schemes under State SCSP; only activities not fundable from other SCSP schemes qualify  

**Sources**:  
- Eligibility criteria from KEY FACTS and guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf  
- Geographic scope from KEY FACTS and states-districts-villages-covered page  
- Financial year timing from KEY FACTS and guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf (Chapter 6)  
- UC requirement from KEY FACTS and guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf (Chapter 6, point 9e)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation/Registration Certificate of State/UT Government  
- PAN Card of State/UT Government  
- Audited Financial Statements / ITR of State/UT Government  
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (if applicable for any implementing agency under State/UT)  
- Authorization Letter from Principal Secretary of SC Welfare Department  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Annual Action Plan (AAP) for the financial year  
- Perspective Plan (for Grants-in-Aid component, covering up to 2025-26)  
- Utilization Certificate (UC) in GFR 12C format for previously released funds  
- Letter of Credentials for State/District Level Officers (sent to email of Principal Secretary)  
- Login credentials for web-portal access (to be obtained via support.pmagy-msje@gov.in)  
- Project proposals under Adarsh Gram, Grants-in-Aid, and Administrative Expenses components  
- Village Development Plan (VDP) finalized and approved by Gram Sabha and DL-PACC (for Adarsh Gram)  
- Details of works to be undertaken from Gap-filling funds (for Adarsh Gram)  
- Status of unspent funds and utilization plan  
- Details of Institute/University registration (for Hostel component): Institute name, UDISE/AISHE Code, NIRF Ranking, category, administrative ministry/state, department, funding category, nodal officer contacts, geographic location  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size 5 MB per document)  
- File names must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_StateName_YYYYMM.pdf` (e.g., `PM-AJAY_AAP_Maharashtra_202504.pdf`)  
- All certificates must be valid and not expired  
- UCs must be in GFR 12C format with proper signature, designation, date, and disclosure of actual expenditure  
- VDP must show approval by Gram Sabha and DL-PACC with clear timelines and budget  
- Institute/University registration form must be fully filled, signed, and stamped (as per format-for-institute-university-registration.pdf)  
- All documents must be in English or Hindi; if in regional language, certified translation must be attached

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is **not explicitly required** for the overall PM-AJAY scheme application. However, for specific components:  
- **Adarsh Gram**: Village Development Plan (VDP) serves as the planning document (not a formal DPR).  
- **Grants-in-Aid**: Project proposals must include activity-wise targets and financial requirements; format is provisioned in the online portal.  
- **Hostel Construction/Repair**: A detailed proposal with cost estimates, student list, and institutional commitment is required (as per guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf, Chapter 4, point 5c and 6xii).  

**Recommendation**: Verify with the nodal officer (Under Secretary, Shri Sewak Paul) whether a DPR is needed for specific project types under Grants-in-Aid or Hostel components.  

**Sources**:  
- guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf (Chapter 6, point c: "format for submitting the Perspective Plan...")  
- guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf (Chapter 4, point 5c: need assessment exercise; 6xii: policy & commitment scrutiny)  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Login Securely**: Use only the credentials sent to the Principal Secretary’s email; do not share or reuse passwords.  
2. **Component Selection**: Clearly select the correct component (Adarsh Gram, Grants-in-Aid, or Hostel) before starting the form.  
3. **Financial Accuracy**: Ensure all financial figures match the notional allocation and are in ₹ Lakh (as per portal format).  
4. **Activity-Wise Breakup**: For Perspective Plan, provide year-wise and activity-wise (Income, Skill, Infra, Tutoring, Hybrid) financial and physical targets.  
5. **VDP Linkage**: For Adarsh Gram, reference the approved VDP ID and Gram Sabha approval date in the project details.  
6. **UC Upload**: Attach Utilization Certificates in GFR 12C format; ensure they are for the correct financial year and component.  
7. **Declaration**: The authorized officer (Principal Secretary or designated nominee) must sign the declaration before submission.  
8. **Portal Timeout**: Complete submission in one session; the portal may time out after 15 minutes of inactivity.  

**Sources**:  
- Portal structure from aap-report-list/2025-2026 and aap-report-list-details pages (shows FYs wise break-up, Intervention wise Projects)  
- Login instructions from signin page: "All Institutes or Universities have to send the signed REGISTRATION FORM to email id support.pmagy-msje@gov.in to obtain the login details."  
- Financial format from notional-report and aap-report-list pages (₹ in Lakh)  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All mandatory fields in the application form are filled (no blank entries)  
- [ ] Financial totals match the sum of activity-wise and component-wise breakdowns  
- [ ] All required documents are attached and clearly labeled  
- [ ] Utilization Certificates are in GFR 12C format with signature, designation, date, and expenditure details  
- [ ] Village Development Plan (VDP) shows Gram Sabha and DL-PACC approval with date  
- [ ] Institute/University registration form (for Hostel) is complete, signed, and stamped  
- [ ] Declaration section is signed by the authorized officer (Principal Secretary or nominee)  
- [ ] Application is submitted under the correct financial year and component  
- [ ] No duplication with State SCSP schemes (verified via project description)

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing "Application Submitted Successfully" with timestamp and Application ID.  
- Store the Application ID/reference number in the case file and set automated reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days.  
- Download and save the submitted Application Form and all attached documents as a single PDF bundle for audit purposes.  
- Notify the Finance and SC Welfare Departments of the submission via official email, attaching the submission screenshot.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the approval letter from PACC via the portal or official communication  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount matches the applied amount (or approved revised amount)  
- [ ] Utilize funds strictly as per the approved project proposal and component-wise allocation  
- [ ] Submit Utilization Certificates (UCs) in GFR 12C format within the stipulated time (typically within 12 months of fund release)  
- [ ] Monitor physical and financial progress monthly and update on the portal https://pmajay.dosje.gov.in  
- [ ] For Adarsh Gram: Ensure VDP implementation timelines are met; coordinate with DL-PACC for any deviations  
- [ ] For Grants-in-Aid: Track activity-wise targets (Income, Skill, Infra, Tutoring, Hybrid) and report progress  
- [ ] For Hostel: Submit completion certificate to trigger second installment and one-time grant for cot/table/chair  
- [ ] Retain all records (approvals, UCs, progress reports) for audit and future submissions  
- [ ] Calculate and invoice success fee as per consultancy agreement  

**Sources**:  
- Fund release and UC requirements from guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf (Chapter 6, points 8b, 9e)  
- Progress update requirement from guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf (Chapter 5, point 5b(ii): "State/UTs would also be required to indicate in the portal the details of unspent balance...")  
- Hostel installment structure from guidelines-pm-ajay-may2023.pdf (Chapter 4, point 10a)  
- Approval letter: Standard government scheme procedure
