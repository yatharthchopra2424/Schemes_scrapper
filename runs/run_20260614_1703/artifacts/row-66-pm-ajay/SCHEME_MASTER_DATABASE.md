### PM-AJAY
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India (implemented in 28 States/UTs; not covered: Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Meghalaya, Mizoram, A&N Islands, Dadra Nagar Haveli, Daman & Diu, Ladakh, Lakshadweep)
- **Administering Body:** Department of Social Justice and Empowerment, Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://pmajay.dosje.gov.in
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Action Plans (APs) must be submitted before the beginning of each financial year. Perspective Plans for Grants-in-Aid component are to be uploaded on the online portal for consideration by PACC. No fixed rolling deadline mentioned; submission is annual and tied to financial year cycle.

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Not applicable (scheme targets individuals and communities, not businesses)
- Age of business: Not applicable
- Sector: Not applicable
- Revenue cap: Not applicable
- Employee count: Not applicable
- Other: Scheduled Castes persons living below the poverty line are eligible for benefits under Income Generating Schemes and Skill Development Programmes. For Infrastructure Development, villages having 50% or more SC population are eligible for grants under the Scheme. Guidelines from the erstwhile Planning Commission and procedures laid down by the Ministry of Rural Development for selecting beneficiaries through Panchayati Raj institutions may be adopted for defining poverty line and selecting SC families below poverty line.

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Subsidy of Rs 10,000 per beneficiary or 50% of the loan amount for income-generating schemes; up to Rs.21.00 lakh per village for Adarsh Gram component (Rs.20.00 lakh for Gap-filling, Rs.1.00 lakh for administrative expenses); financial assistance up to Rs.50,000 or 50% of project cost for asset acquisition under Comprehensive Livelihood Projects (if loan taken by beneficiary)
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit to State/UT governments based on notional allocation, SC population weightage (50%), and State SCSP to Annual Plan ratio (50%); funds released to implementing agencies for specific components
- Duration of benefit: One-time (for individual beneficiary support); Adarsh Gram Gap-filling funds to be utilized within two years from date of release; Perspective Plans cover up to 2025-26

#### Documents Required
1. Annual Action Plan (AAP)
2. Perspective Plan (for Grants-in-Aid component)
3. Utilization Certificate (UC) in GFR 12C format
4. Letter of Credentials for State/District Level Officers (sent to email of Principal Secretary)
5. Login credentials for web-portal access
6. Project proposals under Adarsh Gram, Grants-in-Aid, and Administrative Expenses components
7. VDP (Village Development Plan) finalized and approved by Gram Sabha and DL-PACC
8. Details of works to be undertaken from Gap-filling funds
9. Status of unspent funds and utilization plan
10. Details of Institute/University registration (for Hostel component): Institute name, UDISE/AISHE Code, NIRF Ranking, category, administrative ministry/state, department, funding category, nodal officer contacts, geographic location

#### Application Process
1. State/UT Governments prepare an Action Plan (AP) before the beginning of each financial year, including separate proposals under Adarsh Gram, Grants-in-Aid, and Administrative Expenses components.
2. For Grants-in-Aid component, States/UTs submit a Perspective Plan for the entire approved scheme period (up to 2025-26) via the online portal https://pmajay.dosje.gov.in, indicating activity-wise targets and financial requirements.
3. The Perspective Plan is placed before the Project Appraisal cum Convergence Committee (PACC) for consideration and approval.
4. Approved projects are conveyed to respective States/UTs.
5. States/UTs may expedite well-performing projects while keeping fund requirements within notional allocation for the financial year.
6. Based on progress of the previous year, States may propose modifications to the Perspective Plan with revised physical targets and financial requirements.
7. District-level projects are appraised by DL-PACC and recommended to SL-PACC (headed by Principal Secretary of SC Welfare Department) for inclusion in the Annual Action Plan (AAP).
8. Funds projection in Perspective Plan should be about 3.5-4 times the Notional Allocation to avoid approval shortfalls if projects are rejected by PACC.
9. Submission of Utilization Certificates (UCs) for funds released earlier is required as part of the AP.
