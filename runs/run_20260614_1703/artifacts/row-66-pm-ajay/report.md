# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
**Scheme Name:** Pradhan Mantri Anusuchit Jaati Abhyuday Yojana (PM-AJAY)  
**Scheme ID:** row-66  
**Ministry / Category:** Social Justice & Empowerment  
**Scheme Type:** Centrally Sponsored Scheme (grant)  
**Geographic Scope:** Pan-India (implemented in 28 States/UTs; not covered: Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Meghalaya, Mizoram, A&N Islands, Dadra Nagar Haveli, Daman & Diu, Ladakh, Lakshadweep)  
**Implementing Agency:** Department of Social Justice and Empowerment, Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India  
**Application Portal:** https://pmajay.dosje.gov.in  
**Status / Deadlines:** Action Plans (APs) must be submitted before the beginning of each financial year. Perspective Plans for Grants-in-Aid component are to be uploaded on the online portal for consideration by PACC. No fixed rolling deadline; submission is annual and tied to financial year cycle.  
**Last Updated:** 2023  
**Fund Size:** ₹95,700 Crore (as per Notional Allocation Report for 2026-27)  
**Max Per Entity:** ₹10,000/- per beneficiary or 50% of loan (under Income Generating Schemes component)  
**Financial Support:** 100% central funding. Under Grants-in-Aid component, subsidy of ₹10,000 per beneficiary or 50% of loan amount for income-generating schemes. Funds released to State/UT governments based on notional allocation, SC population weightage (50%), and State SCSP to Annual Plan ratio (50%). Allocation breakdown: up to 50% for Adarsh Gram (gap-filling), up to 5% for Administration, Monitoring and Evaluation, up to 2% for Hostel construction/repair, balance for Grants-in-aid at District/State level. States/UTs may incur up to 1% of total Central Assistance released for administrative expenses of State Scheduled Caste Corporations.  

### Objectives
1. Reduce poverty of SC communities by generating additional employment opportunities through skill development, income generating schemes, and other initiatives.  
2. Improve socio-economic developmental indicators by ensuring adequate infrastructure and requisite services in SC-dominated villages.  
3. Increase literacy and encourage enrolment of SCs in schools and higher educational institutions by providing adequate residential facilities in quality institutions and residential schools where required, especially in aspirational districts/SC-dominated blocks and elsewhere in India.

> **> Warning:** The proposal for enhancement of subsidy from ₹10,000 to ₹50,000 under the income generation component has been pending Cabinet approval since 2018-19. The current ₹10,000 subsidy is considered insufficient to incentivize beneficiary-level adoption.  
> **> Caveat:** Non-release of funds by State Treasuries to implementation Departments has resulted in poor implementation, especially during pandemic and in states like Karnataka due to poor budgetary planning.  
> **> Key Takeaway:** Savings under any component except Administrative Expenses can be redistributed only to other components/States against viable proposals subject to scheme guidelines. Post-completion infrastructure upkeep and operations must be managed by State Governments/implementing agencies using SCSP allocations.

#### Detailed Steps:
1. **Action Plan Preparation:** State/UT Governments prepare an Action Plan (AP) before the beginning of each financial year, including separate proposals under Adarsh Gram, Grants-in-Aid, and Administrative Expenses components.  
2. **Perspective Plan Submission:** For Grants-in-Aid component, States/UTs submit a Perspective Plan for the entire approved scheme period (up to 2025-26) via the online portal https://pmajay.dosje.gov.in, indicating activity-wise targets and financial requirements.  
3. **PACC Review:** The Perspective Plan is placed before the Project Appraisal cum Convergence Committee (PACC) for consideration and approval.  
4. **Fund Communication:** Approved projects are conveyed to respective States/UTs.  
5. **Project Expediting:** States/UTs may expedite well-performing projects while keeping fund requirements within notional allocation for the financial year.  
6. **Plan Modification:** Based on progress of the previous year, States may propose modifications to the Perspective Plan with revised physical targets and financial requirements.  
7. **District-Level Appraisal:** District-level projects are appraised by DL-PACC and recommended to SL-PACC (headed by Principal Secretary of SC Welfare Department) for inclusion in the Annual Action Plan (AAP).  
8. **Funds Projection:** Funds projection in Perspective Plan should be about 3.5-4 times the Notional Allocation to avoid approval shortfalls if projects are rejected by PACC.  
9. **UC Submission:** Submission of Utilization Certificates (UCs) for funds released earlier is required as part of the AP.  
10. **Hostel Separation:** Grants-in-aid for Hostel construction are not part of Perspective Plan; separate submission required via sub-portal.

## Consultant's Field Guide to Generated Files

### 1. SCHEME_MASTER_DATABASE.md
**Real-time Usage:** Keep this open in a background tab during all client calls. When a client asks "What is the turnover limit?" or "Who administers this?", CTRL+F in this document to give an immediate, authoritative answer without checking the portal.  
*Example:* If a client queries eligibility for hostel funding, search "NIRF" or "hostel eligibility" to instantly retrieve criteria: "Higher educational institutions which are top-ranked as per NIRF... State Institutes funded by Centre/State..."  

### 2. PITCH_AND_SALES_SCRIPTS.md
**Real-time Usage:** Open this file 5 minutes before your first Discovery Call with a lead. Read the "Problem Framing" out loud to hook them, then use the Qualification Checklist to interrogate their eligibility live on the phone. Keep the Objection Handlers table visible so you can immediately counter when they say "We're too small for this."  
*Example:* When a State SC Corporation says "We lack capacity for VDP preparation," use the handler: "The scheme provides Rs 0.8-0.4 lakh/village upfront for capacity building and VLCD training—let’s map this to your district’s needs."  

### 3. APPLICATION_PLAYBOOK.md
**Real-time Usage:** Print this out or pin it to your desktop once the client signs the retainer. Check off each box in "Stage 1" before moving to "Stage 2". Use the "Client Communication Template" to copy-paste directly into your email when chasing them for pending documents.  
*Example:* After client signs, verify Stage 1: "Confirm SC population data source" → tick. When chasing UCs, use template: "Per GFR 12C, please submit UC for FY 2023-24 by [date] to avoid fund recall."  

### 4. CLIENT_ONBOARDING_AND_CRM.md
**Real-time Usage:** Fill this out during or immediately after the onboarding call. Use the Needs Assessment to record their exact pain points. Update the "Compliance Status" table as they email you documents to maintain a single source of truth for what's missing.  
*Example:* During onboarding, note pain point: "Delayed UC submission causing fund holds." In CRM, mark UC status as "Pending" → update to "Received" upon email receipt, triggering next stage.  

### 5. LIVE_CASE_TRACKER.md
**Real-time Usage:** Review this document every morning during your standup. Update the "Stage" column daily. If a case hits "Stage 07 - Under review", use the Escalation Path notes here to know exactly who to call at the government department today.
