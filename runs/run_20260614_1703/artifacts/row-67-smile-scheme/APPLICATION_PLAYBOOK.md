# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian citizen  
- [ ] Applicant belongs to one of the target beneficiary groups:  
  - [ ] Transgender person  
  - [ ] Person engaged in begging  
  - [ ] Marginalized individual (SC/ST/OBC)  
- [ ] Applicant has been identified through a survey conducted by State Government/UT Administration or implementing agency/NGO  
- [ ] Applicant does not currently receive duplicate benefits from other Central or State schemes for the same purpose (self-employment/enterprise development)  
- [ ] Applicant is willing to undergo skill development and vocational training (if required for self-employment assistance)  
- [ ] Applicant has a viable business plan or project proposal for income-generating activity (if applying for self-employment/seed capital)  
- [ ] Applicant is not currently receiving recurring monthly stipends under this scheme (support is for initial setup only)  

> **Note**: There is no specified income ceiling, turnover threshold, employee count, or age of business requirement. Priority is given to those in extreme poverty or without sustainable livelihood.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] Proof of identity (Aadhaar card, Voter ID, Passport, etc.)  
- [ ] Proof of residence (Aadhaar, utility bill, ration card, etc.)  
- [ ] Bank account details (passbook copy or cancelled cheque)  
- [ ] Passport-sized photographs (recent, white background)  
- [ ] PAN card (if available; not mandatory but recommended for financial transactions)  
- [ ] GST registration certificate (if applicable for business activity)  
- [ ] Udyam Registration (if applicable for MSME enterprise)  
- [ ] Income Tax Returns (ITR) of last 2 years (if filed; not mandatory but supportive)  
- [ ] Bank statements of last 6 months  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Certificate of transgender identity (issued by District Magistrate or authorized authority) — *if applicable*  
- [ ] Proof of engagement in begging (affidavit or survey certificate from State Government/UT/NGO)  
- [ ] Business plan or project proposal (for self-employment or enterprise development assistance)  
- [ ] Enrollment/identification certificate from survey conducted by State Government/UT Administration or implementing agency/NGO  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are clear, legible scans or photocopies  
- [ ] File size for each document does not exceed 2 MB  
- [ ] Document names match the applicant’s name as per identity proof  
- [ ] Photographs are recent (within last 6 months), with white background  
- [ ] Affidavits are notarized on stamp paper of appropriate value  
- [ ] Business plan includes: activity description, investment required, expected income, marketing plan, and sustainability  
- [ ] All documents are in English or Hindi; if in regional language, certified translation is attached  
- [ ] No overwriting or tampering visible on certificates

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
✅ **Required** — A **business plan or project proposal** is mandatory for self-employment or enterprise development assistance under the SMILE Scheme.  

**Standard Sections to Include**:  
- Executive Summary  
- Applicant Profile (name, age, gender identity, background)  
- Description of Proposed Business Activity  
- Investment Required (item-wise cost breakdown)  
- Expected Monthly Income and Profitability  
- Marketing and Sales Strategy  
- Sustainability and Growth Plan  
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation  
- Timeline for Implementation  
- Declaration by Applicant  

> **Note**: The business plan must be viable and approved by the implementing agency. It should align with local market opportunities and the applicant’s skills post-training.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Use only the official application format** provided by the State Government/UT Administration or implementing agency — no self-created forms.  
2. **Fill all fields in block letters** (English or Hindi) to avoid processing delays.  
3. **Ensure name consistency** across all documents (especially Aadhaar, bank account, and application form).  
4. **Clearly state the type of assistance sought**: seed capital, subsidy, shelter support, or training.  
5. **Attach the business plan** as a separate annexure and reference it in the form.  
6. **Declare non-availment of duplicate benefits** from other Central/State schemes for the same purpose.  
7. **Sign and date the form** in the presence of an authorized witness (if required by the agency).  
8. **Verify portal-specific notes**: Since the SMILE Scheme is implemented through State-level surveys and agencies, applications are typically submitted **offline** to the District Social Welfare Office or designated NGO — verify submission mode with the nodal officer.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal details (name, DOB, address, gender identity) match identity proof  
- [ ] Bank account details are correct and active  
- [ ] Business plan is complete, realistic, and signed by applicant  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are attached and legible  
- [ ] Declarations regarding citizenship, eligibility, and no duplicate benefits are signed  
- [ ] Photographs meet specifications (recent, white background)  
- [ ] Application form is fully filled with no blank mandatory fields  
- [ ] Copy of entire application set is archived for reference

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- [ ] Submit the complete application package to the **designated implementing agency** (District Social Welfare Office, NGOs, or State Welfare Department) as identified in the survey  
- [ ] Obtain a **signed acknowledgment receipt** with application number, date, and officer’s name/signature  
- [ ] Take a **clear photograph or scan** of the acknowledgment for records  
- [ ] Store the application reference number securely in the case file  
- [ ] Set a follow-up reminder for **15 days post-submission** to check application status  
- [ ] If submitted online (rare), screenshot the confirmation page and save the application ID

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Collect and verify the **approval letter** specifying sanctioned amount and type of assistance (seed capital/subsidy)  
- [ ] Confirm the **disbursement method** (direct benefit transfer to bank account or vendor payment)  
- [ ] Ensure utilization of funds **only for the approved business activity** as per business plan  
- [ ] Maintain **bills, invoices, and receipts** for all expenditures related to the venture  
- [ ] Submit **Utilization Certificate (UC)** in the prescribed format within the stipulated time (usually 3–6 months post-disbursement)  
- [ ] Coordinate with implementing agency for **post-disbursement monitoring and mentorship support**  
- [ ] Track **invoice and success fee** as per consultancy agreement  
- [ ] Assist client in accessing **additional benefits** under the scheme: shelter homes, health cards, counselling, legal aid, or scholarships (if eligible)  
- [ ] Document **outcome and impact** for case closure and future reference  

> **Note**: The SMILE Scheme does not provide recurring monthly stipends. Support is for initial setup and rehabilitation. Beneficiaries are encouraged to achieve sustainability through skill training and enterprise development.
