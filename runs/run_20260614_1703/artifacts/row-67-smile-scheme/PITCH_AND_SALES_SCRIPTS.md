# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I’m reaching out because I specialise in helping transgender individuals and persons engaged in begging access government rehabilitation and livelihood support through the SMILE Scheme. Many people in these communities aren’t aware they qualify for financial assistance to start self-employment ventures, along with shelter, training, and legal aid. I’d like to take just a few minutes to understand your situation and see if this scheme could support your goals — no obligation, just a consultative conversation.

### Discovery Questions
- Are you a transgender person or have you been engaged in begging, as identified through a government or NGO survey?
- Are you an Indian citizen seeking support to start or grow a self-employment venture or income-generating activity?
- Have you completed or are you willing to undergo skill development or vocational training as part of a rehabilitation programme?
- Do you have access to or need temporary shelter with basic amenities like food and sanitation?
- Are you looking for one-time financial assistance to set up a business, such as seed capital or margin money?
- Do you require support with legal aid for identity documentation, counselling, or medical health cards?
- Are you currently receiving similar benefits from another Central or State scheme for the same purpose?
- Are you able to provide a basic business plan or project proposal for your intended venture?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Identified as transgender person or person engaged in begging via State Government/UT Administration or NGO survey  
- [ ] Indian citizen  
- [ ] Belongs to target group (transgender, begging, SC/ST, OBC, marginalized individual)  
- [ ] No income ceiling, but priority to those in extreme poverty or without sustainable livelihood  
- [ ] Willing to undergo skill training and submit a viable business plan  
- [ ] Not receiving duplicate benefits for same purpose from other Central/State schemes  
- [ ] Able to provide identity proof, residence proof, bank details, photographs  
- [ ] Prepared to submit business plan for self-employment assistance  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like you may be eligible for the SMILE Scheme, which provides one-time financial assistance for self-employment, along with shelter, training, and legal support. Let me explain how it works and how we’ve helped others in similar situations access this support.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Transgender individuals and persons engaged in begging often face systemic barriers to stable livelihoods, including lack of shelter, vocational training, financial capital, and legal recognition. Many struggle to access startup funds or support services due to complex application processes and limited awareness of available government schemes.

**Scheme intro:**
The SMILE Scheme (Support for Marginalized Individuals for Livelihood and Enterprise), administered by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India, provides one-time financial assistance in the form of subsidies and seed capital for self-employment and enterprise development. It also offers shelter homes with food and sanitation, skill development training, medical health cards, counselling, legal aid for identity documentation, and educational support. Financial support varies based on the venture and State-level guidelines, focusing on initial setup and rehabilitation rather than recurring stipends.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are a transgender person or person engaged in begging identified through official surveys (State Government/UT Administration or NGO)  
- You are an Indian citizen seeking livelihood support through self-employment or enterprise development  
- You are willing to complete skill training and submit a viable business plan for assessment  
- You are not currently receiving duplicate benefits for the same purpose from another Central or State scheme
