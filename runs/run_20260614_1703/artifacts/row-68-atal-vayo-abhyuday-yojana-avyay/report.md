# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The **Atal Vayo Abhyuday Yojana (AVYAY)** is a central sector grant scheme implemented by the **Department of Social Justice and Empowerment**, Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India. It aims to improve the quality of life of senior citizens across India, with a special focus on **Gap districts**. The scheme operates through the **e-Anudaan portal** (https://grants-msje.gov.in/ngo-login) for all applications and grant management.

### Objectives
- To improve the quality of life of senior citizens by providing basic amenities like shelter, food, medical care, and entertainment opportunities.
- To support NGOs in establishing and maintaining **Senior Citizen Homes (SrCH-25, SrCH-50)** and **Continuous Care Homes (CCH)** for Alzheimer's/dementia patients.
- To promote productive ageing through capacity building and skill development of senior citizens.
- To provide care and protection to vulnerable elderly persons, including those suffering from Alzheimer's and dementia.
- To encourage voluntary action for the welfare of senior citizens.
- To strengthen the outreach and effectiveness of senior citizen welfare programs through **Regional Resource and Training Centres (RRTCs)**.

### Financial Support & Benefits
AVYAY provides **Grant-in-Aid (GIA)** through the e-Anudaan portal. Assistance covers both **recurring and non-recurring expenses** for setting up and running senior citizen care facilities.

#### Grant Structure by Project Type
| Project Type | Beneficiaries | Coverage | Cost Norms Reference |
|--------------|---------------|----------|----------------------|
| Senior Citizen Home (SrCH-25) | 25 beneficiaries (all genders) | Infrastructure, staffing, operational costs, program implementation | Page 45-47 of AVYAY Scheme Guideline |
| Senior Citizen Home (SrCH-50) | 50 beneficiaries (all genders) | Infrastructure, staffing, operational costs, program implementation | Page 45-47 of AVYAY Scheme Guideline |
| Continuous Care Home (CCH) | 20 beneficiaries (Alzheimer's/dementia, all genders) | Infrastructure, staffing, operational costs, program implementation | Page 45-47 of AVYAY Scheme Guideline |
| Regional Resource and Training Centre (RRTC) | N/A (supports state-level programs) | Infrastructure, training facilities, outreach, stakeholder management, financial sustainability | Page 45-47 of AVYAY Scheme Guideline, PDF: 85831719577891.pdf |

#### Disbursement Pattern
- Grants are released in **installments** (1st, 2nd, 3rd/final) based on project progress and compliance.
- For ongoing projects: NGOs must apply for release of 2nd and 3rd installments to obtain remaining grant (e.g., 20% for FY 2024-25, 40% as second installment for FY 2025-26).
- No grants released without UDID number/UDID enrollment ID with Disability Certificate where applicable (DDRS-related).
- All financial transactions processed via e-Anudaan portal workflow.

> **> Key Takeaway**: The quantum and conditions of assistance are detailed in the AVYAY scheme guidelines. Cost norms are specified on pages 45 to 47 of the Scheme Guideline (available at https://socialjustice.gov.in/writereaddata/UploadFile/63491659437974.pdf).

#### Critical Process Notes
- **Deadlines**: Applications for new projects under IPSrC/AVYAY for 2024-25 were accepted from **01st July 2024 till 15th August, 2024** (as per notice dated 01st July, 2024). Deadlines vary by sub-scheme and year; applicants must check current notices on the portal.
- **Portal Maintenance**: The e-Anudaan portal undergoes scheduled maintenance (e.g., 09:00 am on 10th May 2025 to 11:30 pm on 12th May 2025). During maintenance, the portal is inaccessible.
- **Contact**: For queries, email **bharati.gusain@nic.in** with subject indicating the specific scheme (e.g., 'Query regarding new project under IPSrC').
- **Document Upload**: All required documents must be uploaded during online submission; incomplete applications will not be processed.
- **Verification Workflow**: The process involves application verification, checklist filling, inspection report, scrutinizing, screening, recommendation, financial approval, and GIA sanction through the portal.

### Scheme Variants Covered Under AVYAY
AVYAY encompasses multiple sub-schemes, each with specific portals and notices:
- **Integrated Programme for Senior Citizens (IPSrC)**: For SrCH-25, SrCH-50, CCH, and RRTCs.
- **National Action Plan for Senior Citizens (NAPSrC)**
- **National Action Plan for Drug Demand Reduction (NAPDDR)**
- **Support for Marginalised Individuals for Livelihood and Enterprise (SMILE)**
- **Scheme for Residential Education for Students in High Schools in Targeted Areas (SHRESHTA)**

> **> Important**: While this report focuses on AVYAY/IPSrC components (SrCH, CCH, RRTCs), consultants must verify the active scheme and sub-scheme from current portal notices, as multiple schemes operate simultaneously on e-Anudaan.

## Consultant's Field Guide to Generated Files

### 1. SCHEME_MASTER_DATABASE.md
**Real-time Usage:** Keep this open in a background tab during all client calls. When a client asks "What is the turnover limit?" or "Who administers this?", CTRL+F in this document to give an immediate, authoritative answer without checking the portal.  
**Pro Tip:** Use it to quickly confirm GAP district lists during eligibility checks—search for the state/district name to verify if a project location qualifies for IPSrC in Gap districts.

### 2. PITCH_AND_SALES_SCRIPTS.md
**Real-time Usage:** Open this file 5 minutes before your first Discovery Call with a lead. Read the "Problem Framing" out loud to hook them, then use the Qualification Checklist to interrogate their eligibility live on the phone. Keep the Objection Handlers table visible so you can immediately counter when they say "We're too small for this."  
**Pro Tip:** When a client mentions they work with senior citizens but lack infrastructure, pivot to the RRTC opportunity using the script’s "Alternative Pathways" section—it’s often overlooked but has less competition.

### 3. APPLICATION_PLAYBOOK.md
**Real-time Usage:** Print this out or pin it to your desktop once the client signs the retainer. Check off each box in "Stage 1" before moving to "Stage 2". Use the "Client Communication Template" to copy-paste directly into your email when chasing them for pending documents.  
**Pro Tip:** If a client delays sending audit reports, use the "Escalation Triggers" table to send a polite but firm reminder citing the 2-year experience requirement—this often motivates action better than generic follow-ups.

### 4. CLIENT_ONBOARDING_AND_CRM.md
**Real-time Usage:** Fill this out during or immediately after the onboarding call. Use the Needs Assessment to record their exact pain points. Update the "Compliance Status" table as they email you documents to maintain a single source of truth for what's missing.  
**Pro Tip:** Color-code the Compliance Status table: Red = missing, Yellow = received but needs verification, Green = approved. This visual cue prevents oversight during busy periods.

### 5. LIVE_CASE_TRACKER.md
**Real-time Usage:** Review this document every morning during your standup. Update the "Stage" column daily. If a case hits "Stage 07 - Under review", use the Escalation Path notes here to know exactly who to call at the government department today.  
**Pro Tip:** When a case enters "Stage 05 - Document Verification", add a calendar reminder for 48 hours later to check portal status—this catches silent rejections before they become delays.

### 6. FEE_AND_REVENUE_MODEL.md
**Real-time Usage:** Use this file when drafting the proposal. Look at the client's turnover, map them to the pricing tier in the table, and quote that exact Retainer and Success Fee. Use the monthly projection table to update your personal sales pipeline forecast for the quarter.  
**Pro Tip:** For clients near a tier boundary (e.g., ₹4.99 Cr turnover), run both scenarios in the model—sometimes the higher tier’s success fee structure yields better net revenue despite higher retainer.

### 7. CLIENT_PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE.md
**Real-time Usage:** Copy this entire file, paste it into an email or PDF generator, replace the [PLACEHOLDER] tags with the client's actual details gathered from the CRM, and send it immediately after a successful discovery call.  
**Pro Tip:** Before sending, delete all instructions and comment blocks (marked with <!-- -->) to avoid looking unprofessional. The cleanest proposals win trust fastest.

### 8. COMPLIANCE_AND_LEGAL_PACK.md
**Real-time Usage:** Attach sections 8A and 8B as PDFs to the proposal email. Refuse to start Step 1 of the Application Playbook until the client signs these. Use the Disclaimers to protect yourself legally if the client is rejected by the government agency.  
**Pro Tip:** Highlight the indemnity clause in Section 8B during signing—clients who balk often reveal hidden risks (like pending litigation) that could jeopardize the application later.
