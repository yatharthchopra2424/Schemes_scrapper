# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an existing or upcoming accelerator (organization or entity)
- [ ] Accelerator is based in India (Pan-India eligibility)
- [ ] Accelerator has capability to extend acceleration services to start-ups
- [ ] Accelerator is willing to undergo a multilevel screening process for selection
- [ ] Accelerator focuses on IT-based or product-based startups for scaling
- [ ] Accelerator aims to provide customer connect, investor connect, and international market access to start-ups
- [ ] Accelerator is committed to fostering innovation and supporting start-ups through product innovation, development, and growth
- [ ] Accelerator aligns with the objective of driving social impact and scaling solutions for India's challenges

> **Note**: The SAMRIDH scheme supports selected accelerators for extending acceleration services to start-ups. Accelerators are selected through a multilevel screening process. In the first round of cohort, 22 Accelerators spread across 12 states are supporting 175 startups.  
> **Source**: [MeitY Startup Hub - SAMRIDH Scheme](https://msh.meity.gov.in/schemes/samridh)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration Certificate
- PAN Card of the organization
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)
- Audited Financial Statements (last 2 years)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of last 2 years
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration Certificate (for MSME classification, if applicable)
- Cancelled Cheque / Bank Verification Letter
- Authorized Signatory Details and Board Resolution for application

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed profile of the accelerator (including year of establishment, focus areas, past performance)
- List of startups previously accelerated (if any), with outcomes
- Acceleration program methodology and curriculum
- Mentor and expert network details
- Infrastructure and facilities available for acceleration (lab, co-working, etc.)
- Partnerships with industry, investors, or corporate entities
- Plan for extending acceleration services to start-ups (including customer connect, investor connect, international market access)
- Declaration of commitment to support IT-based/product-based startups for scaling
- Any prior recognition or awards received by the accelerator

> **Note**: While the scheme does not explicitly list unique documents beyond universal ones, accelerators must demonstrate capability to extend acceleration services, which requires evidence of past performance, methodology, network, and infrastructure.  
> **Source**: [MeitY Startup Hub - SAMRIDH Scheme](https://msh.meity.gov.in/schemes/samridh), [SAMRIDH Program Details in Newsletter PDF](file:///path/to/1723a271-ea55-4867-880c-ad418fed329a.pdf) (refer to sections on SAMRIDH milestones and accelerator network)

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible and in PDF format (scanned copies acceptable if clear)
- File names must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_YYYYMMDD.pdf` (e.g., `SAMRIDH_IncorporationCertificate_20240515.pdf`)
- File size must not exceed 10 MB per document
- All certificates must be valid and not expired (check dates on GST, Udyam, etc.)
- Where applicable, documents must be self-attested by authorized signatory with stamp and signature
- For financial documents, ensure PAN and entity name match across all submissions
- Language: English or Hindi; if in regional language, provide certified translation

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **DPR Requirement**: Not explicitly mentioned in the evidence for accelerator applications under SAMRIDH.  
- **Action**: Verify with the nodal officer or MeitY Startup Hub whether a Detailed Project Report (DPR) or program proposal is required as part of the accelerator application.  
- **If required, standard sections would include**: Executive Summary, Accelerator Background, Objectives, Methodology, Startup Selection Process, Acceleration Services Plan (customer/investor/international connect), Budget Utilization Plan (for ₹40 lakh matching fund), Monitoring & Evaluation Framework, Sustainability Plan, and Team Details.  
> **Source**: [No DPR mentioned in scheme details; recommend verification](https://msh.meity.gov.in/schemes/samridh)

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official application portal via MeitY Startup Hub website: [https://msh.meity.gov.in](https://msh.meity.gov.in)  
2. Navigate to **Schemes > SAMRIDH** and check for open application windows or Expression of Interest (EoI) forms  
3. Fill all mandatory fields marked with * — do not leave any blank  
4. Ensure the accelerator name matches exactly as per Incorporation Certificate and PAN  
5. Enter correct GSTIN, Udyam number (if applicable), and PAN details — validate format  
6. Clearly describe the accelerator’s focus area (e.g., AI, IoT, deep-tech, sector-specific) and past acceleration experience  
7. Specify the number of startups targeted for acceleration and the support to be provided (mentorship, funding facilitation, market access)  
8. Declare commitment to provide one-to-one matching support and utilize the ₹40 lakh grant for extending acceleration services  
9. Upload all documents in the prescribed format and size limits  
10. Review the entire form before final submission — preview if available  
11. Accept the declaration and consent clauses before submitting  
12. **Portal Note**: The MeitY Startup Hub portal may redirect to SAMARTH 2.0 for scheme applications — ensure login/register on [https://msh.meity.gov.in](https://msh.meity.gov.in)  
> **Source**: [MeitY Startup Hub Portal Guidance](https://msh.meity.gov.in/), [SAMARTH Portal 2.0 Launch Details](file:///path/to/1723a271-ea55-4867-880c-ad418fed329a.pdf) (p.24-27)

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All sections of the application form are fully completed  
- [ ] Uploaded documents match the checklist and are legible  
- [ ] File names follow the naming convention  
- [ ] File sizes are under 10 MB  
- [ ] Authorized signatory has signed and stamped all self-attested documents  
- [ ] Declarations in the form are checked and agreed to  
- [ ] Reference number or application ID is noted post-submission (to be confirmed)  
- [ ] Cross-check eligibility criteria one final time  
- [ ] Confirm that the accelerator intends to use funds for extending acceleration services (not for core operations unless specified)

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the approval letter from MeitY Startup Hub specifying sanctioned amount (up to ₹40 lakh)  
- [ ] Confirm that the support is structured as one-to-one matching funding (i.e., accelerator must raise equivalent funds to unlock full grant)  
- [ ] Register the sanction in internal financial tracking system  
- [ ] Prepare utilization plan for the grant amount in line with extending acceleration services (mentorship, program delivery, market access activities)  
- [ ] Maintain separate books of account for SAMRIDH fund utilization  
- [ ] Submit periodic progress reports as required by MeitY Startup Hub (frequency to be confirmed in approval letter)  
- [ ] Utilize funds only for approved activities under the scheme  
- [ ] Prepare Utilization Certificate (UC) duly certified by Chartered Accountant upon completion of activities  
- [ ] Submit UC along with expenditure proof (bills, vouchers, bank statements) to MeitY Startup Hub  
- [ ] Follow up for release of any tranche-based funding if applicable  
- [ ] Coordinate with client to discuss success fee invoicing as per consultancy agreement  
> **Note**: Post-approval compliance includes proper utilization, reporting, and audit readiness.  
> **Source**: [Scheme Benefits: Matching funding support of up to ₹40 lakh](https://msh.meity.gov.in/schemes/samridh), [Financial Disbursement Details in Newsletter PDF](file:///path/to/1723a271-ea55-4867-880c-ad418fed329a.pdf) (p.2: "disbursed ₹6.73 crore to 23 SAMRIDH Accelerators")
