# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a Safai Karamchari (including Wastepickers) engaged in or employed for any sanitation work  
- [ ] Applicant is an identified Manual Scavenger (as per MS Act, 2013 survey or certified by competent authority)  
- [ ] Applicant is a dependent of a Safai Karamchari or Manual Scavenger (family member aged 18 years & above)  
- [ ] Applicant is a registered co-operative society of Safai Karamcharis  
- [ ] Applicant is a legally constituted association/firm promoted by the target group (Safai Karamcharis/Scavengers)  
- [ ] Applicant possesses a certificate from any one of the following authorities:  
    - Local Revenue Officer  
    - Local Municipal Officer  
    - Cantonment Executive Officer  
    - Railway Officer  
    - Head of Govt. Departments (Schools, Colleges, Forest, Health, Education, Animal Husbandry) with rank not less than Gazetted Officer  
    - Elected Member of Municipal Body  
    - Pradhan of Gram Panchayat  
    - Regional Manager of Regional Rural Banks (RRBs) or Nationalised Banks  
- [ ] If identified as Manual Scavenger under MS Act, 2013 survey, applicant’s name appears in the final list prepared by State Government/UT Administration (no certificate required in this case)  
- [ ] Project proposal/detailing income-generating activity is prepared (required for all loan-based schemes)  
- [ ] For Education Loan: Admission proof in recognized institute/university for professional/technical education (Graduation or higher) is available  
- [ ] No income limit is fixed for availing financial assistance (but priority is given to those with income below double the poverty line, women, and disabled persons from target group)  
- [ ] Financial viability and income-generating capacity of the project are assessable  

> **Sources**:  
> - Eligibility Criteria: https://nskfdc.nic.in/en/content/eligibility-criteria/safai-karmchari  
> - Eligibility Criteria: https://nskfdc.nic.in/en/content/eligibility-criteria/manual-scavengers  
> - Eligibility Criteria: https://nskfdc.nic.in/en/content/home/eligibility-criteria

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] Incorporation/Registration Certificate (for societies, firms, cooperatives)  
- [ ] PAN Card of applicant/entity  
- [ ] Aadhaar Card of applicant  
- [ ] Bank Statement (last 6 months)  
- [ ] Udyam Registration (if applicable for MSME)  
- [ ] GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- [ ] ITR of last 2 years (if applicable)  
- [ ] Passport-sized photographs  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Certificate from Local Revenue Officer **OR**  
- [ ] Certificate from Local Municipal Officer **OR**  
- [ ] Certificate from Cantonment Executive Officer **OR**  
- [ ] Certificate from Railway Officer **OR**  
- [ ] Certificate from Head of Govt. Department (Schools, Colleges, Forest, Health, Education, Animal Husbandry) with rank not less than Gazetted Officer **OR**  
- [ ] Certificate from Elected Member of Municipal Body **OR**  
- [ ] Certificate from Pradhan of Gram Panchayat **OR**  
- [ ] Certificate from Regional Manager of RRB/Nationalised Bank  
- [ ] Proof of identification as Safai Karamchari or Manual Scavenger (e.g., employment proof, ID card)  
- [ ] Proof of dependency (for dependents: relationship proof, age proof showing 18 years & above)  
- [ ] Project proposal/detailing income-generating activity (including cost estimates, viability assessment)  
- [ ] For Education Loan: Admission proof in recognized institute/university for professional/technical education (Graduation or higher)  

> **Note**: Under MS Act, 2013, if the applicant’s name appears in the final list of Manual Scavengers prepared by State Government/UT Administration, no certificate from the above authorities is required.

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible and scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document)  
- [ ] Document filenames match the content (e.g., "Revenue_Officer_Certificate.pdf")  
- [ ] Certificates are issued on official letterhead with seal and signature of issuing authority  
- [ ] Dates on certificates are valid and not expired (unless specified as permanent)  
- [ ] Name on all documents matches exactly as per PAN/Aadhaar  
- [ ] Hindi or English language only; if in regional language, provide certified translation  
- [ ] Project proposal includes: activity description, cost breakdown, income projection, repayment plan  
- [ ] Bank statements show consistent transactions and are stamped by the bank

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Required**: Yes, a detailed project proposal/detailing income-generating activity is mandatory for all loan-based schemes under NSKFDC.  
- **Standard Sections to Include**:  
  1. Executive Summary  
  2. Applicant Background (eligibility proof, experience in sanitation/allied work)  
  3. Project Description (activity, location, objectives)  
  4. Market Analysis (demand, competition, target customers)  
  5. Technical Details (process, equipment, raw materials)  
  6. Financial Plan (unit cost, promoter’s contribution, SCA share, loan amount, repayment schedule)  
  7. Income Generation Projection (monthly/annual revenue, profit estimate)  
  8. Employment Generation (if applicable)  
  9. Risk Assessment & Mitigation Plan  
  10. Conclusion and Prayer for Assistance  

> **Note**: For non-loan based schemes (e.g., awareness camps, exhibitions), a project report is not required, but participation details and expense claims are needed post-event.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official application form via: https://nskfdc.nic.in/en/content/how-apply/loan-application-form  
2. Fill the form in **English or Hindi** only; ensure all fields are completed accurately.  
3. Enter applicant’s name **exactly as per PAN/Aadhaar** (no abbreviations or nicknames).  
4. Select the correct **loan scheme** (e.g., General Term Loan, Education Loan, Mahila Samridhi Yojana) based on the project and eligibility.  
5. Enter project cost details carefully:  
   - For projects ≤ Rs. 2.00 Lac: No promoter’s contribution required  
   - For projects > Rs. 2.00 Lac: Minimum 5% promoter’s contribution + 5% from SCA is mandatory  
6. Specify loan amount requested:  
   - General Term Loan: Up to Rs. 15.00 Lac (max 90% of unit cost)  
   - Education Loan: Up to Rs. 10.00 Lac (India) / Rs. 20.00 Lac (Abroad)  
   - Other schemes: Refer to scheme-specific limits in Key Facts  
7. Enter bank details correctly for disbursement (account must be in applicant’s name).  
8. Upload all documents as per the checklist in Stage 2 (PDF format, max 5 MB each).  
9. Review the form thoroughly before submission; ensure declarations are signed and dated.  
10. Take a screenshot of the completed form before final submission for your records.  

> **Portal-Specific Note**: The form is hosted on the NSKFDC website; ensure stable internet connectivity during filling to avoid session timeout.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All applicant details (name, DOB, contact, PAN, Aadhaar) match supporting documents  
- [ ] Correct loan scheme selected based on project type and beneficiary category  
- [ ] Project cost, loan amount, promoter’s contribution, and SCA share calculated correctly  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents (certificates, dependency proof, project proposal) are attached  
- [ ] Universal documents (PAN, Aadhaar, bank statements) are complete and valid  
- [ ] Declarations in the application form are signed and dated by the applicant  
- [ ] File sizes are within limits (≤5 MB per document) and filenames are appropriate  
- [ ] Application is free of spelling/typo errors, especially in names and addresses  
- [ ] For Education Loan: Admission proof is attached and course is recognized/professional/technical  
- [ ] Submission timestamp is recorded and follow-up reminder set

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Click "Submit" on the application form at https://nskfdc.nic.in/en/content/how-apply/loan-application-form  
- Immediately take a **screenshot** of the confirmation page showing:  
    - Application Reference Number  
    - Date and Time of Submission  
    - Applicant Name  
- Save the screenshot in the case folder with filename: `NSKFDC_Submission_Confirmation_[CaseID].png`  
- Store the **Application Reference Number** in the case tracker and set automated reminders for:  
    - Follow-up at 15 days  
    - Escalation at 30 days if no update  
- Notify the client of successful submission with the reference number and expected timeline

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the **approval letter** from NSKFDC/SCA specifying:  
    - Sanctioned loan amount  
    - Applicable interest rate  
    - Repayment period and moratorium  
    - Any special conditions (e.g., promoter’s contribution verification)  
- [ ] Confirm that the sanctioned amount aligns with the requested scheme limits (e.g., General Term Loan ≤ Rs. 15.00 Lac)  
- [ ] Ensure disbursement is received through the State Channelising Agency (SCA), RRB, or Nationalised Bank as per application  
- [ ] Verify utilization of funds for the approved income-generating activity  
- [ ] Guide client on submitting **Utilization Certificate** (if required by SCA/NSKFDC) within stipulated time  
- [ ] Assist in setting up repayment schedule (auto-debit if possible) to ensure timely repayments and avail interest rebates (if applicable)  
- [ ] For Education Loan: Confirm interest reimbursement eligibility under Ministry of Education scheme (family income ≤ Rs. 4.50 lac/annum)  
- [ ] Document invoice for success fee as per consultancy agreement  
- [ ] Maintain all approval and disbursement records for audit and future reference
