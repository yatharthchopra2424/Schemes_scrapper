# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping Safai Karamcharis, Manual Scavengers, and their dependents access government financial support for income-generating activities. The NSKFDC scheme offers loans up to Rs.15.00 Lac for general term loans and up to Rs.50.00 Lac for cooperative ventures under Swachhta Udyami Yojana, with interest rates as low as 2% p.a. and flexible repayment terms. I’d like to understand your background and goals to see if this could be a fit.

### Discovery Questions
1. Are you engaged in sanitation work, or are you a dependent (family member aged 18+) of someone who is?
2. Do you have a certificate from a local Revenue Officer, Municipal Officer, or another authorised body (e.g., Head of a Govt. Department, Elected Municipal Member, Pradhan of Gram Panchayat)?
3. Are you considering starting or expanding an income-generating activity such as a small business, sanitation-related venture, or education pursuit?
4. What is the approximate cost of the project or activity you’re planning?
5. Are you part of a registered co-operative society, association, or firm promoted by Safai Karamcharis or Manual Scavengers?
6. Have you been identified as a Manual Scavenger in a government survey under the MS Act, 2013?
7. Are you a woman or a person with a disability from the target community? (This helps prioritise your application.)
8. Do you need support for education, such as admission to a recognised technical or professional course?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant is a Safai Karamchari (including wastepickers), identified Manual Scavenger, or dependent thereof (age 18+)
- [ ] Applicant holds a valid certificate from an authorised issuer (Revenue Officer, Municipal Officer, Cantonment Executive Officer, Railway Officer, Gazetted Officer from Govt. Dept., Elected Municipal Member, Pradhan of Gram Panchayat, or Regional Manager of RRB/Nationalised Bank) — OR is identified as a Manual Scavenger under MS Act, 2013 survey and named in the final State/UT list
- [ ] Applicant is proposing a viable income-generating activity or pursuing professional/technical education
- [ ] For loans > Rs.2.00 Lac, applicant can arrange minimum 5% promoter’s contribution (with 5% from SCA)
- [ ] Project cost aligns with scheme limits (e.g., ≤ Rs.15.00 Lac for General Term Loan, ≤ Rs.10.00 Lac for education in India, ≤ Rs.20.00 Lac for abroad, ≤ Rs.50.00 Lac for SHG/JRG/Cooperative under Swachhta Udyami Yojana)
- [ ] No income limit applies, but priority given to those below double the poverty line, women, and disabled persons

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like you may be eligible for financial support under the NSKFDC scheme — particularly the General Term Loan or Education Loan, depending on your goal. Let me explain how this scheme works and how we’ve helped others in similar situations access funding with minimal upfront cost.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many Safai Karamcharis, Manual Scavengers, and their dependents face barriers to starting or growing income-generating activities due to lack of access to affordable finance, despite having viable skills and business ideas. Traditional lenders often require collateral or credit history that may not be available, trapping talented individuals in low-income or informal work.

**Scheme intro:**
The National Safai Karamcharis Finance & Development Corporation (NSKFDC), a wholly owned Govt. of India undertaking under the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, offers targeted loan schemes including General Term Loan (up to Rs.15.00 Lac), Education Loan (up to Rs.10.00 Lac for study in India, Rs.20.00 Lac for abroad), Mahila Samridhi Yojana (up to Rs.1.00 Lac), Mahila Adhikarita Yojana (up to Rs.2.00 Lac), Micro Credit Finance (up to Rs.1.00 Lac), and Swachhta Udyami Yojana (up to Rs.50.00 Lac for SHG/JRG/Cooperative). Interest rates range from 2% to 9% p.a., with repayment periods of 3 to 10 years and moratorium periods. Promoter’s contribution is not required for projects up to Rs.2.00 Lac.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are engaged in sanitation work or are a dependent (age 18+) of someone who is — meeting the core beneficiary criteria
- You can obtain a certificate from an authorised body such as a Revenue Officer, Municipal Officer, or Gazetted Govt. Department official — or you are identified as a Manual Scavenger under MS Act, 2013 and named in the final State list
- Your proposed activity (e.g., [client’s project]) is income-generating and financially viable, which NSKFDC evaluates during approval
- If your project costs under Rs.2.00 Lac, no promoter’s contribution is required — reducing upfront financial burden
