# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The National Safai Karamcharis Finance & Development Corporation (NSKFDC) is a wholly owned Government of India undertaking under the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment (M/o SJ&E), established on 24th January 1997 and operational since October 1997. It serves as an apex corporation for the socio-economic upliftment of Safai Karamcharis, Scavengers, and their dependents throughout India through various loan and non-loan based schemes. NSKFDC has also been designated as the Nodal Agency for implementing the Central Sector National Action for Mechanized Sanitation Ecosystem (NAMASTE) scheme under M/o SJ&E.

### Objectives
- Socio-economic upliftment of Safai Karamcharis, Scavengers, and their dependents  
- Implementation of loan and non-loan based schemes for income generation  
- Rehabilitation of manual scavengers through self-employment schemes  
- Skill development training for target group members aged 18 to 45 years  
- Implementation of the NAMASTE scheme for a mechanized sanitation ecosystem  
- Providing financial assistance without income limits, prioritizing those below double the poverty line  
- Supporting women and disabled persons from the target group  
- Promoting viability and income-generating capacity in project financing  

### Eligibility Matrix
| Eligible Category | Specific Requirements | Certificate Alternatives (if applicable) |
|-------------------|------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Safai Karamcharis (including Wastepickers) | Person engaged in or employed for any sanitation work; includes dependents aged 18+ | Certificate from: Local Revenue Officer, Municipal Officer, Cantonment Executive Officer, Railway Officer, Head of Govt. Departments (Schools, Colleges, Forest, Health, Education, Animal Husbandry) with rank ≥ Gazetted Officer, Elected Member of Municipal Body, Pradhan of Gram Panchayat, Regional Manager of RRB/Nationalised Bank |
| Identified Manual Scavengers | Person engaged in manually cleaning, carrying, disposing of human excreta in insanitary latrines, open drains, pits, or railway tracks before full decomposition | **No certificate required** if name appears in final list of Manual Scavengers prepared by State Governments/UT Administration under MS Act, 2013 survey |
| Dependents of Safai Karamcharis/Scavengers | Family member dependent on Safai Karamchari/Scavenger, aged 18 years & above | Same certificates as Safai Karamcharis |
| Registered Co-operative Societies | Of Safai Karamcharis | Registration certificate from Registrar of Co-operative Societies |
| Legally Constituted Associations/Firms | Promoted by the target group (Safai Karamcharis/Scavengers/dependents) | Certificate of incorporation/registration, promoter declaration |

> **Key Eligibility Notes:**  
> - No income limit is fixed for availing financial assistance.  
> - Priority is accorded to: economic development and rehabilitation of Scavengers; those with income below double the poverty line; Women from the target group; Disabled persons among the target group.  
> - Financial viability and income-generating capacity of the project are considered during evaluation.  
> - For Skill Development Training Programme: age limit is 18 years to 45 years.

> **Key Financial Caveats:**  
> - Promoter’s contribution is **not required** for projects costing up to Rs. 2.00 Lac.  
> - For projects costing **more than Rs. 2.00 Lac**: minimum **5% promoter’s contribution** from beneficiary + **5% from SCA** (State Channelising Agency).  
> - General Term Loan can finance **maximum 90% of unit cost**; remaining 10% must be provided by SCA as loan, subsidy, promoter’s contribution, or other sources.  
> - Education loan interest reimbursement under Ministry of Education scheme **only available** for beneficiaries with family annual income **up to Rs. 4.50 Lac**.  
> - Interest rates range from **2% to 9% p.a.**, with rebates for women and timely repayment.  
> - Repayment periods vary from **3 to 10 years**, with moratorium periods applicable.

## Consultant's Field Guide to Generated Files

### 1. SCHEME_MASTER_DATABASE.md
**Real-time Usage:** Keep this open in a background tab during all client calls. When a client asks "What is the turnover limit?" or "Who administers this?", CTRL+F in this document to give an immediate, authoritative answer without checking the portal.  
*Example:* If a client inquires about the Education Loan limit for abroad studies, search "Education Loan" and "abroad" to quickly confirm Rs. 20.00 Lac as the maximum limit.

### 2. PITCH_AND_SALES_SCRIPTS.md
**Real-time Usage:** Open this file 5 minutes before your first Discovery Call with a lead. Read the "Problem Framing" out loud to hook them, then use the Qualification Checklist to interrogate their eligibility live on the phone. Keep the Objection Handlers table visible so you can immediately counter when they say "We're too small for this."  
*Example:* When a lead states their annual income is Rs. 5.00 Lac (above the Rs. 4.50 Lac threshold for Education Loan interest reimbursement), use the objection handler: "While interest reimbursement requires income ≤ Rs. 4.50 Lac, the loan itself remains available at preferential rates, and you may still qualify for other schemes like General Term Loan with no income cap."

### 3. APPLICATION_PLAYBOOK.md
**Real-time Usage:** Print this out or pin it to your desktop once the client signs the retainer. Check off each box in "Stage 1" before moving to "Stage 2". Use the "Client Communication Template" to copy-paste directly into your email when chasing them for pending documents.  
*Example:* After collecting the client’s certificate from the Municipal Officer and project proposal, check off "Documents Verified" in Stage 1, then proceed to Stage 2 (Approach SCA/Bank) using the predefined email template to introduce the client to the SCA.

### 4. CLIENT_ONBOARDING_AND_CRM.md
**Real-time Usage:** Fill this out during or immediately after the onboarding call. Use the Needs Assessment to record their exact pain points. Update the "Compliance Status" table as they email you documents to maintain a single source of truth for what's missing.  
*Example:* During onboarding, note in the Needs Assessment that the client struggles with high-interest debt. Later, as they send the Gazetted Officer certificate, update the Compliance Status table to reflect "Certificate Received" and flag the pending Project Proposal.

### 5. LIVE_CASE_TRACKER.md
**Real-time Usage:** Review this document every morning during your standup. Update the "Stage" column daily. If a case hits "Stage 07 - Under review", use the Escalation Path notes here to know exactly who to call at the government department today.  
*Example:* When a case shows "Under review" in the tracker, consult the Escalation Path to contact the Regional Manager at the State Channelising Agency (SCA) by 11 AM to inquire about review timelines, using the provided script: "Following up on application [ID] submitted on [date] – any update on evaluation status?"

### 6. FEE_AND_REVENUE_MODEL.md
**Real-time Usage:** Use this file when drafting the proposal. Look at the client's turnover, map them to the pricing tier in the table, and quote that exact Retainer and Success Fee. Use the monthly projection table to update your personal sales pipeline forecast for the quarter.  
*Example:* If a client’s annual turnover is Rs. 8.00 Lac, refer to the pricing tier for turnover < Rs. 10 Lac to quote a Retainer of Rs. 25,000 and Success Fee of 8% of sanctioned loan amount, then add this to your Q3 sales forecast.

### 7. CLIENT_PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE.md
**Real-time Usage:** Copy this entire file, paste it into an email or PDF generator, replace the [PLACEHOLDER] tags with the client's actual details gathered from the CRM, and send it immediately after a successful discovery call.  
*Example:* After a discovery call confirms eligibility for Mahila Samridhi Yojana, paste the template, replace [CLIENT_NAME], [SCHEME_NAME], [LOAN_AMOUNT], etc., with the client’s specifics, and send the proposal within 1 hour while engagement is high.

### 8. COMPLIANCE_AND_LEGAL_PACK.md
**Real-time Usage:** Attach sections 8A and 8B as PDFs to the proposal email. Refuse to start Step 1 of the Application Playbook until the client signs these. Use the Disclaimers to protect yourself legally if the client is rejected by the government agency.  
*Example:* Before initiating document collection, send the Compliance Pack (8A: Service Terms, 8B: Client Declaration) via email. Do not proceed with the Application Playbook until signed copies are received. If the loan is rejected due to project viability, cite the disclaimer: "NSKFDC’s evaluation criteria are sovereign; our role is facilitative, not guaranteeing approval."
