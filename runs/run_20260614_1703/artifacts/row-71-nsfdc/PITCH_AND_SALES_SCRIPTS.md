# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping Scheduled Caste entrepreneurs and professionals access government-backed financial support for income-generating activities, education, and skill development. Many eligible individuals miss out simply because they don’t know where to start or assume the process is too complex. Today, I’d like to understand your goals and see if the NSFDC schemes—offering loans up to 90% of project cost at concessional interest rates—could support your aspirations. Is now a good time for a brief conversation?

### Discovery Questions
1. Are you from the Scheduled Caste community, as this is a core eligibility requirement for NSFDC support?
2. What is your annual family income? (Note: For credit-based schemes, it must not exceed ₹5.00 lakh.)
3. Are you planning to start or expand an income-generating activity, pursue professional/technical education, or enroll in a skill development training programme?
4. If considering a loan, what is the estimated project cost or course fee you need financing for?
5. Do you have any existing partnerships or are you applying as an individual, partnership firm, or co-operative society? (Note: For firms/societies, all members must be from the Scheduled Caste community.)
6. Have you previously approached any State Channelizing Agency (SCA) or Channelizing Agency (CA) for financial assistance?
7. Are you aware of the specific NSFDC schemes like Term Loan, Micro Finance Scheme, Educational Loan Scheme, Udyam Nidhi Yojana, or Aajeevika Microfinance Yojana?
8. What is your timeline for needing funds or starting your activity/education?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant belongs to the Scheduled Caste community
- [ ] Annual family income ≤ ₹5.00 lakh (for credit-based schemes)
- [ ] Individual, partnership firm, or co-operative society (if firm/society: all members are SC)
- [ ] Seeking support for income-generating activity, education, or skill development
- [ ] Project cost/educational expense aligns with scheme-specific limits (e.g., ≤ ₹45 lakh for Term Loan, ≤ ₹40 lakh for Educational Loan)
- [ ] Willing to apply through State Channelizing Agency (SCA) or Channelizing Agency (CA)
- [ ] Not currently employed by a University/College/Educational Institution/Govt if applying for NFSC fellowship
- [ ] No prior default on NSFDC loans (implied via prudential norms)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears you may be eligible for support under the **National Scheduled Castes Finance and Development Corporation (NSFDC)** schemes, which provide loans up to **90% of project cost** at concessional interest rates starting from **6.5% p.a.** Let me share how this could work for your specific situation.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many Scheduled Caste individuals face barriers to accessing affordable finance for starting businesses, pursuing higher education, or gaining employable skills due to high interest rates, lack of collateral, or complex application processes. This limits economic mobility and perpetuates financial exclusion.

**Scheme intro:**
The **National Scheduled Castes Finance and Development Corporation (NSFDC)**, under the Ministry of Social Justice & Empowerment, offers multiple credit and non-credit schemes including Term Loan, Micro Finance Scheme, Educational Loan Scheme, Udyam Nidhi Yojana, and Aajeevika Microfinance Yojana. It provides loans up to **90% of project cost** with scheme-specific maximums (e.g., up to ₹45 lakh for Term Loan, ₹40 lakh for Educational Loan, ₹1.25 lakh for Micro Finance). Interest charged to SCAs/CAs ranges from **2.5% to 5% p.a.**, passed on to beneficiaries at **6.5% to 15% p.a.**, with repayment periods of **3 to 12 years** and moratoriums of **3 months to 1 year**. For skill training, it offers 100% grant to institutes and a stipend of ₹1,500/month per trainee.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You belong to the Scheduled Caste community (core eligibility)
- Your annual family income is within ₹5.00 lakh (meets credit scheme threshold)
- Your proposed activity/education falls under income generation, professional/technical courses, or skill development (aligned with scheme scope)
- You are willing to apply through an SCA/CA (required channel)
