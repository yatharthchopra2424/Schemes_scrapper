## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Beneficiary is a Sewer and Septic Tank Worker (SSW) engaged in cleaning and maintenance of sewers including those managing pumping stations, manholes/machine holes, and sewer lines; or engaged in septic tank cleaning (may or may not be engaged by ULB/parastatal, empanelled/registered/licensed Private Sanitation Service Organisation (PSSO) and other private sanitation service providers)
- [ ] Beneficiary is a Waste Picker defined as a person or group of persons informally engaged in collection and recovery of reusable and recyclable solid waste from the source of waste generation the streets, bins, material recovery facilities, processing and waste disposal facilities for sale to recyclers directly or through intermediaries to earn their livelihood
- [ ] For Waste Pickers: engaged in handling municipal solid waste, including informal Waste Pickers working at streets, door to door, transfer stations, open dumpsites, landfill sites, waste disposal sites, material recovery facilities, waste recovery & recycling, repair facilities etc. and itinerant waste buyers; informal Waste sorters working at waste processing/waste recycling facilities; and informal workers/sorters engaged in this activity for a minimum period of 6 months
- [ ] Beneficiary is a Manual Scavenger (as defined under Manual Scavengers Act, 2013)
- [ ] Beneficiary is a Sanitation Worker (includes waste pickers and those engaged in cleaning of sewers and septic tank, but excludes domestic workers)
- [ ] No income limit specified for availing financial assistance under NSKFDC schemes (though priority given to those below double the poverty line, women, and disabled persons)
- [ ] No registration type, turnover threshold, employee count, sector, or age of business restrictions specified for individual beneficiary eligibility under NAMASTE scheme (eligibility based on occupational identity only)
- [ ] For organizational applicants (e.g., training partners under RFP): must have at least 3 years of incorporation as on 31.03.2026, average annual turnover > ₹40 lakhs (FY2022-23 to FY2024-25), positive net worth for last 2 financial years, at least 3 years of training experience including 1 year in sanitation training, valid PAN/GST, audited accounts, and not blacklisted

- Aadhaar Card

- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (if applicable for MSME benefits)
- ITR (Income Tax Return) for last 2 years (if applicable)
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Voter ID Card
- Driving License
- Any other government issued valid ID proof
- E-KYC validation via Aadhaar (conducted during profiling)
- Proof of occupation (for SSWs: employment details from ULB/parastatal/PSSO or self-declaration; for Waste Pickers: details of work location, duration ≥6 months, and type of waste handling)
- For group projects: beneficiary list with NAMASTE IDs and project share details
- For capital subsidy claims: project proposal with cost estimates, quotations for equipment/vehicles, and implementation plan
- For training partners applying under RFP: Board Resolution, Power of Attorney, audited financial statements, experience certificates, turnover proof, net worth certificate, EMD and Tender Fee payment proof (or MSME exemption certificate), undertaking for not blacklisted, undertaking for no corrupt/fraudulent practice, undertaking for no bankruptcy/insolvency

### Document Quality Check
- File size: <5 MB per document (standard for portal upload)
- File format: PDF, JPG, or PNG only
- Name matching: Applicant name must match exactly across all documents (Aadhaar, PAN, Bank)
- Certification: Self-attested copies acceptable; gazetted officer attestation preferred for income/occupation proofs
- Language: Documents in English or Hindi; if in other language, must be accompanied by certified translation
- Aadhaar: Must be linked to mobile number for OTP-based e-KYC
- Bank account: Must be active and in beneficiary’s name; Aadhaar seeding preferred for DBT
- Photographs: Recent passport-size photo with white background (if required for ID card)
- Undertakings: Must be on stamp paper, notarized, and signed by authorized signatory

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Not required for individual beneficiary enrollment** (profiling-based enrollment via ULB/RO camps)  
- **Required for capital subsidy claims under Swachhta Udyami Yojana (SUY) or direct subsidy**: Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required.  
  - Standard sections: Executive Summary, Beneficiary Details, Project Description, Equipment/Vehicle Specifications, Cost Breakdown, Implementation Timeline, Subsidy Calculation, Loan Details (if any), Sustainability Plan, Undertakings  
  - If unsure, **verify with nodal officer** (ULB/RO or NSKFDC)

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Use only the official NAMASTE portal**: https://namastescheme.com/ or mobile app for data entry during profiling camps  
2. **Ensure Aadhaar number is entered correctly** – errors trigger e-KYC failure and delay validation  
3. **Mobile number must be linked to Aadhaar** to receive OTP for e-KYC verification  
4. **Geo-location access must be enabled** on device for automatic workplace city/town capture  
5. **Select correct role** (Surveyor, Validator, Nodal Officer, Beneficiary) during login – incorrect role blocks functionality  
6. **Enter beneficiary details accurately**: Name, gender, DOB, permanent address as per Aadhaar (auto-fetched post e-KYC)  
7. **For Waste Pickers**: Specify work location (street, door-to-door, transfer station, etc.) and confirm ≥6 months engagement  
8. **Save draft frequently** – use "Draft" option to avoid loss due to connectivity issues  
9. **Submit only after OTP verification** – status must show "e-KYC Completed" before final submission  
10. **After submission, sync immediately** – unsaved data remains only on device and will be lost if app closed  
11. **Retain submission confirmation** – screenshot or note the acknowledgment number/reference  
12. **Monitor status via portal** – check for "Error-Flagged" status and resubmit corrected survey within 7 days (as per validator timeline)

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Collect approval letter or NAMASTE ID card from ULB/RO (distributed by Block Nodal Officer post-validation)  
- [ ] Verify sanctioned amount in approval letter (e.g., upfront capital subsidy: 50% of project cost, max ₹10 lakh for SSWs; 25% for PSSOs/Contractors, max ₹10 lakh per unit)  
- [ ] Confirm enrollment in Ayushman Bharat-Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB-PMJAY) – check eligibility via NHA portal or e-Shram  
- [ ] Schedule occupational safety training attendance (if applicable) – stipend @ ₹1000/day for 5 days via DBT  
- [ ] For capital subsidy claims:  
    - Submit Utilization Certificate (UC) after equipment procurement  
    - Ensure invoice matches approved project cost  
    - Track disbursement to beneficiary’s Aadhaar-seeded bank account  
    - Confirm loan linkage (if applicable) through Channelizing Agencies (SCAs/RRBs/PSBs)  
- [ ] Invoice success fee as per consultancy agreement  
- [ ] Monitor benefit utilization: PPE kit usage, health insurance claims, enterprise development  
- [ ] Document closure and client feedback
