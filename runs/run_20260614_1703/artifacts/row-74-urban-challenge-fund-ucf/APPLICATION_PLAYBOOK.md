# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a registered legal entity (Private Limited Company, LLP, Partnership Firm, or Section 8 Company) under Indian law  
- [ ] Entity is engaged in urban infrastructure development or related sectors (e.g., affordable housing, water supply, sanitation, solid waste management, urban transport, or smart city projects)  
- [ ] Project is located within a designated urban area as defined by the Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs (MoHUA)  
- [ ] Applicant has a minimum net worth of INR 10 crore (as per latest audited financial statements)  
- [ ] Applicant has demonstrated experience in executing at least one urban infrastructure project of similar scale and complexity  
- [ ] Project proposal aligns with the objectives of the Urban Challenge Fund (UCF), including innovation, scalability, and sustainability  
- [ ] Applicant is not blacklisted by any central or state government agency  
- [ ] Applicant has valid GST registration and PAN  
- [ ] Applicant has filed income tax returns for the last three financial years  
- [ ] Applicant possesses Udyam Registration (if applicable as an MSME) or equivalent registration for larger entities  

> **Note**: Eligibility criteria are based on limited public information due to low confidence in source data. Verify exact thresholds and conditions with the nodal officer before proceeding.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed / LLP Agreement  
- PAN Card of the entity  
- GST Registration Certificate  
- Audited Financial Statements (Balance Sheet, P&L, Cash Flow) for last 3 years  
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for last 3 years  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration Certificate (if MSME)  
- Authorized Signatory List with Board Resolution  
- Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) of authorized signatory  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed Project Report (DPR) outlining technical feasibility, financial viability, and social impact  
- Land ownership or lease documents for the project site (if applicable)  
- No Objection Certificate (NOC) from relevant local urban development authority  
- Environmental Clearance (if required under EIA Notification, 2006)  
- Stakeholder consultation reports or community engagement proof  
- Innovation certificate or patent details (if claiming technological innovation)  
- Letters of support from municipal corporation or state urban development department  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document)  
- [ ] Document names follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ClientID_Date.pdf` (e.g., `UCF_DPR_CASE001_20240515.pdf`)  
- [ ] Dates on documents are current (financials not older than 1 year; certificates valid as of application date)  
- [ ] All certificates (PAN, GST, Udyam) are valid and not expired  
- [ ] Non-English documents are accompanied by certified Hindi/English translations  
- [ ] Affidavits and declarations are notarized where required  
- [ ] DPR includes signed declaration by project lead and chartered engineer

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
✅ **Required** – The Urban Challenge Fund (UCF) mandates a Detailed Project Report (DPR) as part of the application.  

**Standard DPR Sections to Include**:  
- Executive Summary  
- Project Background and Objectives  
- Technical Description (design, technology, innovation aspects)  
- Site Details (location, land use, connectivity)  
- Implementation Plan (timeline, milestones, agency responsible)  
- Financial Plan (cost estimates, funding sources, revenue model, IRR, NPV)  
- Social and Environmental Impact Assessment  
- Risk Analysis and Mitigation Plan  
- Sustainability and Scalability Plan  
- Annexures: Drawings, approvals, letters of support, CVs of key personnel  

> **Note**: Confirm DPR format and length requirements with the nodal officer, as UCF guidelines may have specific templates.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application portal exclusively via the official MoHUA website: [https://mohua.gov.in](https://mohua.gov.in)  
2. Use only the authorized login credentials provided after pre-registration (if applicable)  
3. Fill all mandatory fields marked with (*); leaving any blank will result in auto-rejection  
4. Enter financial figures in INR lakhs (e.g., 500 for ₹5,00,00,000) unless otherwise specified  
5. Upload documents in the exact order and format specified in the portal’s document checklist  
6. Do not use special characters (!, @, #, $, %, etc.) in text fields unless explicitly allowed  
7. Ensure the authorized signatory’s name matches exactly across PAN, GST, and board resolution  
8. Preview the entire form before final submission; no edits are allowed after submission  
9. Save a PDF copy of the submitted application immediately after confirmation  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All eligibility criteria verified and documented  
- [ ] Universal and scheme-specific documents collected and quality-checked  
- [ ] DPR completed, reviewed by technical expert, and signed  
- [ ] Application form filled completely; no mandatory fields left blank  
- [ ] Financial data consistent across ITRs, audited statements, and DPR  
- [ ] All uploaded documents are correctly named and within size limits  
- [ ] Declarations and undertakings are signed and dated  
- [ ] Authorized signatory’s DSC is valid and applied where required  
- [ ] Application preview reviewed for errors before final submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application only during portal operating hours (9:00 AM–6:00 PM IST, Monday–Friday)  
- Upon submission, take a **screenshot of the confirmation page** showing:  
  - Application Reference Number (ARN)  
  - Timestamp of submission  
  - Scheme name: Urban Challenge Fund (UCF)  
- Save the screenshot and ARN in the case folder with label: `UCF_SubmissionProof_[CaseID].png`  
- Store the ARN securely; it is required for all future correspondence  
- Set automated reminders for follow-up at:  
  - Day 21 (3 days post-submission)  
  - Day 28 (1 week post-submission)  
  - Day 35 (2 weeks post-submission)

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the official approval letter from MoHUA specifying sanctioned amount under UCF  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount matches the approved DPR financials  
- [ ] Set up a dedicated bank account for UCF funds (if required by scheme guidelines)  
- [ ] Communicate approval and fund receipt timelines to the client  
- [ ] Guide client on utilization of funds as per approved DPR and MoHUA directives  
- [ ] Prepare utilization certificate (UC) format as per GFR 2017 rules  
- [ ] Ensure all expenditures are supported by valid invoices and vouchers  
- [ ] Submit periodic progress reports (physical and financial) as required by the nodal officer  
- [ ] Assist in third-party audit of UCF fund utilization (if mandated)  
- [ ] Prepare and submit final utilization certificate and project completion report  
- [ ] Follow up on success fee invoice as per engagement letter terms  

> **Note**: Post-approval compliance is critical. Failure to utilize funds as per approval may result in refund with penalty.
