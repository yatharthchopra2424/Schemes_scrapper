# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Business is registered as a proprietorship, partnership, LLP, or private limited company under Indian law  
- [ ] Annual turnover does not exceed ₹25 crore (as per MSME classification for eligibility under rural employment schemes)  
- [ ] Employee count is between 10 and 200 workers (aligned with labor-intensive rural enterprise targeting)  
- [ ] Business operates in the rural sector (defined as areas outside municipal corporation limits, per Census 2011)  
- [ ] Business is engaged in agro-processing, handicrafts, textiles, renewable energy, or other livelihood-generating activities as specified under GARIMA guidelines  
- [ ] Business has been operational for at least 1 year (to establish viability for wage subsidy or skill linkage)  
- [ ] Business is registered on the Udyam portal (mandatory for all central government rural employment and enterprise support schemes)  
- [ ] No prior default on government loan or subsidy under any rural development scheme in the last 3 years  
- [ ] Business owner(s) are Indian citizens and not disqualified under any central or state government scheme  

> **Note**: Due to low confidence in the source evidence for GARIMA (Gramin Rozgar Mission), eligibility criteria have been inferred based on typical parameters of similar rural employment and enterprise support schemes under the Ministry of Rural Development. Verify exact thresholds with the nodal officer before proceeding.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation/Registration Certificate (Proprietorship affidavit, Partnership deed, LLP agreement, or CIN for companies)  
- PAN Card of the business entity  
- PAN Card of the authorized signatory / proprietor / partner / director  
- Aadhaar Card of the authorized signatory  
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)  
- Udyam Registration Certificate  
- Bank Statement (last 6 months) of the business current account  
- Income Tax Return (ITR) of the business for the last 2 financial years (if applicable)  
- ITR of the proprietor/partners/directors for the last 2 financial years  
- Electricity Bill or Property Tax Receipt as proof of business address  
- Cancelled cheque or bank passbook copy for account verification  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed project proposal outlining the rural employment generation plan (including number of direct and indirect jobs to be created)  
- Location map and photographs of the business unit/enterprise site (to verify rural setting)  
- List of proposed beneficiaries (employees) with their Aadhaar details and socio-economic category (SC/ST/OBC/Women, if targeting inclusive employment)  
- Skilling/training plan for employees (if the scheme includes capacity building component)  
- Commitment letter on minimum wage compliance and working conditions (as per MGNREGA or state labor norms)  
- Self-declaration of no duplicate benefit availed under any other central/state employment generation scheme  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (no images or scanned photos unless specified)  
- File size for each document must not exceed 5 MB  
- File names must follow the format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ClientName_Date.pdf` (e.g., `GARIMA_Udyam_ABCEnterprises_20240515.pdf`)  
- All certificates (Udyam, GST, PAN, etc.) must be valid and not expired on the date of submission  
- Affidavits and declarations must be on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value (as per state stamp act) and notarized  
- Hindi or English language only; if regional language is used, a certified translation must be attached  
- Bank statements must be stamped and signed by the bank authority

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is **required** for GARIMA applications, as the scheme involves employment-linked financial support and needs viability assessment.  
- Standard sections to include:  
  1. Executive Summary  
  2. Business Profile and Promoter Background  
  3. Rural Location Justification (with Census/GIS verification)  
  4. Employment Generation Plan (direct, indirect, skilled/unskilled, women/youth inclusion)  
  5. Technical Details (process, machinery, raw material sourcing)  
  6. Financial Projections (3–5 year): Cost of project, means of finance, revenue, profitability, break-even  
  7. Employment Cost Breakdown (wages, benefits, training)  
  8. Risk Assessment and Mitigation Strategy  
  9. Sustainability and Social Impact Analysis  
  10. Annexures: Documents, quotes, layouts, approvals  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official GARIMA application portal (verify URL via Ministry of Rural Development or state SRLM website)  
2. Use only the authorized signatory’s login credentials (linked to Udyam and PAN)  
3. Fill all fields in English; do not leave any mandatory field blank — if not applicable, write “NA”  
4. Ensure the total project cost and requested grant/subsidy amount match the DPR exactly  
5. Upload documents in the specified order and format as per portal instructions  
6. Double-check the rural enterprise classification (block/district code) against Census 2011 data  
7. Verify the employee count and wage commitment align with the scheme’s employment targets  
8. Preview the entire application before final submission — no edits allowed after submission in most government portals  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All eligibility criteria verified and documented  
- [ ] DPR completed and internally reviewed for financial and technical soundness  
- [ ] Application form filled completely; no blank mandatory fields  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents attached, correctly named, and within size limits  
- [ ] Declarations signed and notarized where required  
- [ ] Total proposed employment matches DPR and application form  
- [ ] Bank details match Udyam and ITR records  
- [ ] Application previewed and screenshot saved for record  
- [ ] Reference number noted post-submission (to be filled after submission)

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application only after internal review gate clearance  
- Take a screenshot of the final submission confirmation page showing:  
  - Application Reference Number  
  - Date and Time of Submission  
  - Scheme Name: Gramin Rozgar Mission (GARIMA)  
- Save the screenshot in the case folder with naming: `GARIMA_SubmissionConfirmation_[CaseID]_[YYYYMMDD].png`  
- Store the Application Reference Number in the case tracker and set automated follow-up reminders for:  
  - 7 days post-submission  
  - 15 days post-submission  
  - 30 days post-submission

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and sanitize the approval letter from the sanctioning authority  
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned amount against the approved DPR and application  
- [ ] Confirm the disbursement schedule (tranches, if any) and linked milestones  
- [ ] Set up a dedicated bank account for scheme funds (if required)  
- [ ] Initiate utilization of funds as per approved plan and timeline  
- [ ] Maintain auditable records of all expenditures (bills, vouchers, wage registers)  
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate (UC) in GFR 12-A format within stipulated time  
- [ ] Ensure compliance with employment targets and wage commitments  
- [ ] Coordinate with statutory auditor for scheme-specific audit (if applicable)  
- [ ] Track and invoice success fee as per engagement letter (typically 8–12% of sanctioned amount)  
- [ ] Archive all scheme-related documents for minimum 5 years for audit purposes  

> **Note**: Post-approval compliance requirements for GARIMA are inferred from standard rural development scheme protocols. Confirm exact utilization, reporting, and audit norms with the nodal officer upon approval.
