# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping rural entrepreneurs and self-help groups access government livelihood support schemes. The Gramin Rozgar Mission (GARIMA) is designed specifically to create sustainable employment opportunities in rural areas through skill development and micro-enterprise support. I’d like to understand if this could be relevant to your work or community initiative.

### Discovery Questions
- Are you currently working with or supporting individuals in rural areas who are seeking sustainable livelihood opportunities?
- What is the primary occupation or skill set of the beneficiaries you aim to support (e.g., agriculture, handicrafts, small-scale manufacturing)?
- Have you or your organisation previously accessed any government skill development or self-employment schemes?
- What is the approximate size of the group or community you are looking to support through this initiative?
- Are the target beneficiaries primarily from Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, or other marginalised rural communities?
- Do you have a clear plan for how skill training would translate into income-generating activities for participants?
- Are you based in or operating within a designated rural block or district under the Ministry of Rural Development’s purview?
- What is your timeline for launching or scaling a livelihood support programme for rural youth or women?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Target beneficiaries are located in rural areas  
- [ ] Focus on creating self-employment or wage employment opportunities  
- [ ] Beneficiaries include youth, women, or marginalised groups (SC/ST)  
- [ ] Proposal includes skill development linked to livelihood outcomes  
- [ ] Implementing entity is a recognised NGO, SHG federation, or rural development agency  
- [ ] Project aligns with district-level rural development plans  
- [ ] No duplicate funding for the same beneficiaries under similar central schemes  
- [ ] Capacity to manage training, placement, and post-support monitoring  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like your initiative aligns well with the goals of the Gramin Rozgar Mission (GARIMA), which supports rural livelihood creation through skill training and enterprise support. This scheme provides financial assistance for training and placement — let me walk you through how it works.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Problem framing:
Rural youth and women often face barriers to sustainable employment due to lack of market-relevant skills, limited access to capital, and weak linkages to markets. Many skill development programmes fail to translate into actual income generation because they lack follow-up support for placement or enterprise setup.

### Scheme intro:
The Gramin Rozgar Mission (GARIMA), administered by the Ministry of Rural Development, is a livelihood promotion scheme that provides financial support for skill development and placement of rural poor youth. It offers grant-based assistance to training partners and implementing agencies for conducting training programmes and ensuring wage employment or self-employment outcomes for beneficiaries.

### Why they qualify (personalised):
- You are working directly with rural youth or women from marginalised communities  
- Your programme includes skill training linked to specific livelihood pathways  
- You have or can develop partnerships with local employers or market linkages  
- Your organisation has the capacity to manage training delivery and post-training support

Our success comes from rigorous adherence to ministry guidelines and detailed project reporting. |
| "How much do you charge?" | [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT] |
| "I'll try it myself first" | Common mistakes include submitting vague livelihood plans without clear skill-to-income linkages, and failing to demonstrate convergence with other rural development programmes (like MGNREGA or DAY-NRLM). We help avoid these by structuring proposals around measurable outcomes and verified placement plans. |
| "We're too small / too big" | GARIMA typically supports projects implemented by NGOs, SHG federations, or rural training institutes with demonstrated capacity to train at least 50–100 beneficiaries per cycle. There’s no strict upper limit, but proposals must show scalability and cost-effectiveness per beneficiary. If you're working with smaller groups, we can help aggregate or phase the proposal to meet viability thresholds. |
| "I don't have the documents ready" | The three most commonly missing documents are: detailed training curriculum with market linkage plan, MoUs with employers or placement partners, and audited financial statements of the implementing agency for the last two years. |
| "We already work with a CA" | Great — we work alongside CAs. They handle your accounts; we handle the scheme strategy and applications. |
