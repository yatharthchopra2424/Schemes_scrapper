# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping farmers access government schemes that reduce diesel dependence and enhance farm income through solar energy solutions. The PM-KUSUM scheme, launched by the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy, offers substantial subsidies for solar pumps and solar power plants on barren land—directly addressing the challenges of rising diesel costs and unstable farm revenues. I’d like to understand your current farming setup to see if this could be a fit for you.

### Discovery Questions
1. What type of farming activity are you primarily engaged in (e.g., irrigated agriculture, dairy, horticulture)?
2. Do you currently use diesel-powered pumps for irrigation, and if so, what is your approximate monthly diesel expenditure?
3. Is your existing agricultural pump connected to the grid, or are you using an off-grid/diesel pump system?
4. Do you have access to barren or fallow land that is currently unused or underutilized?
5. Have you explored installing solar pumps or solar power plants before, and what challenges did you encounter?
6. Are you aware of any state-level agencies responsible for implementing PM-KUSUM in your state?
7. What is the size of your landholding, and are you the sole owner or a co-owner with clear title?
8. Are you looking to reduce operational costs, generate additional income from solar power sales, or both?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Farmer (individual or collective) engaged in agricultural activities  
- [ ] Seeking to install standalone solar pumps (off-grid)  
- [ ] Seeking to solarize existing grid-connected agricultural pumps  
- [ ] Seeking to install grid-connected solar power plants up to 2MW on barren/fallow land  
- [ ] Land is located in India (pan-India eligibility)  
- [ ] Application to be made through designated State Government department (not central portal directly)  
- [ ] No current engagement with fraudulent websites impersonating PM-KUSUM  
- [ ] Willing to submit required documents as per state-level guidelines  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears you may benefit from the PM-KUSUM scheme, which offers central government subsidies of up to 30% or 50% for solar pump installation or solarization, and allows farmers to set up solar power plants up to 2MW on barren land to sell electricity to the local DISCOM. Let me explain how this works and whether it aligns with your goals.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Farmers face high operational costs due to diesel-dependent irrigation pumps, unstable income from agriculture, and underutilized barren land. Rising fuel prices and unreliable grid supply increase financial strain, while limited access to capital prevents adoption of clean energy solutions that could reduce costs and generate additional revenue.

**Scheme intro:**
Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthan Mahabhiyan (PM-KUSUM), launched by the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE), provides central financial assistance of up to 30% or 50% of the total cost for installing standalone solar pumps, solarizing grid-connected agricultural pumps, and setting up grid-connected solar power plants up to 2MW on barren or fallow land. Farmers can sell the generated electricity to local DISCOMs at tariffs determined by state regulators. The scheme is implemented by designated departments of State Governments.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are a farmer engaged in agricultural activities, meeting the core beneficiary criterion  
- You currently use diesel or grid-connected pumps, making you eligible for solar pump installation or solarization (Components B & C)  
- You have access to barren/fallow land, qualifying you for grid-connected solar power plants up to 2MW (Component A)  
- You are willing to apply through your State Government’s designated department, as required
