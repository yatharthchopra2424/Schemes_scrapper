### PM-KUSUM
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Designated departments of the State Government
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://pmkusum.mnre.gov.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Farmers (individual farmers, farmer producer organizations, cooperatives, etc. engaged in agriculture)
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Agriculture
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Farmers seeking to install standalone solar pumps, solarize existing grid-connected agricultural pumps, or install grid-connected solar power plants up to 2MW on their barren/fallow land are eligible under the scheme, as implemented by designated departments of State Governments.

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Central government subsidy up to 30% or 50% of the total cost for installation of standalone solar pumps and solarization of grid-connected agricultural pumps; opportunity to install grid-connected solar power plants up to 2MW on barren/fallow land and sell electricity to local DISCOM at tariff determined by state regulator
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit (subsidy disbursed to beneficiaries through state nodal agencies)
- Duration of benefit: One-time (for installation); ongoing revenue from sale of electricity (for grid-connected solar power plants)

#### Documents Required
1. Identity proof of farmer (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.)
2. Land ownership documents or lease agreement for barren/fallow land (for Component C)
3. Existing pump details and electricity bill (for solarization under Component B)
4. Bank account details for subsidy transfer
5. Passport-sized photographs
6. Application form (as per state-level guidelines)
7. Any other documents specified by the designated State Government department

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Visit the official PM-KUSUM portal or MNRE website for information.
2. Beware of fraudulent websites and mobile applications impersonating PM-KUSUM.
3. Apply through designated departments of the State Government.
4. Submit required documents as per state-level guidelines.
5. Await approval and installation of solar pumps or power plants.
6. For grid-connected systems, sell electricity to local DISCOM at state-determined tariff.
