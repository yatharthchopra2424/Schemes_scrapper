# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
PM-KUSUM (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthan Mahabhiyan) is a flagship subsidy scheme launched by the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE), Government of India, aimed at de-dieselising the farm sector and enhancing farmers' income through solar energy adoption. The scheme is implemented pan-India by designated departments of State Governments.

### Objectives
- De-dieselisation of the farm sector  
- Enhancing income of farmers  
- Promoting installation of standalone solar pumps  
- Promoting solarization of existing grid-connected agricultural pumps  
- Enabling installation of grid-connected solar power plants up to 2MW on barren/fallow land  
- Facilitating sale of solar power to local DISCOM at state-determined tariffs

### Application Process
The application process is managed at the state level. Farmers must approach designated departments of their respective State Governments. The general steps are:

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Farmer identifies eligibility under PM-KUSUM] --> B[Visits official MNRE portal: https://pmkusum.mnre.gov.in/ or mnre.gov.in]
    B --> C[Calls helpline 1800-180-3333 for guidance if needed]
    C --> D[Approaches designated State Government department (e.g., SNA, DISCOM, Agriculture Dept)]
    D --> E[Submits application with required documents as per state-level guidelines]
    E --> F[State department verifies eligibility and documents]
    F --> G[Approval granted and subsidy sanctioned]
    G --> H[Installation of solar pump/power plant by empaneled vendor]
    H --> I[System commissioned and connected to grid (if applicable)]
    I --> J[For grid-connected systems: Sale of electricity to local DISCOM at state-determined tariff]
    J --> K[Monitoring & maintenance as per scheme guidelines]
    style A fill:#e3f2fd,stroke:#1565c0
    style K fill:#c8e6c9,stroke:#2e7d32
```

#### Key Application Steps (as per portal):
1. Visit the official PM-KUSUM portal (`https://pmkusum.mnre.gov.in/`) or MNRE website (`www.mnre.gov.in`) for authentic information.
2. **Beware of fraudulent websites and mobile applications** impersonating PM-KUSUM (see caveats above).
3. Apply through **designated departments of the State Government** (not online via fake portals).
4. Submit required documents as per **state-level guidelines** (document list varies by state).
5. Await approval and installation of solar pumps or power plants.
6. For grid-connected systems, sell electricity to local DISCOM at tariff determined by the state regulator.
