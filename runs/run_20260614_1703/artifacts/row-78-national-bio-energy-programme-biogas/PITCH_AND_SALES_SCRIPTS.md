# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialize in helping rural households, farmers, institutions, and MSMEs access government support for clean energy solutions like biogas plants. The National Bio Energy Programme - Biogas offers substantial subsidies to reduce dependence on fossil fuels and firewood while improving rural livelihoods. I’d like to understand your energy needs and see if this scheme aligns with your goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your primary motivation for considering a biogas plant—clean cooking, waste management, bio-fertilizer production, or income generation?
2. Are you the owner or authorized representative of the site where the biogas plant would be installed?
3. What is the estimated daily requirement for cooking fuel or energy at your household/institution?
4. Do you have access to sufficient organic waste (cattle dung, kitchen waste, agricultural residue) to feed a biogas plant?
5. What is the size of land available for installing the biogas plant, and do you have legal ownership or long-term usage rights?
6. Are you located in a North Eastern State, hilly area, or island region where higher subsidy rates apply?
7. Have you previously explored or installed any renewable energy systems like solar or biogas?
8. What is your capacity to bear the non-subsidized portion of the plant cost as per scheme guidelines?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant is an individual, farmer, institution, industry, or NGO
- [ ] Applicant is owner or authorized representative of the installation site
- [ ] For community/institutional plants: legal ownership or long-term usage rights of land confirmed
- [ ] Proposed plant conforms to MNRE-approved designs and specifications
- [ ] Beneficiary share contribution can be arranged as per guidelines
- [ ] Plant size: 1–25 m³ (family-size) or above 25 m³ (community/institutional)
- [ ] Location: Pan-India (eligible across all states)
- [ ] Commitment to install via approved turnkey worker or self-construction with technical guidance

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, the National Bio Energy Programme - Biogas appears to be a strong fit. This scheme provides Central Financial Assistance (CFA) as a subsidy—₹9,000 per m³ for general category areas and up to ₹12,000 per m³ in North Eastern States—for family-size plants, and 30–50% of benchmark cost for larger community or institutional plants. Shall I walk you through how it works?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Rural households and institutions face high costs and health risks from relying on firewood, LPG, or diesel for cooking and energy. Indoor air pollution from traditional chulhas causes respiratory issues, while waste management remains a challenge. Additionally, farmers struggle with soil fertility due to lack of affordable organic fertilizer. The National Bio Energy Programme - Biogas addresses these interconnected problems by converting organic waste into clean cooking fuel and nutrient-rich slurry.

**Scheme intro:**
The National Bio Energy Programme - Biogas, implemented by the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE), offers Central Financial Assistance (CFA) as a subsidy for installing biogas plants. For family-size plants (1 to 25 m³ capacity), CFA is ₹9,000 per m³ in general category areas and ₹12,000 per m³ in North Eastern States, including Sikkim. For community or institutional plants (above 25 m³), CFA is 30% of the benchmark cost, rising to 50% in North Eastern States, hilly areas, and islands. The subsidy is released in instalments after verification of plant installation and functionality.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are the owner or authorized representative of the installation site — a core eligibility requirement
- Your location qualifies for either standard or enhanced CFA rates (general or North Eastern/hilly/island)
- You have access to sufficient organic waste feedstock for plant operation
- You can arrange the beneficiary share (non-subsidized portion) of the plant cost
