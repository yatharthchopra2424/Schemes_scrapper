# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialise in helping startups and government agencies leverage Digital India initiatives to drive linguistic inclusivity and digital empowerment. Given your work in [mention their sector if known], I’d like to explore whether the Digital India Bhashini initiative could support your goals around seamless multilingual communication and innovation through startup-government collaboration.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your organisation’s primary focus—are you a startup, government agency, or another type of entity?
2. Are you currently working on any projects involving multilingual communication, language technology, or digital inclusivity?
3. Have you previously collaborated with government agencies or participated in MeitY-led initiatives?
4. What stage is your startup at in terms of incorporation and product development?
5. Are you looking to innovate in areas like AI-driven language translation, speech recognition, or text-to-speech systems?
6. Do you have experience working with Indian language datasets or building tools for linguistic diversity?
7. Are you seeking platforms or frameworks to test, scale, or deploy language technology solutions?
8. What are your current challenges in reaching linguistically diverse users or citizens?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Entity is a startup or government agency  
- [ ] Focus area aligns with linguistic inclusivity, multilingual communication, or language technology innovation  
- [ ] Interest in collaborating with government agencies or participating in MeitY-led programs  
- [ ] Working on or planning to develop solutions for Indian language processing (e.g., translation, transliteration, TTS, ASR)  
- [ ] Open to innovation through startup-government collaboration models  
- [ ] Based in India or targeting Indian linguistic markets  
- [ ] No requirement for direct financial grant (scheme is collaboration-focused, not subsidy-based)  
- [ ] Willingness to engage via Digital India Corporation (DIC) under MeitY  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it seems Digital India Bhashini could be a strong fit—especially given its focus on fostering innovation through startup-government collaboration and enabling seamless multilingual communication. Let me share how this initiative works and how it could support your goals.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many startups and developers face barriers in building inclusive digital products due to fragmented language resources, lack of standardised APIs for Indian languages, and difficulty accessing government collaboration channels. This limits their ability to serve linguistically diverse users across India, especially in regional languages.

**Scheme intro:**
Digital India Bhashini, led by the Digital India Corporation (DIC) under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), is a pan-India initiative that promotes linguistic inclusivity by enabling seamless multilingual communication. It fosters innovation through collaboration between startups and government agencies, supporting the development and deployment of language technology solutions without direct financial disbursement, but through access to shared platforms, datasets, and innovation ecosystems.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are a startup or government agency — the core beneficiary groups for Bhashini collaboration  
- Your work involves or plans to involve Indian language technology (e.g., translation, speech, text processing)  
- You are interested in collaborating with government bodies or leveraging public innovation platforms  
- You aim to scale solutions across linguistically diverse regions of India
