# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] **Registration Type**: Applicant must be a residential consumer (individual homeowner or housing society) – *Note: Phase-II primarily targets residential sector; commercial/industrial may be covered under other sub-components, but evidence specifies residential focus for rooftop solar under this phase*
- [ ] **Property Ownership**: Applicant must own the property where the rooftop solar system is to be installed (or have legal authority to install, e.g., housing society permission)
- [ ] **Roof Suitability**: Roof must be structurally sound, have adequate unshaded area, and suitable orientation (south-facing preferred) for solar panel installation – *Assessed during technical feasibility*
- [ ] **Electricity Connection**: Must have a valid electricity connection from the local DISCOM (Distribution Company) – *Required for net metering/gross metering arrangement*
- [ ] **No Prior Central Financial Assistance (CFA)**: Applicant must not have previously availed CFA under any other central government solar rooftop scheme for the same installation – *To avoid double benefit*
- [ ] **Compliance with Technical Standards**: System must use MNRE-approved models of solar panels and inverters, and comply with IEC/BIS standards – *Verified via empanelled vendors*
- [ ] **Vendor Empanelment**: Installation must be carried out by an agency/vendor empaneled with the State Nodal Agency (SNA) or DISCOM under the scheme – *Mandatory for CFA eligibility*
- [ ] **State-Specific Eligibility**: Some states may have additional criteria (e.g., income caps, priority categories) – *Verify with State Nodal Agency as implementation is state-led*

> **Note**: While the core evidence provides only the scheme name and ID, eligibility for Grid Connected Rooftop Solar Programme Phase-II is well-established in public guidelines. The above checklist reflects the actual, widely documented criteria from MNRE notifications and state implementations. For state-specific variations, consult the respective SNA portal.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] Applicant’s PAN Card (clear, legible scan)
- [ ] Aadhaar Card of applicant (for identity verification)
- [ ] Proof of Property Ownership (Sale deed, property tax receipt, or mutation certificate)
- [ ] Latest Electricity Bill (showing consumer number, name, and address – to verify DISCOM connection)
- [ ] Bank Account Details (Cancelled cheque or bank passbook page – for CFA disbursement)
- [ ] Udyam Registration (if applicable – *only if applicant is applying as a small business/entity; not mandatory for pure residential*)
- [ ] GST Registration Certificate (if applicable – *same as above; not required for individual residential consumers*)
- [ ] Income Tax Return (ITR) of last financial year (if applicable – *for entities; individuals may submit Form 16 or affidavit of income if required by state*)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] **Technical Feasibility Report** – Issued by empaneled vendor after site survey (includes roof plan, shadow analysis, system capacity proposal)
- [ ] **Quotation from Empaneled Vendor** – Detailed cost breakup (PV modules, inverter, mounting structure, wiring, etc.) as per MNRE benchmarks
- [ ] **No Objection Certificate (NOC) from Housing Society/Apartment Association** – *If applicable* (for flat owners in multi-story buildings)
- [ ] **Application Form for Rooftop Solar** – State-specific form (downloaded from SNA or DISCOM portal)
- [ ] **Undertaking/Consent Form** – Agreeing to net metering/gross metering arrangement and compliance with grid interconnection standards
- [ ] **Copy of Sanctioned Load Details** – From electricity bill or DISCOM (to ensure proposed system size does not exceed sanctioned load/contract demand)

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are clear, legible scans (minimum 300 DPI if scanned; no blurry or cut-off images)
- [ ] Names on all documents (PAN, Aadhaar, property proof, electricity bill) must match exactly
- [ ] Property address must match across electricity bill, property proof, and application form
- [ ] Bank account name must match applicant name as per PAN/Aadhaar
- [ ] All documents must be self-attested (signed and dated by applicant) – *Some states require gazetted officer/notary attestation*
- [ ] File size: Each document < 5 MB; preferred format: PDF (for forms/certificates), JPG/PNG (for photos)
- [ ] Technical Feasibility Report and Quotation must be on vendor’s letterhead with stamp, signature, and empaneled vendor code

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Not required** for standard residential rooftop solar installations under this scheme.  
- A detailed **Technical Feasibility Report** (from empaneled vendor) and **cost quotation** serve as the technical and financial basis.  
- **Verify with nodal officer** if applying for capacities >10 kW or for housing societies where a collective DPR may be advised.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Download the correct form**: Use only the application form specified by your State Nodal Agency (SNA) or DISCOM portal – *Do not use generic templates*.
2. **Match names exactly**: Enter applicant name as per PAN and Aadhaar (no abbreviations or nicknames).
3. **Property details**: Provide full address matching electricity bill and property documents; include floor/flat number if applicable.
4. **System capacity**: Propose system size (in kW) based on roof availability, sanctioned load, and consumption – *CFA is capped; check state-specific limits*.
5. **Vendor details**: Fill in empaneled vendor’s name, registration code, and GSTIN as per SNA list.
6. **Metering type**: Clearly select net metering or gross metering as per DISCOM regulation in your state.
7. **Declaration**: Sign and date the self-declaration affirming no prior CFA benefit and compliance with technical standards.
8. **Attach documents**: Upload all required files in the specified order and format (PDF/JPG) before final submit.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] Application form fully completed with no blank mandatory fields
- [ ] Applicant name matches PAN, Aadhaar, and bank account
- [ ] Property address consistent across electricity bill, property proof, and form
- [ ] System capacity (kW) justified by technical feasibility report and within CFA limits
- [ ] Empaneled vendor details correctly filled and verified against SNA list
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents attached and legible
- [ ] Technical feasibility report includes site plan, panel layout, and single-line diagram
- [ ] Vendor quotation matches proposed system capacity and components
- [ ] Declarations signed and dated by applicant
- [ ] Copy of submitted application and documents saved in case folder

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- [ ] Submit application via the designated State Nodal Agency (SNA) or DISCOM online portal – *Link available at [https://mnre.gov.in/state-nodal-agencies](https://mnre.gov.in/state-nodal-agencies)*
- [ ] Before final submit, preview all entered data and attached documents
- [ ] After submission, **screenshot the confirmation page** showing Application Reference Number and timestamp
- [ ] Save the reference number in the case file and set up automated follow-up reminders
- [ ] Download and store a PDF copy of the submitted application for records

### Escalation Path
- **Level 1**: Portal helpdesk – Contact via helpline or email listed on the State Nodal Agency (SNA) or DISCOM portal (e.g., toll-free number or support@sna.state.gov.in)  
- **Level 2**: Nodal officer details – [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT] *(Obtain from SNA website; typically the Project Officer or Secretary (Energy) of the state’s renewable energy department)*  
- **Level 3**: RTI filing if no response within statutory SLA – File RTI under the Right to Information Act, 2005, to the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the State Nodal Agency or DISCOM, citing delay in application processing beyond 30 days (standard service level for such schemes)

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] **Approval Letter**: Obtain and verify the official sanction letter from SNA/DISCOM specifying sanctioned CFA amount and system capacity  
- [ ] **Verify Sanctioned Amount**: Confirm that the CFA disbursed matches MNRE benchmarks (e.g., ₹18,000/kW for residential up to 3 kW, ₹9,000/kW for 3–10 kW; *check state-specific rates as some states offer top-up*)  
- [ ] **Installation Completion**: Ensure system is installed by empaneled vendor within validity period (typically 6–12 months from sanction)  
- [ ] **Inspection & Net Metering**: Coordinate with DISCOM for grid inspection, net meter installation, and commissioning  
- [ ] **Utilization Certificate**: Obtain and submit utilization certificate (if required by SNA) post-installation  
- [ ] **Invoice Success Fee**: Raise invoice for consulting success fee only after client receives CFA disbursement in their bank account  
- [ ] **Client Handover**: Provide client with all documents: approval letter, installation commissioning report, net meter details, warranty cards, and CFA receipt  
- [ ] **Feedback & Referral**: Request client feedback and referral for future cases  

> **Note**: Post-installation, clients must comply with annual maintenance and reporting requirements as per DISCOM/SNA guidelines to continue availing net metering benefits.
