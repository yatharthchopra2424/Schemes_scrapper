## Pricing Principles
1. **Value-based alignment**: Fees are structured to reflect the financial benefit delivered to clients under the Grid Connected Rooftop Solar Programme Phase-II, which offers central financial assistance (CFA) of up to 40% for systems up to 3 kW and 20% for systems between 3 kW and 10 kW for residential consumers, and applicable CFA for other sectors as per MNRE guidelines.
2. **Risk-adjusted pricing**: Success fees account for scheme-specific risks including variable state-level implementation, dependency on DISCOM approvals, and technical feasibility assessments. The scheme’s processing time (typically 3–6 months from application to disbursement, per MNRE portal feedback) justifies a 3-month lag in revenue recognition.
3. **Tiered accessibility**: Retainers are scaled to client capacity, ensuring micro and small enterprises are not excluded despite lower scheme applicability, while mid-market and large clients benefit from reduced success fees reflecting higher deal volumes and lower relative effort.
4. **Transparency and compliance**: All fees are disclosed upfront and aligned with ethical consulting standards; no fees are charged until client eligibility is confirmed via preliminary assessment using MNRE’s portal (https://solarrooftop.gov.in).
