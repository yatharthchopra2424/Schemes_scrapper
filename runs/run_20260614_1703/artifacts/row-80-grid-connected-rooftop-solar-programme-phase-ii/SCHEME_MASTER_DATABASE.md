### Grid Connected Rooftop Solar Programme Phase-II
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE)
- **Portal / Application URL:** [https://solarrooftop.gov.in](https://solarrooftop.gov.in)
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Residential, institutional, social, and government sectors (private commercial and industrial establishments are not eligible)
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Renewable energy (rooftop solar installations)
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Must be a beneficiary under the residential, institutional, social, or government sector; installations must be grid-connected rooftop solar systems

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Central Financial Assistance (CFA) of up to 40% for systems up to 3 kW and 20% for systems between 3 kW and 10 kW for residential sector; for institutional, social, and government sectors, CFA is up to 20% for systems up to 500 kW
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit to implementing agencies/vendors after installation and inspection
- Duration of benefit: One-time subsidy upon successful installation and commissioning

#### Documents Required
1. Proof of identity (Aadhaar, PAN, etc.)
2. Proof of address/ownership of premises
3. Technical details of the proposed solar system (capacity, make, model)
4. Installation certificate from empaneled vendor
5. Inspection report from authorized agency
6. Bank details for subsidy transfer (if applicable)
7. Any other documents as specified by the State Nodal Agency or MNRE

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Empanelled vendor submits project proposal on behalf of beneficiary through the national portal
2. State Nodal Agency validates eligibility and technical feasibility
3. Letter of Intent (LoI) issued upon approval
4. Beneficiary installs the rooftop solar system through the empaneled vendor
5. Post-installation inspection conducted by authorized agency
6. Vendor submits completion report and documents via portal
7. Subsidy amount processed and transferred to vendor’s account
8. Beneficiary receives commissioned system with net metering arrangement
