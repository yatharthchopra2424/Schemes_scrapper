# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Entity is a designated consumer under the Energy Conservation Act, 2001  
- [ ] Entity belongs to a notified sector (thermal power plants, cement, iron & steel, aluminium, fertilizers, pulp & paper, textiles, petrochemicals)  
- [ ] Entity has been assigned specific energy consumption reduction targets under the PAT cycle by BEE  
- [ ] Entity has over-achieved its energy savings targets in the relevant PAT cycle  
- [ ] Entity is located in India (Pan-India scope)  
- [ ] Entity is not engaged in double counting of ESCerts (each certificate traded only once)  

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation certificate  
- PAN card  
- ITR of last 3 financial years  
- GST registration certificate  
- Bank statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam registration (if applicable)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Verified energy audit report from an accredited energy auditor  
- Baseline and assessment year energy consumption data  
- Details of energy efficiency measures implemented  
- Form A (for target fulfillment) as per PAT scheme guidelines  
- Certificate of registration as a designated consumer under EC Act, 2001  
- Board resolution approving participation in PAT scheme  
- No objection certificate from the State Designated Agency (if applicable)  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format  
- File size per document should not exceed 10 MB  
- Names on documents must match the entity’s legal name as per incorporation and PAN  
- Energy audit report must be signed and stamped by an accredited energy auditor  
- Board resolution must be dated and signed by authorized signatory  
- All documents must be in English or accompanied by certified translation

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not explicitly required for ESCert issuance under the Carbon Credit Trading Scheme 2023. However, a detailed energy audit report and Form A (as per PAT guidelines) serve as the technical basis. Verify with the nodal officer (BEE) if any additional reporting is mandated for your specific PAT cycle.  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Ensure all entity details (name, PAN, address) match exactly across documents and the application form  
2. Enter accurate baseline and assessment year energy consumption data as verified by the accredited energy auditor  
3. Clearly state the amount of over-achievement in energy savings (in mtoe) to determine ESCert eligibility  
4. Attach Form A (PAT target fulfillment) as the primary evidence of target over-achievement  
5. Verify that the energy audit report is issued by an auditor accredited by BEE  
6. Confirm that the entity’s designation as a consumer under EC Act, 2001 is current and valid  
7. Do not leave any mandatory field blank; if not applicable, write “NA” with justification  
8. Retain a copy of the submitted application and all supporting documents for audit purposes  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All eligibility criteria verified and documented  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents collected and quality-checked  
- [ ] Application form filled completely with accurate data  
- [ ] Energy audit report and Form A are attached and signed  
- [ ] Declarations signed and dated  
- [ ] Copy of submission saved for records

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit documents and application via the power exchange portal (IEX/PXIL) after BEE issues ESCerts  
- Take a screenshot of the submission confirmation page showing timestamp and reference number  
- Store the reference number securely in the case file  
- Set follow-up reminders for Day 25 and Day 35

### Escalation Path
- Level 1: Portal helpdesk – https://www.iexindia.com/ (support section) or helpline 1800-180-3333  
- Level 2: Nodal officer – Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE), Ministry of Power, Email: bee@beeindia.gov.in  
- Level 3: RTI filing if no response within statutory SLA (typically 30 days for government queries)

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify approval letter or ESCert issuance confirmation from BEE  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned ESCert volume (each ESCert = 1 mtoe of energy saved)  
- [ ] List ESCerts in the entity’s account on the power exchange platform (IEX/PXIL)  
- [ ] Engage a registered broker or use direct trading window to sell ESCerts  
- [ ] Monitor market price and execute sale at optimal time  
- [ ] Ensure transaction settlement as per exchange rules (T+2 basis typically)  
- [ ] Utilize proceeds for further energy efficiency initiatives or as per board approval  
- [ ] File utilization certificate if required by BEE for compliance verification  
- [ ] Track invoice and success fee for consulting services (to be filled by consultant)
