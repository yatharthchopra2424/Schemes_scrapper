### Discovery Questions
1. Are you classified as a designated consumer under the Energy Conservation Act, 2001, and have you been assigned specific energy consumption reduction targets under the PAT cycle by the Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE)?
2. What is your sector (e.g., thermal power, cement, steel, etc.), and has it been notified by the Central Government for participation in the PAT scheme?
3. Have you implemented energy efficiency measures in the current or past PAT cycle, and do you have verified data on energy savings achieved?
4. Do you maintain baseline and assessment year energy consumption data as required for PAT compliance?
5. Have you submitted Form A (target fulfillment) to BEE through an accredited energy auditor in previous cycles?
6. Is your organization registered as a designated consumer with the BEE or State Designated Agency, and do you hold a valid certificate of registration?
7. Has your board approved participation in the PAT scheme, and do you have a board resolution to that effect?
8. Are you aware that ESCerts (Energy Savings Certificates) are only issued for over-achievement of targets, and that under-achievement requires purchasing certificates to comply?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Energy-intensive industries face rising operational costs and regulatory pressure to reduce energy consumption. While many implement efficiency measures, they often lack a direct financial incentive for over-achieving their PAT targets — missing a chance to monetize their efforts and offset investment costs.

**Scheme intro:**
The Carbon Credit Trading Scheme 2023, administered by the Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE) under the Ministry of Power, enables designated consumers to earn financial benefits through the market-based trading of Energy Savings Certificates (ESCerts). Each ESCert represents one metric tonne of oil equivalent (mtoe) of energy saved and can be traded on recognized power exchanges like Indian Energy Exchange (IEX) and Power Exchange India Ltd (PXIL). The scheme does not provide direct subsidies but creates revenue opportunities via ESCert sales, with value determined by market demand and supply.
