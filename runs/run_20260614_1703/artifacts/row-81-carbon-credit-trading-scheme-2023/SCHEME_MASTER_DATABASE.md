### Carbon Credit Trading Scheme 2023
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE), Ministry of Power
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://www.iexindia.com/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Aligned with PAT cycle timelines; issuance and trading of ESCerts occur after target period completion and verification, typically on an annual basis per cycle.

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Designated consumers under the Energy Conservation Act, 2001
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Industries and establishments in sectors notified by the Central Government (such as thermal power plants, cement, iron & steel, aluminium, fertilizers, pulp & paper, textiles, and petrochemicals)
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Entities assigned specific energy consumption reduction targets under the PAT cycle; only entities that over-achieve their energy savings targets are eligible to receive ESCerts

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Financial gains through market-based trading of Energy Savings Certificates (ESCerts); each ESCert represents one metric tonne of oil equivalent (mtoe) of energy saved; value determined by market demand and supply on power exchanges
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit (via sale on power exchanges)
- Duration of benefit: Annual (aligned with PAT cycle)

#### Documents Required
1. Verified energy audit report from an accredited energy auditor
2. Baseline and assessment year energy consumption data
3. Details of energy efficiency measures implemented
4. Form A (for target fulfillment) as per PAT scheme guidelines
5. Certificate of registration as a designated consumer under EC Act, 2001
6. Board resolution approving participation in PAT scheme
7. No objection certificate from the State Designated Agency (if applicable)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Designated consumers are notified of their energy consumption reduction targets under the relevant PAT cycle by the Bureau of Energy Efficiency (BEE).
2. Entities implement energy efficiency measures to achieve or exceed their targets.
3. Verified energy savings data is submitted to BEE through accredited energy auditors.
4. BEE issues Energy Savings Certificates (ESCerts) to entities that over-achieve their targets.
5. ESCerts are deposited in the entity's account on the Power Exchange platform.
6. Entities can sell ESCerts on power exchanges through registered brokers or directly.
7. Shortfall entities can buy ESCerts to meet compliance obligations.
8. Transactions are settled as per exchange rules, and certificates are transferred upon sale.
