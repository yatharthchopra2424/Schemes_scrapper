# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping IT hardware manufacturers access government incentives that directly boost domestic manufacturing and value chain investments—specifically the PLI Scheme for IT Hardware 2.0 from MeitY. Many companies in laptops, tablets, all-in-one PCs, servers, and ultra small form factor segments miss out because they don’t realize they qualify based on their global manufacturing revenue or incremental investment plans. I’d like to understand your current operations to see if this scheme aligns with your growth strategy.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your consolidated global manufacturing revenue for laptops, tablets, all-in-one PCs, or servers in FY 2021-22?  
2. Are you planning incremental investment in plant, machinery, or equipment for IT hardware manufacturing in India on or after April 1, 2023?  
3. Which target segment products do you manufacture or plan to manufacture in India—laptops (invoice value ≥₹30,000), tablets (≥₹15,000), all-in-one PCs, or servers?  
4. Do you have a localization plan for PCB assembly or other components like battery packs, power adapters, or enclosures as per the scheme’s localization schedule?  
5. Are you part of a group of companies where revenue from subsidiaries or affiliates is being considered for qualification?  
6. Have you already claimed incentives under any other PLI scheme, and if so, for which years?  
7. What is your current employment generation plan in India for manufacturing operations?  
8. Are you a domestic company (>50% beneficially owned by resident Indian citizens), a global company, or a hybrid entity?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Consolidated global manufacturing revenue in target segment > INR 5,000 crore (Global Company) OR > INR 10,000 crore for electronics hardware products/sub-assemblies/components  
- [ ] OR for Hybrid: Global arm revenue > INR 5,000 crore (target segment) OR > INR 10,000 crore (electronics hardware); Domestic arm revenue > INR 10 crore (target segment) OR > INR 20 crore (electronics hardware)  
- [ ] OR for Domestic Company: Revenue > INR 10 crore (target segment) OR > INR 20 crore (electronics hardware products/sub-assemblies/components)  
- [ ] Incremental investment planned post-March 31, 2021 meeting annual thresholds (Year 1: ≥₹50 crore for Global, ≥₹25 crore for Hybrid, ≥₹4 crore for Domestic)  
- [ ] Net incremental sales of manufactured goods in target segment over base year (FY 2021-22 for new applicants) meeting thresholds (Year 1: ≥₹1,000 crore Global, ≥₹500 crore Hybrid, ≥₹50 crore Domestic)  
- [ ] Products manufactured in India fall under target segment: laptops, tablets, all-in-one PCs, servers, or ultra small form factor  
- [ ] Only one application submitted per applicant (no prior PLI IT Hardware 2.0 application)  
- [ ] Commitment to use firmware from Indian or trusted foreign sources for servers (if applicable)  
- [ ] Land and building expenditure not considered for investment eligibility  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears you may meet the eligibility thresholds for the PLI Scheme for IT Hardware 2.0—particularly if your global manufacturing revenue in the target segment exceeds INR 5,000 crore and you’re planning incremental investment. This scheme offers financial incentives of up to 5% on net incremental sales over six years, with annual disbursements tied to verified sales and localization milestones. Shall I walk you through how it works?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
IT hardware manufacturers face significant challenges competing globally due to inadequate infrastructure, high finance costs, limited design capabilities, and skill gaps—resulting in a disability of 8.5% to 11% compared to other major manufacturing economies. This makes it difficult to scale domestic manufacturing despite rising demand for laptops, tablets, PCs, and servers in India.

**Scheme intro:**
The Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for IT Hardware 2.0, administered by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) through the Project Management Agency (PMA) - IFCI Ltd., provides financial incentives on net incremental sales of manufactured goods in India. Incentives range from 1% to 5% based on localization milestones and year of application, with a total expected outlay of INR 16,939 crore over six years. The application portal is https://pliithw.com/.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your consolidated global manufacturing revenue in the target segment (laptops, tablets, all-in-one PCs, servers) exceeds INR 5,000 crore (or INR 10,000 crore for electronics hardware), meeting the Global Company threshold  
- You are planning incremental investment in plant, machinery, or equipment in India on or after April 1, 2023, aligning with the scheme’s investment thresholds  
- Your product portfolio includes laptops (invoice value ≥₹30,000), tablets (≥₹15,000), all-in-one PCs, or servers—covered under the target segment  
- You intend to localize PCB assembly and at least one additional component/sub-assembly annually as per the localization schedule
