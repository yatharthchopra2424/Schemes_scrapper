## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping steel manufacturers access government incentives for value-added production. The PLI Scheme for Specialty Steel supports domestic companies like yours in manufacturing high-value steel grades—such as coated/plated products, high-strength steel, or electrical steel—by providing financial incentives linked to incremental production. I’d like to understand your current operations to see if this scheme aligns with your growth plans.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your company’s incorporation date under the Companies Act 2013?  
2. Are you currently engaged in end-to-end manufacturing of specialty steel grades, where input materials (iron ore/scrap/sponge iron/pellets) are melted and poured within India?  
3. What is your net worth (including group companies), and does it meet or exceed 30% of your total committed investment for expansion?  
4. Which specialty steel product sub-categories are you planning to manufacture (e.g., galvanneal/GI-auto-gr, HR coil, CRGO, or alloy steel wires)?  
5. Are you proposing capacity addition (new facility) or augmentation (existing facility), and what is the committed investment and unit capacity for each?  
6. What is your committed year-on-year incremental production rate for the applied product sub-category (refer to Annexure-III thresholds)?  
7. Do you plan to undertake third-party value addition, and if so, is it limited to 20% of total value addition?  
8. Have you made any investments in the proposed plant facilities after the scheme notification date (04.11.2025 for PLI 1.2 or 06.01.2025 for PLI 1.1)?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Indian steel manufacturers currently operate at the low end of the value chain, with average realization per ton of ₹51,000–₹58,000, compared to import values of ₹1,46,000–₹1,83,000. This gap limits profitability and hinders technological upgradation, especially for specialty steel grades that are either not produced domestically or in insufficient quantities, leading to significant forex outflow.

**Scheme intro:**
The PLI Scheme for Specialty Steel, administered by the Ministry of Steel, Government of India, is a production-linked subsidy offering financial incentives calculated as:  
Incentive = (A) × (B or C, whichever is lowest) × (PLI rate as applicable)/100,  
where A = Eligible incremental production derived from sales,  
B = Weighted average sales price in current year,  
C = Weighted average sales price in base year (2019-20 for PLI 1.0/1.1; 2024-25 for PLI 1.2).  
The PLI rate varies by slab (A, B, C) and year, ranging from 3% to 15%. The annual incentive payable is capped at **Rs. 200 crores per eligible company** across all product categories. Incentive disbursement begins from FY 2026-27 for production period FY 2025-26 to FY 2029-30 (PLI 1.1) or FY 2026-27 to FY 2030-31 (PLI 1.2).

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are a company registered in India under the Companies Act 2013, desirous of manufacturing identified specialty steel grades.  
- You ensure end-to-end manufacturing domestically, with input material melted and poured within India using iron ore/scrap/sponge iron/pellets.  
- Your net worth (including group companies) is not less than 30% of your total committed investment.  
- You limit third-party value addition to a maximum of 20%.  
- Your committed investment and unit capacity meet the minimum thresholds for your chosen product sub-category (e.g., for HR coil sheets API Gr 52<=X<=70: min. investment Rs. 2,750 Cr, min. capacity 4,500 tonnes/year, min. incremental production rate 25%).  
- Your investment is made on or after the scheme notification date (04.11.2025 for PLI 1.2).
