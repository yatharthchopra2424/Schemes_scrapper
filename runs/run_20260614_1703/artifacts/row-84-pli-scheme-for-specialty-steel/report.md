# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Scheme Overview
The **PLI Scheme for Specialty Steel** is a subsidy-type scheme implemented by the **Ministry of Steel, Government of India** with a pan-India geographic scope. The scheme aims to promote manufacturing of specialty steel grades within the country, help the Indian steel industry mature in terms of technology, move up the value chain, incentivize incremental production of eligible specialty steel products, and support capacity addition and augmentation through new and existing facilities. The implementing agency is the **Ministry of Steel**, and the application portal is **[https://plimos.mecon.co.in](https://plimos.mecon.co.in)**. The scheme was last updated in 2025.

### Objectives
- Promote manufacturing of specialty steel grades within the country  
- Help the Indian steel industry mature in terms of technology  
- Move up the value chain in the steel industry  
- Incentivize incremental production of eligible specialty steel products  
- Support capacity addition and augmentation through new and existing facilities  

### Eligibility Matrix
| Criteria | Requirement | Source |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Company Registration | Must be registered in India under the Companies Act 2013 | Key Facts, Gazette Notification (2021), PLI 1.2 Guidelines |
| Manufacturing Activity | Engaged in end-to-end manufacturing of identified specialty steel grades; input material must be melted and poured within India using iron ore/scrap/sponge iron/pellets | Key Facts, Gazette Notification (2021), PLI 1.2 Guidelines |
| Net Worth | Net worth of the company (including group companies) shall not be less than 30% of the total committed investment | Key Facts, Gazette Notification (2021), PLI 1.2 Guidelines |
| Third-Party Value Addition | Allowed up to 20% of total value addition; incentive can only be claimed by the company that manufactured the end product | Key Facts, Gazette Notification (2021), PLI 1.2 Guidelines |
| Investment Timing | Investments made on or after the date of scheme notification (04.11.2025 for PLI 1.2) are eligible; investments before notification are not eligible | Key Facts, Gazette Notification (2021), PLI 1.2 FAQs |
| Joint Ventures | Eligible to participate | Key Facts, Gazette Notification (2021) |
| Financial Standing | Accounts must not be declared as Non-performing assets (NPA) per RBI guidelines; no insolvency proceedings admitted in NCLT; not a willful defaulter or reported as fraud | Gazette Notification (2021), PLI 1.2 Guidelines |
| Product Sub-Category Commitment | Must meet minimum qualifying thresholds for unit capacity, investment, and year-on-year incremental production rate as per Annexure-III | Gazette Notification (2021), PLI 1.2 Guidelines, Annexure-III |
| MoU Requirement | Selected companies must sign a separate MoU with the Ministry of Steel for each product sub-category applied for | Key Facts, Draft MoU Documents |

### Application Process
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Start: Register on PLI Web Portal] --> B[Fill Application Form Online within 30 days of PLI Scheme 1.2 Launch]
    B --> C[Pay Non-Refundable Application Fee of Rs 1 Lakh + GST]
    C --> D[Submit Signed Copy of Online Application + Documents within 10 days of Online Submission]
    D --> E[Application Scrutiny by PMA for Completeness of Supporting Documents]
    E --> F{Is Application Complete?}
    F -->|No| G[One-Time Correction Window of 15 Days Notified After Closure]
    G --> H[Resubmit Corrected Documents Only]
    H --> E
    F -->|Yes| I[Application Evaluation & Ranking by PMA]
    I --> J[Selection of Eligible Applicants Based on Commitment to Investment & Incremental Production (50% Weightage Each)]
    J --> K[Selected Companies Sign MoU with Ministry of Steel]
    K --> L[Submit Performance Security of 0.5% of Committed Investment as Bank Guarantee]
    L --> M[Submit Undertaking Consenting to Audit of Manufacturing Facilities]
    M --> N[Commence Production & File Annual Incentive Claims within 7 Months of FY End]
    N --> O[PMA Scrutinizes Claim & Verifies Eligibility]
    O --> P[Competent Authority Approves Incentive Disbursement]
    P --> Q[Incentive Disbursed to Applicant's Bank Account]
    Q --> R[End: Scheme Tenure (5 Years Production, 5 Years Disbursement)]
```

### Key Caveats & Critical Notes
> **Warning**: Incentive disbursement is limited to the budgeted outlay and capped at Rs. 200 crores per eligible company per annum.  
> **Warning**: The scheme period is for a maximum of five years, with incentive disbursement from FY 2026-27 to FY 2030-31 (PLI 1.2). In case of deferment by up to two years for specific product categories, incentive release will be from FY 2026-27 to FY 2030-31 (or FY 2027-28 to FY 2031-32 if deferred).  
> **Warning**: The actual permissible investment must be more than the limiting investment for a product sub-category to be eligible for incentive.  
> **Warning**: Performance security of 0.5% of committed investment must be submitted and is valid throughout the scheme period.  
> **Warning**: Claims for disbursement of incentive must be filed within seven months from the end of the financial year to which the claim pertains.  
> **Warning**: Investments made before the date of notification of the scheme (04.11.2025) are not eligible under permissible investment.  
> **Warning**: The selected company must enter into a separate MoU with the Ministry of Steel for each product sub-category applied for.  
> **Warning**: If a company fails to meet committed production for two consecutive years during the scheme period, the performance security (0.5% of committed investment) may be invoked subject to approval by the competent authority.  
> **Warning**: In case of manipulation, misrepresentation, or false information, the applicant must refund the entire incentive amount along with interest calculated at twice the rate of 3 years SBI MCLR prevailing on the date of disbursement, compounded annually.  
> **Warning**: The net worth criteria must be checked with respect to total committed investment across all applied product sub-categories if applying for multiple sub-categories.  
> **Warning**: For capacity augmentation, 50% of the threshold investment shall be applicable.  
> **Warning**: Investment made in refurbishing/relocation of existing plant & equipment within India shall not be considered as permissible investment.  
> **Warning**: The project(s) under PLI Scheme 1.0 for which MoU has been signed shall not qualify to participate in PLI Scheme 1.1.  
> **Warning**: Production eligible for incentive in a year shall be limited to committed production for that year.  
> **Warning**: The eligible production for claiming incentive shall not exceed the committed unit capacity.
