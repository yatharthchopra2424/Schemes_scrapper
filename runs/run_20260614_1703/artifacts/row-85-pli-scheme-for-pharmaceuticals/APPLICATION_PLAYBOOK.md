# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a pharmaceutical manufacturer (including MSME)
- [ ] Applicant falls into one of the three groups based on Global Manufacturing Revenue (GMR) from pharmaceutical goods and/or in vitro diagnostic medical devices in FY 2019-20:
  - Group A: GMR ≥ Rs 5,000 crore
  - Group B: GMR between Rs 500 crore and Rs 5,000 crore
  - Group C: GMR < Rs 500 crore (with sub-group for MSME)
- [ ] Minimum cumulative investment over five years commitment:
  - Group A: Rs 1,000 crore
  - Group B: Rs 250 crore
  - Group C: Rs 50 crore
- [ ] Minimum year-on-year percentage growth in sales commitment:
  - Group A/B/C: 200/50/10 in FY 2021-22, increasing to 1000/250/50 by FY 2025-26
- [ ] For first year of production: Achieve threshold sales specified in guidelines
- [ ] For subsequent years: Minimum 7% year-on-year growth required
- [ ] Applicant must not have been declared bankrupt, wilful defaulter, or reported as fraud by any bank/financial institution
- [ ] Second-hand/used/refurbished plant, machinery, equipment, utilities, or R&D equipment is not eligible for investment consideration
- [ ] Expenditure on consumables and raw material used for manufacturing is not considered as eligible investment
- [ ] Investment made under other PLI schemes (e.g., PLI for Bulk Drugs) cannot be double-counted for eligibility under this scheme
- [ ] Minimum repayment period of loan (if applicable) shall not be less than 3 years
- [ ] At least 50% of total sanctioned loan amount must be on components eligible under the scheme for interest subvention or capital subsidy
- [ ] Loan proposals availing benefit under this scheme cannot avail benefits under any other technology up-gradation scheme of Government or State Government or autonomous institutions/PSUs

**Application Portal:** https://pli-pharma.udyamimitra.in  
**Application Window:** 60 days starting from 2nd June, 2021 to 31st July, 2021 (both dates inclusive). Subsequent windows may be notified; refer to latest notices for current status.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation/Registration
- PAN of the entity
- ITR (Income Tax Returns)
- GST registration certificate
- Bank statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (for MSMEs)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Application form as per Appendix K
- Undertaking in format given in Appendix E
- Undertaking in format given in Appendix G consenting audit of manufacturing site/offices
- Supporting documents as per application form
- Application fee payment receipt
- Documents evidencing financial closure (for benefit-share cases)
- Duly signed funding agreement
- Share allotment agreement (if benefit-share via share allotment)
- Report validating product/technology at claimed TRL (if required)
- Project plan based on sequential milestones
- Valuation report of IP proposed to be in-licensed from Academia (if applicable)
- Academic intent letter (if applicable)
- Details of funding received (if any)

### Document Quality Check
- File size: Each document must be under 5 MB unless otherwise specified
- File naming: Must match the document type (e.g., "PAN_Card.pdf", "GST_Certificate.pdf")
- All documents must be self-attested or certified by a Chartered Accountant/Company Secretary where applicable
- Documents must be in English or Hindi; if in another language, a certified translation must be attached
- Scanned copies must be clear, legible, and in PDF format
- Undertakings must be on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value and notarized if required
- Financial documents (ITR, bank statements) must be for the relevant financial years as specified in guidelines

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required for this scheme. Standard sections include:  
- Executive Summary  
- Project Background and Objectives  
- Technical Details (including product/technology validation at claimed TRL)  
- Investment Plan (cumulative investment over five years)  
- Sales Projections (year-on-year growth, incremental sales over base year FY 2019-20)  
- Implementation Schedule with Milestones  
- Financial Viability Report  
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan  
- Compliance with Eligibility Criteria (Group classification, investment, growth thresholds)  
- Details of Funding Arrangement  
- Undertakings (Appendix E and G)  
- Annexures as specified in the application form  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Read Guidelines Thoroughly:** Refer to the Operational Guidelines (Dated-1st June, 2021) and all corrigenda before filling the form.  
2. **Group Classification:** Accurately determine and declare your group (A/B/C) based on GMR from pharmaceutical goods and/or IVD in FY 2019-20.  
3. **Investment Commitment:** Enter the exact minimum cumulative investment amount as per your group (A: Rs 1,000 crore, B: Rs 250 crore, C: Rs 50 crore).  
4. **Sales Growth Commitment:** Specify the year-on-year percentage growth in sales as per the trajectory (200/50/10 in FY 2021-22 to 1000/250/50 by FY 2025-26 for A/B/C).  
5. **First Year Sales Threshold:** If applicable, enter the threshold sales for the first year of production as specified in guidelines.  
6. **Subsequent Year Growth:** Confirm minimum 7% year-on-year growth for years after the first.  
7. **Undertakings:** Ensure Appendices E and G are filled, signed, and uploaded as per format.  
8. **Application Fee:** Pay the non-refundable fee electronically and upload the receipt.  
9. **Benefit-Share Cases:** If applicable, upload documents evidencing financial closure, funding agreement, and share allotment agreement.  
10. **Technology Validation:** If claiming benefits based on new product/technology, attach the TRL validation report.  
11. **Academic Collaboration:** If in-licensing IP from Academia, include valuation report and academic intent letter.  
12. **Double-Check:** Verify that no second-hand/refurbished equipment is claimed as eligible investment.  
13. **Declaration:** Confirm that the applicant has not been declared bankrupt, wilful defaulter, or fraud by any bank/financial institution.  
14. **PLI Scheme Disclosure:** Declare that investment under other PLI schemes (e.g., Bulk Drugs) is not double-counted.  
15. **Loan Terms:** If availing loan, confirm minimum repayment period ≥ 3 years and that ≥50% of loan is for eligible components.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All fields in the application form are complete and accurate  
- [ ] Group classification (A/B/C) is correctly determined and declared  
- [ ] Investment commitment matches group-specific minimum (A: 1,000 Cr, B: 250 Cr, C: 50 Cr)  
- [ ] Sales growth commitment follows the prescribed trajectory (200/50/10 → 1000/250/50)  
- [ ] First-year sales threshold (if applicable) is specified  
- [ ] Subsequent year growth ≥7% is confirmed  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are attached and legible  
- [ ] Universal documents (PAN, ITR, GST, Udyam, etc.) are valid and up-to-date  
- [ ] Undertakings (Appendix E and G) are signed and notarized where required  
- [ ] Application fee payment receipt is uploaded  
- [ ] No second-hand/refurbished equipment is claimed as eligible investment  
- [ ] No double-counting of investment from other PLI schemes  
- [ ] Loan compliance verified (if applicable): min 3-year repayment, ≥50% eligible components  
- [ ] Declarations on bankruptcy, defaulter, fraud status are made  
- [ ] Benefit-share documents (if applicable) are complete: financial closure, funding agreement, share allotment  
- [ ] TRL validation report attached (if claiming product/technology benefits)  
- [ ] Academic intent letter and IP valuation report attached (if in-licensing from Academia)  
- [ ] All documents are in PDF format, clearly named, and under size limits  
- [ ] Final review done by senior consultant before submission

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the approval letter from the Department of Pharmaceuticals  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount and incentive rate applicable based on goods category and financial year  
- [ ] Establish manufacturing facilities and commence production (FY 2021-22 as gestation period)  
- [ ] Begin periodic submission of incentive claims based on incremental sales over base year (FY 2019-20)  
- [ ] Ensure claims are verified by PMA and State Screening Committee (SSC) before disbursement  
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate (UC) in GFR-12A format via PFMS portal for fund utilization  
- [ ] Maintain records for audit by Comptroller & Auditor General of India  
- [ ] Track compliance with minimum cumulative investment and year-on-year sales growth thresholds  
- [ ] Prepare for success fee invoice to client upon receipt of incentive disbursement  
- [ ] Monitor for any corrigenda or guideline updates that may affect ongoing eligibility
