# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping pharmaceutical manufacturers access government incentives that directly support production growth and technological advancement. The Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for Pharmaceuticals is designed specifically for companies like yours that are looking to scale up manufacturing of high-value pharmaceutical goods and integrate into global value chains. I’d like to understand your current operations and growth plans to see if this scheme aligns with your objectives.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your company’s Global Manufacturing Revenue (GMR) from pharmaceutical goods and/or in vitro diagnostic medical devices in FY 2019-20?  
2. Are you planning to make a minimum cumulative investment over the next five years (Group A: ₹1,000 crore, Group B: ₹250 crore, Group C: ₹50 crore)?  
3. What is your year-on-year sales growth trajectory, and are you targeting the minimum growth thresholds (e.g., 200% for Group A in FY 2021-22, increasing to 1000% by FY 2025-26)?  
4. Are you manufacturing Category 1 & 2 goods (eligible for 10% incentive) or Category 3 goods (eligible for 5% incentive)?  
5. Have you incurred any expenditure on second-hand or refurbished plant, machinery, or equipment for this project?  
6. Are you currently availing benefits under any other PLI scheme (e.g., PLI for Bulk Drugs) that might affect eligibility?  
7. Do you have the Certificate of Incorporation/Registration and PAN of the entity ready for application?  
8. Are you prepared to submit an application fee and supporting documents such as undertaking in Appendix E and G, and project plan based on sequential milestones?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] GMR ≥ ₹5,000 crore (Group A) or between ₹500 crore and ₹5,000 crore (Group B) or < ₹500 crore (Group C, with MSME sub-group)  
- [ ] Minimum cumulative investment over five years: Group A: ₹1,000 crore, Group B: ₹250 crore, Group C: ₹50 crore  
- [ ] Minimum year-on-year sales growth: Group A/B/C: 200/50/10 in FY 2021-22, increasing to 1000/250/50 by FY 2025-26  
- [ ] For first year of production: threshold sales as per guidelines; subsequent years: minimum 7% year-on-year growth  
- [ ] No bankruptcy, wilful default, or fraud reported by any bank/financial institution  
- [ ] No second-hand/used/refurbished plant, machinery, equipment, utilities, or R&D equipment claimed for investment  
- [ ] No expenditure on consumables and raw material considered as eligible investment  
- [ ] Investment not double-counted under other PLI schemes (e.g., PLI for Bulk Drugs)  
- [ ] Minimum repayment period of loan (if applicable) ≥ 3 years  
- [ ] At least 50% of total sanctioned loan amount on components eligible under the scheme  
- [ ] Loan proposals not availing benefits under any other technology up-gradation scheme of Government/State Government/autonomous institutions/PSUs  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your company may meet the eligibility thresholds for the PLI Scheme for Pharmaceuticals, which offers financial incentives of up to 10% on incremental sales over the base year (FY 2019-20) for up to six years. Let me walk you through how this scheme works and how we can support your application.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Pharmaceutical manufacturers face high capital expenditure and risk when scaling up production of high-value goods, especially when aiming to meet global quality standards and penetrate international markets. Many struggle to justify investments in technological upgradation and capacity expansion due to uncertain returns and long payback periods.

**Scheme intro:**
The Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for Pharmaceuticals, administered by the Department of Pharmaceuticals, Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers, provides financial incentives based on incremental sales of eligible pharmaceutical goods over the base year (FY 2019-20). Incentive rates are 10% for FY 2022-23 to FY 2025-26, 8% for 2026-27, and 6% for 2027-28 for Category 1 & 2 goods; and 5% for FY 2022-23 to FY 2025-26, 4% for 2026-27, and 3% for 2027-28 for Category 3 goods. The scheme has a total financial outlay of ₹15,000 crore and is disbursed for a maximum of six years, contingent on achieving minimum cumulative investment and year-on-year sales growth thresholds.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your company’s GMR in FY 2019-20 places you in [Group A/B/C] based on the threshold of ≥ ₹5,000 crore / ₹500 crore–₹5,000 crore / < ₹500 crore

- You are targeting year-on-year sales growth aligned with the scheme’s thresholds: starting at [percentage]% in FY 2021-22 and scaling to [percentage]% by FY 2025-26  
- Your product portfolio includes [Category 1 & 2 / Category 3] goods, eligible for the corresponding incentive rates
