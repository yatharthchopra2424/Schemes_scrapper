# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Pharmaceutical manufacturing units in a cluster who have come together to form a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) to execute the project of developing common facility.
- [ ] Minimum of 5 pharma units as members of the SPV.
- [ ] SPV should be a separate legal entity registered under the Companies Act or Registration of Societies Act.
- [ ] Pharma enterprises shall hold at least 51% equity of the SPV.
- [ ] Combined net worth of members of SPV shall be equivalent to total grant amount applied for.
- [ ] Each SPV member must have a net worth of at least 1.5 times of their proposed equity contribution.
- [ ] SPV members shall be legally independent entities without any related-party relationship with each other as described under Accounting Standard (AS) 18 of the Companies (Accounting Standard) Rules, 2006.
- [ ] For APICF: Incentive is 70% of approved project cost or Rs. 20 crore, whichever is less (90% or Rs. 20 crore for Himalayan and North Eastern States).
- [ ] For RPTUAS: Incentive based on average turnover: 20% of investment for turnover Rs. 1-50 crore, 15% for Rs. 50-250 crore, and 10% for Rs. 250-500 crore, subject to a maximum of Rs. 2 crore per plant.
- [ ] For PMPDS: Support provided for studies, awareness programs, database creation, and industry promotion.
- [ ] Application Portal: https://spi.udyamimitra.in
- [ ] Status: Rolling basis — applications accepted throughout the year as per notifications; specific windows may be opened for sub-schemes (e.g., API-CF invitation from 11.12.2024 to 13.01.2025).

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration of SPV
- PAN of the entity
- ITR (Income Tax Returns)
- GST registration certificate
- Bank statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (for MSMEs)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed project report with gap analysis
- CA certified expenditure statement
- Revised Schedule M certificate or WHO-GMP certificate
- Undertaking for fund utilization
- Bank sanction letter for loan component (if applicable)
- Net worth certificate of SPV members
- Shareholding pattern of SPV
- Undertaking of legally independent entities without related-party relationships

### Document Quality Check
- File size: Each document should be under 10 MB unless specified otherwise.
- File naming: Use format `SchemeName_DocumentType_ClientID_Date.pdf` (e.g., `SPI_DPR_Case001_20241201.pdf`).
- All documents must be legible, scanned in color if original is colored, and text-searchable where applicable.
- Certificates (e.g., Revised Schedule M, WHO-GMP) must be valid and issued by authorized bodies.
- Undertakings must be on company letterhead, signed, and stamped.
- Bank sanction letter must be on bank letterhead, signed, and dated.
- CA certified expenditure statement must be signed and stamped by a practicing Chartered Accountant.
- All documents must be in English or accompanied by a certified translation.

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required for this scheme.  
**Standard Sections to Include:**  
- Executive Summary  
- Introduction and Background  
- Objectives of the Project  
- Cluster Profile and SPV Details  
- Gap Analysis  
- Technical Description of Proposed Facilities  
- Implementation Schedule (with milestones)  
- Financial Projections and Viability Report  
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan  
- Environmental and Safety Compliance  
- O&M Plan and User Charge Mechanism  
- Statutory Clearances Status  
- Annexures (including supporting documents)  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application portal at https://spi.udyamimitra.in using valid credentials.  
2. Select the correct sub-scheme (APICF, RPTUAS, or PMPDS) based on the project type.  
3. Fill all mandatory fields marked with *; incomplete forms will be rejected.  
4. Ensure SPV details (name, registration number, date of incorporation) match the Certificate of Incorporation exactly.  
5. Enter financial figures (project cost, equity contribution, loan amount) consistently across all sections and supporting documents.  
6. Upload documents in the prescribed format (PDF, max size as per portal limits) and verify successful upload.  
7. Confirm that the undertaking for fund utilization and legally independent entities is signed and uploaded.  
8. Review the application thoroughly before final submission; use the preview function if available.  
9. Save a copy of the submitted application and acknowledgment receipt.  
10. Note down the application reference number immediately after submission.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All fields in the application form are complete and accurate.  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are attached and legible.  
- [ ] Universal documents (PAN, ITR, GST, Bank Statements, Udyam) are attached.  
- [ ] Undertakings are signed, stamped, and on letterhead.  
- [ ] CA certified expenditure statement is signed and stamped by a practicing CA.  
- [ ] Bank sanction letter (if applicable) is on bank letterhead and signed.  
- [ ] Net worth certificates are issued by a certified CA or bank.  
- [ ] Shareholding pattern reflects at least 51% equity held by pharma enterprises.  
- [ ] Declarations and consents are signed where required.  
- [ ] Application reference number has been generated and recorded.

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a screenshot of the submission confirmation page showing the application reference number and timestamp.  
- Store the reference number securely in the case file and set up automated reminders for follow-up.  
- Save a PDF copy of the submitted application and all uploaded documents for audit purposes.  
- Notify the client of successful submission with the reference number and expected timelines.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the approval letter from the Scheme Steering Committee (SSC).  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount matches the approved project cost and incentive limits (e.g., up to Rs. 20 crore for APICF, up to Rs. 2 crore per plant for RPTUAS).  
- [ ] Submit required documents for first instalment release (e.g., Revised Schedule M or WHO-GMP certificate, CA certified expenditure statement, utilization certificate format).  
- [ ] Track disbursement of the first instalment (50% of eligible amount, up to Rs. 1 crore) within 30 days of document submission.  
- [ ] Monitor milestone achievement for subsequent instalments as per the approval terms.  
- [ ] Ensure utilization certificates are submitted in GFR-12A format via PFMS portal, duly certified by CA and countersigned by Head of SPV.  
- [ ] Coordinate with the client for successful utilization of funds and project completion.  
- [ ] Prepare and submit final utilization certificate and project completion report.  
- [ ] Follow up on success fee invoice as per the consultancy agreement.
