## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping pharmaceutical manufacturing units access government support for cluster development and technology upgradation. The Scheme for Strengthening of Pharmaceuticals Industry (SPI) is designed specifically for pharma clusters looking to build common facilities or upgrade production to meet WHO-GMP and Schedule M standards. I’d like to understand your current setup to see if this aligns with your growth plans.

### Discovery Questions
1. How many pharmaceutical manufacturing units are you collaborating with in your cluster, and are they legally independent entities?
2. Have you formed or are you planning to form a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) registered under the Companies Act or Societies Registration Act?
3. What is the combined net worth of the SPV members, and does each member have a net worth at least 1.5 times their proposed equity contribution?
4. Do pharma enterprises hold at least 51% equity in the SPV?
5. Are you seeking support for creating common facilities (like R&D labs, testing centres, effluent treatment plants) or for upgrading technology to meet revised Schedule M or WHO-GMP standards?
6. What is the average annual turnover of the units applying for technology upgradation support?
7. Have you already incurred eligible expenditure on machinery or civil works after January 1, 2024?
8. Are you located in a Himalayan or North Eastern State, which may qualify for higher subsidy rates?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Minimum 5 pharma units as members of the SPV  
- [ ] SPV is a separate legal entity registered under Companies Act or Societies Registration Act  
- [ ] Pharma enterprises hold at least 51% equity in the SPV  
- [ ] Combined net worth of SPV members equals total grant amount applied for  
- [ ] Each SPV member has net worth ≥ 1.5× their proposed equity contribution  
- [ ] SPV members are legally independent entities with no related-party relationships (AS 18)  
- [ ] For APICF: Project cost supports common facilities (R&D, testing, ETP, logistics, training)  
- [ ] For RPTUAS: Units are MSMEs with proven track record seeking tech upgradation to meet Schedule M/WHO-GMP  
- [ ] For RPTUAS: Loan component eligible under scheme; at least 50% of sanctioned loan on eligible activities  
- [ ] For RPTUAS: Incentive based on turnover — 20% (₹1–50 Cr), 15% (₹50–250 Cr), 10% (₹250–500 Cr), max ₹2 Cr/plant  
- [ ] For APICF in Himalayan/N.E. States: Up to 90% or ₹20 Cr, whichever is less  
- [ ] Expenditure claimed incurred after January 1, 2024 (for RPTUAS)  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your cluster may qualify for the Scheme for Strengthening of Pharmaceuticals Industry (SPI), which offers up to ₹20 crore in capital subsidy for common facilities or up to ₹2 crore per plant for technology upgradation. Let me walk you through how this works.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Pharma clusters often struggle with isolated infrastructure, high upgrade costs, and difficulty meeting evolving regulatory standards like Schedule M and WHO-GMP. MSMEs face financing barriers when trying to modernize plants, and lack shared R&D or testing facilities limits competitiveness and quality consistency.

**Scheme intro:**
The Scheme for Strengthening of Pharmaceuticals Industry (SPI), administered by the Department of Pharmaceuticals, Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers, Government of India, provides capital subsidy and grant-in-aid through three sub-schemes:  
- **Assistance to Pharmaceutical Industry for Common Facilities (APICF):** Up to 70% of approved project cost or ₹20 crore (whichever is less); 90% or ₹20 crore for Himalayan and North Eastern States  
- **Revamped Pharmaceutical Technology Upgradation Assistance Scheme (RPTUAS):** 20% of investment for turnover ₹1–50 Cr, 15% for ₹50–250 Cr, 10% for ₹250–500 Cr, max ₹2 crore per plant  
- **Pharmaceutical & Medical Devices Promotion and Development Scheme (PMPDS):** Support for studies, awareness programs, database creation, and industry-academia collaboration  
Applications are accepted via the portal: https://spi.udyamimitra.in

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your cluster has ≥5 pharma units forming an SPV with pharma enterprises holding ≥51% equity  
- Each member meets the net worth criterion (≥1.5× equity contribution) and combined net worth matches grant sought  
- SPV members are legally independent with no related-party relationships (AS 18)  
- You’re pursuing eligible activities such as setting up common testing centres or upgrading machinery to meet Schedule M standards
