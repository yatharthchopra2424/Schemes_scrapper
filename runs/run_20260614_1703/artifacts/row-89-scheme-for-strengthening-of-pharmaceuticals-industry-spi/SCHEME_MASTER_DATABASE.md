### Scheme for Strengthening of Pharmaceuticals Industry (SPI)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department of Pharmaceuticals, Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://spi.udyamimitra.in
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Applications are accepted throughout the year as per notifications; specific windows may be opened for sub-schemes (e.g., API-CF invitation from 11.12.2024 to 13.01.2025)

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Pharmaceutical manufacturing units in a cluster who have come together to form a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Pharmaceutical manufacturing
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: 
  - Minimum of 5 pharma units as members of SPV
  - SPV should be a separate legal entity registered under the Companies Act or Registration of Societies Act
  - Pharma enterprises shall hold at least 51% equity of the SPV
  - Combined net worth of members of SPV shall be equivalent to total grant amount applied for
  - Each SPV member must have a net worth of at least 1.5 times of their proposed equity contribution
  - SPV members shall be legally independent entities without any related-party relationship as described under Accounting Standard (AS) 18 of the Companies (Accounting Standard) Rules, 2006

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: 
  - For APICF: Up to Rs. 20 crore for APICF (70% of project cost or Rs. 20 crore, whichever is less); up to Rs. 2 crore per plant for RPTUAS; support for PMPDS activities as per approval
  - For APICF in Himalayan and North Eastern States: 90% or Rs. 20 crore (whichever is less)
  - For RPTUAS: 20% of investment for turnover Rs. 1-50 crore, 15% for Rs. 50-250 crore, and 10% for Rs. 250-500 crore, subject to a maximum of Rs. 2 crore per plant
- Disbursement mode: Reimbursement (disbursement is made in instalments based on milestone achievement)
- Duration of benefit: One-time (financial support provided as capital subsidy or grant-in-aid)

#### Documents Required
1. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration of SPV
2. PAN of the entity
3. Detailed project report with gap analysis
4. CA certified expenditure statement
5. Revised Schedule M certificate or WHO-GMP certificate
6. Undertaking for fund utilization
7. Bank sanction letter for loan component (if applicable)
8. Net worth certificate of SPV members
9. Shareholding pattern of SPV
10. Undertaking of legally independent entities without related-party relationships

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Pharmaceutical units form a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) with minimum 5 pharma units and at least 51% equity held by pharma enterprises.
2. SPV submits application through the Project Management Consultant (PMC) portal with detailed project proposal, gap analysis, and required documents.
3. PMC examines the proposal, processes loan application if applicable, and forwards to lending institution for sanction within 90 days.
4. PMC scrutinizes proposal, verifies eligibility, and submits appraisal report with recommendations to Technical Committee within one month.
5. Technical Committee evaluates and recommends to Scheme Steering Committee (SSC).
6. SSC approves the project and sanctions financial support.
7. Upon approval, applicant submits requisite documents (e.g., Revised Schedule M or WHO-GMP certificate, CA certified expenditure) for instalment release.
8. First instalment (50% of eligible amount, up to Rs. 1 crore) released within 30 days of document submission.
9. Subsequent instalments released upon verification of milestone achievement and submission of required documents.
