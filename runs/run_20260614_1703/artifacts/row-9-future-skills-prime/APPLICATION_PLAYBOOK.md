# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a student or professional seeking to acquire skills in digital technology domains
- [ ] Applicant is interested in domains such as artificial intelligence, data analytics, cloud computing, cybersecurity, or related fields
- [ ] Applicant seeks certification aligned with National Occupational Standards (NOS) and National Skills Qualification Framework (NSQF)
- [ ] Applicant aims to enhance employability in digital technology roles (e.g., machine learning engineer, data scientist, business intelligence analyst, information security analyst)
- [ ] Applicant is open to industry-backed programs offered by NASSCOM and MeitY
- [ ] Applicant understands that certification is subject to successful completion of training and assessment
- [ ] Applicant acknowledges that program availability and course offerings may vary based on industry demand and updates to NOS/NSQF

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Identity proof (Aadhaar card, PAN card, passport, or voter ID)
- Address proof (utility bill, rental agreement, or Aadhaar)
- Educational certificates (if applicable)
- Updated resume/CV
- Bank account details (for potential stipend or fee payment references)
- Passport-sized photographs

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- No scheme-specific documents are required beyond universal documents, as the scheme does not provide direct financial support and focuses on skill development through enrollment in courses.

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be clear, legible scans or photographs
- File size should not exceed 5 MB per document
- File names must match the applicant’s name as per identity proof
- Documents must be in PDF or JPEG/PNG format
- Self-attested copies are acceptable where certification is not required
- Language: Documents in English or Hindi are preferred; other Indian languages may require certified translation

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not required. The Future Skills PRIME scheme focuses on individual skill development and certification through enrollment in training programs. No project report or Detailed Project Report (DPR) is needed for application.  
**Note:** Verify with the nodal officer if any special program requires a proposal (though standard individual enrollment does not).

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official portal: https://futureskillsprime.in/  
2. Create an account using a valid email ID and mobile number  
3. Complete the profile with accurate personal, educational, and professional details  
4. Browse available courses under emerging technologies (AI, data analytics, cloud, cybersecurity, etc.)  
5. Select a course that aligns with your career objectives  
6. Review course details, including duration, assessment criteria, and certification outcome  
7. Proceed to enrollment and accept the terms and conditions  
8. Complete any prerequisite screening or eligibility questions if prompted  
9. Confirm enrollment and note down the enrollment ID or reference number  
10. Save a screenshot of the confirmation page for your records  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] Applicant profile is complete and accurate  
- [ ] Selected course is clearly aligned with career goals and scheme objectives  
- [ ] All mandatory fields in the enrollment form are filled  
- [ ] Terms and conditions have been reviewed and accepted  
- [ ] Enrollment confirmation has been received and saved  
- [ ] No discrepancies in personal details (name, email, contact) across entries  
- [ ] Applicant understands the assessment and certification process  

---

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit enrollment via the official portal: https://futureskillsprime.in/  
- Upon successful enrollment, take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing enrollment ID, course name, and timestamp  
- Store the screenshot and enrollment reference number in the client’s case folder  
- Set a follow-up reminder for 7 days after enrollment to confirm access to learning materials  
- Schedule another reminder before the course end date to track progress and assessment readiness

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Confirm enrollment activation and access to course content  
- [ ] Monitor learner’s progress through the platform (if shared with consent)  
- [ ] Ensure completion of all modules and assignments  
- [ ] Verify eligibility for final assessment  
- [ ] Confirm successful completion of training and assessment  
- [ ] Download and validate the issued certificate from the portal  
- [ ] Verify that the certificate is aligned with NOS and NSQF standards  
- [ ] Assist the learner in updating their resume and professional profiles (e.g., LinkedIn) with the new certification  
- [ ] Provide guidance on leveraging the certification for job applications or career advancement  
- [ ] Collect feedback on the learning experience for internal quality improvement  
- [ ] Close the case upon successful certification or formally document discontinuation with reasons
