# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Entity registered in India as a company under Companies Act, 2013 or previous company law (excluding one-person companies, companies with unlimited liability, dividend-paying companies, or companies whose objects include promotion of commerce, art, science, sports, education, research, social welfare, religion, charity, or environmental protection, or whose profits may only be applied for promoting said objects)
- [ ] Entity registered as a Limited Liability Partnership under the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008
- [ ] Entity registered as a partnership firm other than a sole proprietorship firm under the Indian Partnership Act, 1932
- [ ] For Early Stage Projects: Applicant must be a startup with a valid certificate from the Inter-Ministerial Board of Certification under DPIIT notification G.S.R. 127(E) dated 19.2.2019, OR an MSME with a valid Udyam Registration Certificate under MSME Ministry notification S.O. 2119(E) dated 26.6.2020
- [ ] No key managerial personnel convicted of an offence involving moral turpitude or corruption and sentenced to imprisonment for six months or more
- [ ] Applicant or its holding company has not filed for insolvency, winding-up, liquidation, or resolution proceedings
- [ ] Applicant must disclose all companies, LLPs, and registered partnership firms belonging to its related group in the application
- [ ] Total financial assistance to applicants belonging to a related group will not exceed 5% of the total financial outlay of the Scheme, unless the Central Government decides otherwise

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation Certificate
- PAN Card
- ITR (last 3 years)
- GST Registration Certificate
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration Certificate (if applicable)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Application form prescribed in Appendix K
- Undertaking in the format given in Appendix E
- Undertaking in the format given in Appendix G consenting audit of manufacturing site/offices
- Project plan based on sequential milestones to be achieved including associated expenditure and timeframes
- Documents evidencing financial closure for the approved total project cost
- Duly signed funding agreement with the approved project plan annexed thereto
- If opting for benefit-share by way of share allotment: duly signed share allotment agreement
- If required by Central Government: report to validate that the product or technology which the project proposed to take to higher TRLs is at TRL claimed in the application, furnished by an agency from among those specified on the Scheme portal
- Valid contract executed between the applicant and Academia, or in case such contract has not yet been executed: academic intent letter signed by an officer of Academia duly authorised in this behalf, downloadable from the Scheme portal
- Valuation report of the IP proposed to be in-licensed from Academia, furnished by an agency from among those specified for this purpose on the Scheme portal based on fair value assessment of such IP, or in case such assessment has not been done: estimate of the value of that IP in the project plan as referred to in paragraph 3.4.5 and in the aforesaid valid contract or academic intent letter

### Document Quality Check
- File size: Each document must be under 10 MB
- File naming: Must match the document type (e.g., "Undertaking_Appendix_E.pdf")
- Certification: All certificates must be valid and not expired
- Language: Documents must be in English or Hindi; if in another language, a certified translation must be provided
- Scans: Must be legible, clear, and in PDF format

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required for this scheme as part of the project plan based on sequential milestones. The DPR must include:
- Executive Summary
- Project Objectives and Scope
- Technical Description (including TRL progression)
- Market Analysis and Commercialization Strategy
- Detailed Project Plan with Milestones, Descriptors, and Associated Expenditure
- Financial Plan (including co-funding structure and utilization of instalments)
- Implementation Schedule (Gantt chart or PERT/CPM)
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan
- Monitoring and Evaluation Framework
- Annexures: Team CVs, Letters of Support, Quotations, Regulatory Clearances

If any section is unclear, verify with the nodal officer or PMA.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Use only the online portal maintained by the Project Management Agency (PMA) at https://pharma-dept.gov.in/schemes; if unavailable, submit physically to PMA.
2. Fill all fields in the application form (Appendix K) accurately; incomplete forms will be returned and must be revised within 15 days of communication.
3. Upload all scheme-specific and universal documents as per the checklist in Stage 2.
4. Submit the undertaking in Appendix E and Appendix G (consenting to audit) as separate uploads.
5. Attach the project plan with sequential milestones, expenditure, and timeframes as a single PDF.
6. Ensure the academic intent letter or valid contract with Academia is included if applicable.
7. Attach the IP valuation report or estimate as required.
8. Pay the non-refundable application fee electronically as mentioned in Appendix C.
9. Save the application reference number immediately after submission.
10. Do not submit after the Application Window closes; late applications will not be accepted.
11. Retain a screenshot of the submission confirmation page.
11. If the portal shows an error, contact PMA helpdesk immediately and retain the ticket number.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All fields in the application form (Appendix K) are complete
- [ ] All universal documents are attached and valid
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are attached and valid
- [ ] Undertakings (Appendix E and G) are signed and uploaded

- [ ] Academic intent letter or valid contract with Academia is attached (if applicable)
- [ ] IP valuation report or estimate is attached (if applicable)
- [ ] Application fee payment receipt is attached
- [ ] Declarations and undertakings are signed by authorized signatory
- [ ] Application reference number is saved and communicated to client
- [ ] Screenshot of submission confirmation is stored

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the online portal at https://pharma-dept.gov.in/schemes
- Immediately after submission, take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing the application reference number and timestamp
- Store the reference number in the case file and communicate it to the client
- Set a follow-up reminder for 15 working days post-submission to check for PMA communication (in case of incomplete application)
- If the application is marked incomplete, complete and resubmit within 15 days of PMA communication to avoid closure

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and store the approval letter from the Central Government
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned amount of financial assistance against the approved total project cost
- [ ] Confirm that the fourth instalment is at least 15% of the approved total financial assistance and will be disbursed on a reimbursement basis
- [ ] Ensure co-funding is arranged such that total co-funding equals approved total project cost less approved total financial assistance
- [ ] Verify that the proportion of total co-funding to approved total financial assistance is maintained at each instalment disbursement stage
- [ ] Open a project-specific dedicated account in a Scheduled Commercial Bank prior to execution of the funding agreement
- [ ] Submit documents evidencing financial closure for the approved total project cost within 60 days of conveying acceptance
- [ ] Execute the duly signed funding agreement with the approved project plan annexed
- [ ] If opting for benefit-share via share allotment, execute the duly signed share allotment agreement
- [ ] If required, submit the TRL validation report from an agency specified on the Scheme portal
- [ ] Ensure cumulative co-funding in the required proportion is credited into the project account before each disbursement
- [ ] Monitor milestone achievement for disbursement of milestone-linked financial assistance in four instalments
- [ ] Prepare for inspection and special audit (including forensic audit) by the Central Government on the project account
- [ ] Maintain records for utilization certificate submission as per GFR-2017
- [ ] Track benefit-sharing mechanisms (share allotment or revenue sharing) as per the funding agreement
- [ ] Confirm that financial assistance is disbursed into the project-specific dedicated account
- [ ] Prepare for potential modifications to the approved project plan if permitted in writing by the Central Government
- [ ] If waitlisted, ensure acceptance of waitlisting within 10 days of communication; waitlist retained for 180 days
- [ ] Implement the approved project plan as per milestone descriptors, expenditure, and timeframe unless modified in writing by Central Government
- [ ] Engage with the Project Management Agency (PMA) for monitoring and reporting as required
- [ ] Prepare for mid-term and end-term evaluations by the Scheme Steering Committee (SSC)
- [ ] Submit quarterly and annual progress reports to PMA/SSC as per monitoring framework
- [ ] Ensure compliance with all terms of the funding agreement and share allotment agreement (if applicable)
- [ ] Utilize the financial assistance only for the purposes specified in the project plan for each instalment
- [ ] Retain all records for audit and verification purposes for a minimum of 5 years post-completion
