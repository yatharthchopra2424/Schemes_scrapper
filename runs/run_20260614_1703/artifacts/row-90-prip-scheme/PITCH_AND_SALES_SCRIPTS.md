# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialise in helping pharmaceutical, MedTech, and life sciences innovators access government funding for high-impact R&D. The PRIP Scheme from the Department of Pharmaceuticals is designed specifically for industry-academia collaboration and strategic innovation in public health priority areas. I’d like to understand your current R&D initiatives to see if this aligns with your goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. What stage is your project at—early-stage (TRL 1–3) or later-stage (TRL 4–6)?
2. Are you working on a new medicine, complex generic/biosimilar, or novel medical device?
3. Does your project address a public health priority area such as neglected tropical diseases, rare diseases, antimicrobial resistance, or outbreak/pandemic pathogens?
4. Are you collaborating with or planning to collaborate with a government academic or research institution (e.g., NIPER, IIT, AIIMS)?
5. What is your company’s legal structure—are you a registered company, LLP, or partnership firm (excluding sole proprietorship)?
6. Do you have key managerial personnel with any convictions involving moral turpitude or corruption?
7. Has your company or its holding company filed for insolvency, winding-up, or liquidation proceedings?
8. For early-stage projects, do you hold a valid DPIIT startup certificate or Udyam MSME registration?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Entity registered in India under Companies Act 2013, previous company law, LLP Act 2008, or Partnership Act 1932 (not OPC, unlimited liability, dividend-paying, or object-restricted)
- [ ] For early-stage: Valid DPIIT startup certificate (G.S.R. 127(E) 19.2.2019) OR valid Udyam MSME registration (S.O. 2119(E) 26.6.2020)
- [ ] No key managerial personnel convicted of offence involving moral turpitude or corruption with imprisonment ≥6 months
- [ ] No insolvency, winding-up, liquidation, or resolution proceedings by applicant or holding company
- [ ] Project involves R&D for new medicine, complex generic/biosimilar, or novel medical device
- [ ] Project targets TRL 1–3 (early-stage) or TRL 4–6 (later-stage) in a priority area
- [ ] Willing to collaborate with government academic/research institution for in-licensing IP or joint development
- [ ] Prepared to maintain co-funding proportion at each instalment disbursement stage

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, your project appears to align well with the PRIP Scheme’s focus on strategic innovation and industry-academia collaboration. The scheme offers grant-in-aid of up to ₹5 crore for early-stage projects or up to ₹100 crore for later-stage Strategic Priority Innovations, disbursed in four milestone-linked instalments. Shall I walk you through how it works?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Pharma and MedTech innovators struggle to fund high-risk, high-reward R&D—especially for public health priorities with low market potential—due to limited access to patient capital and the high cost of preclinical and clinical validation.

**Scheme intro:**
The Promotion of Research and Innovation in Pharma MedTech Sector (PRIP) Scheme, administered by the Department of Pharmaceuticals, Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers, Government of India, provides grant-in-aid for Centres of Excellence at NIPERs, Strategic Priority Innovation projects, and industry-academia collaboration for in-licensing of intellectual property. Financial assistance is disbursed in four instalments, with the fourth instalment being at least 15% of the approved total financial assistance on a reimbursement basis.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are developing a [new medicine/complex generic/novel medical device] in a public health priority area  
- You are engaged in or planning industry-academia collaboration for IP in-licensing or joint R&D  
- Your project targets TRL [1–3 or 4–6], meeting eligibility for Early Stage or Later Stage Projects  
- Your entity is a registered company/LLP/partnership firm in India with no disqualifying legal or compliance issues

Applications are evaluated by the Scheme Steering Committee with technical input from CDSCO and academia. | "How much do you charge?" | [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT] |
| "I'll try it myself first" | Common mistakes include missing the academic intent letter or valuation report for IP in-licensing, and failing to maintain the required co-funding proportion before each instalment disbursement. We help avoid these pitfalls. |
| "We're too small / too big" | The scheme explicitly welcomes startups (with DPIIT certificate) and MSMEs (with Udyam registration) for Early Stage Projects. There is no upper turnover cap for eligibility—only co-funding requirements scale with project size. |
| "I don't have the documents ready" | The three most commonly missing documents are: (1) academic intent letter or valid contract with Academia, (2) valuation report of the IP proposed for in-licensing, and (3) documents evidencing financial closure for the approved total project cost. |
