### PRIP Scheme
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department of Pharmaceuticals, Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://pharma-dept.gov.in/schemes
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Window:** The Scheme shall be open for applications during the Application Window. No application shall be accepted after the end of the Application Window.

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Company registered under the Companies Act, 2013 or under any previous company law (excluding one-person companies, companies with unlimited liability, dividend-paying companies, or companies whose objects include promotion of commerce, art, science, sports, education, research, social welfare, religion, charity, or environmental protection, or whose profits may only be applied for promoting said objects), a Limited Liability Partnership registered under the Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008, or a partnership firm other than a sole proprietorship firm registered under the Indian Partnership Act, 1932.
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Pharma MedTech Sector
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: For Early Stage Projects, the applicant must be a startup with a valid certificate from the Inter-Ministerial Board of Certification under DPIIT notification G.S.R. 127(E) dated 19.2.2019, or an MSME with a valid Udyam Registration Certificate under MSME Ministry notification S.O. 2119(E) dated 26.6.2020. Applicants are ineligible if any key managerial personnel is convicted of an offence involving moral turpitude or corruption and sentenced to imprisonment for six months or more, or if the applicant or its holding company has filed for insolvency, winding-up, liquidation, or resolution proceedings.

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Financial assistance is provided as grant-in-aid disbursed in four instalments, with the fourth instalment being at least 15% of the approved total project cost and disbursed on a reimbursement basis. Financial assistance for Centres of Excellence at NIPERs, Strategic Priority Innovation projects, and industry-academia collaboration for in-licensing of intellectual property. The scheme provides grant-in-aid for research and development, infrastructure development, equipment procurement, and other eligible activities.
- Disbursement mode: Reimbursement
- Duration of benefit: One-time (disbursed in four instalments)

8. If required by Central Government: report to validate that the product or technology which the project proposed to take to higher TRLs is at TRL claimed in the application, furnished by an agency from among those specified on the Scheme portal
9. Valid contract executed between the applicant and Academia, or in case such contract has not yet been executed: academic intent letter signed by an officer of Academia duly authorised in this behalf, downloadable from the Scheme portal
10. Valuation report of the IP proposed to be in-licensed from Academia, furnished by an agency from among those specified for this purpose on the Scheme portal based on fair value assessment of such IP, or in case such assessment has not been done: estimate of the value of that IP in the project plan as referred to in paragraph 3.4.5 and in the aforesaid valid contract or academic intent letter

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. The applicant submits the application as per the application form prescribed in Appendix K through the online portal maintained by the Project Management Agency (PMA).
2. The PMA conducts an examination as per the checklist in Appendix I within 15 working days of receipt of the application or any subsequent submission of a revised application if the original was returned as incomplete.
3. If the application is found to be incomplete, the PMA informs the applicant accordingly, and the applicant must complete the incomplete application within 15 days of such communication, failing which the application will be closed.
4. A non-refundable application fee, as mentioned in Appendix C, is payable electronically for each application.
5. All applications are submitted through the online portal maintained by the PMA; if the portal is not available, applications may be submitted in physical form to the PMA.
6. The applicant submits an undertaking in the format given in Appendix E and also an undertaking in the format given in Appendix G, consenting to audit of their manufacturing site/offices for verification of information/data submitted along with the application.
7. As part of the application, the applicant submits a plan based on sequential milestones to be achieved, including associated expenditure and timeframes ('project plan'), with each milestone consisting of objective, measurable technical and non-technical markers ('milestone descriptors') selected from a catalogue accessible on the Scheme portal.
8. The Central Government considers the project plan without modification or with such modifications as to timeframes, project cost, milestone descriptor, or milestone as it may deem fit and accords its in-principle approval to such submitted or modified project plan ('in-principle approved project plan'), or includes it as a waitlisted project, or rejects the same.
9. If in-principle approval is accorded with modifications, the communication conveying the same may specify such modifications or require the applicant to carry out changes in the project plan in line with directions contained in such communication and resubmit the plan.
10. Upon conveying acceptance and submission (to the satisfaction of the Central Government), the project plan or the modified project plan is referred to as the approved project plan.
11. Within a period of 60 days from the date of conveying acceptance and submissions, the applicant submits: (i) documents evidencing financial closure for the approved total project cost, (ii) duly signed funding agreement with the approved project plan annexed thereto, (iii) if the applicant has opted for benefit-share by way of share allotment, duly signed share allotment agreement, and (iv) if the Central Government has required a report to validate that the product or technology which the project proposed to take to higher TRLs is at TRL claimed in the application, such report furnished by an agency from among those specified on the Scheme portal for this purpose.
12. Failure to convey acceptance or make any submission as aforesaid within the specified period (or such extended period or periods as the Central Government may permit) renders the in-principle approval liable to be withdrawn.
13. A waitlisted project is retained in the waitlist for a period of 180 days from the date on which its waitlisting is communicated to the applicant, along with the approved project plan, for acceptance of the waitlisting within a period of 10 days of such communication.
14. Every beneficiary and waitlisted applicant is required to implement the approved project plan as per the milestone descriptors and associated expenditure and timeframe specified therein, in accordance with the provisions of the Scheme and these guidelines, unless the Central Government permits modification of the same in writing.
15. In case the Central Government permits any modification, the reference to 'approved project plan' is construed as a reference to the modified project plan.
16. In case of the share allotment option for benefit-share, an applicant company may either allot its own shares or the shares of a company incorporated by it as a dedicated, special purpose vehicle for purposes of implementation of the project and fulfilment of all obligations of the applicant/beneficiary under the Scheme and these guidelines.
17. Disbursement of milestone-linked financial assistance is done in the following manner: (a) Number of instalments: Disbursement of financial assistance is made in four instalments. The fourth instalment will be at least 15% of the approved total financial assistance and will be disbursed on a reimbursement basis. (b) Co-funding: Total co-funding will be required to the extent specified in the Scheme and would equal the approved total project cost less the approved total financial assistance, and the proportion of total co-funding to the approved total financial assistance is required to be maintained at the stage of disbursement of each instalment. Accordingly, the beneficiary is required to ensure that cumulative co-funding in said proportion to the sum of such instalment and financial assistance previously disbursed is credited into the project account before such disbursement.
