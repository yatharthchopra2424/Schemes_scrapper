# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Scheme Overview
The **PRIP Scheme** (Scheme for Promotion of Research and Innovation in Pharma MedTech Sector) is a **grant**-type initiative implemented by the **Department of Pharmaceuticals, Ministry of Chemicals and Fertilizers, Government of India**. It operates with a **Pan-India** geographic scope and targets **industry, academia, startups, and MSMEs**. The scheme was last updated in **2025** and remains open for applications during the designated **Application Window**, with no submissions accepted after its closure. The official application portal is: **https://pharma-dept.gov.in/schemes**.

### Objectives
The PRIP Scheme aims to:
- Establish **Centres of Excellence (CoEs)** at NIPERs to build specific research capacities in identified priority areas through industry collaboration.
- Support **Strategic Priority Innovation (SPI)** projects addressing India’s public health concerns with relatively lower market potential (e.g., neglected tropical diseases, rare diseases, antimicrobial resistance, outbreak/pandemic pathogens, vaccine-preventable diseases).
- Facilitate **industry-academia collaboration** for in-licensing of intellectual property from reputed government academic and research institutions to develop, translate, and commercialize institutional IP.
- Strengthen industry-academia linkage and promote joint R&D activities.
- Ensure prudent fiscal management and effective resource utilization aligned with scheme objectives.
- Monitor implementation of approved proposals and research programmes via the Steering Committee.
- Consider and decide requests for modification in research programmes and deliverables for Centres of Excellence.
- Review implementation of approved proposals and associated deliverables finalised by the committee.

### Benefits & Financial Support
Financial assistance is provided as **grant-in-aid** disbursed in **four instalments**, with the **fourth instalment being at least 15% of the approved total project cost** and disbursed on a **reimbursement basis**.

#### Disbursement Structure
| **Instalment** | **Disbursement Trigger** | **Utilisation Purpose** | **Co-funding Requirement** |
|----------------|--------------------------|-------------------------|----------------------------|
| **Instalment 1** | Evidencing financial closure for approved total project cost; Execution of funding agreement | Project initiation, land/building procurement (if applicable), initial setup | Co-funding must be credited into project account before disbursement; proportion of total co-funding to approved total financial assistance must be maintained at each stage |
| **Instalment 2** | Achievement of Milestone 1 (as per project plan) | Equipment procurement, civil works, initial R&D activities | Same as above |
| **Instalment 3** | Achievement of Milestone 2 (as per project plan) | Continued R&D, prototype development, testing | Same as above |
| **Instalment 4** | Achievement of Milestone 3 (as per project plan); **at least 15% of approved total financial assistance** | Final validation, commercialisation activities, reimbursement of eligible expenses | Same as above; disbursed on reimbursement basis |

#### Key Financial Rules
- **Total co-funding** = Approved total project cost – Approved total financial assistance.
- The **proportion of total co-funding to approved total financial assistance** must be maintained at **each instalment disbursement stage**.
- Beneficiary must ensure **cumulative co-funding in the required proportion** is credited into the project account **before each disbursement**.
- Financial assistance is disbursed into a **project-specific dedicated account** in a Scheduled Commercial Bank, subject to inspection and special audit (including forensic audit) by the Central Government.
- The Central Government may consider the project plan **with or without modifications** and accord **in-principle approval**.
- Upon acceptance, the applicant must submit documents evidencing financial closure within **60 days**, execute a duly signed funding agreement (with approved project plan annexed), and, if opting for benefit-share via share allotment, execute a duly signed share allotment agreement.
- **Co-funding is required** to the extent that total co-funding equals the approved total project cost less the approved total financial assistance.

#### Benefit-Sharing Mechanisms
The scheme provides for benefit-sharing mechanisms such as:
- **Share allotment**: Allotment of shares to the Central Government of aggregate value equal to the total financial assistance disbursed (reckoned on fair value assessment basis).
- **Revenue sharing**: Fixed or tiered rate payouts based on net sales from commercialised output.
  - *Early Stage Projects*: 
    - Fixed rate: 5% of net sales/year (discharged when payments = total financial assistance disbursed).
    - Tiered rate: 2% (years 1–3), 4% (years 4–6), 6% (year 7+).
  - *Later Stage Projects*:
    - Fixed rate: 10% of net sales/year (discharged when payments = 150% of total financial assistance disbursed).
    - Tiered rate: 4% (years 1–3), 8% (years 4–6), 12% (year 7+).
- For projects involving collaboration with Academia, the independently assessed fair value of transferred capital assets is **netted out** from the total financial assistance disbursed for benefit-share computation.

### Application Process
The application process is strictly governed and involves multiple stages with defined timelines.

#### Step-by-Step Process
1. **Submission**: Applicant submits application via the online portal maintained by the Project Management Agency (PMA) as per the form prescribed in **Appendix K**. If the portal is unavailable, physical submission to PMA is allowed.
2. **PMA Examination**: PMA conducts an examination as per the checklist in **Appendix I** within **15 working days** of receipt of the application or any subsequent submission of a revised application (if original was returned as incomplete).
3. **Incomplete Application Handling**: If incomplete, PMA informs the applicant, who must complete it within **15 days** of such communication. Failure to do so results in application closure.
4. **Application Fee**: A **non-refundable application fee** (as mentioned in **Appendix C**) is payable electronically for each application.
5. **Undertakings**: Applicant submits:
   - Undertaking in format given in **Appendix E**.
   - Undertaking in format given in **Appendix G** (consenting to audit of manufacturing site/offices).
6. **Project Plan Submission**: As part of the application, applicant submits a plan based on **sequential milestones** to be achieved, including associated expenditure and timeframes ('project plan'), with each milestone consisting of objective, measurable technical and non-technical markers ('milestone descriptors') selected from a catalogue accessible on the Scheme portal.
7. **In-Principle Approval**: Central Government considers the project plan (without modification or with modifications to timeframes, project cost, milestone descriptor, or milestone) and accords **in-principle approval** to the submitted or modified project plan ('in-principle approved project plan'), includes it as a waitlisted project, or rejects it.
8. **Modification Handling**: If in-principle approval is accorded with modifications, the communication may specify such modifications or require the applicant to carry out changes in the project plan in line with directions and resubmit the plan.
9. **Acceptance & Submission**: Upon conveying acceptance and submission (to the satisfaction of the Central Government), the project plan (or modified project plan) is referred to as the **approved project plan**.
10. **Post-Approval Submission (within 60 days)**: Applicant must submit:
    - Documents evidencing financial closure for the approved total project cost.
    - Duly signed funding agreement with the approved project plan annexed thereto.
    - If opting for benefit-share via share allotment: duly signed share allotment agreement.
    - If required by Central Government: report validating that the product/technology proposed to be taken to higher TRLs is at TRL claimed in the application, furnished by an agency from those specified on the Scheme portal.
11. **Failure to Comply**: Failure to convey acceptance or make any submission within the specified period (or extended period permitted by Central Government) renders the in-principle approval liable to be withdrawn.
12. **Waitlisted Projects**: Retained in the waitlist for **180 days** from the date waitlisting is communicated to the applicant, along with the approved project plan, for acceptance of the waitlisting within **10 days** of such communication.
13. **Implementation**: Every beneficiary and waitlisted applicant must implement the approved project plan as per the milestone descriptors and associated expenditure and timeframe specified therein, in accordance with the Scheme and guidelines, unless the Central Government permits modification in writing.
14. **Modification During Implementation**: If Central Government permits modification, the reference to 'approved project plan' is construed as a reference to the **modified project plan**.
15. **Share Allotment Option**: For benefit-share via share allotment, an applicant company may either allot its own shares or the shares of a company incorporated by it as a dedicated, special purpose vehicle (SPV) for implementation of the project and fulfilment of all obligations under the Scheme.
16. **Disbursement of Milestone-Linked Financial Assistance**: Done in four instalments as per milestone achievement, with the fourth instalment being at least 15% of the approved total financial assistance on a reimbursement basis.

### Key Caveats & Critical Notes
> **WARNING**: Total financial assistance to applicants belonging to a related group will not exceed **5% of the total financial outlay of the Scheme**, unless the Central Government decides otherwise. Applicants must disclose all companies, LLPs, and registered partnership firms belonging to their related group in the application, regardless of whether such entities are applicants under the Scheme.

> **WARNING**: The Central Government reserves the right to amend the Scheme and these guidelines from time to time. All expressions used in the guidelines have the same meaning as in the Scheme.

> **WARNING**: Financial assistance will be disbursed into a **project-specific dedicated account** opened by the applicant in a Scheduled Commercial Bank **prior to execution of the funding agreement**. Such account will be subject to inspection and special audit, including **forensic audit**, by the Central Government.

> **WARNING**: The Central Government will have the right to call for more information in respect of financial closure and take assistance from any instrumentality of the State or from parties from whom co-funding for financial closure is to be sourced, for the purpose of verifying any documents submitted to satisfy itself as to the veracity of the financial closure claimed.

> **WARNING**: A waitlisted project will be retained in the waitlist for a period of **180 days** from the date on which its waitlisting is communicated to the applicant, along with the approved project plan, for acceptance of the waitlisting within a period of **10 days** of such communication.

> **WARNING**: Every beneficiary and waitlisted applicant will be required to implement the approved project plan as per the milestone descriptors and associated expenditure and timeframe specified therein, in accordance with the provisions of the Scheme and these guidelines, unless the Central Government permits modification of the same in writing.
