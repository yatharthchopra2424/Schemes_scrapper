# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I’m calling from [Your Firm Name], and we specialize in helping poor and vulnerable families access government healthcare schemes like Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY. This scheme provides free cashless treatment up to ₹5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization. I’d like to understand your family’s situation to see if you might qualify for this benefit—no obligation, just a quick chat.

### Discovery Questions
1. Are you currently residing in a rural or urban area of India?
2. What is your family’s primary source of income? (e.g., manual casual labour, domestic work, street vending, construction, ragpicking, etc.)
3. Do you or any family member belong to an SC/ST household?
4. Is your household headed by a female with no adult male member aged 16–59?
5. Does your household have only one room and no adult member aged 16–59?
6. Are there any disabled members in your family with no able-bodied adult to support them?
7. Do you possess a ration card or have you been identified in the Socio Economic Caste Census (SECC) database?
8. Have you or any family member previously received an Ayushman Card or availed cashless treatment under PM-JAY?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Family identified as poor/vulnerable via SECC database (rural/urban deprivation/occupational criteria)
- [ ] Household meets at least one eligibility criterion: one-room dwelling, no adult aged 16–59, female-headed with no adult male, disabled members with no able-bodied adult, SC/ST, landless with manual casual labour income
- [ ] Urban occupational category: ragpicker, beggar, domestic worker, street vendor, construction worker, etc.
- [ ] Family size and age: no cap (all members covered)
- [ ] Resident of India (scheme is pan-India)
- [ ] Not currently covered under another similar government health insurance scheme (optional check)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your family may meet the eligibility criteria for Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY. This scheme provides ₹5 lakhs per family per year for cashless secondary and tertiary hospitalization at any empanelled hospital across India. Would you like me to explain how it works and how we can help you apply?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Poor and vulnerable families often face catastrophic health expenses due to hospitalization for serious illnesses like cancer, orthopaedic issues, or urology conditions. Without financial protection, they risk falling into poverty or delaying critical treatment due to cost concerns—especially for secondary and tertiary care that requires advanced medical intervention.

**Scheme intro:**
Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB PM-JAY), administered by the National Health Authority (NHA), is a centrally funded health assurance scheme providing a health cover of ₹5 lakhs per family per year on a family floater basis for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization. The scheme is active across 36 States/UTs, including West Bengal as the latest addition, with over 44.2 million Ayushman Cards issued and ₹1.50 lakh crore authorized for hospital admissions as of June 2026.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your family is identified through SECC data based on deprivation and occupational criteria (e.g., manual casual labour, ragpicking, domestic work, etc.)
- You reside in a household meeting vulnerability indicators such as female-headed with no adult male, or having disabled members without able-bodied support
- There is no cap on family size or age—all members are covered under the same ₹5 lakh annual cover
- The scheme covers pre-existing diseases from day one and includes 1,949 procedures across 27 specialties, including medical oncology and orthopaedics
