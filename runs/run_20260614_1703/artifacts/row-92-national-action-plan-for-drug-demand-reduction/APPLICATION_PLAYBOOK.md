# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Organisation is an NGO, Trust, or Charitable institution
- [ ] Organisation has been registered for at least two years
- [ ] Organisation has a minimum of two years' experience in the social sector at the time of applying
- [ ] Organisation has obtained a NGO Unique ID from the NGO-DARPAN portal (https://ngodarpan.gov.in)
- [ ] Organisation has submitted PAN during registration on the e-Anudaan portal
- [ ] For IPSrC/AVYAY sub-scheme under NAPDDR: Project must be proposed in designated Gap districts as per the tentative list annexed in the guidelines (refer to Annexure in Notice No. AG-15039/37/2021-Sr.C-I dated 01st July, 2024)
- [ ] Organisation is applying exclusively through the e-Anudaan portal (https://grants-msje.gov.in/ngo-login)
- [ ] Project proposal conforms to the prescribed proforma and scheme guidelines (AVYAY scheme guideline uploaded on Ministry's website and e-Anudaan portal)
- [ ] For DDRS scheme from FY 2023-24: UDID number or UDID enrollment ID with disability certificate is mandatory (if applicable to the specific sub-scheme being applied for)

> **Note**: Eligibility varies by sub-scheme (e.g., DDAC, Garima Greh, Senior Citizen Homes, Continuous Care Homes, RRTC). Always verify the specific notice for the target sub-scheme.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation / Registration  
- [ ] PAN of the organisation  
- [ ] Bank account details (cancelled cheque or bank statement)  
- [ ] Financial statements and audit reports (for last 2–3 years)  
- [ ] Udyam Registration (if applicable, though not explicitly mandatory for this scheme; NGO-DARPAN is primary)  
- [ ] Authorization letter from authorized signatory  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] NGO Unique ID from NGO-DARPAN portal (mandatory pre-requisite)  
- [ ] Project proposal in prescribed proforma (specific to sub-scheme: e.g., SrCH-25, SrCH-50, CCH, DDAC, Garima Greh, RRTC)  
- [ ] For DDRS scheme from FY 2023-24: UDID number or UDID enrollment ID with disability certificate  
- [ ] Inspection report (where applicable, e.g., for ongoing projects or post-installment release)  
- [ ] Details of beneficiaries (for ongoing projects)  
- [ ] For RRTC applications: Evidence of infrastructure, training capacity, outreach, stakeholder management, financial capacity, qualified staff, and experience in social and senior citizen sectors (as per eligibility matrix in 85831719577891.pdf)  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible scans (PDF format preferred)  
- [ ] File size does not exceed portal limits (typically 5–10 MB per file; verify on e-Anudaan portal)  
- [ ] Document names match the content (e.g., "PAN_Card.pdf", "Registration_Certificate.pdf")  
- [ ] Where required, documents are self-attested or certified by a gazetted officer/notary  
- [ ] All text is in English or Hindi; if in another language, provide a certified translation  
- [ ] Financial documents are audited and signed by a Chartered Accountant  
- [ ] Project proposal follows the prescribed format and includes all sections as per scheme guidelines

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a **Project Proposal in prescribed proforma** is required (referred to as DPR/Project Report in scheme documents).  
**Standard Sections to Include**:  
- Executive Summary  
- Organisation Background and Experience  
- Project Rationale and Needs Assessment (aligned with NAPDDR objectives: preventive education, counseling, treatment, rehabilitation, social reintegration, skill development under PM-DAKSH)  
- Project Description (activities, methodology, timeline)  
- Beneficiary Details (target group, number, selection criteria)  
- Infrastructure and Resources (facility details, equipment, geo-tagging plan)  
- Staffing Plan (qualifications and roles)  
- Budget Breakdown (recurring and non-recurring expenses, aligned with cost norms in AVYAY scheme guideline)  
- Sustainability Plan  
- Monitoring and Evaluation Framework  
- Annexures (mandatory documents: NGO Unique ID, PAN, registration certificate, financial statements, etc.)  

> **Note**: The exact proforma varies by sub-scheme (e.g., SrCH-25, DDAC, Garima Greh, RRTC). Download the latest proforma from the e-Anudaan portal under the relevant scheme notice.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Register on NGO-DARPAN first** (https://ngodarpan.gov.in) to obtain NGO Unique ID — this is mandatory before accessing e-Anudaan.  
2. **Access e-Anudaan portal** at https://grants-msje.gov.in/ngo-login and log in using credentials. Ensure NGO Unique ID and PAN are submitted during registration.  
3. **Navigate to the correct scheme**: Go to 'Notice Inviting Applications' and select the relevant NAPDDR sub-scheme (e.g., NAPDDR-DDAC, AVYAY/IPSrC, SMILE-Garima Greh).  
4. **Fill all mandatory fields** — do not leave any asterisk-marked (*) fields blank.  
5. **Upload documents in the correct order and format** as specified in the portal’s checklist (e.g., NGO Unique ID first, then PAN, then project proposal).  
6. **Geo-tagging requirement**: Ensure the project facility’s geo-tagging details are included in the proposal or uploaded as a separate document if required (ministry-supported institutions are geo-tagged for accessibility).  
7. **Save as draft frequently** to avoid data loss during portal maintenance or connectivity issues.  
8. **Review the application preview** before final submission to verify all attachments and entries.  
9. **Submit only before the deadline** — late submissions are not accepted; portal access may be restricted during maintenance (e.g., scheduled maintenance from 09:00 am on 10th May 2025 to 11:30 pm on 12th May 2025, as per portal notice).  
10. **After submission, download and save the acknowledgment receipt** with the application reference number.

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a **screenshot of the submission confirmation page** showing the application reference number, date, and time stamp  
- **Store the reference number securely** in the case file and communicate it to the client  
- **Set follow-up reminders**:  
  - Initial verification status check: 7 days after submission  
  - Inspection scheduling follow-up: 15–20 days after submission  
  - Financial approval inquiry: 30–45 days after submission  
- Monitor the e-Anudaan portal regularly for status updates (application verification, checklist filling, inspection report scrutiny, screening, recommendation, financial approval, GIA sanction)

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and save the **approval letter** from the e-Anudaan portal or via email  
- [ ] Verify the **sanctioned amount** and **installment schedule** (e.g., 1st, 2nd, 3rd/final) as per project progress and document submission  
- [ ] Prepare for **utilization certificate** submission after utilization of each installment (required for next tranche release)  
- [ ] Ensure **geo-tagging of the facility** is completed and updated on the portal (ministry-supported institutions are geo-tagged for accessibility)  
- [ ] Coordinate with the client for **success fee invoicing** as per the retainer agreement  
- [ ] Monitor compliance with scheme guidelines (e.g., beneficiary tracking, training under PM-DAKSH, inspection readiness)  
- [ ] Prepare for **post-sanction inspection** by district, state, or central authorities  
- [ ] Maintain records of all communications, submissions, and approvals for audit purposes
