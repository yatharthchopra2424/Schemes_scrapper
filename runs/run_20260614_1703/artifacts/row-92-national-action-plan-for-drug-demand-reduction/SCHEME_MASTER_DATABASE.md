### National Action Plan for Drug Demand Reduction
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department of Social Justice and Empowerment, Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://grants-msje.gov.in/ngo-login
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Varies by scheme and notice; deadlines specified in individual notices (e.g., IPSrC/AVYAY applications accepted from 01st July 2024 till 15th August 2024; RRTC applications from 01st July 2024 till 31st July 2024)

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: NGOs, Trusts, and Charitable institutions
- Age of business: Registered for at least two years
- Sector: Social sector (minimum two years' experience in the social sector at the time of applying)
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Must register on the NGO-DARPAN portal of NITI Aayog to obtain a NGO Unique ID before applying; for specific sub-schemes like IPSrC under AVYAY, applicants must propose projects in designated Gap districts; applications must be submitted exclusively through the e-Anudaan portal

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Financial assistance as Grant-in-Aid (GIA) for recurring and non-recurring expenses related to establishment and operation of projects (e.g., DDACs, Garima Greh, Senior Citizen Homes, Continuous Care Homes); quantum of assistance detailed in respective scheme guidelines
- Disbursement mode: Installments (e.g., 1st, 2nd, 3rd/final) based on project progress and submission of required documents and inspection reports
- Duration of benefit: One-time grant per project approval, disbursed in installments over project duration

#### Documents Required
1. NGO Unique ID from NGO-DARPAN portal
2. PAN of the organisation
3. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
4. Project proposal in prescribed proforma
5. UDID number or UDID enrollment ID with disability certificate (for DDRS scheme from FY 2023-24)
6. Inspection report (where applicable)
7. Financial statements and audit reports
8. Bank account details
9. Authorization letter from authorized signatory
10. Details of beneficiaries (for ongoing projects)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Register on the NGO-DARPAN portal (https://ngodarpan.gov.in) to obtain a NGO Unique ID
2. Access the e-Anudaan portal at https://grants-msje.gov.in/ngo-login
3. Log in using credentials and ensure NGO Unique ID and PAN are submitted during registration
4. Navigate to the relevant scheme (e.g., NAPDDR, DDAC, Garima Greh, AVYAY/IPSrC) under 'Notice Inviting Applications'
5. Fill in the application form with project details
6. Upload prescribed documents including NGO Unique ID, PAN, project proposal, and other scheme-specific documents
7. Submit the application before the deadline
8. Await verification, inspection, and approval by district, state, and central authorities
9. Upon approval, Grant-in-Aid will be sanctioned and disbursed in installments
