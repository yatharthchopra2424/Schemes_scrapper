# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The **National Action Plan for Drug Demand Reduction (NAPDDR)** is a centrally sponsored grant scheme implemented by the **Department of Social Justice and Empowerment**, Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment, Government of India. It operates on a **Pan-India** geographic scope and provides **Grant-in-Aid (GIA)** to eligible NGOs for establishing and operating projects focused on substance abuse prevention, treatment, rehabilitation, and social reintegration. The scheme also extends support to senior citizen homes, transgender shelters (Garima Greh), and continuous care homes under its sub-schemes like AVYAY/IPSrC and SMILE.

### Objectives
The scheme aims to achieve the following objectives:
- Preventive education and awareness generation against alcoholism and substance (drugs) abuse
- Counseling, treatment, and rehabilitation of drug-dependent persons
- Social reintegration of recovered individuals
- Capacity building and skill development of beneficiaries under the PM-DAKSH programme
- Support for setting up and running District De-Addiction Centres (DDACs)
- Support for Garima Greh (shelters for transgender persons) under the SMILE scheme
- Support for Senior Citizen Homes and Continuous Care Homes under AVYAY/IPSrC
- Geo-tagging of ministry-supported institutions for better service accessibility

### Eligibility Matrix
Eligibility is determined based on organisational registration, experience, and compliance with portal requirements. The following table summarises the key eligibility criteria:

| **Eligibility Criteria** | **Details** | **Source** |
|--------------------------|-----------|------------|
| **Organisational Type** | NGOs, Trusts, and Charitable institutions | Key Facts |
| **Registration Duration** | Minimum of two years' registration at the time of applying | Key Facts, PDF: 14471719837115.pdf (Point 3) |
| **Sector Experience** | At least two years' experience in the social sector at the time of applying | Key Facts, PDF: 14471719837115.pdf (Point 3) |
| **NGO-DARPAN Registration** | Must register on NGO-DARPAN portal (https://ngodarpan.gov.in) to obtain a NGO Unique ID before applying on e-Anudaan | Key Facts, Application Process |
| **PAN Requirement** | Permanent Account Number (PAN) of the organisation is mandatory | Key Facts, Required Documents |
| **Project Proposal** | Must be submitted in the prescribed proforma as per scheme guidelines | Key Facts, Application Process, PDF: 14471719837115.pdf (Point 3) |
| **Geographical Restriction (for specific sub-schemes)** | For IPSrC/AVYAY: Projects must be proposed in designated **Gap districts** as per the tentative list annexed in the guidelines (e.g., Annexure in PDF: 14471719837115.pdf) | Key Facts, PDF: 14471719837115.pdf (Point 1, Annexure) |
| **Exclusions (for RRTCs under AVYAY/IPSrC)** | Organisations from Delhi, Himachal Pradesh, Karnataka, Maharashtra, Manipur, Mizoram, Odisha, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, and West Bengal are **not eligible** to apply for RRTCs | PDF: 85831719577891.pdf (Point 2) |
| **UDID Requirement (for DDRS from FY 2023-24)** | UDID number or UDID enrollment ID with disability certificate is mandatory; no grants will be released without it | Key Facts, Key Caveats, Crawled Page: https://grants-msje.gov.in (text: "from FY 2023-24 UDID number / UDID enrollment number with disability certificate is mandatory for availing Grant-in-Aid under DDRS") |
| **Exclusive Portal Use** | Applications must be submitted **exclusively** through the e-Anudaan portal (https://grants-msje.gov.in/ngo-login); no other means shall be entertained | Key Facts, Key Caveats, Application Process, PDF: 14471719837115.pdf (Point 4) |

> **> Warning:** Failure to meet any eligibility criterion — especially NGO-DARPAN registration, UDID for DDRS (post-FY 2023-24), or geographical eligibility for Gap district schemes — will result in automatic rejection. Always verify the latest notice on the e-Anudaan portal for sub-scheme-specific restrictions.

### Benefits & Financial Support
Financial assistance is provided as **Grant-in-Aid (GIA)** for both recurring and non-recurring expenses. The quantum, cost norms, and disbursement conditions are detailed in the respective scheme guidelines (e.g., AVYAY scheme guideline) available on the Ministry’s website and e-Anudaan portal.

| **Benefit Type** | **Details** | **Source** |
|------------------|-----------|------------|
| **Financial Support** | Grant-in-Aid (GIA) for establishment and operation of projects including DDACs, Garima Greh, Senior Citizen Homes, and Continuous Care Homes | Key Facts |
| **Cost Norms** | Detailed in scheme guidelines; e.g., for SrCH-25, SrCH-50, and CCH projects under IPSrC/AVYAY (refer to pages 45–47 of AVYAY scheme guideline) | PDF: 14471719837115.pdf (Point 2) |
| **Disbursement Mode** | Released in installments (e.g., 1st, 2nd, 3rd/final) based on project progress, submission of required documents, and inspection reports | Key Facts |
| **Non-Financial Benefits** | Technical support, guidance on scheme guidelines, access to training under PM-DAKSH for beneficiaries, geo-tagging of facilities, and facilitation of monitoring/inspection via e-Anudaan portal | Key Facts |
| **Portal Automation** | e-Anudaan automates application verification, checklist filling, inspection report scrutiny, screening, recommendation, financial approval, and GIA sanction | Key Facts, Crawled Page: https://grants-msje.gov.in |
| **Maintenance-Related Guidance** | NGOs are advised to submit applications for 2nd and 3rd installments for FY 2024-25 and 2025-26 to obtain remaining 20% grant for FY 2024-25 and 40% as second installment for FY 2025-26 | Crawled Page: https://grants-msje.gov.in (Maintenance Activity Notice) |

> **> Key Takeaway:** The scheme does not fix a uniform grant amount; instead, it follows **project-specific cost norms** defined in the respective guidelines. Consultants must refer to the latest AVYAY, NAPDDR, or SMILE scheme guideline on the Ministry’s website to determine exact financial eligibility and quantum.

### Timelines & Deadlines
Deadlines are **notice-specific** and vary by sub-scheme and project type. Examples from the evidence include:
- **IPSrC/AVYAY (New Projects in Gap Districts):** Applications accepted from **01st July 2024 to 15th August 2024** (PDF: 14471719837115.pdf, Point 6)
- **RRTCs under AVYAY/IPSrC:** Applications accepted from **01st July 2024 to 31st July 2024** (PDF: 85831719577891.pdf, Point 3)
- **Ongoing Projects (1st Installment for FY 2024-25):** Deadline extended to **15th July 2024** (PDF: 33281719834645.pdf)
- **DDAC Setup:** Last date for submission extended to **30th September 2025** (Crawled Page: https://grants-msje.gov.in, Notice: "Extension of last date for setting up DDAC on e-Anudaan portal till 30.09.2025")

> **> Reminder:** There is **no universal deadline**. Applicants **must** check the latest notice on the e-Anudaan portal (https://grants-msje.gov.in/ngo-login) under "Notice Inviting Applications" for exact dates.

### Key Caveats
- Applications must be submitted **exclusively** through the e-Anudaan portal; no other means accepted.
- For DDRS scheme from FY 2023-24, **UDID number or UDID enrollment ID with disability certificate is mandatory**; no grants released without it.
- NGOs must first register on **NGO-DARPAN** and obtain a **NGO Unique ID** before applying on e-Anudaan.
- The Ministry reserves the right to **cancel notices, invite afresh proposals, or take final decisions** without assigning any reason.
- Project proposals must conform to the **prescribed proforma and scheme guidelines**.
- Some schemes are **restricted to specific districts** (e.g., Gap districts for AVYAY/IPSrC).
- During portal maintenance, the system is **inaccessible**; plan submissions accordingly.
