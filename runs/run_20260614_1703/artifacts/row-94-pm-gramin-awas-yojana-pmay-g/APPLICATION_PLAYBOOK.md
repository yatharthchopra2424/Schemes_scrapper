# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Beneficiary is a houseless household (no pucca house owned anywhere in India)
- [ ] Household lives in a kutcha house (zero, one, or two rooms) with walls and roof made of grass, thatch, bamboo, wood, mud, or unburnt brick
- [ ] Household is identified in Socio Economic and Caste Census (SECC) 2011 data
- [ ] Household falls under priority categories: SCs/STs, freed bonded labourers, non-tribal forest dwellers, manual scavengers, legally released bonded labourers, or persons with disabilities
- [ ] Household is located in a rural area (as per Census definition)
- [ ] Household has not previously received assistance under PMAY-G or any other rural housing scheme for pucca house construction
- [ ] Beneficiary consents to house ownership in the name of a woman or joint ownership (to promote women's empowerment)
- [ ] Household agrees to construct a new pucca house (upgradation of existing kutcha house is not permitted)
- [ ] Household agrees to geo-tag construction progress at different stages for instalment verification
- [ ] Household has or will open a bank/post office account linked with Aadhaar for Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT)

> **Note**: Final eligibility is confirmed through Gram Sabha verification and SECC 2011 data. Consultants must verify SECC inclusion and Gram Sabha resolution.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Aadhaar card of the beneficiary
- PAN card (if applicable for financial transactions)
- Latest bank statement (showing account linked to Aadhaar)
- Udyam Registration (if applicable for any allied income activity)
- Income Tax Return (ITR) - if applicable (though many beneficiaries may be non-filers)
- Voter ID or other government-issued identity proof
- Domicile certificate (to confirm rural residence)
- Caste certificate (if claiming SC/ST or other priority category benefits)
- Disability certificate (if applicable)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Consent form signed by the beneficiary (as per PMAY-G guidelines)
- Geo-tagged photographs at different construction stages (foundation, lintel, roof, completion) – to be collected during construction, but initial intent and plan must be documented
- SECC 2011 verification extract (obtained from Gram Sabha or block office)
- Gram Sabha resolution approving the beneficiary list
- Household survey form (as per SECC 2011 parameters)
- Bank/post office passbook or certificate showing Aadhaar-linking and account details
- Undertaking that the beneficiary does not own a pucca house anywhere in India

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF or clear JPEG/PNG format
- File size per document: maximum 2 MB
- File names must follow format: `CaseID_DocumentType_BeneficiaryName.pdf` (e.g., `CASE001_Aadhaar_RameshKumar.pdf`)
- Aadhaar number must be visible but can be masked except for last 4 digits for privacy (as per UIDAI guidelines)
- Bank account details must clearly show account holder name, account number, IFSC, and Aadhaar-linking status
- Consent form must be signed or thumb-impressed by the beneficiary and witnessed by Gram Panchayat official
- Geo-tagged photos must include latitude, longitude, timestamp, and be clearly linked to construction stage
- All certificates (caste, disability, domicile) must be issued by competent authority and valid (typically within 6 months unless permanent)
- Documents must be in Hindi or English; vernacular documents must be accompanied by certified translation

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not required. PMAY-G does not require a Detailed Project Report (DPR) for individual beneficiary houses. However, beneficiaries must select a house design from the rural housing technology menu available at the Gram Panchayat or block office.  
**Action**: Verify with nodal officer if any local variation requires a simple construction plan (not a formal DPR).

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Use only the official PMAY-G portal**: https://pmayg.nic.in/ – do not use third-party sites.
2. **Beneficiary identification must be pre-verified** via SECC 2011 and Gram Sabha – the form will auto-populate or require SECC code.
3. **Enter Aadhaar number correctly** and ensure it is linked to the bank/post office account for DBT.
4. **Select correct geographical zone** (plain area, hilly state, difficult area, or IAP district) to determine correct assistance amount (1.20 lakh or 1.30 lakh).
5. **Upload all scheme-specific documents** in the prescribed format and size.
6. **Mark consent explicitly** – the form requires digital confirmation of beneficiary consent for woman/joint ownership and new construction.
7. **Declare no ownership of pucca house** anywhere in India – this is a mandatory self-declaration.
8. **Save draft before final submission** and generate acknowledgment – no edits allowed after submission.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] Beneficiary name matches across Aadhaar, bank account, and consent form
- [ ] Aadhaar is linked to the bank/post office account (verify via NPCI mapper or bank certificate)
- [ ] Consent form is signed and includes clause for woman/joint ownership
- [ ] Geo-tagged photo plan is documented (even if photos to be taken later)
- [ ] SECC 2011 inclusion verified and Gram Sabha resolution attached
- [ ] Declaration of no pucca house ownership signed
- [ ] Correct zone selected (plain/hilly/difficult/IAP) for assistance amount
- [ ] All documents uploaded are legible and within size limits
- [ ] Application saved as draft and reviewed before final submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a screenshot of the final submission confirmation page showing Application ID and timestamp
- Save the Application ID in the case file and set up automated reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days
- Download and store the acknowledgment receipt from the portal
- Notify the beneficiary via SMS/WhatsApp with the Application ID and next steps (awaiting first instalment after foundation geo-tag)

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify approval letter/sanction order from PMAY-G portal or DRDA office  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned amount (INR 1.20 lakh for plain areas, INR 1.30 lakh for hilly/difficult/IAP areas)  
- [ ] Verify first instalment has been credited to the beneficiary’s Aadhaar-linked bank/post office account via DBT  
- [ ] Guide beneficiary to take geo-tagged photograph at foundation level for second instalment release  
- [ ] Monitor construction progress and ensure timely submission of geo-tagged photos at lintel and roof levels  
- [ ] Ensure convergence benefits (toilet under SBM-G, electricity under Saubhagya, LPG under Ujjwala, water under JJM) are pursued in parallel  
- [ ] Assist in obtaining completion certificate from Gram Panchayat after final geo-tagged verification  
- [ ] Track utilization and ensure funds are used only for new pucca house construction  
- [ ] Prepare and submit utilization certificate (if required by DRDA)  
- [ ] Invoice success fee as per consultancy agreement (to be filled by consultant)  

> **Note**: The scheme targets completion by 2024. Monitor for any extension announcements via https://pmayg.nic.in/
