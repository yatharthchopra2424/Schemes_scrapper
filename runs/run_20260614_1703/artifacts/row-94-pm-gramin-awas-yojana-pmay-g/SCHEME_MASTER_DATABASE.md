### PM Gramin Awas Yojana (PMAY-G)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Ministry of Rural Development, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://pmayg.nic.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open (beneficiary identification and sanctioning done periodically based on fund availability and SECC data)

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Not applicable (individual/household beneficiary scheme)
- Age of business: Not applicable
- Sector: Rural housing
- Revenue cap: Not applicable (based on socio-economic criteria, not revenue)
- Employee count: Not applicable
- Other: 
  - Beneficiaries identified based on housing deprivation parameters and socio-economic criteria as per Socio Economic and Caste Census (SECC) 2011
  - Covers houseless households and households living in zero, one, or two-room kutcha houses (walls and roof made of grass, thatch, bamboo, wood, mud, unburnt brick, etc.)
  - Priority given to SCs/STs, freed bonded labourers, non-tribal forest dwellers, manual scavengers, legally released bonded labourers, and persons with disabilities
  - Must not own a pucca house anywhere in India

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: 
  - INR 1.20 lakh in plain areas
  - INR 1.30 lakh in hilly states, difficult areas, and IAP (Integrated Action Plan) districts
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit (via Direct Benefit Transfer - DBT to beneficiary's bank/Post Office account linked with Aadhaar)
- Duration of benefit: One-time (provided in three instalments based on construction progress)

#### Documents Required
1. Aadhaar number
2. Bank/Post Office account details linked with Aadhaar
3. Consent form from the beneficiary
4. Geo-tagged photographs at different construction stages

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Beneficiary identification is done through SECC 2011 data and Gram Sabha verification
2. Eligible beneficiaries are intimated and their consent is obtained
3. The beneficiary selects a house design from the rural housing technology menu
4. The beneficiary opens a bank/Post Office account linked with Aadhaar for DBT
5. Construction begins after the first instalment is released
6. Second and third instalments are released upon verification of construction stages via geo-tagged photographs
7. Upon completion, the house is verified and the beneficiary is facilitated for convergence benefits
