# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Target beneficiary is a government agency, public institution, or end-user in agriculture, aviation, power, water resources, disaster management, or general public
- [ ] Beneficiary seeks access to real-time weather forecasts, nowcasts, extended range forecasts, or sector-specific advisories (e.g., Gramin Krishi Mausam Seva for farmers)
- [ ] Beneficiary requires aviation meteorological services for flight safety (access via Aerodrome Meteorological Offices or Meteorological Watch Offices)
- [ ] Beneficiary is a power grid operator seeking weather-informed scheduling via the Weather Power Portal (developed with POSOCO)
- [ ] Beneficiary is a state or central agency needing hydrometeorological or environmental data (e.g., flood forecasting, air quality, ozone monitoring)
- [ ] Beneficiary is a farmer seeking agromet advisories via SMS on registered mobile number through the mKisan portal
- [ ] Beneficiary is part of the general public accessing forecasts, warnings, and data via IMD website (mausam.imd.gov.in), UMANG app, or district-wise warning GIS portals
- [ ] No formal application or registration is required for accessing general weather services (services are publicly disseminated)

> **Note**: Mission Mausam is a public service initiative with no direct financial transfers or formal application process for end-users. Services are automatically available through IMD’s official channels.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] Proof of identity (Aadhaar, PAN, passport)
- [ ] Proof of address (utility bill, rental agreement)
- [ ] Bank account details (for any potential future financial interface, though not currently required)
- [ ] Udyam Registration (if applicable for MSMEs in beneficiary sectors)
- [ ] GSTIN (if applicable for commercial entities in aviation, power, etc.)
- [ ] Latest ITR (if applicable for institutional beneficiaries)
- [ ] Authorization letter (if applying on behalf of an organization)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] **No scheme-specific documents required** — Mission Mausam does not require formal application or registration for end-users to access services. Services are disseminated publicly through IMD’s portals, SMS advisories, and bulletins.

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] File size: Under 5 MB for uploads (if any future portal interaction arises)
- [ ] File naming: Clear, descriptive names (e.g., "Agency_ID_Proof.pdf")
- [ ] Language: Hindi or English (as supported by IMD’s multilingual portal)
- [ ] Certification: Self-attested copies acceptable; notarization not required for public service access
- [ ] Validity: Documents must be current (e.g., ITR not older than 1 year, Udyam active)

### Client Communication Template
**Subject**: Accessing Mission Mausam Services – No Application Required  

Dear [Client Name],  

Thank you for your interest in Mission Mausam, the Ministry of Earth Sciences’ initiative implemented by the India Meteorological Department (IMD).  

Please note that **Mission Mausam does not require a formal application or registration** for end-users to access its services. The scheme provides real-time weather forecasts, sector-specific advisories (e.g., for agriculture, aviation, power, disaster management), and early warnings for extreme weather events through publicly available channels.  

**How to Access Services**:  
- **Farmers**: Receive Gramin Krishi Mausam Seva (GKMS) advisories via SMS on registered mobile numbers through the mKisan portal.  
- **Aviation Users**: Access meteorological data through Aerodrome Meteorological Offices (AMOs) and Meteorological Watch Offices (MWOs) at major airports.  
- **Power Sector**: Utilize the Weather Power Portal (developed with POSOCO) for grid management.  
- **General Public & Agencies**: Access forecasts, warnings, and data via:  
  - IMD Website: [https://mausam.imd.gov.in/](https://mausam.imd.gov.in/)  
  - Mobile Apps: UMANG, Mausam App  
  - District-wise Warning GIS Portals  
  - SMS Advisories (for registered users)  

Services are available **year-round on a rolling basis**, updated in real time. No deadline applies for general access.  

For specialized data products requiring registration or authorization (e.g., certain satellite or radar data), please contact the nodal officer:

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Not required**. Mission Mausam does not involve project-based funding or direct financial transfers to beneficiaries. It is a public service infrastructure scheme.  
- **Flag**: Verify with nodal officer only if seeking access to specialized data products (e.g., raw satellite feeds, customized model outputs) that may require a data sharing agreement.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
- **Not applicable**. There is no application form for end-user access to Mission Mausam services.  
- **Protocol Notes**:  
  1. Do not attempt to fill a non-existent application form — this may cause confusion or delays.  
  2. Focus instead on guiding the client to the correct access channel (portal, SMS, office) based on their sector.  
  3. For aviation users: Confirm access to MWO/AMO at their nearest international airport.  
  4. For power sector: Confirm registration on the Weather Power Portal (if applicable).  
  5. For farmers: Verify mobile number is registered with mKisan for GKMS SMS advisories.  
  6. For general public: Confirm ability to access mausam.imd.gov.in or UMANG app.  
  7. For data product requests: Prepare a formal request letter to IMD’s Data Service Portal.  
  8. Always cite the official portal: [https://mausam.imd.gov.in/](https://mausam.imd.gov.in/) as the primary access point.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] Client’s sector and use case clearly mapped to an IMD service channel (e.g., GKMS, MWO, Weather Power Portal)  
- [ ] Client informed that no application, fee, or registration is needed for general access  
- [ ] Client provided with correct portal URL, contact details, and access instructions  
- [ ] Any request for specialized data documented and routed to IMD’s Data Service Portal or nodal officer  
- [ ] Follow-up timeline established based on client’s need (e.g., pre-monsoon advisory setup)
