## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon, I'm calling from [Your Firm Name]. We specialize in helping government agencies, public institutions, and sector-specific users like farmers, aviation authorities, power grid operators, and disaster management teams access real-time weather services and early warning systems through the Mission Mausam initiative by the India Meteorological Department. Before we explore whether this could support your operations, I’d like to understand your current challenges with weather-dependent decision-making.

### Discovery Questions
1. What sector do you operate in—agriculture, aviation, power, water resources, disaster management, or another weather-sensitive field?
2. How do you currently receive weather forecasts, nowcasts, or early warnings for cyclones, thunderstorms, heatwaves, or flash floods?
3. Are you accessing sector-specific advisories like Gramin Krishi Mausam Seva (GKMS) for farmers or aviation meteorological services for flight safety?
4. Do you face delays or gaps in receiving impact-based forecasts for extreme weather events such as floods or heatwaves?
5. Are you utilizing IMD’s digital platforms—such as the mausam.imd.gov.in portal, mobile apps like UMANG, or district-wise warning GIS portals—for real-time weather data?
6. Have you registered for SMS-based agromet advisories through the mKisan portal if you’re in agriculture?
7. Do you rely on institutional channels like Aerodrome Meteorological Offices or Meteorological Watch Offices for aviation operations?
8. Are you involved in grid management or hydrological planning where weather-informed scheduling could improve operational efficiency?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Operates in one of the target sectors: farmers, aviation sector, power sector, water resource managers, disaster management agencies, or general public
- [ ] Requires real-time weather forecasts, nowcasts, or extended range forecasts

- [ ] Seeks early warnings for cyclones, thunderstorms, heatwaves, or flash floods via SMS, web, or mobile apps

- [ ] Located in India (Pan-India geographic scope)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like Mission Mausam—the India Meteorological Department’s national weather service initiative—could directly support your operational needs by providing free, real-time weather insights and early warnings. ## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Weather-sensitive sectors like agriculture, aviation, power, and disaster management face operational risks due to delayed or inaccurate weather information, leading to crop losses, flight disruptions, grid instability, flood damage, and inadequate emergency preparedness—especially when early warnings for extreme events like cyclones, heatwaves, or flash floods are not received in time.

**Scheme intro:**
Mission Mausam, implemented by the India Meteorological Department (IMD) under the Ministry of Earth Sciences, Government of India, is a centrally funded public service initiative that delivers real-time weather forecasts, nowcasts, and extended range forecasts; sector-specific advisories such as Gramin Krishi Mausam Seva (GKMS) for farmers, aviation meteorological services for flight safety, hydrometeorological support for flood forecasting, and environmental monitoring including air quality and ozone services. It provides early warnings for cyclones, thunderstorms, heatwaves, and flash floods through SMS, web portals, and mobile applications. The scheme enables secure and economic operation of the Indian power grid via weather-informed scheduling, supports agricultural planning with crop-specific advisories, and enhances disaster preparedness through impact-based forecasting. Benefits also include access to climate data, research collaboration, and training programs for capacity building. Financial support is provided through budgetary allocations for infrastructure development, including procurement of Doppler Weather Radars (DWRs), automatic weather stations (AWS), high-performance computing systems for numerical weather prediction, and satellite data reception systems.
