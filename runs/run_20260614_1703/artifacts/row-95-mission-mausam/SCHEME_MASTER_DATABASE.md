### Mission Mausam
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Recognition
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** India Meteorological Department (IMD), Ministry of Earth Sciences, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://mausam.imd.gov.in/
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Government agencies, public institutions, and end-users across sectors such as agriculture, aviation, power, water resources, and disaster management
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Agriculture, aviation, power, water resources, disaster management, general public
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Direct beneficiaries include farmers receiving agromet advisories, aviation authorities using meteorological watch offices, power grid operators utilizing weather portals for grid management, and state and central agencies accessing hydrometeorological and environmental data. No formal application process for end-users as services are disseminated publicly through IMD’s portals, SMS advisories, and bulletins.

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Real-time weather forecasts, nowcasts, and extended range forecasts; sector-specific advisories such as Gramin Krishi Mausam Seva (GKMS) for farmers, aviation meteorological services for flight safety, hydrometeorological support for flood forecasting, and environmental monitoring including air quality and ozone services. Early warnings for cyclones, thunderstorms, heatwaves, and flash floods through SMS, web portals, and mobile applications. Enables secure and economic operation of the Indian power grid via weather-informed scheduling, supports agricultural planning with crop-specific advisories, and enhances disaster preparedness through impact-based forecasting. Benefits also include access to climate data, research collaboration, and training programs for capacity building.
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit (services provided free of charge through public dissemination)
- Duration of benefit: Continuous (year-round, real-time updates)

#### Documents Required
1. No formal application or registration required for accessing general weather services
2. For specialized services (e.g., DWR network), registration or authorization may be required
3. Farmers receive agromet advisories via SMS on registered mobile numbers through the mKisan portal
4. Aviation users access meteorological data through Aerodrome Meteorological Offices and Meteorological Watch Offices
5. Power sector users utilize the Weather Power Portal developed in collaboration with POSOCO
6. General public and agencies can access forecasts, warnings, and data through the IMD website (mausam.imd.gov.in), mobile apps like UMANG, and district-wise warning GIS portals

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. No direct application process for end-users as it is a public service initiative
2. Services are automatically available to beneficiaries through IMD’s official channels
3. Farmers receive agromet advisories via SMS on registered mobile numbers through the mKisan portal
