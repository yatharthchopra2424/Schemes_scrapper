# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] The applicant is a registered entity under Indian law (e.g., company, society, trust, academic institution, or research organization)
- [ ] The applicant is engaged in activities related to deep ocean exploration, technology development, or marine scientific research
- [ ] The applicant has the technical and financial capacity to undertake deep ocean mission-related projects
- [ ] The project proposal aligns with the objectives of the Deep Ocean Mission (DOM) as outlined by the Ministry of Earth Sciences
- [ ] The applicant is not blacklisted by any Government of India ministry or department
- [ ] The applicant possesses necessary clearances or is eligible to obtain them for oceanic research activities (if applicable)
- [ ] The applicant is based in India and operates within the jurisdiction of the Government of India

> **Note**: Eligibility criteria for the Deep Ocean Mission are not explicitly detailed in the available evidence. The above checklist is derived from the scheme’s nature as a scientific and technological mission under the Ministry of Earth Sciences, targeting research and development in deep ocean exploration. Applicants are typically research institutions, academia, or industry partners with relevant expertise. Confirm specific eligibility with the nodal officer.

**Sources**:  
- Scheme portal: https://moes.gov.in/schemes/dom

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation / Registration Certificate
- [ ] PAN Card of the organization
- [ ] Audited Financial Statements (last 2 years)
- [ ] Income Tax Returns (ITR) of the last 2 years
- [ ] GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)
- [ ] Bank Statement (last 6 months)
- [ ] Udyam Registration Certificate (for MSMEs, if applicable)
- [ ] Authorization Letter / Board Resolution authorizing the application
- [ ] CVs of Principal Investigator and key team members
- [ ] Project Proposal / Detailed Project Report (DPR)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Technical feasibility report detailing deep ocean technology or exploration methodology
- [ ] Clearance or No Objection Certificate (NOC) from relevant maritime or environmental authorities (if applicable)
- [ ] Letters of collaboration or MoUs with partner institutions (if joint project)
- [ ] Details of prior experience in oceanic research, marine technology, or polar/expedition projects
- [ ] Utilization Plan for funds, including milestones and deliverables aligned with DOM objectives
- [ ] Ethical clearance certificate (if biological sampling or marine biodiversity work is involved)
- [ ] Security clearance undertaking (for projects involving sensitive oceanic data or strategic areas)

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible, scanned in PDF format (minimum 300 DPI if scanned)
- [ ] File names follow the format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ApplicantName_Date.pdf` (e.g., `DeepOceanMission_ITR2023_ABCResearch_20240515.pdf`)
- [ ] All certificates are valid and not expired (check validity dates)
- [ ] Documents are in English or accompanied by a certified English translation
- [ ] Financial documents are audited and signed by a Chartered Accountant
- [ ] Project report is signed and stamped by the Head of Institution or authorized signatory
- [ ] Declarations and undertakings are on stamp paper of requisite value (if required) and notarized

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is required for the Deep Ocean Mission application. Based on the scheme’s focus on scientific and technological development in deep ocean exploration, the DPR must include the following standard sections:

- Executive Summary  
- Introduction and Background (linking to DOM objectives)  
- Objectives and Scope of Work  
- Methodology and Technical Approach  
- Work Plan with Timelines (Gantt chart or milestone-based)  
- Budget Breakdown (capital, operational, manpower, consumables, travel, etc.)  
- Expected Outcomes and Deliverables  
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan  
- Sustainability and Utilization Plan post-funding  
- Details of Collaborating Institutions (if any)  
- CVs of Principal Investigator and Key Personnel  
- Declaration of Originality and No Duplicate Funding  
- Undertaking on Compliance with Government Guidelines  

> **Note**: While the DOM portal does not explicitly publish a DPR template, applicants are advised to align with standard scientific project formats used by the Ministry of Earth Sciences. Verify exact DPR requirements with the nodal officer during pre-submission consultation.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
Follow these 8 critical rules when filling the application form on the DOM portal:

1. **Access the correct portal**: Use only the official URL: https://moes.gov.in/schemes/dom  
2. **Register first**: Create a user account using official email and mobile number before initiating the application  
3. **Fill all mandatory fields**: Do not leave any asterisk-marked (*) fields blank; the system will not allow submission  
4. **Use precise project title**: Reflect deep ocean focus (e.g., “Development of Autonomous Underwater Vehicle for Polymetallic Nodule Exploration”)  
5. **Select correct scheme**: Ensure “Deep Ocean Mission” is selected from the dropdown list of schemes  
6. **Budget accuracy**: Enter financial details matching the DPR exactly; round off to nearest thousand INR  
7. **Upload documents in prescribed order**: Follow the sequence as listed in the document checklist to avoid mismatch during verification  
8. **Preview before final submit**: Use the “Preview Application” option to check for errors; once submitted, edits may not be allowed  

> **Portal Note**: The DOM application interface is hosted on the Ministry of Earth Sciences website. If the form is not directly visible, look for “Apply Online” or “Scheme Application” section under the DOM page. If unavailable, applications may be submitted via email to the nodal officer — confirm mode of submission during eligibility check.

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify the official approval letter from the Ministry of Earth Sciences  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount, duration, and disbursement schedule as per approval  
- [ ] Share approval documents with the client and obtain acknowledgment  
- [ ] Guide the client in opening a dedicated bank account for DOM funds (if required)  
- [ ] Assist in preparing the first utilization certificate and progress report as per DOM guidelines  
- [ ] Track milestone completion and ensure timely submission of technical and financial reports  
- [ ] Verify that invoices for vendor payments are retained for audit purposes  
- [ ] Confirm client’s eligibility for success fee invoicing as per consultancy agreement  
- [ ] Maintain all records (approval, correspondence, reports) for minimum 5 years post-project completion  

> **Note**: Post-approval compliance for DOM projects includes submission of half-yearly progress reports, annual utilization certificates, and a final project completion report. Technical deliverables (e.g., prototypes, data, publications) may also be required. Confirm specific reporting format with the nodal officer.
