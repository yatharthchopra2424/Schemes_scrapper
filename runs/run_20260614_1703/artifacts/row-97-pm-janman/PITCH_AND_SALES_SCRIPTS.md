## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping tribal welfare departments and implementing agencies access central government grants for Multi-Purpose Centres (MPCs) under the PM-JANMAN mission. This scheme provides up to Rs. 60 lakh per MPC for construction and essential services in Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Group (PVTG) habitations. I’d like to understand your current infrastructure gaps and see if this aligns with your goals for PVTG development.

### Discovery Questions
1. Which state and district are you working in, and does it fall under the 18 States and 1 UT (Andaman & Nicobar Islands) covered by PM-JANMAN?
2. Are you focusing on Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs) as your target beneficiary?
3. What is the approximate PVTG population in the habitation/village you’re targeting? (Eligible range: 50–100 PVTG population)
4. Does the habitation lack a Van Dhan Vikas Kendra (VDVK) and a Skill Centre?
5. Is there an Anganwadi Centre or Health Sub-Centre missing in the habitation? (Priority is given based on absence of these)
6. Has the state updated habitation-wise survey data on the PM Gati Shakti Portal for this area?
7. Is approximately half an acre (2200 sq ft built-up) of government/public land available for MPC construction, with ownership transferable to the State Tribal Welfare Department?
8. Has stakeholder consultation at the Gram Sabha/Panchayat level been conducted for land, location, design, and construction oversight?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)

- [ ] All necessary central/state clearances obtainable
- [ ] Recommendation of District Level Coordination Committee (DLCC) obtainable
- [ ] Approval of Principal Secretary (TWD) obtainable

Based on your responses, your habitation appears to meet the core eligibility criteria for PM-JANMAN’s Multi-Purpose Centre intervention. This scheme offers a 100% grant of up to Rs. 60 lakh per MPC for construction and essential services—exactly the kind of support that can bridge critical infrastructure gaps in PVTG areas. ## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
PVTG habitations often lack basic infrastructure like Anganwadi centres, health sub-centres, skill centres, and livelihood support due to remoteness, low population density, and exclusion from existing schemes. This results in poor access to nutrition, healthcare, education, and income opportunities, perpetuating socio-economic vulnerability.

**Scheme intro:**
Pradhan Mantri Janjati Adivasi Nyaya Maha Abhiyan (PM-JANMAN), administered by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, provides a 100% grant of up to Rs. 60 lakh per Multi-Purpose Centre (MPC) for construction only. The scheme covers 18 States and 1 Union Territory (Andaman & Nicobar Islands) and targets 75 Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs). Funds are released in two instalments: 50% upfront and the balance after submission of Statement of Expenditure (SoE) and Utilisation Certificate (UC).

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your habitation has a PVTG population within the 50–100 range, meeting the primary demographic threshold.
- It lacks both a VDVK centre and a Skill Centre, satisfying the key infrastructure gap criterion.
- Priority is given due to the absence of Anganwadi and Health Sub-Centres, aligning with the scheme’s sequencing.
- The state has updated survey data on the PM Gati Shakti Portal and conducted Gram Sabha consultations, fulfilling procedural prerequisites.
