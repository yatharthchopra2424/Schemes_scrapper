# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
**PM-JANMAN (Pradhan Mantri Janjati Adivasi Nyaya Maha Abhiyan)** is a Central Sector Grant scheme launched by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA) on November 15, 2023, during Janjatiya Gaurav Divas in Khunti, Jharkhand. The scheme targets **75 Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs)** residing in **18 States and 1 Union Territory (Andaman & Nicobar Islands)** who were unreached by existing schemes and require multi-sectoral handholding. The mission period runs from **2023-24 to 2025-26**, with a total fund size of **Rs. 24,000 Crore**. The scheme aims to improve socio-economic conditions by saturating households and habitations with basic facilities, including safe housing, clean drinking water, education, health, nutrition, road and telecom connectivity, electrification, and sustainable livelihoods.

### Objectives
- Improve socio-economic conditions of PVTGs by saturating households and habitations with basic facilities  
- Provide access to secure and habitable housing for every PVTG household  
- Guarantee access to clean and safe drinking water sources and proper sanitation facilities  
- Enhance accessibility to quality education with focus on increasing enrollment and retention among PVTG children  
- Ensure easy access to healthcare services, regular check-ups, and nutrition programs to improve health outcomes  
- Establish road infrastructure, electricity, and telecom connectivity to facilitate economic opportunities and access to information  
- Promote sustainable livelihood options within or near PVTG habitations to reduce dependency on marginal resources  
- Provide vocational education and skilling opportunities for economic empowerment  

### Eligibility Matrix
| Criterion | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Target Beneficiaries** | Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs) |
| **Geographic Scope** | 18 States and 1 Union Territory (Andaman & Nicobar Islands) |
| **Habitation Criteria** | 50-100 PVTG population lacking VDVK centres and Skill Centres |
| **Priority Order for Habitations** | 1. Habitations without Anganwadi Centre and Health Sub-Centres<br>2. Habitations without Health Sub-Centre<br>3. Habitations without Anganwadi Centres |
| **Land Requirement** | Approximately half acre (2200 sq ft built-up area) for MPC construction |
| **State Obligations** | Update habitation-wise survey data on PM Gati Shakti Portal; conduct stakeholder consultations at Gram Sabha/Panchayat level for land, location, design, and construction oversight |
| **Implementing Agency** | Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA) |
| **Application Portal** | [https://tribal.nic.in/PM-JANMAN.aspx](https://tribal.nic.in/PM-JANMAN.aspx) |

### Benefits & Financial Support
| Component | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Financial Grant** | 100% grant up to **Rs. 60 lakh per Multi-Purpose Centre (MPC)** for construction only |
| **Fund Release Mechanism** | Two instalments:<br>- First instalment: 50% upon approval<br>- Final instalment: Balance after submission of Statement of Expenditure (SoE) and Utilisation Certificate (UC) from the State, and utilization of funds as per DOE guidelines for SNA accounts |
| **Recurring Costs** | Borne by respective line Ministries/Departments and State Governments as per PM-JANMAN guidelines (Anganwadi, ANM, ASHA workers, drug availability) |
| **Services Provided** | Nutrition (Anganwadi worker), health (ANM, ASHA), skill & vocational education (Rural Self Employment Training Institutes), livelihood generation (VDVKs), financial inclusion (Corporate BC-NRLM), community cultural programmes (Gram Panchayat/Traditional leaders) |
| **Technical & Administrative Support** | Through Tribal Welfare Department coordination with State Women and Child Development and Health departments |
| **Monitoring** | Via District Level Coordination Committee (DLCC) and State Level Coordination Committee (SLCC); data updation on PM Gati Shakti Portal for physical and financial progress |
| **Key Caveats** | - Construction must be carried out only on Government/Public land<br>- Construction cost including furniture, fixture, fittings, and other basic requirements must not exceed Rs. 60 lakh<br>- State must ensure land identified is adequate for construction and ownership transferred to State TWD<br>- Recommendation of DLCC and approval of Principal Secretary on the proposal must be obtained<br>- Technical scrutiny of estimates must be done by competent authority as per DFPR applicable in the state<br>- Project must be executed by Govt. agency in a transparent manner<br>- All necessary clearances under Central or State Act/Rules/Regulations must be obtained by the State<br>- State TWD must submit detailed report to MoTA after complying with stipulations |

### Application Process Flowchart
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[State Government identifies eligible PVTG habitations/villages<br/>(50-100 PVTG population, lacking VDVK and Skill Centres)] --> B[State updates habitation-wise survey data on PM Gati Shakti Portal]
    B --> C[State conducts stakeholder consultation at Gram Sabha/Panchayat level<br/>for land, location, design, and construction oversight]
    C --> D[State ensures land availability (~half acre)<br/>and transfers ownership to State Tribal Welfare Department (TWD)]
    D --> E[State prepares Detailed Project Report (DPR)<br/>with cost estimates approved by competent authority<br/>and agency executing the project]
    E --> F[State submits proposal through Principal Secretary/Secretary,<br/>Tribal Welfare Department for approval by Project Approval Committee (PAC)<br/>at Ministry of Tribal Affairs]
    F --> G[Proposal examined by PM-JANMAN-associated division<br/>before sending for approval by Committee chaired by Mission Director/JS of PM-JANMAN]
    G --> H[Approval communicated after financial concurrence<br/>of Internal Finance Division (IFD)]
    H --> I[Funds released in two instalments:<br/>- 50% first instalment<br/>- Balance after SoE and UC submission]
    I --> J[State updates physical and financial progress<br/>on PM Gati Shakti Portal via dedicated module]
    J --> K[State TWD submits detailed report to MoTA<br/>after complying with stipulations<br/>(land ownership, construction on Govt/Public land,<br/>DLCC recommendation, Principal Secretary approval,<br/>transparent execution by Govt. agency,<br/>cost not exceeding Rs. 60 lakh,<br/>technical scrutiny by competent authority,<br/>clearances obtained)]
```

> **Source**: Application process details extracted from Scheme Key Facts and Citizens' Charter (Page 6, PM-JANMAN Division section). Portal URL: [https://tribal.nic.in/PM-JANMAN.aspx](https://tribal.nic.in/PM-JANMAN.aspx)

## Consultant's Field Guide to Generated Files

### 1. SCHEME_MASTER_DATABASE.md
**Real-time Usage**: Keep this open in a background tab during all client calls. When a client asks "What is the turnover limit?" or "Who administers this?", CTRL+F in this document to give an immediate, authoritative answer without checking the portal.  
*Example*: During a call with a Tribal Welfare Department official, if they ask "What is the max grant per MPC?", search "Rs. 60 lakh" to confirm the exact figure and cite the source instantly.

### 2. PITCH_AND_SALES_SCRIPTS.md
**Real-time Usage**: Open this file 5 minutes before your first Discovery Call with a lead. Read the "Problem Framing" out loud to hook them, then use the Qualification Checklist to interrogate their eligibility live on the phone. Keep the Objection Handlers table visible so you can immediately counter when they say "We're too small for this."  
*Example*: If a State TWD representative says "Our habitations are below 50 PVTG population", use the objection handler: "PM-JANMAN specifically targets 50-100 PVTG populations; smaller habitations may converge with other DA-JGUA interventions like TMMCs or CoCs."

### 3. APPLICATION_PLAYBOOK.md
**Real-time Usage**: Print this out or pin it to your desktop once the client signs the retainer. Check off each box in "Stage 1" before moving to "Stage 2". Use the "Client Communication Template" to copy-paste directly into your email when chasing them for pending documents.  
*Example*: After retaining a client from Jharkhand TWD, use Stage 1 checklist to verify land ownership transfer to TWD before initiating DPR preparation. Send the UC chasing template when the State delays SoE submission.

### 4. CLIENT_ONBOARDING_AND_CRM.md
**Real-time Usage**: Fill this out during or immediately after the onboarding call. Use the Needs Assessment to record their exact pain points. Update the "Compliance Status" table as they email you documents to maintain a single source of truth for what's missing.  
*Example*: During onboarding with Odisha TWD, record their pain point: "Delayed land transfer due to Gram Sabha approvals". When they email the land ownership letter, update Compliance Status from "Pending" to "Received".

### 5. LIVE_CASE_TRACKER.md
**Real-time Usage**: Review this document every morning during your standup. Update the "Stage" column daily. If a case hits "Stage 07 - Under review", use the Escalation Path notes here to know exactly who to call at the government department today.  
*Example*: When a Chhattisgarh MPC proposal reaches "Stage 07 - Under review", call the Joint Secretary (PVTG) at MoTA using the escalation path: "Contact JS (PVTG) → Director (PVTG) → Under Secretary (PVTG) for status update."

### 6. FEE_AND_REVENUE_MODEL.md
**Real-time Usage**: Use this file when drafting the proposal. Look at the client's turnover, map them to the pricing tier in the table, and quote that exact Retainer and Success Fee. Use the monthly projection table to update your personal sales pipeline forecast for the quarter.  
*Example*: For a Maharashtra TWD client with ₹500 Cr turnover, apply Tier 2 pricing: Retainer ₹2.5 lakh + Success Fee 8% of sanctioned amount. Update pipeline forecast when Telangana TWD signs retainer.

### 7. CLIENT_PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE.md
**Real-time Usage**: Copy this entire file, paste it into an email or PDF generator, replace the [PLACEHOLDER] tags with the client's actual details gathered from the CRM, and send it immediately after a successful discovery call.  
*Example*: After a discovery call with Tripura TWD confirming 17 new MPCs, replace [CLIENT_NAME], [TARGET_MPCs], [ESTIMATED_GRANT] and send the proposal within 2 hours.

### 8. COMPLIANCE_AND_LEGAL_PACK.md
**Real-time Usage**: Attach sections 8A and 8B as PDFs to the proposal email. Refuse to start Step 1 of the Application Playbook until the client signs these. Use the Disclaimers to protect yourself legally if the client is rejected by the government agency.  
*Example*: Before initiating DPR preparation for Gujarat TWD, obtain signed 8A (Land Ownership Undertaking) and 8B (Clearances Checklist). If PAC rejects the proposal due to missing DLCC minutes, cite the disclaimer: "Client responsible for securing all State-level approvals per Section 8B."
