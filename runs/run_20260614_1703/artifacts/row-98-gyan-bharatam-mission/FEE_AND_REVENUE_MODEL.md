## Pricing Principles
1. **Value-Based Alignment**: Fees are structured to reflect the strategic value delivered, with success fees tied to measurable outcomes such as manuscript digitization, conservation, or inclusion in the National Digital Repository under the Gyan Bharatam Mission.
2. **Risk Sharing**: Success fees are calibrated to the scheme’s moderate risk profile — while funding is secured (Rs 491.66 crore sanctioned for 2025–2031), outcomes depend on institutional partnerships, MoUs, and custodian cooperation, which may affect timelines and deliverables.
3. **Transparency & Predictability**: Retainers cover fixed advisory costs (eligibility assessment, documentation, portal navigation), while success fees are only payable upon verified achievement of scheme benefits (e.g., scholar registration, manuscript submission, or MoU facilitation).
4. **Scheme-Specific Note**: The Gyan Bharatam Mission offers non-financial benefits (access to digitized manuscripts, scholar recognition, collaboration opportunities) rather than direct monetary grants. Processing time varies based on manuscript volume and institutional readiness, but the rolling application basis allows for predictable engagement cycles. Success fees are therefore applied to facilitation of tangible outcomes (e.g., MoUs, digitization projects, scholar enrollment) rather than direct cash disbursements.
