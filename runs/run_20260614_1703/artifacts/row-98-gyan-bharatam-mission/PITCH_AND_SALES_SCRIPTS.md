# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I’m calling from [Your Firm Name], and we specialize in helping individuals and institutions like yours access government initiatives that preserve India’s cultural heritage. The Gyan Bharatam Mission is a national effort by the Ministry of Culture to safeguard, digitize, and share manuscript treasures—whether you hold a personal collection, manage a library, or represent an academic or religious institution. I’d like to understand your work with manuscripts and see if this mission aligns with your goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. What types of manuscripts do you currently hold or manage (e.g., palm-leaf, birch bark, paper, cloth, or other materials)?
2. Are these manuscripts in Sanskrit, Persian, regional languages, or other scripts such as Devanagari, Grantha, or Modi?
3. Is your institution or personal collection based in India, and are you interested in contributing to national heritage preservation?
4. Have you previously participated in any digitization, conservation, or scholarly collaboration initiatives?
5. Are you looking to register as a recognized scholar or contributor to access manuscript repositories and collaborate with researchers?
6. Would you like to participate in workshops, conferences, or webinars on manuscriptology, translation, or digital preservation?
7. Are you facing challenges in conserving, cataloging, or accessing your manuscript collection due to resource or expertise constraints?
8. Are you open to collaborating with institutions like the Ministry of Culture or academic centres under a formal partnership (e.g., MoU) for preservation efforts?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Holds manuscripts in any format (palm-leaf, birch bark, paper, cloth, etc.)
- [ ] Manuscripts are in Indian languages/scripts (Sanskrit, Persian, regional, etc.)
- [ ] Based in India (individual or institution: library, monastery, temple, academic body, citizen contributor)
- [ ] Interested in preservation, digitization, or sharing of manuscript heritage
- [ ] Willing to register as a Gyan Bharatam Scholar or participate in community network
- [ ] Open to collaboration via MoUs or institutional partnerships
- [ ] Seeking access to digitized repositories, workshops, or scholarly collaboration
- [ ] Not restricted by custodian policies that prohibit sharing or digitization (to be verified)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like the Gyan Bharatam Mission could support your work in preserving and sharing India’s manuscript heritage. This Ministry of Culture initiative offers access to digitized repositories, scholar recognition, and collaborative opportunities—all designed to help custodians like you safeguard and amplify the value of your manuscripts.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Many valuable manuscripts across India remain vulnerable to deterioration, lack proper cataloging, or are inaccessible to researchers and the public due to limited digitization and conservation resources. Custodians often struggle to preserve these treasures while seeking ways to share their knowledge widely.

**Scheme intro:**
The Gyan Bharatam Mission, led by the Ministry of Culture, Government of India, is a national initiative to survey, conserve, digitize, and share India’s manuscript heritage through the creation of a National Digital Repository. With a sanctioned fund of Rs 491.66 crore for 2025–2031, it supports individuals and institutions in preserving manuscripts and gaining recognition as part of a living network of scholars and knowledge-keepers.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You hold manuscripts in formats such as palm-leaf, birch bark, paper, or cloth, in Indian languages or scripts  
- You are based in India as an individual scholar, institution (library, monastery, temple), or citizen contributor  
- You are interested in contributing to preservation, digitization, or scholarly collaboration  
- You seek access to digitized repositories, workshops, or formal partnerships via MoUs
