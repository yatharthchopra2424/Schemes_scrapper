Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is Gyan Bharatam?

Gyan Bharatam is a national mission led by the Ministry of Culture to digitally preserve, catalog, and share India’s rich manuscript heritage with the world.

What types of manuscripts does Gyan Bharatam preserve?

We preserve ancient manuscripts in various formats—palm-leaf, birch bark, paper—and in multiple languages, including Sanskrit, Persian, regional languages, and more.

How can I search for manuscripts on the website?

You can search by theme, script, region, or keyword using the homepage search bar to explore India's timeless knowledge.

How does Gyan Bharatam ensure manuscript preservation?

Manuscripts are conserved using scientific methods, digitized using sustainable technology, and protected by trained experts.

Can I contribute or share an old manuscript I possess?

Yes! If you hold a piece of India’s heritage, use our digitisation requests page. We’ll help you document, digitize, and preserve your manuscript.

What events and workshops does Gyan Bharatam organize?

We host workshops, conferences, webinars, and academic exchange programs to promote research and awareness about India’s manuscript culture.

Can I access publications or research on Indian manuscripts?

Yes—explore our Publications section for books, research papers, and digital editions.

How can I contact Gyan Bharatam?

Contact us via email (gyanbharatam2025@gmail.com), phone (+91-11-23315625), or social media (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube).

Who can access the digitized manuscripts?

The digital manuscripts are accessible to researchers, students, and the general public interested in India’s cultural heritage.

Are there special collections or featured manuscripts on the website?

Yes. We highlight unique manuscripts, including the Manuscript of the Week, notable works in science, literature, and more.

What is the “Five Pillars” approach of Gyan Bharatam?

Our work is based on five pillars: Survey & Cataloguing, Conservation & Capacity Building, Technology & Digitization, Linguistics & Translation, and Research, Outreach & Publication.

How are manuscripts identified for preservation?

Manuscripts are mapped and recorded across India through standardized national surveys and collaboration with local institutions.

Do you offer educational resources for students or researchers?

Yes. We provide study materials, curated book lists, and details about participating in manuscript studies and conferences.

Can I request the digitisation of a manuscript in my possession?

Absolutely! Use our “Get in Touch” or digitisation request links to submit details. Our team will guide you through the process.

Are there resources available for various languages or scripts?

Yes. Manuscripts are available in multiple languages and scripts, and our catalog supports theme/script/region-based searches.

What partnerships does Gyan Bharatam have?

We work with government agencies, academic institutions, heritage organizations, and UNESCO for projects, research, and recognition.

How do you ensure the authenticity of cataloged manuscripts?

Manuscripts are authenticated by expert teams using established scholarly, scientific, and archival standards.

Is there a way to join or contribute to the Gyan Bharatam mission?

Yes! Researchers, scholars, and heritage enthusiasts can participate via our workshops, volunteer opportunities, and collaborative projects.

How can I stay updated on news or developments?

Check our Press Releases section and follow us on social media for latest activities, events, and new publications.

Are there privacy and accessibility options on the site?

Our website offers accessibility features (e.g., dyslexia/ADHD modes, color options, text-to-speech) and follows strict privacy guidelines detailed in our Privacy Policy.
