### Modified UDAN Scheme
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Subsidy
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Ministry of Civil Aviation (MoCA), implemented by Airports Authority of India (AAI)
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://www.aai.aero/en/rcs-udan
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open (bids invited periodically for different routes)

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Scheduled airline operators
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Aviation (airline operations)
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Must meet technical and financial criteria set by MoCA and AAI, including aircraft suitability, operational capability, and compliance with regulatory requirements; preference given to zero-VGF bids; open to domestic and international airlines including code-sharing arrangements

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Viability Gap Funding (VGF) for up to 50% of aircraft capacity; concessional excise duty of 2% on ATF for three years; reimbursement of GST on RCS seats (for UDAN 1-3); VGF indexed to ATF prices and inflation; central government provides 80% of VGF (90% for North Eastern States and Union Territories) through Regional Connectivity Fund (RCF)
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit (via RCF) and tax offsets (excise duty concession, GST reimbursement)
- Duration of benefit: Up to 10 years from commencement of operations

#### Documents Required
1. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
2. Air Operator’s Permit (AOP)
3. Aircraft Registration and Airworthiness Certificates
4. Financial Statements and Bank Guarantees
5. Route Proposal Document
6. Bid Submission Form
7. Security Clearance (if applicable)
8. Undertaking for Compliance with Scheme Guidelines
9. Details of Aircraft Fleet and Seating Capacity
10. Operational Plan and Schedule

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Identify route and submit proposal through the UDAN portal.
2. Participate in transparent, competitive bidding process for route allocation.
3. Qualify as a bidder based on technical and financial criteria.
4. Submit bid quoting VGF per seat for the route.
5. Win contract by quoting lowest VGF per seat.
6. Sign agreement with MoCA/AAI.
7. Commence flight operations within stipulated time.
8. Operate under scheme for up to 10 years with periodic review.
9. Claim VGF and concessions as per eligibility.
10. Submit periodic reports on operations, passenger load factor, and compliance.
