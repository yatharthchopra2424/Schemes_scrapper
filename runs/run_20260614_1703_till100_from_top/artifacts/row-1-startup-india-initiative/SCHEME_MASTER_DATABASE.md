### Startup India Initiative
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Recognition
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://www.startupindia.gov.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Private limited company, partnership firm, limited liability partnership, Multi-State Cooperative Society, or Cooperative Society
- Age of business: Up to 10 years from incorporation/registration (up to 20 years for DeepTech startups)
- Sector: Innovation, development or improvement of products or processes or services, or scalable business model with high potential for employment generation or wealth creation
- Revenue cap: Turnover not exceeding ₹200 crore in any financial year since incorporation (₹300 crore for DeepTech startups)
- Employee count: 
- Other: Entity must not be formed by splitting up or reconstruction of an existing business; must be working towards innovation, development or improvement of products or processes or services, or have a scalable business model with high potential for employment generation or wealth creation

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Access to self-certification compliance under 9 labour and environment laws, 80% rebate on patent filing fees, income tax exemption for 3 consecutive financial years out of first ten years, exemption from prior experience/turnover in public procurement, exemption from Earnest Money Deposit (EMD) in government tenders, faster winding up within 90 days under Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, access to Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS), Credit Guarantee Scheme for Startups (CGSS), tax exemption on investments above fair market value, and access to various government schemes and initiatives including Seed Fund Scheme, MAARG mentorship platform, Startup India Investor Connect, BHASKAR knowledge registry, and State/UT incentives
- Disbursement mode: Multiple modes including tax offset, direct credit, reimbursement, and facilitation
- Duration of benefit: Varies by benefit (e.g., tax exemption for 3 years, self-certification for 3 years, recognition valid for 10/20 years)

#### Documents Required
1. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration
2. PAN of the entity
3. Pitch deck or business description
4. Authorization letter from authorized signatory
5. Details of funding received (if any)
6. Memorandum of Association for Pvt. Ltd. / LLP Deed
7. Board Resolution (if any)
8. Annual Accounts of the startup for the last three financial years
9. Income Tax returns for the last three financial years
10. Incorporation number (CIN)
11. LLPIN (if applicable)
12. Registered Mobile Number
13. Email ID of Authorized Representative

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Register on the Startup India portal (www.startupindia.gov.in) to get a Bhaskar ID
2. Log in to the Startup India portal and navigate to the 'Get Recognised' section
3. Fill out the DPIIT recognition application form with details about the startup's uniqueness and innovation
4. Upload required documents including Certificate of Incorporation/Registration, PAN, and business description/pitch deck
5. Submit the application for review
6. Alternatively, apply via National Single Window System (NSWS) at www.nsws.gov.in by logging in, adding 'Registration as a Startup' to approvals, filling the form, and submitting
7. Track application status on NSWS or Startup India dashboard
8. Upon approval, download the DPIIT Recognition Certificate from NSWS, Startup India portal, or DigiLocker
