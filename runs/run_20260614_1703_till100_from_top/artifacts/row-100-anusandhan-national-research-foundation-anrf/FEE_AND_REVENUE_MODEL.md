## Pricing Principles
1. **Value-Based Alignment**: Fees are calibrated to the actual financial benefit delivered under the ANRF scheme, with success fees reflecting the grant amount secured (e.g., up to ₹5 Cr for ARG, ₹60 lakh + overheads for IRG, or actual travel/registration costs for ITS).
2. **Risk-Adjusted Pricing**: Success fees account for the scheme’s competitive nature and rigorous multi-stage review (pre-proposal → full proposal → presentation → Empowered Technical Committee for >₹1Cr proposals), with lower fees for higher-value, lower-success-probability enterprise engagements.
3. **Retainer for Effort Certainty**: Retainers cover fixed costs of eligibility assessment, document preparation, portal navigation, and compliance with ANRF’s detailed requirements (e.g., endorsement letters, biodata, plagiarism undertakings, utilization certificates).
4. **Scheme-Specific Note**: The ANRF scheme has a **moderate to high risk profile** due to its competitive selection, strict eligibility (Ph.D. requirement, regular position, service duration constraints), and limited re-submission windows. However, evidence shows rolling calls and defined program windows (e.g., Ramanujan Fellowships open Jan–Dec 2026, MAHA Water Program Jun–Jul 2026), enabling predictable engagement cycles. Processing time averages 3–6 months from submission to approval, justifying a 3-month lag in revenue recognition.
