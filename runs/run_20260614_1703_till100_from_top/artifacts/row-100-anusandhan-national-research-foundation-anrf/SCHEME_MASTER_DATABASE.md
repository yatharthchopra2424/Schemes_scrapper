### Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF)
- **Portal / Application URL:** www.anrfonline.in
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Rolling basis — calls for proposals are notified through the website 'www.anrfonline.in'. Specific programs have defined windows: e.g., Call for nominations under Ramanujan Fellowships is open from 1 January 2026 to 31 December 2026 (5:00 pm); Call for Pre-Proposals under MAHA WATER Program is open from 01-June-2026 to 10-July-2026 (5:00 PM); International Travel Support (ITS) applications are accepted only for events whose start date is between 13 August, 2026 and 12 September, 2026 including both these dates (as of 14 June, 2026).

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Researchers; academicians; scientists; faculty members; post-doctoral fellows; research institutions; universities; NGOs; private R&D institutions; deeptech startups/MSMEs
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Natural sciences, including mathematical sciences, engineering and technology, environmental and earth sciences, health and agriculture, and scientific and technological interfaces of humanities and social sciences
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: 
  - Applicants (Principal Investigator and Co-Principal Investigator(s)) should be Indian citizens. Foreign nationals (including OCI and NRI) are also eligible to apply provided they fulfil the eligibility criteria notified.
  - The applicant(s) must hold a Ph.D. degree in Sciences, Mathematics and Engineering. Applicants with Master’s Degree in medicine, surgery, or dental sciences (M.D. / M.S. / M.D.S. / M.V.Sc.) can apply two years after obtaining degree.
  - Under Quantitative Social Sciences area, PI and Co-Investigator(s) should hold Ph.D. degree in Science, Mathematics, Engineering, M.D. / M.S. / M.D.S. / M.V.Sc. degree or Ph.D degree in any Social Sciences area.
  - The applicant(s) (PIs and Co-PIs) must hold a regular position in a recognized academic institution or National Research Laboratory or in any other recognized research organization in India.
  - Private Academic institutions with valid UGC / AICTE / PCI approval, Private R&D Institutions with valid DSIR-SIRO recognition and Voluntary and Non-Governmental Organizations registered under NITI-AAYOG Darpan portal are eligible to host a project.
  - NGOs must have a valid DARPAN portal registration, and DSIR SIRO recognition to receive direct grant-in-aid under the program.
  - The term 'Regular' in the context of ARG refers to applicants who are appointed against a sanctioned post or on a tenure-track post of an Institute/University, and are likely to be renewed after the end of the tenure.
  - Research Associates, Post-doctoral fellows, Ad-hoc faculties, Guest Faculties, Visiting Scientists, Consultants, Project Fellows, Faculty members/researchers whose contract is renewed every year are not eligible to apply.
  - INSPIRE Faculty Fellowship, Ramanujan Fellowship, and Ramalingaswamy Fellowship holders are also eligible to apply as PI or Co-PI subject to fulfilling the service criteria as indicated below.
  - Service Duration: In general, PI should have at least 'the duration of the proposed project plus one year' of official service remaining at the time of submission of proposals (for purposes of computation, the last date for submission of proposals will be taken).
  - PI who will be superannuating during the duration of the project or already superannuated are also eligible to apply.
  - In such cases, a Faculty/Scientist from the host institute who has at least the duration of the proposed project plus one year of official service remaining should be associated as a Co-PI.
  - Under no condition will ANRF projects be allowed to continue with a PI or Co-PI aged 70.
  - Investigators can hold a maximum of two ANRF (or erstwhile SERB) projects at any point of time.
  - Investigators become eligible to apply for another project within six months from the date of completion of the ongoing project.
  - Application for another project more than six months prior to the completion will not be considered.

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: 
  - ARG Program: ₹ 5 Crores (maximum for project funding)
  - IRG: ₹ 60 lakh plus overheads for a period of three years
  - ITS: To & fro economy class air fare by the shortest route, airport tax and visa fees; Registration fee as per actual or Rs. 50,000/- whichever is less (for young scientist Age< 35 on the date of start of event)
- Disbursement mode: Reimbursement (for ITS); Direct credit (for ARG and IRG based on context of utilization certificates and statements of expenditure)
- Duration of benefit: 
  - ARG: Maximum period of five years (with phased support beyond three years: 3+1 for 4 years and 3+1+1 for five years)
  - IRG: Three years
  - ITS: One-time support per eligible event

#### Documents Required
1. Endorsement letter duly signed and stamped by competent authority of the institute
2. Copy of letter of acceptance of the presenting paper (oral/poster) by the organizers
3. Copy of the abstract of the paper to be presented
4. Biodata with complete list of Scientific / Technical publications and Patents, if any
5. Bio-data of the Applicant
6. Certificate from Principal Investigator
7. Endorsement Letter from the Head of Institution
8. Category Certificate
9. Plagiarism undertaking
10. Audited / interim Utilization Certificate (UC)
11. Statement of Expenditure (SE) for the financial year in the correct format
12. Annual / interim progress report, as per approved format
13. List of publications / patents, if any, from the project
14. List of Assets acquired till that Financial Year
15. Final Consolidated Statement of Expenditure (SE), giving expenditure financial year wise (FY) from date of start till date of completion

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Visit 'www.anrfonline.in'.
2. Click on 'SIGN IN / REGISTER' section.
3. If not a registered user, click on 'NEW USER'.
4. Fill all the required details in the registration form.
5. Click on the 'Submit' button.
6. After successful submission, a 'Verification mail' will be sent to the given mail id.
7. Click on the verification link to complete registration.
8. After login, go to Menu --> Proposal Submission --> Form Submission --> Select scheme and start filling the form online.
9. Submit the proposal through the website 'www.anrfonline.in'.
10. A pre-proposal must be submitted in the prescribed format by the deadline.
11. Applicants whose pre-proposals are shortlisted will be invited to submit a full proposal.
12. These full proposals will undergo a rigorous review by subject matter experts.
13. PIs may also be called for a presentation, whenever required.
14. Larger value proposals (exceeding ₹1 Cr) will undergo another round of evaluation at level III by the Empowered Technical Committee, with specific emphasis on impact, and depth of collaboration.
