# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Startup is a technology startup (focus on ICT, deep-tech, or emerging technologies)
- [ ] Startup is registered as a legal entity in India (Private Limited, LLP, or equivalent)
- [ ] Startup is located in a Tier-II or Tier-III city in India (as per official classification)
- [ ] Startup is at one of the following stages: idea validation (EiR), product piloting (Pilot Funding), market fundraising (Investment Support), or deep-tech solution development (Deep-Tech Funding)
- [ ] Startup has not previously received funding under GENESIS for the same component
- [ ] Startup team includes at least one founder who is an Indian citizen
- [ ] Startup is not a subsidiary of a larger corporation or public sector undertaking
- [ ] Startup has a clear problem statement and proposed solution aligned with national priorities (e.g., healthcare, agriculture, education, sustainability, deep-tech)
- [ ] Startup has a minimum viable product (MVP) or prototype for Pilot Funding and Investment Support components
- [ ] Startup has a scalable business model with potential for job creation and revenue generation

> **Note**: Specific eligibility criteria for individual components (EiR, Pilot Funding, Investment Support, Deep-Tech Funding) are not detailed in the evidence. Verify with nodal officer or refer to the Administrative Approval and Scheme Brochure (PDFs) for exact thresholds.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed
- PAN Card of the startup
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)
- Udyam Registration Certificate
- Bank Statement (last 6 months)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of the startup for last 2 financial years (if applicable)
- Audited Financial Statements (if applicable)
- Founder(s) PAN and Aadhaar
- Board Resolution authorizing application (for companies)
- Cancelled cheque or bank verification letter

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed Project Report (DPR) or Business Plan outlining the innovation, technology, and impact
- Proof of concept / prototype demonstration (video, photos, or lab report) for Pilot Funding and Deep-Tech Funding
- Market validation documents (customer letters, LOIs, pilot results) for Investment Support
- Incubation certificate or letter of support from a recognized incubator/accelerator (if applicable)
- For EiR: Idea note or concept note highlighting the problem and proposed solution
- For Deep-Tech Funding: Technical whitepaper or patent application details (if any)
- For Investment Support: Term sheet or pitch deck used for fundraising
- For Pilot Funding: Work order or purchase order from a pilot customer (if available)

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document)
- File names must follow the format: `StartupName_DocumentType_Date.pdf` (e.g., `ABC_Tech_PAN_20240515.pdf`)
- All certificates must be valid and not expired
- Documents in regional languages must be accompanied by a certified English translation
- Affidavits and declarations must be on non-judicial stamp paper and notarized where required
- Udyam and GST certificates must reflect the current registered address of the startup
- Bank statements must show the startup’s name and account number clearly

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) or comprehensive business plan is required for all components of GENESIS.  
**Standard sections to include**:
- Executive Summary
- Problem Statement and Innovation
- Technology Description and Novelty
- Market Analysis and Target Audience (Tier-II/III cities focus)
- Business Model and Revenue Strategy
- Go-to-Market Plan and Pilot/Implementation Strategy
- Financial Projections (3–5 years)
- Funding Request and Utilization Plan (aligned with EiR/Pilot/Investment/Deep-Tech components)
- Team Background and Capabilities
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan
- Social Impact and Job Creation Potential (especially in Tier-II/III cities)
- Annexures: Prototypes, certificates, letters of support

> **Note**: Verify exact DPR format and length requirements with the nodal officer, as the evidence does not specify a template.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the application portal via the MeitY Startup Hub website: https://msh.meity.gov.in/schemes/genesis  
2. Register or log in using the startup’s official email and mobile number  
3. Select “Apply for GENESIS” and choose the relevant component (EiR, Pilot Funding, Investment Support, Deep-Tech Funding)  
4. Fill all mandatory fields marked with * — do not leave any blank  
5. Enter the startup’s official name exactly as per Certificate of Incorporation  
6. Provide the registered address in Tier-II/Tier-III city format (city, state, pincode)  
7. Upload documents in the specified order and format as per the portal instructions  
8. Declare that the startup has not received duplicate funding for the same activity under any other government scheme  
9. Confirm that all information provided is true and correct to the best of knowledge  
10. Preview the entire application before final submission to avoid errors  
11. Do not use special characters or emojis in text fields  
12. Keep a copy of the filled form for internal records  

> **Portal Note**: The application process is not explicitly detailed in the evidence; refer to the Administrative Approval and Scheme Brochure (PDFs) for exact steps. Use the MeitY Mitra AI assistant on the SAMARTH portal for real-time guidance.

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the official portal: https://msh.meity.gov.in/schemes/genesis  
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing the application reference number and timestamp  
- Save the reference number in the case file and set up automated reminders for follow-up  
- Download and store the submitted application PDF for records  
- Notify the client of successful submission with the reference number  
- Mark submission date in the tracker and initiate follow-up cycle

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify the approval letter from MeitY Startup Hub (check for sanctioned amount, component, and validity period)  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount matches the applied component (EiR: up to ₹10L, Pilot: up to ₹40L, Investment: up to ₹50L, Deep-Tech: up to ₹1Cr)  
- [ ] Sign and return any grant agreement or undertaking letter within the stipulated time  
- [ ] Set up a dedicated bank account for scheme funds (if required)  
- [ ] Begin utilization of funds as per the approved plan and maintain detailed records  
- [ ] Submit periodic progress reports (quarterly) as specified in the approval terms  
- [ ] Prepare and submit Utilization Certificate (UC) along with audited expenditure statements  
- [ ] Ensure all purchases and payments are supported by valid invoices and vouchers  
- [ ] Coordinate with the nodal officer for any mid-term review or site visit  
- [ ] Upon project completion, submit final report and UC for closure  
- [ ] Follow up for release of any retained or success-fee component (if applicable)  
- [ ] Maintain all scheme-related documents for audit purposes for a minimum of 5 years  

> **Note**: Funding figures are subject to change as per MeitY committee suggestions; verify final amounts in the approval letter.
