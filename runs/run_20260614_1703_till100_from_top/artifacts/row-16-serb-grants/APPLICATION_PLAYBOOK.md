# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian national
- [ ] Applicant holds a Ph.D. in science or engineering (if applicable to the specific grant type)
- [ ] Applicant is a regular faculty or scientist in a recognized academic institution or R&D laboratory
- [ ] Applicant is affiliated with a SERB-recognized organization
- [ ] Research objectives align with the specific SERB grant scheme being applied for (e.g., Core Research Grant, Early Career Research Award, Ramanujan Fellowship)
- [ ] Project proposal is within the scope of basic research in science and engineering
- [ ] Applicant has not previously received a non-transferable SERB fellowship (if applying for a fellowship scheme)
- [ ] Host institution is willing to provide institutional endorsement and host the project/fellowship

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- PAN card of applicant and host institution
- Aadhaar card of applicant
- Bank account details (cancelled cheque or bank statement)
- GST registration certificate (if applicable to institution)
- Udyam Registration (for MSME institutions, if applicable)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) of applicant and institution for last 3 years
- Audited financial statements of host institution
- Incorporation/registration certificate of host institution

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Curriculum Vitae (CV) of applicant
- List of publications (with DOI/PMID where available)
- Detailed project proposal with objectives, methodology, and timeline
- Detailed budget breakdown (manpower, consumables, travel, contingency, overheads)
- Institutional endorsement letter from host institution
- Proof of Ph.D. degree (if applicable to the grant type)
- Endorsement from host institution (duplicate or supplementary to institutional letter)
- Ethics clearance certificate (if project involves human/animal subjects, biohazards, etc.)

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible scans or certified true copies
- File names must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ApplicantName.pdf`
- File size must not exceed 10 MB per document
- All certificates must be self-attested or gazetted officer attested
- Documents must be in English or accompanied by certified translation
- Dates on documents must be valid and not expired (where applicable)
- Budget must be itemized and justified with quotations where possible
- Project proposal must include clear milestones and deliverables

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
A Detailed Project Report (DPR) is not explicitly mandated as a separate document for SERB Grants. However, a comprehensive **project proposal** is required, which must include:
- Background and rationale
- Clear objectives and hypotheses
- Detailed methodology
- Work plan with timelines and milestones
- Expected outcomes and deliverables
- Budget justification
- References and preliminary data (if any)
Applicants should verify with the nodal officer or SERB scheme-specific guidelines whether a formal DPR is required for their specific grant type (e.g., CRG, ECRA, Ramanujan).

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Register first** on the SERB online portal (serbonline.in) before starting the application.
2. **Select the correct grant scheme** (e.g., Core Research Grant, Early Career Research Award) that matches your eligibility and research objectives.
3. **Fill all mandatory fields** – incomplete applications will not be accepted.
4. **Upload documents in the specified format** (PDF only, under 10 MB each) as per the portal’s document checklist.
5. **Ensure budget totals match** the sum of all heads (manpower, consumables, etc.) and are justified in the proposal.
6. **Double-check applicant and host institution details** for spelling and accuracy (name, address, PAN, etc.).
7. **Save draft periodically** – the portal may time out after inactivity.
8. **Preview the full application** before final submission to verify all sections are complete and documents are attached.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All sections of the application form are filled
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are uploaded and legible
- [ ] Budget is itemized, justified, and matches the proposal
- [ ] Project proposal includes clear objectives, methodology, timeline, and expected outcomes
- [ ] Institutional endorsement letter is on letterhead, signed, and dated
- [ ] Proof of Ph.D. is included (if required)
- [ ] Ethics clearance is attached (if applicable)
- [ ] Declarations (originality, no plagiarism, compliance) are signed
- [ ] Application is previewed and verified for completeness
- [ ] Reference number or draft ID is noted for tracking

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a screenshot of the final submission confirmation page showing the application reference number and timestamp
- Save the reference number in the case file and set up a follow-up reminder
- Download and store a PDF copy of the submitted application for records
- Set calendar reminders for:
  - 7 days after submission: check for acknowledgment
  - 15 days after submission: inquire if no update received
  - At regular intervals until outcome is communicated

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify the official approval letter from SERB
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount, project duration, and disbursement schedule
- [ ] Sign and return any required grant agreement or bond
- [ ] Ensure funds are received in the designated institutional account
- [ ] Begin project implementation as per approved timeline and budget
- [ ] Maintain auditable records of all expenditures (vouchers, bills, bank statements)
- [ ] Prepare for mid-term evaluation (if applicable) – submit progress reports as required
- [ ] Utilize funds strictly as per approved budget; seek prior approval for any deviations
- [ ] Acknowledge SERB support in all publications, patents, and presentations arising from the project
- [ ] Submit utilization certificate and statement of expenditure at project completion
- [ ] Coordinate with finance team to process success fee invoice as per consultancy agreement
- [ ] Retain all project documents for audit and future reference (minimum 5 years)
