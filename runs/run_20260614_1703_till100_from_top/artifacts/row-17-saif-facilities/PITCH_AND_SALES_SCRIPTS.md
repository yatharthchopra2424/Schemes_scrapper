# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping academic institutions, national laboratories, and industry R&D entities access sophisticated scientific infrastructure without heavy capital investment. Many organizations struggle with the high cost of acquiring and maintaining advanced analytical equipment. I’d like to understand your current research setup and see if the SAIF Facilities program—managed by the Department of Science & Technology—could support your R&D goals through shared access to high-end instruments.

### Discovery Questions
1. What type of research or development activities is your organization currently engaged in?
2. Do you have ongoing projects that require access to specialized analytical instruments or high-end equipment?
3. Are you an academic institution, national laboratory, or industry entity involved in scientific R&D?
4. What is your current process for accessing sophisticated equipment—do you own it, outsource, or collaborate?
5. Have you faced challenges with equipment availability, scheduling, or high user charges when trying to access advanced facilities?
6. Are you looking to reduce capital expenditure on research infrastructure while enhancing your R&D capabilities?
7. Is your organization interested in promoting collaborative R&D through shared facility usage?
8. Do you have a project proposal ready that justifies the need for specific facility access?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Academic institution, national laboratory, or industry entity engaged in scientific R&D
- [ ] Has a defined project proposal requiring facility use
- [ ] Seeking access to sophisticated analytical instruments or high-end equipment
- [ ] Interested in reducing capital expenditure through shared usage
- [ ] Open to collaborative R&D and resource optimization
- [ ] Willing to follow facility access protocols (prior approval, utilization reporting)
- [ ] Prepared to potentially pay nominal user charges (if applicable)
- [ ] Located in India (Pan-India scheme)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like the SAIF Facilities program could be a strong fit—it provides access to DST’s network of sophisticated instruments without requiring you to purchase or maintain them directly. Let me explain how it works and how it could support your R&D objectives.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Research institutions and industry R&D units often face high capital costs when acquiring sophisticated analytical instruments, leading to underutilization, duplication of efforts, and limited access to advanced technology—especially for smaller organizations. This hinders scientific collaboration and slows innovation cycles.

**Scheme intro:**
The SAIF Facilities (Sophisticated Analytical Instrument Facilities) scheme, administered by the Department of Science & Technology (DST), provides access to a pan-India network of high-end analytical instruments and shared research infrastructure. It does not offer direct financial grants or loans but enables users to book and utilize sophisticated equipment across SAIF centers, paying only nominal user charges where applicable.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are engaged in scientific research and development activities (academic institution, national laboratory, or industry)
- You have a specific project proposal justifying the need for facility access
- You seek to reduce capital expenditure through shared usage of high-end equipment
- You are open to collaborative R&D and following institutional access protocols

## 3. Objection Handlers

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I've heard these schemes never get approved" | SAIF Facilities is not a grant scheme—it’s an access platform. Approval is based on project merit and facility availability, not competitive funding. Many institutions and industries routinely access SAIF centers for R&D work—approval is granted when there’s alignment with facility capabilities and project justification. |
| "How much do you charge?" | [TO BE FILLED BY CONSULTANT] |
| "I'll try it myself first" | Common mistakes include applying without a clear project proposal or attempting to use equipment without prior approval from the facility in-charge—both are mandatory under SAIF guidelines. We help ensure your application meets all procedural requirements to avoid delays. |
| "We're too small / too big" | SAIF Facilities serves all sizes—from individual research scholars to large national labs and industry entities. Access is based on project needs and institutional authorization, not revenue or employee thresholds. Whether you're a small startup or a multinational R&D unit, if you need the instrument and have a valid use case, you can apply. |
| "I don't have the documents ready" | The three most commonly missing documents are: (1) a detailed project proposal justifying facility use, (2) an institutional authorization letter, and (3) specifics on intended use of the facility (e.g., instrument type, duration, samples). We help clients prepare these correctly the first time. |
