# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I’m calling from [Your Firm Name], and we specialise in helping private sector enterprises, startups, and industries working in sunrise and strategic sectors—like AI, biotech, quantum, and clean energy—access long-term, innovation-driven financing through the Research Development & Innovation (RDI) Scheme. This scheme, backed by a ₹1 lakh crore corpus from the Department of Science & Technology, is designed to catalyse private sector investment in R&D. Before we explore whether this fits your goals, I’d like to understand your current R&D initiatives and funding challenges to see if we can add value.

### Discovery Questions
1. What stage is your R&D-intensive technology at currently—have you achieved laboratory-level prototypes or beyond (TRL 4 or higher)?  
2. Are you registered in India under Indian laws, with principal place of business/operations in India, and under the control of resident Indian citizens?  
3. Do you retain ownership of IPR developed through your R&D, registered in India under Indian laws/rules?  
4. Is your project focused on any of the RDIF Priority Sectors: energy security and transition, climate action, deep technology (quantum, robotics, space), AI applications in agriculture/health/education, biotech/biomanufacturing/synthetic biology/pharma/medical devices, or digital economy including digital agriculture?  
5. Are you seeking financing for transformative projects at TRL 4 and above, or for acquisition of critical/strategic technologies within a wider product development project?  
6. What proportion of your total project cost are you looking to finance through external sources, and are you prepared to source the remaining 50% from self-financing or commercial sources?  
7. Are you open to financing modes such as low-interest long-term loans (typically unsecured), equity financing (especially for startups), or contributions to Deep-Tech Fund of Funds (FoF)?  
8. Do you have experience complying with GoI directions on security, export control, and other strategic concerns (e.g., SCOMET list)?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Private sector enterprises and startups developing RDI-intensive technologies face high uncertainty, failure risks, and extended gestation periods, making it difficult to secure conventional financing. Investors often lack understanding of scientifically complex projects, leading to underinvestment in innovation critical for India’s economic security, strategic purpose, and self-reliance—especially in sunrise sectors like AI, biotech, quantum, and clean energy.

**Scheme intro:**
The Research Development & Innovation (RDI) Scheme, administered by the Department of Science & Technology (DST) under the Ministry of Science & Technology, is a grant-type scheme providing long-term, innovation-driven financing through low-interest long-term loans (typically unsecured), equity financing (especially for startups), and contributions to Deep-Tech Fund of Funds (FoF). It finances up to 50% of the total assessed project cost, with the remaining 50% to be sourced by the project proponent from self-financing or commercial sources. In exceptional cases or strategic sectors, this limit may be relaxed with EGoS approval. The scheme does not provide grants or short-term loans.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Engaged in developing RDI-intensive technology at Technology Readiness Level 4 and above  
- Registered in India under Indian laws, with principal place of business/operations in India  
- Under control of resident Indian citizens and retains ownership of IPR registered in India  
- Focused on RDIF Priority Sectors such as [specific sector from discovery, e.g., AI applications in health] or other sectors vital for economic security and Atmanirbharta
