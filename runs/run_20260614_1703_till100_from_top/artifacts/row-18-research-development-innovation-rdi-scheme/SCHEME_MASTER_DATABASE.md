### Research Development & Innovation (RDI) Scheme
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department of Science & Technology (DST), Ministry of Science & Technology
- **Portal / Application URL:** www.rdifund.anrf.gov.in
- **Current Status:** Open
- **Window:** Applications must be submitted by 1700 hours IST on 31 January 2026.

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Any legal entity registered in India (including companies under Companies Act, 2013, partnerships under Indian Partnership Act, 1932, LLPs under Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008, and startups as defined in DPIIT Notification G.S.R. 127(E) dated 19 February 2019)
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: RDI-intensive technologies (Technology Readiness Level 4 and above) in sunrise sectors including energy security and transition, climate action, deep technology (quantum computing, robotics, space), AI applications in agriculture/health/education, biotechnology/biomanufacturing/synthetic biology/pharma/medical devices, digital economy including digital agriculture, and other sectors deemed necessary for public interest or strategic self-reliance
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Must be registered in India under Indian laws, under control of resident Indian citizens, retain ownership of IPR registered in India under Indian laws/rules, and comply with GoI directions on security, export control, and other strategic concerns

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Financing up to 50% of the total assessed project cost (with remaining 50% sourced by project proponent); in exceptional cases or strategic sectors, this limit may be relaxed with EGoS approval
- Disbursement mode: Low-interest long-term loans (typically unsecured), equity financing (especially for startups), contributions to Deep-Tech Fund of Funds (FoF) or other ROI-oriented FoFs
- Duration of benefit: Long-term (loan tenor up to 20 years; AIF contribution term up to 15 years inclusive of extension)

#### Documents Required
1. Certificate of Registration for the current Fund from SEBI under Regulation 6 of SEBI (Alternative Investment Funds) Regulations, 2012
2. Names and details of Trustees (if Fund corpus is a Scheme of a Trust)
3. Names and details of Directors, with DIN for Investment Manager/Asset Management Company or LLP
4. GST Registration Certificate and PAN Card for Investment Manager/Asset Management Company or LLP
5. Undertaking that the Applicant has no conflict of interest regarding RDIF decision-making
6. Private Placement Memorandum of Fund submitted to SEBI
7. Details on funds raised so far, supported by signed Contribution Agreements
8. Trustee/Investment Manager Profile Briefs with examples of success in managing teams, leading deals/exits, driving scale-up, emerging technologies, product management, finance/investment, mentoring startups, building processes for performance/risk management, raising third-party capital, deal-sourcing/due diligence, portfolio management, exits, relevant priority sectors in India, TRLs from Level 4 onward, and venture development stages
9. Relevant chapters in Private Placement Memorandum (e.g., Trustee or Investment Manager profiles)
10. Curriculum Vitae/Biodata containing names and experience profiles of Partners, Investment Committee Members, Advisory Board Members, and number of funds with which individual has been associated (with exit details)
11. Number of years Fund Management Team has worked together
12. Manpower Details (Category, Number of personnel, Key expertise/role)
13. Tabulated Portfolio Summary of investees of each Fund (including entry/exit valuations, Net IRR, MOIC, DPIC, Yield on Loans/Portfolio Yield for debt AIFs, Vintage Year Performance)
14. Investment Thesis Note or Private Placement Memorandum showing technological focus, industry sector focus, market focus, technology maturity ranges (TRLs), and venture development stage
15. Documentation of prior investments in RDI-intensive technology firms (with profiles on startup investments) and exits from RDI-intensive firms (with exit route details)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Register on the RDIF Management Portal at www.rdifund.anrf.gov.in.
2. Submit application via the portal’s workflows by the prescribed deadline.
3. Provide information and supporting documents related to Quality criteria in Digital Sealed Cover 1.
4. Provide undertakings related to Cost criteria in Digital Sealed Cover 2.
5. Applications are evaluated using the Quality and Cost Based Selection (QCBS) method with 80% weightage to Quality criteria and 20% to Cost criteria.
6. Quality criteria assess General Competence, R&D and RDI-Intensive Technology Focus, and ‘Crowd In’ of private capital.
7. Cost criteria for AIFs focus on management fees and carried interest; for Loan modality, on net interest margins and management fees.
8. Selected SLFMs must invest RDIF funds only in companies including startups advancing RDI-intensive technologies at TRL 4 and above, working in RDIF Priority Sectors, and in accordance with Funding Guidelines.
