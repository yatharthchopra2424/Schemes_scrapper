# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The Research Development & Innovation (RDI) Scheme is a flagship initiative under the Department of Science and Technology (DST), Ministry of Science & Technology, Government of India. Approved by the Union Cabinet on 1 July 2025 and launched by the Hon’ble Prime Minister at ESTIC 2025 on 3 November 2025, the scheme represents India’s largest investment in research-led innovation with a total outlay of ₹1 lakh crore over six years, including ₹20,000 crore in FY 2025-26. It aims to catalyse private sector investment in R&D, accelerate breakthrough technologies, and advance the national vision of Viksit Bharat 2047. The scheme is implemented as a Special Purpose Fund (SPF) under the Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF) and operates as an Independent Business Unit (IBU).

**Application Portal**: [www.rdifund.anrf.gov.in](https://rdifund.anrf.gov.in)  
**Deadline**: Applications must be submitted by 1700 hours IST on 31 January 2026  
**Last Updated**: 2026  
**Implementing Agency**: Department of Science & Technology (DST), Ministry of Science & Technology  
**Geographic Scope**: Pan-India  
**Scheme Type**: Grant (though financing is via loans/equity, not direct grants)  
**Ministry / Category**: Science & Technology  
**Scheme ID**: row-18  
**Contact Details**:

### Objectives
The RDI Scheme is designed to achieve the following strategic objectives:
- Encourage the private sector to scale up RDI in sunrise domains and in other sectors relevant for economic security, strategic purpose, and self-reliance  
- Finance transformative projects at higher levels of Technology Readiness Level (TRL 4 and above)  
- Support the acquisition of technologies which are critical or of high strategic importance  
- Facilitate the establishment of a dedicated Deep-Tech Fund of Funds  

These objectives are derived from the Implementation Guidelines (Part A, Section 3) and reinforced across multiple evidence sources including the scheme’s homepage, objectives page, and FAQ document.

### Eligibility Matrix
Eligibility applies to **Second Level Fund Managers (SLFMs)** seeking RDIF funding, not the end beneficiaries (Eligible Technology Entities - ETEs). SLFMs must meet criteria to receive RDIF capital, which they then deploy to ETEs.

| **Eligibility Criteria for SLFMs** | **Details** | **Source** |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Entity Type** | Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs), Development Finance Institutions (DFIs), Non-Banking Finance Companies (NBFCs), Focused Research Organisations (FROs) | Implementation Guidelines (Part A, Para 4.2); FAQ Q1, Q9, Q15 |
| **Legal Registration** | Must be registered in India under Indian laws (Companies Act, 2013; Indian Partnership Act, 1932; LLP Act, 2008; etc.) | Implementation Guidelines (Part A, Para 6.1(i)); Glossary (Eligible Technology Entities) |
| **Control & Ownership** | Must be under control of resident Indian citizens; registered global headquarters in India; retain ownership of IPR registered in India | Implementation Guidelines (Part A, Para 6.1(ii), (iii), 6.2); FAQ Q90 |
| **IPR Compliance** | Must register and retain ownership of IPR developed via RDIF funding under Indian law | Implementation Guidelines (Part A, Para 6.2); Glossary (Intellectual Property Rights) |
| **Strategic Compliance** | Must comply with GoI directions on security, export control, and other strategic concerns (including SCOMET list) | Implementation Guidelines (Part A, Para 6.3); Glossary (Control) |
| **Conflict of Interest** | Must provide undertaking of no conflict of interest regarding RDIF decision-making | Required Documents (Item 5); Annexure A.4; FAQ Q11 |
| **Focus Areas** | Must focus on RDI-intensive technologies (TRL 4+) and RDIF Priority Sectors; SLFMs investing in both priority and other sectors may receive reduced RDIF contribution | Implementation Guidelines (Part A, Para 5, 8, 11.2); FAQ Q18, Q26, Q27, Q28, Q29, Q30, Q31 |
| **Government Entities** | Government entities and CPSEs eligible only if partnering with private sector on strategic projects | Implementation Guidelines (Part A, Para 15.2); FAQ Q7, Q90 |
| **Fund Size** | No minimum or maximum fund size restriction for AIFs; eligibility based on quality and cost criteria | FAQ Q2, Q10, Q13, Q16, Q76 |

| **FRO Specific** | FROs must have funded >5 companies/startups in previous 3 years via equity, debt, or grant | FAQ Q17 |

> **Note**: Eligible Technology Entities (ETEs) – the ultimate recipients of SLFM investments – must be:  
> - Legal entities registered in India under applicable laws  
> - Engaged in developing RDI-intensive technology at TRL 4 and above  
> - Under control of resident Indian citizens  
> - Have registered global headquarters in India  
> - Retain ownership of IPR registered in India  
> - Comply with GoI security, export control, and strategic directions  
> *(Source: Implementation Guidelines, Part A, Para 6.1-6.3; Glossary)*

The RDI Scheme provides **long-term, innovation-driven financing** – **not grants or short-term loans**. Financing is routed through SLFMs to ETEs.

> **Blockquote: Critical Financial Constraints**  
> > The scheme does **not** provide grants or short-term loans. Financing is limited to **50%** of total project cost, with the remaining **50%** to be sourced by the project proponent (self or commercial sources), except in exceptional cases or strategic sectors with EGoS approval. RDIF funds must be used **exclusively** to support RDI-intensive technologies (TRL 4 and above) in RDIF Priority Sectors. SLFMs must invest **all** RDIF-contributed funds plus matching private capital only in Eligible Technology Entities focusing on RDI-intensive technologies and RDIF Priority Sectors.  
> > *(Source: Key Facts; Implementation Guidelines, Part A, Para 7.2, 8.1, 11.2; Part B, Para 5, 6, 7, 8.1; FAQ Q49, Q63, Q24)*

### Application Process
The application process for SLFMs is governed by the Notice Inviting Applications (NIA) and follows a Quality and Cost Based Selection (QCBS) method with **80% weightage to Quality** and **20% to Cost**.

#### Step-by-Step Process
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Start: RDIF Releases NIA] --> B[Applicants Submit Bid Documents via Portal]
    B --> C{RDIF Screening: Basic Compliance}
    C -->|Fail| D[Application Rejected]
    C -->|Pass| E[RDIF Prepares Investment Memoranda]
    E --> F[Expert Advisory Committee (EAC) Evaluation]
    F --> G[EAC Recommends SLFMs to Executive Council (EC)]
    G --> H[EC Deliberates & Recommends SLFMs to EGoS]
    H --> I[EGoS Approves Final SLFM List]
    I --> J[RDIF Issues Provisional Letter of Intent (LOI)]
    J --> K[SLFM Executes Contribution/Loan Agreement with Private Investors]
    K --> L[RDIF Finalizes Contribution/Loan Agreement with SLFM]
    L --> M[SLFM Deploys Funds to Eligible Technology Entities (ETEs)]
    M --> N[End: Monitoring & Reporting]
```

#### Detailed Steps & Requirements
1. **Register on RDIF Management Portal**: Access via [www.rdifund.anrf.gov.in](https://rdifund.anrf.gov.in)  
2. **Submit Application via Portal Workflows**: By 1700 hours IST on 31 January 2026 (NIA Modification-Clarification-RDIF-NIA-2026.pdf, Page 2)  
3. **Provide Information in Digital Sealed Cover 1**: Quality criteria documents  
4. **Provide Undertakings in Digital Sealed Cover 2**: Cost criteria documents  
5. **Evaluation Method**: QCBS with 80% Quality, 20% Cost weightage  
   - **Quality Criteria (80%)**:  
     - General Competence (Managing Investments & Operational Risks)  
     - R&D & RDI-Intensive Technology Focus (Orientation & Competence)  
   - **Cost Criteria (20%)**:  
     - For AIFs: Management Fee & Carried Interest (10% each)  
     - For Loan Financing (DFIs/NBFCs/FROs): Net Interest Margin & Management Fee (10% each)  
6. **Selection Timeline**: Expected completion within 8–12 weeks from application closing date (FAQ Q78)  
7. **Post-Selection**:  
   - Provisional LOI issued within reasonable time-period  
   - Upon receipt of SLFM’s Contribution/Loan Agreements with private investors, RDIF finalizes its own agreement  
   - Selected SLFMs must invest **all** RDIF funds + matching private capital exclusively in ETEs advancing RDI-intensive technologies (TRL 4+) in Priority Sectors  
8. **Ongoing Compliance**:  
   - SLFMs must submit 6-monthly and annual reports to RDIF  
   - RDIF monitors investments to ensure ≤50% funding cap per project/funding round is not exceeded  
   - RDIF retains right to audit SLFMs

### Key Caveats & Warnings
> **Blockquote: Scheme-Wide Restrictions**  
> > 1. **No Grants/Short-Term Loans**: RDIF provides **only** long-term financing (loans/equity/FoF).  
> > 2. **50% Funding Cap**: RDIF contribution ≤50% of project cost/funding round; balance from self/commercial sources (exceptions require EGoS approval).  
> > 3. **Exclusive Use**: RDIF funds **must** be used **only** for RDI-intensive technologies (TRL 4+) in RDIF Priority Sectors.  
> > 4. **SLFM Investment Rule**: SLFMs must invest **all** RDIF funds + matching private capital **only** in ETEs meeting RDI-intensive tech & Priority Sector criteria.  
> > 5. **Government/CPSE Eligibility**: Only eligible if partnering with private sector on strategic projects.  
> > 6. **IPR Compliance**: Must register and retain ownership of IPR in India under Indian law.  
> > 7. **Strategic Compliance**: Must adhere to GoI directions on security, export control, and other strategic concerns (SCOMET list).  
> > 8. **No Prescribed Tech/Sector Lists**: Priority sectors and RDI-intensive tech criteria are guiding frameworks; SLFMs use professional judgement within guidelines.  
> > 9. **Continuous Monitoring**: RDIF requires 6-monthly/annual reports, site visits, and joint reviews; retains audit rights.  
> > 10. **Exit Flexibility**: No prescribed exit strategies; SLFMs use professional judgement within funding guidelines.  
> > *(Source: Key Facts; Implementation Guidelines, Part A, Para 7.2, 8.1-8.2, 11.2, 13.2, 15.2-15.3; Part B, Para 5, 6, 7, 8.1-8.3; Part C, Para 3-4, 6.1-6.3, 7-10; FAQ Q24, Q33, Q49, Q63, Q90)*
