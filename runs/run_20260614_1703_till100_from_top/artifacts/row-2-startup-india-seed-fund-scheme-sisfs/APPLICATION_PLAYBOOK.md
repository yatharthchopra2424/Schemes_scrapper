# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Startup is recognized by DPIIT (Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade)
- [ ] Startup is incorporated not more than 2 years ago at the time of application
- [ ] Startup has a business idea to develop a product or service with market fit, viable commercialization, and scope of scaling
- [ ] Startup is using technology in its core product or service, or business model, or distribution model, or methodology to solve the problem being targeted
- [ ] Startup has not received more than Rs 10 lakh of monetary support under any other Central or State Government scheme (excluding prize money from competitions and grand challenges, subsidized working space, founder monthly allowance, access to labs, or access to prototyping facility)
- [ ] Shareholding by Indian promoters in the startup is at least 51% at the time of application to incubator for the scheme (as per Companies Act, 2013 and SEBI (ICDR) Regulations, 2018)
- [ ] Startup applicant has not previously received seed support in the form of grant or debt/convertible debentures under this scheme (each can be availed only once as per guidelines)
- [ ] Startup operates in a sector eligible for preference (social impact, waste management, water management, financial inclusion, education, agriculture, food processing, biotechnology, healthcare, energy, mobility, defence, space, railways, oil and gas, textiles, etc.) – though sector-agnostic, preference is given to these sectors

> **Sources**:  
> - Eligibility criteria from KEY FACTS and supporting evidence (crawled pages: https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/about, https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/faq, PDF: guidelines-for-startup-india-seed-fund-scheme.pdf)  
> - Portal: https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/  
> - Deadline: Last date for submission of startup applications is 31 May 2026 (per KEY FACTS and crawled pages)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration  
- PAN of the entity  
- ITR (Income Tax Returns) for last 2 financial years (if applicable)  
- GST registration certificate  
- Bank statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration (MSME) certificate  
- Aadhaar and PAN of authorized signatory  
- Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) for e-signing forms  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Pitch deck or business description  
- Authorization letter from authorized signatory  
- Details of funding received (if any) – including amount, source, and purpose  
- Board Resolution / Authorization Letter / POA (Power of Attorney) – sample available for download on portal  
- Self-declaration (to be checked during application form submission)  

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be in PDF or JPEG/PNG format  
- File size must not exceed 5 MB per document  
- File names must match the document type (e.g., "Certificate_of_Incorporation.pdf")  
- All certificates must be valid and not expired  
- Documents must be in English or Hindi; if in another language, a certified translation must be attached  
- Scanned copies must be legible, with all details clearly visible  
- Authorization letter and Board Resolution must be on company letterhead and signed by authorized signatory  
- PAN and Aadhaar details must match the entity and signatory records

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- **Not required** as a standalone document. However, the application form requires details equivalent to a DPR:  
  - Entity details  
  - Team profile  
  - Problem statement  
  - Product/service overview  
  - Business model  
  - Customer profile  
  - Market size  
  - Quantum of funds needed  
  - Projected utilization plan for funds  
- If further clarification is needed, **verify with nodal officer** via email: dipp-startups@nic.in  

> **Source**:  
> - Application Process in KEY FACTS: "Fill or upload necessary details as per the fields in the application form (including entity details, team profile, problem statement, product/service overview, business model, customer profile, market size, quantum of funds needed, projected utilization plan for funds, etc.)"  
> - Crawled page: https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/faq – "What all can I use the seed fund for?" and application steps  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Login using Startup India credentials** – Ensure you are DPIIT-recognized; if not, complete recognition first via https://www.startupindia.gov.in/content/sih/en/startupgov/startup_recognition_page.html  
2. **Navigate to https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/** and click "Login"  
3. **Click 'Apply Now'** – Do not proceed if not logged in  
4. **Fill all mandatory fields** – Entity details, team profile, problem statement, product/service overview, business model, customer profile, market size, quantum of funds needed, projected utilization plan  
5. **Recheck Entity Details page** – These are auto-populated for DPIIT-recognized startups and cannot be edited; verify accuracy before clicking "Save and Next"  
6. **Select up to 3 incubator preferences** – In order of preference; once submitted, this order **cannot be changed**  
7. **Select funding instrument** – Choose Grant, Debt, or Debenture under funding details to view relevant incubator preference list  
8. **Check the Self Declaration box** – Confirm compliance with eligibility criteria before final submission  
9. **Submit application** – After submission, track progress via Startup India portal dashboard using login credentials  
10. **If rejected, wait 6 weeks** – Re-application is allowed only after 6 weeks from rejection notification  

> **Source**:  
> - Application Process in KEY FACTS (steps 1–12)  
> - Crawled FAQ: "How can I apply to SISFS?", "Is the SISFS application process completely online?", "I had filled an order of preference for incubators while applying. Can I change this preference order after application?"  
> - Crawled page: https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/faq  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All application form fields are complete and accurate  
- [ ] Entity details match Certificate of Incorporation and PAN  
- [ ] Team profile includes all founders and key members with roles  
- [ ] Problem statement clearly defines market gap and real-world problem  
- [ ] Product/service overview includes technology use case  
- [ ] Business model is scalable and viable  
- [ ] Market size is quantified and realistic  
- [ ] Quantum of funds requested aligns with eligibility (≤ Rs. 20 Lakh grant or ≤ Rs. 50 Lakh investment)  
- [ ] Projected utilization plan is milestone-based and tied to fund tranches  
- [ ] Incubator preferences are selected in order (max 3) and funding instrument chosen  
- [ ] Self-declaration box is checked  
- [ ] All scheme-specific documents are attached and meet quality checks  
- [ ] No fees are being charged to the startup (as per scheme guidelines)  
- [ ] Application is submitted before 31 May 2026

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a **screenshot of the confirmation page** showing "Application submitted successfully" with timestamp  
- Store the **application reference number** displayed after submission  
- Save a copy of the submitted application (PDF download available on portal after submission)  
- Set calendar reminders for:  
  - Follow-up at 15 days (to check for interim update request)  
  - Follow-up at 30 days (if no status update)  
  - Follow-up at 45 days (ISMC decision timeline)  
- Log all communication in case file

- **Level 1**: Portal helpdesk – Contact via the grievance form: https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/contact (select "Login Issue" or "Other Technical Issue")

- **Level 3**: RTI filing – If no response within statutory SLA (typically 30 days for government queries), file an RTI application via https://rtionline.gov.in/

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify approval letter from incubator via Startup India portal dashboard  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned amount (grant: up to Rs. 20 Lakh; investment: up to Rs. 50 Lakh via convertible debentures/debt)  
- [ ] Sign legal agreement with incubator before first installment release (mandatory as per guidelines)  
- [ ] Ensure funds are disbursed to the startup’s company bank account (not personal account)  
- [ ] For grants:  
  - Submit interim progress update and utilization certificate to trigger subsequent installments  
  - Progress reporting required at least once every 15 days via videoconference or physical meeting  
  - Updates must be shared on the scheme dashboard at least monthly  
- [ ] For debt/convertible debentures:  
  - Adhere to interest rate (not more than prevailing repo rate)  
  - Tenure fixed at sanctioning (not more than 60 months)  
  - Moratorium of up to 12 months may be availed  
- [ ] Prepare for final submission:  
  - Final report  
  - Audited utilization certificate  
- [ ] Track utilization for success fee invoicing (if applicable per firm policy)  
- [ ] Ensure seed fund is **not used for creation of any facilities** (strict prohibition)  
- [ ] Monitor progress indicators:  
  - Progress of proof of concept, prototype development, product development, field trials, market launch  
  - Quantum of external funding raised (angel, VC, loans)  
  - Jobs created  
  - Turnover of startup  

> **Source**:  
> - Benefits and disbursement guidelines in KEY FACTS  
> - FAQ: "How often do I have to report back my progress after receiving seed fund?" – "Each startup will be required to touch base with the incubator team and share updates with them at least once in 15 days via videoconferences or physical meetings. These updates should be shared on the scheme dashboard at least on a monthly basis."
