# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I specialize in helping early-stage, DPIIT-recognized startups access government seed funding to validate their ideas and scale without dilution. The Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS) is designed specifically for startups like yours—innovative, tech-driven, and incorporated within the last 2 years—to provide non-dilutive grants and soft loans for proof of concept, prototype development, and market entry. I’d love to learn about your venture and see if this could be a fit.

### Discovery Questions
1. When was your startup incorporated, and do you have your DPIIT recognition certificate handy?  
2. What stage is your startup at—are you validating a proof of concept, building a prototype, or ready for market entry and scaling?  
3. Is your core product or service technology-driven, either in the product itself, business model, or distribution method?  
4. Have you received more than ₹10 lakh in monetary support from any other Central or State Government scheme (excluding prize money, subsidized workspace, or lab access)?  
5. What is the current shareholding pattern—do Indian promoters hold at least 51% of equity?  
6. Which sector are you operating in? (We prioritize social impact, waste/water management, financial inclusion, education, agriculture, food processing, biotech, healthcare, energy, mobility, defence, space, railways, oil & gas, textiles, etc.)  
7. How much funding are you seeking, and for what specific use—grant for validation or debt for commercialization/scaling?  
8. Do you have a clear utilization plan and milestones for how the funds will be deployed over time?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] DPIIT-recognized startup  
- [ ] Incorporated not more than 2 years ago  
- [ ] Business idea with market fit, viable commercialization, and scope of scaling  
- [ ] Uses technology in core product/service, business model, or distribution model  
- [ ] Not received >₹10 lakh from other Central/State Govt schemes (excl. prize money, subsidized space, founder allowance, lab/prototyping access)  
- [ ] Indian promoter shareholding ≥51%  
- [ ] Has not previously availed seed support under SISFS for grant or debt/convertible debenture  
- [ ] Seeking funding for PoC/prototype/product trials (grant) OR market entry/commercialization/scaling (debt)  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, it sounds like you’re aligned with the Startup India Seed Fund Scheme’s goals—especially given your DPIIT recognition, incorporation timeline, and tech-driven approach. The SISFS offers up to ₹20 lakhs as a grant for validation activities or up to ₹50 lakhs via convertible debentures for scaling—both disbursed through approved incubators. Let me walk you through how it works and whether it’s the right next step for you.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**  
Early-stage startups struggle to access capital during the proof of concept and prototype phase, as angel investors and VCs typically wait for validation, and banks require asset-backed collateral. This gap causes many viable ideas to stall before they can demonstrate market fit or scalability.

**Scheme intro:**  
The Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS), launched by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT), Ministry of Commerce and Industry, provides financial assistance of up to ₹20 lakhs as a grant for validation of proof of concept, prototype development, or product trials, and up to ₹50 lakhs via convertible debentures or debt instruments for market entry, commercialization, or scaling. The grant is disbursed in milestone-based installments, and debt carries interest not exceeding the prevailing repo rate, with a tenure of up to 60 months and a possible 12-month moratorium. Funds are transferred directly to the startup’s company bank account.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**  
- You are a DPIIT-recognized startup incorporated within the last 2 years  
- Your venture is technology-driven and targets a scalable solution with market fit  
- Indian promoters hold at least 51% equity, meeting the ownership criterion  
- You have not exceeded ₹10 lakh in prior Central/State Govt monetary support (excl. exemptions)
