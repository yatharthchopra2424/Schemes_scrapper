# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Overview
The **Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS)** is a pan-India grant and debt/convertible debenture scheme implemented by the **Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT)**, Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of India. Launched on 19th April 2021 by Hon’ble Shri Piyush Goyal, the scheme has a total fund size of **INR 945 Crore** and aims to support **3,600 entrepreneurs** through **300 incubators** over four years (2021–2025). The scheme provides financial assistance to DPIIT-recognized startups incorporated not more than 2 years ago at the time of application.

**Application Portal:** https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/  
**Status / Deadlines:**  
- Last date for submission of startup applications: **31 May 2026**  
- Incubators to complete 100% startup selection by: **30 June 2026**  
**Last Updated:** 2024  
**Confidence:** High  

### Objectives
The scheme aims to:
- Provide financial assistance to startups for proof of concept, prototype development, product trials, market entry, and commercialization  
- Enable startups to graduate to a level where they can raise investments from angel investors/venture capitalists or seek loans from commercial banks/financial institutions  
- Address capital inadequacy in the seed and 'Proof of Concept' development stage for startups with good business ideas  
- Have a multiplier effect in validation of business ideas leading to employment generation  
- Support startups through eligible incubators across India with disbursement of seed fund  
- Evaluate and select incubators for allotment of Seed Funds via Experts Advisory Committee (EAC)  
- Monitor progress and ensure efficient utilization of funds towards fulfilment of scheme objectives

> **> WARNING:**  
> - Application once filled and submitted is **final**; incubator preference order **cannot be changed** later.  
> - Startups **must not** use seed fund for creation of any facilities (strictly prohibited).  
> - Grants are **non-refundable**; debt/convertible debentures **must be repaid** per terms.  
> - Incubators must **not** disburse seed fund to incubatees using funding from any third-party private entity.  
> - Shareholding by Indian promoters **must be ≥51%** at time of application to incubator.  
> - A startup will **not receive seed support more than once each** for grant and debt/convertible debenture.

> A startup can receive **both** grant (up to ₹20L) and debt/convertible debenture (up to ₹50L) **once each**, for a **maximum total support of ₹70 Lakhs** under the scheme.

### Application Process (Mermaid Flowchart)
```mermaid
flowchart TD
    A[Start: Ensure DPIIT Recognition] --> B{Already DPIIT Recognized?}
    B -->|No| C[Apply for DPIIT Recognition via https://www.startupindia.gov.in/content/sih/en/startupgov/startup_recognition_page.html]
    B -->|Yes| D[Navigate to SISFS Portal: https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/]
    C --> D
    D --> E[Click 'Login' and enter Startup India credentials]
    E --> F[Click 'Apply Now']
    F --> G[Fill/upload: Entity details, team profile, problem statement, product/service overview, business model, customer profile, market size, quantum of funds needed, projected utilization plan]
    G --> H[Recheck Entity Details page and click 'Save and Next']
    H --> I[Select up to 3 incubator preferences in order + funding instrument (Grant/Debt/Debenture) to view incubator list]
    I --> J[Click 'Save and Next']
    J --> K[Check Self Declaration box and click 'Submit']
    K --> L[Application submitted successfully]
    L --> M[Track application status on Startup India portal dashboard using login credentials]
    M --> N{Application Status}
    N -->|Approved by Incubator| O[Incubator executes legal agreement with startup]
    N -->|Rejected| P[May reapply after 6 weeks]
    O --> Q[Disbursement of first installment: Grant ≤60 days from application receipt; Debt similar timeline subject to due diligence]
    Q --> R[Submit interim progress update & utilization certificate every 15 days (VC/physical) + monthly on dashboard]
    R --> S[Subsequent installments released upon milestone achievement]
    S --> T[Final report & audited utilization certificate required at project completion]
    T --> U[End: Funds received in company bank account]
```

> **> IMPORTANT NOTES ON APPLICATION:**  
> - Application is **completely online**; no physical document submission required.  
> - **No application fees** charged by scheme or incubators.  
> - Startup **shall not be charged any fees** by incubator for selection, disbursement, incubation, or monitoring.  
> - Applicants can apply to **up to 3 incubators** in order of preference; funding disbursed by highest-preference incubator that selects them.  
> - Incubator Seed Management Committee (ISMC) evaluates applications using criteria: Need for Idea (p), Feasibility (q), Potential Impact (r), Novelty (s), Team (t), Fund Utilization Plan (u), Additional Parameters (v), Presentation (w). Weightages (p,q,r,s,t,u,v,w) assigned differently by each incubator (sum = 100%).  
> - ISMC must evaluate and select startups **within 45 days** of application receipt.  
> - All applicants can track application status **real-time** on Startup India portal.  
> - Rejected applicants notified via email and may reapply after **6 weeks**.  

### Fund Flow & Disbursement Mechanics
- **Grant Disbursement:** Milestone-based; first tranche ≤60 days post-application; subsequent tranches require interim progress report + utilization certificate.  
- **Debt/Convertible Debenture Disbursement:** Interest ≤ prevailing repo rate; tenure ≤60 months; moratorium ≤12 months; unsecured.  
- **Funds Disbursed To:** Startup’s **company bank account** (not personal account).  
- **Reporting Requirements:**  
  - Progress reporting: **at least once every 15 days** via videoconference or physical meeting.  
  - Dashboard updates: **at least monthly**.  
  - Interim progress reports + utilization certificates required to trigger subsequent grant installments.  
  - Final report + audited utilization certificate required at project completion.  
- **Incubator Obligations:**  
  - Maintain exclusive, project-specific Trust and Retention Account (TRA) with nationalized bank.  
  - Use grant **only** for disbursal to eligible startups; **not** for facility creation or other expenses.  
  - Management fee (5%) used for administrative expenditure, selection/due diligence, progress monitoring.  
  - Report funds sanctioned/received/disbursed per startup per financial year.  
  - Submit detailed utilization and audited expenditure reports per financial year.  

### Key Caveats & Conditions
- Seed fund **shall not be used** for creation of any facilities.  
- Grant is **non-refundable**; debt/convertible debentures **must be repaid** per agreed terms (interest ≤ repo rate, tenure ≤5 years, moratorium ≤12 months).  
- Startup must **not have received >Rs. 10 lakh** from other Central/State Govt schemes (exclusions apply).  
- Indian promoter shareholding **must be ≥51%** at time of application.  
- Startup can avail grant and debt/convertible debenture **each only once**.  
- Incubator must **not** disburse seed fund using third-party private funding.  
- Incubator eligibility: If not govt-assisted, must be operational ≥3 years, have ≥10 startups under physical incubation, and present audited annual reports for last 2 years.  
- Application **once submitted is final**; incubator preference order **cannot be altered**.  
- Grievance redressal: Email `dipp-startups@nic.in`, incubator queries to `sisfs@investindia.org.in`, or use grievance form at https://seedfund.startupindia.gov.in/contact.
