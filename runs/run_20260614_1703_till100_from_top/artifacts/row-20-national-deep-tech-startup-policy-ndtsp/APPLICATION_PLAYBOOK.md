# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] The entity is incorporated or registered in India as a private limited company (Companies Act, 2013), partnership firm (Partnership Act, 1932), limited liability partnership (LLP Act, 2008), Multi-State Cooperative Society (Multi-State Cooperative Societies Act, 2002), or Cooperative Society under any State/UT Act.
- [ ] The entity is within a period of twenty years from the date of incorporation or registration (extended for Deep Tech Startups).
- [ ] The entity has a turnover for any financial year since incorporation not exceeding three hundred crore rupees (₹300 Cr).
- [ ] The entity is working towards innovation, development, or improvement of products or processes or services, or is a scalable business model with high potential for employment generation or wealth creation.
- [ ] The entity is working on producing a solution based on new knowledge/advancements within a scientific or engineering discipline or multiple disciplines, which is yet to be developed or is in the process of being developed.
- [ ] The entity has a high percentage of expenditure on research and development (R&D) activities as a percentage of revenue/funding.
- [ ] The entity owns or is in the process of creating significant novel intellectual property (IP) and is taking steps to commercialize the same.
- [ ] The entity is facing extended development timelines, long gestation periods, high capital and infrastructure requirements, and carries large technical or scientific uncertainty.
- [ ] The entity is not formed by splitting up or reconstruction of an existing business.

**Sources:**  
- Gazette Notification G.S.R. 108(E) dated February 4, 2026 (Deep Tech Startup Definition)  
- Gazette Notification S.O. 1860(E) dated April 13, 2026 (Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0)  
- NDTSP Key Facts (Eligibility section)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation/Registration  
- PAN Card  
- GST Registration Certificate  
- Audited Financial Statements (last 3 years)  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration Certificate  
- ITR (last 3 years)  
- Memorandum of Association / LLP Agreement / Partnership Deed  
- Board Resolution (for application authorization)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Write-up on nature of business highlighting innovation, development, or improvement of products/services, scalability, employment generation, or wealth creation  
- Supporting documents demonstrating innovation, wealth creation, and employment generation (e.g., product/service descriptions, IPR filings, user/revenue growth metrics, awards, funding details, employment data)  
- Documents proving R&D expenditure as a high percentage of revenue/funding  
- Evidence of novel IP creation or ownership (patent filings, copyrights, trade secrets)  
- Steps taken toward commercialization of IP  
- Details of extended development timelines, gestation periods, capital/infrastructure requirements  
- Technical/scientific uncertainty assessment  
- Declaration that entity is not formed by splitting/reconstruction of existing business

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format  
- File size per document: < 5 MB  
- File names must match document type (e.g., "PAN_Card.pdf", "Audited_FY2023.pdf")  
- All certificates must be valid and not expired  
- Declarations and undertakings must be signed and dated  
- Documents in regional languages must be accompanied by certified English/Hindi translation  
- Audited financial statements must be certified by a Chartered Accountant  
- IP documents must reflect filing/status with appropriate authority (CGPDTM, etc.)

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not explicitly required for NDTSP recognition or FoF access. However, for fund disbursement under the Deep Tech FoF, a detailed project report may be sought by the Alternative Investment Fund (AIF).  
**Action:** Verify with the nodal officer or AIF during due diligence.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Use the **DPIIT Startup India portal** (https://www.startupindia.gov.in) for Section 80-IAC recognition.  
2. Select “Apply for Recognition as Startup” and indicate interest in Deep Tech Startup benefits.  
3. Fill **Form-1** (as per Gazette Notification) with:  
   - Startup name, date of incorporation, registration number  
   - Address and business location  
   - Nature of business (emphasize deep tech: scientific/engineering novelty, R&D intensity, IP, long gestation)  
   - Contact details (phone, email)  
   - Income Tax PAN  
   - Existing/proposed activities  
   - Copy of MoA/LLP deed/Board resolution/registration details  
   - Annual audited accounts (last 3 years, if applicable)  
   - Supporting documents for innovation, wealth creation, employment generation  
4. Upload all scheme-specific documents as per Stage 2.  
5. In the declaration, self-certify that information is true and no relevant information is concealed.  
6. Authorized signatory must sign and date the form.  
7. Double-check that turnover does not exceed ₹300 Cr in any financial year.  
8. Confirm entity is not formed by splitting/reconstruction of existing business.  
9. Save application reference number upon submission.

**Portal Notes:**  
- The DPIIT portal is the single window for startup recognition.  
- Deep Tech Startup recognition follows the same process but requires additional evidence per Gazette Notification G.S.R. 108(E).  
- Recognition enables access to Fund of Funds, tax benefits, and other incentives.

- [ ] Declaration signed and dated by authorized signatory

- [ ] All file names standardized and sizes < 5 MB

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit application via https://www.startupindia.gov.in  
- Take screenshot of confirmation page showing **Application Reference Number**  
- Save reference number in case file and notify client  
- Set follow-up reminders:  
  - Day 25: Check application status  
  - Day 30: Escalate if no update  
  - Day 45: Final escalation if no response

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify approval letter/certificate for Section 80-IAC recognition  
- [ ] Confirm recognition includes Deep Tech Startup eligibility (check for explicit mention or FoF segment access)  
- [ ] Verify sanctioned benefits: access to Deep Tech Fund of Funds (FoF), regulatory sandboxes, IP support, talent pool  
- [ ] Initiate engagement with Alternative Investment Funds (AIFs) under Startup India FoF 2.0 (Segment 1: Deep Tech)  
- [ ] Prepare utilization plan for funds as per AIF requirements  
- [ ] Track fund disbursement in tranches based on milestone progress  
- [ ] Maintain records for utilization certificate submission  
- [ ] Plan for success fee invoicing upon fund closure or exit (as per consultancy agreement)  
- [ ] Monitor compliance with FoF operational guidelines (segment-specific flexibilities, investment moderation, etc.)  
- [ ] Annual reporting to AIF and PSA on progress, IP development, and commercialization milestones  

**Sources:**  
- Gazette Notification S.O. 1860(E) dated April 13, 2026 (Startup India Fund of Funds 2.0 – segmentation, operational guidelines)  
- Gazette Notification G.S.R. 108(E) dated February 4, 2026 (Deep Tech Startup Definition)  
- NDTSP Key Facts (Benefits, Objectives, Implementing Agency)  
- PSA Directory (for Scientific Secretary contact)  
- Startup India Portal (https://www.startupindia.gov.in) – implied as application gateway for recognition and FoF access
