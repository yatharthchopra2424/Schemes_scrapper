# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] The applicant is a startup recognized by DPIIT or a research institution, student, government agency, or industry entity.
- [ ] The applicant operates within India (Pan-India geographic scope).
- [ ] The applicant’s proposed activity aligns with one or more pillars of the IndiaAI Mission: National Center on AI, Data Management Office, Skilling in AI, or Responsible AI governance.
- [ ] The applicant seeks access to AI research and development facilities, data infrastructure, skill development programs, or responsible AI frameworks.
- [ ] The applicant is not applying for a direct financial grant or subsidy, as the IndiaAI Mission does not provide direct financial support to individual beneficiaries based on the evidence.
- [ ] The applicant understands that benefits are non-financial and include access to infrastructure, training, research collaboration, and governance frameworks.

> **Note**: The IndiaAI Mission is an ongoing national program with no explicit eligibility criteria, turnover thresholds, employee counts, or age-of-business requirements detailed in the evidence. Eligibility is inferred from alignment with program pillars and target beneficiary categories.

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration (for startups, agencies, or institutions)
- PAN Card of the entity or authorized signatory
- Latest Income Tax Return (ITR) – if applicable
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)
- Bank Statement (last 3 months)
- Udyam Registration (for MSMEs/startups)
- Aadhaar and PAN of authorized signatory
- Board Resolution authorizing application (for companies/institutions)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Project proposal outlining alignment with IndiaAI Mission pillars (National Center on AI, Data Management Office, Skilling in AI, Responsible AI)
- Detailed work plan showing intended use of AI infrastructure, data access, skilling programs, or governance frameworks
- Letter of intent or endorsement from a collaborating AI research institution, government body, or industry partner (if applicable)
- CVs of key personnel involved in the AI initiative
- For skilling beneficiaries: educational qualifications or proof of enrollment (students/researchers)
- For startups: pitch deck or prototype description related to AI innovation
- For government agencies: official letter requesting access to AI resources under the mission

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (max file size: 5 MB per document)
- Names on documents must match exactly as per PAN and Aadhaar
- Certificates must be valid and not expired (where applicable)
- Documents must be in English or accompanied by a certified Hindi/English translation
- Undertakings and declarations must be signed and dated
- Project proposals must include clear objectives, methodology, and expected outcomes

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
- A formal Detailed Project Report (DPR) is **not explicitly required** for the IndiaAI Mission based on the evidence.
- However, a structured **project proposal** is essential to demonstrate alignment with the mission’s pillars.
- If submitting to a nodal officer or seeking formal collaboration, include:  
  **Executive Summary, Objectives, Methodology, Expected Outcomes, Budget (if self-funded), Timeline, Team Details, and Alignment with IndiaAI Pillars**.
- **Verify with the nodal officer** whether a DPR is needed for specific benefit access (e.g., using National Center on AI facilities).

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the initiative page: https://digitalindia.gov.in/initiative/national-program-on-artificial-intelligence/
2. Review the four pillars: National Center on AI, Data Management Office, Skilling in AI, Responsible AI.
3. Clearly map your proposal to one or more pillars in the project description.
4. Do not enter financial figures in “grant amount” fields — the mission does not provide direct subsidies.
5. Use precise terminology: “seeking access to,” “collaborating with,” “utilizing infrastructure of.”
6. Attach all scheme-specific documents as PDFs labeled clearly.
7. Ensure authorized signatory completes and signs the declaration section.
8. Preview the form before final submission to avoid errors.

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents are attached and correctly named
- [ ] Project proposal clearly links to at least one IndiaAI Mission pillar
- [ ] No claims of direct financial support are made (in line with evidence)
- [ ] Authorized signatory has signed all declarations
- [ ] Contact details are accurate and functional
- [ ] No duplicate or blank fields in the form
- [ ] Screenshot of completed form taken for records

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the application via the initiative page: https://digitalindia.gov.in/initiative/national-program-on-artificial-intelligence/
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation screen showing submission timestamp and reference number (if generated)
- Save the reference number in the case file with label: `IndiaAI_Mission_Submission_[Case ID]_[Timestamp]`
- Set an internal reminder to follow up after 15 working days if no acknowledgment is received

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and save the approval letter or access authorization from MeitY or its designated agency
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned benefits (e.g., access duration to National Center on AI, enrollment in skilling program, data access rights)
- [ ] Onboard the client to the approved resource or program (e.g., schedule lab access, enroll in AI course)
- [ ] Monitor utilization and maintain logs of resource usage or training completion
- [ ] Assist in preparing utilization certificate or progress report if required by MeitY
- [ ] Track milestone achievement and client outcomes (e.g., prototype developed, skill certified, paper published)
- [ ] Invoice success fee as per engagement agreement upon confirmation of benefit access
- [ ] Maintain records for audit and future reference

> **Note**: Since the IndiaAI Mission does not disburse direct financial aid, “sanctioned amount” refers to the value of access granted (e.g., hours of compute, training slots). Utilization certificates may be in the form of progress reports or completion certificates issued by MeitY or its partners.
