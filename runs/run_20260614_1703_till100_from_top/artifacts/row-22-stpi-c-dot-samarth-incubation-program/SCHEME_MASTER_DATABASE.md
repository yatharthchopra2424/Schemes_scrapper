### STPI + C-DOT Samarth Incubation Program
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Incubation
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Software Technology Parks of India (STPI), Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://stpi.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Always Open

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Startups and entrepreneurs
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Emerging technologies such as AI, IoT, Blockchain, FinTech, MedTech, AgriTech, Cybersecurity, AR/VR, and other domain-specific areas
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Must be engaged in technology-driven product development and demonstrate potential for scalability and market impact; open to innovators from Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Seed funding support facilitated through linkages with venture capitalists, angel investors, and government funding schemes (specific quantum not guaranteed)
- Disbursement mode: Not directly granted by STPI in all cases; facilitated through funding partners
- Duration of benefit: Not specified (incubation-based support)

#### Documents Required
1. Business plan or project proposal
2. Team details and resumes
3. Proof of concept or prototype (if available)
4. Certificate of Incorporation or registration (for registered entities)
5. PAN card of the entity or promoters
6. Bank account details
7. Any existing funding or investment details
8. Intellectual property details (if applicable)

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Identify the relevant Centre of Entrepreneurship (CoE) based on your technology domain and location.
2. Visit the STPI website or contact the respective CoE for program details and application guidelines.
3. Submit an online application through the STPI portal or designated application channel.
4. Provide necessary documents including business plan, team details, and proof of innovation.
5. Undergo screening and evaluation by the selection committee.
6. If shortlisted, participate in interviews or pitch sessions.
7. Receive selection notification and complete onboarding formalities.
8. Begin incubation with access to infrastructure, mentorship, and support services.
