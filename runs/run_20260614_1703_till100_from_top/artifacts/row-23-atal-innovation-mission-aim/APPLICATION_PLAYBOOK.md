# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- ✅ **Registration Type**: School must be recognized by Ministry of Education (MoE) and managed by State/Union Territory/Central Government, Local body (Municipality/Nagar Nigam), Private Trusts/Society or Tribal/Social welfare Department  
- ✅ **Grade Level**: School must have at least Grade/Class VI to X  
- ✅ **Infrastructure**:  
  - All weather area (1,500 sq. ft.) in regular/plain areas  
  - All weather area (1,000 sq. ft.) in Hilly/Himalayan and Island states/UTs  
  - Computer Lab with internet facility  
  - Steady electricity connection  
  - Science Lab (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)  
  - Library and Playground  
- ✅ **Faculty**: Dedicated & qualified staff: Mathematics, Sciences and Computer teachers  
- ✅ **Reach**:  
  - Enrolment – Min. 400 students in Class/Grade VI – X  
  - Enrolment – Min. 250 students in Class/Grade VI – X in hilly states  
  - Regular attendance of 75% & above of the staff & enrolled students over the past 3 years  

**Source**: ATL Selection Criteria from ATL Guidebook (Section 3.1)  
**Portal**: https://aim.gov.in/atl.php

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- School registration certificate  
- PAN card of school/institution  
- ITR of last 3 years (if applicable)  
- GST registration (if applicable)  
- Bank statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam registration (if applicable)  
- Audited financial statements  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Declaration form by the school Principal on school letterhead  
- Memorandum of Agreement (MoA)  
- Bond (applicable for non-governmental schools only)  
- Bank passbook detail  
- ATL lab layout  
- PFMS registration proof  
- GeM ID and Government Email ID (to be obtained during process)  

**Source**: ATL Compliance Process (Section 3.2) from ATL Guidebook  

### Document Quality Check
- File size: Under 5 MB per document  
- File naming: Clear and consistent (e.g., `SchoolName_Declaration.pdf`)  
- All documents must be legible scans or certified copies  
- Language: English or Hindi; vernacular languages require certified translation  
- Declarations and MoA must be signed and stamped by authorized signatory  
- Bank documents must show active account in school’s name  

### Client Communication Template
**Subject**: Application Invitation for Atal Tinkering Labs (ATL) – AIM Grant-in-Aid Opportunity  

Dear [Client Name],  

This is to inform you about the Atal Tinkering Labs (ATL) scheme under the Atal Innovation Mission (AIM), NITI Aayog, Government of India. The scheme provides a grant-in-aid of up to ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for establishment and ₹10 lakhs for operational & maintenance over 5 years) to eligible schools for setting up innovation labs.  

**Eligibility Highlights**:  
- Schools with Grades VI to X recognized by MoE  
- Minimum 1,000–1,500 sq. ft. space (based on location)  
- Dedicated science, math, and computer faculty  
- Minimum enrolment of 400 students (250 in hilly areas)

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not required for ATL application. However, schools must prepare a brief project proposal as part of the online application form covering:  
- Vision for the ATL  
- Planned activities and student engagement  
- Integration with school curriculum  
- Community outreach plans  
- Sustainability model post-grant period  

**Note**: Verify with nodal officer if any additional project report is required during specific tranches.  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official ATL application portal only when announced open on aim.gov.in  
2. Register school details accurately (name, address, UDISE code, contact info)  
3. Fill in infrastructure details exactly as per eligibility criteria (no approximations)  
4. Upload all documents in PDF format, clearly labeled  
5. Ensure Principal’s declaration is on official letterhead with wet signature and stamp  
6. Double-check bank details for PFMS compliance (account must be in school’s name)  
7. Submit only after reviewing all fields; incomplete applications are rejected outright  
8. Save application reference number immediately upon submission  

**Portal-Specific Note**: The ATL online application portal is a seamless platform developed for schools to submit applications (ATL Guidebook, Section 3.2).  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All eligibility criteria met and verified  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents collected and quality-checked  
- [ ] Application form filled completely with accurate data  
- [ ] Declarations signed and stamped  
- [ ] Documents uploaded in correct format and size  
- [ ] Application reference number saved and shared with client  
- [ ] Follow-up reminder set for 7 days post-submission

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take screenshot of submission confirmation page showing application reference number  
- Store reference number securely in case file  
- Set automated follow-up reminders for:  
  - 7 days post-submission (to check status)  
  - 30 days post-submission (to escalate if no update)  
  - Every 15 days thereafter until response received

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify approval letter from AIM/NITI Aayog  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned amount (₹10 lakhs for Tranche 1)  
- [ ] Activate GeM ID and Government Email ID as per AIM initiation  
- [ ] Procure ATL equipment through GeM portal within 3 months of fund receipt  
- [ ] Set up ATL space per design and layout guidelines  
- [ ] Constitute ATL Advisory Board  
- [ ] Conduct Initial Orientation Workshop for ATL In-charge  
- [ ] Update monthly activities on MyATL Dashboard (https://atl.aim.gov.in/)  
- [ ] Submit Utilization Certificate (UC) in GFR-12A format at end of each financial year  
- [ ] Track milestone for subsequent tranche release (based on performance and UC submission)  
- [ ] Plan for invoice of success fee as per consultancy agreement
