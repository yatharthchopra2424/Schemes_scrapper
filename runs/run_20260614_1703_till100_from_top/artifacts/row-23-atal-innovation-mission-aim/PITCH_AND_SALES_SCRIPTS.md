# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon! I’m reaching out because Atal Innovation Mission (AIM), NITI Aayog’s flagship initiative, is actively supporting innovation and entrepreneurship across India—from school-level Atal Tinkering Labs to startup funding up to INR 1 crore through programs like the Atal New India Challenge. I’d love to understand your organization’s innovation goals and see if any of AIM’s programs align with your needs.

### Discovery Questions
1. What type of organization are you (school, university, startup, MSME, NGO, or corporate), and what is your primary focus area (e.g., education, technology, social impact)?
2. Are you looking to establish innovation infrastructure (like a lab or incubator), seek funding for a prototype, or access mentorship and ecosystem support?
3. For schools: Do you have grades 6–12, a minimum of 400 students (or 250 in hilly areas), and dedicated science/computer labs?
4. For startups/MSMEs: Is your entity DPIIT-recognized, with majority Indian stakeholding, and do you have a working prototype of your solution?
5. What is your timeline for launching or scaling an innovation initiative—are you planning within the next 3–6 months?
6. Do you have access to matching funds if applying for programs like Atal Community Innovation Centres (ACIC), which require equal or greater partner funding?
7. Are your founders or team members within the age brackets for youth-focused programs (e.g., 15–29 years, extended to 35 for women, PwDs, LGBTIQA+, tribal, or minority entrepreneurs)?
8. What specific outcomes are you aiming for—skill development, job creation, prototype validation, market access, or grant funding?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] School with grades VI–XII, minimum 400 enrolment (250 in hilly states), science lab, computer lab with internet, steady electricity, library/playground  
- [ ] Startup/MSME: DPIIT-recognized, >51% Indian stakeholding, working prototype  
- [ ] ACIC applicant: From underserved region, with partner providing equal/greater matching funds  
- [ ] Youth Co:Lab applicant: Founder aged 15–29 (or up to 35 for women, PwDs, LGBTIQA+, tribal, minority)  
- [ ] ANIC applicant: Prototype-stage innovation solving national sectoral challenges  
- [ ] AIM-PRIME applicant: Science-based deep tech idea, technology developer or incubation manager  
- [ ] ATL/AIC/ACIC: Institution eligible to apply (school/university/corporate/NGO)  
- [ ] Willing to submit Utilization Certificates and progress reports post-grant  

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it sounds like [Atal Innovation Mission’s Atal Tinkering Lab / Atal Incubation Centre / Atal New India Challenge / Youth Co:Lab] could be a strong fit—offering grant-in-aid of up to INR 1 crore, mentorship from 6200+ Mentors of Change, and access to innovation infrastructure. Let me walk you through how it works and why it’s relevant to your goals.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Schools struggle to provide hands-on innovation education due to lack of funding, equipment, and structured curriculum; startups face the "Valley of Death" in prototyping and commercialization due to limited access to seed funding, mentorship, and lab facilities; youth-led ventures, especially from underserved or marginalized communities, often lack age-inclusive platforms to scale their ideas.

**Scheme intro:**
Atal Innovation Mission (AIM), NITI Aayog, Government of India’s flagship innovation initiative, offers multiple programs: Atal Tinkering Labs (ATL) provide grant-in-aid of up to INR 20 lakhs (INR 10 lakhs for setup, INR 10 lakhs for O&M over 5 years) to schools; Atal Incubation Centres (AIC) support universities and corporates to build startup ecosystems; Atal New India Challenge (ANIC) offers up to INR 1 crore in grant-in-aid for prototype-stage startups solving national challenges; Youth Co:Lab provides seed grants of INR 2.2–3.5 lakhs to youth-led startups; Atal Community Innovation Centres (ACIC) offer up to INR 2.5 crores with matching partner funding for underserved regions.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You have grades VI–XII with over 400 students and a science/computer lab → eligible for ATL  
- Your startup is DPIIT-recognized with a working prototype and >51% Indian stakeholding → eligible for ANIC  
- Your team includes founders aged 18–29 (or up to 35 for women/PwDs) → eligible for Youth Co:Lab  
- You’re based in an underserved region and can secure partner matching funds → eligible for ACIC
