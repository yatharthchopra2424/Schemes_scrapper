### Atal Innovation Mission (AIM)
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Atal Innovation Mission (AIM), NITI Aayog, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://aim.gov.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Rolling basis — application portals for ATL, AIC, and ACIC are not currently open; specific programs like Youth Co:Lab have fixed deadlines (e.g., apply before midnight on 20th March 2026 for Youth Co:Lab 2026).

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Varies by sub-program: schools (ATL), universities/institutions/corporates (AIC), underserved regions with partnership (ACIC), Indian companies/MSMEs/DPIIT-recognized startups (ANIC), youth-led startups (Youth Co:Lab), technology developers/incubation managers/scientists/academicians (AIM-PRIME)
- Age of business: Not specified for most programs; for Youth Co:Lab, founders aged 15-29 (extended to 35 for women, PwDs, LGBTIQA+, tribal, and minority entrepreneurs)
- Sector: Varies by sub-program: education (ATL), innovation/entrepreneurship (AIC, ACIC, ANIC), deep tech (AIM-PRIME), social innovation (Youth Co:Lab)
- Revenue cap: Not explicitly mentioned in evidence
- Employee count: Not mentioned in evidence
- Other: 
  - For ATL: Schools must have at least Grade VI to X, minimum enrollment of 400 students (250 in hilly states), dedicated science and computer labs, steady electricity, library, playground, and qualified staff
  - For AIC: Universities, institutions, and corporates can apply
  - For ACIC: Applicants must be from underserved regions with partnership and matching funding from partners (equal or greater than AIM grant)
  - For ANIC: Indian companies (MSMEs or DPIIT-recognized startups) with a working prototype and majority Indian stakeholding (>51%)
  - For Youth Co:Lab: Youth-led startups with founders aged 15-29 (age relaxation to 35 for specific groups); solution must align with Responsible Consumption sub-themes
  - For AIM-PRIME: Technology developers, incubation managers, scientists, and academicians with science-based deep tech ideas; applicants must be Indian citizens advancing a science-based technology with significant knowledge intensity or novelty

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: 
  - ATL: Grant-in-aid for establishment (amount not specified in evidence, but guidebook states up to INR 20 lakhs with INR 10 lakhs for capital and INR 10 lakhs for O&M over 5 years)
  - AIC: Not specified as direct grant; provides infrastructure, mentorship, funding support, partnerships, networking, co-working spaces, lab facilities
  - ACIC: Up to INR 2.5 crores from AIM subject to equal or greater matching funding from partners
  - ANIC: Grant-in-aid of up to INR 1 crore for startups over 12–18 months
  - Youth Co:Lab: Seed grants of INR 2.2–3.5 lakhs to top 6 startups
  - AIM-PRIME: Guidance and training but no direct financial grant
  - Fund of Funds for Startups (FFS): INR 10,000 Cr managed by SIDBI (mentioned in context but not directly under AIM)
- Disbursement mode: 
  - ATL: Direct credit (tranches released based on compliance and performance)
  - AIC: Not specified as direct financial disbursement; support via infrastructure and ecosystem access
  - ACIC: Direct credit (subject to matching funding)
  - ANIC: Direct credit (grant-in-aid)
  - Youth Co:Lab: Direct credit (seed grants)
  - AIM-PRIME: No direct financial disbursement; non-monetary support only
- Duration of benefit: 
  - ATL: Five years (operational and maintenance expenses over five years)
  - AIC: Ongoing ecosystem support
  - ACIC: Not specified; implies ongoing support post-establishment
  - ANIC: 12–18 months
  - Youth Co:Lab: One-time seed grant with post-program support (springboard, immersion, recognition)
  - AIM-PRIME: 9 months (training and guidance duration)

#### Documents Required
1. Declaration form by school Principal on school letterhead
2. Memorandum of Agreement (MoA)
3. Bond (for non-governmental schools only)
4. Bank passbook detail
5. ATL lab layout
6. Utilization Certificate (UC) in GFR-12A format (annual requirement)
7. Audited Statement of Account
8. Bank Statement/Passbook
9. Interest remittance acknowledgement
10. Tax Declaration
11. School registration documents (implied via PFMS compliance)
12. For Youth Co:Lab: Application form with team details, solution description, alignment with theme, pitch deck (optional but recommended)
13. For ANIC: Prototype details, company registration, DPIIT recognition (if startup), shareholding pattern
14. For ACIC: Partnership agreement, matching funding commitment documents
15. For ATL: GeM procurement compliance documents, MyATL Dashboard activation

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Visit the official AIM website (aim.gov.in) to check for open applications under specific programs (ATL, AIC, ACIC, ANIC, etc.).
2. Note that application portals for ATL, AIC, and ACIC are currently not open (as per notice on homepage).
3. For open programs, register on the designated portal (e.g., atl.aim.gov.in for ATL).
4. Fill out the application form with required details (school/institution info, project proposal, etc.).
5. Upload necessary documents (certificates, prototypes, team details, etc.).
6. Submit the application and await evaluation.
7. Shortlisted candidates may be invited for interviews, presentations, or field verification.
8. Final selection is communicated via email or portal.
9. Selected entities receive grant-in-aid and must submit utilization certificates and progress reports.
10. Post-selection, beneficiaries gain access to mentorship, infrastructure, and ecosystem support.
