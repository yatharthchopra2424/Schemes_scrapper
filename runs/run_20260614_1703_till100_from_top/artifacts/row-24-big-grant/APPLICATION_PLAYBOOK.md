# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Company/LLP registered under the Indian Companies Act, 1956/2013  
- [ ] Incorporation date not earlier than 5 years from the date of launch of the particular BIG call  
- [ ] Minimum 51% of capital beneficially owned by resident Indian citizen(s) and/or Indian companies ultimately owned and controlled by resident Indian citizens  
- [ ] Application represented by a Project Leader  
- [ ] Company/LLP has its own in-house R&D facility that is functional and adequate to execute the project  
- [ ] If no functional and adequate laboratory, the company must be incubated at an incubator  
- [ ] No promoter holding ≥20% of shares is a co-promoter of another ineligible company or partner in another ineligible LLP (unless the latter operates in a non-biotech domain)  
- [ ] Company and promoters are not in default of loan repayment to BIRAC  
- [ ] Project objectives and deliverables have not received funding support from any other agency  
- [ ] Undertaking provided that the project with same objectives and deliverables has not received funding from any other agency

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Registration Certificate of Company  
- Company’s PAN Card  
- Memorandum of Association of company  
- Article of Association Company  
- GST Registration Certificate  
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)  
- Udyam Registration Certificate  
- ITR (last 2 years)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Undertaking from Scientific Advisors/Mentors to serve as honorary advisors  
- Undertaking from the key members of the technical team  
- Letter of interest/intent from the incubator  
- Evidence of access to unique facility(ies) needed to execute a project  
- Evidence of access to key samples needed to execute the project (e.g., bio-fluids)  
- Letter of acceptance or MoU with incubator  
- Formal agreements/MoUs with other key facility(ies) providers/suppliers  
- Declaration document on ethical/legal/safety/regulatory issues  
- Presentation  
- Additional Relevant Document  
- Details of Equipment Accessories  

### Document Quality Check
- File size: Each document < 10 MB  
- File name: Must match document type (e.g., "Registration_Certificate.pdf")  
- All certificates must be clear, legible, and certified where applicable  
- Documents in English or accompanied by certified translation  
- Undertakings and declarations must be signed and dated  
- Presentation must be in PDF format

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Not explicitly required as a standalone DPR; however, the application requires a detailed proposal including concept note, objectives, methodology, novelty, opportunity, challenges, preliminary work, TRL, outcomes, commercialization plan, IP details, and references. Verify with nodal officer if a formal DPR is expected.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Fill all mandatory fields in the online application form on the BIRAC BIG portal (https://birac.nic.in/big.php)  
2. Ensure Proposal Summary does not exceed 200 words  
3. Clearly state Objectives and Proposed Approach covering methodology, scope, boundaries, and data analysis  
4. Detail Novelty with tabular comparison and concrete market data  
5. Describe Opportunity with societal and market impact  
6. List Challenges or risk factors and critical success factors  
7. Provide status of preliminary work done  
8. Specify current and expected Technology Readiness Level (TRL)  
9. Define Proposed end-outcomes (product, service, technology, IP, etc.)  
10. Outline Future Plan of Commercialization with market entry strategy, timelines, milestones, and data analysis  
11. Disclose Intellectual Property details including patent numbers, titles, assignees, and freedom-to-operate analysis  
12. Upload Relevant References  
13. Upload declaration document on ethical/legal/safety/regulatory issues  
14. Upload Presentation in prescribed format  
15. Upload Details of Equipment Accessories  
16. Do not submit incomplete applications; requests for changes post-submission will not be entertained  
17. Avoid providing incorrect information intentionally, as it may lead to disqualification

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify approval letter from BIRAC/BIG Partner  
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned amount (up to INR 50 lakhs)  
- [ ] Review Grant Agreement and milestone-based disbursement schedule:  
  - First tranche: INR 20 lakhs upon signing of Grant Agreement  
  - Second tranche(s): Up to INR 20 lakhs each based on satisfactory progress  
  - Final tranche: Up to INR 5 lakhs upon submission and acceptance of Final Utilization Certificate (UC), Statement of Expenditure (SoE), and Project Completion Report  
- [ ] Ensure utilization of funds is milestone-based and within 18-month project period  
- [ ] Prepare and submit periodic reports and utilization certificates to BIG Partner as per schedule  
- [ ] Arrange for mandatory interaction with at least 2 investors during the 18-month project period  
- [ ] Submit Final Technical Report, UC, SoE, and Project Completion Report for final tranche release  
- [ ] Track and report invoice for success fee as per consultancy agreement
