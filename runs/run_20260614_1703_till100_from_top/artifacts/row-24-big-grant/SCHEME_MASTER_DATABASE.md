### BIG Grant
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC), a Public Sector Enterprise, set up by Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://birac.nic.in/big.php
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Call announced twice a year on 1st January and 1st July; open for about 45 days

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Company/LLP registered under Indian Companies Act, 1956/2013
- Age of business: Incorporation date not earlier than 5 years from the date of launch of a particular BIG call
- Sector: Healthcare, Lifesciences, Diagnostics, Medical Devices, Drugs, Vaccines, Drug Formulations and delivery systems, Industrial Biotechnology, Agriculture, Secondary agriculture, Waste Management, Sanitation, Clean Energy and related areas
- Revenue cap: Not specified in evidence
- Employee count: Not specified in evidence
- Other: 
  - Minimum 51% of capital beneficially owned by resident Indian citizen(s) and/or Indian companies ultimately owned and controlled by resident Indian citizens
  - Application must be represented by a Project Leader
  - Must have own in-house R&D facility that is functional and adequate to execute the project; if not, must be incubated at an incubator
  - If any promoter holding ≥20% shares is a co-promoter of another ineligible company or partner in another ineligible LLP, applicant is ineligible (exception if the latter operates in non-biotech domain)
  - Companies in default of loan repayment to BIRAC, as well as their promoters, are deemed ineligible
  - BIG support can be provided only once to any startup
  - Proposed objectives and deliverables must not have received funding support from any other agency
  - Undertaking by applicant that the project with same objectives and deliverables has not received funding from any other agency

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Up to INR 50 lakhs
- Disbursement mode: Grant-in-aid released in milestone-based installments (Direct credit with final tranche as reimbursement)
- Duration of benefit: Up to 18 months

#### Documents Required
1. Registration Certificate Of Company
2. Company’s PAN Card
3. Memorandum of Association of company
4. Article of Association Company
5. Undertaking from Scientific Advisors/ Mentors to serve as honorary advisors
6. Undertaking from the key members of the technical team
7. Letter of interest/intent from the incubator
8. Evidence of access to unique facility(ies) needed to execute a project
9. Evidence of access to key samples needed to execute the project (eg: bio-fluids)
10. Letter of acceptance or MoU with incubator
11. Formal agreements/ MoUs with other key facility(ies) providers/ suppliers
12. Declaration document on ethical/legal/safety/regulatory issues
13. Presentation
14. Additional Relevant Document
15. Details of Equipment Accessories

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. BIRAC issues a national call inviting applications under BIG at least twice a year (typically on 1st of January & 1st of July).
2. The Call for Proposals is advertised through one or more means that may include BIRAC website, newspapers, journals and magazines.
3. The Call for Proposals is typically open for a period of 30 days.
4. The BIG Scheme is implemented across the country through BIRAC’s BIG Partners. The Call will mention the names of BIG Partners for the particular call.
5. Application for BIG needs to be submitted online ONLY through BIRAC website www.birac.nic.in.
6. Applicants are advised to fill-up and submit their applications early without waiting for the last date in order to avoid last-minute rush. Please note that the online system will stop accepting applications automatically at the specific date and time mentioned in the Call details.
7. Applicants are advised to provide sufficient details in their applications to allow for an informed and fair evaluation/review.
8. Requests for changes in a proposal once submitted will not be entertained.
9. Providing incorrect information intentionally is viewed adversely and may lead to disqualification.
