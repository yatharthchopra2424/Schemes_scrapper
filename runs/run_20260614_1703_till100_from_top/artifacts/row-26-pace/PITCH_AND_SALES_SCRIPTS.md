# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping academic institutions, universities, research foundations, and NGOs in the biotechnology sector access government grants that support early-stage innovation. The PACE scheme—Promoting Academic Research Conversion to Enterprise—is designed specifically to help academia develop proof-of-concept innovations and validate them with industrial partners, offering up to Rs. 50 lakhs in grant funding. I’d like to understand your current research activities and see if PACE aligns with your goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your institution’s registration status—are you UGC-affiliated, AICTE-approved, or hold CSIR/DSIR/SIRO certification?
2. Has your Principal Investigator completed at least one extramural-funded project in India with a minimum duration of 3 years in the same research area as the proposed project?
3. Does the PI have at least one publication (first or lead author) in Scopus/Web of Science or a filed patent in the same research area?
4. Do you have evidence of proof of principle (POP) and preliminary data supporting your proposal?
5. Are you applying individually or in collaboration with an academic or industrial partner?
6. If collaborating with industry, has the company/LLP been incorporated for more than 5 years and holds at least 51% Indian citizen shareholding?
7. For CRS applications, do you have validation-ready data (TRL-3) and a commitment letter from the industrial partner for first right of refusal on commercialization?
8. Are you currently managing no more than two ongoing projects as primary applicant or two as collaborator with BIRAC?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Academic institute/university/NGO/research foundation with government accreditation (UGC/AICTE/CSIR/DSIR/SIRO)
- [ ] PI is a permanent faculty of the applicant entity
- [ ] For AIR: PI has completed ≥1 extramural-funded project in India (≥3 years duration, same research area)
- [ ] For AIR: PI has authored ≥1 publication (Scopus/Web of Science, first/lead author) or filed patent in same research area
- [ ] For AIR: Evidence of proof of principle (POP) and preliminary data submitted
- [ ] For AIR: If industry partner involved, company/LLP >5 years old and ≥51% Indian citizen shareholding
- [ ] For CRS: Evidence of proof of concept (TRL-3) and validation-ready data
- [ ] For CRS: Same industrial partner as in prior AIR project (if applicable), with commitment letter for first right of refusal
- [ ] For CRS: Industrial partner incorporated under Companies Act, 2013 or LLP Act, 2008 with ≥51% Indian citizen shareholding
- [ ] No more than two ongoing projects as primary applicant or two as collaborator with BIRAC
- [ ] Undertaking of no duplicate funding from other agencies for same/similar proposal

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your project aligns well with the PACE scheme’s AIR or CRS components. PACE offers up to Rs. 50 lakhs in grant funding for proof-of-concept development and validation, with no ceiling for CRS funding. Let me walk you through how it works and whether it’s the right fit.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Academic researchers often struggle to translate lab innovations into viable products due to limited funding for proof-of-concept development and validation. Without early-stage support, promising technologies fail to reach industry readiness, delaying societal impact and commercialization opportunities.

**Scheme intro:**
Promoting Academic Research Conversion to Enterprise (PACE), administered by the Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC) under the Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Government of India, offers grant funding for two tracks: Academic Innovation Research (AIR) for proof-of-concept development (up to Rs. 50 lakhs) and Contract Research Scheme (CRS) for validation with industrial partners (no funding ceiling). Applications are submitted via http://www.birac.nic.in/ during two annual calls (April–May and September–October).

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your institution holds government accreditation (UGC/AICTE/CSIR/DSIR/SIRO), meeting academic eligibility.
- Your PI has completed at least one extramural-funded project of 3+ years in the same research area.
- You have published proof-of-principle data and preliminary evidence ready for submission.
- [IF INDUSTRY PARTNER] Your collaborator is incorporated for >5 years with ≥51% Indian citizen shareholding.
- You are within the limit of ≤2 ongoing projects as primary applicant/collaborator with BIRAC.
