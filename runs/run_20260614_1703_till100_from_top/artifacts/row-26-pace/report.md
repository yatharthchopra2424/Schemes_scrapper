# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Scheme Overview
**Scheme Name:** Promoting Academic Research Conversion to Enterprise (PACE)  
**Scheme ID:** row-26  
**Ministry / Category:** Biotechnology  
**Scheme Type:** Grant  
**Geographic Scope:** Pan-India  
**Implementing Agency:** Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC), a Public Sector Enterprise, set up by Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Government of India  
**Application Portal:** [http://www.birac.nic.in/](http://www.birac.nic.in/)  
**Status / Deadlines:** Two regular calls per year:  
- 1st April – 15th May  
- 1st September – 15th October  
Besides regular calls, BIRAC may announce need-based additional calls with separate terms described in respective RFPs.  
**Last Updated:** 2025  
**Confidence:** High  

### Objectives
- To encourage/support academic collaborations  
- For development of products and technologies up to proof of concept  
- For validation of products/technologies towards commercialization  
- To stimulate technological innovation  

### Scheme Components
PACE consists of two distinct components:  
1. **Academic Innovation Research (AIR):** Promotes development of Proof-of-concept (PoC) for a process/product by academia with or without industry/LLP involvement.  
2. **Contract Research Scheme (CRS):** Aims at validation of a process or prototype (developed by academia) by the industrial partner/LLP.  

### Eligibility Matrix
| Eligibility Criteria | AIR Scheme | CRS Scheme |
|----------------------|------------|------------|
| **Primary Applicant** | Academic Institute, University, NGO, Research Foundation, registered/accredited by a government body (Public or Private Institute, University, NGO, Research Foundation or trust/society), National research laboratories with well-established research support system | Same as AIR, but **mandatory** industrial partner/LLP involvement |
| **Applicant Type** | Can apply individually or jointly with academic and/or industrial partner | Must apply jointly with industrial partner/LLP |
| **PI Requirement** | PI must be a permanent faculty of the applicant entity | Same as AIR |
| **Registration/Accreditation** | Proper registration/accreditation from government body mandatory (UGC affiliation certification, AICTE, CSIR/DSIR/SIRO certificate etc.) | Same as AIR |
| **Industrial Partner Eligibility** | Optional for AIR; if participating: Company incorporated under Companies Act, 2013 OR minimum 51% shares held by Indian Citizens holding Indian passport (excluding PIO/OCI); adequate in-house facility OR incubated with recognized Incubation Facility | **Mandatory**; same criteria as above for AIR industry participation |
| **LLP Eligibility** | Optional for AIR; if participating: Minimum half of LLP partners must be Indian citizens; adequate in-house facility OR incubated with recognized Incubation Facility | **Mandatory**; same criteria as above for AIR LLP participation |
| **Technical Criteria** | PI must have:<br>- Completed ≥1 extramural funded project in India (min. 3 years cumulative duration, same research area)<br>- Funded by Govt. agencies or Industry<br>- Project/s executed as PI (not Co-PI)<br>- Authored ≥1 publication (not abstract) indexed in Scopus/Web of Science OR patents (filed) in same research area (first/lead author)<br>- Evidence of Proof of Principle (POP) and preliminary data (compulsory)<br>- Justification on how project completion would be CRS ready (strategy for taking outcomes toward product development with industrial partner)<br>- For agriculture proposals: viable product/technology as outcome for advanced trials by industry/authorized national agencies<br>- Final technical milestone: technology/result near to industry readiness<br>- If industry/LLP participation: collaborating company/LLP must be >5 years after incorporation | Evidence of Proof of Concept (PoC) i.e., TRL-3 and validation ready data supporting the proposal (compulsory)<br>- Proposals that received AIR funding must have same industrial partner who collaborated for AIR project (any deviation must be justified with clarity on IP governance)<br>- CRS proposal must be accompanied by Commitment Letter from industrial partner to exercise first right for monetizing the product/technology |
| **Ongoing Project Limit** | Number of ongoing projects must not exceed two as primary applicant and two as collaborator | Same as AIR |
| **Duplicate Funding Undertaking** | Applicant must submit undertaking at time of submission that there is no duplicate funding from any other funding agency for same/similar proposal | Same as AIR |

### Benefits & Financial Support
| Scheme | Funding Support (Grant) Details | Non-Recurring Cost Limit | IPR Provisions |
|--------|----------------------------------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **AIR** | Total cost of project must not exceed Rs. 50 lakhs | Non-recurring cost must not exceed 10% of total cost | IP rights may be with academia alone, or jointly shared between academia and industry (if academia establishes PoC with industry) as per understanding between partners. Although IP rights reside with academia, industry partner has first right of refusal for commercial exploitation of New IP. |

### Financial Support Key Points (from Funding Guidelines)
- **AIR projects:** Ceiling of Rs. 50 lakhs on project cost  
- **CRS scheme:** No ceiling on project cost  
- **Funding to Research Institutions:**  
  - For Centre/State-owned: Up to 100% Grant-in-aid irrespective of total cost  
  - For Private Research Institutions (no prior BIRAC Grant-in-aid):  
    - If budget < Rs. 50 lakhs: Up to 100% Grant-in-aid  
    - If budget ≥ Rs. 50 lakhs: BIRAC contribution = Rs. 50 lakhs + up to 70% of amount exceeding Rs. 50 lakhs  
  - For Private Research Institutions (with prior BIRAC Grant-in-aid): BIRAC contribution restricted to max 70% of total budget (min 30% from institution)  
- **Funding to Companies (as collaborators):**  
  - For Start-ups (<10 years incorporation, no prior BIRAC Grant-in-aid):  
    - If budget < Rs. 50 lakhs: Up to 100% Grant-in-aid  
    - If budget ≥ Rs. 50 lakhs: BIRAC contribution = Rs. 50 lakhs + up to 70% of amount exceeding Rs. 50 lakhs  
  - For Start-ups (≥10 years incorporation or with prior BIRAC Grant-in-aid) AND ALL OTHER COMPANIES (>10 years existence): BIRAC contribution restricted to max 70% of total budget (min 30% from company)  
- **Key Points:**  
  - Approved funds released in instalments based on milestones  
  - Non-recurring cost **not allowable** for collaborating industry partner under CRS  
  - No provision of overheads under Recurring Budget head  
  - Manpower salaries must be as per prevailing DST norms for Academic organizations  

### Application Process
**How to Apply:**  
Proposals under AIR or CRS are required to be submitted online only.  

**Step-by-Step Process:**  
1. Log on to BIRAC website: [http://www.birac.nic.in/](http://www.birac.nic.in/)  
2. If registered user, log-in using credentials; else register institution via "New User Registration"  
3. For new user registration: computer-generated password sent to provided email-id; password can be changed later  
4. Once logged in, navigate to page displaying PACE link  
5. Click on PACE link under Programmes; active call will be highlighted  
6. Click on active call against which you wish to submit proposal  
7. Further details on proposal submission available in PACE User Guide on website  

**Important Notes:**  
- Academic User Registration is open only when the call is active  
- Online Proposal Submission can be done **only during an active call**  
- Advertisement for Call for proposals released in all national dailies and some biotechnology related magazines  
- Intimation of active call displayed on DBT and BIRAC websites  
- **Please note:**  
  - Number of ongoing projects must not exceed two as primary applicant and two as collaborator  
  - Applicant must submit undertaking at time of submission that there is no duplicate funding from any other funding agency for same/similar proposal  

### Evaluation and Decision-Making Criteria
**For Both AIR and CRS Schemes:**  
Proposals evaluated based on:  
a) Technical strength of the proposal  
b) Novelty  
c) Generation of preliminary data  
d) National/social relevance  
e) Investigators' credentials and competency of academia and partnering company (if any) to carry out proposed research  
f) Clarity of milestones  
g) Commercial potential or translational capacity  
h) Overall success of earlier projects in terms of accomplishment of all sanctioned milestones  
i) Any other relevant aspect  

**Process:**  
- Submitted proposals undergo initial eligibility check  
- Eligible proposals shortlisted by Area Review Panels (ARPs) for presentation  
- PIs of shortlisted proposals make presentations in respective ARPs  
- ARP selects proposal for further consideration based on merit  
- Expert Review (wherever required)  
- Final review and decision for funding by Technical Expert Committee (TEC) for AIR; by Apex Committee (comprising Senior Technical Experts from various ministries, academic institutions, industry, and Government departments, chaired by Secretary, DBT & Chairman, BIRAC) for CRS  
- Members of ARP, TEC, and Apex Committee sign no-conflict and confidentiality agreements  
- Reviewers maintain complete confidentiality about proposals  
- **Please note:** Decision of committee at any stage is final; reconsideration requests not entertained. Applicant may reapply in next call providing clarifications to committee’s comments/recommendations.  

### Key Caveats
> **Proposals submitted in collaboration with companies defaulting on repayment of loan or are irregular with regard to repayment of instalments to BIRAC would be considered ineligible**  
>   
> **Applicant who had withdrawn their proposal after approval from Apex committee or whose project was foreclosed due to inadequate funds or any other irregularity would be debarred from submitting fresh proposals for next 2 calls (1 year) unless the withdrawal was due to papers not being ready**  
>   
> **Proposals submitted by a company/subsidiary/sister concern either as main applicant or collaborator, who has been fund recipient in any of the schemes of BIRAC in past and has defaulted on (i) repayment of loan and/or interest, or is irregular with regard to repayment of instalments (ii) payment of unspent balance (if any) in the earlier BIRAC supported projects (iii) payment of Royalty to BIRAC would not be considered for financial assistance by BIRAC**  
>   
> **Proposal of any company having a Director of a defaulting company (as detailed above in point no. 4) would not be considered for financial assistance by BIRAC**  
>   
> **The number of ongoing projects at a given point of time must not exceed two as primary applicant and two as collaborator**  
>   
> **The applicant must submit an undertaking at the time of submission of the proposal that there is no duplicate funding from any other funding agency for the same or similar proposal**
