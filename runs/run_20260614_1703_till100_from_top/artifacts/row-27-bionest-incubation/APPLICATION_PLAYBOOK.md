# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian institution (University/Research Institute/College/Management Institute/Business School/Business Incubator/Biotech-Park/State government S&T body/Biotech State Council/Research Hospital)
- [ ] Incorporation of an Indian legal entity is recommended for Bioincubators (not mandatory for new applicants from Indian Institutions)
- [ ] If applicant is a Private Company/Not for profit Section 8 company/Trust/Society: Legal entity must have at least 51% Indian Citizen stakeholders (owners/partners/trustees/members/associates/directors)
- [ ] For Category 4 (State Government-owned Section-8 companies/Councils): Minimum 50% contribution from applicant required for total project cost
- [ ] For Private entities/Institutions: Minimum 30% contribution required for total project cost
- [ ] For Government Academic Institutions (Centre/State-owned): Support provided as Grant-in-aid
- [ ] For Central Government owned Section-8 company(ies): Support provided as Grant-in-aid
- [ ] Applicant must have focus on translational activities where innovations can be converted to products/technologies
- [ ] Applicant must have potential to transform into future biotech hubs and accelerate entrepreneurial ecosystem
- [ ] Host Institute must have adequate expertise and infrastructure to support incubation activity
- [ ] Host Institute must have preparedness to support Biotechnology-based entrepreneurship (infrastructural facilities, dedicated space, instrument support, technical/business mentoring)
- [ ] Project must demonstrate innovative content and pipeline of potential incubatees
- [ ] Strength of Mentoring support at Host Institute must be assessed (ability to provide training/information for enterprise development)
- [ ] Strength of Business development support at Incubator must be assessed (technical assistance for regulatory understanding, compliance readiness, investment readiness, go-to-market validation, peer learning, on-ground support, alliances)
- [ ] Application portal: https://birac.nic.in/bionest.php
- [ ] Last updated: 2026 (per evidence)

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Incorporation Certificate
- PAN Card
- ITR (last 3 years)
- GST Registration Certificate
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration (if applicable)
- Audited Financial Statements
- Board Resolution authorizing application
- Authorized Signatory Details

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed Project Proposal (downloaded from portal, filled, and converted to PDF)
- Proof of land/space allocation (minimum 10,000 sq ft recommended, exclusively earmarked for BioNEST centre)
- Detailed space utilization plan (if joint application, commitment to total space of 10,000 sq ft or more)
- Infrastructure and equipment list (specialized and advanced equipment for biotech startups)
- Mentoring and business support plan (technical and business mentorship strategy)
- IP and Technology management plan (enabling services for IP, legal, regulatory guidance)
- Resource mobilization and networking platform plan
- Governance model document (efficient governance model for bioincubator)
- For Category 2a: Proof of operational status of existing non-biotech incubator and track record in promoting technology-based entrepreneurship
- For Category 2b: Proof of proven capabilities to support biotech startups and plan to integrate with BioNEST network
- For Category 3: Proof of existing BIRAC-funded BioNEST incubator completion of Phase 1 and operational facility
- For Category 4: State Government commitment to provide land/built-up space and 50% of total project cost
- Shareholding pattern certificate from CA/CS (if private entity, showing ≥51% Indian Citizen stakeholders)
- Utilization Certificate format (for half-yearly reporting post-approval)
- Statement of Accounts format

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible and in English or Hindi
- [ ] File size for each document < 10 MB
- [ ] File names follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_VersionDate.pdf` (e.g., BioNEST_Proposal_V1_2026.pdf)
- [ ] All certificates are latest (not older than 6 months unless specified)
- [ ] PAN, GST, Udyam numbers match across documents
- [ ] Bank statements show consistent transactions and are stamped by bank
- [ ] Audited financial statements are signed and sealed by CA
- [ ] Board resolution is on letterhead, signed, and dated
- [ ] Proposal form is filled completely, converted to PDF, and uploaded as per portal instructions
- [ ] All declarations are signed and dated
- [ ] Space allocation documents clearly show 10,000+ sq ft exclusively for BioNEST

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a Detailed Project Proposal (DPR) is required. As per the portal guidance (Step-15 in evidence):  
"After clicking on 'Proposal Details', below page will open. Download the proposal form by clicking on 'Click here to download format'. Fill the form and convert into PDF and upload."

Standard sections to include in the DPR:
- Executive Summary
- Project Background and Rationale
- Objectives (aligned with BioNEST: support startups, connect industry-academia, IP/tech management, legal support, resource mobilization, networking, governance model)
- Detailed Description of Activities (space setup, instrumentation, mentoring programs, seed fund linkage if applicable)
- Implementation Plan (timeline, milestones, responsible persons)
- Budget Breakdown (capital and operational expenses, applicant contribution, BIRAC grant sought)
- Sustainability Plan (post-funding self-sufficiency or host institute support)
- Expected Outcomes (number of startups to be supported, jobs created, IPs facilitated, incubation space utilized)
- Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan
- Annexures (space allocation documents, infrastructure lists, mentorship CVs, governance structure)

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. **Register first**: Go to BIRAC home page → Click "Click here to Register" → Fill registration details → Reset password via email link → Login using credentials
2. **Navigate to BioNEST**: After login → Click "BioNEST" tab → Click "Click here to submit new proposal"
3. **Fill Basic Information completely**: Include applicant name, address, contact details, type of institution, registration number, PAN, GST
4. **Download proposal form**: In Proposal Details section → Click "Click here to download format" → Fill all sections offline → Convert to PDF → Upload
5. **Fill Project Coordinator/Lead details**: Name, designation, contact, email, qualifications
6. **Ensure all fields are mandatory**: Portal will not allow progression if any required field is empty
7. **Verify document uploads**: Confirm PDF proposal is uploaded correctly and is legible
8. **Final review before submit**: Check all entered data matches uploaded documents (especially space, budget, applicant details)
9. **Submit only once**: Click "Final submit" – no edits possible after submission

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All application form fields are filled (Basic Information, Proposal Details uploaded, Project Coordinator details)
- [ ] Detailed Project Proposal PDF is uploaded and matches the downloaded format
- [ ] Space allocation proof shows ≥10,000 sq ft exclusively for BioNEST (or joint commitment met)
- [ ] Budget clearly shows applicant contribution (30% for private, 50% for State Govt Section-8, 0% for Govt academic)
- [ ] All universal documents are attached and valid
- [ ] Scheme-specific documents are complete (mentoring, IP, governance, resource plans)
- [ ] Declarations and undertakings are signed and dated
- [ ] File names and sizes comply with quality check
- [ ] Application reflects alignment with BioNEST objectives: startup support, industry-academia linkage, mentorship, IP/legal support, resource mobilization, networking, governance model
- [ ] No contradictory information between form and documents

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify approval letter from BIRAC (check for sanctioned amount, duration, and specific terms)
- [ ] Confirm sanctioned grant amount matches application request and adheres to funding norms (Grant-in-aid for Capital/Operational expenses)
- [ ] For private entities: Verify 30% applicant contribution is arranged; for State Govt Section-8: verify 50% applicant share is in place
- [ ] Ensure Utilization Certificate (UC) format is understood and ready for half-yearly submission
- [ ] Confirm Statement of Accounts (SOA) process with BIRAC finance team
- [ ] Track milestone-based disbursement (if applicable) and ensure compliance with timelines
- [ ] Monitor utilization of funds for approved purposes only (space setup, equipment, mentoring programs)
- [ ] Prepare for success fee invoicing as per consultancy agreement (typically 10-15% of sanctioned grant upon disbursement)
- [ ] Maintain all records for audit and future renewal/extension applications (initial funding up to 3 years, possible 2-year extension based on performance)
- [ ] Submit first half-yearly progress report including UC, SOA, and project progress (beneficiary startups supported, jobs created, IPs facilitated)
- [ ] Ensure BioNEST centre becomes operational and starts supporting startups as per proposal timelines
- [ ] Plan for sustainability post-funding period (self-sufficiency or host institute support) as outlined in DPR
