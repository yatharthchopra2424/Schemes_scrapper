# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping biotech and life sciences institutions access government incubation support through BIRAC’s BioNEST scheme. Many universities, research institutes, and biotech parks we work with are looking to establish or scale incubation facilities to nurture startups but need clarity on eligibility and funding. I’d like to understand your current capabilities and goals to see if BioNEST aligns with your mission to support biotech entrepreneurship.

### Discovery Questions
1. What type of institution are you representing (e.g., university, research institute, biotech park, Section 8 company)?
2. Are you looking to establish a new bioincubation facility or strengthen/integrate an existing one?
3. Do you have dedicated space (ideally 10,000 sq ft or more) earmarked for bioincubation activities?
4. What is your current operational experience in supporting biotech or life science startups (e.g., number of startups mentored, years of operation)?
5. Do you have in-house capacity for IP & Technology Management, legal support, and business mentoring for startups?
6. Are you prepared to contribute at least 30% of the total project cost if you’re a private entity, or 50% if you’re a State Government-owned Section 8 company?
7. Has your institute been involved in translational research where innovations can be converted to products or technologies?
8. Do you have experience managing early-stage funding schemes or grants (e.g., BIG, SEED, or similar)?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Indian institution (University/Research Institute/College/Management Institute/Business School/Business Incubator/Biotech-Park/State Govt S&T Body/Biotech State Council/Research Hospital)
- [ ] For private entities: Minimum 51% Indian citizen stakeholders
- [ ] For State Govt-owned Section 8 companies: Applicant ready to contribute 50% of project cost
- [ ] For private entities/institutions: Ready to contribute minimum 30% of total project cost
- [ ] Space: Can allocate ~10,000 sq ft or more exclusively for BioNEST centre
- [ ] Focus: On translational activities where innovations can be converted to products/technologies
- [ ] Has expertise/infrastructure to support incubation activity
- [ ] Prepared to establish governance model for bioincubator
- [ ] Willing to provide IP, legal, regulatory, mentoring, and networking support to startups

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it seems your institute has strong potential to host a BioNEST centre. The BIRAC BioNEST scheme provides grant-in-aid support for both capital and operational expenses to establish bioincubation facilities that nurture biotech startups through industry-academia linkage, mentorship, and IP support. Would you like me to walk you through how it works and what support you could access?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Biotech startups require specialized infrastructure, mentorship, and regulatory support that generic incubators often lack. Institutions wanting to support biotech entrepreneurship face gaps in funding for space, equipment, and operational costs, limiting their ability to nurture early-stage ventures from lab to market.

**Scheme intro:**
BIRAC BioNEST (Bioincubators Nurturing Entrepreneurship for Scaling Technologies), implemented by the Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council (BIRAC), a Public Sector Enterprise under the Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Government of India, provides grant-in-aid support for both capital and operational expenses to establish or strengthen bioincubation facilities. Funding is aligned with the Bio-RIDE umbrella scheme and based on project scope as adjudged by the BioNEST committee.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Indian institution including Universities/Research Institutes/Colleges/Management Institutes/Business Schools/Business Incubators/Biotech-Parks/State Govt S&T Bodies/Biotech State Councils/Research Hospitals
- Incorporation of an Indian legal entity with ≥51% Indian citizen stakeholders recommended (though not mandatory for new applicants from academic institutions)
- Ready to contribute minimum 30% of total project cost (private entities) or 50% (State Govt-owned Section 8 companies)
- Capable of providing incubation space (~10,000 sq ft or more) exclusively for BioNEST activities
- Positioned to support translational research where innovations can be converted to products/technologies
