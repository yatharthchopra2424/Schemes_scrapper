# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an individual, startup, or MSME with an innovative business idea
- [ ] Idea is technology-driven and has potential for scalability
- [ ] Applicant is registered as an MSME or in the process of registration
- [ ] Idea is novel, feasible, and capable of generating employment and economic value
- [ ] Preference may be given to ideas from SC/ST, women, differently-abled, and aspirational districts (verify if applicable)

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- PAN card of applicant(s)
- Aadhaar card of applicant(s)
- Udyam Registration Certificate (or application proof)
- GST registration certificate and returns (if applicable)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for past 1-3 years
- Bank statements (6-12 months)
- Business address and ownership proof
- Financial statements (Balance Sheet, P&L statement, cash flow statement)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Project proposal document
- Proof of identity of applicant(s)
- MSME registration or application proof
- Details of the innovative idea or technology
- Financial plan and budget estimate
- Background/qualification of promoters
- Any existing IP or prior work related to the idea

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible and in PDF or JPEG format
- File size should not exceed 5 MB per document
- Document names must match the applicant's name as per PAN/Aadhaar
- Scanned copies must be certified if required (self-attestation acceptable for most)
- Documents must be in English or Hindi; if in another language, provide certified translation
- Ensure all declarations are signed and dated

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a detailed project proposal is required. It must include:
- Business idea description
- Innovation quotient
- Market potential
- Financial plan and budget estimate
- Details of the innovative idea or technology
- Background/qualification of promoters
- Any existing IP or prior work related to the idea

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Identify an approved incubator under the MSME Incubation Scheme (list available on msme.gov.in or through MSME-DFOs)
2. Prepare the project proposal as per the scheme-specific document list
3. Ensure all claims in the proposal are supported by data and documents
4. Highlight novelty, feasibility, and scalability of the idea
5. Include employment generation and economic value potential
6. Mention any preference factors (SC/ST, women, differently-abled, aspirational districts) if applicable
7. Attach all required documents in the specified format
8. Do not submit pure trading or service-only ideas without innovation (scheme does not support these)

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All sections of the project proposal are complete
- [ ] All scheme-specific and universal documents are attached
- [ ] Documents are legible, properly named, and within size limits
- [ ] Declarations and undertakings are signed and dated
- [ ] Financial plan is realistic and matches the idea stage
- [ ] Innovation and scalability are clearly articulated
- [ ] No prohibited activities (pure trading/service without innovation) are included
- [ ] Proposal aligns with scheme objectives: early validation, proof-of-concept, mentorship, infrastructure support

---

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the project proposal and documents directly to the selected approved incubator (not via a central portal)
- Obtain an acknowledgement receipt from the incubator with a unique application/reference number
- Store the reference number securely and note the date of submission
- Set follow-up reminders for 7 days after submission if no acknowledgment is received
- Schedule a progress check-in with the incubator after 15 days

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and review the approval letter from the incubator/MSME authorities
- [ ] Verify the sanctioned amount and disbursement schedule (staged based on milestones)
- [ ] Sign the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between the incubator and the incubatee
- [ ] Ensure funds are utilized strictly for approved project activities (mentorship, infrastructure, prototype development, etc.)
- [ ] Submit regular progress reports as required by the incubator and MSME authorities
- [ ] Prepare utilization certificate upon completion of each milestone
- [ ] Track milestone achievement for timely disbursement of next installment
- [ ] Ensure compliance with monitoring and reporting requirements to avoid fund recovery or blacklisting
- [ ] Plan for post-incubation support: market linkage, IPR filing assistance, fundraising guidance
- [ ] Maintain records of all communications, approvals, and financial transactions for audit purposes
