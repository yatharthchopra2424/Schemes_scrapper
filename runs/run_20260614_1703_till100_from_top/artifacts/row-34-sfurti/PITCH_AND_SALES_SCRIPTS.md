# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping traditional artisan clusters and producer collectives access government grants for infrastructure, technology upgradation, and market development. The SFURTI scheme from the Ministry of MSME is designed specifically for groups like yours—organizing traditional industries into competitive collectives with support for Common Facility Centres, machinery, working capital, and skill development. Before we explore if this fits your cluster, I’d like to understand your current operations and goals to ensure we’re aligned.

### Discovery Questions
1. What traditional industry or craft does your cluster focus on (e.g., khadi, village industries, coir, handicrafts)?
2. How many artisans or producers are currently part of your collective or engaged with your organization?
3. What is the legal structure of your organization—are you registered as an NGO, SPV, Producer Company, or Government/PRI body?
4. How long has your organization been working in this sector or with artisans in the proposed district?
5. Do you have identified land (owned or leased for 15+ years) for a Common Facility Centre (CFC) or worksheds?
6. Have you already formed a project-specific Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) as a 'for profit' entity with artisan members?
7. What percentage of your beneficiaries belong to marginalized communities (SCs, STs, women, disadvantaged groups)?
8. Are you seeking support for hard interventions like machinery procurement, CFC construction, working capital, or soft interventions like training, design development, and market promotion?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Organization is registered as an Implementing Agency (IA) on the SFURTI portal or eligible to register
- [ ] Focus on traditional industries: Khadi, Village Industries, or Coir sectors
- [ ] Target beneficiaries include traditional artisans/producers, with emphasis on SCs, STs, women, or disadvantaged communities
- [ ] Land availability confirmed (owned or registered lease deed for 15+ years) for CFC construction
- [ ] IA/SPV share of 10% (5% for NER and Hilly areas) of project cost can be contributed in cash
- [ ] Project-specific SPV formed as a 'for profit' entity with artisan members
- [ ] Minimum 100 artisans (50 for NER/Hilly areas) engaged or targetable
- [ ] Organization has presence and engagement in the proposed cluster/district
- [ ] Proposed project aligns with SFURTI objectives: collective formation, income enhancement, marketability, skill development, and common facilities
- [ ] No duplication of financial assistance from other Central/State schemes for the same cluster components

### Transition to Pitch
Based on what you’ve shared, your cluster appears to align well with the SFURTI scheme’s focus on organizing traditional artisans into collectives with infrastructure and market support. The scheme offers financial assistance for hard interventions like CFCs, machinery, and working capital—up to Rs. 2.5 crore for regular clusters and Rs. 5 crore for major clusters—along with soft interventions for skill development and market promotion. Shall I walk you through how this could work for your specific group?

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
Traditional artisan clusters often struggle with fragmented operations, limited access to modern infrastructure, outdated technology, weak market linkages, and inconsistent incomes. Many lack the resources to invest in Common Facility Centres, machinery, or working capital, hindering their ability to scale, improve product quality, and compete in domestic or e-commerce markets.

**Scheme intro:**
The Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI), administered by the Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME), provides grant-based financial support for hard interventions including Common Facility Centres (CFCs) with IP-enabled CCTV, worksheds, machinery procurement, working capital and raw material banks (RMBs), tools and technological upgradation, warehousing, and training centers. Financial assistance includes working capital up to 20% of project cost as a revolving fund for raw material procurement, direct wages, electricity, and water costs—recoupable from product sales. Support also covers soft interventions like skill development, design intervention, market promotion, e-Commerce initiatives, brand building, and institutional linkages. The scheme is delivered through Nodal Agencies (NAs) such as KVIC for Khadi and Village Industry clusters, Coir Board for Coir-based clusters, and other national-level institutions with sectoral expertise.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- Your organization works directly with traditional artisans in the Khadi/Village Industries/Coir sector, meeting the core beneficiary focus
- You have or can secure land with a registered sale or lease deed (15+ years) in the IA/SPV’s name for CFC construction
- You are prepared to form a project-specific SPV as a 'for profit' entity with artisan members contributing at least 10% (5% for NER/Hilly areas) of project cost
- Your cluster includes a significant proportion of artisans from marginalized communities (SCs, STs, women), aligning with SFURTI’s inclusion mandate

| "I don't have the documents ready" | The three most commonly missing documents are: (1) Audited Statements of Income & Expenditure and Balance Sheet for the last three years, (2) Registered lease or sale deed for land (15+ years) in the IA/SPV’s name, and (3) Self-attested comparative table showing present vs. projected artisan income increase with justification.
