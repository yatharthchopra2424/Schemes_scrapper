# Comprehensive Scheme Masterclass & File Guide

## Scheme Deep Dive

### Scheme Overview
The **Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries (SFURTI)** is a grant-based initiative by the **Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME)**, Government of India, aimed at organizing traditional industries and artisans into collectives for long-term sustainability, enhanced marketability, and improved livelihoods. The scheme operates on a **Pan-India** geographic scope and was last updated in **2022**. The official application portal is: **https://sfurti.msme.gov.in**.

### Objectives
SFURTI aims to achieve the following objectives:
- Organize traditional industries and artisans into collectives for competitiveness and long-term sustainability.
- Provide sustained employment for traditional industry artisans and producers.
- Enhance marketability of products through new products, design intervention, improved packaging, and marketing infrastructure.
- Equip artisans with improved skills and capabilities through training and exposure visits.
- Promote collective business enterprises of traditional artisans and producers.
- Make provision for common facilities and improved tools and equipment for optimum utilization of infrastructure.
- Set up multi-product clusters with integrated value chains and market-driven approaches.
- Strengthen cluster governance systems for equitable distribution of surplus.
- Focus on penetrating uncovered districts, aspirational districts, etc., with special focus on endangered art and crafts.
- Support traditional artisans with special focus on disadvantaged communities (SCs, STs, women, etc.).
- Ensure convergence from the design stage with each activity of cluster formation and operations.
- Identify and understand target customers, develop product lines to meet requirements, and undertake brand unification.
- Shift from supply-driven to market-driven selling models with right branding, product mix, positioning, and pricing.
- Tap E-Commerce as a major marketing channel.
- Make substantial investment in product design, quality improvement, R&D, and emerging technologies to standardize quality benchmarks.
- Promote latest technological advancements (Industry 4.0, Blockchain, digitization, green energy, etc.) to take local to global in line with 'Atma Nirbhar Bharat'.
- Encourage and promote green and sustainable products and processes.

### Eligibility Matrix
Eligibility criteria are categorized by applicant type (Implementing Agency - IA) and include mandatory requirements for land, SPV formation, beneficiary authentication, and financial contribution.

| **Eligibility Criteria** | **Details** | **Source** |
|--------------------------|-------------|------------|
| **Applicant Type** | Any Implementing Agency (IA) interested in submitting a proposal under SFURTI Scheme | Key Facts |
| **IA Registration** | Must be registered as an Implementing Agency on the SFURTI portal to create user ID and password | Key Facts, User Manual |
| **Concept Note Submission** | Must first submit a Concept note along with necessary documents on the SFURTI portal | Key Facts |
| **Nodal Agency Selection** | Proposals submitted online to Nodal Agencies (NAs): KVIC for Khadi and Village Industry clusters, Coir Board for Coir-based clusters, and other national-level institutions with sectoral expertise in Traditional Industries, competence in cluster/collective development, project management, and facilitation of market access, operating in the field for at least five years | Key Facts |
| **SPV Formation** | IA must authenticate the list of artisans, form a project-specific SPV as a 'for profit' entity | Key Facts |
| **Land Availability** | Ensure land availability for CFC construction (owned or leased for 15+ years) | Key Facts |
| **Financial Contribution** | Confirm IA/SPV's share of 10% (5% for NER and Hilly areas) of Hard Intervention cost | Key Facts, Guidelines |
| **Target Beneficiaries** | Traditional artisans; producers; SCs; STs; women; disadvantaged communities | Key Facts |
| **Land Documentation** | Registered sale or lease deed (long term of 15 years or more) in IA/SPV's name for land availability; Registered lease deed (if land is leased by SPV) | Key Facts |
| **No Prior Financial Assistance** | IA must undertake that no similar support has been availed for the same activities under the cluster from any other scheme of Government of India or State Government | Guidelines |

> **> Important Caveat**: The IA/SPV share is 10% of Hard Intervention cost in general areas, but reduced to 5% for North Eastern Region (NER) and Hilly areas. This contribution must be in cash and deposited in a dedicated joint account.

### Benefits & Financial Support
SFURTI provides financial support for both hard and soft interventions, with specific limits and conditions.

#### Financial Support Details
| **Intervention Type** | **Details** | **Maximum Limit** | **Source** |
|-----------------------|-------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Hard Interventions** | Creation of Common Facility Centres (CFCs) and worksheds with IP-enabled CCTV cameras; procurement of machineries; working capital and raw material banks (RMBs); tools and technological upgradation (e.g., charkha upgradation, tool-kit distribution); warehousing facilities; training centers | 90% of project cost (IA/SPV contributes 10% in cash; 5% for NER/Hilly areas) | Key Facts, Guidelines |
| **Working Capital** | Up to 20% of project cost for raw material procurement, direct wages, electricity and water costs; provided as a revolving fund for initial production cycle, to be recouped from product sales | 20% of project cost | Key Facts, Guidelines |
| **Soft Interventions** | Skill development and capacity building; machine handling and maintenance training; institution development; exposure visits; market promotion initiatives; design and product development; participation in seminars/workshops on technology upgradation | 100% funded (no IA contribution required) | Guidelines |
| **Thematic Interventions** | Brand building and promotion campaigns; new media marketing; e-Commerce initiatives; innovation; R&D initiatives; technological upgradation; developing institutional linkages | 100% funded (no IA contribution required) | Guidelines |
| **Total Project Cost** | Financial assistance capped at Rs. 5 crore per cluster | Rs. 5 crore | Guidelines |
| **Cluster Type Limits** | Regular Clusters (up to 500 artisans): Rs. 2.50 crore; Major Clusters (more than 500 artisans): Rs. 5.00 crore | Rs. 2.50 crore / Rs. 5.00 crore | Guidelines |
| **Minimum Artisans** | Clusters must have at least 100 artisans (50 for NER & Hilly areas) | 100 / 50 artisans | Guidelines |
| **IA Cost Coverage** | 8% of Hard Intervention cost or Rs. 20 lakh (whichever is less) for IA expenses including CDE remuneration | Rs. 20 lakh | Guidelines |
| **TA Cost Coverage** | 100% of TA cost (Rs. 15 lakh or 100% of Hard Intervention cost, whichever is less); additional 1% (up to Rs. 18 lakh) for NER/J&K/Hilly areas | Rs. 15 lakh / Rs. 18 lakh | Guidelines |
| **NA Administrative Fees** | 4% of total budget allocation for Nodal Agency administrative expenses | 4% of project cost | Guidelines |
| **Scheme Management Expenses** | 2% of total budget allocation earmarked for Ministry-level management (monitoring, publicity, PMU, etc.) | 2% of project cost | Guidelines |
| **Thematic Intervention Allocation** | Additional 5% of total budget allocation can be utilized by NA for thematic interventions (national marketing, brand promotion, CDE training, etc.) | 5% of project cost | Guidelines |

> **> Critical Note**: Working Capital support is a **revolving fund** that must be **recouped from the sale of products**. The need for Working Capital expenses must be clearly spelt out in business plans. Assistance for raw material procurement may be leveraged with financial institutions for enhanced credit.

### Application Process
The application process is entirely online via the SFURTI portal and involves sequential steps from IA registration to scorecard submission to the selected Nodal Agency.

#### Mermaid Flowchart: SFURTI Application Process
```mermaid

    A[Start: Register as Implementing Agency on SFURTI portal] --> B[Create User ID and Password]
    B --> C[Log in to portal]
    C --> D[Download Instruction/Guidelines for Concept Application and Scorecard]
    D --> E[Select 'Scorecard' from dashboard]
    E --> F[Fill basic details form and save]
    F --> G[Select 'Edit' option to proceed after saving Cluster Details]
    G --> H[Upload duly filled Concept Application Form]
    H --> I[Fill details against each indicator]
    I --> J[Select options and upload supporting evidence simultaneously before moving to next indicator]
    J --> K[Upload supporting evidence for each indicator (PDF, max 5 MB per indicator)]

    L --> M[View scorecard from 'View Scorecard' tab]
    M --> N[Select preferred Nodal Agency from drop-down list]
    N --> O[Click forward button to submit scorecard with application and documents to Nodal Agency]
    O --> P[Submission intimation message displayed]
    P --> Q[End: Scorecard can be printed if required]
```

#### Step-by-Step Breakdown
1. **Registration**: IA registers on the SFURTI portal to obtain user credentials.
2. **Login & Guidelines Download**: Post-login, download instructions/user manual as reference.
3. **Scorecard Selection**: Navigate to dashboard and select 'Scorecard'.
4. **Basic Details**: Fill and save basic details form.
5. **Edit Option**: Select 'Edit' after saving cluster details to begin form filling.
6. **Concept Application Upload**: Upload duly filled Concept Application Form (new applicants download format from dashboard).
7. **Indicator-wise Filling**: For each indicator:
   - Select appropriate options (radio/checkbox).
   - Automatically assigned marks based on scoring criteria.
   - Upload supporting evidence (PDF, max 5 MB) simultaneously before proceeding.
8. **Multiple Options**: For indicators allowing multiple selections, check all applicable boxes.
9. **Scorecard View**: Use 'View Scorecard' tab to review completed scorecard.
10. **Nodal Agency Selection**: Choose preferred Nodal Agency from drop-down list.
11. **Submission**: Click forward button to submit scorecard, application, and documents to selected Nodal Agency.
12. **Confirmation**: Submission intimation message displayed; scorecard printable if needed.

#### Supporting Evidence Requirements
- **Document Format**: PDF only, max 5 MB per indicator.
- **Multiple Documents**: Must be merged into a single PDF per indicator.
- **Mandatory Upload**: At least one supporting document per indicator (except 'Nature of District', which uses Annexure list).
- **Evidence Types**: Project Initiation/Completion Reports, Evaluation/Monitoring reports, Certificate of Incorporation, year-wise project summary, village-wise beneficiary summary, intervention summary, audited statements (last 3 years), Utilisation Certificate, Grant Letter, comparative income table, registered lease/sale deed (15+ years), etc.
- **Scoring Basis**: Marks auto-assigned based on selected options and uploaded evidence.

#### Scoring & Qualification
- **Total Score**: 100 marks.
- **Minimum Qualifying Score**: 60% (60 marks) to proceed to next stage.
- **Sections**: Scorecard divided into:
  - Assessment of Implementing Agency
  - Assessment of Project Concept
- **Validation**: Nodal Agency verifies uploaded documents and validates scores against each indicator.
- **Outcome**: 
  - If score ≥ 60: Concept forwarded for Detailed Project Report (DPR) development.
  - If score < 60: Proposal returned to IA for revision.

> **> Key Reminder**: The IA must upload the Concept Application Form **before** filling the online scorecard; otherwise, the scorecard cannot be filled.
