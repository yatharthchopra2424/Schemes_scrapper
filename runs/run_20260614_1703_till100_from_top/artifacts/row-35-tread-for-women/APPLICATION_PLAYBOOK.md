# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Woman entrepreneur above 18 years of age
- [ ] Engaged in or planning to set up a micro or small enterprise
- [ ] Enterprise operates in the non-farm sector
- [ ] Preference given to applicants from SC/ST, OBC, minority, or differently abled communities (if applicable)
- [ ] Project report prepared for the proposed business idea
- [ ] Beneficiary willing to contribute at least 70% of project cost from own sources or loan
- [ ] Grant will not be used for land acquisition or working capital

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- PAN card
- Aadhaar card
- Udyam Registration Certificate
- Bank account details (cancelled cheque or bank statement)
- Income Tax Returns (ITR) for past 1-3 years
- GST certificate and returns (if applicable)
- Business address proof

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Project report
- Proof of identity (Aadhaar, PAN, etc.)
- Address proof
- Educational qualification certificates (if applicable)
- Caste certificate (for SC/ST/OBC beneficiaries)
- Disability certificate (if applicable)
- Quotations for machinery and equipment
- Land/building ownership or lease documents
- No objection certificate from pollution control board (if applicable)

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible and in PDF or clear image format
- File size should not exceed 5 MB per document
- Names on documents must match exactly as per application
- Self-attested copies acceptable; gazetted officer attestation preferred for affidavits
- Documents must be in English or Hindi; certified translation required for other languages

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
Yes, a project report is required. Standard sections include:  
- Executive summary  
- Business profile and promoter details  
- Project description and objectives  
- Market analysis and demand assessment  
- Technical details (process, machinery, technology)  
- Financial projections (cost, revenue, profitability, break-even)  
- Implementation schedule  
- Risk assessment and mitigation plan  
- Employment generation details  
- Sustainability and social impact  

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Download the application form from the designated implementing agency’s portal (KVIC, Coir Board, or MSME-DFO)  
2. Fill all fields in block letters to avoid rejection  
3. Ensure project cost is clearly stated; grant requested must not exceed 30% of project cost or INR 10 lakhs, whichever is lower  
4. Clearly mention the 70% beneficiary contribution (own funds or loan)  
5. Attach all required documents in the specified order  
6. Sign and date the declaration section  
7. Verify that the enterprise is in the non-farm sector before submission  
8. Retain a copy of the submitted application for records  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All eligibility criteria verified and documented  
- [ ] Project report completed with all standard sections  
- [ ] Application form filled completely and accurately  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents attached  
- [ ] Declarations signed and dated  
- [ ] Grant amount does not exceed INR 10 lakhs or 30% of project cost  
- [ ] Beneficiary contribution of at least 70% confirmed  
- [ ] Enterprise confirmed to be in non-farm sector  
- [ ] Copy of application stored with reference number noted  

---

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Submit the completed application to the designated implementing agency (KVIC, Coir Board, or MSME-DFO) either online or in person  
- Obtain and screenshot the submission acknowledgment or receipt  
- Store the application reference number securely  
- Set follow-up reminders for 15 days after submission if no update is received

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify the sanction letter specifying the approved grant amount  
- [ ] Confirm the sanctioned amount does not exceed INR 10 lakhs or 30% of project cost  
- [ ] Arrange for the beneficiary’s 70% contribution (own funds or loan disbursement)  
- [ ] Utilize grant funds strictly as per the approved project plan (installment-based disbursement tied to progress)  
- [ ] Ensure grant is not used for land acquisition or working capital  
- [ ] Submit utilization reports and progress updates as required by the implementing agency  
- [ ] Maintain all bills, vouchers, and records of expenditure for audit  
- [ ] Prepare and submit final utilization certificate upon project completion  
- [ ] Follow up for success fee payment as per consultancy agreement (to be filled by consultant)
