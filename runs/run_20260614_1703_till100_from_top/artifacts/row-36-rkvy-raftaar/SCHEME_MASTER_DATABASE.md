### RKVY-RAFTAAR
- **Scheme ID:** SCH-001
- **Category:** Grant
- **Geography:** Pan-India
- **Administering Body:** Department of Agriculture, Cooperation & Farmers Welfare, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, Government of India
- **Portal / Application URL:** https://rkvy.nic.in/
- **Current Status:** Rolling
- **Window:** Applications are invited periodically by State Agriculture Departments; specific deadlines vary by state and are notified through official communications.

#### Eligibility Criteria
- Business type: Agri-startups, agri-entrepreneurs, farmers, farmer producer organizations (FPOs), self-help groups (SHGs), cooperatives, NGOs, and MSMEs engaged in agriculture and allied sectors
- Age of business: Not specified
- Sector: Agriculture and allied sectors
- Revenue cap: Not specified
- Employee count: Not specified
- Other: Projects must be innovative, feasible, and have potential for scalability and impact on farmers' income; preference given to projects promoting value addition, post-harvest management, and market linkage

#### What It Offers
- Amount / Benefit: Grant amount varies based on project proposal and is sanctioned by the State Level Sanctioning Committee (SLSC); funds released in installments based on project milestones and utilization certificates
- Disbursement mode: Direct credit (in installments based on milestones)
- Duration of benefit: Project-based (disbursed in installments over project duration)

#### Documents Required
1. Detailed Project Report (DPR)
2. Certificate of Incorporation / Registration (for startups, FPOs, NGOs, etc.)
3. PAN of the applicant entity
4. Aadhaar of authorized signatory
5. Bank account details
6. Land lease/ownership documents (if applicable)
7. Quotations for equipment and machinery
8. Undertaking of compliance with scheme guidelines

#### Application Process (numbered steps)
1. Identify a viable agri-project idea aligned with RKVY-RAFTAAR objectives.
2. Prepare a detailed project report (DPR) including technical, financial, and economic feasibility.
3. Submit the application through the State Agriculture Department or designated nodal agency.
4. Application is screened by the State Level Sanctioning Committee (SLSC) for eligibility and merit.
5. Upon approval, grant is sanctioned and released in installments.
6. Project implementation begins with monitoring and evaluation by the implementing agency.
7. Submit utilization certificates and progress reports for subsequent fund releases.
