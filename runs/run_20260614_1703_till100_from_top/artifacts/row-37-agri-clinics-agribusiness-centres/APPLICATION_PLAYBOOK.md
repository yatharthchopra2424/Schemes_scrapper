# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is an individual with agricultural graduation (B.Sc. Agriculture or allied fields) or equivalent qualification  
- [ ] Applicant has completed training from a Nodal Training Institute (NTI) accredited under the scheme  
- [ ] Applicant is setting up an Agri Clinic or Agribusiness Centre (AC&ABC) for providing paid extension services to farmers  
- [ ] Applicant is an Indian citizen  
- [ ] Applicant is aged between 18 and 60 years  
- [ ] Applicant has not availed benefit under any other similar central government scheme for the same activity  
- [ ] Project is for establishment of a new Agri Clinic or Agribusiness Centre (expansion of existing unit not eligible)  
- [ ] Applicant has secured or can secure loan from a bank/financial institution for the project (scheme provides back-ended subsidy, not direct grant)  
- [ ] Project activity falls under permissible services: crop husbandry, horticulture, animal husbandry, fisheries, agro-forestry, agro-processing, market linkage, input supply, or other farmer advisory services  

> **Note**: Eligibility criteria are based on scheme guidelines for Agri Clinics & Agribusiness Centres (AC&ABC) implemented by MANAGE (National Institute of Agricultural Extension Management) under Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare. Despite low confidence in structured extraction, these criteria are well-established in public scheme documentation.

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- [ ] PAN Card of applicant  
- [ ] Aadhaar Card of applicant  
- [ ] Bank passbook or cancelled cheque (for loan disbursement and subsidy transfer)  
- [ ] Udyam Registration Certificate (if applicable for business entity)  
- [ ] Income Tax Returns (ITR) of last 2 years (if applicable)  
- [ ] GST Registration Certificate (if turnover exceeds threshold)  
- [ ] Bank statement of last 6 months  
- [ ] Passport-sized photographs (white background)  
- [ ] Educational certificates (B.Sc. Agriculture or allied degree)  
- [ ] Training completion certificate from Nodal Training Institute (NTI)  

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- [ ] Detailed Project Report (DPR) outlining services to be offered, operational plan, and financial projections  
- [ ] Letter of intent or sanction letter from bank/financial institution for term loan (minimum 15% of project cost as promoter’s contribution)  
- [ ] Land ownership/lease agreement for the proposed AC&ABC unit (minimum 1 year lease if rented)  
- [ ] Quotations for equipment, furniture, and IT infrastructure (as per DPR)  
- [ ] Registration certificate of business entity (if applicable: Proprietorship, Partnership, LLP, or Company)  
- [ ] Affidavit declaring no duplicate benefit from other central government schemes  
- [ ] CV/resume of applicant highlighting agricultural training and experience  

### Document Quality Check
- [ ] All documents are legible, scanned in PDF format (minimum 300 DPI)  
- [ ] File names follow format: `SchemeName_ApplicantName_DocumentType_Date.pdf` (e.g., `ACABC_RajuSharma_PAN_20240515.pdf`)  
- [ ] Photographs are recent (within last 6 months), with white background, and clearly show full face  
- [ ] Educational and training certificates are self-attested; notarization not required unless specified by nodal officer  
- [ ] Bank documents show active account in applicant’s name  
- [ ] Land documents are registered and valid for intended use  
- [ ] Affidavit is on non-judicial stamp paper of appropriate value (as per state norms) and duly notarized

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
**Required**: Yes, a Detailed Project Report (DPR) is mandatory for the Agri Clinics & Agribusiness Centres (AC&ABC) scheme.  

**Standard Sections to Include**:  
1. Executive Summary  
2. Applicant Profile (qualification, training, experience)  
3. Project Description (services offered: advisory, input supply, market linkage, agro-processing, etc.)  
4. Location Details (address, land ownership/lease, connectivity)  
5. Operational Plan (working hours, staffing, service delivery model)  
6. Marketing & Outreach Strategy (farmer engagement, partnerships with FPOs/KVKs)  
7. Financial Plan:  
   - Total project cost (breakup: civil works, equipment, IT, working capital)  
   - Means of finance (promoter’s contribution, bank loan, expected subsidy)  
   - 3-year projected income & expenditure statement  
   - Break-even analysis  
   - Debt service coverage ratio (DSCR)  
8. Risk Assessment & Mitigation Plan  
9. Annexures: Quotations, land documents, training certificate, bank sanction letter  

> **Note**: DPR must be realistic and bankable. MANAGE and NTIs often provide templates; verify with your assigned NTI.

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Obtain the application form from the designated Nodal Training Institute (NTI) or download from MANAGE website (https://manage.gov.in) under AC&ABC section.  
2. Fill all fields in **Block Letters** using black ink; do not leave any field blank — write “NA” if not applicable.  
3. Ensure applicant’s name matches exactly as per PAN and Aadhaar across all fields.  
4. Enter correct NTI code and training completion details (date, course name, duration).  
5. In the “Project Details” section, clearly list services to be offered (use standardized terms: e.g., “Crop Advisory”, “Input Supply”, “Market Linkage”).  
6. Provide accurate project cost estimates based on quotations; avoid overestimation to prevent scrutiny delays.  
7. Double-check bank loan details: sanction amount, rate of interest, repayment tenure, and disbursement status.  
8. Sign the declaration form affirming no duplicate benefit and accuracy of information; get it witnessed if required by NTI.  
9. Attach self-attested copies of all documents as per checklist; do not submit originals unless specifically requested.  
10. Submit the completed form to the NTI coordinator within the stipulated window; obtain acknowledgment receipt.  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] Application form is completely filled with no blank fields (NA used where applicable)  
- [ ] Applicant name, PAN, and Aadhaar details match across form and documents  
- [ ] Training certificate details (NTI, date, course) are correctly entered  
- [ ] Project cost in DPR matches the sum of quotations and bank loan sanction  
- [ ] Promoter’s contribution is at least 15% of total project cost  
- [ ] Bank loan sanction letter is attached and valid (not older than 3 months)  
- [ ] All documents are self-attested, legible, and in correct format  
- [ ] Declaration form is signed and dated  
- [ ] DPR includes all 9 standard sections and is financially realistic  
- [ ] Application is submitted to the correct NTI with acknowledgment received

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- [ ] Submit the completed application form and document set to the Nodal Training Institute (NTI) coordinator  
- [ ] Obtain a signed acknowledgment receipt with date, application number, and NTI stamp  
- [ ] Screenshot or scan the acknowledgment and save in case folder: `CaseID_ACABC_Acknowledgment.pdf`  
- [ ] Note the expected date of SLSC (State Level Screening Committee) scrutiny (usually monthly)  
- [ ] Set calendar reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days post-submission  
- [ ] Store all communication and documents in the scheme-specific folder: `/ACABC/CaseID/`

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Receive and verify approval letter from SLSC/NTI specifying sanctioned subsidy amount and release schedule  
- [ ] Confirm that the sanctioned amount aligns with scheme norms (max 44% of project cost, capped at ₹16.5 lakh general / ₹18.7 lakh special category)  
- [ ] Ensure bank loan disbursement has commenced as per loan sanction terms  
- [ ] Monitor unit establishment progress against DPR timelines  
- [ ] After commercial operation begins, submit Utilization Certificate (UC) and expenditure proof to NTI for first subsidy installment  
- [ ] For subsequent installments, provide proof of loan utilization, asset verification, and operational status  
- [ ] Maintain records of all invoices, payment vouchers, and asset register for audit purposes  
- [ ] Coordinate with client to claim success fee upon verified receipt of first subsidy installment  
- [ ] Advise client on compliance: display AC&ABC board, maintain service records, and renew NTI affiliation if required  
- [ ] Schedule post-utilization follow-up at 6 and 12 months to ensure sustained operations and eligibility for any top-up support  

> **Note**: Subsidy is released in 2–3 installments linked to verification of unit establishment and loan utilization. No subsidy is disbursed upfront.
