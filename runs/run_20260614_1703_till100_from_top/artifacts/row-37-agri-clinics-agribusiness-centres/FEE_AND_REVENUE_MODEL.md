## Pricing Principles
1. **Value-Aligned Fees**: Success fees are calibrated to the actual financial benefit delivered under the Agri Clinics & Agribusiness Centres scheme, ensuring clients pay only for realized outcomes.
2. **Risk-Adjusted Pricing**: Given the scheme’s low confidence rating and variable processing times observed in implementation, retainer fees cover upfront advisory work, while success fees are contingent on approval and disbursement.
3. **Tiered Accessibility**: Pricing tiers align with the scheme’s target beneficiary profile — primarily individuals with agricultural qualifications setting up agribusiness ventures — making services accessible to early-stage agri-entrepreneurs.
4. **Transparency & Predictability**: Clear fee structures reduce client uncertainty; retainers cover eligibility assessment and application preparation, while success fees are triggered only upon formal sanction of benefits.

*Note: The scheme’s moderate risk profile — stemming from field verification requirements, dependency on NABARD or state agency coordination, and variable disbursement timelines — supports a success fee range of 4–8% depending on client tier, reflecting both effort and outcome uncertainty.*

## Invoice Templates

### Retainer Invoice
**Description**: Retainer fee for advisory services under the Agri Clinics & Agribusiness Centres scheme (Scheme ID: row-37), including eligibility assessment, business plan preparation, documentation support, and application submission to the implementing agency (typically NABARD or State Department of Agriculture).

### Success Fee Invoice
**Description**: Success fee for successful sanction of benefits under the Agri Clinics & Agribusiness Centres scheme (Scheme ID: row-37), implemented by the National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) in coordination with State Governments, upon receipt of formal approval letter or sanction order by the client.
