# Application Playbook

## How to Use This File
For each new client case, assign it a Case ID, pick the target scheme, and follow the steps below.

---

## Stage 1: Eligibility Verification (Day 1–3)

### Checklist
- [ ] Applicant is a registered startup or innovation entity (as per NABARD's definition under the Startup & Innovation Fund)
- [ ] Entity is registered under the Companies Act, 2013, LLP Act, 2008, or as a partnership firm
- [ ] Business is engaged in agriculture, allied activities, or rural innovation sectors
- [ ] Entity has been in operation for not more than 7 years from date of incorporation/registration
- [ ] Annual turnover does not exceed INR 25 crore in any preceding financial year
- [ ] Entity is recognized by DPIIT as a startup (or eligible for such recognition)
- [ ] Project proposal demonstrates innovation in agriculture, rural livelihood, or allied sectors
- [ ] Applicant has not availed similar funding from other government schemes for the same project
- [ ] Promoters/directors are Indian nationals and not defaulters to any financial institution
- [ ] Entity has a valid Udyam Registration (if applicable) or equivalent MSME registration

> *Note: Eligibility criteria are inferred from NABARD’s general startup innovation framework due to limited scheme-specific details in evidence. Verify exact thresholds with NABARD nodal officer.*

---

## Stage 2: Document Collection (Day 3–10)

### Universal Documents (always required)
- Certificate of Incorporation / Registration (ROC, LLP, or Partnership deed)
- PAN Card of the entity
- PAN Cards of all promoters/directors
- Aadhaar Cards of all promoters/directors
- GST Registration Certificate (if applicable)
- Audited Financial Statements (ITR, Balance Sheet, P&L) for last 2–3 years
- Bank Statements (last 6 months)
- Udyam Registration Certificate (if MSME)
- Cancelled cheque or bank verification letter
- Passport-sized photographs of promoters/directors
- Board Resolution authorizing application (for companies/LLPs)
- KYC documents of promoters/directors (as per RBI guidelines)

### Scheme-Specific Documents
- Detailed Project Report (DPR) outlining innovation, technology, scalability, and impact on agriculture/rural sectors
- Proof of DPIIT startup recognition (certificate or application acknowledgment)
- Sector-specific licenses or certifications (e.g., FSSAI for food processing, pollution control board for agri-waste)
- Letters of intent or MoUs with farmer producer organizations (FPOs), cooperatives, or rural institutions
- Patent filings, copyrights, or technical validation reports (if innovation involves IP)
- Utilization plan for funds (phase-wise allocation)
- CVs/resumes of key technical personnel
- Proof of own contribution (minimum 10–20% as per typical NABARD norms)

### Document Quality Check
- All documents must be legible, scanned in PDF format (minimum 300 DPI)
- File names must follow format: `SchemeName_DocumentType_ApplicantName_Date.pdf` (e.g., `NABARD_InnovationFund_DPR_ABCStartups_20240515.pdf`)
- No password protection or encryption on submitted files
- All certificates must be valid and not expired (check issue/expiry dates)
- Documents in regional languages must be accompanied by certified Hindi/English translations
- Financial documents must be certified by a Chartered Accountant (CA)
- DPR must be signed and stamped by the applicant and CA/technical expert
- Maximum file size per document: 10 MB; total upload size must not exceed portal limits

## Stage 3: Application Drafting (Day 10–18)

### Project Report / DPR (if required)
✅ **Required**  
Standard sections to include:  
1. Executive Summary  
2. Promoter Profile and Team Expertise  
3. Problem Statement in Agriculture/Rural Sector  
4. Innovative Solution (Technology, Process, or Model)  
5. Market Analysis and Scalability Potential  
6. Technical Feasibility and IP Status (if any)  
7. Implementation Timeline (Gantt Chart)  
8. Financial Projections (5-year)  
9. Fund Utilization Plan (Phase-wise: CAPEX, OPEX, WC)  
10. Social Impact Metrics (e.g., farmers reached, jobs created, income enhancement)  
11. Risk Assessment and Mitigation Plan  
12. Annexures: Licenses, Patents, MoUs, Auditor Certificates  

> *DPR must be prepared by a CA or technical expert and signed/stamped by applicant and certifier.*

### Application Form Filling Protocol
1. Access the official NABARD portal (verify active URL with nodal officer; current evidence shows https://nabard.org returns 404)  
2. Select “Startup & Innovation Fund” under “Innovation & Refinance” or “Venture Capital” section  
3. Fill all mandatory fields marked with * — do not leave any blank  
4. Enter entity details exactly as per Incorporation Certificate and PAN  
5. Turnover and employee count must match audited financials and EPF/ESIC records  
6. Describe innovation clearly in the “Project Description” field (max 500 words; avoid jargon)  
7. Upload documents in prescribed format and size — verify each upload opens correctly  
8. Declarations: Self-certify no default, no duplicate funding, and accuracy of information  
9. Preview form before final submit — no edits allowed after submission  
10. Save application reference number immediately upon successful submission  

### Internal Review Gate
- [ ] All personal and entity details match supporting documents  
- [ ] Turnover, employee count, and age of business meet eligibility thresholds  
- [ ] DPR is complete, signed, and aligned with form entries  
- [ ] All universal and scheme-specific documents are attached and named correctly  
- [ ] File sizes are within limits and formats are PDF (unless specified otherwise)  
- [ ] Declarations are signed and dated  
- [ ] Application preview reviewed for errors or omissions  
- [ ] Reference number protocol established for post-submission tracking

## Stage 4: Submission & Follow-Up (Day 18–30)

### Submission Protocol
- Take a screenshot of the confirmation page showing Application Reference Number and timestamp  
- Store the reference number in the case file and share with client via email  
- Save a PDF copy of the submitted application and all uploaded documents  
- Set calendar reminders for follow-up at 15, 30, and 45 days post-submission  
- Monitor registered email and portal dashboard for updates or queries

## Stage 5: Post-Approval (Day 30–90+)

### Approval Actions
- [ ] Obtain and verify approval letter from NABARD specifying sanctioned amount, terms, and conditions  
- [ ] Confirm disbursement schedule and linkage to milestones (if tranche-based)  
- [ ] Open separate bank account for fund utilization (if required by NABARD)  
- [ ] Notify client of utilization certificate (UC) submission timelines and format  
- [ ] Guide client on maintaining proper records of invoices, vouchers, and expenditure  
- [ ] Prepare utilization certificate as per NABARD format, duly certified by CA  
- [ ] Submit UC and supporting documents before deadline to avoid refund or penalty  
- [ ] Track project progress against DPR milestones for internal reporting  
- [ ] Coordinate with client for success fee invoicing upon verified fund utilization  
- [ ] Maintain all records for audit and potential post-approval monitoring by NABARD
