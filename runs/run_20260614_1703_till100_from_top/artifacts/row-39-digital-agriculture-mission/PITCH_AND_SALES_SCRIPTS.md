# Pitch & Sales Scripts

## Philosophy
We are advisors, not salespeople. We diagnose first. We only pitch a scheme if it fits the client. We never oversell success rates.

---

## 1. Discovery Call Script (First Contact — 20 min)

### Opening (2 min)
Good morning/afternoon. I specialize in helping state governments, UT administrations, agri-startups, research institutions, and farming communities access central government funding for digital agriculture initiatives. The Digital Agriculture Mission is designed to build a farmer-centric digital ecosystem using technologies like AI, IoT, and blockchain to improve transparency, efficiency, and policy-making in agriculture. I’d like to understand your current digital agriculture initiatives and see if this mission aligns with your goals.

### Discovery Questions
1. What is your organization’s current stage in implementing digital agriculture technologies such as AI, IoT, or blockchain for farming or policy purposes?
2. Are you proposing a project under the Digital Agriculture Mission for Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI), Digital General Crop Estimation Survey (DGCES), support to MNCFC, or IT initiatives for states/academic institutions?
3. Do you have a Detailed Project Report (DPR), financial implications, clear timelines, and technology details ready for submission?
4. Is your project focused on building scalable digital solutions using emerging technologies aligned with Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) in Agriculture?
5. Are you planning to submit your proposal preferably in the first quarter but not later than the second quarter of the Financial Year?
6. Does your project involve manpower deployment, infrastructure procurement, or utilization of existing state funds for Digital Crop Survey (DCS) activities?
7. Are you prepared to share cost-benefit analysis and satisfactory status reports via PFMS for fund tranche releases?
8. Do you accept the Centre’s co-ownership of intellectual property created during the pilot and agree to share outcomes with the Central Government?

### Qualification Checklist (internal — don't read aloud)
- [ ] Applicant is a State/UT, academic/research institution, or entity proposing IT initiatives under the Mission
- [ ] Project involves DPI for Agriculture, DGCES, support to MNCFC, or IT initiatives for States/Academic & Research Institutions
- [ ] Proposal includes DPR, financial implications, clear timelines, and technology involved
- [ ] Submission timeline: preferably Q1, not later than Q2 of Financial Year
- [ ] Willing to submit satisfactory status reports via PFMS for 4 equal tranche releases
- [ ] Accepts Centre’s co-ownership of IP and agrees to share outcomes and cost-benefit analysis
- [ ] Pilot project lifecycle preferably one year, extendable up to two years with prior approval
- [ ] Basic infrastructure (hardware & manpower) costs borne by the State/UT (if applicable)

### Transition to Pitch
Based on your responses, it appears your initiative around [specific technology/project mentioned] aligns well with the Digital Agriculture Mission’s goals of building a farmer-centric digital ecosystem. The mission provides financial assistance for DCS pilots, manpower deployment, infrastructure procurement, and 100% funding for academic institutions building scalable digital solutions—exactly the support you’re seeking.

## 2. Scheme Pitch (5 min)

### Structure: Problem → Scheme → Value → Proof → Next Step

**Problem framing:**
State governments, UT administrations, and agri-tech entities face challenges in adopting modern digital agriculture technologies due to high upfront costs, lack of integrated systems, and fragmented data. This hinders evidence-based policy making, real-time crop surveying, soil mapping, and efficient scheme delivery—especially for farmers who need timely advisories and transparent access to benefits.

**Scheme intro:**
The Digital Agriculture Mission, implemented by the Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (DA&FW), Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Government of India, provides financial assistance for Digital Crop Survey (DCS) pilots, manpower deployment for DCS/Help Desk/PMU, procurement of DCS-related infrastructure, and utilization of existing state funds for DCS activities. Academic and research institutes may receive 100% funds for building scalable digital solutions using advanced technologies aligned with Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) in Agriculture, subject to recommendation by a High-level Evaluation Committee. The mission has a fund size of ₹2,817 Crore.

**Why they qualify (personalised):**
- You are proposing a project involving Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) for Agriculture, Digital General Crop Estimation Survey (DGCES), support to MNCFC, or IT initiatives for States/Academic & Research Institutions
- Your proposal includes a Detailed Project Report (DPR), financial implications, clear timelines, and technology involved
- You plan to submit preferably in the first quarter but not later than the second quarter of the Financial Year
- You are prepared to share cost-benefit analysis and satisfactory status reports via PFMS for fund releases
